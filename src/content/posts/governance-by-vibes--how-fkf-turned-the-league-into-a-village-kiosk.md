If you thought Kenyan football was finally moving past the "dark ages," I have a bridge in Nyayo Stadium to sell you. The Sports Disputes Tribunal (SDT) just slapped the Football Kenya Federation (FKF) across the face, reminding everyone that running a league requires actual brains, not just a flashy title. By quashing that ridiculous 1-1 decision for the abandoned Gor Mahia vs Nairobi United match, the SDT basically confirmed that the FKF is making up rules as they go. They were trying to use a 2025 rule book that hasn't even been approved by the General Assembly yet. It is the sporting equivalent of [Faith Kipyegon's Exit Strategy: Is the Track Too Small for the Smiling Assassin's Ego?](/posts/faith-kipyegon-s-exit-strategy--is-the-track-too-small-for-the-smiling-assassin-s-ego) - pure chaos and questionable management decisions that leave fans wondering who is actually in charge of the script.

Lordvick Aduda, who has seen enough of this circus to know where the clowns hide their makeup, pointed out the obvious. How do you run a top-flight league without functional judicial bodies? The Disciplinary, Appeals, and Ethics Committees are currently ghosts because the General Assembly hasn't bothered to give them a mandate. This means every "ruling" made so far is about as legal as a back-alley deal in River Road. It is embarrassing, but then again, when has shame ever stopped a Kenyan administrator from collecting a paycheck? Aduda is right to ask where this leaves the league as we head into the second leg.

Remember when everyone cheered for Hussein Mohammed and his promised "fresh start"? Well, the smell in the air isn't exactly fresh. Aduda isn't buying the hype, and neither should you. Systemic rot doesn't disappear just because you changed the guy sitting in the big chair. When the Leagues and Competitions Committee (LCC) starts awarding points for unfinished matches like they are handing out sweets at a political rally, you know the integrity of the league has been flushed down the toilet. They decided a match ended 1-1 at the 59th minute because they felt like it, despite having zero authority to make that call.

Then there is the absolute comedy of errors in the National Super League involving Mully Children's Family (MCF). The club quit, cited "financial constraints," and then magically reappeared three weeks later after a VIP visit from the federation bigwigs. The funny part? The LCC chairman, Peter 'Kass Kass' Kamau, had no idea they were even back in the fixtures. If the committee supposedly running the league doesn't know who is playing on Saturday, then who is actually pulling the strings? It is a shadow government of football, where decisions are made in closed offices and the rules are whatever the boss decided over lunch.

We are heading into the business end of the season with a league that has no legal backbone, no clear rules, and a federation that treats the SDT like a minor annoyance. Nairobi United lost their appeal for a full win, the FKF lost its last shred of credibility, and the fans are the biggest losers of all. In this country, we don't play football; we play "guess the regulation." It is a pathetic state of affairs that proves one thing: in Kenyan sports, the only thing that is consistent is the incompetence.
