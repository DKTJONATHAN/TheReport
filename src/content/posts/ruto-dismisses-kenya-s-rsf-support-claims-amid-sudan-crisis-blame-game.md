Ruto Denies Kenya Backs RSF in Sudan Mess, Blames Everyone Else

Ruto claims both sides in Sudan are equally to blame for the unending mess. Apparently, Kenya isn't secretly fueling the RSF, despite whispers of Kenyan passports floating around. He's now teaming up with Egypt, the US, Saudi Arabia, and the UAE to sort out this "scuffle," which has been going on for three years and turned Sudan into a disaster zone. Typical.

So, according to our President, both the SAF and RSF are two peas in a pod, born from the same coup and now they've decided to drag Sudan through the mud. He's calling the chaos "unprecedented," which is rich coming from a man who seems to think a bit of tough talk will solve a full-blown humanitarian crisis. It’s like watching someone try to put out a fire with a teacup, and frankly, it reminds me a bit of how some local businesses get squeezed out when the big players move in, like those [Mombasa Tea Exporters Drowning: Mid East Chaos Meets Port Greed](/posts/mombasa-tea-exporters-drowning--mid-east-chaos-meets-port-greed) I read about.

Ruto’s quick to deny any Kenyan involvement with the RSF, even though some reports suggest otherwise. It's a classic deflection, isn't it? Blame the rumour mill, blame the propaganda, anything but admit that maybe, just maybe, Kenya's hands aren't entirely clean in this regional mess. He’s talking about bringing "these two gentlemen" to the table, as if this is just a simple disagreement between chaps, not a brutal war that’s created the world's biggest hunger and displacement crises.

Meanwhile, the UAE, which is apparently arming the RSF, is also playing the denial game. Conveniently, they've got a long-standing beef with the Sudanese Muslim Brotherhood, which they claim is part of the SAF. It’s all very convenient, isn't it? Everyone’s got their own agenda, their own historical grudges, and Sudan is just the unfortunate playground. The US is even throwing in their two cents, labelling various Brotherhood branches as terrorist groups, because, you know, that's exactly what the region needs right now - more labels and more division.

The "Quad" - Egypt, the US, Saudi Arabia, and the UAE - are supposed to be brokering peace, but their talks have been stalled for months. It's a bit like trying to get a straight answer out of a politician; it's always promising, but rarely delivers. Ruto's pushing for "tough talk," but what Sudan needs is actual action, not more diplomatic posturing. Women, children, and families are suffering, and all we get are denials and blame games.

This whole situation is just another reminder of how predictable these conflicts are. It’s the same old story, different country, different leaders. Millions displaced, tens of thousands dead, and our leaders are busy with press conferences and denials. Makes you wonder if anyone genuinely cares about the suffering, or if it's all just a performance.







President Ruto is out here trying to spin the Sudan disaster, claiming both sides are equally to blame. Apparently, Kenya isn't some shadowy supporter of the RSF, despite whispers of Kenyan passports being handed out like party favors. He’s now hobnobbing with Egypt, the US, Saudi Arabia, and the UAE - the so-called Quad - to somehow fix this mess that's been festering for three years and turned Sudan into a wasteland. Honestly, it's a classic case of pointing fingers to avoid looking at your own shadow.

According to our esteemed President, the SAF and RSF are just two sides of the same rotten coin, products of the same coup that tossed out a civilian government. Now they're squabbling and have, as he so eloquently put it, "taken the country to the dogs." He's calling the ensuing mayhem and refugee situation "unprecedented," which is a bit rich when you consider how many of these regional squabbles unfold in a depressingly similar fashion. It’s almost as predictable as the way certain economic pressures can affect local businesses, leaving them struggling to stay afloat, a bit like the tales I've heard about those [Mombasa Tea Exporters Drowning: Mid East Chaos Meets Port Greed](/posts/mombasa-tea-exporters-drowning--mid-east-chaos-meets-port-greed).

Ruto's also busy distancing Kenya from any RSF ties, despite claims that individuals linked to the group were facilitated with Kenyan passports. It’s the oldest trick in the book: deny, deny, deny, and then blame the "rumours and propaganda." He reckons this blame game is just a distraction from getting a ceasefire, which, let's be honest, has been about as successful as trying to herd cats. He’s all for "tough talk" and bringing "these two gentlemen" to the table, as if a war of this magnitude is just a minor inconvenience.

Meanwhile, the UAE, a frequent suspect in arming the RSF, is also singing the denial tune. It’s a convenient narrative, especially with their historical opposition to the Sudanese Muslim Brotherhood, which they paint as integral to the SAF. The US, ever eager to get involved, has even declared some Brotherhood branches terrorist organizations, adding another layer of complexity to an already explosive situation. The RSF themselves are facing accusations of mass atrocities, including genocide against black Sudanese, but that doesn't seem to be slowing down anyone's agenda.

The peace talks, spearheaded by the Quad, have been going nowhere for months. Talks mediated by the US and Saudi Arabia in January were supposedly being studied, but here we are, still talking about war and displacement. Since April 2023, tens of thousands have died, over 11 million people are on the run, and the world's largest hunger and displacement crises are unfolding. Ruto’s promise to do "whatever it takes" rings a bit hollow when the reality on the ground is just continued suffering for women, children, and families.

This whole saga is just another testament to the political theatre we're constantly subjected to. Leaders making pronouncements, denying responsibility, and forming alliances, all while the human cost continues to mount. It's a cynical dance, and the people of Sudan are the ones paying the ultimate price for this predictable, disheartening performance.
