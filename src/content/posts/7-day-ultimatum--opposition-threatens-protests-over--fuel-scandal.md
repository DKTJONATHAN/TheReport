7-DAY FUEL SCANDAL ULTIMATUM: OPPOSITION THREATENS PROTESTS AGAINST RUTO'S DEAL

Opposition's ultimatum to Ruto: Cancel G-to-G fuel deal, scrap taxes, or face mass action within seven days. Government claims measures are in place to cushion Kenyans.

A worker adjusts fuel prices on a price board at a Petrol Station in Eldoret City, Uasin Gishu County, on April 15, 2026, after the Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority announced a price increase.

CRIME
Fuel, Taxes, Protests

Fuel

This whole "government-to-government" fuel deal is just another way to line pockets, isn't it? The opposition is shouting about record pump prices and politically connected oil marketers getting the gravy. Rigathi Gachagua is out there, all righteous indignation, demanding the cancellation of this framework. He even wants the Energy and Trade CSs out and prosecuted. Honestly, the only "scandal" here is how these guys keep finding new ways to milk us dry. They're saying if Ruto doesn't pull his head out of the clouds in seven days, there will be nationwide protests. Good luck with that.

President Ruto, meanwhile, is busy defending his administration, claiming they've already reduced VAT on fuel from 13 to eight percent and secured a Sh6.5 billion subsidy. He even had the nerve to say the world is facing these challenges, so what do they expect? He's touring Kisii, opening bridges and roads, while the rest of us are staring at empty fuel tanks and price boards that look like they're setting fire to our salaries. He dismisses protests, asking if a demonstration will magically lower global oil prices. It’s a classic deflection, isn't it?

The opposition isn't buying it. They're demanding the suspension of road maintenance and affordable housing levies, and a halt to increased NSSF deductions. They argue the government is using geopolitical tensions as an excuse to renegotiate pricing for their own profit. This latest fuel price hike, up by Sh28.69 for petrol and a whopping Sh40.30 for diesel, is the steepest in ages. They're even questioning the arrest of some energy officials, saying they were just following orders, and the "real culprits are at the top." It's all about who you know in this country, and if you're not connected, you're just fuel for the fire.

The situation is dire, with long queues at filling stations and reports of hoarding and black market fuel sales. It’s a perfect storm of government mismanagement and corporate greed. You’d think with all the talk of "cushioning Kenyans," something would actually reach us. Remember that time Ruto promised a nuclear power plant for Siaya? That felt like a lot of hot air then, and this fuel situation feels just as hollow. The government assures us of fuel availability, yet stations are running dry. It’s a charade, designed to keep us occupied while they continue their games.
