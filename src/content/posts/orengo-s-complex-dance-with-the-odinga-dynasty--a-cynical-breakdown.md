Orengo's Dynasty Dance: A Tightrope Walk to Nowhere

This is not a story about principled stands. It's about James Orengo, that man from Siaya, doing his usual tightrope walk between the Odinga clan's political dreams and his own stubborn ego. Loyalty? Sure, he’s got that in spades, but independence? That’s what makes people clutch their pearls and wonder what his game is. It’s a real drama, folks, if you like that sort of thing.

The public handshakes and backslaps? Please. It's all just posturing for the 2027 elections. They smell opportunity, especially with Raila gone. The "cathedral" is indeed being "partitioned," as they so dramatically put it. Orengo, the big man of opposition in Luo Nyanza, is playing his part, or so he thinks. But this whole thing is starting to feel like a broken record.

His "hard-line" stance against the government is just him trying to carve out his own little kingdom within ODM. He's setting himself against his own party structures, which, frankly, is a classic move for anyone who thinks they’re smarter than everyone else. The question isn't whether Orengo is loyal or independent; it's how long this charade can last before it all crumbles. He's dancing with a dynasty, alright, but the music is bound to stop, and he might just find himself falling. Don't expect any real change; just more political theater.







James Orengo, the governor of Siaya, is doing his usual tightrope act, a dance with the Odinga dynasty that’s more about optics than real substance. His loyalty is often touted, but his independence? That’s the wink and nod that keeps us all guessing. He’s embraced their vision, sure, but only when it suits him. The rest of the time, he’s pushing back, a masterclass in self-preservation disguised as principle.

The constant public displays of unity, especially with 2027 looming, are nothing but calculated moves. It’s a reunion tour for the faithful, a promise of more of the same. With Raila no longer around to hold things together, the cracks are showing, and everyone’s scrambling for their piece of the pie.

His so-called hard-line stance against the broad-based government? That's just Orengo trying to position himself as the lone ranger, a man who can't be controlled. It's a move that’s predictably set him against the very ODM party structures he claims to represent.
