US Ebola Facility Barred in Kenya: Another Day, Another Dollar for the Americans

WARNING: The US's grand plan to build an Ebola quarantine centre in Kenya, meant to protect their own citizens, has been put on hold by a Kenyan court. While the Americans claim it's about proximity and timely treatment, many here smell a rat and are rightfully worried about being turned into a giant, makeshift containment zone.

IMAGE CAPTION: Kenyan officials are not exactly rolling out the red carpet for this US Ebola facility.







So, the Yanks want to plonk an Ebola isolation centre right here in our backyard, a mere stone's throw from where the real mess is happening in Congo and Uganda. Apparently, for *their* citizens. Because heaven forbid any infected American has to rough it in their own country. The Kenyan government, predictably, has been as quiet as a church mouse on this whole deal, while MPs in Laikipia are squawking louder than a broken-down matatu. They're seeing "no logic" in hosting a facility for a disease that hasn't even touched our soil. Color me shocked.

The High Court has finally stepped in, slamming the brakes on this whole operation until they can sort through the mess. Justice Nyaundi has made it clear: no foreign government is setting up shop to ship their potential Ebola cases here. This comes after the Katiba Institute raised alarms about an "imminent threat to life." It seems some people here still believe in protecting Kenyan lives over foreign convenience. This whole debacle just adds another layer to the ongoing discussion about how our leaders often seem to be selling continental dreams while ignoring the ground reality for us ordinary folks, much like [The $3 Trillion Mirage: Ruto Sells Continental Dreams While Nairobi Suffers the Ground Reality](/posts/the--3-trillion-mirage--ruto-sells-continental-dreams-while-nairobi-suffers-the-ground-reality).

The doctors are absolutely fuming, and rightly so. They're calling it "backdoor negotiations" and demanding to know what shady deals have been struck. Why Kenya, they ask, when we're not the epicentre? It's an "apartheid healthcare model" they're warning against, and frankly, I don't blame them. The KMPDU has given the government an ultimatum - spill the beans or face industrial action. It’s clear that Kenya is a sovereign republic, not some dumping ground for the West's health nightmares.

President Ruto, of course, has been doing his usual diplomatic song and dance, talking about cooperation and global action. He conveniently avoided mentioning the US facility directly, while his US counterpart, Secretary of State Marco Rubio, was busy dangling a $13.5 million carrot for "preparedness efforts." It’s all a bit too neat, isn't it? They'll give us a few bob, we'll risk our biosecurity. It's the same old story, really. The Americans don't want it on their soil, so they're happy to ship it over here, assuming we'll just roll over and accept it. Well, not this time, it seems.
