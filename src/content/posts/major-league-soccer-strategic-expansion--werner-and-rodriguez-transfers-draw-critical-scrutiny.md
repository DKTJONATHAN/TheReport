Tim Howard, the former United States National Team goalkeeper, has voiced significant reservations concerning the arrival of Werner. Speaking on the Unfiltered Soccer platform, Howard highlighted a perceived decline in the clinical efficiency of the German striker during his recent tenures at Chelsea, RB Leipzig, and Tottenham Hotspur. Howard characterized the struggles of Werner as a psychological hurdle, likening the situation to the "yips" often observed in professional baseball. This skepticism is shared by Landon Donovan, who questioned whether the transition to the North American league would facilitate a recovery in the scoring form of the player.

This influx of veteran talent follows a trend established in the 2025 summer window, which saw Son Heung-min and Thomas Muller join LAFC and the Vancouver Whitecaps, respectively. While these signings enhance the global visibility of the league, they also place a spotlight on the operational risks associated with aging stars. For instance, the recent competitive activity of Werner has been minimal, totaling only 14 minutes of play during the 2025-2026 season at Leipzig prior to his move.

In contrast to the skepticism surrounding Werner, analyst expectations for James Rodriguez remain high. Despite a period of inactivity following his departure from Club Leon in Liga MX, Rodriguez is viewed as a high-caliber technical asset. Howard noted that, in the absence of Lionel Messi, Rodriguez possesses the potential to be the most dominant player on the pitch. This acquisition is particularly strategic for Minnesota United as the player seeks to maintain fitness ahead of the 2026 World Cup.

The trend of integrating high-profile international players mirrors broader market shifts in service-oriented industries. Much like the [Global CX Outsourcing Market Projected to Reach $171 Billion by 2028 Driven by AI Integration](/posts/global-cx-outsourcing-market-projected-to-reach--171-billion-by-2028-driven-by-ai-integration), where organizations are increasingly relying on specialized external talent to drive growth, MLS franchises are leveraging global "brand" players to enhance their competitive and commercial standing.

As the 2026 season progresses, the performance of these athletes will serve as a litmus test for the recruitment strategy of the league. The San Jose Earthquakes are scheduled to face Inter Miami in a highly anticipated match during the current cycle, a fixture that will likely provide the first major assessment of the impact of Werner in his new environment. The financial and reputational stakes remain high as MLS continues its trajectory toward becoming a top-tier global football entity.
