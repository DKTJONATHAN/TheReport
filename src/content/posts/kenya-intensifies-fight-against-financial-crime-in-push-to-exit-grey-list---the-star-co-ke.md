Kenya Intensifies Fight Against Financial Crime: Grey List Exit Still a Mirage?

WARNING: More government high-fives while the real issues fester. Expect more meetings, less actual change.

Photo of officials in suits shaking hands. Probably discussing how to appear busy.

CRIME
MONEY LAUNDERING, CORRUPTION, GREY LIST

MEETING

Another day, another high-powered breakfast for Kenya's finest. DPP Renson Ingonga, DCI boss Mohammed Amin, EACC's Abdi Mohamud, and Naphtaly Rono from the Financial Reporting Centre are all smiles, no doubt, about their "intensified efforts" to get us off that FATF grey list. They met, they aligned, they coordinated. Sounds like a lot of hot air and lukewarm tea, if you ask me. The goal? To secure Kenya's removal. Because what else are they paid the big bucks for?

This whole "whole-of-government approach" is just fancy talk for shuffling papers between the same old faces. They're tweaking their AML/CFT framework, whatever that is. Probably involves more forms and less actual consequence for the big fish. They're focused on "outstanding technical and operational requirements." Translation: they're trying to tick boxes for some international suits so they can stop being embarrassed. Don't expect any real change in how things are done on the ground, though. It's all about the optics, darling.

They're even talking about improving "inter-agency coordination" and "information sharing." Bless their hearts. We've seen this play out before, haven't we? Remember all the promises after [Ruto's 1 Billion Trees: Another Rhino Charge for Empty Promises?](/posts/ruto-s-1-billion-trees--another-rhino-charge-for-empty-promises)? More committees, more jargon, more nothing. They'll claim they're boosting efforts to recover proceeds of crime, but let's be real, most of that money is long gone, laundered through offshore accounts and whispered deals.

The real kicker? They're preparing for an "upcoming face-to-face engagement" with some Africa Joint Group. This is where they'll try to convince everyone they're doing a bang-up job. Expect a lot of impressive-sounding statistics that are probably just as harmonised and measurable as a mirage. They've adopted a "coordinated roadmap," which is just a fancy way of saying they've agreed to keep having these meetings. They're confident their "continued collaboration" will lead to a "successful exit." Sure, and I'm confident pigs will fly in formation.
