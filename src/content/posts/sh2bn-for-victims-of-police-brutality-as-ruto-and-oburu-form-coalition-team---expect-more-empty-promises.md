So, President William Ruto and ODM's Oburu Oginga, Raila's brother who's now trying to fill some big shoes, have decided to form an eight-member committee to plan for the 2027 elections. Because, you know, that's what Kenya needs right now - more committees. And as a cherry on top, they're promising Sh2 billion to compensate victims of police brutality. They say they'll do it by June. *June*. We've heard this tune before, haven't we? It's like déjà vu, but with more expensive suits and less actual change.

This whole compensation drama is tied to that 10-point agenda signed with Raila Odinga last year. Raila's gone now, bless his departed soul, but his legacy seems to be getting twisted and stretched like cheap mitumba. The court processes are the usual excuse for the delay. And now, suddenly, Sh2 billion is magically appearing in the supplementary budget. Funny how money appears when it's time to make promises, but disappears when it's time for action. It’s almost as if life itself can’t be compensated, but our gullible public can be placated with figures.

The Agnes Zani-led committee, which sounds like it's from a bad movie plot, released its report. Apparently, IPOA handled 820 cases of police misconduct and got 35 convictions. Thirty-five convictions out of 820 cases? That's a joke, and not a funny one. And the Demonstration Bill, which would have silenced any dissent, was withdrawn. Small mercies, I guess. But the drama in the Parliamentary Group meeting tells you everything you need to know. Embu Governor Cecily Mbarire complaining about the two-thirds gender principle, and Mathare MP Anthony Oluoch demanding the report be updated to match the President's "more information." It's all theatre, my friends. The same old song and dance.

Now, the critics are calling it a betrayal of Raila Odinga's legacy. And ODM co-deputy leader Godfrey Osotsi is calling out the "pro-regime voices within ODM" for claiming the agreement had no timeline. He’s right, of course. This whole narrative about the broad-based government being formed way before the MoU is just a convenient excuse. It smells like the same old political maneuvering we saw with the "Nyayo House Clearance Sale: Your Identity for the Highest Bidder." Promises are made, then reinterpreted, then broken.

Ruto and Oburu are playing the "we made the commitment to the people of Kenya" card. Of course, you did. And the people of Kenya are still waiting. They say 80 percent of commitments have been implemented. Eighty percent? Let's see what that translates to on the ground. Reduced inflation? Fertiliser subsidies? Expanded health coverage? It sounds good on paper, like all these glossy reports. But the reality on the streets of Nairobi is a different story. The rising cost of living, the endless hustle, the feeling that our national security is just a side hustle for cartels.

The report brags about teacher recruitment, classrooms, laboratories, housing units, markets, roads, electricity connectivity, and increased revenue to counties. All sounds impressive, but remember, this is from a committee set up to justify the current arrangement. They even listed the Conflict-of-Interest Act, 2025, as an achievement. A law that's supposed to prevent corruption, passed *after* multiple instances of it, and then hailed as a victory. It’s like congratulating a firefighter for finally putting out a fire they started. We’ve seen this playbook before, and it always ends the same way: with the same people in power, and the rest of us just hoping for a slightly less broken future.
