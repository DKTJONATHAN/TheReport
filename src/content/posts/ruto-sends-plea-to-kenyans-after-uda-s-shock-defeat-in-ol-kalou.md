Ruto's Peace Plea Rings Hollow After UDA's Ol Kalou Humiliation











So, the big man, President Ruto, is out here telling us to hold hands and sing Kumbaya as we inch closer to 2027. Apparently, elections are not "a matter of life and death." Funny, because the way things are usually done around here, they practically are. This sudden sermon on peace comes right after his UDA party got absolutely clobbered in the Ol Kalou by election. Thirty-five thousand votes for the other guy, Sammy Douglas Kamau Waweru of the DCP, and a measly five thousand for UDA's man. Ouch. That’s not just a defeat, that’s a political evisceration.

You’d think after that, the focus would be on figuring out what went wrong, not lecturing us on national unity. But no, Ruto, while hosting some Bungoma leaders - probably to discuss how to keep *their* constituents from doing the same to UDA - insisted that Kenya will still be here after the votes are counted. Groundbreaking stuff, really. His point? We should be concentrating on improving lives, not this "political competition" nonsense. Reminds me of those same old promises we keep hearing, like the 'Made in Kenya' label, which often feels like [another empty promise for the common mwananchi](/posts/the--made-in-kenya--label--another-empty-promise-for-the-common-mwananchi).

The Ol Kalou by election, we're told, attracted "national attention." Yes, because it turned into a full-blown proxy war between UDA and the increasingly ambitious DCP, strongly backed by Deputy President Gachagua. Top leaders on both sides were out there, stirring the pot, making promises, probably doing whatever it takes to win. And now, DCP walks away with a victory that’s set to boost their clout in the Mt Kenya region. So much for focusing on development and unity when the party faithful are busy flexing their political muscles.

Ruto’s plea for peace, coming after such a stark reminder of political realities, sounds more like damage control than genuine concern. It’s the usual script: when your side gets hammered, suddenly it’s time for high-minded pronouncements. When the other side wins, it’s a sign of the people's will. When your own side loses badly, it’s time to remind everyone that elections aren't the end of the world. We’ve seen this play out so many times, it's like watching a rerun of [slaps, screams, and shoddy scripts](/posts/slaps--screams--and-shoddy-scripts--the-rise-of-the-60-second-brain-rot). The real question is, will anyone actually listen, or will the political temperature just keep climbing towards 2027? My money's on the latter.
