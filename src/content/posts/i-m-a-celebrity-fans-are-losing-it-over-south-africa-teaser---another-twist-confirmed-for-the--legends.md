So, the grand return of *I'm A Celebrity... South Africa* has been confirmed for April, and ITV has seen fit to grace us with a teaser trailer. Because what we *really* needed was a reminder that Harry Redknapp, Gemma Collins, and Beverley Callard are still a thing. Joining them in this televised descent into mild chaos are Sir Mo Farah - because nothing says 'legend' like a slightly awkward Olympian - Scarlett Moffatt, David Haye, Adam Thomas, Ashley Roberts, Seann Walsh, Sinitta (yes, *that* Sinitta), Craig Charles, and Jimmy Bullard. A veritable who's who of people who peaked at various points in the last decade.

The animated trailer, optimistically titled 'Where Legends Are Made,' shows these 'legends' reliving their finest, or perhaps most embarrassing, moments. Ant and Dec, bless their eternally youthful faces, narrate the saga. Honestly, it's enough to make you wonder if they ever get tired of pretending these people are actual heroes. It's all about the "tougher" conditions and "relentless twists and turns," which, let's be honest, is just ITV's way of saying they'll run out of bug spray and start rationing biscuits.

But the real drama, as always, isn't in the alleged "tough trials." It's in the comments section. The masses, united in their collective impatience, are all shouting the same thing: "When does it start?!" Seriously, people. Is a date *that* important when you know the outcome will be the same? Someone will cry, someone will get bitten by something questionable, and Ant and Dec will make a fortune. It's the circle of life, reality TV edition. It’s almost as predictable as a Hollywood couple trying to be 'private' and failing miserably, like that whole Zendaya and Tom situation we’ve seen before.

This year's "exciting new element" is that the public gets to vote for the "ultimate Legend." Because, clearly, after weeks of manufactured drama, what we need is more pressure on the already over-hyped contestants. They'll be faced with "big decisions" and "bigger consequences," which probably means deciding whether to eat a grub or wear a questionable hat. Frankly, I'd rather watch the binmen advise on recycling than tune into this predictable circus. But hey, at least the 'legends' get another paycheck.
