So, Round 24 of the FKF Premier League kicks off with the usual fanfare - Posta Rangers hosting Kenya Police. One team is desperate to stop the bleeding, the other is looking to climb the greasy pole. Frankly, it’s another Friday afternoon where the only real winner is likely the bookies. Rangers, bless their hearts, are staring down the barrel of a 13th-place finish, hovering just above the relegation muck. Eleven league matches without a win? That’s not a slump, that’s a lifestyle.

Their recent form reads like a cautionary tale. Losses to Murang’a SEAL and AFC Leopards are expected, but the draws against Gor Mahia and Tusker? Pure luck, probably. Three goals scored and seven conceded in their last five? They’re struggling at both ends, a classic recipe for disaster. At least midfielder Jackson Macharia managed to poke one in against Gor Mahia, a lone flicker of hope in a dark tunnel. Coach Charles Omondi is apparently thinking of fielding a proper lineup this time, which is a novel concept after all that cup game rotation.

Meanwhile, Kenya Police are strutting in like they own the place, currently lounging in fifth. Two wins in their last three, including 2-0 routs over Bandari and Sofapaka, show they’re not exactly slouches. They’ve only conceded 14 goals all season, which means they’ve actually figured out how to keep the ball out of their own net, unlike some others we could mention. Their defense is tight, their attack is efficient, and they're not making the kind of mistakes that send fans into a frenzy of despair. It’s almost as if they’re trying to win.

Sure, historically, these matches have been "competitive." That's code for "most ended in a draw or a narrow Police win." The reverse fixture? A glorious 0-0. Thrilling. But let's be real. Posta Rangers are masters of snatching defeat from the jaws of victory, while Kenya Police are quietly, efficiently picking up points. It’s like comparing a sputtering old bus to a slightly less sputtering, but still questionable, matatu. If you’re looking for excitement, you’re better off reading about [why men are no longer getting laid](/posts/why-men-are-nolonger-getting-laid).

The Police are favored, and frankly, they should be. They have form, they have a defense, and they’re not actively trying to give the game away. Posta Rangers are just… there. They might pull off a surprise, but betting on that is like betting on the Kenyan currency suddenly appreciating. It’s a long shot, and you’ll likely end up losing your shirt. Don't say I didn't warn you.

This whole FKF Premier League season has been a rollercoaster of disappointment for some, a mild amusement for others. Just another day in Kenyan football, where hope is a rare commodity, and the predictable often prevails.
