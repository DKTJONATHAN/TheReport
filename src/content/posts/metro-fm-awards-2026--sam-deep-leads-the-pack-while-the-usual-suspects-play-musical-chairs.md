So, the Metro FM Music Awards circus has rolled into town once again, and honestly, are we even surprised? Sam Deep has managed to snag the most nominations for 2026, proving that if you stay in the industry's good graces, the rewards will follow. The announcement at The Forum in Hyde Park was dripping with enough fake smiles to fill a landfill. Sam is up for Best Male and Artist of the Year, while his track "Shela" is being pushed in four different categories. It is the kind of corporate-sanctioned dominance that makes you wonder if the public voting is just a polite suggestion.

Then we have DJ Maphorisa, who seems to have a permanent residency in the nominee list. He is chasing Sam Deep with nods for "Abantwana Bakho" and "Dlala Ka Yona." It is the same cycle every year: the big names keep the gate locked while everyone else begs for a feature. In this industry, we are often distracted by the glitz while ignoring the systemic rot. It reminds me of [the 10k circus: why we’re still catching small fry while the sharks swim free](/posts/the-10k-circus--why-we-re-still-catching-small-fry-while-the-sharks-swim-free), because while these heavyweights collect trophies, the real talent is often left outside the VIP tent. Maphorisa is even nominated for a "Viral Challenge," which is basically an award for being lucky on TikTok.

Zee Nxumalo is also in the mix, and she is apparently a triple threat now. She is up for Best Female Artist, Artist of the Year, and - wait for it - Best Styled Artist. Because apparently, we are still awarding people for having a decent tailor and a stylist on speed dial. She is featured on everything from Kwaito to Maskandi, which is a great way to ensure you have a hand in every cookie jar. It is less about artistic vision and more about being a human billboard for every genre that is currently trending. If you throw enough spaghetti at the wall, some of it is bound to get a nomination.

The rest of the list is a predictable stroll through the archives. Mandisi Dyantyis and Judith Sephuma are holding down the Jazz fort because heaven forbid the committee finds someone new to listen to. The Hip Hop category is basically a high school reunion featuring Stogie T and Nasty C. It is all very safe, very corporate, and frankly, very boring. When the awards ceremony hits the Durban ICC on April 25, expect a lot of "thank you to the fans" speeches that were definitely written by PR interns. We will be watching, mostly to see who trips on the red carpet or whose "live" vocals finally expose the truth.
