February kicked off with the usual suspects on Twitter clutching their pearls over the supposed "death" of amapiano. Apparently, a few weeks without a viral dance challenge means the entire genre has fallen off a cliff. Of course, the gatekeepers were quick to jump in with their poetic defenses. Charisse C. took to her Substack to call these critics "extractors" who don’t plant seeds. It is all very deep and philosophical for a genre that is mostly used to sell overpriced vodka in Sandton. The reality is much simpler: the capitalist machine is just hungry for the next shiny toy to package and sell to the masses.

Speaking of packaging, the ego-olympics are in full swing. DBN Gogo announced a 24-hour non-stop set for April to prove that women have the "stamina" for the job. It is a bold move, but Kelvin Momo clearly felt some type of way about it. He conveniently announced his own one-man concert for the very next day. Nothing says "industry sisterhood" like a male peer immediately trying to suck the oxygen out of the room with a back-to-back booking. While these artists claim to be building a community, it often feels like the same [EXPOSED: The Secret Corporate Scam Behind AfroFuture’s New 'Friendship' Anthem](/posts/exposed--the-secret-corporate-scam-behind-afrofuture-s-new--friendship--anthem) we see whenever a scene gets too big for its boots.

Uncle Waffles is also continuing her world domination tour, heading to Europe to "dance" with Kaytranada’s fans. We get it, the girl can move, but one wonders if the "musical knowledge" DBN Gogo is so worried about actually matters when you have a great PR team and a signature pose. Meanwhile, the industry is trying to balance the "vibes" with actual tragedy. Producers Stixx and Nvcho both lost their fathers within days of each other. The electronic community is "rallying," which is usually industry-speak for posting a crying emoji on Instagram before heading back to the studio to churn out another heater.

The music itself is coming at us faster than we can hit delete. Al Xapo and Benzoo are already pushing "Stance" - yet another sub-genre name we have to pretend to understand. Apparently, it is a mix of sgija and private school piano that "bursts into flames." It sounds more like a marketing gimmick for people who get bored every five minutes. Then you have Jaydecai trying to sell us "soulful amapiano" for our Sunday deep-cleaning sessions. It is nice music, sure, but the "restraint" they are being praised for is often just a lack of new ideas hidden behind a slow tempo.

Finally, we have the veterans like Mr JazziQ and Myztro trying to stay relevant. JazziQ is busy christening the year with "Wololo" and running his club, Vibes on Main, because he knows better than anyone that the log drum gravy train eventually hits a buffer. He is smart enough to pivot to real estate while the kids are still arguing about whether the genre is dead. Myztro is also moving fast to avoid being "Maphorisa’s little brother" forever. It is a frantic, messy, and loud ecosystem where everyone is terrified of being the one who didn't see the next trend coming. Just keep dancing, I guess, even if the spaceship is supposedly on fire.
