So, reMarkable, the company that charges a premium for a glorified digital notepad, is apparently attempting to broaden its appeal with a "mass market" device. According to leaks from the ever-reliable @evleaks, this new model will be called the "Paper Pure" and is slated for a Q2 launch. The implication is that it will be less niche than their previous overpriced offerings.

Let's not get our hopes up. "Mass market" for reMarkable likely means "slightly less extortionate." The current models, the Paper Pro and Paper Pro Move, are priced at a ludicrous $630 and $450 respectively. For that kind of money, you get a screen that mocks the very idea of color and a user experience that deliberately omits anything resembling a modern tablet's functionality.

The real question isn't *if* they're making it cheaper, but *how*. Expect a lower resolution screen that makes reading a chore. A slower chip is a given, meaning glacial refresh rates that will test the patience of anyone who isn't a masochist. Color E Ink? Don't hold your breath. Battery life? Probably slashed to the bone. These are the inevitable compromises when a company trying to justify a luxury price tag attempts to enter the real world.

They're hoping to entice students, a demographic known for its bottomless pockets, I presume. The article muses about smaller form factors, citing devices like the Boox Palma 2 Pro. This is just a distraction from the core issue: reMarkable's business model relies on perceived value that evaporates the moment you compare it to any other device on the market that actually *does* things.

Of course, they'll insist the stylus and handwriting experience remain "robust." That's their entire schtick, the fig leaf they hide behind to justify the inflated prices. They aren't selling a tablet; they're selling an illusion of focused productivity, a digital analog to a bygone era that conveniently ignores the actual progress in portable computing. This "Paper Pure" will likely be another expensive experiment for those who can afford to be disappointed.
