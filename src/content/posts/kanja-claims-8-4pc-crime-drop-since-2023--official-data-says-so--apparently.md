So, the Big Man in charge of the police, Douglas Kanja, is out here telling us crime has dropped. A whole 5.1 per cent compared to this year, and a whopping 8.4 per cent since 2023. He's calling it "progress" in policing reforms. Sounds nice and shiny, doesn't it? Especially when he's standing there at some fancy academy with all his top brass, looking all serious.

He wants us to believe this is all thanks to better policing and all hands on deck. He's talking about professionalism, community trust, and reducing corruption. Right. Because we all know how quickly corruption disappears when you just *ask* it to. And let's not forget the new recruits, the fancy equipment, and the "broader transformation agenda." It's a whole symphony of promises, isn't it?

But here's the kicker: are these "reported" cases the *only* cases? Because back here in the real Nairobi, the ones you don't see in the official stats are the ones that really mess up your day. You know, the petty theft that makes you clutch your bag tighter, the "opportunistic" muggings, the ones that leave you feeling more vulnerable than ever. It’s easy to claim a drop when you’re only looking at what’s officially on paper.

And speaking of things that aren't always reported, remember how we talked about the pursuit of a "cheap miracle"? Well, sometimes the things that are supposed to solve our problems end up creating new ones. It’s like the whole system is a bit like that hidden snake the EACC chairperson warned about; you think you've got it cornered, and then it bites you somewhere else.

Kanja wants commanders to use this conference to "review performance critically" and "identify gaps." Good luck with that. While they're busy polishing their reports and strategizing on paper, the rest of us are just trying to navigate the streets without losing our phones or our dignity. Maybe if they spent less time talking and more time actually making people feel safe, we might actually believe these numbers.

Still, it's good to know they're *trying*, I guess. And hey, at least the DCI managed to find some bhang in Machakos. That’s something, right? Every little bit of "progress" counts, even if it feels like we’re still wading through a swamp.
