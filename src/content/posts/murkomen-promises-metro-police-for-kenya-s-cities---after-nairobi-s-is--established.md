So, Interior Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen has been gallivanting in New York, apparently learning from the NYPD. And what profound wisdom has he brought back? That other major cities in Kenya will get their own metro police units, *once* the Nairobi one is successfully established and operational. Yes, you heard that right. "Once". As if "successful establishment" of anything in this country is a guaranteed event.

This whole song and dance about rolling out a model is just the usual government theatre. They visit shiny foreign institutions, take copious notes that will likely gather dust, and then come back with grand pronouncements that sound good on paper but crumble under the weight of Kenyan reality. Remember that time they were going to fix the roads? Or maybe the talk about improving the Shujaa's performance in international rugby? [Shujaa's Tough Pool in Valladolid - Another Rugby Sevens Test We Probably Won't Win](/posts/shujaa-s-tough-pool-in-valladolid---another-rugby-sevens-test-we-probably-won-t-win), that was a real confidence booster.

The CS, accompanied by Nairobi Governor Sakaja and a host of other senior officials, was apparently impressed by how agencies share information and leverage "real-time intelligence." It’s almost cute, isn't it? They’re talking about intelligence sharing while we’re still struggling with basic service delivery. Perhaps they should spend less time admiring foreign police uniforms and more time figuring out how to make sure officers on the ground actually have functioning radios and enough fuel for their patrol cars.

Let's be clear, the idea of better policing isn't the issue. The issue is the endless cycle of promises that never quite materialize. It’s the cynical expectation that we'll just accept these pronouncements at face value, despite decades of evidence to the contrary. Until we see tangible results, and not just delegation trips and press conferences, these metro police units for other cities will remain just another footnote in the long history of Kenyan good intentions gone astray. It’s the same old story, just with a slightly different uniform.
