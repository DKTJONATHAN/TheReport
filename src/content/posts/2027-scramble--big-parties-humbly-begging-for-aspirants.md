So, the big boys - Ruto’s UDA, Raila’s ODM, and Gachagua’s new pet project, DCP - are apparently "inviting applications" for the 2027 polls. What a joke. This isn't about invitations; it's a desperate plea. They've realised they can't just conjure up candidates out of thin air, especially when the well of talent, or what passes for it, is running drier than a Maasai handshake in August. They’re fishing, folks, and the fish aren't biting like they used to.

And look at this "TIFA poll" handing Kindiki a "major political boost." Please. These polls are as reliable as a boda boda driver on a Sunday morning. They're just fodder for the political circus, designed to create buzz and make certain individuals feel important before the real chaos begins. The parties are "establishing regions where they still command influence." Translation: they're checking if their usual strongholds are still willing to be hoodwinked, or if the voters have finally wised up.

The experts are already chiming in about the "East bloc" being split, weakening Ruto's re-election chances. Is anyone surprised? This country's political landscape is a predictable mess, much like the ["Junk Choppers and Stolen Sheets: The Messy 'Influence' of East African Power Players"](/posts/junk-choppers-and-stolen-sheets--the-messy--influence--of-east-african-power-players) we've seen before. Everyone’s looking out for themselves, scrambling for their piece of the pie, and the voters are just left to pick up the crumbs.

Then there's Oburu Oginga, the valiant defender of ODM, "thwarting UDA's attempts to lure ODM MPs." It's all theater. These defections and counter-moves are just minor skirmishes in the grand war for power. They’ll promise favours, whisper threats, and then the MPs will do what’s best for their own pockets, not for the country. And don't even get me started on Rotich losing Emurua Dikirr, but the Opposition claiming "over 10,000 votes signal growing voter discontent." Discontent? That’s just the polite way of saying people are fed up and tired of the same old song and dance.

The whole thing is just a massive, predictable scramble. These parties, in their panic, are casting a wide net, hoping to catch any warm body with a pulse and a willingness to parrot their empty promises. It’s not about ideology or policy; it’s about numbers and the illusion of support. You see the same pattern repeat itself, whether it’s in the ["Ruai is the New Human Warehouse and Nobody is Surprised"](/posts/ruai-is-the-new-human-warehouse-and-nobody-is-surprised) or the machinations around ["Mt Kenya Unity Faces Eight Political Battles: Prepare For The usual Mess"](/posts/mt-kenya-unity-faces-eight-political-battles--prepare-for-the-usual-mess).

So, while they're busy playing their political games and "inviting applications," remember this: it's all just a desperate attempt to hold onto power. The real agenda isn't for the betterment of Kenya; it's for the continued enrichment and influence of those already at the top. Don't be fooled by the PR.
