2027 Vote Battle Zones: IEBC Voter Enrolment Targets A Sham
The upcoming 2027 election is just another sideshow, with the real drama unfolding behind closed doors as politicians juggle voter arithmetic. Don't be fooled by the fanfare; it's all about manipulating numbers in counties and regions to get their man into State House. The Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) is at the centre of this elaborate dance, setting targets that are as reliable as a politician's promise.

And what do you know, the IEBC is now asking voters who registered before 2012 to get their biometric details captured again. Apparently, some vital data has gone missing, or perhaps it's just another way to weed out "ghost" voters and pad the rolls for whoever has the right connections. They tell us not to panic, but when has a Kenyan election ever been without its "curious" online users noticing their polling stations have conveniently vanished? This is just the usual theatre, much like the [AIU Doping Fight Gets 'Anonymous' Tools: More Theatre Than Truth?](/posts/aiu-doping-fight-gets--anonymous--tools--more-theatre-than-truth) - all show, no substance.

Meanwhile, the grim reaper continues his relentless harvest in Parliament. Plane crashes, murder, Covid-19, cancer - you name it, death has a way of clearing the benches for new faces, often just as corrupt as the last. Effective policing of elections? That starts years in advance, they say. Threat assessments, deployment plans, resource mobilisation - it's a nice thought, but in Kenya, it usually boils down to who greases whose palms the most.

So, while they're busy playing with voter enrolment numbers and telling us to re-register, remember that the real battle is about who controls the narrative and the ballot boxes. The promises of fair play and transparent elections are as hollow as a politician's apology. It's the same old song and dance, just with a slightly updated tune. Just like the [Poison at the Pump: Kenya's Fuel Marking System A Cancer Risk, Millions Exposed](/posts/poison-at-the-pump--kenya-s-fuel-marking-system-a-cancer-risk--millions-exposed), the cracks are showing, and we're all breathing in the fumes.
