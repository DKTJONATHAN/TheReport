Waiguru, Women Leaders Push Ruto Re-election, Coast Residents Told to "Be Wise"

These women are out here singing hosanna for President Ruto's re-election, promising a second term filled with more roads and markets. Apparently, development just magically appears when you're "in government." And hey, if that doesn't convince you, they'll throw in some tents and wheelchairs for good measure.

Kirinyaga Governor Anne Waiguru, leading the charge in Taita Taveta, declared that "Kazi ya President William Ruto inaongea" - his work speaks for itself. She waxed lyrical about the expanded road infrastructure and twelve new markets in her county, conveniently forgetting to mention who's actually footing the bill or if these projects are even finished. She also shushed any talk of Mt Kenya abandoning Ruto, calling Kirinyaga the region's political barometer. So, basically, if the Kirinyaga governor isn't talking, the whole mountain is mum. Right.

Meanwhile, Nakuru Governor Susan Kihika chimed in about Ruto empowering women leaders and ensuring equitable development. Likoni MP Mishi Mboko agreed, citing inclusive leadership and visible development on the Coast. Host Lydia Haika, campaigning for a senatorial seat, also lauded Ruto's "accelerated development" and "strengthened national cohesion." It all sounds very rosy, doesn't it? Like a well-rehearsed play where everyone gets a standing ovation for saying the right things.

The whole "stay in government to get development" spiel is as old as time. It's the classic carrot dangling for anyone who dares to question the status quo. They're basically saying, "Cooperate, don't rock the boat, and maybe, just maybe, you'll get a bit of what the big boys are getting." It’s less about genuine inclusivity and more about ensuring a steady stream of grateful constituents who won't ask too many awkward questions about the ever-rising cost of things, like fuel prices that always seem to be in the news, even when Parliament isn't grilling CSs about it.

The women leaders are quite adamant that Mt Kenya hasn't shifted its position, insisting everyone should focus on development. Rahab Mukami and Irene Njoki are all about rejecting misinformation and focusing on progress. It’s all very noble, this talk of service to the people. But when you hear the same tired lines about "working together" and "staying in government" trotted out by politicians from different regions, you have to wonder who's really benefiting. Is it wananchi, or is it the politicians themselves, securing their own positions and opportunities?

The endorsement of Lydia Haika for the Taita Taveta senatorial seat is also a classic move. "Development-oriented leader," they call her, with a decade of track record to prove it. It’s a neat package: praise the president, get yourself a good word in, and hope the voters buy the whole narrative. Just another day in Kenyan politics, where promises are as plentiful as the dust on a poorly maintained road.

These women are selling a vision of continued development under President Ruto, urging support for his re-election.
Waiguru, Ruto, Coast, Taita Taveta, Women Leaders
Development
Kirinyaga Governor Anne Waiguru and a chorus of other women leaders have been out in Taita Taveta County, singing the praises of President William Ruto and urging Kenyans to back his re-election bid. Their argument? Ruto's performance is leagues ahead of his predecessors, and his administration's commitment to equitable resource sharing and inclusive development is what's transforming lives across the nation.

Waiguru, speaking at a women empowerment event hosted by area Woman Representative Lydia Haika, asserted that "Kazi ya President William Ruto inaongea." She highlighted the immense benefits Kirinyaga has seen through its partnership with the national government, citing expanded road infrastructure and twelve newly constructed markets as tangible proof that working with the current administration delivers results. She firmly dismissed claims that the Mt Kenya region has abandoned Ruto, positioning Kirinyaga as the definitive barometer of the mountain's political sentiment.

The leaders also made a strong case for Haika's election as Senator, touting her decade-long track record as an MP. The underlying message throughout the event was clear: cooperation with the national government is the golden ticket to attracting development projects. Governor Waiguru urged Taita Taveta residents to "be wise" and "stay in government," emphasizing that politics should never trump the interests of the people. She also pointed to the Coast region's benefit from cabinet and principal secretary positions, alongside ongoing investments, as evidence of Ruto's inclusive approach.

Nakuru Governor Susan Kihika echoed these sentiments, stating that President Ruto consistently supports women leaders and fosters equitable development. Likoni MP Mishi Mboko added that the Coast region would rally behind Ruto due to his inclusive leadership and visible development initiatives, advocating for a focus on unity and service delivery over divisive politics. Nyeri Woman Representative Rahab Mukami and Bahati MP Irene Njoki reinforced the message that Mt Kenya remains committed to development-oriented leadership, urging a rejection of misinformation.

The event, which also saw the distribution of various items like tents, wheelchairs, and water tanks, served as a platform to showcase Ruto's supposed achievements. Host Lydia Haika lauded his leadership for accelerating development and strengthening national cohesion, claiming Taita Taveta has seen unprecedented projects under his tenure. The underlying narrative from these women leaders is a consistent push for continued support, framing re-election not just as a political choice, but as a necessity for ongoing progress and the assurance that "our people continue benefiting from projects and opportunities."
