Here we go again. Another grassroots tournament designed to make corporate suits feel like they are saving the world while the actual youth remain as broke as ever. The Boom & Thrive Badminton Tournament in Nanyuki is the latest flavor of the month, promising empowerment and growth to kids who would probably prefer a steady job over a weekend of chasing a feathered ball around a hot hall in Laikipia.

The list of sponsors is longer than the actual player roster. From insurance firms to auto garages, everyone wants a piece of the youth pie. It is the classic Nairobi hustle. You disguise your marketing budget as a charity event and hope nobody notices you are just looking for a tax break and some cheap social media engagement. They call it a sports-linked youth initiative, but we all know it is just a photo op for CEOs in branded polo shirts.

Beyond the competition, the organizers claim they are linking athletic development to life skills. Let us be real about the mentorship part. The sports industry is notoriously ruthless, and these platforms love to talk about leadership until the money runs out. We already saw how quickly the narrative changes and how [Victor Osimhen spills the tea on Napoli's social media meltdown](/posts/from-hero-to-puppet--victor-osimhen-spills-the-tea-on-napoli-s-social-media-meltdown) when things get ugly. One day you are the star, and the next, you are just a puppet for the management.

Then there is the claim that this will stimulate short-term economic activity in Nanyuki. Sure, a few guest houses will fill up and some taxi drivers might get a decent fare, but that is not an economy. It is a temporary fever. Once the tournament ends on March 1, the commercial opportunities will vanish faster than a politician's promise after an election, leaving the town exactly where it was before the shuttlecocks started flying.

As for the 2028 Olympic pipe dream, it is the ultimate distraction. We are talking about qualification efforts for Los Angeles while our local sports infrastructure is crumbling and basic equipment is a luxury for many. Sustained grassroots investment is just a fancy way of saying we are starting from zero because the previous decade of funding disappeared into someone's pocket. Enjoy the tournament, kids, but keep your eyes on your wallet.
