So, Tuesday's papers are out, and guess what? Deputy President Kithure Kindiki's office is feeling a bit peckish, or perhaps just wants to fly around in style, because they're sniffing around for an extra Sh450 million. Mind you, they've already been handed Sh523 million for "hospitality" - that's code for catering and entertainment, in case you thought it was for feeding the hungry. Now they want another Sh350 million, pushing the grand total for wining and dining and whatever else they do to a staggering Sh873 million before June. Just another Tuesday in paradise, I suppose.

And don't even get me started on the helicopters. Apparently, the DP's office has already blown Sh100 million on leasing choppers for the *next* financial year, 2025/26. So they're looking at a cool Sh1.9 billion in total requests, while funding for things like education and technology is being slashed. We're talking Sh4.5 billion cut from primary education, Sh2.8 billion from a data centre that probably won't ever be finished, and Sh1 billion from some digital economy plan. It’s like they’re actively trying to make life harder for everyone else while living it up. It reminds me of how companies play fast and loose with funds, much like how [SAMSUNG’S AI “EXPERIENCE” EXPOSED: THE SECRET COST OF YOUR PRIVACY IN KANAIRO](/posts/samsung-s-ai--experience--exposed--the-secret-cost-of-your-privacy-in-kanairo) showed us.

The MPs are apparently spitting feathers about this, with some calling it a "back door" manoeuvre and questioning the sanity of such huge hospitality budgets when the President is preaching austerity. Moses Mbaruku, the principal administrative secretary, tried to spin it, saying the money is for monitoring government programmes and coordinating initiatives. Monitoring with champagne and caviar, I presume. And the helicopters? Oh, they're just too important to rely on anyone else’s schedule. Disruptions, you see. Clearly, ensuring the DP can land wherever he pleases is more crucial than ensuring kids get a decent education.

Then there’s the whole Raphael Tuju disappearing act. He was arrested after claiming he was abducted, only for the DCI to say he staged the whole thing. Apparently, they tracked his movements and found out he never left home. He just decided to make a dramatic reappearance when the heat was on. Classic. Wasting police resources for a publicity stunt. It’s almost as if public figures think the rules don't apply to them.

Meanwhile, over in Kericho, they've stumbled upon a suspected mass grave with 14 bodies buried secretly. Two people have been arrested, and they're exhuming the bodies because, surprise, surprise, they were smuggled from Nyamira, which apparently doesn't have enough cemeteries. This whole country feels like a bad joke sometimes. And in Emurua Dikirr, the late MP's widow pulled out of the by-election race for "personal reasons." Probably realized what a mess politics is and decided to preserve her sanity. Smart move, unlike the rest of them.
