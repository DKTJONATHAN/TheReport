DJ Maphorisa, the self-appointed president of the Amapiano republic, has suddenly decided he is the Dalai Lama of the South African music scene. Taking to X-formerly Twitter, currently a dumpster fire-Themba Sonnyboy Sekowe called for "unity" among hip hop artists. It is hilarious, really. This comes from the same man who treats the industry like a private fiefdom and never met a track he did not want his name on. He is out here acting like a peace envoy while the genre he abandoned for the Yanos wave struggles to find its footing.

Why the sudden heart for the rappers? Maybe it is because the Amapiano wave is finally hitting that predictable ceiling where everything sounds like the same log-drum loop on repeat. When the hits dry up, these moguls start looking for the next thing to colonize. It is giving very much [Nostalgia Is The Only Currency Left For Our Aging Music Stars](/posts/nostalgia-is-the-only-currency-left-for-our-aging-music-stars) energy. If he cannot innovate, he might as well play the "industry elder" card and pretend he cares about the genre he helped bury under a pile of club remixes.

While Phori is busy playing peacemaker, the rest of the world is actually dealing with real drama. We have Andrew Mountbatten-Windsor getting his house raided again like some common street hustler. He was held for eleven hours for allegedly leaking trade secrets. It is the same old story: the elite think they are untouchable until the law needs a scapegoat. It reminds me of [The 10k Circus: Why We’re Still Catching Small Fry While the Sharks Swim Free](/posts/the-10k-circus--why-we-re-still-catching-small-fry-while-the-sharks-swim-free) because, let us be real, Andrew will probably walk away with a royal slap on the wrist while the system continues to protect its own.

Then you have the global stage where the US trade rep Jamieson Greer is doubling down on tariffs while Iran says they will not "bow" to pressure. Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian is out here vowing defiance while Washington rolls out its military buildup. It is all posturing, just like Maphorisa's tweets. Everyone is acting like they have the upper hand while the world burns and the economy turns into a joke. It is all ego and no substance, whether you are running a country or a recording studio in Midrand.

At the end of the day, Maphorisa’s call for unity is just another PR stunt to keep his name in the mouth of the masses. The hip hop guys do not need a lecture from a man who lives for the drama and the "Phori-is-the-blueprint" narrative. If he really wanted unity, he would stop gatekeeping and start actually supporting talent without needing a massive cut of the publishing. But hey, that would be too much like genuine work, and we know our music moguls prefer to tweet their way to relevance instead.
