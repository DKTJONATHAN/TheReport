Sasini Dumps Nairobi Avocado Plant Amidst Strategy Meltdown

So, Sasini, the company that thought avocado farming was some sort of golden goose, has decided to offload their fancy processing plant in Nairobi. Apparently, their grand diversification beyond tea and coffee wasn't as smooth as they hoped. Now they're flogging this "relatively new" facility, boasting about its "modern infrastructure" and "refrigeration systems" - basically, all the bells and whistles that apparently weren't enough to make it work.

This plant, capable of churning out eight tonnes of avocados per hour, is being sold "as-is, where-is." Which, in Nairobi speak, means "buyer beware." They’re hoping to lure in investors eager to dive headfirst into the avocado export business, as if it's some sort of foolproof venture. Meanwhile, Sasini, after sinking money into this avocado dream between 2017 and 2020, is now conveniently profitable again. Funny how that works, isn't it? Perhaps they’ve realized that chasing trends, much like [Nicole Kidman’s leopard-print look](posts/nicole-kidman-s-leopard-print-look-is-a-distraction-from-the-210-million-pound-divorce-dust-up), is often just a distraction from the real issues.

The company isn't exactly spilling the beans on *why* they're bailing on the avocado processing gig. Maybe the margins just weren't as juicy as they imagined, or perhaps the global market decided it had enough of our avocados. Whatever the reason, this sale is set to shake up their agribusiness portfolio, as they "reassess investments." It sounds like a polite way of saying they’re cutting their losses and trying to salvage something from this whole avocado experiment.

It's a classic Nairobi story: big plans, lots of shiny new infrastructure, and then a swift U-turn when reality bites. While Sasini might be ditching its avocado dreams, the city itself is still churning. We've got airlines launching new routes, folks learning about industrial automation, and the ever-present drama of flash floods - because what’s Nairobi without a bit of chaos?
