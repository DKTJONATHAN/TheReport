Ruto Swears In 37 Judges, Another Day, Another Shambles

So, President Ruto has "presided over the swearing-in of 37 judges." Big deal. They're trotting out the same old song and dance about boosting the Judiciary and tackling a case backlog. You know, the one that's been choking justice for years because the system is as clogged as the Nairobi Expressway on a Friday evening. Thirty-seven more mouths to feed, more likely to get tangled in bureaucracy than actually fix anything.

Of these 37, 24 are apparently heading to the High Court and 13 to the Environment and Land Court. Names are being read out - Robinson Ondieki Kebabe, Joyce Mkambe Gandani, Joseph Maloba Were - as if rattling off a shopping list will magically make justice appear. Meanwhile, the Judicial Service Commission (JSC) was busy advertising for 20 High Court, 15 Court of Appeal, and 10 ELC slots. Funny how the number of appointments doesn't quite match the advertised vacancies, is it? Almost like they're just shuffling deck chairs on the Titanic.

Ruto’s urging them to "do what is right, not easy." That’s rich coming from a man whose administration seems to specialise in the easy way out, usually at the public’s expense. We’ve seen this theatre before. Remember when they promised to clean up the Sh4.8 Billion Fuel Fiasco? It ended up being another masterclass in how corrupt officials and tycoons pull off their schemes, leaving the rest of us holding the bag. These new judges better hope they’re not just new faces in the same old corrupt game.

And let’s not forget the real reason for this grand ceremony: the mounting case backlog. Kenyans have been waiting for years, their lives and livelihoods hanging in the balance, while the system creaks and groans. Adding a few more bodies, no matter how well-intentioned, won't suddenly clear out the mountains of paperwork and the ingrained inertia. It’s a band-aid on a gaping wound.

Honestly, it’s hard to get excited. We’ve seen promises come and go. It’s just another day in Nairobi, another parade of officials announcing "progress" while the everyday Kenyan is still waiting for the wheels of justice to even start turning, let alone deliver a verdict.
