The dollar is currently engaged in a slow-motion collapse, not because the euro has any inherent value, but because the United States has become the primary source of global instability. We are witnessing the cannibalization of the reserve currency by its own architects. Risk appetite remains at post-2008 highs, yet this is not a sign of health. It is a sign of a terminal patient who has become addicted to the morphine of central bank intervention. The risk-on stance of the modern investor is a bet that the house will never be allowed to fail, even as the walls are visibly on fire.

Inflation is being treated as a ghost that will simply vanish, despite oil and gas prices surging by double digits. The ECB technocrats speak of 2 percent targets while energy inventories dwindle and industrial metal prices climb. Their strategy is one of managed delusion. They keep rates steady and wait for a miracle, while the real economy is squeezed by energy-related base effects that are anything but temporary. The disconnect between the spreadsheets in Frankfurt and the reality at the gas pump has never been more cavernous.

Perhaps the most honest indicator of our current trajectory is the rally in defense stocks. While the broader market pretends everything is fine, the smart money is betting on conflict. The outperformance of EU defense firms suggests that the only reliable growth industry left is destruction. We have built a world where the only way to hedge against a failing dollar and a stagnant economy is to invest in the machinery of war. This is the benign environment the bankers speak of: a profitable slide into chaos where the only thing that matters is the next quarterly report.

This trend mirrors the themes seen in the New Delhi AI Summit, where the facade of technological progress barely masks a desperate scramble for dominance. Just as silicon valley titans carve up the digital landscape, central banks and defense contractors are carving up what remains of the physical economy. The global order is no longer being maintained; it is being harvested. As reserves decline and balance sheets are normalized, the plumbing of the financial system will eventually fail, and no amount of hedging into precious metals will save the masses from the fallout of this coordinated institutional decay.
