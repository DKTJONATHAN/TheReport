So, Harambee Stars lost. Again. This time to Estonia, a team whose name alone doesn't exactly scream footballing powerhouse, via penalties after a 1-1 draw. Coach Benni McCarthy, bless his optimistic heart, is raving about their "character" and how they dominated the second half. Yes, they apparently *turned it on* after being thoroughly outplayed for 45 minutes. It’s the classic Kenyan comeback - too little, too late.

McCarthy’s quotes sound like something you’d hear at a funeral reception: "We started very lackluster... but as the game went on, in the second half we started getting the momentum." Momentum. Right. They were "by far the best team" in the second half, apparently, and somehow still couldn't seal the deal. He even lamented missed chances by Lawrence Okoth and Mohammed Bajaber. Chances. Because that's what you get when you're not busy chasing shadows in the first half. It’s almost like the opponents got tired and *then* Kenya decided to show up. Remarkable.

Captain Abud Omar chimed in with his own brand of realism: they struggled to "understand the opponents" in the first half. Shocking. And with "new players in the squad," it's "difficult because we don’t know each other." So, the "good character" and "good personality" only kicked in once they’d figured out which way the ball was supposed to go. It's a miracle they even managed to score, really. This is the same sort of "mobilising pension savings for development" talk that sounds good on paper but never quite translates into actual progress, isn’t it?

Now they’re off to play Grenada, who apparently got thrashed 4-0 by the hosts Rwanda. So, Kenya will be looking to "bounce back." Against a team that just got hammered. Because that's the level we're apparently aiming for now - beating the teams that lose to the teams that beat others. This "FIFA Series" sounds like a fancy way of saying "friendly matches to pad stats and give coaches something to talk about." Don't hold your breath for a miracle. It’s Kenya, after all.
