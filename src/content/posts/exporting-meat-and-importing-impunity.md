Kenya has finally found a new export since the tea prices started acting up, and unfortunately, it is human lives. Since mid-2025, hundreds of our boys have been traded like commodities to fight in the frozen mud of Ukraine on behalf of the Russian army. They are suffering heavy losses while a well-connected recruitment network expands its reach across East Africa. It is the ultimate Kenyan hustle: survive the Nairobi streets just to die in a foreign trench for a handful of rubles.

While the families of these soldiers wait for news that will likely never come, Labour Minister Alfred Mutua is still comfortably sitting in his office. You would think a recruitment scandal of this magnitude would be enough to send a man packing, but this is Kenya. Impunity is the only thing we actually manufacture in bulk. It is just another chapter in the long-running saga of [Saltwater Magic and the Great Kenyan Fire Sale](/posts/saltwater-magic-and-the-great-kenyan-fire-sale) where the nation's soul is auctioned off to the highest bidder while the common man pays the tax.

Over at the National Police Service, the mood is just as toxic. Eliud Lagat, the Deputy Inspector General, is currently enjoying what the insiders call "unlimited influence." Being a close ally of President William Ruto has its perks, but it is causing a massive disturbance within the institution. When the man at the top is more worried about pleasing his boss than following the law, the rank-and-file officers start looking for their own shortcuts. It is not about "protect and serve" anymore; it is about "collect and survive."

Africa Intelligence wants to talk about "identifying power players on the rise," but for those of us on the ground, those players just look like the same vultures in different suits. They talk about low-frequency signals on the horizon, but the alarm bells are already deafening. We are being governed by men who see a recruitment scandal as a minor inconvenience and a war as a business opportunity.

Don't let the daily newsletters and keyword alerts fool you into thinking this is just "diplomatic issues." This is the reality of a country where the police are a private club and the youth are an export product. If you want the scoop on tomorrow's stories, just look at how much the powerful are getting away with today. The crowd is already behind; we are just waiting for the next disaster to be packaged as a "strategic partnership."
