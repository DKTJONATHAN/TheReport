So, the High Court finally decided that some ex-Nairobi City Council bigwig needs to cough up Sh13 million. Why? Because she was neck-deep in the 2008 cemetery land scandal, raking in kickbacks like it was going out of fashion. Apparently, the procurement process was a joke - a "fraudulent and deeply flawed" one, to be precise. The land price, which should have been pocket change, was inflated by nearly ten times. Classic Nairobi.

And it's not just this one. We've seen this script play out before, haven't we? From Kindiki and Matiang'i dusting off files to the Mandera PR circus, it's always the same old song and dance. This "officer in the secret plan" who failed to remit funds to Kericho County? Just another tune in the same symphony of incompetence and corruption that seems to be the soundtrack to our lives.

The whole thing with the land price hike, from a measly Sh30 million to a ludicrous Sh283 million, is just a masterclass in how easily public funds can disappear. This woman, who was the director of legal affairs and chair of the tender committee, basically treated council money like her personal piggy bank. The court's ruling is just a tiny ripple in the ocean of rot that permeates our city.

Meanwhile, President Ruto is out there talking about Kiaraho's life being a testament to rising above "political noise." While that's all well and good for some feel-good story, down here in the trenches, the real noise is the sound of money being siphoned off and deals being cooked up behind closed doors. Perhaps someone should remind him that real change isn't just about inspirational speeches; it's about holding these corrupt individuals accountable.

And then there's this bit about an ad-blocker. Really? In the grand scheme of things, with millions being looted and public services crumbling, they're worried about whether you're seeing an advert? This is the kind of misplaced priority that makes you wonder if anyone in charge is actually paying attention to what matters.

This whole mess is a stark reminder of the "slaps, screams, and shoddy scripts" that seem to dominate our public discourse. We're fed a narrative of progress while the same old bad actors continue to pilfer and pollute the system. Renewing your subscription might keep you informed, but it won't magically fix the deep-seated corruption that's become as predictable as the sunrise.
