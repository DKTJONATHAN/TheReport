KENYA'S EBOLA ALARM: QUARANTINE GAPS EXPOSED AS OFFICIAL PLAYS DEAF











So, the top health official, Mary Muthoni, is running around Malaba looking like a headless chicken, wailing about Ebola. Apparently, Kenya hasn't got a single confirmed case, which is great news, I guess. But hold on, every single one of those 59 suspected cases they've "tested" has come back negative. Fifty-nine negative tests, and they're still panicking. Honestly, the drama.

Muthoni, bless her frantic heart, thinks the sheer *number* of suspected cases is the real problem. "We are at high risk," she chirps, urging everyone to wash their hands. Because obviously, a good scrub is going to stop a deadly virus that's practically breathing down our necks from Uganda. And of all places, Malaba is the "most immediate risk." Shocking, isn't it? A border town next to a country with actual cases. Who would have thought?

The PS also had a go at travellers, telling them to "take the screening seriously." Seriously? In Nairobi, we're lucky if people even acknowledge your existence, let alone submit to a medical check. It's a miracle we haven't had more chaos, given how things operate, a bit like the way Nairobi’s office market is chasing shadows while Kampala cashes in. Enforcement teams are apparently deployed, but I'll believe it when I see it.

But the real kicker, the reason Muthoni can't sleep at night, is the distinct lack of quarantine centres. Forty-seven counties, and not enough places to stick people who might or might not have Ebola. She’s clearly differentiating between quarantine (exposed but not sick) and isolation (confirmed sick), as if anyone not in her ministry cares about the semantics. The priority is getting these centres built, "at whatever cost." Let's just hope that cost doesn't involve more public funds disappearing into thin air.

They're doing training, beefing up labs, and checking airports, which is all very reassuring, I'm sure, for those who have the luxury of thinking about it. Accommodation providers are being told to disinfect beds, a nice thought. The PS keeps repeating the quarantine versus isolation spiel, as if it's going to magically build infrastructure.

Ultimately, with Uganda’s confirmed cases a stone's throw away and hundreds of people streaming across daily, vigilance and hygiene are our best bet. That, and hoping these phantom quarantine centres actually materialize before the real drama starts. Muthoni tells us not to panic and that "we are in control." Right. Let's all just stay calm and keep washing our hands. What else can we do?
