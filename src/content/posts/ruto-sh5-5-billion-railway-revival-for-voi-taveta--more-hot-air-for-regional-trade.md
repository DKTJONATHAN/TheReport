So, President Ruto has been out in Voi, launching the "revival" of the Voi-Mwatate-Taveta railway. Sh5.5 billion of taxpayers' money is being thrown at a line that's been gathering dust for two decades because, surprise surprise, no one was using it. They call it a "key transport corridor," but for years it's been more of a "key forgotten piece of land." Now, suddenly, it's going to magically restore some historic trade route and cut 358 kilometers off a journey to Bujumbura. I've heard more believable tales from hawkers in the city.

The plan is to turn Voi into a "major logistics hub," linking the port to the border. Sounds grand, doesn't it? Just like that Sh950 million stadium they're also apparently building. More white elephant projects to prop up the President's image, no doubt. They’re promising job opportunities for the youth and unlocking economic potential in mining and agriculture. These are the same promises they trot out every election cycle, and we all know how that usually ends - with more empty talk and a few well-connected individuals lining their pockets. It's almost as predictable as the opposition criticising his administration, only this time, he claims he'll "answer them with action." We'll see.

Meanwhile, the President is busy defending his administration's "development record," which, judging by the state of things, is as robust as a wet tissue. He's also having a go at the opposition for their "divisive politics," while simultaneously pushing for "national unity" and urging UDA supporters to participate in grassroots elections. It’s a classic playbook: blame everyone else for the problems, claim to have all the solutions, and then ask for more support to implement them. It makes you wonder if he’s more interested in governance or just in clinging to power.

Honestly, the whole thing feels like a well-rehearsed PR stunt. While they're busy cutting ribbons and inspecting roads that are barely started, actual Kenyans are struggling to make ends meet. Remember when we flagged off that first consignment of goods to China? That felt like a real step forward, unlike these constant announcements of projects that will probably never reach full completion or deliver on their promises. It’s the same old song and dance, just with a different tune and a bigger price tag. It's hard not to feel like we're just being sold more snake oil.
