BUSIA DRUG SEIZURE SHOWS POLICE CRACKDOWN IS JUST DANCING IN CIRCLES

WARNING: Another bus load of suspected 'weed' nabbed in Busia. Police are patting themselves on the back while the real players, as usual, are long gone. Just another Thursday.

The usual suspects, a motorcycle and a bag of green stuff, were left behind by two quick-footed fellows. Apparently, 'cannabis sativa' has a new packaging technique: yellow cello tape. Groundbreaking.

The boys in blue at Mayenje Police Post, bless their diligent hearts, decided to make a show of it. Stopped a motorcycle, the rider and his passenger did the sensible thing and vanished into thin air, leaving their precious cargo for the authorities. Classic Nairobi hustle, Busia style.

Now, the National Police Service is all puffed up, talking about "investigations" and "forensic analysis." What they mean is they've got a kilo of bhang, a dusty motorbike, and two names they'll probably never find. It's a shame, really, considering how these operations seem to be part of a "broader effort" to curb drug trafficking. Sounds like a broken record to me. Just another case of [The Great Monetary Delusion: Sacrificing Stability for Political Survival](/posts/the-great-monetary-delusion--sacrificing-stability-for-political-survival) playing out on a smaller scale; a lot of noise, precious little progress.

The police are promising a "manhunt" and to "intensify efforts." We'll see. Meanwhile, PS Omollo is out there reminding us to be "vigilant" during the long weekend. Because clearly, the biggest threat to our nation's youth is a few stoned bikers, not the systemic rot that allows this to happen in the first place.

All these recovered items, including the motorcycle and the suspected narcotics, have since been secured at Busia Police Station for documentation and forensic analysis. Because nothing says "serious crime fighting" like locking up a few grams of weed and a rusty bike.
