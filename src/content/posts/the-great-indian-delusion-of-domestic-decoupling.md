The focus on AI-driven cybersecurity threats serves a dual purpose. While the dangers of synthetic intelligence are real, the government’s alarmist stance provides the perfect pretext for tightening the digital noose. By framing technological advancement primarily as a security liability, the state justifies deeper incursions into private enterprise and data sovereignty under the guise of national protection. This mirrors a broader trend where the digital panopticon is sold to the public as a necessary safety net.

Reliance on manufacturing as a national savior is equally dubious in the current climate. With global supply chains fracturing and the specter of renewed American protectionism looming over H-1B visas and trade deals, India’s attempt to replace China as the world’s factory floor looks more like a pipe dream than a policy. The reforms promised are often little more than life support for legacy industries that cannot compete with the sheer scale of East Asian automation and state-backed dumping.

Ultimately, the narrative of domestic resilience is a shield against the reality of international irrelevance. If India cannot integrate into a high-tech global economy without falling back on protectionist tropes, it risks becoming a walled garden of stagnation. The institutional foundations are far more brittle than the ministerial speeches suggest, especially as India’s Central Bank Retreats into Survival Mode: /posts/india-s-central-bank-retreats-into-survival-mode. The global stage does not reward those who hide behind the curtains of internal demand while the theater is on fire.
