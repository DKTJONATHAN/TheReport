Only in Kenya can a ghost find a pen and sign off on a multi-billion shilling heist from beyond the grave. The KUSCCO scandal is not just a "sobering wake-up call" as the suits like to say; it is a massive middle finger to every mama mboga and boda boda rider who skipped a meal to save a few coins. We are talking about KSh 13.3 billion vanishing into thin air, or more accurately, into the pockets of 23 senior staff who treated member deposits like a personal lottery win. When a dead official is allegedly signing off on illegal cash withdrawals, you realize the internal controls weren't just weak - they were non-existent.

The "Committee of Experts" is now doing what they do best: writing long reports about "systemic, structural, and regulatory gaps." It is the same tired script we see every time the elite get caught with their hands in the till. They talk about the dual regulatory system as if it is a new discovery. We have over 5,000 unregulated SACCOs operating in a legal grey zone, making the sector look like a Wild West for financial predators. This culture of impunity is everywhere, reminiscent of how [The Parliament Sticker: Your VIP Permit to Kill and Keep Partying](/posts/the-parliament-sticker--your-vip-permit-to-kill-and-keep-partying) grants a certain class of people the right to ignore the law while the rest of us pay the price.

Now they want to talk about "reforms" and "rebranding." They want to transform KUSCCO into a National Cooperative Federation, because apparently, a new name fixes a rotten soul. They are promising an "Approved Persons Regime" to vet leaders for integrity. In this town, "integrity" is usually just a negotiation on how much it costs to clear your name. These people have been meddling in daily operations and acting like de facto executives for years while the actual members were left holding empty bags and broken promises.

The most insulting part is the lack of a safety net. While banks have some level of protection, SACCO members are basically told to pray and hope for the best. There is no functional Deposit Guarantee Fund, no central liquidity facility, and apparently no shame. They want to integrate SACCOs into the National Payment System now, after the damage is done. It is the classic Kenyan move: we build the hospital only after the entire village has died from a preventable cough.

Rebuilding trust will take more than a "Shared Services platform" or real-time monitoring. You cannot monitor a thief who owns the camera. Until these 23 senior staff members and their accomplices are actually held accountable - meaning real jail time, not just a "sanctions register" - every shilling you put into a SACCO is just another contribution to some executive’s new mansion in Karen. The wreckage is here, but the foundation they are building on is still made of the same mud and greed.
