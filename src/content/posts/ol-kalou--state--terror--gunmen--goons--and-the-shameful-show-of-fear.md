So, Ol Kalou decided to give us a little taste of what’s to come, eh? Hooded thugs with assault rifles, waltzing right past the uniformed boys in blue, to remind everyone that “democracy” in Kenya is just a polite word for who has the bigger stick. Unarmed voters and journalists, minding their own business, suddenly find themselves in the middle of a scene that wouldn't look out of place in a bad action movie, except this is real life, and the consequences are far from fictional. It’s the same old song and dance, just a slightly louder, more violent chorus this time.

And the gall of it all? Not just the brazenness of the attack, but the fact that it’s becoming the norm. Armed gangs feeling brave enough to disrupt political events, with not even a whiff of repercussion. It’s like they know the script. They know who’s really in charge, and it certainly isn’t the ballot box. It’s a worrying trend, this creeping normalisation of thuggery in the name of politics. Makes you wonder if we’re not just inching towards chaos, but sprinting.

Meanwhile, we have Ruku apparently leading the charge on some lofty idea of "political independence" for Mt Kenya East. Bless their hearts. While all that high-minded debate is happening, down on the ground, the real politics is being conducted with firearms and stolen phones. One reporter even had her phone snatched while a pistol was pressed to her stomach. That’s not debate; that’s intimidation, pure and simple. And all this while Gachagua's candidate is apparently sweeping the votes with an 85 percent majority over UDA's paltry 13 percent. Convenient, isn't it?

This whole circus really makes you think about the promises made, the systems meant to protect us. It’s reminiscent of the empty promises we’ve heard before, like the ones offered after Kenya's fuel price explosion. [Kenya's Fuel Price Explosion: Protests Rage as Experts Offer Empty Promises](/posts/kenya-s-fuel-price-explosion--protests-rage-as-experts-offer-empty-promises). When the basic services and security are failing, what hope do we have for fair elections? It’s a slippery slope, and Ol Kalou just took a rather aggressive tumble down it.

This isn't just about an election in Ol Kalou. This is about the message sent to every Kenyan: that your vote, your safety, and your voice can be snatched away at gunpoint, with the authorities looking the other way. If this is our preview for the 2027 General Election, then we might as well start preparing for a different kind of show altogether. One where the script is written in bullets and fear.
