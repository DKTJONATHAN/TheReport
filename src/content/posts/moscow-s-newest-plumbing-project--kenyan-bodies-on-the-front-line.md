Festus Omwamba finally ran out of road in Moyale, trying to sneak across the border after his little "recruitment agency" turned into a one-way ticket to a foxhole in Ukraine. They told these boys they were going for plumbing and skilled labor, promising a life better than the one they were scraping together in Nairobi. Instead, they got handed rifles and a prayer in a language they do not speak. It is the classic Kenyan hustle, just with more shrapnel and less chance of making it back to the village in one piece.

Our own intelligence report says the big fish were in on it from the start. Collusion between Kenyan and Russian officials? I am shocked, truly. Not really. It is just like how [Your Pity Is Their Profit: The Sick Business Of Forced Begging](/posts/your-pity-is-their-profit--the-sick-business-of-forced-begging) shows that someone is always making a coin off human misery in this city. In this case, the coin is likely a thick envelope handed over in a smoky office while families wait for news of sons who are currently "missing in action" in a frozen wasteland they cannot even point to on a map.

The Russian Embassy is doing that predictable dance where they say they never issued visas for fighters, but "foreigners can volunteer." It is a beautiful bit of semantics. Meanwhile, survivors like John Kamau describe sitting in safe houses in Nairobi before having their passports snatched the moment they landed in Moscow. It is not exactly "volunteering" when your travel documents are locked in a recruiter's safe and you are pushed into a military camp three days after landing on a tourist visa.

Now we have Musalia Mudavadi talking about a "diplomatic approach" to rein in these "dubious entities." Please. By the time they finish their tea and diplomatic snacks, the 89 Kenyans still on the front line will be lucky to be among the 39 already in the hospital. The government is making "appeals" to Ukraine to release our people from prisons there, as if we did not basically ship them off ourselves. It is a mess of our own making, fueled by the desperation of a youth population that will believe any lie if it promises a way out of this suffocating economy.

Omwamba is the fall guy, the one who forgot to keep his head down long enough. But do not think for a second this trade stops with one man caught at the border. As long as there is a buck to be made and a Kenyan official willing to look the other way, our people will keep being exported like tea and coffee. The only difference is that these exports do not come back in a tin; they come back as a news headline that we will all pretend to be surprised about for a few days before moving on to the next scandal.
