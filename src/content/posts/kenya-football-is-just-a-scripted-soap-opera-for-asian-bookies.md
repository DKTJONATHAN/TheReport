Nicholas Muyoti thinks he is telling us something new. He is crying about "calls from Asia" and integrity like we have not seen this movie before. In this city, if you are not selling something, you are the one being sold. Kenyan football has become a glorified hustle where the final score is decided in a Telegram group before the players even lace up their boots. It is a joke, and the punchline is the fans who still show up to the stadium.

It is easy to talk about "integrity" when your stomach is full, but look at these clubs. When you do not pay players for months, a "gift" from a stranger in Macau starts looking like a blessing from heaven. We are basically asking hungry men to guard a buffet and then acting shocked when the sausages go missing. This is not sport; it is survival of the shadiest. If the clubs cannot pay, the bookies will.

This rot is not just on the grass. From the boardroom to the pitch, the smell of a fix is everywhere. If you think the pitch is dirty, you should see how [Kenyan Parliament Unmasked: Sex, Drugs, Money, and the Scandalous Sins Inside Sodom & Gomorrah](/posts/Kenyan-parliament-is-sodom-and-gomorrah) shows us that the people at the top are running a much bigger racket than a few bought-off goalkeepers. In Kenya, cheating is not a vice; it is a professional requirement.

The federation and the government are busy playing "see no evil, hear no evil" while the game dies a slow death. Muyoti is right to be frustrated, but expecting the FKF to investigate match-fixing is like asking a hyena to audit the butcher shop. They will give us a press release, form a useless committee, and then wait for the next scandal to bury this one. It is the Kenyan way of doing things.

Goalkeepers are out here conceding goals that look like they are practicing for a circus act, and we are supposed to pretend it is just a "technical error." Football in this country is going down the drain, and the only people getting rich are the ones holding the plunger. Do not buy a ticket expecting a fair game; just buy it for the drama, because the results were written weeks ago.
