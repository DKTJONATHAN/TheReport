So, President Ruto has apparently activated his "multi-agency response" because, you know, it's raining. Shocking, I know. He’s on X, no less, sending out his thoughts to the "affected families." Bless his heart. Meanwhile, Nairobi and other parts of this concrete jungle are underwater, roads are rivers, and people are probably scrambling to find higher ground. Just another Tuesday in the tropics, amplified by shoddy infrastructure.

He claims he directed this whole coordinated effort last week. Funny, last week felt like a lot of talk and very little action, much like those [Gas, Planes, and Audacity: Why KCAA is Giving 'I Don't Care' Energy](/posts/gas--planes--and-audacity--why-kcaa-is-giving--i-don-t-care--energy) stories we've seen before. Now, the "emergency services, security agencies, humanitarian teams and technical departments" are supposedly "managing the situation." Let’s see how long that management lasts when the next downpour hits.

Ruto is also making sure everyone knows they're working with county governments. How cozy. And, of course, there’s talk of "emergency food supplies and medical assistance." Let’s hope these supplies actually reach the people and aren’t just rerouted somewhere more convenient. Because, in Nairobi, "delivery" is often a generous term.

He's urging Kenyans to follow advisories from the Meteorological Department and the police. As if people haven't been watching the sky turn black and the water rise. The real advisory should be: don't build where you shouldn't, and the government should actually *fix* the drainage systems instead of just talking about clearing them.

The President assures us the government will "continue taking every necessary step to safeguard lives." We've heard this song before. It usually ends with more promises and a fresh coat of paint on a leaky roof. Let's just hope this "multi-agency response" doesn't drown in its own bureaucracy before it can even start.
