So, the DCI is "intensifying investigations" into the murder of some Reverend Julius Ndumia Ngari. Big surprise. After all the "significant progress" and fancy "forensic examinations" and "intelligence-led investigations" involving a whole squad of units, they've managed to nab two suspects, Felix Odinga Okumu and Berryly Nafua Atenyo. And guess what? They pleaded not guilty. Shocker.

Now, after all that, they’re admitting they need the public’s help to identify someone. Someone captured in images. Apparently, this individual holds "crucial information" that could "unlock further leads." This isn't progress, this is just admitting the whole thing is still a tangled mess. It's like they dusted for prints and found a smudge, then asked everyone if they've seen a smudge.

The whole charade is linked to some criminal gang, notorious for those armed robberies in security uniforms. Sounds about right. Nairobi's streets are a constant reminder that these "criminal gangs" are often just the more organized version of the everyday struggle. It makes you wonder how much of this is truly about justice and how much is about ticking boxes. It’s a bit like trying to solve the puzzle of why modern relationships are engineered to fail, [The Disposable Bond: Why Modern Relationships Are Engineered to Fail](/posts/the-disposable-bond--why-modern-relationships-are-engineered-to-fail), by just looking at the pieces that fell out of the box.

They’ve even released an original and a "digitally enhanced" photo. Because clarity is key when you’ve already got suspects. This "person of interest" could help them find "additional suspects" and the "full circumstances." Basically, they've got a hole in their case, and they're hoping a random citizen will plug it. The DCI "remains unwavering in its commitment," they say. Sure, they are. Unwavering in their commitment to making us think they're on top of things, while they’re still hunting for faces in blurry pictures.
