So, the grand plan for Raila Odinga's final resting place is finally moving. The Odinga family has apparently decided that a giant tomb is the best way to remember the old man. Nine months after he shuffled off this mortal coil, the Kenya Defence Forces - because who else would you trust with building a monument - have been handed the keys to the Kang’o ka Jaramogi site in Siaya. Defence Cabinet Secretary Soipan Tuya showed up, probably to look important while the actual work gets done by soldiers.

And get this, they've earmarked a cool Sh30 million for this project. Thirty million shillings to build a glorified grave. Meanwhile, our hospitals are crumbling and our roads are a joke. But hey, no expense spared when it comes to immortalizing political figures. The National Museums of Kenya are overseeing this whole circus, and they expect the KDF to knock it all down in a mere two months. Speedy work, I guess, but you can bet your last shilling the quality will be as questionable as most government projects.

Defense CS Tuya, ever the politician, used the occasion to preach about national cohesion and warn against divisive politics. Right. Because a mausoleum for one man is exactly what this country needs to unite us. She also, of course, commended the ODM party for their "selflessness" and "support for national unity." Funny how that works when it’s your side getting the state handout. It’s the same old song and dance we’ve heard before, like when [HoPS Koskei, CS Chirchir Show Up For KPA Boss Ruto's Family - Because Of Course They Did]. It’s all about optics and who’s in favor.

Raila Odinga Junior, now the family’s cultural head - whatever that means - gushed about how the mausoleum will tell his father's story for generations. Generations will learn about respect, humility, and service, he says. I’m sure they will. They’ll also learn about how much money can be spent on a fancy tomb while ordinary Kenyans struggle to make ends meet. The plans include exhibition spaces for personal artefacts and vehicles. So, more taxpayer money will be spent preserving dusty old cars. Fantastic.

The whole redevelopment scheme is quite the ambitious undertaking, turning it into a "heritage complex" with galleries, a research library, and digital archives. All designed to document the Odinga family's "contribution to Kenya’s independence struggle and democratic evolution." We’ll see how much of that "evolution" actually benefits the common man. For now, it just looks like another way to siphon off public funds under the guise of national heritage. Just another day in Nairobi.
