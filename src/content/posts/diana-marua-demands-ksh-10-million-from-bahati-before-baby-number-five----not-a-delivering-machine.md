So, the Marua-Bahati saga continues. This time, it’s not about lavish lifestyles or "goals," it’s about money for… creating more humans. Kevin Bahati, bless his idealistic heart, fancies another child, but only when he’s “extremely rich.” Five years from now, apparently. As if that’s a magic number that just appears with a snap of his fingers. This whole "rich enough for another kid" logic is as flimsy as a week-old chapati.

Diana, on the other hand, is the voice of brutal realism here, which, let's be honest, is a rare commodity in their circles. She’s not a vending machine for babies, and frankly, neither is anyone else. She’s still battling the post-pregnancy bulge and, more importantly, has her career to think about. Apparently, "peak of her career" doesn't come with room for nine months of nausea and sleepless nights, no matter how much Bahati dreams of a bigger brood.

Then comes the ultimatum, the pièce de résistance of this domestic drama. By December 31st of this year, if she’s not pregnant, Bahati can kiss that idea goodbye. Unless, of course, he’s willing to pay. And not just pocket change. We’re talking Ksh 10 million. This isn’t about love and family; it’s a business transaction, a price tag on her body, career, and lifestyle sacrifices. It’s a stark reminder that even in this so called "love goals" world, cold, hard cash talks.

This whole Ksh 10 million demand is a predictable outcome for a couple whose lives seem to be perpetually staged for public consumption.
