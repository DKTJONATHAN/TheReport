The ongoing debate between a single mandate focused on price stability and a dual mandate that includes employment is a televised distraction for the economically illiterate. As the latest expert analyses suggest, the distinction is largely inconsequential. Whether a central bank claims to care about jobs or not, the outcome remains the same. When the system overheats, the worker is the first sacrifice on the altar of price stability. The so-called divine coincidence of monetary policy is merely a clinical term for the fact that bankers will always prioritize the currency's value over the citizen's livelihood.

History is being weaponized to justify current austerity. By invoking the stagflation of the 1970s, central bankers are reminding the public that any attempt to prioritize growth over banking interests will result in total economic collapse. This narrative conveniently ignores the fact that the current inflationary surge was exacerbated by the very institutional frameworks that now claim to be the only cure. The modern consensus of a vertical long-run Phillips curve is less of a scientific law and more of a theological dogma used to invalidate any political demand for a more equitable distribution of economic pain.

The sudden pivot toward artificial intelligence in monetary discourse is the most cynical move of all. Faced with a demographic winter and the collapse of the cheap labor model provided by decades of immigration, technocrats are now pinning their hopes on a silicon miracle. They suggest that AI will magically boost productivity and ease supply-side constraints. This is a gamble on a future that does not yet exist to justify the failures of a present that is rapidly deteriorating.

Ultimately, the call for central bank independence is a plea for immunity. As fiscal spaces narrow and political polarization reaches a breaking point, the architects of the global economy are looking for an exit strategy. They know that the scars of the recent inflation episode are permanent. By framing their future actions as a technical necessity rather than a political choice, they hope to remain the untouchable high priests of a crumbling temple.
