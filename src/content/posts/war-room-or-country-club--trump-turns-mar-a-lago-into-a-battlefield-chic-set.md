While the rest of the world is shivering at the thought of World War III, Donald Trump is busy redecorating his Florida lifestyle. The latest photos from Mar-a-Lago show the President in what looks like a DIY bunker, complete with heavy black curtains hiding the usual tacky gold-leaf decor. It is giving "low-budget spy movie," but the consequences are unfortunately very real. Instead of a custom suit, he is sporting a white "USA" hat, looking more like he is about to hit a hole-in-one than sign off on a preemptive strike that just wiped out Iran's Supreme Leader.

The irony is thick enough to choke on. Trump is claiming he wants "PEACE THROUGHOUT THE WORLD" while dropping bombs faster than a socialite drops a boring sponsor. He even took to Truth Social to confirm Ayatollah Khamenei's death, because apparently, official government channels are just too mainstream for this level of drama. It is a reality TV show version of foreign policy where "Justice" is served with a side of oceanfront views and high-speed internet. If this is peace, then I am the Queen of Sheba.

Back home in Kenya, our media is eating this up like it is the season finale of a local soap opera. We see the same level of performative nonsense in our own backyard, where leaders promise us the world while delivering nothing but dust and disappointment. It reminds me of the [Kipchoge Keino Stadium: A Ten-Year Tease That Costs Billions While Our Stars Run on Dirt](/posts/kipchoge-keino-stadium--a-ten-year-tease-that-costs-billions-while-our-stars-run-on-dirt) because, let us face it, politicians everywhere love a good photo op more than they love actual progress. Whether it is a stadium that never opens or a war that never ends, the script remains the same: look busy while the taxpayers foot the bill.

The supporting cast is just as messy as the main lead. You have Marco Rubio briefing leaders like he is the main character in a political thriller, while JD Vance is lurking in the Situation Room back in D.C. like a disgruntled intern left to hold the fort. Meanwhile, the Democrats are calling it a "war of choice" with no endgame. Of course it has no endgame. Endgames do not get high ratings or trending hashtags. This is about the "indicators" and the "preemptive defense," which is just fancy political talk for "we did it because we wanted to see what would happen."

Trump even had the audacity to tell the Iranian people to "take over" their country, promising immunity to any soldiers who decide to just quit. It is the ultimate "I am the captain now" move, delivered from the comfort of a resort where the only real combat usually involves the buffet line. The strikes are set to continue all week, or as long as it takes to keep the headlines buzzing and the public distracted. Welcome to the new era of global diplomacy, where the world is a stage and the script is written in all caps on a failing social media site.
