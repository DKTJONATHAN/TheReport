Ruth Nankabirwa is giving us the ultimate "main character" energy this week, and honestly, the production value is exactly what you would expect from a government-produced social media clip. She hopped onto X to tell petroleum companies to stop acting like desperate clout-chasers. Apparently, the oil moguls are trying to use the Middle East crisis as a "vibe" to hike pump prices, and Ruth is not having it. It is the classic "don't use my name for your drama" move, except instead of a leaked DM, it is the entire country's economy at stake.

The Minister claims that the state-run Uganda National Oil Company (UNOC) has the receipts and the supplies are steady. She basically told the oil companies that their "instability" excuse is a total flop. It is funny how these big corporations always find a way to make a profit when the world is burning, much like a washed-up celebrity starting a podcast the minute they get "canceled." Ruth’s warning was clear: the time does not warrant your greed, sweetie. You cannot just raise prices because you want to "make a profit" during a global crisis.

This whole situation feels like a poorly scripted reality TV episode we have seen a thousand times before. While Uganda tries to keep its cool, the rest of the region is spiraling into the usual political mess. We are used to this kind of political theater where leaders pretend to be for the people while the backroom deals keep flowing. It reminds me of the recent reshuffling in Nairobi, which was nothing more than [Same Old Wine, Slightly Dustier Bottles: Ruto’s Gazette of Rewards](/posts/same-old-wine--slightly-dustier-bottles--ruto-s-gazette-of-rewards) where the faces change but the script remains the same.

Let us not even get started on the collateral damage. Kenya’s tea and flower industries are currently in their "struggle era" because of these same global disruptions. We are out here exporting millions of kilograms of tea to Iran while British Airways and Virgin Atlantic are ghosting the region like a bad Hinge date. If the Middle East corridor shuts down, our fresh produce will be wilting faster than a socialite’s reputation after a leaked voice note. The "contingency plans" being mentioned sound about as stable as a celebrity marriage.

At the end of the day, whether it is UNOC promising "uninterrupted supply" or Vitol playing the supportive partner, we all know how this ends. The prices will go up, the ministers will post more videos, and the public will keep paying for the "aesthetic" of stability. It is a messy, expensive production, and frankly, I am bored of the plot holes. If you are going to rob us at the pump, at least give us a better storyline than "the Middle East made me do it." Everyone is just looking for a payout while the rest of us are stuck in the traffic of their incompetence.
