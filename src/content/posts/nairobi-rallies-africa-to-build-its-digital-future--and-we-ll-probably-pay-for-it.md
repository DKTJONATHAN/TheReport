NAIROBI RALLIES AFRICA TO BUILD ITS DIGITAL FUTURE, AND WE’LL PROBABLY PAY FOR IT

The Deputy President, Prof Kithure Kindiki, at the grand opening of the Connected Africa Summit 2026, did his usual song and dance. Apparently, technology and digital infrastructure are the magic beans that will sprout economic growth and governance transformation. He waxed lyrical about building this digital future "boldly, patiently, strategically and deliberately together." Sounds lovely, doesn't it? As if we haven't heard this before.

He wants us to move beyond "dialogue" to "practical implementation." Good for him. Kenya's been busy with its "Digital Superhighway," laying down kilometres of fibre optic cables and building ICT hubs in every ward - 382 done, more to go. They’re even rolling out public Wi-Fi. All this sounds impressive on paper, but I’ll believe it when I see actual tangible benefits trickling down, not just more government announcements that conveniently coincide with big conferences.

The summit is full of the usual suspects: policymakers, innovators, development partners, and private sector leaders. They're all talking about "collaboration," "policy alignment," and "investment." Malawi's minister wants to ditch foreign financing for local funding. South Sudan is getting its own ICT Authority and a Cybercrime Act. Uganda’s minister is stressed about regional cooperation. It's a symphony of buzzwords, isn't it? Everyone wants a piece of the digital pie, and we, the citizens, are usually left holding the crumbs. Remember how we’re still struggling with basic infrastructure and services? This is just another shiny distraction, like that [Billion Dollar Hospital Ward](/posts/the-billion-dollar-hospital-ward) that never quite delivered.

Deemah AlYahya from the Digital Cooperation Organization reckons Africa's digital economy could hit $70 billion by 2031 if we cut down on fragmentation. The World Bank guy is calling for policy harmonisation and public-private partnerships. It all sounds very optimistic. But let's be real, are these grand plans going to be funded by magic? Or will it be more debt for breakfast, as some might say? This rush to digitise feels like another opportunity for those already connected to get more connected, while the rest of us are still trying to figure out how to pay our electricity bills.

The summit's theme is "Uniting Africa’s Innovation for an Inclusive Digital Market." Inclusive? We’ll see. They're talking about digital public infrastructure and positioning Africa as a global leader. It’s a nice dream. But while they're busy dreaming of global leadership, I'll be here, counting the shillings and wondering how much of this digital transformation will actually benefit the common Kenyan, or if it’s just another elaborate scheme to line pockets and look good on the international stage.

Connected Africa Summit. Building our digital future. Let's hope it's not just another expensive talk shop.
