Oh, look, another Hollywood starlet has miraculously discovered a hidden layer of her identity just in time for a podcast circuit. Anna Camp, the 43-year-old who spent the last decade playing the quintessential blonde-next-door, has officially announced she is bisexual. While she is busy "owning her sexuality" on the "I’ve Never Said This Before" podcast, the rest of us are just wondering why it took two marriages to men and a waning spotlight to reach this "major evolution."

Anna claims she is a "very strong person" for doing this in the public eye, where people are-wait for it-judging and making assumptions. Please. It is 2024 in Hollywood, not 1950s Kansas. Claiming bravery for coming out when you are already established and wealthy is a bit of a stretch. It feels less like a social risk and more like a tactical pivot to stay relevant in an industry that prizes "authenticity" over actual talent these days.

Let us not forget the trail of discarded husbands left in her wake. First there was Michael Mosley, then the high-profile union with her Pitch Perfect co-star Skylar Astin. That marriage was the peak of aca-annoying, lasting just long enough to get the Instagram likes before crumbling in 2019. Now that the heterosexual fairy tale has flopped twice, Anna has decided that "happiness" involves a girlfriend, Jade Whipkey, and a lot of public affirmation.

This sudden urge to be "proud" and "public" about her private life smells a lot like a calculated move. Much like we questioned if [Tiwa Savage’s Berklee 'Intensive': A Four-Day PR Stunt or a Real Career Move?](/posts/tiwa-savage-s-berklee--intensive---a-four-day-pr-stunt-or-a-real-career-move), we have to ask if Anna is truly navigating an evolution or just following a trend. In a world where every celebrity needs a "journey" to sell, being just another straight actress is apparently too boring for the current market.

She made her red-carpet debut with Jade back in June for her movie "Bride Hard." The timing is, as always, impeccable. Nothing says "I am living my truth" quite like a coordinated paparazzi moment to boost a film’s visibility. Anna says she only gets one life and wants to be happy, which is a lovely sentiment that usually translates to "I need people to keep talking about me even if my IMDB page is looking a bit dusty."

At the end of the day, Anna Camp is doing what every B-list star does when the roles get thinner-she is rebranding. Whether she is actually "proud" or just tired of the same old scripts is irrelevant. As long as there is a podcast host willing to nod sympathetically, these stars will keep "evolving" until they find a label that sticks. We see through the glitter, Anna. Stay messy.
