Then there is the talk of AI-powered CCTV cameras. They want to move from NC3 to NC4, whatever those acronyms mean in the world of expensive government tenders. Apparently, these cameras will "analyze data" and tell them where crime happens. Ask any resident in CBD or River Road and they will tell you exactly where the crime is for free. You do not need artificial intelligence when the actual intelligence on the ground is ignored daily.

This obsession with benchmarking against the first world is a classic distraction. While the President is busy with [The $3 Trillion Mirage: Ruto Sells Continental Dreams While Nairobi Suffers the Ground Reality](/posts/the--3-trillion-mirage--ruto-sells-continental-dreams-while-nairobi-suffers-the-ground-reality), his ministers are dreaming of Tokyo and Johannesburg. It is hard to take a "prototype" seriously when the basic infrastructure of the city is held together by hope and duct tape. We are trying to run a first-world surveillance state on a third-world budget and zero accountability.

They also want the business community to open up their private CCTV systems for "interoperability." In Nairobi, that is just code for giving the state a front-row seat to your hustle. It is funny how they want "scientific intervention" for crime, but when it comes to service delivery, they use the most primitive methods available. Investigations will not magically speed up just because the footage is clearer; the bottleneck has always been the humans behind the desk.

We have seen these specialized units before. They start with a lot of noise, shiny new cars, and press conferences, and then they slowly morph into another group of "officers" demanding "kitu kidogo" at every corner. If they want to fix Nairobi, they should start by fixing the lighting and the economy, not by buying fancy gadgets that will likely be vandalized or outdated by next Christmas.
