Kenyan Court Scolds Minister Over US Ebola Site Plan

Just another day in paradise, where our esteemed leaders think they can do whatever they please, even when the courts say "hold your horses." Apparently, our Health Minister, Aden Duale, decided he knew better than the High Court, pushing ahead with a plan to build a quarantine facility for Americans infected with Ebola. Because, you know, Kenya has absolutely zero cases, but why not invite the virus over for tea and biscuits, right?

Justice Patricia Nyaundi Mande is having none of it. She's found Duale in contempt for blatant defiance. He kept building, even after being told multiple times to stop. The plan was to house US nationals exposed to the virus from DRC and Uganda at the Laikipia Air Base. But Kenyans, bless their cynical hearts, weren't having it. They protested, quite rightly, about importing diseases and scoffed at the $13.5 million "contribution" from the US, which felt more like a bribe to look the other way. It smells like another one of those shady deals, not unlike [The Boardroom Coup: How You Got Scammed by a Political Prenup](/posts/the-boardroom-coup--how-you-got-scammed-by-a-political-prenup).

President Ruto, ever the smooth operator, claimed it was a partnership with "friends" who have "walked with Kenya for 30, 40 years." Translation: friends who throw money around and expect us to do their bidding. The Katiba Institute rights group rightly pointed out the secrecy and lack of consultation. They want to know what's really going on, what health and safety assessments were done, and if anyone actually thought this through.

Despite the court's clear orders, Duale, with the kind of arrogance only a Kenyan politician can muster, told parliament he "will not stop it." Now he’s off to face the music, with a potential fine of 200,000 shillings or six months in jail. Let’s hope they throw the book at him, but knowing how these things go, he'll probably get a slap on the wrist and a stern talking-to.

The numbers from DRC and Uganda are grim: hundreds of cases, over 200 deaths, and a disturbing number of healthcare workers falling ill. And our brilliant minds want to build a facility on our soil, potentially jeopardizing our already stretched healthcare system. Doctors and medical professionals are rightly furious, seeing this as a reckless gamble with their lives and ours.
