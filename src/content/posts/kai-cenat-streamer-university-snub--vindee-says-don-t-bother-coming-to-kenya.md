So, Kai Cenat, the big American streamer with his "Streamer University," has managed to offend the entire East African internet scene. Apparently, not a single local creator made the cut for his "Class of 2026." Vindee, one of the supposedly "forward" applicants, is spitting fire, saying if Kai can't even bother to include our talent, he shouldn't even think about gracing Kenya with his presence. Honestly, the nerve of some people.

Vindee, who was apparently busy orchestrating some other event and "missed" a crucial confirmation email - because that's totally how these big-shot programs work, right? - feels snubbed. He's now throwing shade, suggesting a simple phone call would have sorted it. It’s almost as if these digital platforms aren't as sophisticated as they make themselves out to be, or maybe they just don't care. It’s a classic case of them promising the world and delivering… well, nothing for us.

His argument is that a visit from Kai Cenat would be meaningless if it doesn't translate into actual opportunities for regional creators. He's right, of course. What's the point of a celebrity dropping by if they’re not lifting up the local talent? It's a bit like watching those corporate suits from India demanding safety nets for global chaos while ignoring the local markets - a real [The War Dividend: Corporate India Demands a Safety Net for Global Chaos](/posts/the-war-dividend--corporate-india-demands-a-safety-net-for-global-chaos). All talk, no substance.

Vindee was so sure he was in, he even shared what looked like an acceptance email. Then poof, gone. His followers are buzzing, naturally, wondering if it was incompetence or just plain old disregard. This whole drama highlights how easily opportunities can slip through your fingers, especially when you’re not the preferred demographic. It’s enough to make you question the whole online creator ecosystem, but hey, Vindee’s already looking at his next move. Good for him.
