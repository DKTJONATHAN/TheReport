RUTO PROMISES NO MERCY FOR OIL CARTELS AS FUEL SCANDAL DEEPENS, BUT WILL ANYTHING CHANGE?

President Ruto is flexing his muscles, vowing to dismantle "cartels" in the energy sector. Apparently, some big wigs have been playing fast and loose with fuel stocks, creating fake shortages and lining their pockets. Now, heads are rolling - or at least, some top officials have "resigned" faster than you can say "artificial crisis."

President Ruto is shouting from the rooftops that these energy sector "cartels" won't get away with it this time. He's talking tough about accountability, promising to root out corruption that he says has been festering for years. It sounds good on paper, but we've heard this song and dance before, haven't we? It’s almost enough to make you want to watch from home like some people do with ticket sales, but unfortunately, this mess affects us all.

So, the DCI is digging into how senior officials allegedly cooked the books on fuel stocks, creating a phony crisis to justify emergency procurements. Apparently, this emergency shipment was overpriced and dodgy, a clear breach of the standard Government-to-Government deals. They're looking into every paper trail, every import record, and every internal report. It’s a whole drama unfolding, with resignations and arrests making headlines.

Among those who’ve supposedly stepped aside are Petroleum Principal Secretary Mohamed Liban, Kenya Pipeline Company MD Joe Sang, and EPRA Director General Daniel Kiptoo. Chief of Staff Felix Koskei is spilling the beans, claiming these officials fudged stock levels to create panic. This, in turn, allegedly paved the way for a "blatant breach" of procurement procedures, bringing in a shipment that was probably overpriced and not even up to snuff.

The whole thing smells fishy, especially with whispers of a 60,000-metric-tonne fuel consignment rerouted from Angola to Mombasa under murky circumstances. Detectives are now summoning executives from Oryx Energy Limited to shed some light on this particular fuel deal. Whether this is a genuine crackdown or just another political theater to placate a frustrated public remains to be seen. In Nairobi, we've learned to keep our expectations low.
