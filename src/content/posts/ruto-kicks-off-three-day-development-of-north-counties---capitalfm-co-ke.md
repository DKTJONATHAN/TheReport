Ruto's Northern Kenya Tour: More Hot Air Than Kilowatts

WARNING: Another round of presidential promises for Northern Kenya, with billions pledged for electricity and roads. Expect the usual song and dance, and then silence until the next election cycle.

President Ruto, all smiles and grand pronouncements, has landed in Northern Kenya with a briefcase full of billions and a speech filled with "inclusive development." He's busy commissioning solar plants and inspecting roads, the kind of photo ops that make good television but rarely change the day-to-day reality for the average mwananchi. Apparently, KSh8.4 billion is going to connect a measly 53,300 households - that’s a lot of cash for a few bulbs, while the rest of us are still squinting in the dark or paying through the nose for unreliable power.

He’s also throwing around KSh639 million for a hybrid power plant that will bless 8,565 households. And let's not forget the road projects - a cool KSh1 billion for 10km in Griftu town. It’s all about "affirmative action," they say, to speed things up in a region that’s always been an afterthought. We’ve heard this tune before, haven't we? Like [Mbadi’s Statistical Fairytales Cannot Fill Empty Stomachs], these promises are designed to sound good, but the substance is usually as thin as the air in a drought-stricken village.

The President is also keen on education, bringing in 100 extra teachers per constituency. The idea is to train local folks, which sounds noble, but how many of these newly trained teachers will actually stick around when better opportunities beckon elsewhere? And establishing teacher training colleges? Let’s just hope they’re not built on the same shaky foundations as some of these infrastructure projects.

He’s also promised the completion of a 740km road network. "Many times, these roads have been promised," he’s quoted as saying. Oh, believe me, Mr. President, we know. And we’ll believe it when we’re driving on them, not when they’re a blueprint on a glossy brochure. Madaraka Day being celebrated in Wajir for the first time? That's nice. Let's hope the residents get more than just a parade and a few speeches to show for it.
