Xbox is undergoing a significant leadership change as former president Sarah Bond announced her departure from the company after eight years. Her exit coincides with the retirement of long-serving Xbox CEO Phil Spencer and the appointment of Asha Sharma, formerly Microsoft's president of CoreAI, as the new CEO of Microsoft Gaming.

Bond shared her decision in a letter to staff, which was also posted publicly, stating that she will remain as a Special Advisor to Sharma to facilitate a smooth transition. She emphasized that "this is the right time for me to take my next step, both personally and professionally," though she did not disclose her future plans.

In her message, Bond offered strong praise for her successor. "I've had the privilege of spending time with Asha over the last few weeks as we've planned for this transition, and I've seen firsthand her deep commitment to our players, developers, and brand," Bond wrote. She highlighted Sharma's "deep technology and commerce experience, along with a strong track record of building and scaling platforms." Bond expressed excitement for Sharma to lead the next chapter for the Xbox team.

Reflecting on her tenure, Bond expressed pride in the achievements made during her time, noting the rapid growth of PC and cloud gaming, the progress on the next console, and the foundational work towards a more open gaming platform that extends across various devices and reaches a global player base.

She concluded her message by thanking Phil Spencer for his "mentorship and friendship over the years" and Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella for his "sponsorship and support throughout my time at Microsoft."

The leadership overhaul signals a pivotal moment for Microsoft Gaming. Asha Sharma, in her inaugural email to staff as CEO, conveyed a blend of "humility and urgency." She acknowledged the extraordinary achievements of the team over decades while stressing the need for clarity and conviction in a rapidly evolving gaming landscape. Sharma's appointment, particularly given her background as Microsoft's president of CoreAI, signals a strategic emphasis on emerging technologies within the gaming division. Discussions around the future of gaming often intertwine with advancements in artificial intelligence, as explored in articles such as [New Microsoft Gaming CEO Asha Sharma on Defining 'Great Games,' AI Stance, and Succeeding Phil Spencer](/posts/new-microsoft-gaming-ceo-asha-sharma-on-defining--great-games---ai-stance--and-succeeding-phil-spencer).

Phil Spencer, who retired after nearly 40 years with Microsoft, welcomed Sharma to her new role. "Today marks an exciting new chapter for Microsoft Gaming as Asha Sharma steps into the role of CEO, and I want to be the first to welcome her to this incredible team," Spencer stated. The collective leadership changes underscore Microsoft's commitment to adapting its gaming strategy in a competitive and dynamic market.
