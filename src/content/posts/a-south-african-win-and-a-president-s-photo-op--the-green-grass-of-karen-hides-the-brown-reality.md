Another weekend, another opportunity for the upper crust to gather at Karen Country Club and pretend the city is not gasping for air. While South Africa’s Casey Jarvis was busy conquering 72 holes and dodging weather interruptions, the rest of us were just trying to survive the usual Nairobi chaos. It is a "Magical" tournament, they say, but the only magic I see is how quickly our leaders can disappear into a private members-only club the moment a camera is ready to click.

President William Ruto was right there in the thick of it, looking sharp in his Sunday best to hand over a trophy to a foreigner. It must be nice to stand on those perfectly manicured greens while the economy outside the gates is doing a nose-dive. Jarvis gets a milestone moment for his professional career, and we get yet another photo-op of the Commander-in-Chief smiling like the country is not currently on life support.

The 2026 Magical Kenya Open is supposed to put us on the global map, but what does that map actually show? It shows a country where the grass is only green if you have the right membership card. It is hard to celebrate a "growing profile" on the international golfing calendar when you know [the water is just hiding in someone’s bank account](/posts/the-thirst-is-real--but-the-water-is-just-hiding-in-someone-s-bank-account) instead of reaching the dry taps in the estates. 

Jarvis fought through a tightly packed leaderboard to secure his first DP World Tour victory, which is great for him, I suppose. But for the average Kenyan, the only leaderboard we care about is the rising price of basic commodities and the ever-increasing tax burden. A trophy ceremony at Karen does not pay the rent, and it certainly does not make the "hustler" life any easier for those of us who do not spend our Sundays at a country club.

We are told these events bring in international players and spotlight our potential. In reality, it is just another playground for the elite to rub shoulders while the "Magical Kenya" brand is used to paper over the cracks. If only the President put as much effort into fixing the city’s crumbling infrastructure as he does into attending golf ceremonies, maybe we would actually have something worth cheering for. Until then, it is just another day of watching the rich play games while the rest of us pay for the grass they walk on.
