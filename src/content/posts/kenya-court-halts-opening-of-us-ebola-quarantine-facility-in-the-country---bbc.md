KENYA COURT STOPS US EBOLA QUARANTINE FACILITY PLANS, AMERICANS STILL GETTING PRIORITY
WARNING: US Eager to Dump Ebola Risks on Kenya, Court Says No.
US Quarantines Americans Here, Kenyans Get Exposed.
LIFESTYLE
Ebola, US, Kenya
Facility

So, the Yanks thought they could just waltz into Nairobi and set up their own little Ebola spa for their citizens, eh? Well, the courts here, for once, have shown some sense. A judge has slapped a pause on Uncle Sam’s plan to dump an isolation centre on us, citing “grave and imminent risks” to public health. Apparently, keeping their own folks safe from a nasty virus is more important than our lives. Go figure.

These "US medics" were supposedly trained in all sorts of fancy PPE and quarantine techniques. Ready to take care of their own, they said, because Kenya is conveniently "close" to the outbreak in Congo and Uganda. Close enough for them to offload their potential problems, but too dangerous for them to handle on their own soil, I bet. Our own government, as usual, is nowhere to be seen, mumbling about cooperation and global security while potentially inviting a deadly pathogen onto our streets. It smells like the same old story we’ve seen before, where Kenya ends up paying the price for international convenience, much like when [Kenya, Uganda Double Down on Chinese Rail Debt, We're All Paying](/posts/kenya--uganda-double-down-on-chinese-rail-debt--we-re-all-paying).

The doctors are not happy, and frankly, who can blame them? They’re calling it "backdoor negotiations" and demanding transparency. "If it is too dangerous for America, it is too dangerous for Kenya," they rightly pointed out. It’s insulting that they expect us to be their containment colony for a disease they’re desperate to avoid at home. We’re not some kind of geopolitical isolation ward, and our healthcare professionals deserve more respect than to be overshadowed by foreign staff for what they’re calling an "apartheid healthcare model."

President Ruto, ever the diplomat, is talking about transparency and coordinated action, but he conveniently sidesteps the whole “US setting up an Ebola centre here” detail. Meanwhile, the US Secretary of State is on the phone, promising millions in aid. Funny how that aid always comes with strings attached, isn’t it? It’s just another reminder that in this world, it’s always about who has the power and who gets to dictate the terms, and we’re usually on the receiving end of the short straw.

The Katiba Institute and the High Court judge have at least thrown a spanner in the works, recognizing that public interest - our interest - matters. For now, no foreign government will be setting up shop to quarantine or treat anyone exposed to Ebola here until this whole mess is properly thrashed out. Let's just hope this isn't just a temporary reprieve before more "cooperation" deals that put our safety at risk for the sake of foreign convenience.
