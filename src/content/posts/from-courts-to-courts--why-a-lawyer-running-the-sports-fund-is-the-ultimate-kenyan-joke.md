Welcome to Kenya, where you do not need to know a hurdle from a relay baton to head a sports fund. All you need is a law degree and the right friends in high places. George Kithi, a High Court advocate, has just been handed the keys to the Sports, Arts and Social Development Fund (SASDF) by Salim Mvurya. It is a classic Nairobi move. We replaced a decorated retired Lieutenant General and Athletics Kenya boss with a lawyer from the UK. Because clearly, what our struggling athletes need right now is more "legal expertise" and less actual support.

The irony of this fund is as thick as the smog over Jogoo Road. The money comes from taxing the desperate dreams of Kenyans who spend their last hundred bob on betting and lotteries. We are literally funding our national "talent" on the backs of gambling addicts. Mvurya is busy praising the outgoing Jackson Tuwei for "exemplary leadership," but he conveniently forgets to mention that the fund’s kitty has shrunk from Sh16.6 billion to Sh13.5 billion. In this economy, when the government says they are "streamlining" an institution, it usually means the tap is running dry for everyone except the board members.

While these men in sharp suits exchange folders at Talanta Plaza, our stadiums are nothing but grazing grounds for goats. They talk about "nurturing talent" while an Sh11 billion scandal hangs over the Talanta Stadium project like a dark cloud. It is the same old script of [Tea, Tears, and the Art of the Kenyan Heist](/posts/tea--tears--and-the-art-of-the-kenyan-heist), where the only thing being "developed" is the bank accounts of the elite while the youth are told to be patient. We are masters of the PR handover, making sure the cameras capture the smiles while the actual budget for sports disappears into thin air.

Kithi comes in with a Bachelor of Laws from UoN and some International Commercial Law credentials from the UK. That is great for him, but will it stop the next wave of femicide in Iten or ensure a marathoner gets their allowances before they fly out? Probably not. The SASDF was supposed to fund health and education too, but they stripped those functions away in 2022. Now it is just a playground for the "Creative Economy," which is government-speak for "we have no idea what we are doing but it sounds cool in a manifesto."

Do not be fooled by the "handover of duties" nonsense. This is just a change of guard at the feeding trough. As long as the funding comes from betting and the spending is hidden behind "board mandates," the Kenyan athlete will remain a prop for photo ops. Kithi might be an expert in commercial law, but in the court of public opinion, we know a placeholder when we see one. Enjoy the three-year term, Bwana Chairman; I am sure the leather chairs at Talanta Plaza are very comfortable.
