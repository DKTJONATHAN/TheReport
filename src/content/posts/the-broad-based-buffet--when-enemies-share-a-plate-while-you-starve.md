If you still believe that the UDA and ODM merger is about "national transformation," I have a bridge in Kajiado to sell you. President William Ruto stood in Mashuuru and told us with a straight face that this broad-based arrangement isn't about positions or personal interests. It is the same old script, just with a new set of co-stars. While they talk about "historical opportunities" to turn Kenya into a first-world economy, the rest of us are just trying to figure out how to pay for sugar without taking a loan. This isn't a government of national unity; it is a survival pact for an elite class that realized they can't eat alone if the house is burning down.

The promises are rolling out like cheap wallpaper. Ruto is dangling 28 billion shillings for housing and markets in Kajiado, acting as if the national treasury is a bottomless pit. It is easy to promise billions when you are standing on a podium surrounded by sycophants who have already secured their seats at the table. They talk about infrastructure and "deliberate development plans" to mask the fact that the average Kenyan is drowning in taxes. While the elite talk about first-world economies, they forget that [the temple of justice has no ramp, but Runda has a padel court](/posts/the-temple-of-justice-has-no-ramp--but-runda-has-a-padel-court), highlighting the vast chasm between their billion-shilling speeches and the dusty reality of the common man.

Look at the ODM side of this marriage. Junet Mohamed and Ledama ole Kina aren't even pretending to be the "voice of the people" anymore. Ledama was quite clear: they joined UDA as "equal partners" because they didn't want to be in the opposition. Translation? The view is better from the top, and the food tastes better when you are the one holding the fork. They are already salivating over the 2027 General Election, strengthening their parties while the "bottom-up" crowd wonders where the promised jobs went. It is a game of musical chairs where the music never stops for the ruling class.

The irony of a "thanksgiving ceremony" for a Principal Secretary isn't lost on anyone with a brain. In Kenya, we throw parties to celebrate people getting jobs that are supposed to be about service, not status. Governor Joseph ole Lenku says they won't be "ungrateful people," which is just code for saying they will keep singing the President’s praises as long as the development checks keep coming. It is a transactional relationship where your vote is the currency and their "manifesto" is the receipt they’ll lose the moment the election is over.

Ultimately, this broad-based government is just a rebranding of the status quo. They tell us that the people will decide in 2027 based on "track records," but the track record is already clear: more debt, more taxes, and more politicians switching sides whenever the wind blows. They’ll build a few roads and hostels to keep the masses quiet, but the "transformation" they speak of is only for their bank accounts. Don’t get distracted by the handshake or the "10-point agenda." The only thing being transformed here is the speed at which our leaders can abandon their principles for a seat at the buffet.
