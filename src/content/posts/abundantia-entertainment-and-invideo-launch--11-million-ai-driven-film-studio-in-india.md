The announcement was formalized at the India AI Film Festival, held at New Delhi’s Qutb Minar on the sidelines of the India AI Impact Summit 2026. The event, hosted by InVideo and sponsored by global semiconductor leader Nvidia, underscores the accelerating convergence of generative technology and traditional cinematic production within one of the world's largest media markets.

Under the terms of the agreement, the two entities will develop and produce a slate of five AI-driven feature films over the next three years. The collaboration will be managed through Abundantia’s specialized artificial intelligence division, aiON. This unit is tasked with leveraging InVideo’s generative video technology to modernize the conceptualization and production workflows of high-end cinematic content.

Vikram Malhotra, founder and CEO of Abundantia Entertainment, described the initiative as a response to a fundamental shift in the industry. He noted that just as sound, color, and digital technology previously redefined cinema, artificial intelligence now represents the next major inflection point. According to Malhotra, the goal of the aiON division is to empower the filmmaker’s creative voice through technology, ensuring that stories remain emotionally resonant while benefiting from technological breakthroughs.

Sanket Shah, founder and CEO of InVideo, framed the partnership as an expansion of the company’s mission to democratize content creation. InVideo, which is backed by major venture capital firms including Tiger Global and Peak XV, recently collaborated with Google Cloud to develop enterprise-grade production pipelines. Shah emphasized that the alliance with Abundantia is designed to accelerate the creative process, allowing filmmakers to move from initial concept to cinematic execution with greater efficiency.

The studio already has two AI-driven features in development: "Chiranjeevi Hanuman," produced in partnership with Collective Artists Network and slated for a 2026 release, and "Jai Santoshi Mata: Sukh Sampatti Daata." These projects join a portfolio that includes successful titles such as "Airlift," "Sherni," and the Amazon original series "Breathe."

This strategic move comes as the global film industry increasingly explores the operational efficiencies offered by generative AI. By integrating engineers and visual-tech artists directly into the creative process, the Abundantia-InVideo alliance seeks to establish a new standard for tech-enabled storytelling in the South Asian region.
