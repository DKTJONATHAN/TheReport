So, Pep Guardiola and his million-dollar boys from Manchester City decided to grace Real Madrid with their presence, and what a show it was. Three nil. Not even a consolation goal. You’d think with all that money and all those fancy tactics, they’d at least put up a fight. But no, they apparently got "exposed as a tactical novice" by the Spanish giants. The English papers are having a field day, calling them "destroyed" and "torn to pieces." Makes you wonder what all the fuss is about, doesn't it?

The Telegraph and The Guardian are just gleeful, pointing out how Guardiola’s "offensive approach" backfired spectacularly. They’re saying City is "staring into the abyss" ahead of the return leg. Honestly, if you’re going to spend that much, you expect more than just a good old-fashioned spanking. It’s starting to look like a repeat of the last few years - all that domestic dominance, and then they stumble when it actually matters. Just another reminder that money can't always buy you glory, especially not against seasoned winners like Real.

Even Bernardo Silva admits it "looks really bleak now." You don’t say. Saving a penalty just delays the inevitable, doesn’t it? It’s almost comical. Guardiola, bless his heart, still wants to pretend there’s hope. "It is what it is. I felt that we were better than the result," he chirped. Better than the result? With a 3-0 deficit? That’s the kind of delusion that keeps these guys in their jobs, I suppose. It’s a classic case of [digital voyeurism at scale](posts/digital-voyeurism-at-scale--why-darpa-s-x-ray-vision-is-a-lethal-guessing-game), watching the mighty fall from grace.

This whole affair is just another chapter in the ongoing saga of Manchester City's Champions League woes. They’re in danger of failing against Real for the third time in a row. Third time! You'd think they'd learn something, but apparently not. It’s just more fuel for the gossip mill, another reminder that even the richest clubs can be brought down to earth, with a resounding thud.
