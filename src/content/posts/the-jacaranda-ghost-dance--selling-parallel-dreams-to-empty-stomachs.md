Another Sunday in Nairobi, another cloud of dust at Jacaranda Grounds. This time, it is the Linda Mwananchi faction of ODM pretending they have found the secret sauce to fix the economy. James Orengo and Edwin Sifuna are leading this parade, clutching a parallel report on a 10-point MoU that was signed by a man who is no longer with us. It is a classic Kenyan political move: when you cannot provide actual bread, you provide a very long, very boring document about why the bread is missing.

The group says they are focusing on the high cost of living and youth unemployment. We have heard this script so many times it is starting to sound like a scratched CD from the River Road era. They talk about taxation as if they were not part of the system that built the debt trap we are currently suffocating in. This entire spectacle is just a footnote in the [Welcome to the 2027 Rehearsal: Where Passports are Candy and Philanthropy is a Crime](/posts/welcome-to-the-2027-rehearsal--where-passports-are-candy-and-philanthropy-is-a-crime) that has been running since the last ballot boxes were sealed.

While the Linda Mwananchi crew plays at being the voice of the voiceless, the rest of the political class is busy measuring their drapes. Rigathi Gachagua and his allies are already sounding the alarm about plots to destabilize the opposition. It is hilarious to watch them worry about division when the opposition is doing a perfectly fine job of fracturing all on its own. They are more concerned with who gets to be an interim official at the next NDC than whether a graduate in Kayole can find a job that pays more than a bus fare.

Even the side shows are predictable. We have MPs dismissing claims that Senate Speaker Kingi-led parties are being swallowed by the UDA whale. It is a giant game of musical chairs where the music is just the sound of the shilling losing value. They argue about party autonomy and absorption while the average Kenyan is navigating a fiscal minefield. A parallel report will not lower the price of kerosene, but it will certainly keep Sifuna and Orengo in the headlines for another news cycle.

Do not be fooled by the passion at the podium. This rally is not about you or your empty wallet. It is a branding exercise for a faction trying to stay relevant in a post-Raila vacuum. They use words like governance and stakeholders to make the chaos sound professional, but at the end of the day, it is just more political theater at the expense of people who are too tired to keep watching. Jacaranda has seen many promises die in its dirt, and this Sunday will be no different.
