Rights Groups Wailing About Police Brutality, Civic Space Attacks - Same Old Story

These Western Kenya civil society types are making noise again, whining about their precious "civic space" and how the police are supposedly "excessive." Apparently, peaceful protesters are getting roughed up, journalists are being intimidated, and human rights defenders are being "targeted." Oh, the drama. It's the same tired song and dance, a worried erosion of freedoms that were probably never that solid to begin with.

George Bush, a lawyer who probably bills by the word, stands up and declares we're witnessing a "worrying erosion of constitutional freedoms." He’s concerned about intimidation, harassment, and attacks on civic space. He stands in solidarity with everyone who thinks they’re defending democracy. It’s all very noble, but let's be real, most of these "defenders" are just looking for a platform to complain. The report claims excessive force, arbitrary arrests, and enforced disappearances are weakening public confidence. Funny, I thought that was already done by the usual suspects.

And it’s not just the protesters; the journalists documenting this supposed chaos and the community defenders are apparently facing threats. Even places of worship are being dragged into it. Rael Hakai is crying foul about what happened at St Stephen’s ACK Cathedral, calling it "uncalled." Apparently, churches are supposed to be safe havens for discussions on governance and taxation. Newsflash, darling, everywhere is a potential battleground when people get riled up, especially when politicians are involved. It’s not exactly a sanctuary for peace when goons decide to turn it into a political wrestling ring.

The usual suspects are calling for prompt, impartial, and transparent investigations into police brutality, unlawful killings, and disappearances. They’re appealing to IPOA, KNCHR, CAJ, and the DPP to ensure accountability. It’s a nice thought, but let’s not forget how these investigations usually pan out. And while they're at it, they want county governments, faith-based organisations, and the international community to chip in. Because clearly, the State can't handle its own mess, and everyone else needs to pick up the slack. This whole hullabaloo just reminds me of the Attorney General's office hiring scam; another day, another mess, and a whole lot of people pointing fingers.

These groups vow to keep documenting violations and supporting victims. They say an attack on civic space is an attack on democracy. Well, maybe. Or maybe it’s just an attack on people who think they’re more important than they actually are. The protection of freedoms is a shared responsibility, they say. Sure, but when everyone is sharing the responsibility, it often means no one is truly responsible. The police will do what the police do, and these groups will continue to complain. It’s the Nairobi way.
