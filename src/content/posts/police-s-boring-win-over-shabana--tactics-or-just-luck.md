So, Kenya Police FC decided to grace us with their presence in the Mozzart Bet Cup semi-finals by… not losing. A whole 1-0 win over Shabana FC. Riveting stuff. Apparently, it was all about "tactical maturity" and "structural shifts." Honestly, it sounds more like they just decided to bore the opposition to death with their five-man backline in the second half. Knockout football, they say. More like knockout boredom.

The first half was a bit of a dance, with both teams playing their little 4-x-x systems. Shabana tried to get fancy with their playmaker and double pivot, while Police did their usual flexible 4-3-3 thing with a free-roaming number ten. They all seemed very organised, cutting off passing lanes and trying to force long balls. It’s like watching a chess match, except if the pawns were made of concrete and the king was just happy to not concede. This whole "overload-to-isolate" tactic they brag about? It just sounds like passing the ball around until someone makes a mistake.

Then the second half happened, and Police, like any good Kenyan institution, decided pragmatism was the way to go. Weather shifting? Shabana getting a bit of energy? Time to dig in and defend like their lives depended on it. They brought on another defender, turned into a 5-4-1 wall, and basically said, "Come at us, bro, but not too close." It was a masterclass in killing the game. All that attacking flair they might have had vanished, replaced by a desperate attempt to avoid conceding. Reminds me of some of our politicians, actually. Always planning for the worst.

Shabana, bless their hearts, tried. They brought on subs, got a bit more direct, and even managed some long-range efforts. But without that crucial "interior access," as the analysts so eloquently put it, they were just flinging balls into areas where Police’s big lads could clear them. It’s the same old story, isn’t it? Plenty of effort, a few flashy moments, but ultimately, not enough precision. Kind of like hoping your [KUCCPS cut-off points for Bachelor Of Laws and universities offering it](/posts/kuccps-cut-off-points-for-bachelor-of-laws-and-universities-offering-it---a-stairway-to-nowhere) will magically get you a job you’re not qualified for.

So, Police win. They managed the game, they adapted, they defended. They’re apparently capable of going all the way. Or maybe they just got lucky and Shabana couldn't find the net. The real story here is that sometimes, just being defensively solid and a bit lucky is enough in this country. Don't expect any thrilling football, just expect them to show up and get the job done, however uninspiring it might be. It’s the Kenyan way, I suppose.
