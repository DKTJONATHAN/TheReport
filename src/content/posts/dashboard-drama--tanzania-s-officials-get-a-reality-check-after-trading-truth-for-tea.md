Listen, I thought I was the only one whose job description involved sipping tea and dragging the elite, but it turns out the village chairpersons in Arumeru have been trying to steal my niche. Prime Minister Mwigulu Nchemba had to fly all the way to Arusha just to tell these local leaders to put down the phone and pick up a clipboard. He is literally demanding they install special dashboards on their desks because they are too busy with private matters such as gossip to notice that their citizens are suffering. It is giving very much unemployed friend at 2 PM energy, except these people are actually on the government payroll.

The PM did not stutter when he called out the increasing trend of leaders downplaying concerns just to keep their own social circles buzzing. While these officials are busy acting like they are the main characters of a messy reality show, actual residents are being forced to bypass them entirely to beg District Commissioners for help. It is the ultimate embarrassment when your boss has to publicly remind you that your title was not created just for the aesthetic or to give you a fancy business card to flex at the local bar. Power was decentralized to solve problems, not to create a new class of local influencers who are too busy to answer a land dispute query.

The hypocrisy in the air is thicker than a cheap wig at a reunion show. These leaders are out here ignoring village cries while probably debating who wore it best at the last gala. It reminds me of how [Kim Kardashian’s latest 'No Bull' campaign](/posts/kim-kardashian-s-latest--no-bull--campaign-is-actually-piling-it-higher-than-ever) is actually piling it higher than ever, pretending to be authentic while everyone can see through the filter. Tanzania's local leaders are doing the same thing - pretending to serve the people while actually serving nothing but side-eyes and excuses. Dr. Nchemba had to remind them that all citizens are equal before the law, even the ones who do not have wealth to hide behind.

While the PM was busy inspecting the 1.65bn/- expansion of Oltumeti Hospital, he was also bombarded with the kind of drama I usually reserve for my Sunday column. We have got land disputes dating back to 2008 and residents like Lota Jacob who are losing their farms because the local leadership is apparently too preoccupied to intervene. Imagine losing your land because your village chairperson was too deep into a WhatsApp thread to check a court filing. It is not just messy; it is a total dereliction of duty that no amount of fancy radiology buildings can fix if the people in charge are fundamentally uninterious.

Dr. Nchemba even had to direct TANROADS to announce a tender for a 1.5-kilometre road just so expectant mothers could actually reach the hospital without it becoming an off-road survival challenge. It is truly pathetic that it takes a visit from the Prime Minister for the most basic infrastructure needs to get noticed. If these local leaders spent half as much time tracking citizen complaints as they do tracking social media trends, maybe Arumeru West would not be begging for water connections from a project that is already supposedly completed to the tune of 520bn/-. The PM wants dashboards to track resolution status, but honestly, what we need is a total system reboot.
