Eric Omondi, the self-appointed "President of Comedy" turned national savior, is at the center of the spectacle again. The footage shows him painting walls and carrying furniture, ensuring every act of "kindness" is captured in 4K for his followers. It’s a beautiful shop, sure - granite walls and warm lighting in the middle of Utawala’s dust - but let’s be real. In this city, "executive" is just a buzzword we use to justify charging three times the price for a haircut. While we cheer for the new business, the underlying horror is that in Kenya, being sick is a financial death sentence that requires a publicist to solve.

Sam’s vision is noble; he wants to offer free haircuts for special needs children and create an inclusive space. It’s a heart-tugging detail that makes for great captions, but the cynical realist in me knows that charity doesn't pay the KPLC bill or buy cancer medication. We are witnessing the privatization of social welfare. Instead of a functioning healthcare system that supports the disabled and the terminally ill, we have a lottery system where you only get help if your story is "inspiring" enough to move the masses to hit 'send' on their M-Pesa apps.

The launch is set for February 28, and you can bet the Utawala Benedicta area will be crawling with influencers looking for a piece of the "wholesome" pie. They’ll talk about "Kenyans helping Kenyans" to mask the fact that the government has completely abdicated its responsibility. We celebrate a barbershop while our hospitals remain morgues for the poor. It’s a shiny band-aid on a bullet wound, and we are all expected to clap as if the wound isn’t still bleeding.

At the end of the day, when the cameras are packed away and the "well-wishers" move on to the next trending tragedy, Sam is still a man with stage-four reality. We’ve built him a beautiful cage of gypsum and LED lights, but we haven't fixed the system that forced him to beg for a livelihood while fighting for his life. In Nairobi, you don't get a cure; you get a ribbon-cutting ceremony and a prayer. Happy launching, Sam, but God help you when the hype dies down.
