Another day, another gridlock on Outer Ring Road. Kiamaiko residents, bless their stubborn hearts, are back out there for a second day, shouting themselves hoarse over Abdulaziz Duba Molo - Zizou, to those who knew him before he vanished. Yesterday, their "demands for answers" cost someone their life and left others bleeding, courtesy of our ever-vigilant police force. You'd think a death would slow things down, but no, the theatre of the absurd continues.

Zizou, a simple meat seller, supposedly bundled into a car with no number plates on June 23rd. "Unknown men," they say. Funny how often "unknown men" drive cars without plates and vanish people in this city. Relatives have searched every station, but of course, he's nowhere to be found, not booked, not charged. If he committed an "offence," why the shadowy abduction instead of due process? It's a question nobody expects a real answer to.

The predictable chaos follows. Roads blockaded with stones and burning tyres, turning commutes into a nightmare. Hundreds stranded, fuming, as if their daily struggles weren't enough. And because misery loves company, or rather, opportunity, criminal gangs joined the party. Motorists and pedestrians attacked in broad daylight, phones stolen, one man stabbed. Because why waste a good protest when you can also make a quick shilling? It's the Kenyan way - every crisis is an entrepreneur's dream.

Nairobi's Regional Police Commander, Issa Mohamud, has issued the usual platitudes: "missing person inquiry," "restoring order," "working with the family." It's the same tired script we've heard before, a mere echo of [Ruto's 'Fast' Passport Promises: Another Mirage for Kenyans?](/posts/ruto-s--fast--passport-promises--another-mirage-for-kenyans). We're meant to believe detectives will magically unearth Zizou's whereabouts now, after a death and widespread disruption forced their hand. Don't hold your breath; "normalcy" here usually means sweeping the dirt under the rug until the next explosion.

Tension, they say, remains high. Residents vow to keep protesting until Zizou is produced or his location known. And so, the cycle spins on. People disappear, communities erupt, police crack heads, politicians offer empty words, and life in Nairobi continues its relentless, cynical march. Expect more blocked roads, more injury, and likely, no real answers.
