So, the former Inspector General of Police, Japhet Koome, who apparently ordered police commanders to get “firm and decisive” with striking doctors - you know, the folks demanding basic living wages and functional hospitals - is now whining that he shouldn’t be held personally responsible for the chaos that ensued. Apparently, the Court of Appeal has thrown him a bone, allowing him to appeal a decision that found him liable for that April 2024 mess. Because, of course, justice in this country is just a suggestion, easily manipulated by those with the right connections.

Koome’s big excuse? He pleaded with the court to hear his case "out of time," claiming the delay wasn’t his fault. Yeah, right. When has anything ever been the fault of the guys in charge? It’s always the "system," or "unforeseen circumstances," or some other convenient scapegoat. Meanwhile, the doctors, whose strike is now older than some of the politicians making decisions about them, are still demanding a public apology and a retraction of that bloodthirsty directive. It's the same old song and dance, isn't it?

The court, bless its potentially conflicted heart, did manage to decline his initial application to *lift* the judgment that found him liable. So, he can appeal, but he’s still on the hook for now. This isn't exactly a ringing endorsement of police accountability, but it’s a tiny crack in the wall of impunity that these guys usually hide behind. It’s like watching a rat try to wriggle out of a trap, only to find a slightly bigger hole to squeeze through.

This whole charade just highlights the deep rot in our institutions. While doctors are out there risking their lives for peanuts, and while citizens continue to be subjected to the whims of corrupt officials, these power players are busy fighting for their personal comfort. It’s a stark reminder of how easily our basic rights can be trampled, a situation not unlike how some of our youth find themselves entangled in dangerous schemes, as we’ve seen with stories about individuals being shipped off for questionable overseas ventures, essentially a modern-day [Kenya’s Meat Grinder]( /posts/kenya-s-meat-grinder--how-river-road-agents-are-shipping-our-boys-to-die-in-russia). The system is designed to protect the powerful, not the people.
