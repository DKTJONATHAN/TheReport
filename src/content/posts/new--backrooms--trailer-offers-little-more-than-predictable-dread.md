The latest trailer for the A24 produced film, "Backrooms," offers precisely what one would expect: a parade of recognizable actors-Chiwetel Ejiofor, Renate Reinsve, Mark Duplass, Finn Bennett, and Lukita Maxwell-navigating an environment designed to evoke a sense of perpetual, unresolvable dread. Originating as a creepypasta and evolving into a web series, "Backrooms" taps into a primal fear of the disorienting and the infinite, presenting a labyrinth of liminal spaces that defy logical construction.

The premise, as cynically summarized by the film's synopsis, is simple: "A strange doorway appears in the basement of a furniture showroom." This sets the stage for Ejiofor's character to become ensnared in a nonsensical architectural anomaly beneath "Cap’n Clark’s Ottoman Empire." The trailer emphasizes the visual monotony of the yellow-toned rooms, a deliberate choice to amplify the unsettling nature of the environment.

While the article suggests a hope that the film will not explain the origins or purpose of these "Backrooms," this sentiment reveals a fundamental misunderstanding of audience engagement. The true draw, for those susceptible to this particular brand of internet horror, is not the potential for narrative resolution but the perpetuation of the mystery. It’s a calculated play on the desire for the unknown, a void that promises to be filled with manufactured scares. The fact that a 20-year-old filmmaker, Kane Parsons, is making his feature debut with this concept, backed by A24, speaks more to the industry's relentless search for the next viral sensation than to any genuine artistic merit.

The film's release on May 29th will undoubtedly cater to an audience already conditioned to seek out these abstract terrors. The "endless dread" is not a sign of profound storytelling, but rather a well-trodden path in the landscape of digital-age entertainment, designed to exploit anxieties with minimal investment in substance. The true horror, perhaps, lies in the endless cycle of rehashing internet trends into marketable products.
