FORMER ENERGY BIGWIGS OUT ON BAIL OVER SH4.8 BILLION FUEL SCAM
WARNING: This is not over. Just a brief pause before the next round of theatrics in Kenya's endless fuel saga.
The same old story, different faces.
Nairobi
Energy Scandal, Corruption, Bail
Fuel
Three top honchos from the energy sector - a former PS, a former KPC MD, and an ex-EPRA boss - have been sprung from police cells on bail. Days in custody over a Sh4.8 billion fuel import scandal, and poof, they're back on the streets. The Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) is still sniffing around, trying to figure out how much of the public's cash went missing and who exactly is going to pay for it. This isn't about justice, it's about making it look like something's being done.

Their lawyers, of course, are out there spinning tales about how these guys were just following orders, acting on the wisdom of the National Security Council Committee. Apparently, they were just trying to save us from Middle East conflicts by sourcing "emergency" fuel. Emergency for whom, exactly? Because it smells more like an emergency for our wallets. They claim no personal gain, no wrongdoing. Yeah, right. Just like every other politician who claims they didn't know what was happening with the public purse.

The arrests came after weeks of digging into fuel bought outside the government's usual shady deals with Gulf states. This wasn't just any fuel; it was 69 million litres of allegedly substandard stuff, procured through an "emergency" deal. The DCI is now questioning over 20 other people, including the MD of a major petroleum company that apparently handled this whole mess. It’s a tangled web, and we all know who ends up getting caught - us, the taxpayers, when the price goes up and the quality goes down. It’s a familiar script, much like watching how the [TikTok algorithm in Kenya](https://www.example.com/posts/tiktok-algorithm-kenya-ruto-keywords-controversy-data) pushes certain narratives while ignoring others.

The Energy Ministry is already admitting this "emergency" cargo might have cost us a hefty Sh43.4 per litre more than the official channels. Billions lost, just like that. And what does the ruling party say? UDA Secretary General Hassan Omar is talking tough, proposing penalties of five times the projected loss. Sounds good on paper, but we've heard these promises before. It’s all noise to distract from the fact that the system is rigged, and these releases are just a temporary pause in the ongoing circus. The only certainty is that the public purse will continue to be raided.
