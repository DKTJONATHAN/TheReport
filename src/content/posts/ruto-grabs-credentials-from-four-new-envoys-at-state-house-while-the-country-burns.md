So, President Ruto officially let some new faces into the country. Four diplomats, including South Africa's new High Commissioner Salome Zulu-Mmola, presented their credentials at State House. Apparently, this means they're now authorized to "represent South Africa’s interests." Because, you know, Kenya's interests are just automatically aligned. And guess what? Zulu-Mmola brought an invitation from Cyril Ramaphosa for Ruto to visit South Africa. More talk about "strengthening bilateral relations," especially in trade and investment. Because that’s what we need more of right now, isn’t it? Empty promises and more meetings.

Zulu-Mmola even chirped about Aspen Pharmacare wanting to "expand its operations." Sounds great on paper, but let's be real, how much of that actually trickles down to the common mwananchi? We've seen this song and dance before. Meanwhile, the country is apparently still dealing with whispers of Ebola, with CS Duale making pronouncements about strengthened public health systems and a new isolation unit at KNH. Because nothing says "progress" like preparing for a pandemic while you're busy entertaining foreign dignitaries.

And let's not forget the other envoys - Guatemala, Kuwait, and New Zealand. Ruto welcomed them all, reaffirming Kenya’s commitment to "deepening economic partnerships, cultural exchange, and strategic cooperation." It's the usual spiel. "Within the law," he adds. As if anyone expects them to operate outside it. It’s all very official, very neat, and very detached from the daily grind. This whole charade feels like a distraction, a way to look busy while the real problems are festering. It reminds me of how some leaders can spin even a serious security situation into a PR opportunity, like the [NPS calls for calm as security reinforced in Tseikuru following deadly attacks](/posts/nps-calls-for-calm-as-security-reinforced-in-tseikuru-following-deadly-attacks).

The South African visit is supposed to be about trade and investment, which sounds promising, but given the current economic climate, it feels more like a desperate plea for scraps. We’re just trying to keep the lights on and the markets from completely collapsing, if you recall the [Global Economic Cannibalization Masks a Looming Systemic Collapse](/posts/global-economic-cannibalization-masks-a-looming-systemic-collapse).

Oh, and in other news, a suspect was arrested along Waiyaki Way. Details are scarce, as usual. And somewhere in Gilgil, a school dormitory had a disturbance, prompting parents to rush and collect their daughters. Because school safety is apparently a surprise. These are the things that actually matter to people, not another handshake over tea. But sure, let's focus on the diplomatic niceties. It’s easier, isn't it?
