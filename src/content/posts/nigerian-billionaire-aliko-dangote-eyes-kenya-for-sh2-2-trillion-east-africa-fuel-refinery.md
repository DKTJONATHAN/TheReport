Billionaire Dangote Eyeing Kenya for Sh2.2 Trillion Refinery: Another Empty Promise?

This isn't a done deal, just another pipe dream from Africa's richest man, Aliko Dangote. He's sniffing around Kenya for a massive oil refinery project, tossing around Sh2.2 trillion like loose change. But don't hold your breath; this is East Africa, where big talk is cheaper than a cup of chai.

The Nigerian billionaire is apparently "leaning more towards Mombasa" because of its "larger, deeper port." Surprise, surprise. He's also dropping hints about Kenya consuming more, a "bigger economy." Meanwhile, President Ruto is apparently holding all the cards, which in this country usually means whatever the highest bidder whispers in his ear. This whole refinery idea is supposed to cut our dependence on imported fuel, a noble goal, but let's be real, we import everything else, why would fuel be any different?

Remember when we thought Kenyans were making waves in Haiti? Turns out, even that ended with a sick joke. Now we're supposed to believe that a Sh2.2 trillion refinery is just around the corner? This is the same tune we hear every election cycle, promises of economic booms and infrastructure marvels. It’s almost as if these "top influencers" grabbing millions in the creator economy, which is mostly hot air anyway, are operating on the same wavelength.

The East African region is currently at the mercy of Middle Eastern oil prices, exposed to supply disruptions and spikes. Dangote claims he can replicate his Nigerian success here, provided governments "support the initiative." Translation: provided governments grease the right palms. This smells like another opportunity for the usual suspects to make a quick buck, while the common Kenyan continues to struggle with fuel prices.

Dangote might be the richest man in Africa, but that doesn't make him a magician. His decision, he says, hinges on "political direction from Nairobi." Translation: it hinges on how much "support" he gets from the powers that be. Let's see if this Sh2.2 trillion dream actually floats or sinks like so many others before it.
