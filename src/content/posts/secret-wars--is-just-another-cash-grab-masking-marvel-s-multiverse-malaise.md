Marvel is trotting out the same tired song and dance with "Avengers: Doomsday" and "Secret Wars," positioning them as grand finales to their meandering Multiverse Saga. Kevin Feige, the architect of this ever expanding, increasingly convoluted universe, is dishing out cryptic pronouncements about how these films will be "different" from the "Infinity War" and "Endgame" playbook. This is, of course, corporate speak for "we're doing the same thing, just trying to convince you it's fresh."

The talk of "ambitious projects" and "big swings" is merely marketing fluff. The inclusion of "veteran actors from Marvel’s cinematic history" isn't a testament to creative daring, but a calculated appeal to nostalgia, a crutch for a studio struggling to impress on its own merits. They've been granted legal and creative carte blanche to do whatever they want with the multiverse, and the result is a chaotic mess they now desperately need to tie up.

Ultimately, "Avengers: Doomsday" on December 18, and "Avengers: Secret Wars" on December 17, 2027, will likely be more about the spectacle of it all than any profound narrative achievement. It’s an expensive, elaborate swan song for a saga that has overstayed its welcome, a testament to Marvel's ability to leverage audience goodwill into further box office returns, regardless of the artistic merit.
