RUTO TELLS GACHAGUA: YOU DON'T HOLD MT KENYA VOTES IN YOUR POCKET

President Ruto's ego trip to Murang'a County has exposed the shaky foundations of his presidency, where threats and insults are apparently the new currency of political engagement. He jabbed at his former deputy, Rigathi Gachagua, for daring to suggest he commands the Mt Kenya vote, reminding everyone that he's "not God" and his knees are only for divine bowing. It's a grand show of defiance, but for us out here, it's just more political theatre while the cost of living skyrockets.

The President’s tour, a flurry of market inspections and borehole commissions - the usual crumbs for the masses - was overshadowed by his agitated tirade against Gachagua. Ruto accused his former second-in-command of chest-thumping and malice, likening Gachagua's demands to being a "house help." Meanwhile, the ordinary Kenyan is struggling to keep their own homes afloat, grappling with issues like the debt-saturated mirage of digital wealth and wondering if their leaders ever think about them beyond election cycles.

Gachagua, in his own corner, fired back, calling the President's fury the sign of a cornered leader stuck in his own deceit. He claims Ruto won the Mt Kenya vote in 2022 with Bible verses and handshakes, and now he’s reduced to insults and handouts for 2027. The "Fagia wasaliti" campaign Gachagua has launched, aiming to decimate Ruto's support in Mt Kenya, shows just how fractured this political landscape has become. It’s a predictable cycle of betrayal and retribution.

Adding to the drama, Ruto seemed to be paving the way for Deputy President Kithure Kindiki to be his running mate, a move that could complicate his negotiations with ODM factions. Kindiki, for his part, is urging Mt Kenya voters to ditch political anger and embrace development. It’s rich, coming from a government that seems to thrive on division and spectacle. The heckling and burning of UDA effigies during the President's visit paint a clearer picture of public sentiment than any staged rally.

Even the Murang'a Senator, Joe Nyutu, isn't buying Ruto’s narrative, accusing him of "bar talk" instead of addressing real issues like unemployment and the collapsing healthcare system due to unpaid debts. He called out the President for using taxpayer money to hurl insults, reminding him that Mt Kenya’s support in 2022 was met with betrayal. The question of why an "impeached" Gachagua is causing such agitation for the entire government just highlights the pathetic state of our political discourse.
