Darling, have you noticed how every D-list influencer with a Ring Light and a data bundle is suddenly a PhD holder in International Relations? The latest "scandal" rocking the streets of X and TikTok involves a 520-acre farm in Solai, Nakuru. Apparently, an Israeli investor named Erez Rivkin decides to grow some rosemary and suddenly, the internet decides he is building a "Zionist colony." It is giving "desperate for engagement," and frankly, the lack of research is more embarrassing than a leaked DM from a broke socialite.

Between the dairy farms and avocado orchards, we have got people screaming about a secret Jewish enclave. One viral post even used a fake AI image of a man kneeling before a soldier to claim Solai is the next Gaza. Sweetheart, it is a greenhouse, not a garrison. The only thing being "occupied" here is the empty space where common sense used to live in these influencers' heads. While they are busy chasing likes, the actual project is just about export crops and student exchanges. Boring, right?

It is the classic TikTokification of foreign policy. Why read a book or check a land title when you can just scream "colonialism" and get ten thousand retweets? This brand of fake outrage is a lot like [Runda's gated illusions](/posts/from-guatemala-s-stolen-babies-to-runda-s-gated-illusions) where people pretend everything is a deep conspiracy while the real mess is happening right under their noses. We have got people who cannot even locate Nakuru on a map without Google's help trying to dictate Kenya's diplomatic strategy based on a 60-second clip.

The hypocrisy is what really gets me. These same keyboard warriors will happily munch on exported avocados and post thirst traps in front of Israeli-designed irrigation systems without a hint of irony. Kenya has been working with MASHAV for years, training thousands of agronomists. But God forbid someone buys land legally to grow flowers. If a greenhouse in Solai is a "territorial annexation," then my backyard herb garden is practically a sovereign state.

Let's be real: this hysteria is not about "saving" Kenya. It is about clout. It is easier to shout about "neo-colonialism" than it is to admit you do not understand how a freehold title deed works. By turning irrigation pipes into instruments of occupation, these online actors are just exposing how easy it is to manipulate a public that values vibes over verification. Stay woke, but maybe try staying literate too? It is a much better look.
