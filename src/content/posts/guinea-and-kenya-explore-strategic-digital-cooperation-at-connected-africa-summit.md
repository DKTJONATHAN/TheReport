Guinea, Kenya Talk Digital Deals, Probably Just More Hot Air

Another summit, another round of handshakes and promises. This time it's Guinea and Kenya, pretending to forge a "strategic partnership" over digital infrastructure at the Connected Africa Summit. You know, the usual song and dance. Senior officials, fancy titles, all there to discuss how to "improve connectivity" and "exchange public policy experiences." It sounds important, doesn't it? Like they're actually going to achieve something. Don't hold your breath.

Apparently, Kenya's experience with fibre optics is the shining beacon they're all supposed to follow. Because, you know, we've cracked the code here in Nairobi. Never mind the usual potholes and constant power outages, our internet cables are apparently world class. This is supposed to "inform Guinea's ongoing efforts to strengthen its own national digital ecosystem." Translation: They're hoping to copy whatever we did, mistakes and all, and then probably overcharge for it. It’s like Del Monte Kenya getting a police unit; are pineapples really that valuable, or is it just a show? [Del Monte Kenya Gets Police Unit, Because Apparently Pineapples Need Bodyguards Now](/posts/del-monte-kenya-gets-police-unit--because-apparently-pineapples-need-bodyguards-now)

Now they're talking about signing a Memorandum of Understanding. Fancy jargon for a piece of paper that will likely gather dust. It's all about "structuring long-term collaboration" and "coordinated implementation." What that really means is more meetings, more travel, and more photo ops. It’s the African way, where the talk is cheap and the actual work gets done by whoever’s left holding the bag.

The whole thing is couched in terms of "deeper African cooperation" and "accelerating the continent’s overall digital transformation." Sure, why not. While these officials are busy discussing how to integrate the African digital market, actual tech giants are busy reshaping infrastructure with partnerships like Meta and Nvidia's. [Tech Giants Reshape Infrastructure as Meta Expands Nvidia Partnership](/posts/tech-giants-reshape-infrastructure-as-meta-expands-nvidia-partnership) That’s where the real action is, not in some dusty summit hall.

So, what's the takeaway? Guinea and Kenya are going to explore digital cooperation. They'll probably sign some papers, issue some press releases, and then go back to their respective countries to continue doing whatever they were doing before. The "strategic partnership" will be strategic for a few consultants and a lot of travel agents, but for the average person, it'll be just another headline.

This whole affair is a classic case of looking busy without actually doing much. It’s the kind of predictable charade we see time and time again, much like watching governments try to control markets. [Seoul's Desperate Price Fix Signals the End of the Free Market Charade](/posts/seoul-s-desperate-price-fix-signals-the-end-of-the-free-market-charade) They talk a big game about progress, but the reality on the ground rarely changes.
