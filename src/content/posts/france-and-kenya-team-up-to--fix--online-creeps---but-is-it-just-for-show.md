France and Kenya Pretend to Fight Online Creeps - Is Anyone Buying It?

Oh, Kenya, darling, you're all about that digital glow-up, aren't you? Faster internet, slicker apps, the whole nine yards. But surprise, surprise, beneath all that shiny innovation, there's a cesspool of tech-enabled nastiness festering. Apparently, nearly 90% of young adults have seen this digital dumpster fire, and a whopping 39% have been directly burned by it. We're talking cyberbullying, online groping, the works. It’s enough to make you want to go live in a hut with no Wi-Fi.

Now, enter the French Embassy and their big-money pals at Agence Française de Développement (AFD), waving €5 million like a white flag against this digital menace. They're launching a "regional project" to combat "Technology-Facilitated Gender-Based Violence," or TFGBV, as if us common folk don't already know what online perverts are up to. It's all very noble, supporting "feminist civil society organizations" and survivors. Sounds like another day, another dollar for the do-gooders, while the real victims are still navigating the digital wild west.

This four-year extravaganza (2026-2030, mark your calendars!) spans seven African countries, with Kenya getting a direct line to the pity party. They'll be strengthening "local organizations" that fight online harassment, improve "digital security" (as if a password can stop a determined creep), and provide "holistic support" - which I assume means lots of tissues and maybe a strongly worded letter. They're even funding research, because clearly, we haven't spent enough time analyzing the obvious problems. Honestly, it’s almost as exhausting as trying to decipher the latest AU foreign policy pronouncements. [AU’s New Foreign Policy: Five Suits and a PDF Won’t Save Us](/posts/au-s-new-foreign-policy--five-suits-and-a-pdf-won-t-save-us)

French Ambassador Arnaud Suquet is out here talking about "feminist diplomacy," like France suddenly discovered this was a thing. And AFD Director Anne Gaël Chapuis is sharing all the funding details, probably with a perfectly manicured smile. It’s all very official, very important. But let’s be real, while they’re doling out cash and making grand pronouncements, the real battle is happening on our screens, with everyone from aspiring influencers to seasoned journalists caught in the crossfire. Let's hope this isn't just another performative gesture that fades faster than a bad TikTok trend.
