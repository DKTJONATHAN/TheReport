So, Wangui Ngirici, formerly of Kirinyaga, has decided to wake up and smell the coffee - or rather, the burning fuel. She's out here in Mwea, telling everyone what we already know: Ruto and his Kenya Kwanza crew are full of hot air and empty promises. Fuel prices have nearly doubled since Uhuru left, and now Kenyans are paying over Sh214 a litre. Remember those campaign pledges to make life easier? Yeah, those evaporated faster than a puddle in the Nairobi sun.

And it's not just the fuel guzzlers feeling the pinch. Ngirici is also calling out the government for its utter neglect of Mwea farmers. Apparently, the brilliant economic strategy involves flooding the market with cheap imported rice, ensuring local farmers get peanuts for their hard work. It’s a classic case of global capital feasting on local livelihoods, a story we’ve seen before. These farmers are struggling to even cover their production costs, while the government sits back and watches them drown.

Then there's the education mess. Kids being sent home because schools haven't seen a cent of capitation funds. Ngirici points out that under Kibaki and Uhuru, the system actually *worked*. Now, it's just chaos, proving that perhaps those past administrations, for all their flaws, knew a thing or two about running a country. She even claims her own election win was "stolen," which, let's be honest, is a common refrain when things don't go your way.

Ngirici is also trying to rally the youth, urging Gen Z to actually register and vote. Because apparently, if you don't show up, you can't complain when the same old song and dance plays out. She's even hinted at cozying up with "Riggy G" and the opposition, talking about a united front for 2027. It's all the usual political theatre, a scramble for power dressed up as public service.

She also claims she saw this coming, which is why she jumped ship from UDA early. Foresight, or just hedging her bets? Either way, she's positioning herself, possibly for another stab at the Kirinyaga gubernatorial seat, and is even toying with the idea of joining the Democracy for the Citizens Party. It's a real "watch this space" moment, but don't hold your breath for any genuine change.

Meanwhile, the Ministry is busy assuring everyone there are no Ebola cases. Good to know, amidst the economic implosion. And we're told that recent election outcomes show public confidence. Funny, considering how many people are struggling to make ends meet. The cost of fuel is a burden, corruption is a recurring challenge, and resource management is a joke. Just another day in Kenya.
