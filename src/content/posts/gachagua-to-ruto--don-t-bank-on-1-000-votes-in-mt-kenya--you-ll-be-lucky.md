So, Rigathi Gachagua, the former DP and now self-appointed opposition chief, is out here spitting fire, telling President Ruto he won't even scrape 1,000 votes in any Mt Kenya constituency in 2027. He was at his Wamunyoro home, hosting some delegation - you know how these politicians do, always gathering crowds to make pronouncements. Gachagua’s argument? The people of the mountain are fickle, they love to vote people out, and no amount of government spending, no matter how much Ruto "pours" into the region, will change that. He even dropped the Kiswahili gem, "Kura ya kufukuza mtu ni tamu," which basically means the vote to send someone home is a sweet one.

He’s waxing lyrical about history, bringing up poor old Mzee Moi and how he used to struggle in Central. According to Gachagua, Moi only managed 800 votes in Mathira, out of 60,000. Now he’s predicting Ruto will be lucky to hit 1,000 in *any* constituency. This is all happening as the Ol Kalou by-election heats up, a contest Gachagua is framing as a proxy war between him and the President. He’s already accusing Ruto of throwing Ksh1 billion at the constituency, a claim Moses Kuria, bless his heart, tried to dismiss by calling it "development projects" worth over Ksh10 billion. Honestly, it's all just noise before the real circus begins.

Gachagua’s turned his Nyeri home into a political war room, hosting every Tom, Dick, and Jimi Wanjigi imaginable. He's talking about a "sixth administration," a united opposition, and a single presidential candidate to face Ruto. It's all very grand, isn't it? He’s clearly not sitting around twiddling his thumbs since his impeachment. He's building his base, trying to solidify that Mt Kenya support that propelled Ruto to power in the first place. It just goes to show how quickly things change in this country; what was a stronghold can become a battleground faster than you can say "political realignment."

The Mt Kenya region, which overwhelmingly backed Ruto in 2022, is now apparently ripe for the picking by Gachagua and his newly formed Democracy for the Citizens Party. He's on an aggressive campaign, working with other opposition leaders to mount a challenge. Meanwhile, Ruto's team is busy defending his administration’s development record, listing roads, housing, and water projects as proof of commitment. It's the same old song and dance, isn't it? Promises, accusations, and the constant scramble for votes. It’s a bit like watching the KDF finally find a working gun after 18 years of shooting blanks, except here, the bullets are just promises and threats.

It’s all very dramatic, but let's be real. Gachagua’s pronouncements are as reliable as a weather forecast from a drunk uncle. He’s making bold claims, trying to set a narrative and rally his troops. Ruto, on the other hand, is playing the development card, hoping tangible projects will speak louder than Gachagua's rhetoric. The political temperature is rising, and the fight for Mt Kenya is shaping up to be one of the main events for the 2027 election. Whether Gachagua's predictions hold any water or not remains to be seen, but one thing's for sure: the politicking is far from over.
