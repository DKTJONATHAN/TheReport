So, ODM has decided to play musical chairs now that Baba isn't around to call the shots. Oburu Oginga is the new "leader," which is about as exciting as watching paint dry, but at least it's a name some people recognise. Mombasa and Kisii governors, Nassir and Arati, are now deputy leaders. Big deal. It’s just shuffling deck chairs on the Titanic, folks. The real story here isn't who's in, but who's out.

And speaking of out, Godfrey Osotsi got the boot. Didn't even get a proposer, apparently. That’s not just being kicked out; that's being publicly embarrassed. This whole "Linda Mwananchi" faction and their "concerns" about internal democracy? Clearly, that talk didn't sit well with the powers that be. This is the first casualty of the post-Raila era, and mark my words, it won't be the last. It’s a silent purge, plain and simple, designed to keep everyone in line. Just like how [DCI arrests 3 suspects After Migori Armed Robbery](/posts/dci-arrests-3-suspects-after-migori-armed-robbery), they're cleaning house.

Gladys Wanga, the Homa Bay Governor, is now the National Chairperson. She’s clearly got her eye on the prize, positioning herself as a future face of ODM. She’s preaching unity and discipline, which is rich coming from a party that’s always been a hotbed of internal squabbles. She even referenced Raila, saying they learned at his feet. Sure, they learned how to grab power and play dirty.

But the real bombshell? ODM is looking to cozy up to the ruling UDA. Yes, you read that right. They're formalising coalition-building talks with Ruto's lot. This is all about staying relevant and securing "strategic political bargains" for 2027. They’re saying it’s about wisdom and clarity, but it’s really about survival. And don’t forget Wanga’s little clarification: vacant positions can’t be filled at a Special National Delegates Convention. So, Osotsi’s spot stays empty for now. More drama to come, you can bet on it.
