So, the grand lineup for "I'm A Celebrity All Stars 2026" has apparently dropped, and it’s… well, it’s a lineup. We've got the usual suspects, the ones who clearly couldn't hack it in the real world and are now back for a second, or third, bite at the poisoned apple. Harry Redknapp and Jimmy Bullard, anyone? Because the universe clearly needed more of their brand of… whatever it is they do. And don't even get me started on the fact that this whole spectacle is pre-recorded. Honestly, the sheer audacity of calling it "live" when they've already filmed the whole thing and are just waiting to tell us who won. It’s like a cheap magic show; you know the trick, but you’re supposed to pretend to be amazed anyway.

This South African spin-off, which apparently crowned Myleene Klass back in 2023, is now serving up a fresh batch of familiar faces. They’re calling them “iconic All Stars,” but let’s be real, these are the folks who are probably still paying off their last reality TV appearance. We’re talking about Adam Thomas, who’s going back in a decade later, presumably hoping to finally achieve something more than third place. Then there's Ashley Roberts, who narrowly missed out on winning in 2012 and is back for “unfinished business.” Honey, the only unfinished business you have is finding a relevant career.

Scarlett Moffatt is also returning, apparently doing it for her son Jude. Bless her heart, I suppose someone has to fund that child's future, and what better way than by eating bugs on television? David Haye, another third-place finisher, is back to, you guessed it, try and win. And who can forget Liz McDonald from Coronation Street? She’ll be gracing us with her presence, ten years after her first, and clearly not-so-memorable, stint. It’s all very “been there, done that,” isn’t it?

Then we have Seann Walsh, who was apparently overshadowed by Matt Hancock. Ouch. Sinitta is back too, a wildcard who famously spilled tea about Brad Pitt and Simon Cowell. Let's hope she's saved some gossip for this go-round, because frankly, the rest of the lineup is about as exciting as watching paint dry. Mo Gilligan, a veteran of the Covid-era Welsh jungle, is back for a “better go” at the challenges. And Gemma Collins, the icon who famously lasted 72 hours, is surprisingly returning. I’m already predicting a dramatic exit within the first episode.

The truly tragic part is that they're pretending this is a real competition. While the final will have a live viewer vote, the entire series is pre-recorded. This means any drama, any tears, any alleged real-time meltdowns are all carefully curated and edited for our viewing pleasure. It’s all a show, folks. A very expensive, very predictable show. It’s almost as if these celebrities are living in their own little fabricated world, much like some of the influencers we've seen get caught up in [Billion-Shilling Zombies: Why the OG Scammers Put Your Favorite Influencers to Shame](/posts/billion-shilling-zombies--why-the-og-scammers-put-your-favorite-influencers-to-shame).

And the grand finale? It’s happening in London. Because nothing screams "survival in the wilderness" like a plush London studio. This whole charade is a testament to the desperation for fame in this era. They're all desperate for attention, willing to endure anything - or so they claim - to stay relevant. But with a pre-recorded format, how much is even real? It’s a cynical display, and frankly, I’m here for the train wreck.
