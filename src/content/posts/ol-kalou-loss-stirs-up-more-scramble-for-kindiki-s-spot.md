So, the ruling party, UDA, tripped and fell flat on its face in Ol Kalou. And what's the immediate fallout? Not a moment of soul-searching, oh no. Instead, it's the usual Nairobi dance of desperation, a fresh scramble for positions. They're already eyeing Kithure Kindiki's running mate gig, as if a single by-election loss is the only thing standing between him and presidential glory. These people.

This Ol Kalou defeat is being painted as a rejection of Ruto's second term bid by Mt. Kenya, which is just a polite way of saying "we're not that impressed." If that's the case, then Kindiki's dream of being the heir apparent is probably taking a nasty hit. It’s the same old story, isn’t it? When things go south, someone’s got to pay the price, and usually, it’s not the ones pulling the strings.

And let's not forget the prospective buyers of those government houses. Apparently, they’ve suddenly developed a sense of self-preservation and are pulling out. Probably realized the government's idea of a "programme" is more about lining pockets than building futures. It’s a bit like the situation where [researchers are only interested in the chemistry of the rust](/posts/the-ice-is-bleeding-and-researchers-are-only-interested-in-the-chemistry-of-the-rust) when the whole ice is melting. You're focused on the wrong problem.

The headline about Gachagua winning while Ruto, Kindiki, Jubilee, and IEBC face questions is just the cherry on top. It’s a mess, plain and simple. This Ol Kalou by-election is just another chapter in the never-ending saga of Kenyan politics, where losses are opportunities for backroom deals and "rejection" is just a polite word for "we've had enough." Don't expect any real change; just more jostling for the same old seats.
