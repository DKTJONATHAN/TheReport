India thrashed New Zealand in Ahmedabad to retain their crown, but let’s be real-this felt less like a sporting triumph and more like a staged PR stunt for national ego. India winning in style is the "industry plant" move of the cricket world. They have the biggest stadium, the loudest fans, and a narrative that feels suspiciously polished. It is the same kind of manufactured hype we see when a major label forces a "global superstar" down our throats until we’re too exhausted to disagree.

Then we have the absolute tragicomedy that was Australia’s campaign. The former champions suffered a shock loss to Zimbabwe and got sent packing early. It’s giving very much "fading pop star playing at a local mall" vibes. Without their star fast bowlers, the Aussies looked completely lost, proving that once you lose your "it" factor, you’re just another group of guys in matching outfits. Mitchell Marsh’s "bitterly disappointed" comment is the standard apology tour script we’ve heard a thousand times before from artists who peaked in 2016.

The Sanju Samson "redemption" arc is another classic case of the industry trying to sell us a comeback story we didn't ask for. Samson wasn't even in the original plans, but a sudden loss of form for others landed him back in the spotlight. Everyone is acting like his 97-run knock was a miracle, but it feels like the [Bash Luks: The 'Multidimensional' Rebrand Or Just Another Artist Doing Too Much?](/posts/bash-luks--the--multidimensional--rebrand-or-just-another-artist-doing-too-much) situation where a desperate pivot is rebranded as a stroke of genius. Samson’s silent prayer after the win was a nice touch for the cameras, but we know a calculated PR move when we see one.

The real tea, however, was the Pakistan and India "boycott" drama. Pakistan vowed to skip the match, then did a complete U-turn once the ICC likely threatened their bank accounts. When the match finally happened in Colombo, there were no handshakes between the captains. How high-school can you get? This level of pettiness is usually reserved for girl group feuds and leaked group chats. If these teams were in the music industry, they would have been canceled for toxic work environments months ago.

Finally, we have Italy crying over beating Nepal. It’s cute, in a "participation trophy" kind of way. They are the indie artists of this tournament, hoping for one viral moment to justify their existence in a sport dominated by giants. Their captain talked about "Italian passion" as if that makes up for the fact that they’re the lowest-ranked side. In a world of scripted victories and corporate sponsorships, those tears were the only thing that felt remotely real, even if the mainstream media will forget them by next week.
