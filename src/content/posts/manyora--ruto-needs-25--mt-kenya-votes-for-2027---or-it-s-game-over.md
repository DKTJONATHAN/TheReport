So, Herman Manyora, the man who fancies himself the nation's political guru, is at it again, dropping truth bombs that are probably going to land with a thud in State House. Apparently, our President William Ruto needs to pull a rabbit out of a hat and snag at least 25 percent of the Mt Kenya vote if he wants to even *think* about a second term in 2027. As if it's that simple.

The evidence? Look no further than that pathetic Ol Kalou parliamentary by-election. Despite the whole Kenya Kwanza circus descending on the place, complete with all the big wigs and probably even the presidential chopper, their UDA candidate scraped by with a measly 13 percent. Thirteen percent! That's not just a slap on the wrist, it's a full-on face-plant in the dust, proving that the "mountain" is fast becoming a volcano ready to erupt.

Manyora’s diagnosis is that Ruto's surrounded himself with political rejects, the kind of characters who've already been shown the door by their own people. Instead of helping the President, these "traitors" are just dragging him down further in a region that once sang his praises. It's like trying to build a house with rotten timber; it's bound to collapse.

The big professor’s advice? Ruto needs to ditch the hangers-on and find some genuinely respected, experienced leaders from Mt Kenya who still have a shred of credibility. Apparently, these are the folks who can actually talk to the people, unlike the current lot who probably spend more time posing for selfies than doing actual political work. This whole situation smells like the Talanta Stadium saga, where grand promises evaporate into thin air.

If things don't change, and fast, that 13 percent from Ol Kalou is going to be the ceiling, not the floor. Getting to 25 percent will be harder than convincing Nairobi's matatu touts to wear seatbelts. Without that crucial slice of the Mt Kenya pie, Ruto's second-term ambitions are looking as likely as a politician keeping their campaign promises.
