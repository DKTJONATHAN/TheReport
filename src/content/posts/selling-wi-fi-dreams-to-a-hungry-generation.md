Listen, if we could eat transformation and innovation, Kenyans would be the most well-fed people on the continent. The President was in Siaya recently, singing the same old tune about digital hubs and TVET training. He calls the youth the backbone of the country, but in Nairobi, we know that backbone is currently carrying the heavy weight of unbearable taxes and empty pockets. It is funny how every time the government fails to create a stable economy, they tell us to become innovators and job creators instead of just fixing the system.

Let us talk about these digital jobs he keeps preaching about. Apparently, every young person with a smartphone is supposed to be competing for global remote work while working from a village in Siaya. Never mind that the cost of electricity is through the roof and a stable internet connection is still a luxury for many. While he is busy being [Zakayo the Peacemaker: Ruto Plays Global Policeman While Local Celebs Chase Clout](/posts/zakayo-the-peacemaker--ruto-plays-global-policeman-while-local-celebs-chase-clout), we are here trying to figure out how to keep a laptop running without skipping a meal to pay the tokens.

Then there is the NYOTA programme and the affordable housing hustle. They want us to believe that laying bricks for a few weeks on a government site is a sustainable career path. It is a temporary fix for a systemic disaster. Exporting our best brains and skilled workers through labour mobility is just a fancy way of saying the government has officially given up on creating a functional local market. They would rather you go and build another country’s economy because they have made it impossible to survive in your own.

The Siaya Community Digital Hub is just the latest stage for this political theatre. We are tired of being told to take advantage of emerging opportunities that only seem to benefit the people wearing the suits and cutting the ribbons. Training under TVETs is fine, but training for a vacuum is just a waste of time and school fees. The youth are not job seekers by choice; they are job seekers because the environment for creators is toxic, filled with red tape and a taxman who wants a piece of a cake that has not even been baked yet.

At the end of the day, these gazette notices and high-profile launches do not change the reality on the ground. You can call us the driving force of the nation all you want, but even a Ferrari cannot move if the tank is empty and the tires have been taxed off. We see the vibes, we hear the inshaAllah, but we are still waiting for a reality that matches the rhetoric. Until then, these hubs are just expensive rooms with fancy chairs and no future.
