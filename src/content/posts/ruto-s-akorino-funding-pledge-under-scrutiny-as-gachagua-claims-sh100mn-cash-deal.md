Ruto's Akorino Funding Pledge Gets Scrutiny As Gachagua Claims Sh100 Million Cash Deal
The president’s promises to the Akorino church are under a microscope, with former deputy president Gachagua alleging a hefty payout, while Ruto denies any cash for votes.
State House meeting with Akorino leaders prompts whispers of political maneuvering and cash promises.
Politics, Religion, Elections
Money
Nairobi, Kenya, Apr 12 - So, President Ruto shows up at a church thing, promises the Akorino folks some money - 50 teaching jobs and Sh50 million for their SACCOs. All very noble, I'm sure. But then, the air gets thick with talk of cash handouts, especially with Rigathi Gachagua, the ever-loud former DP, shouting about a Sh100 million package waiting in the wings. Ruto, naturally, denies it all, saying State House is open to everyone and he’s just being inclusive. Funny how inclusivity suddenly sounds like a handshake with a fat wallet.

He’s out here talking about how his journey with the Akorino started before he was president, and how no one can divide Kenya along ethnic or religious lines. Noble sentiments, if you ignore the context. Because while he’s making these grand pronouncements about unity, Gachagua is busy painting a picture of political maneuvering, accusing Ruto of "crossing the red line" and trying to "sanitize" the government. It’s a classic Kenyan political dance: promises here, accusations there, and the ordinary Kenyan left wondering who’s telling the truth and what’s really in it for them.

The whole drama is happening against a backdrop of unrest in Kiambu, where a land dispute has turned ugly. Elders are crying foul over an affordable housing project on what they call a historical cultural shrine. Police are deploying tear gas. It’s all very convenient, isn't it? One minute you're talking about inclusivity and state-sanctioned support, the next you're caught in the middle of a land battle and accusations of profiling. Makes you wonder if the "nitawapanga" promise was for the Akorino, or for whoever can cause a distraction.

Ruto’s commitment to "kupanga" - which, in Nairobi slang, can mean anything from arranging to downright orchestrating - wasn't exactly clear on the figures. After the State House feed conveniently cut out, all we’re left with are whispers and Gachagua’s unsubstantiated Sh100 million claim. It’s a messy situation, typical of this city where everything from a church service to a land dispute can turn into a political spectacle. It’s enough to make you think about how the Middle East conflict is making life a hell of a lot more expensive right here at home, adding another layer of worry to all this political theatre.

The competing narratives are as thick as the smog over Industrial Area. One side talks about official pledges and programmes, the other whispers about undeclared cash and political favours. And somewhere in between, the truth is probably buried under a pile of unfulfilled promises and convenient denials. This is Nairobi, after all. We’ve seen it all before. Just another day, another dollar, another scandal.
