CALEB AMISI: KENYA'S PARLIAMENT NEEDS ACCOUNTABLE PUPPETS WHO CAN MOCK THE EXECUTIVE











So, Saboti MP Caleb Amisi has suddenly discovered "accountability," "independent thinking," and "principled leadership." Bless his cotton socks. He’s out here on TV, probably sipping some expensive bottled water while lecturing us about how MPs should dare to disagree with the President. Because, you know, that's *exactly* what happens in this country. A little disagreement, a polite "Sir, with all due respect, I can't vote for that economic suicide bill," and then… what? A promotion? A handshake? More likely, a swift trip to the political wilderness, or worse, an invitation to a very exclusive, very silent meeting.

Amisi's grand idea is that we need MPs who will put "national interest" above party loyalty. This is rich. Most of these folks wouldn't know national interest if it slapped them with a Constituency Development Fund cheque. They're loyal to whoever signs their paycheque, or whoever promises them a smoother ride through the next election cycle. He talks about phasing out the current parliament and bringing in a new one, as if the problem is the faces, not the rotten foundation. We’ve seen this movie before, and the ending is always the same: more talk, less action, and the same old political indiscipline running riot.

He even has the audacity to suggest that Parliament is becoming a "clearinghouse for State House directives," passing laws against public will. You think? After years of watching independent bodies get "captured" and constitutional crises brew, we're just now noticing? It's like complaining your house is on fire *after* the roof has caved in. This whole "constitutional and human rights crisis" he vaguely alludes to, with state violence and corruption? That’s not a crisis, that's the business as usual playbook. And don't even get me started on the money that vanishes into thin air, like during that whole [AFCON 2027: A 45 Billion Shilling Gold-Plated Heist](/posts/afcon-2027--a-45-billion-shilling-gold-plated-heist) fiasco.

Amisi’s call for accountability is just another performative plea in a system designed for anything but. While he's busy dreaming of brave MPs challenging the executive, the reality is that most are either too scared, too compromised, or too comfortable to rock the boat. They're too busy enjoying Nairobi's "soft life," which, as we've seen, is often built on a foundation of [clogged drains and messy truths](/posts/nairobi-s--soft-life--goes-underwater-as-clogged-drains-expose-the-messy-truth).

So, what's the takeaway from Amisi's impassioned speech? That we need better leaders. Groundbreaking. Maybe next week he'll tell us that water is wet. Until these MPs stop being mere echoes of whoever shouts loudest, and actually start doing something that isn't just for show, don't expect any real change. The system is rigged, and a few well-meaning speeches won't fix it. It's just more noise in the grand Nairobi theatre.
