So, Meta's bought Moltbook. Not exactly a shocker, is it? Another company that barely got off the ground, rife with security flaws and questions about whether its own creations were even authentic, has been swallowed whole by the insatiable beast of Big Tech. Moltbook, this supposed "Reddit for AI agents," is now Meta's problem, or rather, Meta's new playground for its "Superintelligence Labs." Because that's what they do, right? Acquire anything that remotely looks like the future, strip it for parts, and rebrand it as their own grand vision.

The whole Moltbook narrative is dripping with the usual Silicon Valley hype, amplified by a healthy dose of skepticism. Launched with promises of autonomous agents making posts and comments, it quickly went viral. Except, surprise, surprise, experts suspect humans were pulling the strings all along. Classic. A platform designed for artificial intelligence ends up being a stage for human deception.

And the security? A dumpster fire. Exposed API keys, people hijacking AI agents - it's a miracle it lasted this long. But for Meta, these are just minor inconveniences, teething problems before they can integrate this "agentic experience" into their existing ecosystem of surveillance and manipulation. "Innovative, secure agentic experiences to everyone," they say. Translation: more ways to understand you, sell to you, and keep you hooked.

The fact that OpenAI snagged the founder of OpenClaw, Moltbook's underlying AI tech, just weeks before this acquisition only adds to the cynical theatre. It's a land grab, plain and simple. Everyone's scrambling for AI talent and early-stage projects, hoping to catch the next big wave before it crashes. Moltbook's future is uncertain, with talk of existing users being able to "keep using it" temporarily. That means it'll be either absorbed, rebranded, or quietly shut down once Meta extracts whatever value it can. Another failed experiment, another acquisition, another step in the relentless march of corporate AI dominance. Don't expect any revolutionary AI consciousness to emerge from this. Expect more data harvesting, more algorithmic control, and more of the same old Meta playbook.
