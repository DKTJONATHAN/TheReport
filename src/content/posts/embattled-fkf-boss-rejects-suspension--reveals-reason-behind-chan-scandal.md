FKF Boss Hussein Mohamed Rejects Suspension, Blames CHAN Scandal Smear Campaign

FKF boss Hussein Mohamed has thrown shade back at his suspension by the Football Kenya Federation, calling it a load of nonsense. He’s saying no money went missing for the 2025 African Nations Championship (CHAN), and this whole drama is just some petty revenge because he’s trying to clean up the mess at FKF. Meanwhile, they’ve put Macdonald Mariga in charge and frozen all of Mohamed’s accounts - because that’s how you solve problems in Kenya, by locking everything down.

Mohamed is out here claiming the Ksh42 million they’re yapping about was for insurance, and guess what? It was CAF’s problem, not FKF’s. He says the procurement was done by the book, according to CAF’s rules. So, according to him, FKF didn’t pay a dime to any insurance company. He’s painting himself as the victim, a good guy trying to fight corruption who’s now the poster boy for some made-up scandal. It’s a classic move, really.

He’s happy to talk to investigators, but only if they’re not chasing these "unfounded and malicious reports." This whole fight for reforms, he says, is inviting resistance. It’s the usual song and dance when someone tries to stir the pot in our country. You know how it is; trying to fix things usually just gets you more trouble. It reminds me a bit of when Caroli Omondi was telling governors to report extortion claims to the right places instead of shouting on social media. This whole FKF mess is just another version of that: people pointing fingers and causing a circus.

While Mohamed is busy playing the martyr, the FKF National Executive Committee (NEC) has decided to freeze all his bank accounts. They say it’s to "preserve evidence and prevent interference." Sounds fancy, but it’s just a way to cut him off at the pass. They’ve even put Deputy President Macdonald Mariga in the acting president seat. So, Mohamed’s out, Mariga’s in, and the money is locked up. Standard procedure for any Kenyan drama.

Mohamed’s convinced this is a smear campaign to undermine his leadership and his “reform agenda.” He’s saying they’re using him as the “poster child in the so-called CHAN scandals.” It’s a desperate act of revenge, apparently. He’s adamant he won’t be distracted from his work, but you can bet this fight is far from over. This is Kenya, after all. Nothing is ever straightforward.

The NEC resolution is clearly aimed at sidelining Mohamed, but he’s not backing down. He’s just as stubborn as a stubborn goat on a slippery hill. This whole thing is less about football and more about power plays and who gets to control the purse strings. And the fans? They’re just left watching the drama unfold, wondering if they’ll ever see a stable FKF.

FKF Boss Hussein Mohamed Rejects Suspension, Names CHAN Scandal Smear Campaign
FKF boss Hussein Mohamed has rejected his suspension by the Football Kenya Federation (FKF), terming the move unjustified and maintaining that no funds were lost during the 2025 African Nations Championship (CHAN). Mohamed’s response comes a day after FKF's National Executive Committee (NEC) announced his suspension over allegations of financial mismanagement. The suspension involved the alleged misappropriation of Ksh42 million linked to CHAN preparations, with NEC also directing Abdullahi Yussuf Ibrahim and Acting CEO Dennis Gicheru to step aside.
