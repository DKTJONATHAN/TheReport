Eight Students Arrested Over Utumishi Girls' Fire Tragedy As DCI Launches Probe - Just Another Day in Kenya

The DCI is "investigating" the Utumishi Girls' fire. Sixteen dead. Apparently, eight students are "persons of interest" for planning and executing this arson. Because, you know, teenage girls are just brimming with malicious intent and arson skills. It’s a miracle we haven’t had more of this if you ask me. The "dedicated investigation team" and all the acronyms - KDF, NYS, KRCS - will surely get to the bottom of it. Just like they always do.

The Meline Waithera Dormitory, a two-storey building, went up in flames. The top floor, packed with 135 double-decker beds, is apparently toast. Sixteen bodies recovered. Sixteen. Lost in a fire. And the DCI is busy analysing burn patterns and electrical installations. Because that's what you do when kids are dead - meticulously document the tragedy. It’s a real comfort to know these highly specialized units are on the scene, processing, documenting, and reconstructing the sequence of events. Just like they're doing with all the other "situations" in this country.

They say they've interviewed students, teachers, and witnesses. They're reviewing CCTV. All in pursuit of the "motive." Because clearly, the motive for burning down a girls' dormitory is something deeply complex and hidden. Perhaps it's related to the general sense of chaos and neglect that pervades so much of our society. It’s a wonder more things don’t go up in flames when you look at the state of affairs, from the rising costs that make life a struggle to the ever-present political drama. You can read about how fuel prices are going up and down, but what about the price of a child's life? This whole situation just reminds me of when we talked about stricter police recruitment measures; more of the same, just dressed up differently.

The police are offering condolences and wishing a "quick recovery." To whom? The eight arrested girls? The survivors? Or perhaps to the nation, for having to endure yet another preventable disaster? They are appealing for calm and asking people to let the investigations continue. Sure, let's just let it continue. Because that’s worked so well for us in the past. Another fire, more deaths, a few arrests to make it look like something’s being done. And then, we move on to the next unfolding disaster. Nairobi realism, I tell you.
