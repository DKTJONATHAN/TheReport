Oh look, another Amapiano "phenomenon" has hit our screens because apparently, we do not have enough songs about heartbreak yet. Eswatini-born Da Moon has teamed up with producer Blaq Major for a track called "UMJOLO," and the PR machine is working overtime to make it sound like the second coming of Brenda Fassie. They met on WhatsApp, which is basically the musical equivalent of a "u up?" text, and now we are supposed to celebrate this "spontaneous digital exchange" as if they spent months in a studio crafting a masterpiece.

Da Moon claims he wrote the lyrics in just a few minutes after hearing the beat. We can tell, honey. In an industry where everyone is recycling the same three chords, bragging about how little time you spent on your "art" is a bold choice. While he is busy carving out a space in Johannesburg, he is making sure to mention his Lavumisa roots at every turn. It is the classic "I have made it big but I am still humble" script that we see more often than [the 'second-hand' scam](/posts/the-second-hand-scam-how-refurbished-electronics-are-being-sold-as-new-in-luthuli-avenue) on Luthuli Avenue.

The song is supposedly an "uplifting" anthem for women leaving toxic relationships. Because nothing says "healing" like a TikTok dance challenge, right? Da Moon is out here telling everyone not to force love and that God will provide a soul mate, while the internet records itself lip-syncing for clout. It is the ultimate irony: a song about "freedom and happiness" being used as fuel for the most addictive, soul-sucking algorithm on the planet. People are flooding the app with creative videos, but let us be real, they are just chasing the next trending sound.

Let us talk about Blaq Major’s sudden obsession with the Eswatini flag. He includes it in every post to show "pride," but let us be realistic: it is a smart move to capture a loyal, untapped fanbase from the Kingdom. Is it a genuine cross-border synergy or just savvy regional marketing to boost his own numbers? While the official release date for the full track is still "being finalised," they are begging fans to keep the momentum building. It is the new industry standard: make the audience do the marketing for free before you even drop the product.

At the end of the day, "UMJOLO" is just the latest entry in the "dating is a scam" genre that dominates social media. We will see if Da Moon actually becomes a "lasting musical success" or if this is just another fifteen-second loop that disappears once the next trend arrives. For now, keep dancing for the likes, because that is apparently the only way to get a "soul mate" or a hit record in 2024. Don't say I didn't warn you when you're still single and the song is forgotten by December.
