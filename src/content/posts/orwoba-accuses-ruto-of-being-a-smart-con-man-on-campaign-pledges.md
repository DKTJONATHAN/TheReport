Orwoba Says Ruto Is A Con Man Who Lied About Pledges

Gloria Orwoba, formerly a nominated Senator, has gone on record calling President William Ruto a "smart con-man," accusing him of pulling the wool over Kenyans' eyes with his election promises. According to her, Ruto has a knack for making people defend him, even when the reality of his administration's performance - or lack thereof - is staring everyone in the face. It's the same old song and dance, isn't it? Promises made, hopes raised, and then... crickets.

Speaking to some local media last Tuesday, Orwoba detailed how Ruto apparently excels at manipulating conversations, turning criticisms into defenses and making supporters believe he's being unfairly targeted. She admitted to being part of the team that drafted the Kenya Kwanza manifesto, a fact that seems to weigh on her now, as she claims they inadvertently set the stage for this "con man" act. This isn't exactly groundbreaking news for anyone who's been watching Kenyan politics; it’s the same playbook we saw when discussing the empty promises surrounding KDF's Laikipia 'Security Efforts'.

This fresh wave of criticism lands squarely on the government's doorstep at a time when Kenyans are already feeling the pinch from rising unemployment, hefty taxes, and the general struggle to make ends meet. It’s easy to see why Orwoba’s comments are resonating. The President, on the other hand, maintains that his administration hasn't over-promised, insisting all manifesto pledges are achievable. He said as much at a retreat last year, claiming they promised "what is doable." Doin'able for who, exactly? The ones already swimming in cash, I presume.

The entire debacle reopens the age-old question: were these ambitious promises mere political theatrics designed to sell a dream, or is there a genuine plan that just hasn't kicked in yet? Given the current economic climate, and the consistent pattern of unmet expectations, it's hard to be optimistic. It feels like we're perpetually walking into a situation where the talk is grand, but the walk is more like a stumble.

Orwoba's blunt assessment is a stark reminder that while leaders might be adept at persuasion, the people bear the brunt of the consequences when those persuasive words don't translate into tangible improvements. It's a narrative that's become all too familiar, much like the never ending story of the IMF Debt Trap Killing Kenyan Dreams.







Former nominated Senator Gloria Orwoba has unleashed a verbal assault on President William Ruto, labeling him a "smart con-man" who has perfected the art of convincing people despite a growing chorus of discontent over unfulfilled promises. In an interview with a local station last Tuesday, Orwoba asserted that Ruto possesses an uncanny ability to engage critics and leave them not only defending him but also convinced that he is being unfairly scrutinized.

According to Orwoba, the President has honed the skill of transforming potentially damaging conversations into moments of political salvation by persuading those around him to disregard criticisms leveled against his administration. "William Ruto is smart enough to meet you and let you ask him questions. He will then make you leave the boardroom inspired and convinced. So you will be ready to defend him and even claim that he was being unfairly accused. He is a smart con-man," she stated.

These remarks surface amid mounting debate about the Kenya Kwanza administration's track record, particularly concerning the economic pledges made during the 2022 General Election campaign. Orwoba, who revealed her involvement in drafting the Kenya Kwanza manifesto, admitted that she now believes those promises inadvertently set unrealistic expectations for millions of Kenyans. "Having been part of the team that drafted the manifesto, I think we participated in setting the stage for a con man," she confessed.

Her criticism intensifies the pressure on the government at a time when public dissatisfaction is escalating due to unemployment, taxation, and the high cost of living. This stands in stark contrast to President Ruto's own defense of his administration during a National Executive Retreat last year, where he insisted that all commitments in the Kenya Kwanza manifesto remain achievable despite public criticism. He was quoted saying, "The accusation I often get is that we over-promised. But I want to tell you, we did not over-promise. We promised what is doable."

The President further reiterated his administration's commitment to delivering on every pledge made to Kenyans. "All the commitments we made to the people of Kenya are documented in our manifesto, and I am very clear in my mind that we will deliver on that manifesto successfully," Ruto had declared. Orwoba's statements effectively reignite the debate on whether the government's ambitious promises were grounded in reality or simply political rhetoric designed to sell false hope, a narrative that feels all too familiar, much like the issues surrounding [KDF's Laikipia 'Security Efforts': More Talk, Less Action](/posts/kdf-s-laikipia--security-efforts---more-talk--less-action).
