So, DEFABA, the military's boxing club, apparently "asserted supremacy" in some Mombasa competition. Great. While they're busy flexing their muscles and accumulating points, the rest of us are trying to figure out how to pay for basic necessities. They’re all about discipline, resilience, and excellence, mirroring the "warrior ethos." Sounds like a fancy way of saying they get paid to punch each other while we foot the bill.

This "national boxing league" saw 18 teams and 86 blokes and 16 ladies duking it out. DEFABA, with their 16-strong squad, managed to outscore Team Police. Big deal. They talk about technical mastery and determination, but what about the nation's actual problems? Is this the kind of investment the Kenya Defence Forces are making? Seems more like a way to keep the brass entertained and justify their budgets. It's a familiar tune, much like how [Sh500m Airbus H130 Chopper For Kenyan Company: Another Toy For The Elite?](/posts/sh500m-airbus-h130-chopper-for-kenyan-company--another-toy-for-the-elite) highlighted the disconnect between military spending and public needs.

The "standout performances" included a super heavyweight stunning the national champion and a Lance Corporal winning a "fiercely contested bout." Wow, riveting stuff. All this, while the country grapples with its own battles. They're already looking ahead to the next leg, sharpening their "competitive edge." Meanwhile, the average Kenyan is just trying to survive another day.

This whole DEFABA victory is just another distraction, another bit of feel-good news for a select few. While they parade their trophies and bask in the glory of their "dominance," remember who's ultimately funding this whole charade. It's us. And for what? So a few soldiers can get some shiny medals and the higher-ups can have more stories to tell at their fancy dinners.
