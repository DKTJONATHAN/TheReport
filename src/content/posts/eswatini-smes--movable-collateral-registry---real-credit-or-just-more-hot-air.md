Eswatini SMEs: Collateral Conundrum or Another Bureaucratic Boondoggle?

The promise of easier credit for Eswatini's small businesses sounds good on paper, but knowing how things work here, expect more red tape than real money.

The idea of using your jalopy or a few cows to get a loan might seem like a breakthrough, but history shows us these grand plans often end up benefiting the suits, not the struggling entrepreneurs.

This whole "movable collateral registry" sounds like a lot of hot air, another way for banks to look like they're doing something while still keeping the cash locked down. They talk about global best practices, but here in Eswatini, we're still trying to figure out basic infrastructure. Remember how Kenya's AGOA negotiations were marred by governance issues? That's the kind of mess you can expect when good intentions meet shaky execution.

They claim this will unlock billions and empower women and youth. Sure, and next they'll be promising free chai and mandazi for everyone. The reality is, the system will likely be so complicated, full of loopholes and corruptible, that only those already connected will benefit. The rest will be left chasing shadows, trying to understand depreciation rates for their goats while bankers count their bonuses.

And don't even get me started on "implementation fidelity." That's just fancy talk for "we'll see if it works, and if it doesn't, tough luck." Expect the digital infrastructure to be clunky, the legal clarity to be a maze, and the capacity building to consist of a one-day seminar that everyone sleeps through. It's just another layer of bureaucracy waiting to happen, designed to look good in reports but do very little for the average person trying to make a living.

Ultimately, this registry is just another attempt to dress up the same old problems. They’re talking about innovation and unlocking potential, but what they’re really doing is creating another system that will be exploited by the few and frustrate the many. We’ve seen this movie before, and it rarely has a happy ending for the ordinary emaSwati.
