POLICE FREE ENERGY OFFICIALS FIRED OVER FUEL IMPORTS AS ODPP SILENT ON CHARGES

The usual song and dance: powerful people get a slap on the wrist while the wheels of justice grind slower than a matatu on a Monday morning.

Shady fuel deals and inflated prices? Sounds like Nairobi.

Crime

Fuel Scandal, Corruption, Nairobi Realism

Fuel

The ink's barely dry on their resignations, and already Mohamed Liban, Joe Sang, Daniel Kiptoo, Joseph Wafula, and Joel Mburu are out on a measly Sh100,000 bail each. Apparently, the Office of the Director of Public Prosecutions (ODPP) is still figuring out what to charge them with, or perhaps they're just waiting for the right time to conveniently forget about it all. These are the same folks who allegedly cooked the books on fuel stock data, making us believe there was a crisis so they could justify buying substandard fuel at inflated prices. Classic Nairobi playbook, really.

We're talking about a Sh58,744 per metric ton difference, a cool Sh43.4 extra per litre for fuel that might not even be up to scratch. All while President Ruto is out there talking tough about cracking down on "energy sector cartels." It's enough to make you choke on your chai. This whole charade smells suspiciously like the kind of thing we’ve seen before, like when [Kenya's Top Energy Officials Resign Amidst Fuel Scandal Fabrications](/posts/kenya-s-top-energy-officials-resign-amidst-fuel-scandal-fabrications).

The Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) was already flashing charges like abuse of office and conspiracy to commit economic crimes, promising jail time and hefty fines. But here we are, with the accused out on bail and the ODPP playing mute. It's the same old story: the powerful get a cozy little time-out while the rest of us just have to deal with the consequences, like higher fuel prices at the pump.

Even the usual cheerleaders, like Senator Oburu Oginga, are singing praises about professional inquiries, while lawyer Ahmednassir Abdullahi is already sniffing out why only public servants are under the microscope and not the private players involved. It's a circus, and we're just the audience paying for the tickets. Let's see how long this "investigation" actually lasts before everyone conveniently forgets.
