Murang’a Gets Starlink Upgrade: More Hot Air for 170 Health Facilities











So, Murang'a County has apparently decided to throw money at the wall with this whole "digitisation of healthcare" thing. Governor Kang'ata is boasting about upgrading all 170 public health facilities to Starlink internet. They're claiming it's going to be "faster and more cost-effective." Right. Because cable was the real problem, not the chronic underfunding and general chaos that plagues our public services.

Paratus Group, the company providing this fancy new connection, is all smiles, talking about "embracing digital transformation" and reaching "remote areas." Conveniently, they're also getting paid handsomely for it, I'm sure. Meanwhile, the Medical Superintendent at Kirwara Level Four Hospital is already singing praises about "real-time access to data." Let's hope this magical internet doesn't decide to go down when a real emergency hits, unlike some other infrastructure promises we've heard about. Remember when beds were promised but never materialized? [The Mutuini Photo-Op: 400 Beds and a Million Broken Promises](/posts/the-mutuini-photo-op--400-beds-and-a-million-broken-promises).

But wait, there's more political posturing. Kang'ata is also calling for investigations into some grim discoveries and has the audacity to lecture the government on foreign health risks. It's always easier to point fingers elsewhere when your own house is, shall we say, a work in progress. All this while other political factions are busy with rallies and trying to influence parliamentary votes. Just another day in Kenyan politics, where shiny new tech is the latest distraction from the real issues.

The real question is, who's footing the bill for all this Starlink bandwidth? And will it actually translate into better treatment for the average wananchi, or is it just another vanity project for the governor? We've seen these grand digital promises before, and the reality often falls far short of the hype. Let's just hope this upgrade doesn't become another expensive white elephant, gathering dust along with the outdated equipment it's supposed to connect.
