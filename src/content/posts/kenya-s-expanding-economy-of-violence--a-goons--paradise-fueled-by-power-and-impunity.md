So, Nation.Africa’s trying to tell me my subscription’s up? As if I need a reminder that everything, even the "great content," eventually runs out. Just like your patience when these thugs are paraded on the news, only to see more of them on Moi Avenue the next day. They caught three goons for attacking Senator Osotsi, big deal. It’s like swatting flies while the real mosquitoes, the financiers and handlers, are buzzing around in air-conditioned boardrooms, protected by power and, of course, that Kenyan "entrenched impunity."

This isn’t some spontaneous eruption of anger, people. This is an economy, a nasty, profitable one. Behind every thrown stone, every invaded church, every raided property, there’s a chain of command. They’ve got their funders, their strategists, and their foot soldiers. And guess what? They’re usually untouchable, hidden behind the very system that’s supposed to protect us. It’s a predictable cycle, much like how fuel costs always seem to go up for those May-August consignments, leaving everyone else to sweat it out.

Remember Muendo getting caught in that bar brawl? That’s the street-level stuff. While some poor chap is dodging punches from bouncers, the real architects are counting their profits. They’re probably out there making money off misery, much like we’ve seen in other parts of the world where fear is a commodity. It’s a market of misery, and Kenya seems to be expanding its share, just like [Culiacán's 'Fear is Everywhere' Amidst Escalating Cartel War](/posts/culiac-n-s--fear-is-everywhere--amidst-escalating-cartel-war--bbc-reports-on-a-city-in-turmoil).

The headline says it all: "Goons’ paradise: Inside Kenya’s expanding economy of violence." It’s not a question, it’s a statement of fact. The police might parade a few low-level enforcers, but the real power brokers, the ones orchestrating this chaos, will continue to operate in the shadows. They thrive on the instability they create, while we’re left navigating the fallout, wondering when our own peace will be the next item on the chopping block.

And this whole ad-blocker thing? Just another way to squeeze more out of you. If you’ve got the cash for subscriptions, you’ve probably got it for ads too, right? It’s all part of the grand design to keep the money flowing, even if it means plastering you with more distractions while the real problems fester.
