So, Martha Karua is making noise again, demanding the Energy CS Opiyo Wandayi throw in the towel. Apparently, there's a "fuel scandal" unfolding and Kenyans are queuing up from Nyest to Kericho for a drop. Karua's got a point, though; empty government assurances are about as useful as a screen door on a submarine. We’re already hearing about price hikes, which means the already overtaxed Mwananchi will just dig deeper into the hole.

She’s right about the corruption and mismanagement worsening things, even with global tensions. Who in their right mind sells off strategic fuel reserves when the world's a mess? It’s like a farmer selling his last sack of maize before the drought hits. And if the CS truly doesn’t know what’s happening under his nose, then he’s as clueless as a politician promising change. This isn't some small-time hustle; this is the whole damn system.

Karua's also asking where the Sh500 million cash recovery is. Good question, because in this city, money tends to disappear faster than a free meal at a political rally. The lack of accountability is staggering. When the big shots get away with it, corruption becomes the unofficial national sport. Just look at the passport fiasco with that warlord - classic. It’s the same old story, just a different cast of characters looting the till.

This fuel crisis is just another nail in the coffin for our wallets. Transport, food, electricity - everything’s going to go up. The "mwananchi" is being squeezed dry, like a used-up matatu conductor at the end of a long day. It’s no surprise that Karua is positioning herself for 2027. After all, someone has to pretend to care when the government is busy lining its pockets. We've seen fertilizer meant for farmers end up in the hands of profiteers before, so why would fuel be any different? [Keringet Cops Nab 140 Bags of Fertiliser That Was Meant for Farmers, Now Just for Profiteers](/posts/keringet-cops-nab-140-bags-of-fertiliser-that-was-meant-for-farmers--now-just-for-profiteers)

And the cherry on top? CS Wandayi jetted off to Dubai while all this was going down. Official duty, of course. Meanwhile, parliamentary committees are sitting there, frustrated because the "key players" decided a trip to the desert was more important than explaining why Kenyans can’t get fuel. It's a circus, and we're the clowns paying for the tickets.

Karua’s advice to Kenyans? Demand accountability and use your vote to ditch the greedy ones. Easy to say, harder to do when you’re just trying to survive the day. But hey, at least she’s pointing out the obvious: this administration has no real interest in tackling corruption when it benefits them.
