So, the grand plan for 2027 is being sketched out, not in smoke-filled backrooms, but in the dusty ledgers of voter arithmetic. Apparently, the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) has suddenly discovered that some counties are growing, and this apparently changes everything. As if we don't know that politics is a numbers game, and politicians will chase votes like hyenas after a carcass.

And what do you know, the IEBC is asking voters who registered before 2012 to show up and get their biometrics captured again. For those keeping score, that's folks who were probably alive and kicking before some of these politicians were even born. Meanwhile, online, people are panicking because their polling station details have vanished. Typical. It's a constant cycle of confusion, designed to keep us all guessing.

This whole voter registration charade is just another layer in the usual chaos. Remember how we've seen the grim reaper visiting Parliament, snatching leaders for all sorts of reasons, from plane crashes to the usual ailments? It seems life, and death, are just collateral damage in this endless game of power. It’s enough to make you wonder if we should just be documenting all the misfortunes.

Effective policing of elections, they say, begins years in advance. Threat assessments, deployment planning, resource mobilisation - sounds impressive on paper. But in reality, it’s just more jargon to mask the same old song and dance. They're probably already calculating how to manipulate the numbers, just like they always do. It's a predictable script, played out with different actors every election cycle.

This constant shuffling of voter details, the calls for re-registration, the public relations spin - it all reeks of a system desperately trying to appear in control while the wheels are threatening to come off. It's a familiar tune, and one that leaves a bitter taste in the mouth. Just another reminder that in Kenya, elections are less about democracy and more about who can master the art of the numbers game, no matter how messy it gets. It's a cycle that's as reliable as death itself.
