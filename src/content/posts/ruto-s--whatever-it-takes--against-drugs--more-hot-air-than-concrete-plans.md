So, President Ruto is going to do "whatever it takes" to save our youth from the "scourge of substance abuse." Bless his heart. He said this in Tharaka Nithi, at a church service, which is about as predictable as the Kenyan rain. He’s got a "clear plan," he says. We've heard that before, haven't we? Seems like every leader in this country has a "clear plan" for everything, until it's time to actually *do* something.

He’s calling it a "national menace" and wants everyone to unite - families, churches, policymakers, government. Oh, the irony. This is the same man who’s busy forming a "broad-based government" by bringing in everyone, including those who didn't vote for him. So, is this about unity, or is it about consolidating power? It’s a bit rich to lecture us on unity when the political landscape is still a mess of backroom deals.

And then there’s the part about not blaming the kids, but supporting them. That’s a nice sentiment, truly. But let's be honest, how much real support are we talking about? Is it more empty promises, or actual rehabilitation centers with proper funding? We’ve seen it all before, from the annual baptism by sewage in Nairobi to other crises, where the big men just play rescue games. It’s hard to shake the feeling that this is just another talking point to look good.

He’s linking this fight against drugs to his grand vision of national unity. According to him, bringing all Kenyans together, even political rivals, is key to development and peace. It’s a noble idea, I suppose, but unity isn't built on platitudes. It's built on addressing real issues, not just talking about them. While the President is busy uniting the nation for development, other leaders like Justin Muturi are raising concerns about sovereignty, and we’re still dealing with the fallout of things like Gachagua’s allegations. It’s a lot of noise, and I'm still waiting for the actual signal.
