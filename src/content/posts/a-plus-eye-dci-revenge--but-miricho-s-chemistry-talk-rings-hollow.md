A Plus Eye DCI Revenge, But Chemistry Isn't Magic Wand

So, A Plus, the new kids on the Kenya Cup Volleyball block, had a "challenging debut." Shocking, I know. Now their coach, Miricho, is all smiles, gushing about "tangible signs of growth" and how they're "better prepared." This is the same team that got schooled by DCI in their opener, remember? Apparently, those early beatings were just "exposing gaps in cohesion" and "contributing to a clear upward trajectory." Sounds like fancy words for getting their heads kicked in, but hey, whatever floats your boat.

Miricho’s big plan was to "take part to get the experience." And what experience did they get? Limited playing time, hesitation, and probably a healthy dose of embarrassment. They formed last year, which is basically yesterday in sports terms. Now they're claiming they’re "settling into its rhythm" with "improved understanding and chemistry." It’s a nice story, but let’s be real, this isn't some miracle cure. Chemistry doesn't just appear out of thin air, especially when you’re still figuring out who’s who on the court. It’s like trying to build a skyscraper with a few bricks and a lot of hope.

Sure, they've got a few players Miricho's hyping up - Moraa and Shivikhwa are apparently "key influencers" for the middle blockers, and Jelimo is adding "balance." And then there are the "senior players" like Snawa guiding the "younger members." It’s all very heartwarming, like a school play. But let's not get ahead of ourselves. These are debutants, not seasoned pros. The technical bench is "reinforcing structure and communication," which is great, but you can’t just wave a magic wand and expect them to suddenly play like the national team. It’s a long, hard road, and frankly, I've seen too many "promising" teams fizzle out to get too excited. It reminds me of how some companies, like [CZI Cuts 70 Jobs to Focus on AI-Driven Medical Research](/posts/czi-cuts-70-jobs-to-focus-on-ai-driven-medical-research), also talk a big game about innovation while making drastic cuts.

Miricho is "tempering expectations," which is probably wise, but then he goes and says, "If teamwork clicks early, this team can be something special." Oh, really? And what if it doesn't? He's also talking about approaching "each fixture as a final." That’s the kind of talk that gets you burned out before the halfway mark. He expects a "tougher rematch against DCI," which is funny because DCI probably hasn't changed much, while A Plus is supposedly a whole new team. The real kicker? Miricho admits that "sustained success... will also depend on improved funding." Bingo. It always comes down to the money, doesn't it? Without proper sponsorship, all this talk of "consistent competition" is just hot air.
