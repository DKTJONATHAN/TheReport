So, Joe Sang, the big boss at Kenya Pipeline, has been arrested. Apparently, he was busy procuring "substandard fuel." Colour me shocked. It's not like this is the first time we've seen the rot in our state corporations. Pius Mwendwa, the General Manager Finance, is now the acting MD. Let's hope he’s not too busy counting his own coins to notice what's going on.

This whole "adulterated fuel" business smells like a classic Nairobi hustle. More officials are being "sought" as part of the probe. Translation: they're trying to find some lower-level scapegoats to blame while the real beneficiaries continue their merry way. It's the same old playbook, just with a different fuel pump.

Meanwhile, the electoral agency wants voters who registered before 2012 to re-register. Because what we really need right now is more bureaucratic hurdles for ordinary citizens, while the powerful siphon off national resources. Perhaps they should start with auditing the voter rolls for ghosts, instead of inconveniencing actual people.

This whole mess reminds me of how our sovereignty seems to be up for grabs, with passports becoming a "side hustle" for questionable characters.
