GENOA BOSS DE ROSSI SEETHES AT GOSSIP AFTER ROMA WIN, CLAIMS HE’S NOT A SORCERER









So, Daniele De Rossi, the Genoa boss, finally decided to spill some Nairobi-style tea after that win against Roma. Apparently, people have been having a field day questioning his decisions, especially when it comes to picking a lineup against his old stomping ground. He’s out here sounding like he’s being accused of witchcraft for not picking his favourite nephew, when in reality, he’s just trying to win a football match. It’s the usual Nairobi drama - everyone’s an expert, and they’ll drag your name through the mud faster than you can say "kikuyu grass."

De Rossi’s practically frothing at the mouth, calling the speculation "appalling" and "mind-boggling." He’s got 20 years in this game, and suddenly he’s incompetent because he picked a different XI? Please. It’s all about people wanting to stir up trouble, to gossip, to see someone fail. This is not some elaborate swap deal with Roma, it’s just football, mate. He makes choices based on who he thinks has the best chance of getting him three points, simple as that. Honestly, the sheer pettiness is exhausting, but then again, what else is new? It’s like trying to navigate the political landscape; everyone has an agenda.

He’s also defending his player selection process, saying it's not just him deciding on a whim. Sometimes it’s continuity, sometimes it’s a shake-up, and other times it’s because the team’s attitude or fitness has gone down the drain faster than a cheap beer at a *mitumba* market. He even quoted Antonio Conte, telling him to make mistakes with his own head and his own ideas. Smart man, Conte. At least De Rossi isn't afraid to own his decisions, even if the keyboard warriors are out in full force. It's a tough business, and frankly, it reminds me of how some people operate when discussing issues like [the surveillance state does not negotiate](/posts/the-surveillance-state-does-not-negotiate) - quick to judge, slow to understand.

Then there’s the injury update, or lack thereof. Baldanzi is being assessed day-to-day, which is as clear as mud, just like most pronouncements from official sources. He’s also talking about a player being fit for selection but needing workload management. This is the kind of diplomatic language that means absolutely nothing in practice. We’ll see if he plays, we won’t see if he plays. It’s all part of the theatre.

Sabelli is apparently an "important player" who was "underestimated." Fancy that. A player who wasn't playing much suddenly feels important. This is how it always goes in football, and in life. People only get recognition when they're suddenly needed, or when the spotlight hits them. And Leali, the goalkeeper who's been sidelined, is still being professional. Good for him. It’s a constant balancing act, isn’t it? Keeping everyone happy, or at least functional, while trying to win. It’s a mad world, this football.
