If you thought the drama in the Spanish league was limited to what happens on the grass, you clearly haven't been paying attention to the VIP lounges. Lamine Yamal, the teenage wonderkid who isn't even old enough to buy a beer in most countries, is already finding out that fame comes with a side of "influencer" thirst. Word on the digital street is that British model Lily Rowland has been spotted hanging around Barcelona's home stadium like a permanent fixture. Fans are busy analyzing every "like" and "follow" on social media, because apparently, scouting for a girlfriend is now more important than scouting for a left-back. It is the same old story - a rising star, a pretty face with a ring light, and a fan base ready to lose their minds over a couple of emojis.

While Yamal is busy in the DMs, Joan Laporta is busy in the boardroom nursing a very public grudge. The Spanish Referees' Committee finally grew a conscience and admitted that Girona’s winning goal against Barca should have been tossed out for a foul on Jules Kounde. But here is the kicker - they are sorry, but the points are still gone. Laporta is fuming because "oops, our bad" does not fix the league standings. This kind of bureaucratic nonsense is exactly what we see in the [DECAYING DIPLOMACY AND THE MYTH OF TECHNOCRATIC INDEPENDENCE](/posts/decaying-diplomacy-and-the-myth-of-technocratic-independence) where people in suits pretend their systems are perfect until the damage is irreversible. It is a classic case of too little, too late, wrapped in a shiny package of official transparency.

Then we have the Kylian Mbappe circus over at Real Madrid. You would think a man with his bank account would be immune to a bit of criticism, but the French legend Christophe Dugarry is out here acting like Mbappe's personal bodyguard. Dugarry claims the finger-pointing at the superstar is "absurd" and just a distraction from the club's deeper issues. Please. When you move to the Santiago Bernabeu for that kind of money, you are not just a player - you are a target. If he cannot handle the heat, he should have stayed in Paris where the competition is as soft as a croissant. Instead, he is under a microscope, and every missed chance is being treated like a national tragedy.

And let us not forget the messy transfer games being played behind the scenes. Florian Wirtz’s agent has basically admitted he was trying to pimp his client out to Real Madrid before the kid settled for Liverpool for a cool 116 million pounds. Imagine your agent calling up Xabi Alonso and begging him to take you along to Madrid like a lost puppy, only to end up at Anfield. The agent is still holding out hope for a future move to Los Blancos, because clearly, 156 million dollars and a Liverpool jersey just is not enough prestige for some people. It is all about the "marquee" names and the strategic priorities, even if Barcelona is still searching for a number nine while their bank account looks like a desert. The hypocrisy of these clubs claiming they are "broke" while chasing elite talent is the real comedy show here.
