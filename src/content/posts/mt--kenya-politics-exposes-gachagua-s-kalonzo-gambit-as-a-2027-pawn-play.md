Why Mt. Kenya Politics Makes Gachagua's Kalonzo Endorsement a Big Joke

Rigathi Gachagua's sudden love for Kalonzo Musyoka is as genuine as a politician's promise on election eve. The former DP is out here singing Kalonzo's praises, talking about debts owed and support for 2027. But let's be real, this is just Gachagua playing the same old Kenyan political game: use the old hand, then toss him aside when he's no longer useful. Remember how he called Kalonzo a "failed project" when he was still DP? Now that he's out of power, suddenly Kalonzo's a "statesman." The only thing that changed is Gachagua's address.

Don't let anyone fool you with this "Kalonzo Tosha" talk. Gachagua can't deliver Mt. Kenya votes to Kalonzo. The numbers don't lie, and the IEBC tallies scream it. Mt. Kenya backed Uhuru and Ruto by a landslide, leaving Raila-Kalonzo in the dust, barely hitting double digits. In 2017, it was even worse, with Uhuru/Ruto sweeping over 90 percent in Nyeri while the Raila-Kalonzo ticket was, frankly, irrelevant. The same Mt. Kenya voters who rejected Kalonzo twice as Raila's running mate aren't about to suddenly embrace him for president.

Gachagua knows he can't make Kalonzo president. If Kalonzo couldn't even crack 15 percent in Mt. Kenya as Raila's partner, Gachagua isn't going to magically conjure up 50 percent plus one for him now. This isn't about genuine support; it's about Gachagua positioning himself for his own future political ambitions. Kalonzo is just a stepping stone, a political bridge for Gachagua's eventual run for State House. If Kalonzo actually won, Gachagua's own plans would be dead in the water.

This whole alliance is being sold as another "Kibaki Tosha" moment, but it’s a sham. Raila Odinga made real sacrifices in 2002, shelving his own ambitions and throwing his support behind Kibaki. Gachagua isn't doing that. He's not subordinating his own political movement or committing to campaign relentlessly in Mt. Kenya for Kalonzo. He's not even publicly promising to back Kalonzo if the presidential bid fails. This is all cheap rhetoric, unlike the real political sacrifice Raila made. It's a classic example of how Kenyan coalition politics rewards utility, not loyalty.

The real lesson here for Ukambani is to stop waiting for handouts and start negotiating strategically. Don't let your votes be donated emotionally; demand that they be valued. Kalonzo can be president, but not because another politician suddenly feels a moral obligation. It will happen when Kamba politics stops acting like a pawn and starts demanding its price. Until then, Gachagua's promises are just hot air, and Mt. Kenya politics will continue to expose the hollowness of these supposed alliances, much like the pressures on UEFA’s anti-racism framework highlighted in [Lisbon Stoppage Highlights Systemic Pressures on UEFA’s Anti-Racism Framework](/posts/lisbon-stoppage-highlights-systemic-pressures-on-uefa-s-anti-racism-framework).

This isn't about supporting Kalonzo; it's about Gachagua securing his own future. He's learned from the masters of Kenyan betrayal, and now he's teaching the same lesson. For Kamba voters, believing this is anything other than a strategic move by Gachagua is just plain naive. They need to see beyond the promises and recognize the cold, hard arithmetic of Kenyan politics.
