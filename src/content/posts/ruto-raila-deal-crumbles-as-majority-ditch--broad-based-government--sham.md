The latest TIFA poll is just confirming what we all knew: this "broad-based government" charade is on life support. Support has plummeted, and opposition is now the majority opinion. Thirty percent clinging to hope while fifty-six percent have seen through the nonsense. It’s a classic Nairobi story - grand promises followed by a swift kick in the teeth. Remember when support was at 44 percent last November? Seems like a distant dream now, doesn't it?

It’s no surprise, really. The divisions within ODM are clearer than a tout’s intentions when he spots a lone traveller. This Linda Mwananchi movement, with Senator Sifuna leading the charge, is tapping into the raw frustration of the mwananchi. High cost of living? Taxation? Governance? These aren't abstract concepts; they're the everyday grind that has people’s pockets empty and their tempers short. They’re getting large crowds because they’re speaking the truth, unlike the suits in their air-conditioned offices.

And the demands! Oburu Oginga thinks ODM deserves a presidential running mate in 2027. Seriously? This isn't about sharing power; it's about securing seats. Others want the "ten-point agenda" implemented. As if that laundry list was ever going to be anything more than a political handshake prop. The whole thing is devolving into public spats and finger-pointing, with everyone trying to guess Raila’s "real intentions." If only they'd spent less time dissecting intentions and more time fixing the country.

Meanwhile, Ruto’s busy touring ODM strongholds, trying to shore up this sinking ship. It's a desperate move, like trying to patch a leaky roof with tissue paper. And let’s not forget the ghost of Rigathi Gachagua. His constant attacks, even after being unceremoniously removed, are a constant reminder of the internal chaos. It all just adds to the uncertainty, making you wonder if any of these political maneuvers are about progress or just the next election cycle. It’s a messy, fluid situation, and the eventual status of this "BBG" is as clear as the Nairobi air after a dust storm. Even the media is tired of covering these predictable dramas, though they do manage to link us to stories about Kenyan media 'couples' when they're not dissecting political failures.
