Oh, honey, pull up a chair because the irony in this country is officially thicker than the smog on Mombasa Road. We need to talk about Dr. Oburu Ng-ong-a Oginga, the man the political elite unironically refer to as a "youth leader" while he moves at the pace of a slow-motion replay. It is giving "fellow kids" energy, and honestly, if this is what youth looks like in Kenya, I need to start investing in some heavy-duty anti-aging cream immediately. The slow manner of speaking might be his trademark, but the speed at which the hypocrisy is being served is enough to give anyone whiplash.

While Oburu is busy being the world-s oldest teenager, the house of Orange is practically on fire. We are seeing deep cracks thanks to the emergence of Linda Mwananchi, which sounds more like a failed NGO than a political movement. Edwin Sifuna is out here being accused of public contradictions, and the party is clutching its pearls like a group of aunts at a scandalous wedding. It is the kind of drama that makes you realize these [roadside tyrants](/posts/roadside-tyrants--why-the-kenyan-prado-is-a-rolling-symptom-of-our-national-decay) are more interested in their internal soap operas than actually doing anything for the people they claim to represent.

Let us talk about that "unmissable sense of humour" that the insiders keep raving about. Is it a joke, or is it just a confession? When Dr. Oginga says the party-s priority is to "secure a fair share of national opportunities" for its members, we all know what that really means in the 254. It is the same old story of "it is our turn to eat," just dressed up in a fancy suit and a PhD title. They aren't looking for jobs for the actual youth who are struggling on the streets; they are looking for seats at the high table where the buffet is never-ending and the taxpayer picks up the tab.

And then we have Dr. Fred Matiang-i chiming in with his "I guarded the exam" rhetoric. Sir, guarding an exam and guarding a vote are two very different things, especially when the person you are guarding them for seems to be playing a completely different game. It is all part of this elaborate performance where everyone pretends to be a hero while the country watches from the sidelines with empty pockets and exhausted patience. The United Opposition is the new buzzword, but it sounds like just another rebranding of the same old mess that we have been fed for decades.

At the end of the day, whether it is Sifuna leading a breakaway or Oburu pretending to represent the next generation, it is all just theater for the masses. We are being sold a dream by people who have been in power since the invention of the wheel. If you are waiting for these elites to actually change the status quo, you might as well wait for a Prado driver to use an indicator in Nairobi traffic. It is messy, it is loud, and it is exactly the kind of circus we have come to expect from the people who run this town.
