So, Spotify is five years old in Kenya and they want us to celebrate the fact that we have collectively lost our musical identity to the South African log-drum. A 1,404% surge in Amapiano? That is not a cultural exchange, it is a full-blown takeover. We spend all day pretending to support local talent while our playlists look like a Pretoria nightclub. Even with artists like Bien and Njerae trying to fight for space, the data shows we are basically just South Africa's most dedicated fan club. It is the same kind of performative nonsense we see when people claim [The 24-Hour Economy is a Mirage and Your Subscription is a Ransom Note](/posts/the-24-hour-economy-is-a-mirage-and-your-subscription-is-a-ransom-note) while they spend their nights scrolling through endless loops of the same three chords.

But if you are too 'global' for the common riff-raff on Tinder, don't worry. The 'elite' now have Kilele, a private dating app that promises discretion for people with more money than sense. It is funny how 'meaningful connections' now require background checks and income verification. They call it a vetted community, but we all know it is just a high-tech way for the wealthy to hide their messy business behind blurred photos and blocked screenshots. If you need a relationship concierge to find a date, maybe your personality is the problem, not your privacy settings.

The hypocrisy really peaks when you look at the 1,100% growth in Gospel and praise music. Only in Kenya do we stream 'Praise and Worship' on Sunday morning and then log into a 'discreet' dating app by Sunday afternoon. We are apparently very spiritual, yet very eager to join 'Select' circles that require income proof just to say hello. It seems the higher the log-drum beats, the more we need a little Jesus to balance out the lifestyle choices we are making in those 'Select' member chats.

Spotify thinks these 9 million user-generated playlists mean we are 'active cultural participants' or 'tastemakers.' In reality, it just means everyone with a smartphone thinks they are a curator. We are 'exporting our sound' now, apparently. Let's be real: until our artists are making 'Select' circle money without needing a South African feature to get noticed, we are just another market being mined for data. But hey, keep making those playlists and pretending your 'Gold' membership makes you special. We are all just data points in a streaming giant's anniversary report anyway.
