Gor Mahia restored their six-point lead at the top of the FKF Premier League because apparently, nobody else in this country knows how to hold a lead. K’Ogalo dismantled KCB 3-0 at Nyayo Stadium, but the real story was the twenty-minute delay due to a heavy downpour. In Nairobi, if it rains for ten minutes, our world-class facilities turn into cattle dips. Enock Morrison, Shariff Musa, and Samuel Kapen finished the job in the first half, leaving the Bankers looking like they had been liquidated.

While Gor fans celebrate their 21-time record, the rest of the league is a shambles of poor planning and broken promises. Over at the Police Sacco Stadium in South C, the defending champions played out a barren draw against APS Bomet in what can only be described as a swamp. This facility is supposedly a designated training ground for the 2027 Africa Cup of Nations, yet the drainage is non-existent. It is another classic case of [Nairobi’s Favorite Game: Hunt the Whistleblower While the Thief Escapes](/posts/nairobi-s-favorite-game--hunt-the-whistleblower-while-the-thief-escapes) where everyone blames the clouds instead of the people who signed off on the construction.

AFC Leopards tried to make things interesting by beating Posta Rangers 2-1 on Saturday, temporarily closing the gap to three points. But let us be real for a moment. Ingwe is just playing with the emotions of their long-suffering fans. Being second to Gor Mahia is the natural order of things in this city, and hoping for a different result is like hoping a Matatu will give you back your change without a fight. They have 40 points from 20 matches, but the shadow of K’Ogalo is long and very cold.

Down at the bottom of the table, the misery is actually impressive. Ulinzi Stars, the so-called soldiers, are currently rooted in the relegation zone after losing 2-1 to Mara Sugar. Imagine being a military side and losing to a team named after a sweetener. They are sitting 16th with a pathetic 18 points, just a few steps away from joining Sofapaka at the very bottom. Mara Sugar finally ended a seven-match winless run, but in this league, winning one game is just a temporary distraction from the inevitable slide back into mediocrity.

As for KCB, their title hopes are officially in the ICU. Losing eight matches in a single season is not the resume of a champion; it is the resume of a team that has given up. They sit eighth with 29 points, watching the trophy move further away with every muddy tackle. We are told to be excited about the growth of the local game, but when you look at the waterlogged pitches and the same teams winning every year, it is hard to feel anything but tired. The only thing consistent about Kenyan football is the disappointment.
