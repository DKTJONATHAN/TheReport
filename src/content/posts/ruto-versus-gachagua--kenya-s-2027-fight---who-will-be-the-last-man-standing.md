So, the dynamic duo of Ruto and Gachagua, who once pulled off the impossible by snatching victory from Raila's hands back in 2022, are now at each other's throats. Feels like just yesterday they were inseparable, building that "formidable campaign machine." Now? It's all about who gets the biggest slice of the pie, and who can stab the other in the back most effectively. Classic Nairobi politics, really.

And of course, to even get a whiff of this "news" about their little spat, we're expected to sell our souls. Accept cookies, they say. Give us your IP address, your browsing habits, your deepest fears - all so they can "personalize your experience" and serve you ads. For free news, mind you. Or, you know, pay a euro to subscribe and supposedly dodge the advertising cookies. Like that’s going to make a difference. It's all a scam to get your data, one way or another.

They claim their journalists deliver "trusted, independent news." Independent? From what, common sense? They're so busy chasing their own tails, these politicians, and the media is just there to amplify the noise. Remember that whole "Zakayo's First-World Fever Dream and the Sh550 Million Alliance Handout" thing? That was just a preview of the kind of circus we're in. All about deals and power plays, with us ordinary folks left to pick up the tab or offer up our personal information as payment.

This "battle for Kenya in 2027" sounds less like a vision for the country and more like a reality TV show. Who will laugh last? Probably the ones who sold our data to advertisers, while these two egos continue their petty squabble for the top seat. You think either of them actually cares about the average Kenyan struggling to make ends meet? Please. They're too busy plotting their next move, their next betrayal.

It’s a shame, really. All this energy spent on infighting and grandstanding. Meanwhile, real issues, like that time a 17-year-old girl was reportedly "rescued" after a ransom demand in Malindi, probably get buried under the avalanche of political mudslinging. But hey, at least we'll get targeted ads for whatever nonsense these politicians are peddling.

So, as Ruto and Gachagua circle each other like hyenas over a carcass, remember this: you're either paying with your money or your privacy. And in Nairobi, the currency of betrayal and self-interest is always accepted. Don't expect any miracles from this fight. Just more of the same.
