Tuju High Court: Police Out, Arrest Blocked - Another Drama Unfolds

Former CS Raphael Tuju is crying foul, claiming police are out to get him and are treating him like a common criminal. He's running to the High Court to stop his arrest and have the goons guarding his hospital bed removed. Typical politician, always trying to pull a fast one.

The man's already holed up in Karen Hospital, under police watch no less, after apparently going to the police station voluntarily. Now he's whining about being manhandled and his pre-existing injuries getting worse. Convenient, that. He even claims to have been followed by "suspicious unmarked vehicles" - sounds like someone's been watching too many cop shows.

Now he wants anticipatory bail and for the authorities to stop breathing down his neck. His lawyers are babbling about constitutional rights and unlawful custodial control, as if the rules don't apply to these guys when they're in trouble. And the cherry on top? He skipped court today, with his lawyers mumbling about him being sick. Sounds familiar, doesn't it? It’s like a scene from [Happy Birthday To The King of Denials: Andrew Finally Gets His Cuffs](/posts/happy-birthday-to-the-king-of-denials--andrew-finally-gets-his-cuffs).

His lawyers are practically weeping about him fearing for his life, but these are the same people who probably caused a lot of headaches for others when they were in power. The claim is that his "uninformed actions" are unjustified and malicious. Well, surprise, surprise. If he gets his way, he'll appear when summoned, of course. We'll see about that.
