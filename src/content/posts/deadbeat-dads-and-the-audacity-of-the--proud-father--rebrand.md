Darling, the audacity of the Kenyan man needs to be studied as a terminal illness. Last month, we all watched that Mama Fua go viral after her son crushed the KPSEA exams. It was a beautiful moment until the "Ghost of Fathers Past" decided to manifest. This man, who hasn’t paid a single cent in fees or attended a single school meeting, suddenly grew a spine just in time for the photo op. He didn’t show up for the struggle, but he was front and center for the success, ready to harvest a crop he never planted. It is the ultimate heist, stealing the emotional labor of a woman who scrubbed floors just to keep her kid in a classroom.

Of course, the drama didn't stay in the slums. A certain public figure recently took to the socials to air out her own "sperm donor" problems, and the internet did exactly what it always does: it blamed the woman. Her kids are thriving, and suddenly the man who abandoned them to "find himself" or build a new life elsewhere is posting their pictures for likes. When she called out his performance-based parenting, the "Men’s Conference" delegates rushed to the comments to call her bitter. It is fascinating how a woman can carry the weight of two parents for a decade, yet the second she demands accountability, she is the villain of the story.

The hypocrisy is truly staggering. We have built a social circus where maternal abandonment is treated like a horror movie, but paternal abandonment is just a "complicated chapter." When a man walks out, we say he wasn't ready or he needed space. When a woman even breathes a word of frustration, she is unnatural and heartless. This whole mess reminds me of the [KPSEA Clout-Chasing: Why Your Social Media 'Leakers' Are More Fraudulent Than a Lip-Syncing Pop Star](/posts/kpsea-clout-chasing--why-your-social-media--leakers--are-more-fraudulent-than-a-lip-syncing-pop-star) situation; everyone wants to be associated with the win, but nobody wants to be honest about the fraud happening behind the scenes. These men aren't returning because they found their conscience; they are returning because the children became an asset they could brag about.

Let’s be real- biology is a biological fact, but fatherhood is a subscription service that these men forgot to pay for. You don't get to opt out of the sleepless nights, the hunger, and the grinding poverty, only to opt back in when the child is a success story. Society keeps telling these women to be "gracious" and let the men back in for the sake of the children. But what about the children’s sake during the years of absence? Every time we applaud a deadbeat for "stepping up" once the hard work is done, we are spitting in the face of the mothers who did the heavy lifting alone. 

The Mama Fua and our disgruntled celeb aren't bitter; they are simply refusing to participate in a lie. They know that a man who only remembers he is a father when there is a trophy to hold is just a fan with matching DNA. We need to stop treating fatherhood as an optional hobby and start holding these men to the same impossible standards we set for mothers. Until then, these "proud fathers" can keep their captions and their clout-chasing. If you weren't there for the brick-by-brick struggle, don't show up to cut the ribbon at the completion ceremony. It is tacky, it is transparent, and frankly, it is pathetic.
