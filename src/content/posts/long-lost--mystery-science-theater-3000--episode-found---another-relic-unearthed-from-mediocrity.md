It appears that the digital age, in its infinite capacity to unearth and display utter dross, has gifted us with another piece of forgotten television history. A *Mystery Science Theater 3000* episode, dating back to its humble beginnings on KTMA in 1988, has been discovered and dutifully uploaded to YouTube. This particular cinematic treasure, designated *K03* and also known as *Star Force: Alien Fugitive II*, is less an "episode" and more a Frankensteinian compilation of footage from the Japanese sci-fi series *Star Wolf*.

The discovery, attributed to a fan who stumbled upon a recording at a Minneapolis garage sale, is being hailed as a triumph for completionists. Apparently, the show's creators themselves were less than enthusiastic about their early KTMA output, and in some cases, lacked the legal standing to even access it. This finding, therefore, allows them to complete their collection of dubious early works, a feat that will undoubtedly thrill their fanbase. The sentiment echoes the recent resurfacing of "lost" *Doctor Who* episodes, suggesting a collective societal amnesia that eagerly embraces the reintroduction of once-discarded media.

But do not despair, for the relentless churn of entertainment ensures that the *MST3K* brand will continue to plague us. Four new episodes, funded through the marvel of crowdfunding and likely destined for similar critical indifference, are on their way courtesy of RiffTrax. It seems the demand for mocking subpar cinema, no matter how dated or poorly conceived, remains a surprisingly resilient market. This resurrection of *K03* is merely another data point in the ever-growing archive of content we are compelled to consume, whether it offers genuine value or simply fills the void.
