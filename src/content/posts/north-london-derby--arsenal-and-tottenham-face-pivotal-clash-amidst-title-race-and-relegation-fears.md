The appointment of Tudor marks a strategic shift for Tottenham, a club currently enduring a woeful eight-match winless streak. The new head coach faces an immediate crisis with twelve senior players unavailable due to injury or suspension. Key absences include James Maddison, Cristian Romero, and Richarlison, forcing a makeshift lineup that includes players such as Joao Palhinha and Randal Kolo Muani. Tudor has emphasized a shift toward defensive resilience and collective mentality as the primary tools for survival, stating that his first goal is for the squad to become a team that suffers and fights together.

Arsenal manager Mikel Arteta remains focused on the title race despite a recent dip in form. Following draws against Brentford and bottom-club Wolves, the Gunners' lead has been narrowed to just two points. Arteta has called for a strong reaction from his squad, highlighting the unpredictable nature of their recent setbacks where they conceded an equalizer despite a low probability of doing so. The inclusion of players like Viktor Gyokeres and Martin Zubimendi in the projected lineup suggests a commitment to maintaining the offensive pressure that has defined their season.
