Oh look, another day, another "shocking" revelation from our beloved national heroes. Just when you thought the headlines couldn't get more predictable, the Anti-Doping Agency of Kenya (ADAK) dropped a list of 27 names that reads more like a "Who is Who" of career-ending choices. At the top of this hall of shame is none other than Rita Jeptoo. At 45, she really should know better, but apparently, the lure of anabolic steroids is stronger than common sense. After already serving a four-year ban and trying to play the "reformed athlete" card, she is back in the doghouse. Honestly, it is giving "I did it again" energy, and not in a cute Britney Spears sort of way.

Then we have Wiseman Were, the Commonwealth bronze medalist who seems to think testing schedules are mere suggestions. Missing three tests since August 2025? That is not a scheduling conflict; that is a professional game of hide-and-seek where the stakes are your entire reputation. He is suspended until further notice, which in Kenyan sports-speak usually means "until we find a way to make everyone forget this happened." It is pathetic how these athletes think they can outrun the system while their PR teams scramble to polish a tarnished crown.

The rot isn't just on the track anymore. The list has expanded to include football and basketball players, proving that the culture of "shortcuts" is officially the new national pastime. We are looking at over 140 athletes suspended since 2017. Kenya is leading the world in something, but unfortunately, it is the leaderboard for being caught with your hand in the cookie jar. It feels like we are [opening the gates to god-knows-what](/posts/opening-the-gates-to-god-knows-what) when it comes to the integrity of our local sports scene. If this is the "gold standard" we are exporting, I would rather stick to watching scripted reality TV where at least the cheating is part of the plot.

Let us not forget the heavy hitters like Ruth Chepngetich and Benard Kibet Koech. Ruth, our world record holder, got caught with diuretics in her system back in July. Because nothing says "world-class performance" like a chemical boost to help you shed water weight or hide other substances. It is the same tired story: win the race, take the check, and then act surprised when the lab results come back glowing. The hypocrisy is loud, and the silence from their "loyal fans" is even louder.

The funniest part of this whole circus? ADAK just got off the World Anti-Doping Agency non-compliance watch list. They barely cleaned their own house before realizing it was still full of trash. While the agency tries to look professional by suspending everyone in sight, the image of the Kenyan athlete as a clean, hardworking underdog is officially dead. Grab your popcorn, because as these hearings drag on, the excuses are only going to get more creative. Stay messy, Kenya.
