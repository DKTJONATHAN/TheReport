Let us talk about the audacity of hope. You are sitting at a Java House in CBD, scrolling through your phone, and you see an advertisement for a construction job in Russia. They promise six-figure salaries and free housing. But in this city, we know that when a deal smells like fresh paint and cheap vodka, it is usually a trap. This is not just the usual [Cloud-Cuckoo-Land: The 'Raila Airways' Grift is High-Altitude Delusion](/posts/cloud-cuckoo-land--the--raila-airways--grift-is-high-altitude-delusion) that we are used to from our local politicians; this one comes with a draft notice and a one-way ticket to a frozen trench in Donbas.

Our young women are being targeted with even more precision. They tell 18-year-olds they are going to Russia for "civilian employment" under something called the Alabuga Start program. They think they are going to work in a nice office or a hotel. Instead, they are stuck in a special economic zone assembling UAVs. These are the drones that go boom. It is a long way from the beauty parlors of River Road, and there is no HR department to complain to when the supervisor hands you a wrench and tells you to build a flying bomb for a war you do not understand.

Even the church is in on the grift now. The Russian Orthodox Church has been setting up shop across Africa, building churches and paying local priests high salaries to preach about "traditional values." It is just a fancy way of building a loyal network for the Kremlin. They use these seminaries and cultural centers like "Russky Dom" to fish for recruits who are tired of being broke. It is a holy front for a very unholy business, proving once again that in the game of global power, even a priest's robe is just another piece of camouflage.

The Kenyan government is finally waking up from its long nap, accusing Moscow of mass recruitment after a high-profile scandal broke. We have had people promised jobs in hotels who ended up in the front lines. Nigeria is already counting the bodies from what the military calls "meat assaults." That is the actual term they use for sending foreigners into the most dangerous sectors just to see where the bullets are coming from. It turns out that "exorbitant salary" is very hard to spend when you are buried in the mud of a country you cannot even find on a map.

If you are still thinking about signing a contract with a shady employment agency, remember that Nairobi might be tough, but at least the sun is out and the bullets are not flying. Do not let a "Russky Dom" scholarship or a "security guard" job description fool you into becoming a statistic. The only thing Russia is giving out for free right now is a chance to be part of a tragedy. Stay home, keep your hustle local, and leave the "meat assaults" to the people who actually think they are fighting for something other than a paycheck that will never clear.
