Oh, honey, the "future" is looking a lot more like a voyeuristic nightmare than a sci-fi dream. We all saw the influencers preening in their Meta smart glasses, acting like they were living in 2045 while the rest of us were stuck in the present. But while these "content creators" were busy trying to look high-tech for the gram, they were actually livestreaming their most intimate moments to human contractors right here in Nairobi. According to a scathing report from Swedish publications, workers at the outsourcing firm Sama have been catching glimpses of everything from naked bodies to sensitive bank details. It turns out that being an early adopter of tech just means you are providing free entertainment for someone in an office in Westlands.

The hypocrisy is absolutely dripping, darling. Meta loves to talk about "privacy" and "encryption" in their glossy press releases, but the reality is much more low-rent. These contractors are reportedly reviewing footage of bathroom visits and "intimate moments" because the automatic face-blurring tech is about as reliable as a celebrity's apology tour. You think you are just asking "Hey Meta" for the weather, but you are actually opening a window into your living room for a stranger to peer through. It is giving very much "Big Brother," but without the prize money or the clout.

Speaking of clout, this entire mess reminds me that [clout chasing comes with a bill: cera imani and the odpc reality check](/posts/clout-chasing-comes-with-a-bill--cera-imani-and-the-odpc-reality-check). People are so desperate to be part of the next big trend that they forget to read the fine print. Meta’s terms of use are basically a "gotcha" moment waiting to happen, buried under layers of corporate jargon. They claim they only review data to "improve the experience," which is tech-speak for "we own your life once you put these frames on your face." If you are dumb enough to wear a camera on your face while checking your banking app, maybe you deserve the heart attack when your balance leaks.

The most hilarious part? These glasses do not even process data locally on your phone. Everything has to go through Meta’s infrastructure, meaning your "private" queries are taking a round trip to a server where a human might just decide to transcribe your deepest, darkest secrets. One worker literally told reporters, "We see everything." That includes you watching pornography or scrolling through your scandalous DMs. Imagine thinking you look like a tech mogul when you actually just look like a fool who is paying to be spied on.

Meta’s response was the typical corporate word salad, claiming they take steps to protect privacy while admitting that, yes, they use contractors to "improve" things. It is the same old story: we are the product, and our privacy is the price of admission. So, the next time you see a social media starlet rocking those Ray-Bans and acting like they are the main character, just remember there is a guy in an office block nearby who probably knows exactly what their bathroom tiles look like. Stay cynical, kids, because the only thing these smart glasses are actually "smart" at is exposing how little we value our own secrets.
