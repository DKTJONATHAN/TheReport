The refusal to send aging Mirage jets to Ukraine exposes the hollow core of European strategic autonomy. Despite the lofty rhetoric of a unified front, the Greek military establishment admits it cannot bridge the price gap for new replacements and, more tellingly, fears the Russian response. This is the structural paralysis of the modern European state: too poor to upgrade and too frightened to commit. Talk of collaboration on artificial intelligence in defense is merely a technocratic smoke-screen to distract from the fact that the continent has lost the ability to maintain basic industrial capacity.

The economic data presented at the Delphi Forum confirms the grim trajectory. With a growth rate of 13.5 percent compared to the American 87 percent since 2008, Europe has effectively resigned from the global future. The capital flight of 300 billion euros annually to the US is not a mistake; it is a rational exit strategy for investors who realize that European Banking Unions are just drawers full of yellowing paper. As noted in /posts/the-technocratic-hallucination-of-a-fading-europe, the obsession with regulation over innovation has turned the continent into a museum.

Industry leaders are finally saying the quiet part out loud. Europe lost the battery race to China because it preferred writing reports and setting climate targets to building real ecosystems. Now, with energy costs 2.5 times higher than its competitors, the European industrial base is committing suicide. The demand for the lowest price in infrastructure projects, as highlighted by Cenergy, is a race to the bottom that ensures the continent will wake up to find its factories have been dismantled and relocated to more hospitable jurisdictions.

Even the supposed triumphs of the Greek market reek of desperation. A 4 billion euro capital increase for the Public Power Corporation and a 2 billion euro investment plan for Mykonos and Santorini are not signs of health, but of a cannibal economy. Flooding already saturated islands with more concrete to appease private equity funds is the ultimate act of short-termism. It is the liquidation of natural capital to sustain a political class that measures success by how many hand-kisses a French President can deliver at a dinner party while the structural foundations of the state continue to erode.
