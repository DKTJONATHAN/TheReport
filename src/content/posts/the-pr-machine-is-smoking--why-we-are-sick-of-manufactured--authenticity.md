Listen, if I have to see one more "accidental" viral moment from a pop star who just happens to have a three-record deal in her back pocket, I might actually scream. The industry loves to talk about the pulse of the entertainment world, but let's be real - that pulse is mostly just the sound of a PR agent's heart racing as they try to scrub a leaked video or manufacture a "feud" for streams. We are living in an era where talent is optional, but a curated "struggle" is mandatory for the algorithm to even notice you.

It is the same old story every single week. These kids go from their bedrooms to the red carpet in six months, and we are supposed to believe it happened because they are just so relatable. Please. Half of these influencers are one bad decision away from a legal disaster and a public apology tour. Just look at the mess that was [from TikTok tiaras to handcuffs: the Nasir Egeh comedy show](/posts/from-tiktok-tiaras-to-handcuffs--the-nasir-egeh-comedy-show), proving that the jump from viral fame to a court date is shorter than a fifteen-second clip.

The hypocrisy is what really gets me. You have celebrities crying on Instagram Live about how "exhausted" they are while sitting in a ten-million-dollar mansion. They want the clout of being an underdog without actually having to deal with the reality of a nine-to-five. They claim to be the "voice of the people" while their stylists are paid more than your annual rent just to make them look like they haven't showered. It is a performance, and the audience is the only one not getting a paycheck for participating in the delusion.

Even the "seasoned veterans" are getting desperate. They see some teenager with a ring light getting all the attention, and suddenly they are trying to use Gen-Z slang in their captions like a cool aunt who has had one too many mimosas. It is embarrassing. There is no dignity in the chase for relevance anymore. They will sell you a detox tea that makes you bloat, a crypto-scam that makes you broke, or a "raw" documentary about their trauma that makes you bored - all while pretending it is a gift for the "fans."

We are told these are "fascinating narratives," but they are really just recycled scripts with a fresh coat of glitter. The industry doesn't want art; it wants an algorithm-friendly face that won't talk back to the board of directors. So, the next time you see a "new sensation" rising, ask yourself who is holding the ladder. Chances are, it is someone with a very expensive suit and a very cheap soul, waiting for you to click "like" so they can cash another check.
