So, there's this lawyer, Willis Otieno, who thinks Kenyans are daft. He’s taken to X, bless his heart, telling us all that the Kenyan state supposedly belongs to everyone equally. Newsflash, Mr. Otieno, we’ve been in this country long enough to know what that’s worth. He’s rambling about how leaders trying to exclude communities from government is "inaccurate and unhelpful." As if anyone in power has ever cared about accuracy or national cohesion when there are votes to harvest from tribal silos.

Otieno goes on to say that the real measure of leadership isn't which ethnic group is occupying the shiny offices, but whether the policies implemented actually improve lives. He points out the rising cost of living, unemployment, and strained public services affecting everyone from Nandi to Nyanza and beyond. This is supposed to be some profound revelation, but for many of us grinding it out in Nairobi, it's just Tuesday. It's almost as if [Shiny Rocks and Shady Bots: Why Kenya-s Elite Are Panicking Over Their Legacy](https://www.example.com/posts/shiny-rocks-and-shady-bots--why-kenya-s-elite-are-panicking-over-their-legacy) were onto something.

Then we have President Ruto, who decided to grace Northern Kenyans with an apology for "decades of historical marginalisation and economic neglect." Bless his heart, he’s going to make it a "thing of the past." Meanwhile, back in the city, the matatu fares keep climbing and the ugali portion gets smaller. The apology is framed as a "structural turning point," a "national declaration." It’s all very grand, isn’t it? Sounds like the same old song and dance, just with a different geographical flavour.

The man says this isn’t just "ceremonial gesture," but "a moment of affirmation that Madaraka, our freedom, our dignity, and our self-determination were never meant for some Kenyans, never meant for some region and withheld for others." Oh, really? Because from where I'm standing, it feels like a lot of those things are still being withheld for a whole lot of Kenyans. But hey, at least they got an apology from the big man himself. That'll probably pay the rent.
