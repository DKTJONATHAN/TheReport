THE LINE, bless their creative hearts, have partnered with ITV Creative to whip up an animated centerpiece for the upcoming *I'm a Celebrity... South Africa! All Stars* promo. Apparently, it's all set against the "dramatic backdrop of South Africa." Dramatic? Honey, the only drama I'm interested in is when these so called "all stars" start revealing the *real* reason they're desperate for airtime. This 20-second spot, directed by JuanPe Arroyo, kicks off with Ant & Dec doing their usual schtick by a fire. As Ant drones on about "iconic" campmates, the flames supposedly morph into a "surreal animated jungle." Frankly, the only thing surreal here is the idea that anyone still cares.

We're treated to glimpses of returning "all stars" like Sir Mo Farah, Gemma Collins, and David Haye. The producers are practically weeping about the "crucial and challenging" task of making animated celebrities. Laura Hancock, the producer, whines that it was "essential that the celebrities were instantly recognisable." Oh, I'm sure they nailed that. It's not like Gemma Collins's entire brand is built on being… well, Gemma Collins. The real challenge, I suspect, was making them look less bored than they probably are about being dragged back for another round of manufactured hardship.

Producer JuanPe Arroyo chirps about wanting fans to shout, "I know who that is!" Well, duh. That's the point of bringing back people whose careers are clinging to the last vestiges of fame. He also babbles about a "Celebrity Animated Universe." Please. The only universe these people inhabit is the one where their Instagram followers outnumber their actual accomplishments. This whole "surreal, high-energy storytelling" sounds suspiciously like a desperate attempt to inject life into a show that's frankly been phoning it in for years. It's a "dynamic, nostalgic and joyfully chaotic tribute" to a franchise that's more tired than a week-old cup of instant coffee.

Honestly, the only thing exciting about this is the potential for more behind-the-scenes whispers. Will Sir Mo Farah finally admit he's just doing it for the payday? Will Gemma Collins have a meltdown that rivals some of the real-life drama we've seen, like the financial woes some young professionals face, caught in a debt trap that feels just as inescapable as any jungle trial? [The 'Soft Life' Debt Trap: The Rising Rate of Loan App Suicides Among Young Professionals](/posts/the-soft-life-debt-trap-the-rising-rate-of-loan-app-suicides-among-young-professionals). Let's be real, the only thing this animation is proving is that even with fancy graphics, you can't polish a turd. And if those "dirty great big bugs" are anything like the personalities on display, I'll pass.
