Ruto Welcomes New Ambassadors, Urges Stronger Ties - Because We Need More Handouts

WARNING: Another day, another photo op at State House. President Ruto, fresh off his latest pronouncements, has welcomed a fresh batch of diplomats, all keen to "strengthen" ties. Translation: they want access to our markets and we want their money. Let's see how long these "mutual benefits" actually benefit anyone but them.

President William Ruto on Wednesday decided to play host at State House, Nairobi, rolling out the red carpet for three new envoys: Portugal's Paulo Neves Pocinho, the UK's Matt Baugh, and Namibia's Gabriel Pandureni Sinimbo. He greeted them with the usual platitudes, urging them to deepen cooperation and expand opportunities. Sounds lovely, doesn't it? Probably just code for "please invest in our projects and send cash."

The President's speech was a masterclass in diplomatic niceties, a warm welcome followed by an even warmer encouragement for these gentlemen to "deepen cooperation and expand opportunities for the mutual benefit of our countries." We’ve heard this song and dance before. Let's hope this time, "mutual benefit" doesn't just mean more contracts for foreign firms and more speeches for our leaders. After all, we've seen how "cooperation" can unravel, especially when dealing with certain international players, as seen in the [US Rights Group raises alarm over Kenya's passport scandal, cites Ruto's links with Sudan's RSF](/posts/us-rights-group-raises-alarm-over-kenya-s-passport-scandal--cites-ruto-s-links-with-sudan-s-rsf).

These envoys, with their impressive CVs and decades of experience, are now tasked with officially kicking off their missions. Ambassador Pocinho, seasoned in Pakistan and Qatar, Ambassador Sinimbo, with his Tanzania and India postings, and High Commissioner Baugh, who once navigated the choppy waters of Somalia, are all here to enhance bilateral cooperation. Let's hope their expertise translates into something tangible for the common Kenyan, not just another notch on their diplomatic belts.

The ceremony, attended by the usual suspects like Deputy President Kithure Kindiki and Musalia Mudavadi, marked the formal start of their tours. This means more meetings, more handshakes, and more pronouncements about enhanced trade, security, and development. We’ll be watching to see if these grand pronouncements actually lead to any real change, or if it's just more of the same old song and dance while the clock ticks on issues like [KUCCPS warns thousands risk missing out by failing to submit applications before midnight](/posts/kuccps-warns-thousands-risk-missing-out-by-failing-to-submit-applications-before-midnight).
