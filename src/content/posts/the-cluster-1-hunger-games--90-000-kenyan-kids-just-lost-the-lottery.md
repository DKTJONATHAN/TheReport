Welcome to the annual hunger games of the Kenyan education system. We have over 100,000 Grade 10 students pinning their entire future on 20 schools. It is the same old script: everyone wants a piece of the "Cluster 1" magic because the rest of the system is a graveyard of broken promises. There are only 10,000 slots available, meaning 90,000 children are about to learn their first lesson in Kenyan reality-you are probably not invited to the table.

Principal Secretary Julius Bitok is out here talking about "academic records" and "superior facilities" like we do not know the truth. These elite schools are admitting kids far beyond their capacity, turning dormitories into human Tetris experiments. While the big names suffocate under the weight of desperate parents, hundreds of smaller secondary schools are sitting empty, facing a literal existential crisis because nobody trusts the government to provide quality outside of a few gated compounds.

This is the beautiful mess of the CBE system rollout. We are told it is a new era, but the magnetic pull of the old guard proves that nothing has changed. In this country, education is just another product we cannot afford, proving once again that [Kenya is Not a Country; It’s a Limited Liability Company](/posts/kenya-is-not-a-country--it-s-a-limited-liability-company) where only the shareholders in the top tier schools actually get any real return on investment. The rest of the "learners" are just statistics to be filed away in under-funded classrooms.

Now, as these kids squeeze into their overcrowded classrooms, they are immediately hit with registration for national assessments. It is a conveyor belt of stress designed to filter out the "weak" before they even get a chance to breathe. The system does not want to actually educate you; it wants to rank you, file you, and eventually forget you unless you happen to wear the right school tie.

So, here we go again. More than 90,000 kids will be "shuffled" into schools they never wanted, while the elite institutions continue to sell the dream of success to the luckiest few. It is not an education system-it is a lottery where the house always wins and the "small" schools are left to rot in the sun. Welcome to Grade 10, kids. Try not to get crushed in the scramble.
