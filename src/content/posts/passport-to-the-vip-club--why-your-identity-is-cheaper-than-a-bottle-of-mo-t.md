Darling, while you are busy queuing at Nyayo House at 4 AM just to be told the "system is down," the real VIPs are getting the red carpet treatment. A leaked list has basically confirmed what we all suspected: the Kenyan passport is the latest "must-have" accessory for international men of mystery and questionable wealth. It turns out that being "one of us" is less about birthright and more about who you know in high places. It is truly peak comedy watching the government talk about "national pride" while handing out citizenship like party favors at a billionaire's birthday bash.

Take Wicknell Chivayo, for example. The Zimbabwean businessman loves a good photo-op with the Head of State almost as much as he loves a flashy designer suit and a private jet. While he is being probed for massive graft back home, he is apparently being welcomed here with open arms and a fresh travel document. It really gives off [The 'Wash Wash' Pandemic: Why Nairobi's Club Scene is Actually a Money Laundering Masterclass](/posts/the-wash-wash-pandemic-why-nairobis-club-scene-is-actually-a-money-laundering-masterclass) energy, doesn't it? If you have enough zeros in your bank account, who cares about a little thing like the seven-year residency requirement or actually living in the country?

But wait, the plot thickens and gets much messier. The guest list includes the extended family of Sudan's RSF leader, Hemedti. We are talking about individuals linked to some of the most brutal conflicts on the continent, yet they are walking around with the same document you use to go on a budget trip to Dubai. It is a bit ironic that the government is selling our sovereignty for the price of beans - or maybe a very expensive bottle of champagne at a private party in Runda. Our identity is being sold to the highest bidder, and honestly, the price tag seems shockingly low.

Political activist Boniface Mwangi is the one who let the cat out of the bag, and the hypocrisy is absolutely deafening. We are told to be proud of our "sacrosanct" identity while it is being auctioned off to warlords and foreign high-rollers who probably cannot even say "Hujambo" without a script. Even former Chief Justice David Maraga is chiming in, calling for immediate revocations, but we all know how these things go in the 254. When power meets convenience, the rules suddenly become very "flexible."

At the end of the day, it is just another day in a country where the laws are for the "small people" and the passports are for the highest bidders. While the Ministry of Interior stays silent and ignores all the awkward questions, the rest of us are left wondering if our national identity even means anything anymore. It is all just one big club, and honey, you are definitely not invited unless you have a questionable background and a very deep pocket. Stay petty, stay skeptical, and maybe check if your neighbor is actually a foreign general in a very good disguise.
