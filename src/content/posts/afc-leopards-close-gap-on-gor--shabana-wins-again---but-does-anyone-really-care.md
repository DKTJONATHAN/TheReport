AFC Leopards Close Gap On Gor, Shabana Wins Again - But Does Anyone Really Care?
The usual Kenyan Premier League drama unfolds, with AFC Leopards clinging to a sliver of hope and Shabana desperately trying to salvage some dignity. Meanwhile, goal-scoring records are threatened, as if that's going to change anything.
Tyson Otieno's "stunning strike" was enough to give AFC Leopards a 1-0 win over Kariobangi Sharks, a result that brings them four points closer to a Gor Mahia side that wasn't even playing. Apparently, national team duty is more important than local league games, a familiar story of priorities being all out of whack. So, Leopards are chasing a title they haven't sniffed since the last millennium. Don't hold your breath.

Shabana, bless their hearts, managed to scrape a 1-0 victory against Murang'a Seal. This win comes after a string of embarrassing losses, including a 5-1 thrashing by the very Leopards who barely scraped by this week. It's their interim coach's first win, a tiny glimmer of light in what has been a dark season. The opposing coach blamed "wastefulness in front of goal," a classic excuse when your team just isn't good enough. It’s like watching the same old movie, just with different actors who keep forgetting their lines.

The Golden Boot race is apparently heating up, with Ulinzi Stars striker Paul Okoth closing the gap on leader Joseph Waithira. Waithira hasn't scored in five matches, giving Okoth a chance to catch up. This entire chase for individual glory feels a bit like rearranging deck chairs on the Titanic. Does it matter if Waithira or Okoth scores 15 or 20 goals when the overall state of the league, much like the global economy, seems perpetually stuck in cycles of boom and bust, not unlike the narratives we see about [Washington’s $50 Million ‘Gift’ to African Film: More Gatekeeping or Just New Suits?](/posts/washington-s--50-million--gift--to-african-film--more-gatekeeping-or-just-new-suits)?

Meanwhile, APS Bomet and Nairobi United played out a goalless draw, a match that was actually halted by rain. Because, of course, it was. Heavy rain in Nairobi - who would have thought? This is the kind of unpredictability that makes you wonder if the league is even trying, or if it's just embracing the chaos.

Sofapaka continues to languish at the bottom, their woes compounded by a loss to Ulinzi Stars, who are themselves just trying to avoid relegation. It’s a dogfight at the bottom, a desperate scramble for survival that mirrors the struggles of [The Rust Belt’s New Overlords: Exporting Decay to the Highest Bidder](/posts/the-rust-belt-s-new-overlords--exporting-decay-to-the-highest-bidder). At the end of the day, a win is a win, a loss is a loss, and the league table keeps shuffling. Don't expect any miracles, just the usual predictable drama.
