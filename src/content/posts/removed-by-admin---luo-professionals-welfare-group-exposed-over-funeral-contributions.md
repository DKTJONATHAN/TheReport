'Removed by Admin': The WhatsApp Alert That Exposed Another Luo Welfare Group's Shenanigans

These "professionals" thought they could pull a fast one. Now they're in court, proving that when money is involved, even the "educated" ones can't resist a bit of greed. Another day, another Kenyan group discovering that promises are cheaper than actual payouts.

Just when you thought Nairobi gossip couldn't get more predictable, here we are. This time it’s the Luo Professionals Welfare Group, or whatever they call themselves, showing us all how it’s done when it comes to managing funeral contributions. Apparently, "welfare" translates to "we take a cut and hope nobody notices."

The whole mess kicked off when some members actually bothered to ask where their money was going. They were promised a refund after a year from funeral contributions, a neat little ten percent the group so graciously held onto. Funny how that ten percent never seems to find its way back to the grieving families. It’s almost as if the management saw it as their personal ATM, not a lifeline for bereaved members. This whole saga is a classic example of how easily people can get fleeced, whether it's from a welfare group or, say, when a government tries to push through dubious deals.

And the drama doesn't stop there. We've got relatives fighting over an estate and a car, the kind of petty squabbles that make you wonder if they're actually mourning or just sizing up the spoils. Then, to top it all off, the CEO is sharing erotic innuendo on social media. Because when you're busy mismanaging funds and causing family feuds, what better way to unwind than with some low-brow digital sleaze? It's a real testament to the priorities of these "professionals."

The petitioner's private WhatsApp chats, complete with screenshots, being used as evidence by her niece? That's just the icing on the cake. It’s a digital mudslinging match, proving that in Nairobi, even family disputes end up on your phone screen for everyone to see. It’s a shame the only thing these people seem good at collecting is drama, not those promised funeral refunds.

This whole debacle is about as surprising as Zuckerberg's Meta buying up another obscure online venture. It just goes to show, the more things change, the more people will always find new and inventive ways to exploit the vulnerable, especially when there's a quick shilling to be made.
