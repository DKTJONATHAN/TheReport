It is 2026 and the government is still trying to sell us the same old safari-and-beach combo as if it were a fresh drop from a luxury fashion house. At the ITB Berlin event, they unveiled Experience Wonder, a campaign that reeks of desperation and expensive PR consultants. Principal Secretary John Ololtuaa is out here talking about the Cradle of Humankind like it is a brand new discovery, hoping that 5 million international visitors will suddenly develop an interest in history by 2027. Honestly, the level of delusion required to set these targets is almost impressive.

The real kicker is the new Magical Kenya Destination Passport. It is a gamified digital tool because, apparently, Millennials and Gen Z are so brain-rotted by social media that they cannot appreciate a sunset without receiving a digital badge for it. CEO June Chepkemei claims travellers crave authentic experiences, but this is just another way to encourage people to treat our national heritage like a game of Pokemon Go. It is giving major [OPERATION EPIC FAIL: WHY OUR SOCIAL MEDIA STARS THINK THEY ARE GOING TO WAR](/posts/operation-epic-fail--why-our-social-media-stars-think-they-are-going-to-war) energy, where every trip to the Mara is just a backdrop for a mediocre thirst trap.

Let us be real about who this campaign is actually for. They are thirsty for the US and European markets, chasing that 12.8 percent arrival rate like a groupie at a backstage door. They are calling it tourism diplomacy and talking about increased flight connectivity from North America. It is all very shiny on paper, but we know the drill. The government is streamlining electronic travel authorisations to make it easier for colonizer-chic tourists to land, while the rest of us are still trying to figure out why the "infrastructure investment" never seems to reach the roads we actually drive on.

And then there is the buzzword of the century: sustainability. They claim they want to protect the environment while simultaneously inviting 5 million people to stomp through the Maasai Mara. It is the ultimate PR shield. You cannot claim to be a leader in responsible tourism while actively trying to turn our national parks into a crowded theme park for people who think a wellness retreat means taking selfies in a bathrobe. Kenya is certainly an Experience Wonder, mostly because we are all wondering when the hype will finally crash into the reality of our over-saturated Instagram feeds.
