So, here we are again. Kenya's duty-free access to the U.S. market, under that AGOA thing, is facing another "review." And guess who's doing the reviewing? None other than the Trump administration, looking at President William Ruto's "governance record." It's like watching a bad rerun, isn't it? The same old song and dance about human rights abuses and the corruption that's as Kenyan as ugali.

The U.S. Trade Representative, USTR for short, has kicked off its annual eligibility check. They want comments, they're holding a public hearing. As if they don't already know what's happening on the ground. "Mounting concerns" they say, about the very things we live with every single day: human rights getting trampled and corruption so thick you could cut it with a panga. It's not "mounting concerns" to us; it's just "life."

Remember that one-year extension Trump signed? Gave our textile and apparel exporters a "reprieve" from those nasty tariffs. A temporary band-aid, that's all it was. It didn't fix the fundamental problems that make us eligible for this scrutiny in the first place. It just kicked the can down the road, ensuring we'd be right back here, begging for scraps while the same old issues fester beneath the surface.

Now comes the July 23 hearing. They'll listen, they'll deliberate, and then... well, then probably very little will change for the ordinary mwananchi. Big powers always have their own agendas, and our leaders are just pawns in a larger game. It reminds you of how [Blood and Oil Fuel the Dollar's Hegemony](/posts/blood-and-oil-fuel-the-dollar-s-hegemony), doesn't it? Global politics and trade are rarely about genuine concern for the common person; it's about leverage and protecting interests.

So, don't hold your breath for a grand transformation. This "test" is just another performance. Our leaders will make promises, the U.S. will make demands, and we, the people, will keep paying the price. It's the Kenyan way, the cynical truth that no amount of duty-free access can ever truly whitewash.
