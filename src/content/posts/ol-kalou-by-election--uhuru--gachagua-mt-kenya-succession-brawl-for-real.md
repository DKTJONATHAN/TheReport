So, the Ol Kalou by-election is the latest episode in the grand Nairobi drama, featuring former President Uhuru Kenyatta's Jubilee and Rigathi Gachagua's DCP. They’re calling it a "love hate relationship." Honestly, it looks more like a marriage of convenience that’s soured faster than yesterday's mandazi. Jubilee’s deputy secretary general, Zack Kinuthia, is already muttering about an inevitable fallout. Surprise, surprise. These politicians always realise a divided opposition is the best way to keep the current regime firmly in power. It’s the oldest trick in the book, and we’re supposed to be surprised.

Gachagua, bless his ambitious heart, is apparently bracing for "financial pressure." Probably means he’s worried about the campaign kitty running dry, or maybe just the cost of maintaining that presidential hopeful image. And let's not forget his court charges for uttering nonsense that does zilch for social cohesion. It’s just another Tuesday in Kenyan politics, where words are cheap and actions even cheaper, especially when they get you in front of a judge.

Then there’s Waiguru’s "apology" to Gachagua, which is less an apology and more a subtle signal of the mountain of a conundrum facing these top dogs eyeing seats in 2027. It’s all about positioning, isn’t it? The latest poll saying Gachagua is the second favourite for president? Please. We’ve seen polls before; they’re as reliable as a KQ flight arriving on time. Remember the chaos with [BAGGAGE BLUES AND BOLLYWOOD BUSTS: KQ’S MUMBAI MELTDOWN](/posts/baggage-blues-and-bollywood-busts--kq-s-mumbai-meltdown)? That’s how reliable these "latest polls" usually are.

This whole Ol Kalou charade is just a microcosm of the larger Mt Kenya succession battle. Uhuru’s Jubilee and Gachagua’s DCP are not enemies; they are rivals in a zero-sum game. They know they can't stand each other, but they also know that fragmenting the opposition is the only way to ensure their individual ambitions, however hollow, have a chance. The voters, as always, are left holding the short end of the stick, watching the same old song and dance with slightly different faces. It’s a cycle, and we’re just here to witness the inevitable disappointment.
