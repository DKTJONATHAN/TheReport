Gachagua Defends Energy Chiefs, Claims They Brought Cheap Fuel Against Ruto's Wishes

The former Deputy President isn't holding back, accusing President Ruto of orchestrating a fuel scandal to protect his own pocket, while the real heroes trying to help Kenyans get thrown to the wolves.

This whole fuel drama? It's not about substandard fuel, as the President's goons would have you believe. According to Rigathi Gachagua, former DP and now a full-time Ruto critic, this is pure, unadulterated greed. The arrests of petroleum sector officials are just a smokescreen, a convenient way to silence anyone who dares to bring cheaper fuel into the country and mess with Ruto’s precious G-to-G arrangements. Apparently, "national interest" is just a fancy term for "Ruto’s profit margin."

Gachagua spills the beans, claiming two companies, conveniently linked to Ruto's inner circle and their proxies, are raking it in. While the rest of us brace for astronomical price hikes, these "heroes" in the energy sector tried to get us a break. Their crime? Competing with the President's personal business empire. It's a classic case of the powerful crushing anyone who threatens their gravy train. It's the kind of shady deal-making that makes you wonder if anyone in this government remembers the concept of public service, or if it's all just one big, elaborate scam, much like how some people might perceive the financial machinations in stories about [Beyond the Gates: Melinda French Gates and the Price of Private Betrayal](/posts/beyond-the-gates--melinda-french-gates-and-the-price-of-private-betrayal).

The former DP doesn't mince words, calling the arrested officials patriots who will be dragged to court on trumped-up charges. Their only offense? Denying William Ruto his ill-gotten gains. He also throws in a jab about the government's tax policies, the VAT hikes and fuel levies essentially funding Ruto's supposed bribery budget. Stabilizing fuel prices? Don't hold your breath, because that would mean cutting into his profits.

And it’s not just the fuel. Gachagua highlights how the DCI has become a personal enforcement arm for Ruto, intimidating anyone who steps out of line. Even the Kenya Pipeline Company isn't safe, with whispers of a Ugandan CEO being pushed through, all part of some opaque deal. Pension funds and unclaimed assets are apparently being plundered to buy up this critical infrastructure. It’s a free-for-all, with public funds being treated like personal ATMs.

Even Kiharu MP Ndindi Nyoro echoes these sentiments, pointing out the G-to-G system is being exploited and that leaders are using crises to line their pockets. They’re calling for immediate action - slash the VAT, ditch the levy - but you know that’s not happening as long as there’s profit to be made. The resignation of top petroleum officials after investigations into overpriced and possibly substandard emergency fuel shipments only confirms what many of us already suspected: it’s always about the money.
