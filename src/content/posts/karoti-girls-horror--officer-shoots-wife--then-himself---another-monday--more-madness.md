So, Monday morning in Kirinyaga County. Karoti Girls High School. Apparently, a senior Administration Police officer decided to bring his personal drama to school grounds. Before dawn, the man allegedly shot his wife dead, then turned the gun on himself. Police are calling it a domestic dispute. Shocker. The kind of "dispute" that leaves teachers, students, and the whole neighborhood staring at the abyss.

And this is just the latest headline in our never-ending soap opera. We've had dismembered remains found in Nyeri, priests gunned down. Reverend Kanyua and his mates were apparently overhearing police chatter about some unidentified body found that very morning. It’s a constant stream of gruesome news, isn't it? Makes you wonder if they’re even trying to solve these crimes, or just filing them away until some poor soul stumbles upon a clue by sheer, dumb luck.

Remember that lecturer and devolution expert murdered back in 2003? Still unresolved, more than two decades later. They say he was caught by coincidence a year later at Kitui Level 5 Hospital. A year. Imagine that. It’s the kind of incompetence that makes you question everything. It's not just the violence; it's the utter lack of accountability, the unresolved cases piling up like unread emails.

It's all so predictable, isn't it? This endless cycle of violence, followed by hushed investigations and often, no justice. We're all just trying to navigate this mess, whether it's dodging the taxman's ever-expanding digital reach, as seen with KRA's AI, or trying to understand who's watching what with all these new gadgets.
