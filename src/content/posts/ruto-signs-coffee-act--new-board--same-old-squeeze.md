Ruto's Coffee Board Shuffle: More Bureaucrats, Same Old Brew

Nairobi, Kenya - So, President Ruto has signed this new Coffee Act, apparently to shake up our beloved coffee industry. It seems the powers that be have decided the Agriculture and Food Authority was doing such a bang-up job regulating our precious beans that it needed a new boss. Now, the Coffee Board of Kenya is back in the driver's seat, tasked with "regulating and promoting" everything from processing licences to market strategies. Because what this industry really needs is more paperwork and more people to pay.

This new board will be busy, no doubt. They’ll be collecting "industry data" - which probably means more forms for farmers to fill out while their coffee rots - and doing "market intelligence." Oh, and promoting Kenyan coffee locally and internationally. Let's just hope they're better at that than some of our past sporting ventures. Remember how [Harambee Stars: Where Professionalism Goes to Die](/posts/harambee-stars--where-professionalism-goes-to-die) were supposed to represent us? I'm not holding my breath for a coffee renaissance.

They're also setting up a new Coffee Research and Training Institute. Apparently, the old one wasn't cutting it. So, more government bodies, more salaries, all to develop new coffee varieties and fight diseases. Sounds like a classic Kenyan solution: when something isn't working, add more layers of bureaucracy. We'll see if this institute can actually get anything done or if it's just another expensive project destined to underperform.

And just to add to the festive atmosphere of new laws, Ruto also signed amendments to the Railway Development Levy. Now, this levy is going to fund a wider range of railway infrastructure, not just the Standard Gauge Railway. Because, of course, we need *more* railway projects to siphon funds into. A new fund and a new board will oversee it all, promising to rehabilitate existing systems. Let's hope they don't fall into the same trap as those in charge of [Same Forest, Same Monkeys: How Nick Mwendwa and Friends ‘Ate’ CHAN 2018](/posts/same-forest--same-monkeys--how-nick-mwendwa-and-friends--ate--chan-2018).

Meanwhile, the Meteorology Act, 2023, blesses us with the Kenya Meteorological Service Authority. More regulation, more coordination of weather forecasting and early warning systems. Because our weather has always been so predictable, right? They'll be the "principal technical adviser" to governments. Let's just hope their advice isn't as reliable as some of the promises about infrastructure projects we've heard before.

Ultimately, it's the same old story. New laws, new boards, new committees. We’ll be paying for all this with our taxes and levies, while the actual results remain to be seen. Expect more meetings, more reports, and hopefully, just maybe, slightly better coffee. But don’t bet your last shilling on it.
