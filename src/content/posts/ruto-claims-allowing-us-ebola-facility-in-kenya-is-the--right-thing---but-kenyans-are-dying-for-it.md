RUTO SAYS LETTING AMERICANS BUILD EBOLA FACILITY IN KENYA IS THE ‘RIGHT THING’

Ruto’s claims of doing the “right thing” by letting the Americans build an Ebola quarantine facility ring hollow, especially with dead Kenyans in Nanyuki to prove it.

The President’s assurance that refusing the U.S. request would be “inhuman” is a rather convenient spin, isn’t it? More like, it’s convenient for the Americans, and profitable for some Kenyans, while others pay the ultimate price. We’ve seen this kind of thing before, where grand pronouncements mask less noble intentions, like the tales of FROM GUATEMALA’S STOLEN BABIES TO RUNDA’S GATED ILLUSIONS.

Despite court orders and public outcry, the U.S. is pushing ahead with this 50-bed unit on an air force base. Ruto claims he can look everyone in the eye and declare this the “right thing.” Perhaps he’s looking at a different set of eyes, because the ones in Nanyuki that are now closed forever don't seem to agree.

The U.S. embassy is busy “resolving objections” - a polite way of saying they’re smoothing things over, probably with the usual handshakes and whatever else makes things ‘convenient’ for them. Meanwhile, an Ebola outbreak simmers in the DRC and spills into Uganda, and here we are, setting up a welcome mat, or rather, a quarantine mat, for Americans.

This whole affair stinks of the same old song and dance. We’re told it’s for our own good, or for humanitarian reasons, but at the end of the day, who truly benefits? It’s a question that echoes through the empty promises of events like the Moi Forces Academy Career Day: More Fancy Talk Than Future Fortune.

Ruto might be doing the “right thing” in his eyes, but for the families in Nanyuki mourning their dead, and for anyone with a shred of common sense, it’s a hard pill to swallow.
