Saturday night in Nairobi used to be about finding a good vibe, but for Governor Orengo's staff, it was about finding a spot on a cold police station floor. The bodyguard was snatched at 7.30 pm, followed by eight others including the communications officer. It is the classic Kenyan greeting - if the big man is coming to town, the locals who might talk back need to be tucked away in a cell until the dust settles.

The Governor says the police are under strict instructions to keep his people locked up until Sunday evening, right after President Ruto finishes his tour of Siaya. This is what [Kenya in Transition: Economic Hurdles, Political Fractures, and Global Security Shifts](/posts/kenya-in-transition--economic-hurdles--political-fractures--and-global-security-shifts) looks like on the ground. It is not about policy or progress; it is about making sure the "homecoming" of a PS happens in a sterile, dissent-free environment where the only noise is the sound of sycophants clapping.

Meanwhile, Babu Owino is busy on Facebook, claiming the DCI from Kakamega are hunting him down. It is a total circus. In this country, the police can find a politician's personal assistant in an hour but cannot find a stolen car to save their lives. They are using the DCI like a delivery service for intimidation, moving officers across county lines just to make sure everyone knows who really holds the remote control.

Orengo is out here shouting about constitutional democracy and human rights, but let's be real. Those are just fancy words for lawyers to use in courtrooms while their clients rot in a cell. The Linda Mwananchi team wanted to present a report at Jacaranda about some MoU, but the government decided a "parallel report" was a bit too much truth for one weekend. They would rather have you focus on the church fundraisers and the scripted speeches.

This is the same path we have walked for decades. We are told we are in a new era, but the boots on the neck feel exactly the same. The arrests are arbitrary, the timing is tactical, and the justice is non-existent. If you are still waiting for a government that respects the law more than it respects its own ego, you are going to be waiting in the dark for a very long time.
