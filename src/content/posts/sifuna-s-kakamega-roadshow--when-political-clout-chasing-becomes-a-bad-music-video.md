Edwin Sifuna and his squad finally touched down in Kakamega, and honestly, if you thought clout chasing was limited to D-list Instagram influencers, you have clearly not been paying attention to the Linda Mwananchi roadshow. Sifuna led his "sea of humanity" through the streets like a man who knows his best camera angles, turning a political movement into nothing more than a glorified street parade. The convoy snaked through town toward Amalemba Grounds, proving once again that if you have enough tinted windows and a loud enough speaker, people will follow you anywhere just to see what the fuss is about.

The soundtrack to this mess was, predictably, the "Jeshi la Baba" anthem, which blared until the crowd reached a manufactured fever pitch. It is the same kind of performative energy we see with [Prayers, Pains, and Plastic Vibes: The Latest Music 'Healers'](/posts/prayers--pains--and-plastic-vibes--the-latest-music--healers) where the melody is just a cheap distraction from the total lack of actual substance. Babu Owino and Sifuna spent most of the time standing on top of their vehicles like urban explorers, because apparently, you cannot save the country if your designer shoes are touching the actual dirt the "mwananchi" walks on.

The real tea, however, came from the regional police commander, who basically told the world he found out about this "grand event" on social media. Imagine being a high-ranking official and getting your security intel from a TikTok scroll because the organizers were too busy chasing likes to send a formal letter. It is peak Gen-Z behavior from a group of middle-aged men who are clearly more concerned with their digital footprint than with actual protocol or safety.

Despite the lack of an official invite, the police were out in full force anyway, because we all know how much this country loves a taxpayer-funded spectacle. The heavy security presence and the sighting of the rival "TUTAM" group in their branded reflector jackets made the whole scene look less like a rally and more like a high-stakes turf war between rival fandoms. The optics of these [Billion-Shilling Badges: The High Cost of Police 'Prosperity'](/posts/billion-shilling-badges--the-high-cost-of-police--prosperity) being used to babysit political egos in Kakamega is exactly the kind of hypocrisy we have come to expect.

Governor Fernandes Barasa tried to play the role of the "magnanimous host," welcoming the Sifuna delegation despite being the face of the rival Linda Ground movement. It was the political equivalent of a "no-filter" selfie that has clearly been edited to death before posting. He preached political tolerance while his own supporters were likely side-eyeing the entire procession from the sidelines, waiting for their turn to cause a scene. It is all just a big game of brand management where the "people" are just the free labor for the next campaign poster.

As the caravan heads to Vihiga, we are left wondering when the actual work starts and the endless photo-ops end. Sifuna and his allies like James Orengo and Richard Onyonka are experts at drawing a crowd, but as any seasoned gossip knows, a big opening night does not mean the show has any legs. For now, Kakamega is just another stop on a tour that feels way more about the "vibe" than any actual value for the people they claim to protect.
