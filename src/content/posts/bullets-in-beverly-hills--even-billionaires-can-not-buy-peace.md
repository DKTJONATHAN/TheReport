So, Rihanna got shot at. Ten rounds through the gate of her Beverly Hills mansion while she was sitting right inside. It proves that money is just a shiny fence that does not actually stop bullets. While we are here struggling with kanjo and rogue bodabodas, the billionaires in the West are dodging 30-year-old women with bad aim and big guns. It is the same script, just a different currency and a more expensive neighborhood where the police actually show up before the body is cold.

You see these shows like "The Trend" or "The Wicked Edition" trying to make life look like a comedy, but the reality is much darker. Whether you are a global pop star or a hustler in River Road, someone is always waiting to take a shot at you. It is almost as bad as the stories we hear about [Ruto's and friends ksh2.1 trillion theft: auditor general exposes biggest government heist since independence](/posts/ruto-and-friends-ksh-2-trillion-theft-from-2022-to-2025) where the bullets are economic and the victims are the entire nation. Security is a myth sold to people who can afford the premium subscription of life.

The LAPD responded at 1:21 p.m., probably with more urgency than our local police respond to a mugging in the CBD. They caught the suspect, but the damage is done. The wall has a hole, and the illusion of safety is shattered. We spend so much time watching "Business Redefined" or listening to "Nation Reports" thinking we can strategize our way out of chaos, but chaos does not read the memo. It just shows up at your gate with a vehicle and a grudge, regardless of how many Grammys you have on your shelf.

Rihanna was home, and luckily, nobody was hurt. But let us be real, this is just another day in a world that is losing its collective mind. From Beverly Hills to the streets of Nairobi, the "vibrant digital creative economy" we keep hearing about in podcasts is just a fancy way of saying we are all one bad day away from a tragic headline. If Rihanna can get shot at in her own house, what makes you think your "tokens" and your double-locked gate in Kilimani will save you from the real world?
