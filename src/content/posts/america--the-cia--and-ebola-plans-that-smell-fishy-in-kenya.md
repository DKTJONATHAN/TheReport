At first, it was a fancy medical facility for "Americans exposed to Ebola." Now, Health Cabinet Secretary Adan Duale is telling us it’s just one of 23 centres for a "deadly disease" we’re supposedly preparing for. Funny how the story keeps changing faster than the price of fuel these days. Remember when DP Kindiki and CS Wandayi were defending that G-to-G fuel deal, blaming it all on a "global crisis"? Seems like every crisis is just an excuse for something else, doesn’t it? [/posts/dp-kindiki--cs-wandayi-defend-g-to-g-deal--link-fuel-price-surge-to-global-crisis]

Meanwhile, "public health experts" are practically begging the government to bring any sick Americans *home* for treatment. But no, our esteemed CS Duale is too busy asking the High Court to dismiss petitions questioning this whole setup. Why? Because transparency is apparently a dirty word when you’re dealing with the Americans and, let’s be honest, probably the CIA. They always have their fingers in something that smells of secrets and Shilling.

And get this: officials can’t even agree on who gets to use this supposed Ebola facility. One minute it’s for Americans, the next it’s for Kenyans, or maybe both? It’s like trying to get a straight answer about where all that money from the California Bear-Suit luxury car scam ended up. [/posts/california-bear-suit-luxury-car-scam-ends-in-insurance-fraud-sentences-for-3---another-day--another-dollar-stolen]

The High Court has now ordered Duale to spill the beans within seven days. Seven days! As if they’ll suddenly remember what they’re doing. My bet? He’ll give some half-baked explanation that makes even less sense than the original story. This whole "Ebola plan" is just another red flag waving in our faces, a classic Nairobi spectacle of confusion and potential graft, while the real issues - like ensuring our own people have access to basic medical care - get conveniently ignored.
