So, the boffins have finally figured it out. This whole "aid industry" is just a fancy way of keeping Kenya in the dirt, propping up historical and geographic inequalities. They talk about "state-led crisis response" like it's some new invention, but what they really mean is we're still waiting for the government to actually *govern* and sort out the mess instead of relying on handouts. Other countries, richer ones, they’re sorting themselves out. We’re still here, begging.

And why are we still begging? Because, surprise surprise, the system is rigged. This paper calls it an "unconducive political economy of aid." I call it a feeding frenzy for the connected. While the big shots collect their salaries and consultants fly in and out, the real problems - the deeply entrenched inequality and the rotten governance - just get worse. It's a perfect storm, really. Every drought, every flood, every sniffle becomes a full blown crisis because no one’s built the proper infrastructure to handle it. It’s like that time after the rains, when the annual Nairobi swim turned into a death trap because the drainage was a joke. [/posts/the-annual-nairobi-swim--death-by-a-thousand-puddles]

They’ve got evidence of "improving state-led response," which I suspect means someone finally remembered to fill out a form in triplicate. But real progress? That's a pipe dream when the same old power structures are still in place. The aid money flows, the local politicians get their cut, and the people on the ground are left to pick up the pieces. It's a cycle as old as time.

Now, with all these "major aid cuts" looming, things are about to get even uglier. The paper talks about "galvanising change." Let's be real. Change happens when people are pushed to the brink, not when committees write papers. We need a government that actually functions, that sees its people as more than just pawns in some international charity game.

But who am I kidding? This is Kenya. We'll probably just get more meetings, more reports, and more promises. Meanwhile, the "perpetual humanitarian crisis" will keep on churning, fueled by our own glorious leaders and their foreign benefactors. It’s a masterclass in maintaining the status quo, and we’re all paying the price.

So, email it, save it, enquire about it. It’s all just noise. Until the fundamental inequalities and the corrupt governance are addressed, we’re just rearranging deck chairs on the Titanic. And the iceberg? That’s already here.
