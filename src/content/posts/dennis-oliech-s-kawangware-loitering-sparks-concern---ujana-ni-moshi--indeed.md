The internet's been buzzing, or rather, weeping, over a recent photo of Dennis Oliech. The man who once had us glued to our screens now looks like he's seen better days. Fans, those fickle creatures, are suddenly concerned. It’s almost as if they forgot that fame, like a bad phone battery, drains quickly.

Now, the rumour mill is churning with claims that the former Harambee Stars captain has been spotted "loitering" in Kawangware. Loitering. In Nairobi, that's practically a sport. It begs the question: is this the natural progression for someone who lived life in the fast lane, or just another reminder that "glory days" are about as permanent as a Kenyan election promise?

During his heyday, Oliech was the poster boy for Kenyan success - flashy cars, parties, and women. A classic tale. But careers fade, and so does the money. Reports of financial struggles and the burden of caring for his mother have been circulating. Caring for family is noble, but it's also expensive, and not everyone plans for the twilight of their football career like they plan for a crucial match. It's a harsh reality, much like how streaming services have become a predictable treadmill of mediocrity.

People are now calling for support for the "legend." It's easy to call for support when you're not the one footing the bill. Let's be real, the same fans who celebrated his goals are now dissecting his appearance and where he's seen. The pity party is in full swing, but how much of that pity translates into actual, sustained help? We'll see. For now, the photos are out, the whispers are loud, and the legend is, for all intents and purposes, looking rather ordinary.
