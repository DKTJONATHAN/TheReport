The Executive Office of the President, in their infinite wisdom, has confirmed what we all suspected: this Sh4.8 billion fuel shipment was a backroom deal, a slap in the face to any pretense of proper procedure. Of course, it was bought at inflated prices. What else would you expect when the "government-to-government" framework is just a suggestion for those who aren't in the know? This is classic Nairobi, where the rules are for the mwananchi, not the fat cats.

And the timing? Pure comedy, if it wasn't so infuriating. A contract signed on March 25th, 2026, and *poof*, the vessel MT Paloma is already here two days later, laden with 68,000 metric tonnes. The cargo records tell a tale of its own, loaded in February and then somehow departing Mombasa on March 30th. It’s as if the fuel sprouted legs and walked itself onto the ship. This is the kind of elaborate charade that makes you wonder if they think we’re all still living in the Stone Age.

Naturally, a Mombasa-based business tycoon is at the heart of this little operation, with the DCI now playing catch-up. It’s always a tycoon, isn't it? They're like roaches, always scurrying out from under the rocks when the lights go on, but rarely getting squashed. We've seen this kind of rot before, like when [Police Federation Men Arrested for Fraud, Exposing Internal Rot](/posts/police-federation-men-arrested-for-fraud--exposing-internal-rot). It’s the same old song, different verse, and we’re all forced to hum along to the tune of our own exploitation.

So, while you and I are trying to figure out how to stretch our budgets, these folks are busy orchestrating multi-billion shilling schemes. They sign contracts that don't exist for fuel that arrived before the ink was dry, all while claiming to be serving the nation. It’s a masterclass in deception, a testament to the fact that in this country, honesty is a liability and corruption is a career path.

This whole circus is brought to you by the same system that churns out endless media shows discussing problems without ever fixing them. From [The Politalk podcast](/posts/the-politalk-podcast) to shows promising to fix the nation, the talking is cheap. The real action is happening behind closed doors, where billions are being made on the backs of the people who can least afford it.

We're left with the crumbs, the inflated prices, and the hollow promises. The Sh4.8 billion is gone, likely lining pockets from the Ministry of Energy all the way up. And the worst part? We'll probably be asked to foot the bill for their 'mistakes' one way or another. Just another Tuesday in Kenya.
