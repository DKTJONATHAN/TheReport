IT'S MT KENYA PEOPLE WHO ARE THE REAL BOSSES, SUNDRIES THE NAIROBI REALIST CLAIMS

SUDI'S HOT AIR ABOUT MT KENYA POWERING RUTO'S PRESIDENCY SHOWS HOW KSK POLITICS REALLY WORKS - IT'S ALL ABOUT WHO OWNS THE BALLOT BOX.

Oscar Sudi, bless his cotton socks and tribal loyalty, has once again reminded us where the real power lies in this country. According to him, speaking in Molo (of all places, as if Molo is the epicentre of national decision-making), it was the folks from Mt Kenya who single-handedly shoved William Ruto into State House. He's basically saying, "Don't come crying to me if things aren't going your way, you voted for him, remember?" It’s a classic case of "we put him there, so he owes us," and Sudi is just the messenger shouting it from the rooftops, probably hoping for a few more nods and handshakes in return.

Sudi's whole spiel is a masterclass in political theatre, designed to get the Mt Kenya voting bloc to feel all warm and fuzzy about their supposed kingmaking status, while simultaneously shutting down any grumbling from those who are now two years into their Ruto experiment and wondering if they accidentally voted for their own economic downfall. He's telling them, "Don't you dare turn on him now, you haven't even seen the full 'benefits' of your vote yet." It's like a con artist telling their mark to be patient, the big payout is just around the corner, definitely.

The MP's insistence on "political honesty" is rich, coming from someone who seems to be playing the tribal card with all the subtlety of a herd of wildebeest. He’s basically saying, "Face it, Kenya, Ruto wouldn't be president if it weren't for the Kikuyu vote." And while there might be a kernel of truth in that - because let's be honest, every election is a tribal census in disguise - he’s framing it as if this is some grand revelation, instead of the well-worn playbook it is. It's the same old song, different verse, sung to keep the bases happy and the opposition divided.

And then there's the thinly veiled threat to those who dare to question the current administration, especially from the Mt Kenya region itself. Sudi's warning that "no president serves only one term" is less about a belief in long-term leadership and more about a strategic message to the powers that be: "We delivered, so don't forget us." He’s practically dangling the prospect of future elections like a carrot, reminding everyone that the Mt Kenya vote is a commodity to be courted, not a civic duty to be taken for granted. It’s all about the next election, and who delivered whom.

It's also amusing that he's praising Ruto for "continuing to work in the mountain" because he knows where his "chair" came from. This is less praise and more a pointed reminder to both Ruto and the Mt Kenya electorate: the president is aware of his debt, and they are aware of their leverage. Meanwhile, Rigathi Gachagua, who is apparently no longer the golden boy from the mountain, is out there spitting fire, claiming Ruto's support is vanishing faster than a free beer at a political rally. It’s all a predictable dance of alliances and disses, and the voter is just the pawn in their elaborate game, hoping for a crumb from the table. Frankly, it’s a miracle any government projects get delivered when the focus is perpetually on who owes whom and who voted for whom. Remember when Muturi saw a "Mega Scandal" brewing? That's the kind of thing that gets lost in this tribal tug-of-war.

Sudi is basically telling everyone to chill out and wait for their "benefits" to trickle down, while simultaneously reminding the president that the hand that feeds him is the one that also holds the ballot. It’s a cynical display of transactional politics, where loyalty is bought and sold, and the common mwananchi is left holding the empty bag, wondering if their vote was worth the promises that seem to evaporate faster than morning mist. We've seen this movie before, and the ending is usually the same for the ordinary Kenyan.
