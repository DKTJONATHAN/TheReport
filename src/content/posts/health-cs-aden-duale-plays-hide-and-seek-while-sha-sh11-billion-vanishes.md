Honey, if you thought the local music scene was messy, wait until you see the absolute circus happening at the Social Health Authority (SHA). Our very own Health CS Aden Duale and CEO Mercy Mwangangi are currently starring in a high-stakes game of 'Where is the Money?' while Kenyans are left holding empty medicine bottles. Apparently, Sh11 billion has just vanished into thin air. It is giving major 'failed luxury festival' vibes, but instead of bad sandwiches and wet tents, people are actually losing their lives because they cannot get healthcare.

On Tuesday night, the ODPP decided to drop some last-minute drama by approving charges against eight individuals. Talk about a staged plot twist! But let us be real- this smells like a desperate PR move to stop MPs from ripping them a new one during their scheduled meeting. Critics are already calling these arrests 'cosmetic,' and honestly, I have seen better-applied foundation on a budget influencer. It is all for show, meant to create the illusion of accountability while the real big fish keep swimming in their pools of stolen cash.

Instead of facing the music in front of the National Assembly, Duale pulled a classic 'celebrity ghosting' move. He skipped the meeting entirely to preside over the launch of Lenacapavir, a fancy new HIV prevention drug. It is the ultimate distraction- look at this shiny new advancement over here while we ignore the massive house fire behind us! This feels like yet another [The Sh44 Billion Mirage: Motsepe’s ‘Confidence’ vs. Our Empty Pockets](/posts/the-sh44-billion-mirage--motsepe-s--confidence--vs--our-empty-pockets) situation where big numbers and 'groundbreaking' announcements are used to mask the fact that the average Kenyan is getting absolutely nothing but a lighter wallet.

And can we talk about this 'Digital Superhighway' that cost us a staggering Sh104 billion? If I spent that much on a security system and still had unlicensed clinics hacking into my bank account like it was public Wi-Fi at a mall, I would be demanding a refund. It turns out that 'unauthorised access' is the new trend, with rogue actors treating our collective health records like a free-for-all buffet. Why are we paying billions for high-end tech when the front door is wide open and the guards are apparently taking a nap?

The list of implicated hospitals reads like a directory of 'Who Not to Trust' in the medical world. From Danaba Care to Dimtu Nursing Home, these places are accused of everything from operating without licenses to straight-up conspiracy to defraud. It is a messy web of directors and fake claims that would make a soap opera writer blush. Meanwhile, actual doctors are out here begging for reimbursements and hospitals are closing because the SHA is 'too busy' to pay out legitimate claims.

At the end of the day, it is the same old script: name a few scapegoats, issue a stern-sounding press release, and wait for the public to get distracted by the next social media scandal. Even the ODPP files mention dates in 2026- are they investigating crimes from the future now? Until we actually see some of these high-flyers in a prison jumpsuit instead of a designer suit, this is all just expensive theater. We are tired of the 'investigations' that lead nowhere while our 2.75 per cent contributions disappear into the abyss.
