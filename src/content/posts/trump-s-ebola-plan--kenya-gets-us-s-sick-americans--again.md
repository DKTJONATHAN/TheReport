So, the Yanks are at it again, aren't they? Apparently, when one of their own touches Ebola, they'd rather ship them off to Nairobi than deal with the mess on their own turf. The Trump administration, in its infinite wisdom, is reportedly planning to set up a quarantine facility in Kenya for its Ebola-exposed citizens. Because why have a problem in your own backyard when you can export it to someone else's, right? It's the American way, I suppose.

Wall Street Journal says they'll even be sending their own public health officers to staff this new "resort." And it's not just for observation; the New York Times is quoted saying they'll be providing actual *treatment* in Kenya. So, not only do we get to clean up their mess, but we also get to nurse their sick back to health. It's almost heartwarming, if you're into that sort of thing. This whole situation just screams of the same old story, like when ["The IMF Debt Trap Killing Kenyan Dreams"](/posts/test-article) started to unfold.

Never mind that Kenya has diligently screened thousands and hasn't recorded a single case. But hey, proximity to Uganda and South Sudan means we're a prime candidate for an outbreak anyway. It's not like our borders are exactlyFort Knox, especially when the Americans are the ones potentially bringing the party to town. The thought of hosting Ebola patients, even for our "friends" across the ocean, is enough to make anyone’s stomach churn.

They're so keen on avoiding this on their soil that they've even been funneling all their at-risk travellers through Washington Dulles for "enhanced screening." Now, suddenly, Kenya is the preferred destination for their contagion. It’s a real testament to their commitment to global health, isn't it? Just another day in this beautiful, chaotic city, where we're always expected to pick up the pieces.
