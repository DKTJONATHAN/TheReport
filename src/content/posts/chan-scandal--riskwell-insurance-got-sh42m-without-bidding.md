Chan Scandal: Riskwell Insurance Got Sh42m Without Bidding, EACC Says

The Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission is finally looking into how Football Kenya Federation allegedly chucked Sh42 million to Riskwell Insurance for a tournament that barely happened. Apparently, bidding was optional, especially when it came to lining pockets.

It seems our Kenyan coffers are just an endless ATM for those in the know. This Chan tournament, meant for 2024 but somehow squeezed into August 2025, is just another chapter in the grand Kenyan playbook of procurement drama. While the fans will be kept busy in "fan zones" to avoid overcrowding at venues no one can afford to get into, the real action is happening behind closed doors.

The EACC is prioritizing this, they say. Prioritizing is a funny word when you consider how long it takes for anything to actually happen. Maybe they’re just writing a new chapter in the book of how to triple debt and ignore potholes, much like we saw with Wavinya’s road to riches. [Wavinya’s Road To Riches: How To Triple Debt and Ignore Potholes](/posts/wavinya-s-road-to-riches--how-to-triple-debt-and-ignore-potholes). It’s a miracle we haven’t seen more serious charges or actual convictions.

Meanwhile, our Sports CS, bless his optimistic heart, is appointing teams to "work for Kenya." One hopes these teams are less interested in football-related scandals and more in, you know, actual governance. But then again, this country thrives on manipulation of sports competitions, and soon it might even be a criminal offense. What a joke.

At least the Sh42 million has been "remitted to CAF," which is probably just code for "moved to another offshore account." The EACC’s prioritization is a good show, but don't hold your breath waiting for justice. This is Kenya, after all.
