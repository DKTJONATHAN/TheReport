In Nairobi, we say "character development" happens to everyone, but Bishop Ben Kiengei just gave the youth a masterclass in betrayal. One minute he is at a funeral for a protestor, weeping with the Gen Z crowd and swearing that the pulpit is holy ground, the next minute he is adjusting the microphone for President Ruto. It is the classic Kenyan bait-and-switch. These "men of God" have a price tag, and apparently, the President’s presence is the ultimate currency for a church in Ruiru.

Bishop Ben Kiengei, or "Guka wa Nairobi" as he likes to be called, has finally shown his true colors. It is funny how fast "holy ground" becomes a political rally when the state motorcade pulls up. The youth who looked up to him as a voice of reason are now staring at their screens in disbelief. But why are we shocked? This is a man who balances comedy, radio, and preaching. It is all one big performance, and the audience just realized they paid for a ticket to a show they did not want to see.

The hypocrisy is thick enough to choke on. This is the same man who stood over the grave of Bonface Kariuki, the mask vendor killed during the protests. He played the role of the grieving shepherd perfectly. Now, he is hosting the very administration accused of the violence. It is reminiscent of the scammers we see every day; it is just like [from flexing on IG to filtering jail bars](/posts/from-flexing-on-ig-to-filtering-jail-bars--the-27--entrepreneurs--interpol-just-dragged) where the glamorous facade eventually cracks to reveal a very different, uglier reality underneath the filters.

The Jesus Christ Compassion Church seems to have a very selective definition of compassion. Apparently, it does not extend to the feelings of the youth who are still nursing wounds from the streets. Instead, it is about the "clout" and the proximity to power. Hosting Ruto in Ruiru was not a mistake or a lapse in judgment; it was a calculated move to remind everyone that in the kingdom of Kiengei, the government is the true deity. The backlash is loud, but the Bishop seems to have his ears blocked by the sound of sirens.

If you are still looking for morality in a Nairobi pulpit, you are probably the same person waiting for a matatu at a bus stop that does not exist. The youth are slamming him on social media, calling him out for cozying up to the system, but Kiengei is likely laughing all the way to the bank. In this city, loyalty is a luxury that "Guka" clearly cannot afford. The only thing "fixed" about this nation is the game, and the preachers are the ones holding the deck.

Let this be a lesson to the Gen Z crowd. Do not put your faith in a man who uses a ring light and a radio frequency to reach you. When the "Guka" of Nairobi tells you he is on your side, check your pockets. He is just another entrepreneur in a collar, looking for the next big endorsement deal, even if it comes from the very people you were fighting against. The pulpit is no longer a place of worship; it is just another podium for hire.
