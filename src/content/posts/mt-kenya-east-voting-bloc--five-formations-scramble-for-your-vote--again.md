So, the "vicious scramble" for Mt Kenya East is on. Deputy President Kithure Kindiki, Rigathi Gachagua, Fred Matiang'i, Anne Waiguru, and Kawira Mwangaza - a veritable who's who of people who want your attention, and more importantly, your vote. They're all forming "formations," which is a fancy word for "groups trying to get ahead." Meru, Tharaka Nithi, Embu, and now Kirinyaga are being eyed like ripe mangoes ready for picking. It's the same old song and dance, just with different faces.

The "impeached DP" is apparently "silently assembling a machine." Translation: they're probably making calls to disgruntled former allies of… well, everyone. It’s a game of musical chairs, where nobody really wins, but a few get to sit down for a while. Meanwhile, one of them is back in the news for remarks about a schoolgirl. Because when you're vying for power, what better way to connect with the electorate than by making questionable comments about minors? Peak political strategy.

Let's be real, this "progress" they preach about, this idea of listening to one another and having "well-considered and articulated logical responses," is a fantasy. This is Kenya. This is politics. It's about who can shout the loudest, who has the deepest pockets, and who can promise you the moon and stars without a plan to even deliver a pebble. The entire industrialization of poverty is fueled by these promises, isn't it? We've seen it time and again, with schemes like Ruto's Affordable Housing Pitch that, frankly, don't make anyone laugh but the politicians laughing all the way to the bank.

What we're witnessing is the predictable circus. They'll trot out their usual talking points, whip up a storm of tribal loyalties, and then, once the votes are counted, they'll disappear until the next election. It's a well-oiled machine, much like the digital empires built by companies like Transsion, taking over markets and data, while we're left arguing over who promised what. The real issue isn't their "formations," it's our willingness to fall for the same old con.
