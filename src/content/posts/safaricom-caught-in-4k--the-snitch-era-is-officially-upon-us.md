Just when you thought your private DMs and midnight calls were safe, the corporate world reminds us that "privacy" is just a marketing buzzword. David Mokaya, a student from Moi University who clearly has a flair for the dramatic, is currently dragging Safaricom through the High Court mud. Why? Because apparently, the green giant decided to play snitch to the DCI, handing over his personal data without so much as a "please" or a court order.

This whole mess started back in 2024 when Mokaya, operating under the very humble handle "LANDLORD," allegedly posted some spicy fiction on X about the President’s body leaving a funeral home. Talk about looking for trouble in all the wrong places. He was hauled to court, but in a plot twist worthy of a Nigerian soap opera, he was acquitted this past February because the prosecution couldn't prove a thing. It turns out, even with Safaricom handing over call records like party favors, the state still fumbled the bag.

Now, Mokaya is seeking revenge, and honestly, we are here for the pettiness. He is demanding that the court stop Safaricom from sharing his location and metadata without a lawful order. It is the kind of [CHARACTER DEVELOPMENT: THE NAIROBI CIRCUS AFTER THE LEAK](/posts/character-development--the-nairobi-circus-after-the-leak) that keeps the city's elite looking over their shoulders. We all know Nairobi is one giant village where everyone is in everyone else's business, but seeing a major telco get called out for its lack of boundaries is a refreshing change of pace.

The hypocrisy of it all is what really stings. These companies spend millions on ads telling us how much they value our "connections," yet they are the first to drop your coordinates the moment the authorities come knocking. Mokaya might just be a student, but he is highlighting a massive problem for every social media clout-chaser and political commentator out there. If your service provider won't protect your data, who will? Stay safe out there, because your phone is basically a tracking device with a pretty screen.
