Well, well, well, look what the cat dragged in! The Aga Khan Fund for Economic Development, bless their polite and dignified hearts, have decided to offload their 54.08% stake in Nation Media Group PLC. And who’s the lucky buyer? Tanzania’s resident dollar billionaire, Rostam Aziz, a man whose resume reads like a cautionary tale. This former CCM parliamentarian, linked to a corruption scandal that sent a prime minister packing, is now the new kingpin of Kenyan media. Sixty years of editorial independence? Please. It’s all about who’s holding the purse strings now, and apparently, Aziz's vehicle, Taarifa Ltd, has them.

This isn’t just a minor acquisition, darling. We’re talking about the Daily Nation, Business Daily, NTV Kenya, The EastAfrican, and even major players in Uganda and Tanzania. Aziz now commands a media empire that reaches over 62 million digital users. In Kenya, a country where press freedom is apparently taking a nosedive from 102nd to 117th in the global rankings, this is less about journalism and more about consolidated power. And let's not forget, this is the same man who allegedly forced Nation Media Group to pull down stories about him. Talk about full circle, or should I say, a full-on hostile takeover!

The juicy part? How did this all go down? It seems Aziz’s Taifa Gas Investments SEZ Ltd finally got its much-coveted gas plant approved in Mombasa after years of regulatory hurdles. And guess who personally presided over the groundbreaking ceremony? None other than President William Ruto himself, who practically declared Aziz a national hero for overcoming "government shenanigans." Oh, the sweet symphony of political favors! It’s no secret that Aziz was seen as allied with Ruto during Uhuru Kenyatta’s reign, and now that the man is in power, doors are swinging open, and media empires are apparently on the table. It’s enough to make you wonder if all this talk of a free press is just a pretty façade, especially when you consider the digital panopticon we're all living in. And let’s not gloss over the Richmond scandal that still clings to Aziz like cheap perfume. Back in 2007-2008, this gem of a deal involving emergency diesel generators supposedly led to Tanzania losing over $120 million and a prime minister’s resignation. Aziz was named in the parliamentary report as having a crucial role. He’s always denied it, of course, but the association, shall we say, *persists*. He even stepped down as an MP citing "dirty politics." Now, he owns the very media that might have once scrutinized him. The irony is thicker than a cheap celebrity biopic.

Nation Media Group itself isn't exactly swimming in cash, either. After a glorious profit peak in 2013, they've been bleeding money for the last two years, hitting a net loss of Ksh 254.4 million in 2024. They've axed regional newsrooms and slashed staff. So, while they might be looking for a savior, are they getting a knight in shining armor or a dragon hoarding all the gold? With mounting government debts for advertising and a press freedom ranking in freefall, the future of journalism under Mr. Aziz's gilded hand looks… complicated. One can only imagine the "editorial independence" they'll be enjoying now.

So, as Kenya hurtles towards another election, remember who’s now holding the reins of its most powerful media houses. A man with a presidential golf buddy and a past that’s more dramatic than any reality TV show. It’s not just about news; it’s about narrative control, and darling, Rostam Aziz just bought the biggest stage in town.
