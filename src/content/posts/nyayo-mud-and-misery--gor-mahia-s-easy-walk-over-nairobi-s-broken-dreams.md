The rain in Nairobi is the only thing consistent about this city, and on Sunday, it did its best to wash away whatever dignity KCB FC had left. Nyayo Stadium turned into a giant puddle, but Gor Mahia did not seem to mind. They treated the bankers like a small-time SACCO, putting three goals past them before the halftime whistle even blew. It is the same old story in the FKF Premier League; Gor Mahia shows up, the opponent forgets how to play football, and the fans go home wet and disappointed by the lack of competition.

Enock Morisson and Shariff Musa spent the first forty-five minutes making KCB look like they had never seen a ball before. By the time Samuel Kapen hammered in the third goal, the match was effectively over. KCB has not beaten Gor Mahia since January 2023, and at this rate, they will not beat them until the Nairobi Expressway starts offering free rides. They are seventeen points behind the leaders now, a gap so wide you could fit all of Nairobi’s uncollected garbage inside it and still have room for more.

Over at the Police Sacco Stadium, the "Forces Derby" was less of a football match and more of a synchronized swimming event without the grace. Kenya Police FC, a team built on enough money to fix the city’s drainage, managed a pathetic 0-0 draw against APS Bomet. When your star winger gets injured in the warm-up and your goalkeeper pulls a muscle just by kicking the ball, you know the universe is trying to tell you to stay in bed. They are now thirteen points off the pace, and their title defense is looking like a bad joke told at a funeral.

It is a special kind of tragedy to watch a team with a massive budget fail to score against a team fighting for survival. Kenya Police are basically a walking metaphor for the state of our public service; high funding, zero output. They spent ninety minutes splashing around in the mud, proving that [the luxury of hitting rock bottom](/posts/the-luxury-of-hitting-rock-bottom) is a state of mind they have fully embraced. If you cannot beat a newly-promoted side at home, you have no business pretending to be champions.

Even the small victories in this league feel like a delay of the inevitable. Mara Sugar managed to beat Ulinzi Stars 2-1 in Awendo, their first win since mid-December. For Ulinzi, the descent continues. They have lost eleven matches out of twenty-one, sitting comfortably in the relegation zone like they actually enjoy the view from the bottom. It is a mess from top to bottom, with only Gor Mahia looking like they have a plan that does not involve praying for the referee to blow the whistle early.

The league is becoming a one-horse race where the other horses have broken legs and the horse in the lead is just bored. K’Ogalo are six points clear and haven’t lost a game since December. While everyone else is searching for answers under rain-filled skies and complaining about the pitch, Gor Mahia is busy collecting points. The rest of these teams are just here to fill the fixtures and provide us with more reasons to be cynical about the state of Kenyan football.
