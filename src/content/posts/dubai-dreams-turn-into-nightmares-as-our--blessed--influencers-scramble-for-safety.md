The "God Did" captions are looking a little shaky this week, aren't they? While your favorite influencers have spent years posting filtered photos of luxury suites in Dubai and Manama to make you feel poor, the literal ground beneath their designer heels is shaking. As explosions rock the Gulf states and Iran settles scores for the death of their supreme leader, the "soft life" crowd is suddenly realizing that a Birkin bag makes for a terrible helmet. It is all fun and games until the skyline you are using as a backdrop for your "manifestation" video becomes a target for a retaliatory strike.

Naturally, the Kenyan government is doing the heavy lifting for people who usually act like they are too posh for a Kenyan passport. PS Roseline Njogu and Dr. Sing’oei Korir held a "hybrid meeting" on Tuesday to coordinate how to save our citizens from the unfolding mess. It is peak irony that the same people who brag about escaping the "struggles" of Nairobi are now clicking on government links faster than they would a discount code for Shein. The Ministry of Foreign and Diaspora Affairs is practically begging people to register for evacuation, reminding everyone that when the missiles fly, your "international business mogul" status means absolutely nothing to a consular officer.

President William Ruto, never one to miss a chance for a viral statement, has jumped into the fray to condemn the strikes across the UAE, Qatar, and Saudi Arabia. It is a classic move straight out of [The Strategic Playbook Behind President William Ruto’s 2027 Re-election Campaign](/posts/Rutos-strategic-reelection-psyops) to play the role of the regional peacemaker while his citizens are hiding under five-star hotel beds. He is tweeting about "threats to international peace" while the influencers are probably wondering if they can claim "war zone survivor" as a new personality trait for their TikTok bios.

The reality is that the Middle East "haven" is officially closed for business. With three people dead in the UAE and explosions heard for two days straight in Dubai and Doha, the facade of untouchable luxury has crumbled. We have spent years watching these "socialites" sell us a dream of easy money and peace in the desert, but now they are facing the same "regional instability" they thought they were too rich to experience. The government’s emergency hotlines are the new must-have accessory, replacing the overpriced cocktails and desert safaris.

If you are one of those "working" in the Gulf, maybe it is time to stop pretending you are too busy for the embassy. The ministry is being very clear: register now or don't complain when the "seamless information flow" doesn't include your personal rescue mission. We see through the hypocrisy of loving the Gulf for its gold but running back to Kenya the moment the bill for that luxury comes in the form of regional warfare. Stay safe, I guess, but let's keep the same energy when you land back at JKIA with nothing but your luggage and a very humbled ego.
