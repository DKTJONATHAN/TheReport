Just when we thought Ronney Onyango was the next big thing, he decides to pull a disappearing act that would make a toxic ex proud. The Harambee Stars defender has been slapped with a provisional suspension by the Anti-Doping Agency of Kenya (ADAK), and no, it is not because he was caught with a suspicious smoothie. It is because he simply could not be bothered to tell anyone where he was. In the world of professional sports, this is called a "whereabouts failure," but in the real world, we just call it being messy and unprofessional.

Imagine being at the peak of your career, with European scouts supposedly watching your every move, only to faff it all up because you forgot to update your location settings. ADAK requires athletes to be reachable for unannounced tests, but apparently, Ronney was too busy playing the superstar to manage his own schedule. It is the ultimate face-palm moment for Gor Mahia and the national team. While he was busy offering "attacking width" on the pitch, he was clearly offering zero transparency off it.

This whole situation is giving me major flashbacks to other instances of national embarrassment and administrative fumbles. It is the kind of [Roadside Chaos and Digital Delusions: KeNHA enters the chat](/posts/roadside-chaos-and-digital-delusions--kenha-enters-the-chat) that we have come to expect from our local institutions and the stars they produce. Head coach Benni McCarthy must be wondering what he walked into. He is trying to build a squad for the FIFA Series in March 23-31, 2026, and his "dependable" right-back is now sidelined because he could not handle a simple filing requirement.

The timing is almost poetic in its tragedy. There was all this buzz about him moving to big clubs in Europe or even Tanzania, with rumors flying around social media like confetti. Now, the only move he is making is from the starting lineup to the spectator stands. If he gets hit with the full two-year ban, those transfer links will evaporate faster than a celebrity apology after a leaked video. It is a classic case of fumbling the bag right at the finish line when the stakes are highest.

While the technical bench scrambles to find replacements like Daniel Sakari or Alphonce Omija, the rest of us are left wondering why these athletes find it so hard to follow basic rules. It is not rocket science; it is just a form. But I guess when you start believing your own hype, you think you are above the paperwork. ADAK is still reviewing the case, but for now, Ronney’s career is on ice, and the glow-up we were all promised has turned into a total blackout. One thing is for sure: McCarthy has a lot more on his plate than just defensive drills.
