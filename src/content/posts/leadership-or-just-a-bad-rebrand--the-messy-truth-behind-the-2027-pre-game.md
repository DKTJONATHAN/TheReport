Capital FM wants us to believe that March is some holy month for "deliberate conversation" about leadership, but let’s call it what it really is: a high-stakes rebranding exercise for people who are terrified of losing their seats. We are being told to ask if we are being led or just reacting, but the answer is obvious. We are all just extras in a chaotic reality show where the script is written in broken promises and the lighting is paid for by our own taxes. It is all about "visibility" and "direction" now that the 2027 season finale is creeping up on the horizon.

Take the Nairobi Governor’s seat, for instance. It is the ultimate poisoned chalice. Since 2013, the city has chewed up every "big man" who thought they could handle the heat. Now, Moses Kuria is trying to audition for the role of the guy who unseats Johnson Sakaja. It is giving major "Main Character Energy" versus "Desperate Spin-off" vibes. While these guys fight over who gets to wear the crown, the rest of us are dealing with the economic strain of a "lived reality" that looks nothing like their glossy boardrooms. This whole performative circus reminds me of how [NO PHONE, NO CLOUT: IEBC TRIES TO STOP THE TIKTOK-IFICATION OF KENYAN ELECTIONS](/posts/no-phone--no-clout--iebc-tries-to-stop-the-tiktok-ification-of-kenyan-elections) showed us that for our leaders, if it didn’t happen on a screen with a filter, it didn’t happen at all.

Then we have the "mentorship" moments that make my eyes roll into the back of my head. Winnie Odinga is out here telling young girls to "stand firm," which is easy to say when you have a political dynasty as your safety net. It is the peak of "nepo baby" wisdom. Meanwhile, Rigathi Gachagua is busy calling State House a "crime scene" like he wasn’t a lead actor in the pilot episode. The hypocrisy is so thick you could trip over it. They talk about "accountability" and "transparency" like they just discovered the words in a dictionary last night, yet the "outcomes" they keep bragging about are invisible to anyone living outside of a motorcade.

Ruto is currently trying to manifest a 2027 landslide by flirting with ODM strongholds, which is essentially the political version of trying to slide into an ex’s DMs after you already moved out. It is a messy situationship that nobody actually wants to see succeed. The "youth-led civic activism" they keep mentioning isn’t just a "demand for better leadership" - it is a collective scream of boredom and frustration from a generation that has realized the "national conversation" is just a bunch of loud voices with zero solutions. In this town, leadership isn't measured by change; it's measured by how long you can keep the cameras rolling before the public realizes the set is falling apart.
