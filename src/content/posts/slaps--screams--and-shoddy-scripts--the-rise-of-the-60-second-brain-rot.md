If you thought TikTok dances were the peak of brain rot, honey, pull up a chair. South Korea is ditching those high-budget K-dramas for something they call "micro-dramas." It is essentially 60 seconds of a woman getting slapped or a billionaire miraculously waking from a coma, all shot vertically so you do not even have to tilt your wrist. Director Kang Mi-so is out here screaming for "more anger" because apparently, if someone is not catching a stray hand to the face every minute, we will all just scroll away. It is low-budget, high-decibel nonsense designed for people who have the attention span of a goldfish on espresso.

It is the "fast fashion" of the film world, and just like that cheap polyester dress you bought online, it is falling apart at the seams. These shows, like "The Return of the Nation's Heir," are filmed at breakneck speed. We are talking four episodes in four hours. They do not care about "character development" or "logic." They just want to hook you with a cliffhanger every 120 seconds so you will eventually cough up your coins on their apps. It is a digital slot machine disguised as entertainment, and let us be real, the acting is giving high school drama club on a sugar rush.

But here is the real kicker: they are bringing in the robots. These studios are using generative AI to create actors who do not exist and backgrounds that were never built. Why pay a talented human when a computer can spit out a "billionaire" in a fake Ferrari for ten percent of the cost? It is the same kind of desperation we see when legacy media tries to pivot to tech to hide their lack of vision. Honestly, it reminds me of [Old Radios, New Lies: KBC’s AI PR Stunt Won’t Save a Sinking Ship](/posts/old-radios--new-lies--kbc-s-ai-pr-stunt-won-t-save-a-sinking-ship) where they think slapping an AI filter on a dying format will make it fresh. Spoiler alert: it does not.

Fans claim they are "sucked in" because they are "time-poor," but since when did being busy become an excuse for having zero taste? They are literally watching 63 episodes of a man in a wheelchair moving his pinky finger and calling it "art the world needs." If this is the art we need, then the world is officially in the gutter. The CEO of Vigloo is even out here dreaming of an Oscar. Honey, the only thing these shows deserve is a "Least Effort" award and a permanent delete from my algorithm. We are trading Parasite for a one-minute slap-fest, and you all are clapping like it is progress.
