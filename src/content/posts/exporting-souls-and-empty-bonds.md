So now they want us to pay for the privilege of reading about how the government has failed us again. Nation.Africa is asking for a subscription like we have money to throw around in this economy. They want you to renew your "premium access" just to find out that the same people who cannot fix a pothole in Nairobi are in charge of your life when you go to the Middle East. It is a joke that is not even funny anymore.

Alfred Mutua and Edith Okoki were at Bunge Tower this week, looking busy while discussing the decade-long legal lacuna in the migrant labour sector. Imagine that. For ten years, there has been a Sh1.5 million security bond meant to bring home distressed workers or their remains, yet thousands of Kenyans are still stuck in limbo. It is the classic Kenyan way: collect the money first, then figure out why it is not working ten years later while families cry for their kin.

This government loves exporting labour because they have failed to create a single decent job here. They send our sisters away with promises of exclusive stories of success, but the reality is much darker. Whether you are at home or abroad, the state has no interest in your safety. Honestly, when you look at the track record, [The Blue Shroud: Why Police Custody is the Most Dangerous Place in Kenya](/posts/the-blue-shroud--why-police-custody-is-the-most-dangerous-place-in-kenya) is just one part of a larger pattern where being a Kenyan citizen is a high-risk lifestyle choice.

Meanwhile, Gladys Wanga is out here accepting resignations and declaring seats vacant like it is a game of musical chairs. They are all playing for power while the rest of us are fighting over land that the court just dismissed another claim for. Whether it is squatters or private firms, someone is always trying to grab a piece of the soil while the President is busy trying to reassert authority over Mt Kenya. It is all a noisy distraction from the fact that the system is fundamentally broken.

And if you think you can escape the noise by using an ad-blocker, think again. The site will just tell you to disable it and reload. You cannot even enjoy your frustration in peace. It is either you pay with your data, pay with your shillings, or pay with your sanity. Welcome to the Nairobi reality, where even the news is a trap designed to remind you that you are on your own.
