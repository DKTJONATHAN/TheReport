US Rights Group Raises Alarm Over Kenya's Passport Scandal: Are We All Just Pawns in Ruto's Game?

Human rights crusaders are spitting feathers, darling, over Kenya's alleged passport-peddling scheme. Turns out, some seriously shady Sudanese characters, the kind who are apparently bankrolling genocidal nutjobs, are getting their hands on shiny new Kenyan passports. And guess who's staying conspicuously silent? Our own government, that's who. It's almost like they're *trying* to make us look bad.

This whole kerfuffle started when some US-based do-gooders, the Human Rights Foundation, decided to spill the tea on X. They're not just mildly concerned, oh no. They're *alarmed*, apparently linking this whole passport-for-peanuts operation to President Ruto's "controversial alliance" with the very folks accused of war crimes. Suddenly, Kenya's looking less like a beacon of democracy and more like a pit stop for sanctioned warlords. Are we really going to let our nation's credibility get dragged through the mud for pocket change? It makes you wonder if the global order is dying and we're just paying for the funeral, doesn't it?

The juicy details are even juicier. We're talking over 25 Sudanese nationals, including the brother of a big-shot RSF commander who's apparently sanctioned up to his eyeballs for, you know, actual atrocities. And it’s not just a one-off; a leaked list shows passports being churned out for both Sudanese and Zimbabwean nationals with questionable credentials. It's like an episode of "The Real Housewives of International Sanctions Evasion." One brave activist even quipped that they're selling these passports for the "price of peanuts." Honestly, the lack of shame is almost impressive.

And the silence from our own Kenyan Immigration Department, headed by Evelyn Cheluget, and Interior CS Kipchumba Murkomen? Deafening. While Kenyans are rightfully outraged and wondering about the integrity of their own travel documents, the powers that be are playing the silent treatment. This international outcry, however, is likely to crank up the heat, and frankly, I'm here for the drama. Let's see how long they can keep this charade going.

Yet, the immigration department, under Director General Evelyn Cheluget, and the Interior Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen, have offered nothing but a deafening silence. It’s the kind of silence that screams guilt, or at the very least, extreme incompetence. With the Human Rights Foundation now involved, expect the spotlight to intensify.
