MPs, IEBC Plotting More Rules For 2027 Elections, While Ruto Eyes His Second Term

The usual song and dance. Parliamentarians and the electoral commission are off to a retreat, ostensibly to "set new rules" and "timelines" for next year's election. What it really means is more bureaucratic mumbo jumbo and a chance for them to escape Nairobi for a few days. Meanwhile, President Ruto, bless his ambitious heart, is busy consolidating his power, prepping for his re-election bid. Because that's what politicians do - they campaign, even when they're supposed to be governing.

The IEBC, bless their struggling souls, is flapping about deadlines and bills, muttering about how failure to settle these financial matters could "affect its operations." It's the same old story, isn't it? The people meant to ensure a fair contest are begging for funds, while those in power are busy playing their "permanent campaign modes." It's a wonder anything gets done in this country.

And let's not forget the other players in this grand charade. Intelligence officers are apparently also having meetings, planning how to combat money laundering, terrorism, and other criminal activities. One can only hope their plans are more effective than the electoral body's budget management. The IEBC also has the "daunting task" of bringing on new voters. As if convincing existing ones wasn't hard enough.

This whole affair feels like another round of the same old wargames for the elites, with empty pockets for the rest of us. [Wargames for the Elites and Empty Pockets for the Rest](/posts/wargames-for-the-elites-and-empty-pockets-for-the-rest). They'll tweak the rules, make some pronouncements, and then we'll all be back here in a few years, debating the same tired issues.
