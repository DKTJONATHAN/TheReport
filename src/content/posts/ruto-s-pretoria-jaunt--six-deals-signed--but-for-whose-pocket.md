Ruto's Pretoria Jaunt: Six Deals Signed, But For Whose Pocket?

Don't get too excited about those six shiny new agreements between Kenya and South Africa. President Ruto was all smiles in Pretoria, shaking hands and signing papers like there's no tomorrow. But let's be real, how much of this is just hot air and photo ops, and how much will actually trickle down to the mwananchi?

The official line is all about "deepening bilateral ties," "strengthening economic cooperation," and "people to people" stuff. Shipping and maritime, gender equality, vocational training, arts, culture, heritage, sports - sounds lovely, doesn't it? But remember, the last time we heard about "fixing" things online, it turned out to be [France and Kenya Team Up to 'Fix' Online Creeps - But Is It Just for Show?](/posts/france-and-kenya-team-up-to--fix--online-creeps---but-is-it-just-for-show). These deals are probably just more avenues for the usual suspects to make a quick buck.

South Africa, with its 60 plus companies already here, sees us as a good market. And Ruto is talking about unlocking trade through the AfCFTA. That sounds grand, but have you seen the prices of basic goods lately? This "guided trade initiative" sounds like another way to get our raw materials out and finished products back in, at a price that probably won't benefit the average Kenyan.

Ramaphosa is boasting about increased Kenyan exports and how the AfCFTA is "transforming intra-African trade." For whom, exactly? It’s easy to praise these agreements when you're the one holding the purse strings. We’ve seen this song and dance before, especially when it comes to our resources. This feels less like partnership and more like another round of the safari sell out.
