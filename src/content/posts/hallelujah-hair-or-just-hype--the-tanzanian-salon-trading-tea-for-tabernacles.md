Dar es Salaam has officially lost its mind, or maybe it just found a very lucrative way to guilt-trip its clientele into paying more for a basic retouch. Chido Point hair salon has decided that gossiping about who is sleeping with whose husband is "so last year." Instead, they have pivoted to "hair and hymns," turning their Sunday shifts into a full-blown revival meeting. Because nothing says professional hairstyling like a woman with a pair of shears and her eyes closed, praying to the heavens while your edges are at stake.

The viral video making the rounds shows stylists singing their lungs out while customers sit there, presumably wondering if their closure is going to be straight or if the stylist is too busy "being moved" to notice the glue dripping down their forehead. With over 90,000 followers, the salon knows exactly what it is doing. This is not about salvation; it is about the algorithm. One stylist is captured staring at the ceiling like she is seeing a vision of a five-star review, while the bio claims they are a "haven" for those seeking more than just a hair-do. Please, we all know you are just seeking a viral moment.

The internet, being as gullible as ever, is eating it up. Comments sections are filled with people "catching the spirit" through a smartphone screen, praising the salon for replacing "usengenyaji" with the Holy Spirit. But let us be real for a second. If the Holy Spirit is in charge of the salon, does that mean the prices are going down? Or is there a "spirit surcharge" added to the bill? It feels like another case of [The $3 Trillion Mirage: Ruto Sells Continental Dreams While Nairobi Suffers the Ground Reality](/posts/the--3-trillion-mirage--ruto-sells-continental-dreams-while-nairobi-suffers-the-ground-reality), where a shiny, spiritual exterior is sold to mask the mundane reality of life. In this case, the reality is just overpriced braids and a very loud choir in a confined space.

The hypocrisy is what really gets me. These are the same salons where the most scandalous secrets are born, and suddenly, because it is Sunday, everyone is a saint. One stylist looks so immersed in worship that I would be terrified for my scalp. You cannot tell me you are providing "healing" and "prophetic braiding" without a side of clout-chasing. If you want to pray, go to church. If you want a lace front, go to the salon. Mixing the two just feels like a desperate attempt to stay relevant in an industry that is already saturated with fake personalities.

At the end of the day, Chido Point is just the latest example of how religion can be packaged and sold as a lifestyle brand. They are promising to "heal hearts" while they trim split ends. I suppose it is a clever way to keep people from complaining about the wait-times. You can hardly yell at a stylist who is halfway through a chorus of a gospel hit. It is all fun and games until someone gets poked in the eye with a comb because the stylist was "soaking in the spiritual environment" instead of looking at what she was doing. Keep your hymns; I will take my gossip and a straight parting, thanks.
