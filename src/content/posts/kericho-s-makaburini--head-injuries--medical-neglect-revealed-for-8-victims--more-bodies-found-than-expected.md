So, Kericho has decided to join the big leagues of public scandals. Thirty-three bodies, some of them kids, dug up from a hole in the ground. And guess what? Five of them had their skulls bashed in with blunt objects. Blunt objects. Not even a quick, clean shot. This isn't your average Nairobi traffic accident, this is something far more sinister. Dr. Njoroge, the government pathologist, is rattling off causes of death like he's reading a grocery list: choking, pneumonia, infections, blood clots. Sounds like a lovely way to go, doesn't it? Especially for those little ones.

And the body parts? Surgical amputations due to chronic illness, apparently. Just specimens. Because when you're burying people in a mass grave, the last thing you need is proper paperwork, right? The Nyamira County government apparently thought they could just sweep this whole mess under the rug, disposing of bodies without following any procedures. They even tried to pull a fast one with court orders and public notices that didn't add up. It’s enough to make you wonder if the whole system is just one big, corrupt charade.

The DCI is now wading through the muck, looking into extrajudicial killings and even harvesting of body parts. Because when you've got kids with head injuries in a secret grave, what else are you supposed to think? It’s not like this is the first time we've seen the rot beneath the surface. Remember those stories about Forex influencers and their illusion of wealth? This is the real-world consequence of unchecked greed and a disregard for human life.

The law clearly states that deaths need to be registered, and you need a burial permit. But apparently, the Births and Deaths Act is just a suggestion for some people. The fact that bodies were stuffed into gunny bags and transported like sacks of potatoes is just… well, it’s Kenya. Dignity and proper procedure are for people who can afford them, I guess. The Nyamira Public Health Officer and the cemetery caretaker are cooling their heels in custody for 30 days. A drop in the ocean, most likely, but at least it’s something.
