Hilary Duff is out here doing the most to prove she is just as insecure as the rest of us, despite having a Disney-funded bank account. The former teen idol recently admitted she is terrified her husband, Matthew Koma, might trade her in for "some coolio indie songwriter." It is the classic Hollywood paradox: you can have the fame, the family, and the filter-perfect life, but you still wake up wondering if your husband is secretly dreaming of a girl with a banjo and a thrift-store wardrobe. It is relatable in a very "first-world problem" kind of way, but let us be real-if Matthew leaves, it will be for someone with a better curated vinyl collection, not because Hilary is not "cool" enough.

Speaking of people who are definitely not cool, the "Ex-Prince" Andrew has finally found himself in the back of a police van. Arrested on suspicion of misconduct, the royal family’s favorite problem child is facing a reckoning that has been years in the making. While the public is constantly being monitored by tech giants like [Meta who are readying real-time facial recognition](/posts/meta-readies-real-time-facial-recognition-for-smart-glasses--reportedly-timing-launch-with-political-turmoil), it seems the elite can only hide behind palace walls for so long. The police statement is vague, but the optics are crystal clear: the crown cannot protect you when the misconduct finally catches up to the cameras.

Down in South Carolina, we have a missing mom mystery that feels like a rejected script from a mid-tier thriller. She disappeared across state lines and was found days later in a hospital with a list of "unanswered questions" longer than a CVS receipt. While the internet detectives are busy spinning theories about foul play or secret lives, the reality is usually much more grim and messy. Whether it is a mental health break or a desperate attempt to escape the suburban grind, the media circus surrounding these disappearances always manages to ignore the human cost in favor of clicks.

Finally, we have Tyler Perry, the undisputed king of high-budget struggle porn. Season 2 Part 2 of "Beauty in Black" hits Netflix on March 19, and the synopsis is exactly what you would expect. We have backstabbing families, underground trafficking rings, and a sex worker turned business mogul. Perry knows his audience loves a "rags-to-riches-via-trauma" story, and he is delivering all eight episodes right on schedule. It is the same formulaic chaos we have seen a dozen times before, but as long as people keep watching Kimmie call the shots, Tyler will keep building his empire on the backs of fictionalized suffering.
