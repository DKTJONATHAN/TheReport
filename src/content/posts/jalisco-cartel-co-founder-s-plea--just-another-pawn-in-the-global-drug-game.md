So, Érick Valencia Salazar, also known as "El 85" and a co-founder of the Jalisco New Generation Cartel (CJNG), has decided to play nice with the Americans and plead guilty to drug trafficking. The US Department of Justice is crowing about this, naturally. They snagged him in Mexico back in 2022, and now he's conveniently been bundled with other alleged kingpins and shipped to the States for a show trial.

The Drug Enforcement Administration, in their infinite wisdom, declared that Salazar helped build the CJNG into a "ruthless organization that uses violence as a business model." Groundbreaking stuff. Apparently, they've been "murdering for control in Mexico while flooding the United States with poison." Who knew? It's almost as if these cartels are driven by profit, a concept that seems to elude many in positions of power.

His sentencing is set for July 31st, and the mandatory minimum is a whole 10 years. A slap on the wrist, really, for someone who helped orchestrate so much misery. He's admitted to conspiracy to distribute over 5 kilograms of cocaine. Big deal. This is the same cartel that, just recently, decided to paint 20 Mexican states red with violence because their alleged leader, "El Mencho," might have kicked the bucket. Real stability, that.

Let's be clear. This guilty plea is not a victory for humanity; it's a minor operational adjustment for a multi-billion dollar industry. Valencia Salazar is likely just a cog, and a replaceable one at that, in a machine designed for maximum profit through human degradation. While he rots in a US prison for a decade, the pipeline of drugs, violence, and corruption will continue to flow, probably with new faces filling the void he leaves. The real "business model" here is the insatiable demand fueled by addiction and the relentless pursuit of illicit gains, a game that will continue long after "El 85" has faded into obscurity.
