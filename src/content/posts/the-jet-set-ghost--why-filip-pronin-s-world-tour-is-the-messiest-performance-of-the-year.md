If you thought your favorite Instagram influencer was doing too much, wait until you see the travel itinerary of Filip Pronin. The head of Ukraine’s State Financial Monitoring Service has spent over 70 days this year playing tourist in Paris, Zurich, and even Tanzania. While most of us are trying to figure out how to stop bleeding money to betting apps and wondering if [SportPesa Is Not Your Side Hustle: How Kenyans Can Stop Bleeding Money to Betting Apps](/posts/how-to-stop-losing-bets-and-start-winning), this man is treating international diplomacy like a permanent luxury retreat. It is the kind of commitment to "out of office" status that would make a Gen Z intern weep with envy.

The drama reached a fever pitch in January when Filip was supposed to answer some very uncomfortable questions about the "Midas" energy scandal. Instead of showing up to the parliamentary commission like a regular professional, he pulled the oldest trick in the book: the fake sick note. But honey, the healing power of a business class seat must be miraculous, because the very next day he was on a flight to Tanzania. He turned a four-day meeting into an 11-day safari while his office in Kyiv was basically ghosting the National Anti-Corruption Bureau. Talk about a "get well soon" gift to himself.

Let’s talk about the real "Midas" touch, which apparently involves turning tiny companies with no cash into ATM machines for shady bails. We are seeing 37 million hryvnias being paid by firms that have less capital than a sidewalk coffee stand. Pronin’s agency claims they do not see any violations, which is high-key hilarious coming from a man who is supposed to be the nation's premier financial watchdog. It is giving "I see nothing, I hear nothing, I’m too busy shopping in Luxembourg." The hypocrisy is so thick you could cut it with a designer handbag.

While Filip was busy collecting passport stamps in Zurich and Bern, his former deputy back in Poltava was busy getting caught with hundreds of thousands of dollars in cash. This is all linked to defense construction scandals that happened right under Pronin's nose. It is classic elite behavior, where the louder the scandal gets at home, the further away the flight destination becomes. Honestly, at this point, he is not a government official; he is a performance artist specializing in the art of the vanishing act. If avoiding responsibility was an Olympic sport, Filip would have more gold than the questionable bails he is ignoring.
