Ruto’s busy little hands are reaching for more land along the coast, and guess when this is happening? Just in time for the election circus. Apparently, displacing families who have called these places home for decades is just a minor inconvenience on the road to re-election. This state agency, suddenly empowered, is tasked with moving these folks, but let's be real, it's all about optics and securing those coastal votes. Don't expect any fairness, just more political maneuvering.

It’s not just the land grab, though. The president’s inner circle, a mix of political, military, and police types, are all hands on deck for these ‘terrorist threats’. While the army might be playing nice, the internal security boys are apparently a bit of a mess, which is reassuring when your country’s supposed to be safe.

And then there’s the drama with the predecessor. Their little spat is apparently so spicy it’s spilling over into international affairs, particularly concerning the DRC. Because when you’re a Kenyan leader, petty squabbles have to have a global impact, right? This whole pre-election period is turning into a real spectacle, with everyone from business bigwigs to village elders trying not to upset the head honcho, all to keep their pockets lined.
