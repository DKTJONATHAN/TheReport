The previous match against Como highlighted both the resilience and the shortcomings of the Milanese side. After Nico Paz opened the scoring for the visitors in the 32nd minute, it required a second-half effort from Rafael Leao to restore parity. Despite intense pressure from the hosts in the closing stages, the Como defense remained organized, frustrating Milan's attempts to secure a victory.

The fallout from that encounter continues to impact the squad. Manager Massimiliano Allegri will be absent from the touchline tonight, serving a suspension following his protests regarding a contested officiating decision involving Alexis Saelemaekers. Furthermore, the defensive unit will be without Strahinja Pavlovic, who was sidelined during the previous match, though medical assessments suggest he has avoided a long-term injury.

Parma arrives at the San Siro in a position of relative strength, having secured consecutive victories against Bologna and Verona. Currently positioned 13th in the table with 29 points, Carlos Cuesta's side has established an eight-point buffer from the relegation zone. Their confidence is further bolstered by a 2-2 draw achieved against Milan earlier in the season, suggesting they possess the tactical capability to disrupt the hosts' rhythm.

Tactically, both sides are expected to utilize a 3-5-2 formation. For Milan, the partnership of Rafael Leao and Niclas Fullkrug upfront remains the focal point of the attack, supported by a midfield anchored by Luka Modric and Adrien Rabiot. Parma will look to maintain their upward trajectory, relying on the creative output of Bernabe and the defensive solidity of their back three.

As the race for the Italian title enters a critical phase, the outcome of this fixture will determine whether Milan can maintain pressure on Inter or if the gap will become insurmountable.
