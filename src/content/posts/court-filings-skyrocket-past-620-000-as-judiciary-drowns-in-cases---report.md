So, the big news from our "esteemed" Kenya National Bureau of Statistics is that the courts are drowning. 621,525 new cases splashed into the system last financial year. That’s a significant jump from the previous year's 516,121. Apparently, more Kenyans are now officially realizing that the only way to sort out their mess is to throw it at the legal system. Good luck with that.

And get this - while the filings are soaring, the Judiciary is spinning tales about how they're somehow "improving" their case disposal rate. They managed to clear 647,666 cases, up from 509,754. They're even claiming the backlog has shrunk. Sounds impressive on paper, doesn't it? It’s like a doctor bragging about performing more amputations while the number of patients needing them keeps going up. The pressure cooker is just getting hotter.

Meanwhile, the police force seems to be shrinking. The National Police Service is at a ratio of 1:525 civilians, which is a nice way of saying they're spread thinner than cheap tissue. We’re told reported crimes have dropped slightly, but with fewer officers, you have to wonder if people are just giving up on reporting or if the criminals are just getting bolder. The number of illegal firearms recovered did jump, which is a small comfort, I suppose, if you’re not the one getting shot.

It’s the same old story across the board. Police numbers are down, even as the Directorate of Criminal Investigations claims modest growth. And in the prisons? Warders are outnumbered by inmates, and probation officers are juggling so many non-custodial offenders, you’d think they were running a circus. It’s a classic case of demand outstripping supply, and the "rule of law" is looking more like a suggestion. Honestly, sometimes it feels like we're just shuffling deck chairs on the Titanic. It's all connected, isn't it?
