Our Kenyan "heavyweights" are at it again, acting like a nomination for the 43rd edition of the International Reggae and World Music Awards (IRAWMA) is equivalent to a Grammy win. Redsan, who hasn't had a club banger since the early 2000s, is out here calling himself the "baddest" and the "king." He’s nominated for Best African Dancehall Entertainer, a title he seems to think is his birthright. "GOD WINS EVERY TIME," he posted on Instagram, which is basically musician-speak for "I’m nervous because I lost to Shatta Wale last year and I’m terrified it’ll happen again."

The competition this year isn't just "stiff" - it is a brick wall. Redsan is facing off against Zimbabwe’s Winky D and the reigning champion Shatta Wale, who actually has a fanbase that does more than just double-tap on thirst traps. Then you have Sheebah Karungi bringing the heat from Uganda. Redsan is hoping to overturn his "near miss" from last year, but let's be honest, unless he’s been hiding some secret talent under those cargo pants, he’s just there to make up the numbers.

Then we have Willy Paul, the man who changes musical genres more often than he changes his socks. Pozze is nominated for Best Afrobeat Entertainer, which is hilarious considering he spent half his career telling us he was a gospel artist. He is up against Diamond Platnumz, who is basically a god in Tanzania, and Tyla, who is currently the darling of the international scene. While these stars are chasing plastic trophies to prove they still matter, some people are actually building legacies. For example, [Joseph Mutati: The Quiet Mwingi Crypto Genius Who Built Flitaa While Forex Gurus Faked Ferraris](/posts/joseph-mutati-mwingi-crypto-genius) proves that real moves are made in silence, not through desperate "link in bio" captions.

Nigeria is also bringing the heat with Flavour N'abania and Mr. P, who actually know how to perform without relying on a backing track that’s louder than their own voices. Willy Paul has "electric stage presence," sure, if by electric you mean jumping around and hoping no one notices he’s lip-syncing. Being in the same category as Tyla is a win in itself for Pozze, but let’s not pretend he has a snowball’s chance in hell of taking that trophy home to Saldido Records.

At the end of the day, IRAWMA has been around for four decades, trying to bridge the gap between Jamaica and Africa. It’s a prestigious platform, but our local stars use it as a temporary ego boost. They’ll post the "Vote for Me" graphics for two weeks, and if they lose, they’ll go back to posting cryptic messages about how the industry is rigged and how they are "too big" for local awards anyway. It’s the same old script, just a different year. 

Whether Redsan finally beats Shatta Wale or Willy Paul gets humbled by Diamond, one thing is for sure: the drama on social media will be far more entertaining than the actual awards ceremony. We are waiting to see who will be the first to blame "haters" when the results don't go their way. Grab your popcorn, because the entitlement is about to hit an all-time high.
