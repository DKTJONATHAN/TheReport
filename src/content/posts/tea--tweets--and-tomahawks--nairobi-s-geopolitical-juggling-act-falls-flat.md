Ruto is back on X, sounding like he is the UN Secretary General while the world literally burns. He is talking about international peace and security because it costs nothing to tweet from a climate-controlled office in State House. But for those of us on the ground in Nairobi, we know this isn't about world peace - it is about the fact that our tea has nowhere to go. First, Sudan stopped buying our leaves because we couldn't stop flirting with the RSF, and now the Middle East is turning into a bonfire that threatens to incinerate our remaining balance sheets.

Let us look at the strategic partnership with Iran that was hyped up in 2023. We were bragging about an eight-fold increase in tea exports to Tehran like we had discovered a gold mine. Now, with the US and Israel taking out Iranian bigwigs like they are characters in a bad action movie, that strategic move looks more like a gamble gone wrong. It is the same old story of Nairobi trying to eat from every plate at the buffet and ending up with a massive stomach ache when the bill arrives.

The UAE deal signed in early 2025 was supposed to be our golden ticket to the big leagues. But when missiles are flying across the Gulf, a piece of paper signed in a fancy room in Abu Dhabi doesn't mean much. We have thousands of Kenyans working over there, sending back the remittances that keep this economy from flatlining. If the region goes dark, those workers come home to a country that can't even fix its own potholes, as we saw with the [roadside chaos and digital delusions](/posts/roadside-chaos-and-digital-delusions--kenha-enters-the-chat) of our local infrastructure experts who prefer PR to pavement.

It is almost funny to hear our leaders preach about indispensable frameworks for peace. This is the same government that seems to think a well-worded post on social media solves a global crisis. While they play at being global diplomats, the reality is that we are just a small fish in a very shark-infested pond. We talk about diversifying partnerships, but all we are doing is diversifying the ways we can get caught in the crossfire of other people's ancient grudges.

In the end, this is just more proof that Kenyan foreign policy is a mix of desperation and high-end PR. We are essentially sending our people to be [Moscow’s newest plumbing project](/posts/moscow-s-newest-plumbing-project--kenyan-bodies-on-the-front-line) while simultaneously worrying about Iranian drone strikes hitting our trade routes. It is a mess. We are a tea superpower with no customers and a diplomatic hub that can't even keep its own backyard clean. Ruto can keep his X posts; the rest of us are just waiting for the price of unga to go up again because of a war we have no business in.
