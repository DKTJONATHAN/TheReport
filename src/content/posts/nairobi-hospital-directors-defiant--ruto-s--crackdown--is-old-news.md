Ruto's Nairobi Hospital 'Crackdown' Is Just More Theatre

WARNING: Another day, another headline meant to make you think something's changing. It's the same old song and dance, just with different names. Don't expect much to be different in the end.

Nairobi Hospital directors are playing brave, or maybe just broke, after a little drama in court.

Politics. It always has to be politics, doesn't it? Dr. Wanyoike, bless his naive heart, thinks this whole thing has gone "political." As if. This is Kenya, mboya. Everything is political. And when it involves money and people in high places, it’s always a show.

So, we've got Dr. Job Lukuru Obwaka, 80 years old, Dr. Chris Bichange Nyamaratandi, lawyer Samson Mbuthia Kinyanjui, and Dr. Valerie Akinyi Gaya. They all denied their charges, of course. Who pleads guilty in this country, eh? Especially when there's Sh5 million bond to be had for each of them. They're accused of trousering over Sh8 million from some insurer. Sounds about right. The board chairman, his vice, and the ex-chairman are apparently cooling their heels, at least for now. It’s a real page-turner, almost as gripping as figuring out why [Kenya's National Team is Scavenging on Facebook Marketplace](/posts/from-fife-with-love--why-is-kenya-s-national-team-scavenging-on-facebook-marketplace).

This whole "crackdown" narrative is just another way to keep us distracted. Like all those pronouncements from State House, they're designed to make you feel like things are being handled. Meanwhile, the usual suspects get their day in court, deny everything, get out on bail, and life goes on for them. For the rest of us? We just get to watch the spectacle and wonder if any of it actually sticks. It’s all a bit of a performance, isn't it? Keeps the peasants entertained.
