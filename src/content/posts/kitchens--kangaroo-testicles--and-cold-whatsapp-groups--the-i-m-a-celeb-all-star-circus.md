Scarlett Moffatt is back in the jungle, or the "bush" in South Africa, but let’s be real - she isn’t there for the personal growth. While most reality stars spin some tired yarn about "testing their limits," Scarlett hit us with the only honest thing to ever come out of a celebrity's mouth: she wants a new kitchen. Ten years after Gogglebox made her a household name for literally sitting on a sofa, she is back to eating kangaroo genitals just to pay the contractor. At least someone is admitting the industry is just one big, shiny mortgage-payment scheme.

Of course, the "all-star" camaraderie is already showing its cracks before the first episode even airs. At the Ham Yard Hotel launch, the ever-energetic Sam Thompson tried to play nice by asking about the cast WhatsApp group. The silence was probably deafening. Scarlett dropped the act, admitting that the "social bubble" has already burst. We all know how this works - they film the show, pretend to be soulmates for the cameras, and then hit "Leave Group" the second they land at Heathrow. The South African backdrop is becoming quite the hotspot for these staged displays of celebrity bonding, much like how [Tems’ Leading Vibe Initiative Heads To South Africa: Empowerment Or Just Another High-End Hennessy Ad?](/posts/tems--leading-vibe-initiative-heads-to-south-africa--empowerment-or-just-another-high-end-hennessy-ad) lately.

Then there is the arrival of the "GC" herself. Gemma Collins is reportedly ready to erupt, warning her fellow campmates that she is "not a joke." It is the same old script, isn't it? Bring in a professional pot-stirrer to ensure the ratings don't plummet into the South African dirt. Scarlett played coy about the drama, but with Gemma involved, we know the "peaks and troughs" she mentioned are mostly just people screaming over who gets the last bit of rice while praying for a viral moment that will lead to a PrettyLittleThing deal.

Ant and Dec are busy selling us the "hardest series ever" narrative, bragging about a five-hour eating challenge that sounds more like a human rights violation than entertainment. Adam Thomas and the rest of the crew are echoing the company line, insisting this trip to South Africa was much tougher than their first stints. But let’s be honest - the only thing "tougher" this time around is the competition for those dwindling ITV endorsements. They are all "strong characters," which is just code for "people who won't stop talking until they get their own spin-off."

In the end, the public gets to vote for a "legend," a title that has lost all meaning in a world where being a legend just means you survived a decade of being C-list. Whether it is Scarlett’s kitchen or Gemma’s ego, the motivations are as transparent as a glass house. They will scream, they will cry, and they will pretend to be friends for the reunion special, all while checking their bank balances to see if the kangaroo testicles were worth the renovation costs. It is messy, it is desperate, and I will be watching every second of it.
