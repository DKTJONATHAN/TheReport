If you thought the 2027 elections would offer a real choice, I have a bridge in Kajiado to sell you. President Ruto and the remains of ODM are now calling their upcoming political marriage friendly fire. It is a cute term for a cartel agreement where they pretend to fight over MCA seats while sharing the big plate at State House. This broad-based government nonsense is just a rebranding of the old-school Nusu Mkate system, only this time, they are not even trying to hide the greed.

They claim this arrangement is for national unity, but we all know it is about job security for the political class. When Ruto says he will accommodate losers in government, he is basically telling us to prepare for a bloated wage bill. It is the ultimate scam, not unlike the desperate moves we see in the [From Nairobi Hustle to Russian Meat Grinders: The Ultimate Scammer Season](/posts/from-nairobi-hustle-to-russian-meat-grinders--the-ultimate-scammer-season) where people sell their souls for a promise of a better life that never arrives. We are being asked to cheer as they create more positions for their friends while the rest of us struggle with the cost of living.

Look at Junet Mohamed and the rest of the ODM brigade. They are now bragging about how they would have taken 60 percent of the government if Raila was around. It was never about the rights of the common person; it was always about the percentage of the tender cake. They have moved from the streets to the boardroom, and they are not planning to leave the warmth of the government cold anytime soon. They are negotiating as equal partners to ensure that no matter who you vote for, the same faces will be laughing all the way to the bank.

The promises of billions for markets and hostels in Kajiado are just the usual seasoning for the main course of lies. We have heard about historical land injustices being solved every election cycle since 1963. Now, we are told that friendly fire will strengthen democracy. In reality, it just means the opposition has been effectively neutralized and bought off. Democracy in Kenya has become a private members club where the entrance fee is your loyalty to the two big men at the top.

As we approach 2027, the script is already written. The electorate will be given a scorecard that looks more like a work of fiction, and the losers will be accommodated in some newly created office. It is a closed loop of political survival that leaves the taxpayer as the only one actually getting burnt by this fire. If this is what national unity looks like, then we might as well admit that the only thing being united is the appetite of our leaders.
