So, the government wants *our* input on this new AI thing. "Trusted and inclusive AI ecosystem," they chirp. Right. Because when has anything the government touches ever been truly trusted or inclusive for the average Kenyan? They're probably just looking for more data to mine, another way to track us, while pretending to be all about progress.

Eng John Tanui, the Principal Secretary for ICT and Digital Economy, is all smiles and promises. "Contributions received from Kenyans will play a critical role." Sure they will. Just like our "contributions" to that last election or that last infrastructure project. We fill out the forms, we tick the boxes, and then they do whatever they were going to do anyway. It's a performance, plain and simple.

Meanwhile, "over 884,000 Kenyan TikTok videos removed in three months over violations." That's the real "digital economy" they're interested in - policing what we say and do online. They can’t even manage to keep that under control without a mass deletion, but they expect us to believe they’ll build some utopian AI future? It’s a joke.

And let’s not forget the backdrop to all this. We’ve got the Grammy Awards President talking to Ruto, probably about how to make music sound more artificial, while down on the ground, families are crying foul. Remember that Kitengela family? [CCTV Shows Businessman Abducted](/posts/kitengela-family-cries-foul--produce-him-in-court--cctv-shows-businessman-abducted) - that’s the reality of our "digital economy," isn't it? Abductions and ransom demands, while they’re busy drafting policies about AI.

So, by all means, send in your proposals by August 4. Tell them how you want your future robots to treat you. Just don't expect them to listen. They've got their own agenda, and it probably involves more control, more data, and less actual benefit for us. The elite’s immunity remains intact, while our lives are devalued.

This whole AI policy consultation is just another piece of theatre. A way to tick a box, claim they're being democratic, and then proceed with the same old song and dance. We’ll give them our ideas, they’ll file them away, and then the AI will arrive in Kenya, probably with a hefty price tag and a few more loopholes for the connected few.
