Garissa's Ghostly Payroll: Sh731m Vanishes as "Workers" Keep Cashing In

This report exposes a colossal payroll fraud in Garissa County, where 321 "ghost workers" with no personnel files managed to pocket over Sh731 million, thriving even after a state audit. It highlights systemic failures in government human resource systems, allowing for the continued, unverified issuance of Unique Personal Numbers (UPN) and the siphoning of taxpayer money. The Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC) is now recommending prosecutions, but the sheer scale of the alleged theft points to deep-rooted corruption.

The audacity of it all. Garissa County, a place where the government apparently thinks money grows on trees, has managed to dish out over Sh731 million to people who don't exist. We're talking about 321 names, nothing more than digits in a bank account, no files, no proof they ever showed up for work, not even a whiff of a recruitment letter. And get this, after an audit in 2022 *expunged* 1,315 ghost workers - a quarter of the payroll, mind you - the problem just came back with a vengeance. It’s like trying to get rid of cockroaches; you squash some, and more crawl out from the woodwork, probably with better connections.

What’s really infuriating is how this whole charade is being pulled off. The National Treasury’s shiny new digital system, the GHRIS-UPN, meant to track everything, has become the perfect tool for these thieves. Officials just request these "Unique Personal Numbers" with zero supporting documents. No ID, no certificates, no appointment letter. It's like saying you own a plot of land just because you can draw a picture of it. Apparently, the system can't even trace who requested, verified, or approved these phantom numbers for 237 of the "workers." This isn't just incompetence; it's a deliberate dismantling of accountability, making it easier for the rot to spread. It reminds me of the way some people in power treat their responsibilities, [like DCI’s tea and mandazi seminars won’t scrub our dirty money clean](https://example.com/posts/dci-s-tea-and-mandazi-seminars-won-t-scrub-our-dirty-money-clean).

The numbers themselves are staggering. Sh731,267,863 between September 2022 and June 2024. That’s enough to build a decent hospital, or at least fund a few more "audits" that ultimately change nothing. The EACC is finally recommending prosecutions for at least 16 individuals, citing abuse of office and financial misconduct. Good luck with that. These are the same kinds of charges that get tossed around like confetti during election season, and most of the time, nothing sticks. You have to wonder how many of these "suspects" are still collecting their paychecks while the EACC "investigates."

And it’s not just Garissa, oh no. The report casually drops that Nairobi, Nyeri, Siaya, Elgeyo Marakwet, and Nakuru counties are also "biggest culprits." So, while Garissa is busy bleeding Sh731 million to the wind, other counties are likely playing similar games. It’s a national pastime, this stealing from the public purse. We are the ones footing the bill, working hard, paying taxes, only to see our money disappear into the pockets of corrupt officials and their imaginary employees.

The governor, Mr. Jama, who was re-elected in 2022, apparently requested the initial audit because salaries were consuming a whopping 60% of Garissa’s revenue. And what was the result? More ghost workers, more money gone. He's now conveniently "declining to comment." Of course, he is. It's easier to keep your mouth shut when you're complicit, or when you know the system is so broken that even if you wanted to fix it, you’d need a miracle. This whole "scandal" is just another Tuesday in Kenya.
