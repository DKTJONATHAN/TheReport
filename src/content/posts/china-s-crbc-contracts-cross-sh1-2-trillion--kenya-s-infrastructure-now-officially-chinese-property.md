China's CRBC Contracts Cross Sh1.2 Trillion: Kenya's Infrastructure Now Officially Chinese Property

Be prepared to kiss your inheritance goodbye, because Kenya's infrastructure is officially on the Chinese menu, and the bill is well over Sh1.2 trillion. The latest dish? Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA), being "modernised" by the China Road and Bridge Corporation (CRBC). This isn't just about a new terminal; it's about cementing CRBC's grip, adding another massive project to their collection which already includes the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) and that glorified traffic jam, the Nairobi Expressway.

Transport and Roads Cabinet Secretary Davis Chirchir, with a straight face, announced that CRBC outbid over 40 firms for the Sh154.2 billion JKIA deal. The plan is to boost passenger capacity from a measly 7.5 million to a whopping 22 million. This whole "Look East" policy is really paying off, isn't it? China’s been the go-to for every big-money project, funded by loans that probably come with more strings attached than a cheap guitar. It's the Public Private Partnership model, which basically means investors fund it, build it, and then milk it dry for years to recoup their money. Moja Expressway is already set to maintain the Nairobi Expressway for 27 years. Twenty-seven years! We'll be paying for that monstrosity until our grandchildren are collecting their pensions.

This JKIA deal isn't a surprise. Just nine months ago, CRBC snagged the Sh170 billion Nairobi-Nakuru-Mau Summit toll highway contract, a project previously awarded to French firms before President Ruto decided their Sh190 billion price tag was too much. Suddenly, CRBC’s Sh170 billion seemed like a bargain, didn't it? It's all about that sweet, sweet Chinese financing. Remember the SGR? That $5.08 billion (Sh658.1 billion) behemoth was fully funded by loans from the China Export-Import Bank. It's a pattern we've seen since Mwai Kibaki's era, with CRBC becoming the undisputed king of Kenyan mega-projects. It makes you wonder if our own contractors are just playing dress-up.

The audacity of it all is almost impressive. Parliament tried to throw a bone to local firms by proposing to limit foreign companies bidding on contracts below Sh20 billion, but that got shot down faster than a cheap drone. Apparently, Kenyan firms just "can't afford" to deliver high-value contracts. So, they’re left to compete with the Chinese giants for scraps. It’s a cycle of state incompetence that never ends, a bit like those endless road constructions. Even the Sh38.8 billion Galana dam, initially meant to be a PPP, is now being handed over to CRBC under an EPCF framework. Because why not give them more?

And it's not just roads and airports. CRBC is building the Sh46 billion Talanta Stadium, meant to host the 2027 African Cup of Nations. We're co-hosting, so naturally, we need a massive new stadium. Then there's the Sh38.8 billion Galana dam, spreading their influence into agriculture. The PPP model has become their golden ticket, allowing their subsidiaries to operate projects for decades, ensuring they recoup their investment and then some. It's a well-oiled machine, and we're just the grease.

The Chinese firm’s growing influence was further exhibited when it snatched the Nairobi-Nakuru-Mau Summit highway project from a consortium of French firms, highlighting China’s deepening reach in Kenya’s grand infrastructural projects. It's a clear sign that the "Look East" policy is more like a "Look to China and Do Whatever They Say" policy. Meanwhile, we're left with the debt and the ongoing feeling that we're just passengers in our own development story.
