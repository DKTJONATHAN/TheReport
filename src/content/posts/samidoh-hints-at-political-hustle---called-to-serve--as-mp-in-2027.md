So, Samidoh Muchoki, the mugithi chap who’s been serenading us with tales of love and… well, more love, is now eyeing parliament. Apparently, the siren song of elective office is too loud to ignore. He's been cozying up to Rigathi Gachagua, who, as we all know, is busy setting up his own little political kingdom with the Democracy for the Citizens Party (DCP). Because clearly, what Kenyan politics needs is more musicians and fewer people who actually understand how to manage a budget.

This DCP outfit, launched with much fanfare in 2025, is all about "youth empowerment," reserving a quarter of their tickets for young hopefuls. Sounds noble, right? Except when your star recruit is a musician whose primary qualification seems to be knowing the right people. Samidoh himself has been dropping hints for ages, and now he’s gone and plastered his face on a DCP poster, urging us all to register to vote. As if we needed more reason to be wary of the whole process, especially after seeing how easily some folks get caught up in debt traps trying to live the "soft life" advertised everywhere.

His fans are apparently clamoring for him to run, and his latest social media stunt with a green tick next to his name basically screams "MP incoming." It's all very strategic, very calculated. The man even admitted that "everything in life is taking a risk." Oh, really, Samidoh? Like the risk of spending public funds on more roadside anthems instead of essential services? Or the risk of us, the taxpayers, realizing we’ve elected another celebrity with no real plan beyond a catchy tune?

This whole "Called to serve" narrative is as old as time. First, they entertain us, then they want to govern us. It’s a classic Kenyan playbook. From drivers ending up in Russian trenches to the latest political hopefuls, it’s always a hustle. Let’s just hope Samidoh’s parliamentary ambitions are more substantial than his song lyrics, because frankly, our country deserves better than another glorified karaoke session in the august house.
