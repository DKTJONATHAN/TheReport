Just when you thought Nairobi couldn't get more chaotic, the Kenya Civil Aviation Authority (KCAA) has decided to gaslight the entire internet. Literally. They just confirmed that the petrol station popping up under JKIA's flight path along Mombasa Road is "perfectly fine." Because nothing says aviation safety like a giant tank of flammable liquid right where planes are trying to touch down. It is giving very much "we need the money, ignore the physics" energy, and frankly, we are exhausted.

The KCAA issued a statement late Monday basically telling us to pipe down. They claim the project underwent a "full technical review" and meets all safety regulations. It is the same kind of rehearsed nonsense we see when influencers try to explain away their latest scam. We are supposed to believe that building a gas station on a flight path is a stroke of genius rather than a disaster waiting to happen. The audacity is honestly sky-high, and the "safety protocols" they keep mentioning feel more like suggestions than rules.

While the regulator is busy preaching about "established aviation safety protocols," the rest of us are left wondering whose pockets are getting lined this time. It is all part of the same messy system where the elite play by different rules while the rest of us struggle. Whether it is questionable construction or [The High-Cost of Staged Smiles and Lakeside Revolutions](/posts/the-high-cost-of-staged-smiles-and-lakeside-revolutions), the script remains the same: look the other way while the big players make their moves. Activists are already pointing fingers at the elite interests involved, and honestly, can we blame them?

Social media has been buzzing with concerns, but KCAA is standing its ground. They want us to believe their "detailed evaluation process" is foolproof. Honey, we have seen "foolproof" plans in this city turn into flooded basements and crumbling walls within a week. If you are flying into JKIA soon, maybe say a quick prayer that the pilot does not decide to take a "pit stop" at the pump before hitting the tarmac. The "technical review" excuse is the ultimate PR shield for things that simply do not make sense to anyone with a functioning brain.

At the end of the day, this is just another Tuesday in the 254. We get told that up is down, fire is water, and a gas station on a runway approach is just "standard urban development." Stay cynical, Nairobi. Because if we do not laugh at the sheer stupidity of these "approved" projects, we will definitely be crying when the first "unexpected" incident makes the headlines. It is all fun and games until the "technical review" meets reality at 200 miles per hour.
