Darling, just when you thought the Kenyan government couldn't get any messier, they go and drop the ball on literal infants. Health CS Aden Duale-who usually spends his time looking stern for the cameras-is now telling us that the Rotavac vaccine is basically out of stock. Apparently, the manufacturer in India, Bharat Biotech, is upgrading its facility. Isn't that just the most convenient excuse? It’s giving "I'm taking a social media hiatus to find myself" vibes when you actually just ran out of content and money.

The Ministry of Health is acting like this is some minor glitch, but honey, they only have 4,000 doses left. That’s enough to last less than a month for the entire country. Meanwhile, they expect us to wait until the end of April for a shipment that was supposed to be here in January. It’s the same old story of poor planning wrapped in a shiny press release. It reminds me of [The State House Handshake Circus: New Envoys, Same Old Problems](/posts/the-state-house-handshake-circus--new-envoys--same-old-problems) where we see big smiles and zero actual results for the common person.

And let’s talk about the advice they’re giving parents. Wash your hands? Use safe water? Maintain a clean household? Oh, please. If the government could provide the basics, maybe we wouldn't be so reliant on a single manufacturer’s upgrades. It is the ultimate gaslight to tell people to "strengthen hygiene" because you failed to secure the one thing that actually prevents the rotavirus drama. It's like a celebrity makeup artist telling you to "just have better skin" after they lose their entire kit and ruin your look.

Duale says protecting children is his "highest priority," but the math just isn't mathing. While the elites are busy with their high-end events and political maneuvering, mothers in the villages are going to be told to come back tomorrow at the clinic. It’s a classic case of the people in power being completely out of touch with the reality of the streets. Stay tuned, because this "temporary disruption" is likely to last longer than a typical celebrity marriage.
