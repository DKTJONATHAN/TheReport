The narrative that Bitcoin acts as a hedge against global conflict is a convenient lie sold to those looking for an exit from the burning building of traditional finance. Even as tensions flare in the Middle East, the token remains down forty percent from its previous heights. It is a parasitic passenger on the back of global instability, rising only because the alternatives are being systematically destroyed by war and incompetence.

Bloomberg and its ilk sell the dream of a dynamic network of information, yet the fine print reveals the true cost of entry. To participate in this information economy is to surrender every scrap of privacy to third parties and arbitration clauses. We are not just trading coins; we are trading our digital autonomy for the privilege of watching our purchasing power evaporate in a slightly more modern way than our ancestors did.

This entire spectacle is another circus for the hungry to watch the broke. Much like the government loyalty schemes used to buy support with a grandchildren-tax, crypto is the new way to extract value before the next generation even arrives. It is a global mechanism for wealth redistribution that provides the illusion of choice while keeping the participants firmly locked within the established corridors of power and surveillance.
