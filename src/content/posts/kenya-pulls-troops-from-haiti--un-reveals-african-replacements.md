NAIROBI'S FINEST OUT OF HAITI, AFRICAN REPLACEMENTS JUST ANOTHER SHOW

WARNING: Kenyan troops are heading home from Haiti, but don't expect any miracles. The UN is just shuffling the deck, bringing in more African forces to deal with a mess that's likely to get worse before it gets better.

"Another deployment, another photo op. At least they're home for good this time."

CRIME
Haiti, Kenya, UN

ARRIVAL

So, the brave Kenyan police officers, the ones we sent to play peacekeepers in Haiti, are finally coming back. Good for them. Saw them being greeted like heroes at JKIA, Inspector General and all. Apparently, it's all part of some "major restructuring" of this multinational mission. What a surprise. They’re bringing in troops from Chad now. Because apparently, the "Gang Suppression Force" needs a fresh batch of faces to… well, to suppress gangs. As if that's worked so far.

This whole transition is being spun by the UN as a way to "strengthen Haiti’s capacity." Yeah, right. It’s more like a fancy musical chairs game with international security. Our guys get phased out as the new lot, fresh from training in the US, get their turn. The Ministry of Interior is all proud, announcing the "safe return" of the second contingent. Safe return. Meanwhile, Haiti is still a hot mess.

The UN is planning to have this "restructured force" fully in place by October, with numbers going up to 5,500. Enhanced powers, they say, including arrests. Because that's what Haiti needs, more arrests. Not actual solutions, just more people with badges and guns. This is starting to sound like one of those convoluted political dramas we sometimes see back home, like [Sifuna’s Kakamega Tour: High Drama, Low Protocol, and a Smashed Windscreen](/posts/sifuna-s-kakamega-tour--high-drama--low-protocol--and-a-smashed-windscreen).

Even a UN Human Rights Expert, Mr. O’Neill, is throwing out warnings. Critical months ahead, he says, but there's a "window for improvement." Oh, joy. A "window." For improvement. As if this is just a leaky faucet and not a collapsing country. He’s optimistic that if the international community acts swiftly, the gangs, while disruptive, are not insurmountable. Swiftly? We’re talking about the international community here, the same one that seems to take its sweet time with everything that actually matters.

The original headline is a bit too cheerful, calling it an "African Replacement" like it's some kind of upgrade. Let's be clear, this is just Africa taking over a problem that other continents couldn't, or wouldn't, solve properly. It's a badge of honor for some, I suppose, but for the people stuck in Haiti, it’s just more of the same, different flags.
