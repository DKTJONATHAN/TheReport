So, the UN agency IFAD is "opening an audit" into Sh1.55 billion that vanished from a rural development project in Kenya. Imagine that. Funds meant for small-scale farmers, siphoned by senior Treasury officials for personal gain. It's almost predictable, isn't it? The same old song and dance: international partners pump money in, local hands find a way to redirect it to their pockets, and then, much later, someone starts asking questions.

The Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC) already did the legwork, pointing out the "weak management systems" - a polite way of saying the gates were wide open for plunder. Sh799.84 million withdrawn in cash by a single accountant, Billy Otieno Obango, after the project, PROFIT, had officially closed in 2019. Think about that: the project was done, the oversight "ceased," and *then* the real harvesting began. Properties bought in Nairobi, Machakos, Uasin Gishu. This isn't just opportunistic; it's calculated.

These officials, like Obango and Gladys Julliet Chepkarat, didn't just walk away with the leftovers. The EACC claims they cooked up fake payment vouchers and even opened new bank accounts in PROFIT's name *after* it ended, to keep the money flowing. It's a masterclass in exploiting the system, a system often touted to help Kenyans, much like the endless talk of [Ruto's MSMEs, Youth Bet On Kenya's Industrial Future: Promises, Promises.](/posts/ruto-s-msmes--youth-bet-on-kenya-s-industrial-future--promises--promises). The High Court has frozen their assets, but how many other silent beneficiaries are out there, hiding in plain sight?

IFAD, meanwhile, offers its standard corporate platitudes: "zero-tolerance approach to fraud," "ensuring resources are used for intended purposes." Empty words, really, when billions keep disappearing from projects they fund. They've committed billions more to Kenya for food security and climate change. One has to wonder, at what point does "zero-tolerance" become "zero-impact" on the actual problem? The cycle continues, and ordinary Kenyans bear the cost.
