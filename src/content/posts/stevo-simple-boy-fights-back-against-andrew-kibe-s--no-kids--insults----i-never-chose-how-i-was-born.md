So, Stevo Simple Boy, the guy who made "ugali" a national talking point, has finally decided to bark back at Andrew Kibe. Apparently, Kibe, in his infinite wisdom, thinks people like Stevo shouldn't be procreating. As if Kibe himself is some paragon of virtue. This is Nairobi, folks, where everyone's got an opinion, especially when it comes to who gets to pass on their genes.

Stevo, bless his simple heart, took to Instagram to explain that Kibe should keep his beef with him and leave the kid out of it. He's got a point, I suppose. No one asked for Kibe's parenting advice, and his pronouncements are about as useful as a screen door on a submarine. Stevo claims he chose to be a good person and work hard, which is, you know, nice. But let's be real, "choosing" to be a good person is a luxury some of us can't afford when we're busy trying to make rent.

He’s going on about all children being a blessing and how no child deserves to be judged. While I appreciate the sentiment, Stevo, this is Kenya. We judge everyone. Especially when they’re in the public eye. And frankly, between Kibe’s loudmouth pronouncements and Stevo’s latest outburst, this whole saga is more entertaining than watching [Darrien Luke’s 'WOZA': A Brave Cultural Fusion or Just Another Durban Cliché?](/posts/darrien-luke-s--woza---a-brave-cultural-fusion-or-just-another-durban-clich).

Stevo says he’s going to keep moving forward and leave judgment to God. Cute. In Nairobi, we leave judgment to the comment section, and trust me, it’s a lot more brutal and a lot faster. This entire drama is just another Tuesday. People saying outrageous things, others getting offended, and the rest of us watching for the show. God’s got bigger things to worry about than who Kibe thinks should be allowed to have babies.
