Ruto's Global Housing Summit: More Hot Air Than Affordable Bricks

WARNING: President Ruto jets off to Azerbaijan, not to build houses, but to talk about building houses. Expect a lot of fancy talk and zero concrete progress for Kenyans struggling for shelter.

A well-dressed man on his way to talk about the problems of the poor.





So, the President has jetted off to some far-flung place called Azerbaijan. For what, you ask? To go and *champion* Kenya's "affordable housing programme." Apparently, we're the global leaders now. I suppose that’s one way to spin the fact that most Kenyans can barely afford a cardboard box, let alone a "programme." UN-Habitat even invited us, recognizing our "leadership and success." Success in what? In making promises? In collecting deductions from salaries for houses nobody can afford?

State House Spokesperson Hussein Mohamed, bless his optimistic heart, issued a statement saying this World Urban Forum is where governments and investors will "deliberate on solutions to the growing global housing crisis." Deliberate. Yes, that's what they do best. Sit in air-conditioned rooms, sip fancy coffee, and deliberate while the actual crisis rages on. Our President is expected to "showcase the impact" of this housing programme. The impact? For whom? The consultants? The developers? Certainly not for the average mwananchi whose rent keeps going up while their salary stays put.

This whole thing smells of another foreign trip disguised as national duty. They want to "mobilise partnerships and investments" to "expand and scale" this programme. Translation: they want more money from abroad to pour into a system that’s already as leaky as a sieve. They’re even going to talk about the "Nairobi Rivers Regeneration Programme." Now, *that's* a programme that needs showcasing - maybe for its ability to attract flies and its contribution to the city’s distinct aroma. It's like they're polishing a turd and hoping the world buys the shine.

And the cherry on top? Ruto is attending as the "global champion for adequate housing." Global champion. Right. This reminds me of how some of these global scrambles for resources, like the [Congo Cobalt Spill Shines Light on Dirty Global Scramble](/posts/congo-cobalt-spill-shines-light-on-dirty-global-scramble), end up benefiting a select few while the masses get nothing but the crumbs. Let's hope this trip doesn't result in more bullet shells and bloodshed, metaphorically speaking, for Kenyans who are already struggling. It’s all talk, no walk, and definitely no bricks.
