Kenya Minister Says US Ebola Facility Plans Halted After Court Order, Because Nairobi Knew Better

WARNING: Government's Ebola quarantine facility drama continues. Protests, deaths, and a health minister playing catch up to reality. Expect more chaos.

US-run Ebola quarantine facility plans in Kenya are finally on hold, after the Health Minister decided to, you know, acknowledge the existence of a court order. Apparently, his deep contemplation of the situation took him all the way to being held in contempt. Classic Nairobi.

The entire circus surrounding this US-backed Ebola quarantine facility at Laikipia airbase has been a masterclass in government incompetence. We’re talking protests, three people dead, and a health minister who seemingly only functions when dragged before a judge. The idea was to house potential US citizens evacuated from the DRC, a country already drowning in this mess. Why anyone thought this was a good idea here, especially with our own health system looking more fragile than a paper bag in a storm, is beyond me. It’s like they *want* to import problems.

Of course, the Americans had to throw some money at it, a cool $13.5 million for “preparedness.” Sounds nice, doesn't it? But for us on the ground, it just smells like a very expensive way to try and "whitewash" a deal nobody asked for. The fact that we haven't even seen a single Ebola case here makes the whole thing even more suspicious. It's almost as if they're more interested in the payout than actual preparedness. This kind of shady dealings, where everyone benefits but the common man, reminds me of some of the stories I’ve heard about [Wavinya’s Road To Riches: How To Triple Debt and Ignore Potholes](/posts/wavinya-s-road-to-riches--how-to-triple-debt-and-ignore-potholes).

The health minister, Aden Duale, finally got the memo and ordered a "complete cessation" of all construction and site prep. He said this in court, a day after being declared in contempt for ignoring previous stop-work orders. I mean, at least he’s consistent, right? Consistently late. Rights groups and Kenyan doctors have been screaming about this from the rooftops, citing secrecy and the potential to wreck our already struggling healthcare. And for what? So Uncle Sam can have a nice little quarantine spot for his citizens?

The numbers from DRC are grim, over a thousand cases and hundreds dead, with healthcare workers not even safe. Uganda, our neighbour, is already seeing cases. So while the government was busy building fancy isolation beds for foreigners, the real danger was being ignored. This whole debacle just proves that when it comes to our well-being, the people in charge are usually the last to know, or care. It’s a predictable dysfunction, not unlike some we’ve seen elsewhere.
