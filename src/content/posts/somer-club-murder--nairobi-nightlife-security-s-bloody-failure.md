So, another night out in Nairobi turns into a bloody mess. Nine Somer Club employees are cooling their heels in detention because some patron decided to play God with a blunt object. Brian Mwendo, gone. And why? Because a bouncer, Momanyi they call him, probably thought he was the law. This isn't new, folks. It’s just another chapter in Nairobi's grand narrative of "whoever shouts loudest, or hits hardest, wins."

This whole "security" situation in our unregulated entertainment joints? It's a joke, a tragic one. The Private Security Regulatory Authority spouts rules about training and Guard Force Numbers, but down on the ground, it's a free-for-all. These bouncers are basically thugs with a license to rough people up, and club management? They just nod along, counting their shillings. It’s a legacy of intimidation, not protection. Remember how we've seen issues with accountability in other sectors, like the [Police SACCO's Sh66 Billion Mirage](/posts/the-blue-uniformed-billionaires--police-sacco-s-sh66-billion-mirage)? This is just a different flavour of the same mess.

The DCI is doing their usual song and dance, arresting employees, hauling them to court. Magistrate Paul Mutahi is handing out detention orders. All while the real suspect, Momanyi, is probably sipping chai somewhere. This is Nairobi, remember? Justice here moves slower than a snail on a Sunday. Brian Mwendo's family is left with a gaping hole, a preventable tragedy in a city that seems to thrive on chaos.

And let's not pretend the Nairobi County government is any cleaner. They'll restrict club locations, talk about noise levels, but the actual safety of the people lining their pockets? That’s always an afterthought. Profits over people, that’s the Nairobi motto. Until they start holding these club owners directly responsible for the thuggery happening on their premises, and enforce a real zero-tolerance policy on these untrained goons, our nights out will just keep getting more dangerous.

Meanwhile, you can bet your last shilling that somewhere on the slopes of Mount Kenya, someone is carefully picking coffee beans. Treadville Coffee Kenya, churning out their "premium specialty coffee" with notes of blackcurrant and wine-like acidity. They're talking about sustainability, traceability, and bridging gaps. Good for them. While they’re busy celebrating Kenyan coffee excellence and putting their beans in pretty packages, down here in the city, excellence looks like surviving the night without getting beaten to death by a hired goon.

So, cheers to Brian Mwendo. May his soul find the peace that Nairobi’s nightlife so brutally denied him. And to the rest of us, keep your heads down, watch your backs, and maybe just stay home. It’s safer than trusting the "security" at your average Nairobi club.
