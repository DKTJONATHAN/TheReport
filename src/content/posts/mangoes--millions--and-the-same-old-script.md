The MCAs in Makueni have finally woken up from their nap to tell us what we already knew: someone ate the mango money. Sh20 million gone, just like that. While some 23-year-old kid is out there being "captivated" by the allure of mango farming, the veterans are counting losses because the procurement process was handled by people whose only skill is "improvising" budgets. It’s always the same - the farmer sweats under the sun, and the official sweats under the boardroom lights trying to remember which lie they told last week.

And speaking of boardrooms, our former and current presidents are reportedly clashing in closed-door meetings. Probably arguing over who left the bigger mess. Meanwhile, Nairobi County is signing an Sh80 billion "partnership" with the National Government. Eighty billion? In this economy? We all know that money will develop legs and run faster than an Olympic sprinter before it even reaches a single pothole in the CBD. It’s just a new way to fund someone’s 2027 campaign while we’re told to "disable ad-blockers" to support the truth.

Then there’s that wall to ward off Al-Shabaab. A wall? We can’t even keep a website running without begging for fifty bob, but we’re building walls like we’re in a medieval movie. It’s all theatre. Everything in this country is designed to keep you out - whether it’s a security wall, a subscription fee, or a "closed-door meeting" where they decide how much more they can squeeze out of your empty pockets. They talk about "exclusive stories" like we don't see the same corruption every single day for free on the streets.

If you’re tired of the mango drama and the political ego-wars, you might as well go read something that actually happens in the real streets. At least the betrayal in [Burning The Bridges: Chapter 2](/posts/kugongewa-na-kasongo-2) is honest about the heartbreak. In this city, if you’re not being cheated on by Kasongo, you’re being cheated on by the system. Pick your struggle, honey, but don't say Belinda didn't warn you.
