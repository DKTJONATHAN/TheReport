So, Kenya Police FC, the defending champions who are apparently still in the title race. According to their head coach, Nicholas Muyoti, they're just 13 points behind Gor Mahia with mere "handfuls of matches left." Bless his optimistic heart. It sounds like the same old story - big talk, little actual delivery when it matters. It’s the kind of hope you cling to when you’re already packing your bags.

Muyoti’s "remarkable turnaround" narrative is cute. Since he arrived mid-season, he claims confidence is growing and the team is fighting back. He’s happy with the "output" and the "manner in which we have fought back." Look, coach, fighting back from oblivion isn't the same as winning. It’s like bragging about how well you can hold your breath underwater when you're already drowning. The league is running away, and these boys are apparently "in for the fight." We'll see how that plays out when the actual points matter.

Defender Abud Omar chimes in with his praise for "training and preparation." He’s "braced for Gor." Tough team, he says. They'll need to be at their best. Well, no kidding, Abud. Anyone with a functioning brain cell knows Gor Mahia isn't a walk in the park. Meanwhile, Police are on a seven-match unbeaten run. Great. That’s like saying your car runs smoothly as long as you never actually drive it anywhere important. It’s a nice thought, but it won’t win you a championship.

They did manage to dump AFC Leopards out, a 3-0 thumping. That’s something, I suppose. A consolation prize for a team that’s clearly out of its depth. Now they hope to "derail Gor Mahia’s march." Oh, the irony. It’s like a mosquito trying to stop an elephant. Gor Mahia won the first leg 2-0, remember? They're in control. This whole "statement" game sounds more like a desperate plea than a strategic move. It reminds me of how some political promises are made, similar to how we can't even cross the road without a bribe, let alone navigate a fair football league [30 Years to the First World? We Can’t Even Cross the Road Without a Bribe].

Gor Mahia, on the other hand, is busy with actual fan engagement. Discounted ticket bundles, urging people to buy early to avoid the "hustle and delays." That's how you run a proper club, by focusing on the actual game and the fans who support it. Not by spinning tales of title races that are already over. The "Green Army" knows what's up. They're mobilizing for a win, not for a moral victory.

So, Kenya Police FC. Still in the title race? Sure, if you define "in the race" as being somewhere on the same continent as the finish line. It's a nice dream, but dreams don't lift trophies. Let's see what happens when reality bites, which it usually does with a vengeance.
