Kenya Power Throws Nyamira County Into Darkness: Another Planned Blackout Or Just Another Excuse?

So, Kenya Power, the folks who bless us with intermittent light shows, has decided Nyamira County needs a little "me time" on Sunday, March 15, 2026. Apparently, they need to "maintain their electricity infrastructure." Right. Because what better way to celebrate a Sunday than staring into the abyss while they "upgrade and repair" things. It’s almost as exciting as finding out Instagram’s encrypted messages are toast, which, let’s be honest, was more impactful.

The utility firm, in its infinite wisdom, dropped this bombshell on a Saturday evening, just in time to ruin everyone's weekend plans. Because, you know, we *all* have major power-dependent activities lined up for a Sunday between 8 am and 5 pm. Konate Market, Sironga Secondary School, Marindi, Mabundu, Mobembe, Etono, and the poor souls nearby are all on the naughty list. They’re basically being told to go unplug themselves for the day.

Meanwhile, their CEO, Joseph Siror, was out there on Tuesday, March 10, 2026, trying to convince us that electricity isn't expensive. Apparently, it's all about "consumption levels" and not running "five or ten bulbs." Bless his heart. He’s acting like we’re all living in a tiny hut with one low-wattage bulb, pretending the cost of keeping the lights on, and let’s not forget the endless drama of trying to get decent service, isn’t a constant battle. It’s almost as if he’s never had to deal with the “few electricity units despite buying tokens” saga himself.

Siror prattled on about "lifeline tariffs" and how infrastructure "reaches your doorstep." Oh, the generosity! It’s almost as if the constant flickering and the dread of another bill aren’t part of the deal. This whole song and dance about maintenance is just another reminder that when it comes to our power supply, we’re basically just along for the ride, hoping we don’t end up like some of the folks I’ve written about, caught in a vortex of unreleased hustle.
