CAF Reforms: More Hot Air Than Football After AFCON Fiasco











So, CAF, the esteemed Confederation of African Football, has finally decided to grace us with "reforms" and "improvements" after the utter mess of their Africa Cup of Nations final. Apparently, stripping Senegal of a title two months after the fact and leaving two countries claiming to be champions is *not* the kind of impartiality everyone was hoping for. CAF president Patrice Motsepe, bless his heart, is now saying that if ordinary fans *think* referees aren't impartial, then it's a problem. Groundbreaking stuff, really. Who knew public perception mattered?

And speaking of problems, Véron Mosengo-Omba, the man who apparently ran CAF like his personal fiefdom and managed to get investigations into financial wrongdoing dropped, is now off to "personal projects." Convenient, that. He leaves with "peace of mind" and claims to leave CAF "more prosperous than ever." Just like how some Kenyan politicians claim their foreign trips are for the nation's prosperity, eh? [Ruto’s Sh3.1 Billion Foreign Trips: From Nairobi to a Global Money Pit](/posts/ruto-s-sh3-1-billion-foreign-trips--from-nairobi-to-a-global-money-pit). At least Mosengo-Omba can retire; unlike some of our boys who went to Haiti and came back wondering what it was all for.

Motsepe is also busy talking about better pay for referees and VAR operators, and training them with FIFA. Good, good. Because nothing screams integrity like paying people more to do a job they've already proven they can mess up spectacularly. He also confirmed the Women's Africa Cup of Nations is still slated for Morocco, despite being postponed at the last minute for "circumstances we had not foreseen." Honestly, if they hadn't foreseen *that*, I'd be worried about them foreseeing their own meetings.

And for us here in Kenya, Motsepe will be gracing us with his presence in May to check on our "Pamoja" tournament preparations for 2027. Don't worry, he's dismissed any suggestions it might be moved. Because why would you move a tournament that's already being planned? Anyway, the Africa Cup is expanding to 28 teams. Because clearly, more teams means better football, right? It's all about the show, the spectacle, the endless stream of promises. Just don't expect the actual football to suddenly become flawless overnight.
