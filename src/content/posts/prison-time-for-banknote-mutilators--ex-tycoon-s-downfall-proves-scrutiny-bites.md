So, some TikTok clown thinks it's a good look to shred a Sh100 note for clout. The internet, bless its heart, has predictably lost its mind. Half of you are clutching your pearls and spewing legal mumbo jumbo about jail time, while the other half are busy telling everyone to chill out and that it's not *that* serious. Honestly, it's like watching a pack of hyenas fight over a single bone.

But let’s not get bogged down in the petty squabbles of keyboard warriors. The real tea here is the creeping talk about surveillance and how much the public apparently loves being watched. Sixty percent of you, apparently, are perfectly fine with being under the microscope. Makes you wonder if we’re all just a bit too eager to hand over our privacy for… what exactly? A slightly more "secure" existence? Please.

This whole banknote brouhaha also got me thinking about how quickly fortunes can vanish. We've seen it before, haven't we? The spectacular unravelling of once high-flying tycoons in Eastleigh, the ones who seemed untouchable, suddenly brought down by a cocktail of debt, betrayal, and, dare I say it, scrutiny. It’s a harsh lesson that sometimes, protecting what you have is far more crucial than chasing that next big score. We covered a bit of that sticky business with financial woes in the [Kano Scholarship Scandal: Ex-Commissioner's 35 Bank Accounts Exposed!](/posts/kano-scholarship-scandal--ex-commissioner-s-35-bank-accounts-exposed) not too long ago, and the sentiment is eerily similar.

And speaking of people feeling the heat under scrutiny, remember when the US Rights group was all over Kenya's passport scandal? It's a constant reminder that in this game, whether you're tearing up cash or running a country, there's always someone watching, and the consequences can be far more severe than a few angry tweets. So, Mr. TikToker, while you’re busy destroying currency, remember that the system you’re playing with has eyes everywhere, and the legal ramifications are only part of the story. Some fortunes unravel faster than cheap silk.
