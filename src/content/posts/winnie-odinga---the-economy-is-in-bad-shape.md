WINNIE ODINGA: KENYA'S ECONOMY IS A FAKE SUIT ON A BROKE MAN







So, Winnie Odinga, daughter of the great opposition leader, is telling us what we already know: Kenya's economy is doing a spectacular impression of a thriving business while secretly drowning in debt. Ksh10 trillion, she says. That's more money than most of us will ever see, and it's being flushed down the toilet while our leaders strut around in new suits. It’s like watching a neighbor with no food in the house buy a brand-new Mercedes. Utter madness.

She’s right, of course. You can see it on the streets. Youths are staring at their phones, businesses are shutting their doors faster than you can say "hustle," and the farmers, the very people feeding us, are crying over the price of fertilizer. It’s a circus, and the clowns are in charge. And the worst part? These politicians, especially those in government, seem to have forgotten how to actually *talk* to the people they claim to serve. They’re not listening; they’re just making noise.

Take that Silvanus Osoro character. Apparently, he thinks disrespect is a form of negotiation. Shouting at mourners who are already down in the dumps because they don't like the President's show? That's not leadership, that's bullying. Winnie also called out the Treasury CS, John Mbadi, for meddling in ODM politics instead of guarding our money. Steer clear of the political mudslinging, sir. Your job is serious, not a platform for party squabbles.

Then you have the Nairobi Senator, Edwin Sifuna, absolutely demolishing Osoro’s fantasy of leaders breaking bread together in secret. "I would rather die of hunger," he said, and honestly, I don't blame him. The sheer audacity of some of these guys. Osoro himself got a taste of the people's anger, being jeered during his speech. His response? "Si yenu. Ngonjeni matenge yenu." Basically, "This isn't yours. Wait for your leftovers." That's the level of respect we're getting. It reminds me of how even small successes in places like Mombasa can be overshadowed by larger systemic issues, like this ongoing economic rot. And just to add insult to injury, Governor Simba Arati is apparently trying to push Sifuna out of ODM. While all this political drama unfolds, our economy is on life support. It’s a never-ending cycle of promises, betrayals, and economic mismanagement. The people are suffering, and the leaders are busy playing their petty games. It's a joke, but one that’s costing us dearly.

So, yes, Winnie Odinga, the economy is in bad shape. It's not just a bad shape; it's a full-blown medical emergency that our leaders are treating with a band-aid and a condescending lecture. They don't know how to talk to us because they probably don't even see us as people anymore, just voters to be managed and silenced.
