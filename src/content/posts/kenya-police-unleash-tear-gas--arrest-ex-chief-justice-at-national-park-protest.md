Kenya Police Tear Gas Ex-Chief Justice Over National Park Land Grab

So, the geniuses in charge decided it's a grand idea to build on Nairobi National Park. Naturally, the folks who actually care about, you know, *nature*, showed up to protest. And what's the government's solution? Tear gas and arrests. Because that's how we do things in this city: ignore the problem, then beat it into submission.

Of course, they managed to nab a few people, including a former Chief Justice. Because nothing says "we're serious about this" like cuffing a man who used to run the courts. The Kenya Wildlife Service, bless their bureaucratic hearts, are defending the project by saying it's for an animal orphanage. Because clearly, the thousands of animals already living there aren't enough, and a parking lot for over a thousand cars is just what the rhinos and lions need. It's not like we have a history of these kinds of land grabs, is it? Remember when we talked about [Content Vultures and Seizures: Nairobi’s Fake Sympathy on Display](/posts/content-vultures-and-seizures--nairobi-s-fake-sympathy-on-display)? This is just another chapter.

The protesters were waving signs, bless their naive hearts, about "Nature is not vacant land." Sweet. As if the people making these decisions have ever seen a concept like "nature" outside of a PowerPoint presentation. This park is practically in the city's backyard, a place where you can see lions with skyscrapers as a backdrop. And we're going to pave over a chunk of it for a parking lot. Brilliant.

An activist, Nyaguthii Chege, was quoted saying the park is "under threat." Well, no kidding. It's always under threat. From corruption, from greed, from short-sightedness. It’s a miracle anything is left. But hey, at least the animals will have a convenient place for their visitors to park, right? If they can even get to it through the traffic.

This is Nairobi, after all. We're great at building things, especially on top of things we shouldn't. We've got leaders who form panels to pick bosses, probably to decide who gets to approve the next concrete jungle expansion. [Ruto Forms Panels to Pick IPOA, TSC, and IGRTC Bosses - More Chairs to Warm?](/posts/ruto-forms-panels-to-pick-ipoa--tsc--and-igrtc-bosses---more-chairs-to-warm) That's the real business. Not protecting a park.

So, the park shrinks, the animals get squeezed, and we get more concrete. Another victory for progress, I guess. Just don't expect me to shed a tear. Or hold my breath for any real change.
