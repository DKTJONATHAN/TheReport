So, the Big Man himself, President Ruto, has graced us with his presence and a "generous" 12 per cent salary increase for workers. And for the farmers, a whopping 15 per cent. Apparently, this is meant to cushion us from the "rising cost of living." As if we haven't been living with that reality longer than most of these politicians have been in office.

He says it was after "consultations" and responding to "long-standing calls." I'm sure those consultations involved a lot of nodding and very little actual listening. And "long-standing calls"? We’ve been calling for years, and it feels like we’re shouting into a hurricane. This 12 per cent is supposed to "strengthen household incomes." Let's be real, it might buy you an extra kilo of unga, maybe. But the price of everything else? It's still climbing faster than a matatu on Waiyaki Way.

The special treatment for the agricultural sector, a 15 per cent boost, is justified by its "strategic importance." Of course. Someone’s got to grow the food that we’ll be struggling to afford. It’s a nice gesture, I suppose, but for most of us stuck in the city, it feels like they're throwing crumbs while the main course is being devoured by the elite. We're still grappling with the fallout of poor economic decisions, and frankly, when liquidity is a ghost and the exit is narrowing, these small hikes feel like rearranging deck chairs on the Titanic.

The government is under pressure, we're told. They're facing "growing pressure to address inflationary pressures." Well, no kidding. Maybe instead of just talking about it, they could actually *do* something substantial. We’ve seen how "consultations" and "adjustments" can be manipulated. Remember when they tried to pull a fast one with fuel prices? We ended up with those Petroleum PS, KPC, EPRA Bosses Out Amid Fuel Shortage Lies. This salary hike feels like a similar game of smoke and mirrors.

We're still waiting for the "implementation framework and timelines." You can bet your last shilling it will be a bureaucratic maze designed to delay and dilute the impact. By the time this "increase" actually hits our pockets, inflation will have eaten it alive. So, thanks, Mr. President, for the thought. We'll be here, counting our pennies, and hoping the next Labour Day announcement isn't just another ghost of a promise.
