The Pokémon Company is set to host its annual Pokémon Presents broadcast, a tradition marking the anniversary of the original Pokémon Red and Green titles. This year's presentation carries particular weight, as it commemorates 30 years since the franchise's debut on the Game Boy.

The broadcast is confirmed for Friday, February 27th, airing at 2pm UK time, corresponding to 6am Pacific Time and 9am Eastern. This highly anticipated event is expected to unveil a range of new content and updates for the expansive Pokémon universe.

A significant announcement made earlier today confirms the release of *Pokémon FireRed Version* and *Pokémon LeafGreen Version* for Nintendo Switch and the upcoming Switch 2 console. These remakes, originally launched on the Game Boy Advance, will be available for purchase and download exclusively on Pokémon Day, immediately following the conclusion of the broadcast. Unlike some classic titles, these games will not be offered as part of a subscription service, requiring a direct purchase. Pre-orders are currently available via the Nintendo eShop, with the titles retailing for £16.99.

Beyond these classic remakes, the broadcast is also expected to provide updates on popular live-service titles such as *Pokémon Go*, *Pokémon Sleep*, *Pokémon Unite*, and *Pokémon EX Masters*. The continued evolution of these digital ecosystems highlights the increasing reliance on mobile platforms for entertainment and engagement, a trend paralleled in other sectors, including the financial sphere, where innovative solutions like [mobile stock market access via M-Pesa](/posts/kenya-stock-market-mobile-mpesa) are reshaping consumer interaction with services.

Another title likely to receive further details is *Pokémon Pokopia*, a forthcoming game described as Pokémon's interpretation of the *Animal Crossing* genre. Set for release next month on March 5th, early impressions suggest a positive reception. One preview noted, "Mechanics I love mashed up with characters and a world I also have a great affinity for. I haven't felt this way about a Pokémon game since Conquest, the 2012 strategy crossover with Nobunaga's Ambition."

Speculation also surrounds potential announcements regarding the Pokémon park in Japan, as well as the eagerly awaited reveal of Generation 10 Pokémon, a prospect many fans are hoping for. The 30th-anniversary presentation is poised to offer a comprehensive look at the franchise's past, present, and future trajectory.
