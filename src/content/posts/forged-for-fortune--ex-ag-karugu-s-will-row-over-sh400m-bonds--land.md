So, the esteemed former Attorney General James Boro Karugu, the man who apparently left office without a blemish and with a reputation for independent thought, is now the star of a very Kenyan drama. His will, or what someone claims is his will, has turned into a battleground, with forensic reports contradicting each other faster than a politician changes their tune. One minute it's a forgery from August 2025, the next it's genuine as of March 2026. Classic.

And what are they fighting over? Oh, just a measly Sh400 million in Treasury bonds, a building in the CBD, nine vehicles, and prime land scattered across five counties. Enough to keep a small nation happy, or in this case, enough to turn children, senior advocates, and even a Kinangop pastor into suspects. It's the kind of money that makes people forget their morals faster than a Nairobi traffic jam clears after a downpour.

The whole mess is further complicated by two women popping up, claiming to be Karugu's wives. Because, of course, they would. It's never a simple inheritance, is it? It's always a tangled web of supposed relationships and hidden agendas, where everyone's digging for their piece of the pie, and no one wants to be left empty handed. This is Kenya, after all, where wealth breeds suspicion faster than mosquitoes breed in a stagnant puddle.

It's a stark reminder that no matter how high you climb, how clean you try to keep your name, or how independent-minded you think you are, the lure of money has a way of dragging everyone into the muck. This isn't just about a will; it's about greed, desperation, and the ugly truth that sometimes, even the most respected figures can become the center of a criminal investigation. It makes you wonder about the systems we trust, doesn't it?
