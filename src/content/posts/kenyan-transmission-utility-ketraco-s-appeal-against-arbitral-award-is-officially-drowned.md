So, the Kenya Electricity Transmission Company, Ketraco, decided to pull a fast one, eh? Tried to wiggle out of paying a hefty arbitral award, probably thinking they could just stonewall their way out of it. Well, guess what? Three appellate judges, who clearly haven't been bribed by anyone with a vested interest in keeping the lights on (or off, depending on who's paying), have slapped them down. No suspension, no delay. Ketraco has to cough up.

It’s always the same story, isn't it? These parastatals, flush with public money, think they’re untouchable. They make dodgy deals, get caught, and then try to use the legal system as a shield. But this time, the shield cracked. You'd think after all the scandals and mismanaged funds we’ve seen, they’d learn. But no, Kenyan efficiency means repeating the same mistakes with even more flair.

Honestly, it’s exhausting. You’re struggling to make rent, worrying about whether your phone will survive the day without a charge, and these guys are playing games with millions. It makes you wonder if some of these executives are just auditioning for roles in the next big exposé on corruption, right alongside those who profit from things like [Malindi’s Smallest Export: The Child Trafficking Economy](/posts/malindi-s-smallest-export--the-child-trafficking-economy).

And the timing? June 17th, 18th, 19th. They were probably hoping the news would get lost in the usual political noise, or perhaps buried under more pressing issues like whether the next election will be free and fair (spoiler alert: it won't be). But a large arbitral award being upheld against a state company? That’s juicy enough to cut through the nonsense, at least for a little while.

So, what does this mean for the common mwananchi? More money out of the public purse, inevitably passed down as higher electricity tariffs. Because that’s how it works. Ketraco messes up, the taxpayer pays. It’s the circle of Kenyan life, powered by bad decisions and even worse oversight. Just another Tuesday, I suppose.

This whole charade, from the appeal to the dismissal, is just another reminder that while we’re busy trying to survive, the real power players are busy lining their pockets. And when they get caught, they just appeal. Always with the appeals.
