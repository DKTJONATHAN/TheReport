Ruto's Middle East Crisis Shield: More Hot Air Than Real Protection

President William Ruto is out here, as usual, flapping his gums about protecting Kenya from the Middle East kerfuffle. He’s talking about fuel and commodities, big words, big plans, and you know how that usually ends. Let’s just say I’ve seen more convincing promises from Prophet David Owuor about his billion shilling miracles.

The man is warning about logistical nightmares and shipping routes. Apparently, the Strait of Hormuz is causing him sleepless nights, and he's worried about our wallets. He’s promising alternative fuel sources and "tightening oversight." Sounds like more committees, more meetings, and more tea breaks while the prices at the pump continue their steady climb, just like the negative points in our so-called premier league.

Ruto’s also given an ultimatum to oil marketers: no artificial shortages for profit. Good luck with that. These guys have more ways to squeeze us than a dried-up mandazi. You think they’ll listen to a presidential decree when there’s a quick shilling to be made by creating a panic? This sounds like the same old song and dance, a bit like the Mandera PR circus where big words barely cover up the small checks.

He’s tasked the Ministry of Energy with exploring alternative sources. So, instead of fixing the mess we’re already in, we’re busy looking for new suppliers to potentially get us into *different* messes. Progress has been made, he says. Progress towards what? More speeches? More consultations? We’ll see how much "progress" we've made when the next fuel tanker is delayed.

The President hopes diplomacy will de-escalate the conflict. Bless his heart. In this world, diplomacy often takes a backseat to greed and power. Prolonged instability, he warns, will have negative consequences. No kidding, Sherlock. We're the ones who always end up footing the bill, one way or another.
