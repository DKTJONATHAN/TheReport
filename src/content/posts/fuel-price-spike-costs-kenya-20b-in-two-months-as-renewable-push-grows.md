Fuel Price Spike Costs Kenya 20B in Two Months as Greedy Corporations Profit More

Nairobi is getting fleeced, plain and simple. While we're all scrambling to keep our lights on and our tanks full, these big oil companies are practically swimming in cash thanks to that mess in Iran. It’s a KSh18.6 billion to KSh19.5 billion loss for us, folks, just for the first two months of this so-called "Iran war." That's money that could be doing something useful, but no, it’s going straight into the pockets of the already-rich.

Rukiya Khamis from 350.org East Africa called it an "obscene transfer" of wealth, and she's not wrong. These fossil fuel companies are posting record profits while ordinary Kenyans are struggling. It's a "staggering injustice" that they thrive on crisis and scarcity. And this KSh19.5 billion? That's enough to give solar power to 150,000 households. Imagine that. Instead, we're just feeding the beast.

This whole mess highlights how ridiculous it is to be so dependent on fossil fuels. Amos Wemanya from Power Shift Africa says it's not just bad for the planet, it's also indefensible economically. This KSh19.5 billion could have gone into solar, but instead, it's fueling corporate greed. It’s like we’re stuck in a loop, and frankly, it’s exhausting. It reminds me a bit of how sometimes the [Constitution Frees Us? More Like It Chains Us Back To Old Ways](/posts/constitution-frees-us--more-like-it-chains-us-back-to-old-ways) - we’re promised progress, but often end up paying the price.

The "We Pay, They Profit" campaign is spot on. We're being forced to pay more, while these companies are literally profiting from our hardship. They want governments to use public funds for community renewable energy, and frankly, it's about time. It's not just about lower bills; it's about human dignity over corporate greed. And with global oil prices projected to cost the world economy trillions, it’s clear this isn’t sustainable.

Let's be real, the whole fossil fuel era is a disaster. It’s ecologically unsustainable and economically indefensible. As Savio Carvalho from 350.org put it, "We pay, they profit, until we break free from fossil fuels." The less reliant we are, the more protected we are from these price shocks. Renewables are cheaper, and fossil fuels are now just a liability. It's time to stop being naive and demand a system that actually works for us, not just for the fat cats.
