Let's get this straight: Nintendo's Switch 2 isn't designed for convenience, it's designed to milk every last penny out of its user base. They've deliberately obfuscated their controller protocols and video output over USB-C, essentially forcing third-party manufacturers to play catch-up and start their development all over again. This isn't about innovation; it's about control and, more importantly, profit.

Now, we're presented with two "reliable" USB-C docks. The JSAUX OmniCentro Dock clocks in at a palatable $45.99, while Genki's crowdsourced Covert Dock 3 demands a steeper $69.99. Both claim to offer impressive specs like 4K at 120Hz output (though the Switch 2 itself caps out at 4K/60Hz, because why would they give you the full benefit?). They'll scale down to lower resolutions, offer HDR, and fast charging up to 65W, plus a token USB-A port for your wireless dongles or controllers.

The real kicker? Genki's dock seemingly jacks up its price solely because it has some translucent orange plastic. There's no discernable technological advantage, no groundbreaking feature to justify that $24 premium. It's pure market manipulation, preying on the perception that more expensive equals better.

These aren't revolutionary devices anymore. The novelty of compact USB-C hubs has long since worn off. They're useful, sure, for travel or just shuffling your setup around the house. But let's not pretend they're some essential lifeline. They're a concession, a way to work around Nintendo's deliberate design choices.

And don't get too comfortable with these options. Nintendo has a history of locking down its hardware, as evidenced by a November 2025 update that bricked some third-party docks. The promise of upgradeable firmware is a weak defense against a company that can arbitrarily decide to cut off your accessories with a simple system update. It's a constant game of cat and mouse, where the consumer is always on the losing end, footing the bill for each new round of Nintendo's control. So go ahead, spend your money. Just don't expect loyalty or fairness from the giants you're feeding.
