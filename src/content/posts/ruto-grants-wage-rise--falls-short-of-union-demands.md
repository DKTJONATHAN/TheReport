Ruto's Wage Rise: A Pat on the Head, Not a Full Meal

Don't let the Labour Day fanfare fool you. President Ruto has trotted out a supposed "win" for Kenyan workers - a measly 12% on general wages and 15% for those toiling in the fields. Apparently, this is meant to be our government's grand gesture against the biting cost of living. You know, the one they've been actively contributing to with every new tax and every inflated procurement. It's a classic move: throw a few crumbs to keep the masses from outright mutiny while the real economic machinery grinds on, benefiting a select few.

Francis Atwoli, the ever-present voice of the unions, had the audacity to ask for a more substantial 23%. He even threw in a reminder of past administrations, like Kibaki's, that actually managed to offer decent increases. But Ruto's 12%? It’s less of a cushion and more of a thinly veiled insult. It’s the equivalent of telling a drowning man you’ll throw him a single, slightly damp breadstick. And the cherry on top? They celebrate this "achievement" by holding the event away from Nairobi, a pathetic attempt at showing unity while glossing over the fact that the economic pressures they claim to address remain firmly in place.

While they're busy congratulating themselves, remember that the infrastructure holding our country together is often crumbling. We've seen water supply issues plague the capital, and you can bet your last shilling that the fix won't be as swift or as generous as these wage "adjustments." It's a familiar tune, really. The government talks a good game about building the nation, but the foundation seems to be built on quicksand, and the workers are left footing the bill for every crack that appears.

This whole charade is just another reminder that when it comes to real change, don't expect it. The system is rigged, and these small "wins" are designed to keep us pacified, not empowered.
