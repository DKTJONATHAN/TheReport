Just when you thought Maksim Chmerkovskiy had exhausted every reality TV gimmick from Dancing with the Stars to The Traitors, he has found a new way to haunt your screen. The man is now the face of "vertical storytelling," which is a fancy way of saying he is starring in TikTok-style soap operas for people who cannot be bothered to turn their phones sideways. His new project, Wild Silence, debuts on the My Drama app this February, marking what his PR team calls the "next chapter" of his acting career. In reality, it looks like the digital equivalent of those bargain-bin DVDs we used to find at the gas station.

The plot is as predictable as a scripted reality show fight. Maks plays Jack, a reclusive man living in a cabin who rescues a woman named Camilla from a suicide attempt. Because nothing screams "prestige acting" like trauma-bonding in the woods in sixty-second increments. It is the first time a reality TV star has taken a lead role in a U.S. vertical series, which the company, HOLYWATER TECH, claims is "unlocking creators’ potential." I suppose "unlocking potential" is the new industry term for "hiring people who are famous enough to recognize but cheap enough to fit into a mobile app budget."

The "AI-first" company behind this project seems very proud of their "premium" content, but let us be real for a second. We have seen this desperate rush to mobile-first entertainment before, and it usually ends in a pile of forgotten subscriptions. While [the cost of privacy is a rounding error](/posts/the-cost-of-privacy-is-a-rounding-error--google-buys-silence-for--135-million) for the tech giants who actually run the world, these smaller apps are banking on your boredom and your data to keep their "innovative" platforms alive. They want us to believe that watching a former ballroom dancer brood in 9:16 is the future of cinema, but it feels more like a content farm with a better haircut.

Maks claims he was drawn to the "honesty of the story," but we all know the honesty of a paycheck is much more persuasive. He is calling vertical series an "exciting new frontier," which is exactly what every celebrity says when they realize the big-screen roles are not calling. If you are dying to see Jack and Camilla confront their pasts between scrolling through cooking videos and cat memes, then My Drama is waiting for you. For the rest of us, it is just another sign that the line between "celebrity" and "content creator" has finally, mercifully, vanished into the digital void.
