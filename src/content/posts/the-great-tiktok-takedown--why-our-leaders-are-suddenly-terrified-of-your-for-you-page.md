Oh, look who is finally waking up from their taxpayer-funded nap. While we were all distracted by the usual ODM party drama and political musical chairs, the National Assembly quietly decided to put a leash on our favorite digital playground. Remember Bob Ndolo? The man who originally wanted TikTok banned because he could not handle the "explicit sexual content" and "vulgar language"? Well, he has downgraded his mid-life crisis from a total ban to "regulation." It is the classic bait-and-switch that would make even the messiest socialite proud.

Let us be real here. This is not about protecting our "cultural and religious values." If it were, half of our local TV shows and the questionable outfits at celebrity red carpets would have been off the air decades ago. This is about the sheer panic radiating from the August House because they realized 15 million Kenyans are online, and 36 per cent of us are spending over six hours a day watching their every move. The lawmakers are calling this a "dramatic turn," but the only thing truly dramatic is how scared they are of a Gen Z audience that does not need a permission slip to call out their nonsense.

The real tea is that this is a total trap for the 2027 General Election. They are using the "think of the children" excuse to sneak in amendments to the Kenya Information and Communication Act. It is the same old script we have seen in neighboring countries where the internet suddenly "breaks" during voting season. They are coming for the bloggers and the journalists, pretending it is about "age verification" while actually looking for ways to keep us quiet when the real [CHARACTER DEVELOPMENT: THE NAIROBI CIRCUS AFTER THE LEAK](/posts/character-development--the-nairobi-circus-after-the-leak) starts happening again.

Our "dear leaders" want the Communications Authority to have the power to "regulate" social media platforms. Translation: they want a kill-switch for when things get a little too honest for their liking. They are talking about localizing data and cyber security, but we all know they just want to know exactly who is behind the parody accounts making fun of their tailored suits. It is high-level gatekeeping disguised as public safety, and the hypocrisy is honestly exhausting.

So, to all the content creators currently obsessed with their ring lights and "get ready with me" videos, you might want to start paying attention. The government is preparing to put your livelihood under a microscope. If we let them "update" the laws without a fight, the only content left will be boring press releases and politicians cutting ribbons on dusty roads. Stay vigilant, because the "protection" they are offering looks a lot like handcuffs for our free speech.
