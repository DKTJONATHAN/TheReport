So, the UN's latest attempt to "fix" Haiti is rolling out, and surprise, surprise, the Kenyans are getting the boot. Apparently, Chad is sending in their own boys to tango with the gangs. Roberto Alvarez, the Dominican Republic's foreign minister, is out there spinning yarns about new troops training in the US and a gradual handover. Sounds like a plan, right? Except we've heard this tune before.

This whole Gang Suppression Force, or GSF as they call it, is supposed to be the big upgrade from the previous Kenyan-led mission. But what have we seen? Zilch. Nada. A whole lot of talk and a few Kenyan officers trying to hold the fort. They’re waiting for these Chadian forces, who are currently being "trained" in the States. Because nothing says effective gang suppression like a good old American boot camp, eh?

The plan is for these new troops to start showing up in April and the whole 5,500-strong force to be in place by October. The UN bigwigs had already mumbled something about summer or autumn. It's all so vague, like a politician's promise. And this whole thing only has a 12-month mandate, expiring next September. You think they'll sort out years of chaos in that time? Don't make me laugh.

Remember when the previous mission was hobbled by a lack of everything? Troops, cash, equipment. This new one is supposed to be different. It's all voluntary contributions, naturally. Chad pledged troops back in October. So did Benin and Bangladesh. Where are they? Still pledging, I guess. It’s a bit like waiting for a bus in Nairobi during rush hour - you might get there eventually, or you might just give up and walk.

Meanwhile, Haiti continues to be the punchbag. Thousands dead, over a million displaced, all thanks to gangs armed with guns that somehow keep popping up from the US. It’s a predictable cycle, really. The powerful do their thing, the powerless suffer, and the international community sends in a revolving door of troops who mostly just look good in photos. It reminds me of those stories about what goes on behind closed doors, the ones that never quite make it to the light. Like [Ruai’s Guesthouse of Horrors: When the Police Finally Find the Basement](/posts/ruai-s-guesthouse-of-horrors--when-the-police-finally-find-the-basement), this is just another grim tale unfolding, with the same actors playing slightly different roles.

So, the Kenyans pack their bags, the Chadians arrive, and everyone hopes for the best. But let's be real, this is Haiti. It’s a mess, and throwing more soldiers at it without tackling the root causes, like the relentless flow of weapons from across the ocean, is just rearranging the deck chairs on the Titanic. Don't expect miracles. Just expect more headlines.
