So, the DCI boys, all high and mighty, pounced on some Nigerian fella in Ruaka. Apparently, he was part of a big drug network, or so they claim. They got wind of a meeting, rushed to Syokimau like heroes, only to find the party had been cancelled. Classic. Then, *poof*, they’re off to Ruaka, thanks to their "covert sources". Makes you wonder if these sources are just bored Nairobians with too much time.

Turns out, this apartment wasn't exactly a shrine. Found some pellets, packages, scales - the usual suspects. Plus, foreign passports and wads of cash in both currencies. And lo and behold, a Kenyan accomplice was also nabbed. A whole operation, supposedly dismantling a "wider trafficking network." We've heard this song before, haven't we? It's like our own twisted version of "Open Borders, Closed Eyes," except it's just closed eyes to the real rot.

Now they’re cooling their heels in Ruaraka and Pangani, waiting to be paraded in court. Forensic analysis will happen, promises will be made, and then what? A slap on the wrist, a deportation, and a new face shows up next week. This whole "dismantling the wider network" narrative is as reliable as a matatu driver’s schedule. We're supposed to be impressed that they caught a few small fish while the sharks are probably sipping cocktails somewhere.

Honestly, I’m not holding my breath. This is just the usual Nairobi drama. They’ll make noise, get a few headlines, and then it’s back to business as usual. The streets will remain the same, the demand will be met, and we’ll be left with the same old stories. It’s a performance, really. The show must go on, even if the audience knows the ending.
