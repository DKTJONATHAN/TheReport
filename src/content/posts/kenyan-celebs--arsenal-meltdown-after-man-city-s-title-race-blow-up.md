So, Manchester City went and did what they always do, stomped all over Arsenal and blew their precious title race wide open. And naturally, our Kenyan "celebrities," the ones who suddenly remember they’re football fans when it suits them, have taken to social media to wail and moan. It’s not just disappointment; it’s a full-blown, attention-seeking pity party.

Take Khaligraph Jones, for instance. Apparently, wearing an Arsenal jersey in a music video that’s already racked up 17 million views is now the reason for the club’s downfall. He’s begging for his scenes to be removed, calling it “swara.” Seriously? This is the same man who probably thinks every bad deal is tied to a juju man, much like some might view the economic potholes we’re constantly navigating, remember [From Nairobi Hustle to Russian Meat Grinders: The Ultimate Scammer Season](link-to-article)? Football’s just another stage for these theatrics.

Then there’s Dr Ofweneke, who’s decided to "dump" Arsenal. He’d rather support Pritty Vishy, which, frankly, tells you more about his judgment than anything else. It’s a classic case of fleeing the sinking ship when the going gets tough, proving that loyalty is as rare as an honest politician in this town. Dem Wa FB is out here claiming the match is raising her blood pressure while pregnant. Please, darling, this is Nairobi; you think we have time for football-induced stress when rent is due and the matatus are packed tighter than a sardine can?

Actress Trisha Khalid warns us to stay away from angry Arsenal fans. Groundbreaking. And Massawe Japanni, a self-proclaimed "newbie," is done with football. Good riddance. At least some of them, like Wangare Caddie and Usefulidioty, are clinging to some semblance of hope, urging people to ignore the "noise." But let’s be real, this is Arsenal. They’ve perfected the art of snatching defeat from the jaws of victory. Comedian Tom Daktari summed it up perfectly: supporting Arsenal is a useless endeavour, paid or not. No wonder everyone hates them; their entire existence is a drama series no one asked for. The gap is three points now. Let’s see how long this "fight till the end" narrative lasts before the next inevitable capitulation.
