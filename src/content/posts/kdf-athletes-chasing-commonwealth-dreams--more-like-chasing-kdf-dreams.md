So, the Kenya Defence Forces (KDF) did their usual thing at the National Athletics Championships. Dropped 59 medals, the lot of them, because apparently, running fast and throwing heavy things is the pinnacle of military achievement. They call it discipline, resilience, excellence. I call it a distraction from whatever else is going on, which is usually something dodgy. This whole charade is just to select people for some Commonwealth Games in Scotland. As if that's going to fix Kenya.

Mercy Oketch, Garson Ogega, Regan Oketch, Dominic Abuda, Faith Kipsang Jepkomboi. All names to remember, I suppose, if you care about who can run a lap or hurl a piece of metal the furthest. They're all supposedly showcasing values that define success. Funny, I thought success in this country usually involved connections and a willingness to look the other way. Remember that [Kenya Hospital Morgue Bodies Found In Mass Grave, Police Say. So What Else Is New?](/posts/kenya-hospital-morgue-bodies-found-in-mass-grave--police-say--so-what-else-is-new)? That's the kind of "success" that actually matters.

The KDF claims this is all about preparing for international competition. More likely, it's about boosting morale and making the government look good. They talk about teamwork and collective determination, the same stuff they probably drum into recruits before sending them off to God knows where. It’s a nice little showpiece, isn't it? A chance for the boys and girls in uniform to get some glory, while the real issues continue to fester.

And the Ministry of Defence, bless their cotton socks, is probably already planning the next press release, full of platitudes about discipline and national pride. They’ll conveniently forget to mention that while these athletes are training and competing, the country is still grappling with the same old problems. More medals, more photo ops, and probably a few more opportunities for those in power to pat themselves on the back. Just another day in paradise, Nairobi style.
