KDF's DEFABA Declares 2026 National Boxing Champions, Another Win for the System

So, the Defence Forces Amateur Boxing Association (DEFABA), or the KDF as the common folk call them, have gone and crowned themselves the 2026 National Open Boxing Champions. Apparently, it was a "grueling" four days at the Kaloleni Social Hall. Grueling for whom? For the taxpayers footing the bill, I imagine. Seventeen boxers, twelve in the finals, nine gold medals for the men, three for the women. Impressive, I guess, if you're into that sort of thing. They "outclassed" the police, which means they probably had better equipment and more time to train. Discipline and tactical superiority? Or just better funding and a less corrupt system? Let's be real.

They managed to scrape together 25 points, beating the National Police Service by a measly three. Kasarani Club and Kisumu Club trailed way behind with eight points each. Kenya Prisons, bless their hearts, only managed six. This isn't exactly a revelation, is it? The military, with all its resources, always seems to land on its feet, doesn't it? Now they're gearing up for the National League in Nakuru. This little victory is apparently a "morale booster." For whom? The guys getting paid to fight? Good for them, I suppose. It just further proves that the KDF boxers are still "strong and competitive." Of course they are. They're the military. They're practically built for it. It's not exactly discovering the optimal electric toothbrush for sensitive teeth, is it? It's more like the same old story in a different arena.

At the end of the day, it's just another win for the establishment. While the rest of Nairobi is struggling to make ends meet, these guys are knocking each other out for trophies and bragging rights. And who pays for it all? We do. So congratulations to DEFABA. You've defended your honour, and your budget. The rest of us will just keep our heads down and try to survive.







The Defence Forces Amateur Boxing Association (DEFABA) has been crowned the 2026 National Open Boxing Champions after a four-day tournament at Kaloleni Social Hall, outclassing the National Police Service. The event saw 18 teams compete, with DEFABA fielding 17 boxers and securing a dominant victory.

DEFABA's performance was marked by control, discipline, and tactical superiority, earning them admiration from fans. Their 25 points secured them the top spot, leaving the National Police Service with 22 points. With 12 of their boxers reaching the finals, including nine men and three women, DEFABA's strength was evident. Nine of these finalists clinched gold medals, showcasing a formidable talent pool and extensive training infrastructure.

As the team prepares for the upcoming National League in Nakuru, this victory serves as a significant morale boost. It underscores the continued dominance and competitiveness of KDF boxers on the national stage, a predictable outcome for a well-funded institution.
