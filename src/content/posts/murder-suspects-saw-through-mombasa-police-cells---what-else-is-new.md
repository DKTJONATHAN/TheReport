So, two alleged murderers decided they’d had enough of their cozy accommodations at Mwatamba Police Station in Nyali. Apparently, a loaf of bread was all it took to smuggle in a saw chain - a detail that screams "efficiency" if you’re the criminals, and "utter incompetence" if you're anyone else. They sawed through metal grills like they were butter, and poof, gone. Just like that. Makes you wonder what else they’re not screening at these places, doesn't it? Probably nothing good.

The police are now in a frenzy, combing the area and alerting the entire Coast region. Coast Regional Police Commander Ali Nuno confirms detectives are "pursuing several leads." Well, that’s reassuring. Meanwhile, the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) is wading into the mess, reviewing visitor logs and questioning officers. They're probably going to find out that either someone got paid off, or the guards were asleep at their posts - or both. It’s Kenya, after all.

This whole bread-and-saw saga just adds another layer to the ongoing circus of security concerns. We've seen suspects escape before, often with a bit of help, and it always seems to boil down to the same old story. It's almost as if ignoring the obvious warning signs is a national pastime. And let's not forget the common mwananchi who will likely be left dealing with the fallout, just like with [The 'Made in Kenya' Label: Another Empty Promise for the Common Mwananchi](/posts/the--made-in-kenya--label--another-empty-promise-for-the-common-mwananchi).

The suspects were supposed to be in court on Monday. Now they're out there, probably having a laugh, while the police scratch their heads and try to figure out how to avoid looking like complete fools. They’re asking the public for information, warning us not to approach these dangerous individuals. As if we'd want to go near them. The real question is, when will these “security procedures” actually start working? Or is it all just a show?
