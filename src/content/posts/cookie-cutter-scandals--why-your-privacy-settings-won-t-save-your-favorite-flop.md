Everyone is suddenly acting brand new, freaking out about privacy settings and clicking 'Reject all' on cookies as if their entire digital identity hasn't already been auctioned off in a boardroom in Silicon Valley. It is absolutely hilarious to watch these D-list celebrities and struggling artists post those "I do not give Instagram permission" status updates while they simultaneously livestream their messy breakups for clout. Honey, the Yahoo family of brands and every other digital advertising service already knows what you ate for breakfast and exactly who you are subtweeting at 3 AM.

It is all about the "Manage privacy settings" illusion that keeps the masses feeling safe. These stars want us to believe they are in control of their image, but they are just puppets for the apps they own and operate. They cry for privacy the moment a leaked video hits the fan, yet they are the same ones who turn every personal tragedy into a branded content opportunity. It is just like how [Climate Resilience is Just Another PDF for the Suits to File](/posts/climate-resilience-is-just-another-pdf-for-the-suits-to-file) because, let us be real, the people at the top only care about the metrics and the data mining, not the actual humans behind the screens.

Look at the music industry right now. Our local artists are so desperate for a seat at the table that they are signing deals with global entities that have more fine print than a legal textbook. They think they are getting a "gift" of global reach, but they are just handing over their data and their fans' habits to the highest bidder. They talk about "withdrawing consent" only after the check has cleared and the brand has already moved on to the next viral sensation with a better engagement rate and a more exploitable demographic.

We see the game, and we see the hypocrisy. You cannot demand that your "Privacy and Cookie settings" be respected while you are tagging your precise location at every high-end club in Westlands just to prove you are relevant. The "Privacy Policy" is just a suggestion to people who live for the limelight. If you really wanted to be private, you would put the phone down and stop feeding the monster, but we all know that is not happening because your ego requires the "Accept All" validation of the masses to survive.
