So, the latest tea is as bitter as it gets. A supposed Russian national has been playing director in some very private and very non-consensual movies featuring our sisters in Ghana and Kenya. While these women are out here dealing with the trauma of digital exposure, the men in suits are busy debating whether the guy is actually Russian or if his name is even real. Please. Since when did we care about a script's authenticity after the movie has already premiered to the whole world? It is the same old story: clout-chasing leads to a total breach of privacy, and the internet never forgets.

Ghana, however, is not playing. They actually summoned the Russian Ambassador, Sergei Berdnikov, to give him a piece of their mind. It is a bold move, honestly. They are treating this like a full-blown diplomatic crisis because, newsflash, leaking intimate videos is a violation of dignity. The Russians are acting confused, claiming they cannot confirm the guy's nationality. It is giving "I do not know her" energy on a geopolitical scale. At least Accra is trying to secure justice instead of just waiting for the next trending hashtag to take over.

Then we have Kenya. Oh, sweet, bureaucratic Kenya. While Ghana is making moves at the high table, our Ministry of Foreign Affairs is currently "out of office." Gender Cabinet Secretary Hanna Cheptumo gave us a statement that basically said "this is bad," but where is the actual pressure? We have the Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act, which promises fines and jail time, but laws are just ink on paper if nobody is willing to enforce them against a foreigner. It makes you wonder if we can ever achieve a high level of accountability, much like how people ask if [AFCON 2025 vs AFCON 2027: Can East Africa Match Morocco’s Gold Standard?](/posts/afcon-2025-morocco-vs-afcon-2027-east-africa-ambitions) when it comes to organizing anything of value.

The hypocrisy is what really gets me. We talk about "protecting women" and "digital rights" in every fancy hotel conference from Nairobi to Mombasa, but when a real scandal hits, everyone hides behind a "lack of an extradition treaty." It is the ultimate get-out-of-jail-free card. If you are going to let someone record your private business and then let the government shrug it off as a "domestic criminal matter," then what is the point of even having a passport? 

At the end of the day, these girls are being left to fend for themselves while the "diplomatic engagement" moves at the speed of a snail on tranquilizers. Whether he is Sergei or some random guy with a VPN, the damage is done. Ghana is at least trying to bark, but Kenya is just sitting there wagging its tail, hoping the scandal blows over before the next election cycle. Stay safe out there, ladies, because clearly, your privacy is just another file in a cabinet that nobody wants to open.
