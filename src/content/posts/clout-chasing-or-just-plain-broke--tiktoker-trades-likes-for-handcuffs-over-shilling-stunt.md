Darling, the desperation for a viral moment has officially reached rock bottom. We all know that TikTok is a breeding ground for questionable life choices, but one Nairobi content creator decided that the best way to get our attention was to treat the Kenyan shilling like scrap paper. Newsflash: just because you have a ring light and a tripod does not mean you have immunity from the law. Handcuffs were the only thing clicking on a Nairobi street corner yesterday, and frankly, it is the most interesting thing this boy has ever done.

The video, which I refuse to link because we do not reward stupidity with traffic, showed our little star performing "vandalism as art." He was literally defacing banknotes for the sake of a three-second loop. It is the ultimate irony: trying to look rich and unbothered while destroying the very thing that keeps your lights on. If you are going to be a rebel, at least have a point. Being a nuisance for the sake of an algorithm is not a personality trait, it is a cry for help from someone who clearly needs a real job.

This whole mess reeks of the same delusion we see in the upper echelons of the city every single day. We are living in a time where there are [Manners for the Elite, Bills for the Rest of Us](/posts/manners-for-the-elite--bills-for-the-rest-of-us), but this kid forgot he is firmly in the "rest of us" category. You cannot act like a lawless rockstar when you are still living in a bedsitter trying to hit ten thousand followers. The state does not care about your aesthetic or your "artistic vision" when you start messing with the national currency.

The Central Bank of Kenya has zero sense of humor, and why should they? While most of us are out here sweating to earn those shillings, some kid is cutting them up for subversive content. The authorities are finally waking up to the fact that digital conduct is real-world conduct. This arrest is a loud, clear signal that the "internet is not real life" excuse has officially expired. If you want to play with the big boys, you have to follow the big boy rules, or you will find yourself in a cell without a filter.

Predictably, the comments sections are a war zone. You have the Gen Z warriors arguing that it is just paper, while the older generation is practically calling for the guillotine. But let us be real: this is not about art or politics. It is about a generation so addicted to the dopamine hit of a notification that they have lost their grip on reality. Enjoy the arraignment, sweetie; I hear the lighting in the docks is terrible for your skin tone. This is Mercy Wambui, and I am officially done with the nonsense.
