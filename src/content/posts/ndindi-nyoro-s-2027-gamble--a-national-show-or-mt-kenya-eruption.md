So, Ndindi Nyoro is having a big think about 2027. Apparently, his next move is some kind of "defining political crossroads." Sounds dramatic, doesn't it? It’s either he solidifies himself as some big national figure or, more likely, he alienates everyone and ends up with nothing. This is Kenyan politics, folks, always a gamble.

The whole mess hinges on this ridiculous "Tutam" versus "Wantam" nonsense. You’ve got one side pushing for Ruto to get another term, and the other saying "one and done." It’s a classic case of political tribalism disguised as policy. And don't even get me started on the Western Kenya guys questioning the Speaker's "leadership style." Please, they're just looking for a piece of the pie.

Meanwhile, back in reality, some politicians are busy playing the middle ground, waiting to see which way the wind blows. Barely 13 months to the next election and they’re already hedging their bets. It’s a sad commentary on loyalty and principle, or rather, the lack thereof. It reminds me of how everything gets washed away in this country, like the [Nairobi woman drowning in plastic waste while chasing green dreams](/posts/nairobi-woman-drowns-in-plastic-waste-while-chasing-green-dreams). Promises disappear, leaving only the mess.

And then there's Ruto himself, dropping the "truce" and resurrecting the tired "Hustler vs Dynasty" rhetoric. As if we haven't heard that one before. It’s the same old song and dance, just with a different beat. While Ol Kalou might be getting Sh10 billion worth of projects, the rest of us are stuck dealing with the same old political circus. This whole election cycle is just another elaborate distraction.
