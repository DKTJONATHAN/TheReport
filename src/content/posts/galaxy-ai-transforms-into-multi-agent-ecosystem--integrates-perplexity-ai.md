Samsung is poised to fundamentally transform its Galaxy AI offering, moving towards an open, multi-agent ecosystem designed to provide a more cohesive and efficient user experience. Internal research at the company revealed that a significant majority of users, nearly 8 in 10, regularly engage with more than two distinct AI agents. This insight has driven Samsung's strategy to enhance Galaxy AI, making the management of multiple artificial intelligences much smoother for its users.

The revamped Galaxy AI will feature deep integration for various AI agents directly at the operating system (OS) level. This architectural shift is critical for Samsung to maintain its competitive edge in the rapidly evolving AI landscape, allowing for the seamless incorporation of new AI agents while ensuring a consistent and familiar Galaxy experience. By embedding agents at this foundational level, users will gain the ability to control different AIs without the friction of switching between applications or repeatedly issuing commands. Crucially, this system-level integration also provides agents with the necessary context, facilitating more natural and intuitive interactions.

Perplexity has been announced as the inaugural third-party AI agent to join this new ecosystem. Users will be able to invoke Perplexity through a simple voice command, "Hey Plex," or by assigning its activation to the side button, commonly known as the power button, on their device. This integration is slated for "upcoming flagship Galaxy devices," with strong indications pointing towards the imminent Galaxy S26 series as a primary launch platform.

Perplexity's capabilities will be deeply embedded across Samsung's suite of first-party applications, including Gallery, Notes, Calendar, Clock, and Reminder. Furthermore, its functionality will extend to certain third-party applications, demonstrating its versatility. The Perplexity agent is engineered to handle complex, multi-step workflows, promising a significant leap in productivity and intelligent assistance.

While "additional details about supported devices and experiences will be announced soon," industry observers anticipate that the One UI 8.5 update could extend some or all of these advanced features to existing Galaxy S and Galaxy Z foldable models. This would broaden the reach of the new multi-agent capabilities to a wider installed base of premium Samsung devices.

Won-Joon Choi, President, Chief Operating Officer (COO), and Head of the R&D Office, Mobile eXperience (MX) Business at Samsung Electronics, articulated the company's vision, stating, "We’ve been committed to building an open and inclusive integrated AI ecosystem that gives users more choice, flexibility and control to get complex tasks done quickly and easily. Galaxy AI acts as an orchestrator, bringing together different forms of AI into a single, natural, cohesive experience." This strategic embrace of an open, integrated AI ecosystem underscores a broader trend of significant national and corporate investment into foundational technologies, echoing initiatives seen in other critical sectors, such as the recent [launch of Project Vault for critical minerals](/posts/us-launches-12bn-project-vault-for-critical-minerals) aimed at securing essential resources for future industries.

This development follows Samsung's earlier announcement this week regarding an upgraded Bixby for One UI 8.5. The enhanced Bixby will offer more natural language processing for system setting changes, moving beyond rigid command structures, and will incorporate real-time web searches to provide up-to-date answers. This suggests a comprehensive strategy where Bixby continues to serve as a foundational system assistant, while specialized agents like Perplexity integrate to handle specific, complex tasks within a unified AI framework.
