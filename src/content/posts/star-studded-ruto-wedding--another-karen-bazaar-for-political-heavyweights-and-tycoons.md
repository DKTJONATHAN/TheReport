Ruto's Wedding Guest List: Another Day, Another Davos in Karen

So, while the rest of us are busy scrambling to pay rent and avoid Nairobi's potholes, our esteemed leaders and their even more esteemed rich friends decided to have themselves a little shindig. The Karen Blixen Museum, because where else would you celebrate the union of Ronnie Kiprono and Terry Mwendwa when you're trying to out-bling each other? The guest list read like the who's who of Kenya Kwanza, and by "who's who," I mean anyone with a fat wallet or a direct line to State House.

And the cars. Oh, the cars. Apparently, thirty Mercedes-Benz G-Wagons is the new minimum requirement for a "swanky" wedding these days. Must be nice to have a fleet of armored tanks just to pick up your wedding gift. While these high-flyers were posing for photos that will no doubt flood social media, I couldn't help but think of all the other "deals" being cooked up behind those tinted windows. It’s less a wedding, more a high-society networking event where "God bless you" really means "Let's talk about that tender." This feels like another chapter in the saga of [Same Old Wine, Slightly Dustier Bottles: Ruto’s Gazette of Rewards](/posts/same-old-wine--slightly-dustier-bottles--ruto-s-gazette-of-rewards).

Funny how everyone’s all smiles and handshakes, even Kimani Ichung'wah. While his constituents in Kikuyu were apparently having "running battles" with the police, he was busy toasting the happy couple. Priorities, I guess. It's the usual script: feast for the connected, scraps for the rest. You've got governors like Sang, Sudi, Ichung'wah, Omar, Joho, Koech, Mbarire, Waiguru, and Kihika - all supposedly serving the public, but on Saturday, they were serving themselves a heaping plate of cronyism and vanity.

Even the Cabinet Secretaries and Principal Secretaries were there, schmoozing with the tycoons. Interior CS Kipchumba Murkomen, PS Raymone Omollo, Education PS Julius Bitok - front row seats at the spectacle of excess. And let’s not forget the businessmen like DL Langat and Sam Mburu, whose names often pop up in circles where public funds mysteriously vanish. It makes you wonder if Shaffie Weru was just there for the free champagne or if he was scouting for his next gig.

President Ruto himself was there, doling out well wishes and a "unique painting." Because nothing says "congratulations" like a tasteful piece of art that probably cost more than my annual salary. While he’s gifting paintings, remember how we're still dealing with the fallout of deals like the [Cemetery Land Kickback: Ex-City Official Owes Sh13 Million After Fraudulent Deal](/posts/cemetery-land-kickback--ex-city-official-owes-sh13-million-after-fraudulent-deal)? This wedding is just another reminder that for some, life is a perpetual holiday funded by the rest of us.

So, there you have it. Another day, another display of opulence from the powers that be. While they’re celebrating unions and cementing alliances with luxury vehicles and champagne toasts, the rest of Nairobi is just trying to survive. It’s a stark reminder of who’s really in charge and who’s footing the bill for all this glamour.
