So, Willy Paul is throwing a fit because the Africa Forward Summit apparently didn't see fit to grace their stage with his… unique brand of Kenyan representation. He’s out here claiming he'd have made "historiographical" waves. Honestly, the only history he's making is a masterclass in delusion. From Mathare to the world, apparently. The world must be holding its breath, waiting for the next "historical" performance.

And then there's 2Mbili, riding in on his white horse to "advise" Willy Paul. "Revise your rate card," he says. Because clearly, the problem isn't that Willy Paul is more suited to chasing clout than gracing international stages, but that his price tag is too high for foreign concerts. It’s a classic Nairobi move: instead of acknowledging the talent gap, we just fiddle with the numbers. It’s as if adjusting a rate card will magically make him a polished international act, rather than just an overpriced local one.

2Mbili even throws in the Ruger comparison, as if international artists don't have an entire marketing and booking apparatus behind them. They don’t just "adjust their changes" on a whim. They have teams, strategy, and actual talent that aligns with global markets. This whole charade is just another echo of that time at the Moi Forces Academy Career Day - all fancy talk about futures and fortunes, but when you peel back the layers, it’s just more hot air.

The sad truth is, Willy Paul is probably one of the best performers *here*, in our little Kenyan bubble. But "best in East Africa" and "globally recognised" are worlds apart. Until artists and those around them start acknowledging that, they'll keep finding themselves excluded from "high profile events," then whining about it online. It’s the same old song and dance, just with a different venue and a slightly different complaint. At least he’s consistent in his mediocrity.
