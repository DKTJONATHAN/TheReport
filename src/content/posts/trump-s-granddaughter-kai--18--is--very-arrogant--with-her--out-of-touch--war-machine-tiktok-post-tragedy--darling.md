Honestly, can these privileged kids get any more out of touch? Kai Trump, Donald's 18-year-old granddaughter, has managed to outdo herself this time. Fresh off the tragic news of six U.S. soldiers being killed in an attack on Iran, what does this young lady decide to do? Post a TikTok featuring what appear to be "war machines" behind her. The internet, naturally, is not impressed. Commenters are calling her "very arrogant" and "unserious about other people dying." And frankly, who can blame them? It's a level of detachment that would make Marie Antoinette blush.

Some eagle eyed observers are already predicting Kai's future, accusing her of being "indoctrinated into the Trump family grifting business." They recall her previous online activities, selling "garbage" with White House rooms in the background, which they sarcastically note is probably "illegal, but why would a Trump care about that?" It’s a valid point. When you’re born into that world, rules seem to be more like mild suggestions, don't they? And let's not forget the inherent hypocrisy of restricting military families from taking photos while this pampered heiress gets to frolic with what looks like military hardware.

Now, before everyone gets too riled up, a few brave souls have chimed in to defend young Kai. They're calling her a "proud American with unique access" and insisting she "did nothing wrong." Apparently, these aren't "war machines" but "White House helicopters used to transport people." Oh, darling, if only life were that simple. It seems these defenders believe the only problem is her last name, conveniently ignoring the optics of her chosen backdrop given the current geopolitical climate. It's classic deflection, isn't it?

Kai herself, bless her heart, claims she wants "to stay out of politics completely." She even went on Logan Paul's podcast, sounding remarkably like someone trying to distance herself from the family circus. She blames social media for "extremes" and claims she's "in the middle." While she might see herself as a voice of reason, her actions speak louder than her carefully curated podcast pronouncements. It’s hard to be “in the middle” when your selfies are inadvertently fanning the flames of outrage, especially when your own father, Donald Trump Jr., is busy "going to war online." Perhaps she should take a page from her mother Vanessa's book and just… stay offline for a bit. It might be better for everyone's sanity, and especially for the families of those soldiers who are actually fighting these wars.
