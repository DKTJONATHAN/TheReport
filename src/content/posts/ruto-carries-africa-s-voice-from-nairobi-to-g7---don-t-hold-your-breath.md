So, our man Ruto is off to the G7, acting like he’s the sole voice of Africa. From Nairobi to wherever these rich folks gather, he’ll be spouting off about progress and partnership. You know the drill: big speeches, hopeful handshakes, and probably a few platitudes about shared prosperity. It’s a performance, plain and simple.

The real question is, what’s actually going to change for us on the ground? These summits are usually more about the optics for the leaders than actual solutions for the masses. We’ve seen this movie before, haven't we? Promises are made, then conveniently forgotten when it’s time for the next photo opportunity.

Remember that whole saga with the KPC fire sale? That was a prime example of how things *really* work here, a "selling the kitchen to buy a meal" situation if ever there was one. This G7 trip, I suspect, will be more of the same - a lot of noise, a few crumbs tossed our way, and the same old power dynamics playing out.

Africa Intelligence talks about these "power players on the rise" and "low-frequency signals." Well, the signal I'm picking up is that these international gatherings are more for the benefit of the elites than for the average Kenyan struggling to make ends meet. They’ll talk about investment, but will it reach us? Or will it just fill the pockets of a few well-connected individuals?

And let's not forget France and their "growing clout" in East Africa. They trot out their military deals and business summits, but it all feels like a bit of a shell game. They talk about cooperation, but it’s always on their terms, always with an eye on their own strategic advantage.

So, while Ruto is busy trying to impress the G7, let’s keep our expectations grounded. Because in this game, the "voice of Africa" often ends up sounding like a whisper lost in the wind.
