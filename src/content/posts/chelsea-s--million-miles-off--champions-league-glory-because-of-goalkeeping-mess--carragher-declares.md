So, Chelsea. They went to Paris, probably thinking they'd impress someone with their fancy new toys, and ended up looking like a bunch of amateurs who’d wandered onto the wrong pitch. Five goals conceded. Five! And the big man Jamie Carragher, bless his blunt heart, is saying what we’ve all been muttering under our breath for ages: this whole goalkeeping circus is the reason they’ll never win anything of note. It’s not rocket science, is it? You can’t build a winning team on shaky foundations, and right now, Chelsea's foundations are made of wet tissue paper when it comes to the man between the sticks.

Apparently, Liam Rosenior’s tactics were brilliant. Good for him. But what’s the point of a "fantastic" game plan when your goalkeeper decides to gift the opposition a goal with a pass so bad it looked like it was aimed at a pigeon? Filip Jorgensen, bless his Danish soul, played a blinder of an error, paving the way for PSG to run riot. And it wasn’t just him; the defence folded like a cheap suit under pressure. It’s the same old story, isn’t it? Spend a fortune on attackers and midfielders, but forget about the fundamentals. It’s like buying the latest smartphone but forgetting to get a SIM card.

Carragher is right, though. This isn't a new problem; it's been ignored for too long by the people running that club. They’re so busy chasing shiny new forwards that they’ve forgotten the importance of a reliable goalkeeper. It’s a systemic issue, he says, and I’m deadly serious too, they are a million miles off winning the Premier League or the Champions League with this setup. It's a recurring theme, really, much like how we see parliament clearing deals that seem to benefit a select few, as seen with the [PARLIAMENT CLEARS SAFARICOM STAKE SALE WITH SIX CONDITIONS](/posts/parliament-clears-safaricom-stake-sale-with-six-conditions).

The players themselves know it. Wesley Fofana practically admitted they were making "gross errors." At this level, you don't get second chances. You make a mistake, you pay for it. And Chelsea paid dearly in Paris. Three goals in the final 15 minutes? That’s not just bad defending; that’s a complete capitulation. It’s embarrassing, frankly.

So now they have to climb a mountain at Stamford Bridge. A three-goal deficit against PSG. Good luck with that. They’ll probably dust themselves off for a game against Newcastle, where they might even win, before they have to face the reality of their European exit. This season's Champions League dreams are hanging by a thread, and it's all because they can't sort out their most basic problem.
