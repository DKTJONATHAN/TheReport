So, Kenya Power, the same folks who bring us those delightful intermittent flickers, have decided March 11, 2026, is the day to "upgrade" our lives by plunging us into darkness. Apparently, it's all for "reliability." Right. Because what screams reliability more than a scheduled shutdown that ruins your entire day's plans? It's like they're auditioning for a role in a dystopian film, and frankly, they're nailing it.

This "essential network maintenance" is hitting pretty much everywhere. Nairobi's Parklands and Kilimani are getting the full treatment, with outages stretching for hours. Because nothing screams progress like being stuck in traffic without streetlights or trying to run a business on battery power. And it's not just the capital; Machakos, Uasin Gishu, Bungoma, Kakamega, Kisumu, Nyeri, and even parts of Kiambu are on this grand tour of inconvenience. It’s like a celebrity endorsement tour, but instead of freebies, we get no electricity.

What’s particularly amusing is the sheer volume of places affected. From Nzoia Sugar Factory to Koru Market, and from specific roads in Parklands to obscure villages in Kathiani, everyone’s invited to this party of darkness. It makes you wonder if the "upgrades" are just a convenient excuse to cut costs or if they’re truly trying to make our lives better. Given the track record, I’m leaning towards the former. It’s almost as predictable as the latest celebrity scandal, and frankly, less entertaining.

And let's not forget the author of this carefully curated inconvenience, Kenneth Mwenda, a digital writer with a background in finance. Clearly, his expertise in numbers doesn't translate to reliable power supply. Perhaps he should have taken a course in "Digital Investigation Techniques" to uncover why Kenya Power's network is perpetually on the brink of collapse, instead of just reporting on it. Maybe he can use his new skills to investigate where all our money for "maintenance" actually goes. It’s enough to make you wonder if their entire operation is just another [Targaryen power play]( /posts/the-nobility-of-the-lie--why-egg-s-deception-is-just-another-targaryen-power-play).

The fact that this announcement comes after a "safety advisory" due to heavy rainfall is just the cherry on top. So, not only are we going to be in the dark, but we might also be navigating it through a downpour. It's the perfect storm of inconvenience, orchestrated by the very people meant to keep our lights on. Honestly, it’s an impressive display of public relations - or rather, a spectacular lack thereof.

So, as March 11th rolls around, remember to charge your devices, buy candles, and perhaps practice your best "enlightened" poses in the dark. Because according to Kenya Power, this is for our own good. And who are we to argue with the electrical gods, especially when they’re wielding the switch?
