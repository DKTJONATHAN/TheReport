In the world of professional sports, loyalty is a fairy tale we tell children so they will buy overpriced jerseys. Victor Osimhen just gave us a masterclass in how quickly a club will turn on its star the moment things get slightly awkward. After basically carrying Napoli to a title, the club's social media team decided the best way to celebrate their top scorer was to post a TikTok mocking him with a squeaky voice filter. Because nothing says "we value you" like a multi-million-dollar brand acting like a bored Gen-Z troll on a lunch break.

It is the same old story in what I like to call [the meat grinder where the weekend's sporting spectacle is revealed as a total fraud](/posts/the-meat-grinder--why-the-weekend-s-sporting-spectacle-is-a-fraud). These clubs treat players like disposable assets until the assets start having actual feelings. Osimhen claims he was "treated like a dog" and refused to be anyone's "puppet." It is hilarious to watch the facade of "club family values" crumble the second a transfer fee is on the line. The racism and fan harassment that followed just prove that the "love" from the stands is only as deep as the last goal scored.

The drama with Napoli president Aurelio De Laurentiis sounds like a typical bad breakup where one person thinks they have an "understanding" and the other is already ghosting them. Osimhen thought there was a pact to let him leave with dignity, but instead, he found himself left out of the squad registration like an expired carton of milk. It is the ultimate power move to tell a guy who scored 26 goals that he is suddenly invisible because the paperwork got messy and the egos got bruised.

Now that he has landed at Galatasaray on a loan, Osimhen is doing the classic "reclaiming my narrative" tour. He spent months staying silent, probably hoping the toxicity would wash off, but he finally realized that in this industry, if you do not tell your story, the TikTok interns will tell it for you. He says he is happy in Turkey for now, but do not be fooled. This is just another chapter in the endless cycle of celebrity athletes realizing that to their clubs, they are just high-priced entertainment until the next shiny toy comes along.
