So, the big boys are meeting again. Emmanuel Macron, the French president who seems to be everywhere these days, is dragging 30 heads of state to Nairobi for some "Africa Forward Summit." Apparently, Kenya is so special they decided to host it outside of a Francophone country for the first time. Good for us, I guess. Or maybe good for whoever is picking up the tab for all these VVIPs.

And what's in it for Kenya? Well, it seems we're rolling out the red carpet for French soldiers to operate their fancy vehicles, weapons, and ammunition using licenses issued back home. Because, you know, our own licensing systems are clearly not up to par. While other nations are apparently getting wary of France, we're busy seeing the "good in Paris' bad." That sounds like a great strategy for national security, doesn't it?

This whole "strategically positioned to maximise international exposure" gig sounds like a lot of hot air for photo ops and handshakes. Meanwhile, back home, things are… well, you know. We've seen these grand pronouncements before. Remember [The Green Fund Mirage: Planting Promises While the Ground Stays Dry](/posts/the-green-fund-mirage--planting-promises-while-the-ground-stays-dry)? Promises are cheap, especially when they involve international partners.

And let's not forget the local drama that happens while the politicians are busy hobnobbing. We've got a 45-year-old woman being the last known person to see Dr. Job Obwaka alive, only for him to be rushed to the hospital and declared dead on arrival. Then there's Norbert Matara, who apparently decided to end Tracy Nyariki's life in 2024. This is the reality for many, far removed from the glitz of presidential summits.

The whole affair reeks of the same old song and dance. More deals, more pronouncements, more photo opportunities. We'll probably hear about "strengthened partnerships" and "mutual benefits." But for the average Kenyan trying to make ends meet, it’s just another day where the real issues get buried under a mountain of diplomatic niceties. Just another summit, another promise, another day of waiting.
