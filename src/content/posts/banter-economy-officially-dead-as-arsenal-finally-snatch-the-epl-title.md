So, Arsenal actually did it. Twenty-two years. The Invincibles. Now this. Apparently, Manchester City choked harder than a cheap Kenyan chai vendor on a Friday night, and suddenly, Mikel Arteta’s boys are holding the Premier League trophy. The laughter, the endless memes, the comforting certainty that Arsenal would find a way to spectacularly self-destruct - all gone. Poof. Like that time the government promised us affordable housing.

The fallout is predictably pathetic. Across the country, rival fans are reportedly queuing at pharmacies, desperate for anything to numb the existential dread. Manchester United and Chelsea supporters, who spent years pointing fingers at Arsenal’s drought while their own clubs were a shambles - remember when Man United was a "group project"? - are now adrift. Their favourite insult, “At least sisi si wao,” has been summarily repossessed. It’s almost poetic, if you enjoy watching grown men weep over a football match.

And for the content creators? Oh, the humanity! Their reliable bread and butter, the “Next Season FC” meme, is now as stale as yesterday’s ugali. They’ll have to *actually* watch football, maybe even *understand* it, to find new material. Imagine the sheer effort involved. No more waking up and typing out predictable jabs. This is a crisis for the entire banter economy, a gaping hole where easy jokes used to be. It's a hard knock life out here for them, much like the challenges faced by [Police FC](/posts/police-fc-s-ksh100-000-cheque-for-nabwire-s-old-academy--big-dreams--small-change).

Manchester City fans, accustomed to the Premier League being their personal ATM, are experiencing a unique form of corporate pain. It's like their M-Pesa reversal request got denied. And let’s not even start on Chelsea, who are probably contemplating signing the trophy to an eight-year contract. The real kicker? Arsenal are also in the Champions League final. If they win that too, don’t expect any forgiveness or pleasantries from their fans. They’ll be walking around like they personally invented oxygen, issuing statements instead of speaking, and generally becoming insufferable. This whole thing is just another reminder that in football, like in life, sometimes the most predictable narrative turns out to be a complete lie.
