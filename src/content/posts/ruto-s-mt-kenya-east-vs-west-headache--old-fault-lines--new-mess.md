Ruto's Mt Kenya East Vs West Headache: Same Old Politics, New Mess

Another political spat brewing in Mt Kenya, and guess who's caught in the middle? President Ruto, as usual. Looks like his grand plan to keep the region's backing is already going south faster than a cheap suit in a Nairobi downpour. The old East versus West divide, always lurking, is back with a vengeance, and CS Ruku is just the spark to light the bonfire.

It’s the same old story, isn’t it? Leaders jostling for power, trying to carve out their little kingdoms. Ruku’s grand idea of Mt Kenya East going it alone sounds like another pipe dream designed to get him a bigger piece of the pie. And Ruto? He’s trotting out the same tired line about his "long-standing engagement." Please, as if that means anything when the crumbs are being fought over. Remember how promises are made and broken, just like those beds in Mutuini? (/posts/the-mutuini-photo-op--400-beds-and-a-million-broken-promises)

Then you have the former DP, Gachagua, feeling all emboldened by some court judgment. Now he’s talking about leading an "opposition front." That’s rich. It’s all about ego and influence, not about what’s actually good for the people. And this governor asking Sifuna to join hands with Gachagua? That’s like asking a hyena to guard the chicken coop. Pure political theatre.

The government loyalists were hoping some ruling would weaken Gachagua's hold. Ha! Seems like it’s done the opposite, fuelling his resolve. And the former DP's backers are worried about "apathy" in 2027. Of course, they are. Apathy is what happens when people realize their votes don't change a damn thing, especially when politicians are too busy playing their petty games. It's a classic Nairobi move, really - making a lot of noise while the real issues, like how to make a quick buck online while regulations sleep, (/posts/nairobi--sharp-boys--online-schemes--minting-millions-while-regulations-sleep) are ignored.

Ruto needs to remember that this region isn't some monolithic block he can just charm with rhetoric. These are real people with real concerns, tired of being used as political pawns. This East versus West squabble is just another symptom of the rot, another headache for a president who already has more than he can handle.
