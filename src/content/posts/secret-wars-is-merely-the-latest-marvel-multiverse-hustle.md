Let's cut through the corporate spin. Marvel, the purveyor of endless, interconnected mediocrity, is hyping up "Avengers: Doomsday" and "Avengers: Secret Wars" as the grand 

The fact that they're avoiding the "Part I/Part II" label is a transparent attempt to distance themselves from the perceived failure of just repeating the same story twice. But let's be honest, are we really expecting anything truly groundbreaking? Feige calls it "as ambitious a project as we’ve ever embarked on." Of course he does. Every sequel, every new phase, is always pitched as the most ambitious yet. It's the marketing department's job to generate excitement, and in the absence of genuine innovation, they resort to hyperbole.

The "Multiverse Saga," as it's been so grandly titled, has been a sprawling, often incoherent mess. The promise of exploring alternate realities has largely served as an excuse for fan service and a convenient way to reintroduce characters and actors they no longer have the rights to, or simply want to exploit again. The mention of "veteran actors from Marvel’s cinematic history" returning is not a sign of creative genius, but a clear indicator that they're leaning on nostalgia and the goodwill of past successes because their current offerings are proving to be increasingly stale.

So, we have "Avengers: Doomsday" arriving in December 2026, followed by "Avengers: Secret Wars" in December 2027. That's a five year gap between these alleged culminating events. This isn't about a narrative crescendo; it's about maximizing profit by stringing audiences along with promises of a grand finale that will likely be as drawn out and anticlimactic as everything else Marvel has churned out in recent years. Prepare for more CGI battles, more convoluted plotlines, and more opportunities for Marvel to milk its fanbase for every last dollar. This isn't the end of an era; it's just another well-worn path leading to more sequels.
