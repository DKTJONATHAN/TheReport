The Beckham brand is built on two things: soccer and the illusion of a perfect family. But lately, the cracks are wider than Victoria’s vintage sunglasses. For Brooklyn’s 27th birthday, David and Victoria took to Instagram to post the usual "we love you" nostalgia, complete with teary emojis and blurry swimming pool photos. It is a classic move from the celebrity PR handbook - when your kid is no longer on speaking terms with you, just post a picture of them as a baby so the public thinks you are still the "Good Parents." Honestly, it is more transparent than a sheer runway gown.

This whole saga feels like a bad episode of [RECYCLED REALITY: WHY ITV IS DRAGGING US BACK TO SOUTH AFRICA FOR MORE SCRIPTED DRAMA](/posts/recycled-reality--why-itv-is-dragging-us-back-to-south-africa-for-more-scripted-drama), only with more expensive hair extensions and better lighting. While Posh and Becks are busy curating their "united front" on social media, Brooklyn is over here dropping bombs about his wife, Nicola Peltz, being targeted by his mother’s ego. From the alleged last-minute cancellation of a wedding dress to the bizarre claim that his parents pressured him into giving up legal rights to his own name, this is not just a family spat. This is a full-blown war over who owns the Beckham narrative.

Even the siblings are getting involved in the digital manipulation. Cruz Beckham posted his own little trip down memory lane, featuring the brothers in beekeeping outfits. It was a cute attempt at emotional blackmail, but it only highlights how desperate the situation has become. When you have to resort to "remember when we were friends?" posts to get a response from a sibling, you know the group chat is permanently muted. Victoria’s heart-emoji approval of the post just adds another layer of performative parenting to the pile.

And because no A-list drama is complete without famous friends inserting their unasked-for opinions, we have Gordon Ramsay, Snoop Dogg, and Marc Anthony weighing in. Ramsay apparently decided to play the "good parents" card, which resulted in Brooklyn blocking the chef’s kids on social media. It is almost funny to see a grown man unfollowing childhood friends because their dad took his parents' side. It just proves that the Peltz-Beckham household is a very different world now, one where loyalty to the new wife outweighs decades of family history.

Snoop Dogg thinks they will overcome their differences because David is a "family man," but Snoop is clearly smoking something different if he thinks a few Instagram Stories can erase the trauma of public call-outs. Brooklyn’s January post was a tactical nuke, accusing his parents of protecting their image at the expense of innocent people. He is tired of the fake smiles, and quite frankly, so are we. The Beckhams can post all the throwbacks they want, but you cannot Photoshop a relationship back together once the publicists have already drawn their lines in the sand.
