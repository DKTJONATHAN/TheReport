Nairobi has decided to become an aquarium again, and as usual, the price of admission is human life. At least 23 people are dead because the sky opened up and our so-called world-class infrastructure folded like a cheap suit. We have people being swept into rivers and others getting electrocuted because in this city, even the rain is a death sentence. The police are busy giving statements about destruction while the rest of us are just trying not to become part of the body count.

In the industrial neighborhood of Grogan, a man was found pinned under a car after the Nairobi River burst its banks. Imagine that being your end, stuck under a chassis because some greedy developer built on riparian land and the city council looked the other way for a few brown envelopes. It is the same old story of institutional rot. Just like [The Blue Uniform Trap: Where Your Dignity Goes to Die](/posts/the-blue-uniform-trap--where-your-dignity-goes-to-die), our systems are designed to fail the common citizen while the people in power watch from the dry heights of the city.

Now the military is out in the streets. They are rescuing people from cars on roads that are supposed to be the pride of the region. Mombasa Road and Uhuru Highway have turned into literal rivers. If you are stuck in Westlands, you are basically on an island. It is embarrassing that every time a cloud turns grey, the capital of East Africa shuts down and starts counting corpses. The "hub" is just a collection of stalled engines and broken dreams today.

Flights are being diverted to Mombasa because even the airport cannot handle a bit of water. You can have all the fancy terminals you want, but if the runway is a swimming pool, you are just a very expensive parking lot. The Meteorological Department warned us, but in Nairobi, warnings are just suggestions we ignore until the water is at our necks. They say it is a natural disaster, but the lack of drainage is a man-made catastrophe.

Last year we lost hundreds, and here we are again, doing the same dance. The government will tell you to avoid flooded streets like it is a choice we make for fun. We do not need advice; we need a city that does not try to kill us every April. But do not worry, the elite will stay dry while the rest of us keep our heads above the murky water of progress until the next storm hits.
