So, the big news from the presidential phone lines is that Al-Sisi of Egypt, a man who probably dreams in hieroglyphs and water rights, has told Ruto of Kenya that the Nile is, like, super important. As if we didn't know. It's Egypt's lifeline, their precious, and they're not sharing easily. Ruto, ever the diplomat, nodded along, babbling about consensus. Sounds nice, doesn't it? Like a Nairobi matatu conductor promising "just one more passenger."

Then they pivot to Sudan. Apparently, Al-Sisi wants the fighting to stop, calls for peace, and blah blah blah. He’s all about Sudan’s sovereignty, territorial integrity, and institutions. All well and good, but let's not forget who's been meddling and who benefits from a certain kind of regional chaos. Ruto, meanwhile, is showering Al-Sisi with praise for his "role in supporting Sudan" and "containing regional tensions." It's a mutual admiration society, built on carefully worded statements.

They also touched on the Horn of Africa, agreeing to "intensify political consultation and coordination." That's fancy talk for "let's meet more and pretend to solve things." It’s the same old song and dance, a bit of diplomatic posturing to make it look like something is actually happening. You know, like [Ruto's tribalism ban: Another day, another empty promise for Kenya](/posts/ruto-s-tribalism-ban--another-day--another-empty-promise-for-kenya) - sounds good on paper, but the reality on the ground? Different story.

And, of course, the handshake continues. Al-Sisi is chuffed about Egypt and Kenya’s "notable development in relations," particularly in economic, commercial, and investment sectors. Translation: they want to do more business. Ruto is on board, naturally. More deals, more money, more opportunities for those in the know. It's all about lifting ties, just like selling the family silver to fix a leaky roof; you get the immediate cash, but the long-term implications are murky.

They concluded the call with a commitment to "continuing and intensifying joint communication and coordination." In other words, they’ll keep talking. Maybe they'll even have another call. Don't hold your breath for any earth-shattering revelations or genuine breakthroughs. It's just politics, a carefully choreographed performance where everyone smiles and says the right things, while the real issues simmer beneath the surface, much like the Nile water itself.
