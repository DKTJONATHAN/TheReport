So, the noise about fuel prices is back, and guess who’s leading the choir? The usual suspects, Rigathi Gachagua and David Maraga, are out there pointing fingers at President Ruto, screaming about him "profiting" from some so-called oil scandal. It's almost cute, if it wasn't so predictable. Ledama Olekina, bless his cynical heart, actually bothered to look at the facts and realized that, surprise, surprise, the world doesn't revolve around Kenyan politicians and their whingeing.

Ledama, a man who apparently remembers being part of the "making noise" crowd himself, now admits that when the actual facts about fuel pricing came out, he "adjusted." That’s what they call critical thinking, folks. Apparently, it’s a rare commodity these days, especially when progress isn't free and requires actual payment, not just endless complaining from the sidelines. He points out that unlike some, he actually processes information instead of just repeating it.

Then Maraga and Riggy G jump in, demanding to know how the President is profiting. From what, exactly? According to Olekina, Ruto doesn't control global shipping costs or set fuel prices on the international market. It’s not some magic button he presses. If these two learned gentlemen have receipts showing direct profit, they should produce them. Otherwise, they're just peddling the same tired political slogans that get us nowhere, much like the endless cycle of governance by vibes we've seen before.

Olekina’s take is that these taxes, the ones that annoy everyone so much, are actually building the roads they drive on to their fancy homes. The alternative? Going back to potholes, gridlock, and the usual litany of excuses. Rigathi Gachagua, meanwhile, is demanding taxes be removed, and Maraga is spouting about "cartels" and "adulterated oil." It’s all very dramatic, but Olekina’s asking for proof, for receipts. Anything more substantial than hot air and selective amnesia would be a refreshing change.
