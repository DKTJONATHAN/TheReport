Waking up in Nairobi during the rains is less about refreshing the earth and more about wondering if your neighbor is currently floating toward the Indian Ocean. As of Sunday, the body count is at 28, and if you think that number is final, you haven’t been paying attention to how this city is built. On March 6, 2026, we watched cars turn into expensive metal fish, submerged because our drainage systems have the capacity of a soda straw. It is the same tragic comedy every year, just with different faces in the water.

Police Commander George Seda is out there doing the grim math, pulling five more bodies from the muck on Saturday evening. They say more are hidden in flooded parking lots and inaccessible corners. It is a lovely thought, isn't it? Dying in a basement because someone at City Hall decided that planning was just a suggestion. While search teams poke around the silt, residents are left to salvage what is left of their lives from the receding filth in downtown.

Enter the President, right on cue with the standard multi-agency emergency response script. William Ruto has ordered the military and everyone else to start moving people from high-risk areas. It is funny how these areas only become high-risk after the houses are underwater. We are masters at [buying loyalty with our grandchildren's taxes](/posts/buying-loyalty-with-our-grandchildren-s-taxes) while failing to build a single sewer line that actually works when a cloud looks at us funny. Now they are talking about releasing relief food, which is great if you can cook maize while sitting on your roof.

The Ministry of Health is now singing the cholera and malaria song, warning us that the water we are wading through is basically a toxic soup. Contaminated water, mosquitoes, and sanitation systems that have completely checked out. Mary Muthoni wants us to be careful, as if we have a choice in where the sewage flows. Meanwhile, the Kenya Meteorological Department says the rain is just getting started. If you thought 28 was a bad number, just wait until the next downpour hits the same broken streets next week.
