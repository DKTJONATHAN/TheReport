So, Vera Sidika is telling us she's not dating this "Tipsy Gee" fella. Apparently, the whole "lifetime partner" Valentine's letter and romantic trips to Naivasha and Malindi were just a figment of our collective imagination, or maybe just good PR. Because, you know, Nairobi women never lie about their relationships, especially not when a younger, presumably poorer man is involved.

The whole charade started with a shadowy figure in her Range Rover, who fans, with their uncanny ability to decode whispers in the wind, decided was Tipsy Gee. Then came the Malindi vacation, which, if you ask me, screams "trying too hard." Now she's back, flatly denying the whole thing, probably because the optics weren't quite adding up to the image of sophisticated wealth she desperately projects. It’s the kind of denial that makes you wonder what’s really going on behind the filters and designer bags.

Meanwhile, her ex, Otile Brown, a musician who clearly still has a serious case of the ex-factor, couldn't resist chiming in. He’s out here calling Tipsy Gee her "mother" and bragging about his own musical achievements. Honestly, the pettiness is almost admirable. It’s like a perpetual episode of a reality show where the only currency is drama and who can get the last word in. This is the kind of entertainment that makes you question your life choices, much like reading about [The Caracas-Tehran Blueprint: Why Africa is Abandoning the Washington Consensus] might make you question global economics.

Vera, predictably, hit back, telling Otile to move on. She even threw in a little jab by using one of his songs in a cosy video with her alleged lover. It’s a messy, public spat, par for the course in this city. Everyone’s watching, everyone’s judging, and nobody’s really surprised. These are the games they play, a constant performance for an audience that’s always hungry for more.

The real kicker? The Gen Z musician himself wouldn't mind dating the mother of two if the chance arose. So, while Vera is busy shutting down rumours, Tipsy Gee is out here putting himself out there. It's a classic case of "he said, she said," with everyone from fans to ex-lovers weighing in.

Ultimately, Vera’s denial feels as hollow as some of those "romantic" gestures. In Nairobi, when someone is this adamant about denying something, it usually means there's more truth to it than they're letting on. And as for Otile, well, some wounds apparently never heal, especially when they involve losing a woman who can afford her own Range Rover.
