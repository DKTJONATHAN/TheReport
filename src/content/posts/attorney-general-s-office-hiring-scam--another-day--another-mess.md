You think the Attorney General's office is supposed to uphold the rule of law? Ha! Turns out they're too busy breaking it, one fake job at a time. An audit, mind you, ordered by a court because clearly, they can't be trusted to police themselves, has unearthed a hiring scam so deep-rooted it's practically part of the furniture. Candidates who never even bothered to apply are getting jobs, while those with actual qualifications are left staring at their degrees. It’s the Kenyan dream, folks: connections and corruption, not competence.

This isn't just a minor oopsie; it's a full-blown constitutional breach. The Public Service Commission, bless their diligent hearts, found that the State Law Office has been cheerfully ignoring every rule in the book. Apparently, "shortlisting criteria" and "academic qualifications" are optional extras in their recruitment playbook. It's like going to a restaurant that serves you lukewarm water and calls it a fine dining experience. They're mandated to advise the government? More like advising themselves on how to get their cronies hired.

The juicy details are enough to make you spit out your chai. We're talking about people getting State Counsel II positions without a Bachelor of Law degree, or a Kenya School of Law diploma, or even being an admitted advocate. And Legal Clerk Assistants? Some were hired without basic computer skills, and others were appointed despite not even being on the initial applicant list. It makes you wonder if the actual advertisements were just for show, a little theatre to pretend there’s a process. It’s a familiar tune, really, not unlike the worries surrounding how certain individuals land promotions, especially when [President Ruto Warns No Mercy for Oil Cartels as Fuel Scandal Investigation Deepens](/posts/president-ruto-warns-no-mercy-for-oil-cartels-as-fuel-scandal-investigation-deepens).

And the interview panels? They didn't bother with pass marks, rankings, or even explaining why someone got the job. It’s a free-for-all where the "selection criteria" are as clear as a Nairobi smog day. Different applicant lists, some with qualifications, some without, all magically leading to the same outcome: jobs for the chosen few. It’s a masterclass in how to undermine service delivery, drain public funds, and make sure that confidence in the public service is lower than the price of fuel during an election year.

This audit is just confirming what we've known all along: merit is a foreign concept here. It follows a court nullifying over 200 promotions last year because they lacked competitive recruitment and ignored diversity. Justice Byram Ongaya saw right through it, declaring amendments that gave the Attorney General HR power unconstitutional. He's basically told them to stop playing games and let the PSC do its job. But let's be honest, even the PSC has been busy purging forged certificates. It’s a revolving door of incompetence and dishonesty.

So, what's the takeaway? The Attorney General's office, the very body meant to safeguard the law, is orchestrating a hiring free-for-all. It's a stark reminder that in Kenya, the system is rigged, and the only qualification that truly matters is knowing the right people. Don't expect anything to change. This is just another Tuesday in Nairobi.
