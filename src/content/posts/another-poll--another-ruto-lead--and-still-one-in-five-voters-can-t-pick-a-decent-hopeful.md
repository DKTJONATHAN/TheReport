So, Infotrak has given us another set of numbers, and surprise, surprise, President William Ruto is leading the pack for 2027. Thirty-two percent, they say. Like anyone expected different, really. It's not necessarily a ringing endorsement, is it? More like the default setting when no one else has bothered to make a compelling case. Men seem slightly more enamoured than women - thirty-four percent versus thirty percent. Perhaps men have a higher tolerance for the same old song and dance.

Then you look at the 'opposition.' Kalonzo Musyoka trailing at a distant thirteen percent, Edwin Sifuna and Fred Matiang'i neck and neck at twelve. Rigathi Gachagua, the current Deputy President, limps in at a pathetic four percent. Babu Owino and Martha Karua? Barely registering. It's less a crowded field and more a collection of scattered individuals who haven't figured out how to share a single spoon of ugali, let alone a national vision. Where's the grand alternative everyone keeps hoping for?

But the real kicker, the one that tells you everything you need to know about the state of affairs in this country, is the eighteen percent who are 'undecided.' A whole fifth of the electorate, just shrugging their shoulders. Women, bless their hearts, are even more likely to be undecided at twenty percent. It's not about deep philosophical deliberation, is it? It's plain exhaustion. It's the silent scream of 'Is this all there is?' when every new promise just turns into another fuel scam or [energy top brass arrested](/posts/energy-top-brass-arrested--ps-liban--epra-boss--md-sang-nabbed-in-fuel-scam). They're not undecided because they're weighing options, they're undecided because the options are all equally unappealing.

And three percent would rather not disclose their choice. Cowards, or just too tired to articulate their disappointment? Infotrak's Johvine Wanyingo can address journalists all he wants about 'significant blocs' and 'up for grabs,' but what he's really showing us is a nation perpetually stuck. Ruto has a lead, yes, but the truly overwhelming sentiment isn't support, it's a profound, collective sigh. 2027 isn't shaping up to be a race for change, it's just another carousel ride on the same broken political merry-go-round.
