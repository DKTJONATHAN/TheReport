POLICE HOLD 45-YEAR-OLD WOMAN WITH DR O‘BWAKA AS HE KICKS THE BUCKET IN KITENGELA

The usual Nairobi drama: a prominent doctor, a mysterious woman, and a sudden death. Veteran obstetrician and gynaecologist Dr. Job Obwaka, 83 years old and apparently already battling his own demons, has shuffled off this mortal coil. He apparently collapsed on some "personal errands" in Kitengela and was carted off to the hospital in an ambulance, only to be declared dead on arrival. Convenient, isn't it?

Now, the police, bless their diligent hearts, have detained Beatrice Wangare, a 45-year-old woman who was apparently in his company. They're calling her a "person of interest." In Nairobi, that usually means she knows more than she's letting on, or perhaps she was just the last person to see him breathing. The report from Kilimani Police Station, filed after the incident, is as dry as the stories we've covered about infrastructure projects that cost billions while basic services remain a pipe dream, like the [Kipchoge Keino Stadium](posts/kipchoge-keino-stadium--a-ten-year-tease-that-costs-billions-while-our-stars-run-on-dirt).

Of course, it wouldn't be Kenya if there wasn't a conspiracy theory brewing. Former Law Society of Kenya President Nelson Havi is already flapping his gums, blaming "government harassment" and "trumped-up charges" for the doctor's demise. Apparently, interference in Nairobi Hospital's management and legal woes contributed to his declining health. It’s always someone else’s fault when the powerful kick the bucket, isn't it?

Meanwhile, the Health Cabinet Secretary and the medical unions are falling over themselves to praise the departed. "Towering figure," "invaluable contribution," "distinguished practitioner." All very touching, I'm sure. But let's not forget that Dr. Obwaka was recently detained himself over governance issues at the very hospital they're now eulogizing. It seems the medical fraternity has its own share of backstabbing and power plays, much like any other business in this city.

The body is now cooling its heels at Lee Funeral Home, awaiting a postmortem. Because, you know, we need to establish the *exact* cause of death. As if his age and a history of medical conditions weren't enough of a clue. But hey, in Nairobi, the truth is often more complicated, and sometimes, as with the leaks surrounding software updates, the narrative can get twisted by corporate hypocrisy and legal snafus. We'll see what the investigators uncover, or conveniently miss.
