The reality of the situation is that Bitcoin is currently down more than 40 percent from its previous peak. While it might marginally outperform traditional stocks or gold in a vacuum of a few trading days, its long-term trajectory remains a series of failed breakouts and broken promises. Global capital is not seeking refuge in an unstable ledger; it is retreating into the very systems of control that crypto-evangelists claimed to replace. 

Information has become the ultimate currency, and entities like Bloomberg are the gatekeepers. As the fine print of their service agreements reminds us, your data is the actual product being traded. While retail investors stare at candles and charts, the real decision makers are leveraging information networks to cement their hold on the physical and digital world. This mirrors the same expansion of power seen in other sectors, such as how [THE INFRASTRUCTURE OF EXPULSION: ICE USES SHUTDOWN CHAOS TO CEMENT POLICE STATE REAL ESTATE](/posts/the-infrastructure-of-expulsion--ice-uses-shutdown-chaos-to-cement-police-state-real-estate) highlights the way institutional control tightens during periods of perceived instability.

International markets are now waking up to the fact that Bitcoin is a lagging indicator of global anxiety rather than a solution to it. The dream of a decentralized world is being crushed under the weight of institutional integration and the requirement to waive your rights just to access a news feed. When the next major conflict erupts, do not expect a digital coin to save your portfolio. Expect the data-brokers to sell your habits to the highest bidder while the asset classes you trusted continue their slow, predictable decline into irrelevance.
