So, Benni McCarthy, bless his heart, has decided on a 23-man squad for the upcoming FIFA Series in Kigali. Another series of "friendlies" designed to shape the squad for, you guessed it, more "competitive assignments." It’s the usual song and dance, isn’t it? A blend of local talent that probably won't make it abroad and international players who are already showing us how good they aren't.

Clarke Oduor is back, apparently. And Zak Vyner and Zech Obiero are set to grace us with their debuts. Let's hope they're not expecting the red carpet. This whole affair is billed as crucial preparation for the 2027 Africa Cup of Nations, which Kenya is co-hosting. Because nothing screams "ready for greatness" like a few exhibition matches against Estonia, Grenada, and Rwanda. It’s like training for a marathon by jogging to the corner shop.

The team reports to camp on the 22nd and ships off to Rwanda the next day. They'll be building "squad chemistry" and getting "exposure." Translation: They’ll be wasting time and money that could probably be better spent elsewhere, maybe even on actual football pitches that aren't full of potholes. It’s a shame they can’t learn from the infrastructural successes seen in other sporting events, like the [Premier League Market Escalates Amid Milano-Cortina 2026 Winter Success](/posts/premier-league-market-escalates-amid-milano-cortina-2026-winter-success).

The list of players is as predictable as a downpour during a Nairobi wedding: Gor Mahia, Kenya Police, and a few names that sound vaguely familiar from previous disappointments. Then you have the ones playing "abroad," which in this context, means anywhere outside of Kenyan soil. Let’s just hope McCarthy has a magic wand, because this squad is going to need it to pull anything resembling success out of the bag. And the Football Kenya Federation, ever the optimist, is out there claiming they are "dedicated to developing and promoting the beautiful game." They're developing new ways to disappoint us, that's for sure.
