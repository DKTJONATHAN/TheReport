Archers Post is the latest backdrop for the Justified Accord 2026 circus, where the US Army and our very own KDF have decided to play doctor for a weekend. They call it a Medical Civic Action Program, but let us be real: it is a live-action training manual. While 1,200 residents lined up for Role 1 care, the real goal was building readiness for the U.S. joint force. Apparently, our mothers and children are just convenient austere variables for a Massachusetts National Guard medic who cannot find enough pregnant patients back in the States to practice on.

The propaganda is thick enough to choke a camel. They are bragging about bringing portable ultrasounds to places that usually have nothing, as if identifying twins in a remote dispensary changes the fact that those mothers will be back to zero resources the moment the camo-trucks roll out. It is the same old story of [Healing the Nation or Just Healing Their Bank Accounts? The Divas Festival Cash Grab](/posts/healing-the-nation-or-just-healing-their-bank-accounts--the-divas-festival-cash-grab) where the optics look great on a military photo op, but the long-term reality for the Samburu community remains as dry as the Laresoro dust.

The Americans are quite open about it if you read between the lines. Staff Sgt. Kendall admitted they come here because they cannot practice on pediatric, geriatric and pregnant patients in a military setting back home. So, we are the lab rats? We are the resource-limited setting used to sharpen their aerospace medical technician skills? It must be nice to treat a few hundred people, tick a box for multinational interoperability, and then fly back to a country where the taps actually run and the hospitals are not considered austere.

Meanwhile, the KDF is learning new skills and preparing for operational activities. Translation: they are learning how to look busy while the U.S. Africa Command runs the show. They pulled some teeth and did some scans, but the interoperability they keep preaching is just military-speak for making sure everyone knows how to take orders when the next crisis response happens. It is less about healthcare and more about making sure the gears of the war machine are well-oiled.

By the time the dust settles and the photo-op at Archers Post Sub-County Hospital is over, the austere environment will still be there. The local medical teams will go back to having no ultrasound, no dental equipment, and no Massachusetts National Guard to consult with. But hey, at least some Lt. Col. got to see twins on a screen and some Airman got to practice on a real-life Kenyan child before heading back to the non-austere world. Just another day of being a footnote in someone else's training exercise.
