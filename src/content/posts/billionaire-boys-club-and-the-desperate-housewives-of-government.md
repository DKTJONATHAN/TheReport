If you thought the latest celebrity breakup was messy, you clearly haven't been watching the Sh3.5 billion reality show known as the Anglo-Leasing trial. The Kamani brothers, Deepak and Rashmi, just pulled the ultimate "get out of jail free" card thanks to the Court of Appeal. While the rest of us are out here struggling with the cost of living, these two international businessmen have managed to put their graft case on ice. It is the kind of plot armor you only see in bad soap operas, where the villains just never seem to face the music no matter how many billions go missing.

Then we have Dr. Fred Matiang’i, who is out here sounding like a retired club bouncer who still thinks he has the power to check IDs at the door. "I guarded the exam, and it wasn’t stolen," he claims, trying to pivot his old reputation into some kind of political shield. Honey, we all know that the transition from guarding exams to guarding votes is just a desperate attempt to stay relevant in a room that has already moved on. He is giving major "jilted ex" energy, refusing to accept that the spotlight has shifted elsewhere.

Speaking of spotlights, the big boss himself is tired of the cast members fighting behind the scenes. Ruto has officially declared that he will not tolerate government work being sabotaged by internal rivalries. It is giving very much "Real Housewives of State House" vibes. The internal friction is so loud that even the president has to step in and play mediator. However, despite his warnings, [Ruto's 2027 Problem: Why Kenya's President Faces Historic Electoral Backlash](/posts/ruto-2027-problem-electoral-backlash) continues to be the main topic of conversation in every corner of the city, regardless of how much he tries to keep his team in line.

The audacity of our local "stars" reached a new peak this week when six governors decided they were too famous to show up for their appointments. They completely snubbed a summons from the County Public Accounts Committee, proving that once you get a little bit of power, you think you are above the law. It is the same energy as a D-list influencer ghosting a brand deal because they think they are too big for a local activation. They are happy to take the public's money, but when it is time to explain where it went, they are suddenly "too busy" to attend.

Finally, we have the most pathetic move of the week - a certain PS who is reportedly begging for his life. Instead of standing on his own merits, he is out here reaching out to MPs from his backyard to save him from the "wrath of the House." It is the political equivalent of a disgraced YouTuber calling up their old high school friends to leave positive comments on their apology video. If you have to beg your homeboys to save your job, you probably weren't doing it right in the first place. The hypocrisy is truly the only thing that is consistent in this town.
