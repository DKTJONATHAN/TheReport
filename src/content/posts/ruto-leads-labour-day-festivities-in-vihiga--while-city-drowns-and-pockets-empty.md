So, President Ruto is off to Vihiga for Labour Day. Apparently, the whole government, from CS Mutua down, has "logistical arrangements" sorted. Probably means they've got enough bottled water and security to keep the riffraff out, while they all pat themselves on the back. Meanwhile, back in Nairobi, Mombasa Road is turning into a paddling pool thanks to the rain, and traffic is chaos. Just another Tuesday, I suppose, but at least it’s not Vihiga.

Labour CS Alfred Mutua is all smiles, talking about "dignity of labour" and "economic growth." Sounds lovely, doesn't it? He’s inviting everyone to come celebrate. I'm sure the good people of Vihiga are thrilled to host the whole circus. It's a "show of unity," they say. Unity while some are struggling to afford *unga* and others are dodging muggers on Councillor Opundo Road? Right.

Francis Atwoli, bless his heart, is actually speaking sense. He says unions are pushing for a pay rise because fuel prices, transport, food - everything is going up. Workers are being squeezed dry. They're "hopeful" the President will bless them with more money during this grand Vihiga jamboree. I wouldn't hold your breath. Remember Gachagua admitting Kenyan workers are burdened by heavy taxation and stagnant wages? Yeah, that hasn't changed. It’s like [The Green Colonization: Kenya’s Silent Sacrifice for Global Net-Zero](/posts/the-green-colonization-kenya-s-silent-sacrifice-for-global-net-zero) - everyone else benefits while we pay.

And while the big shots are off in Vihiga celebrating "unity," nine suspected gang members were rounded up in Nairobi. Just a reminder of what's really happening on the ground. Oh, and the Bomas International Convention Complex will be done by April 2026. Fantastic. More concrete, more grand plans. Meanwhile, wet weather is expected to continue, making life even more miserable for those of us stuck in the city. Kenya’s economic growth eased slightly in 2025, by the way. Just thought I’d throw that in.
