KUCCPS Cut-Off Points For Bachelor Of Laws And Universities Offering It - More Heartbreak Than Hope

So, you want to be a lawyer in this glorious republic? You think a fancy LLB from a public university is your ticket to riches and a life of intellectual debate? Get real. This article, churned out by some journalist who probably thinks she’s making a difference, is basically a glorified report card on who gets to play pretend courtroom drama. It’s all about cut-off points, my friends, and most of you will be left staring at the wall, wondering where your 30-something cluster points went.

The "transdisciplinary knowledge" and "social, political, and economic contexts" they ramble on about are just fancy words for "we'll teach you enough to parrot the system." And if you think getting the degree is the hard part, wait till you hear about the Kenya School of Law. One year of that nonsense, followed by what, more exams? It's a whole elaborate system designed to filter out the weak, or more likely, those who don’t have connections. This whole process feels like a scene from a bad political drama, where deals are made behind closed doors, not unlike the scandals we've seen before, proving that [Expect More Empty Promises](/posts/sh2bn-for-victims-of-police-brutality-as-ruto-and-oburu-form-coalition-team---expect-more-empty-promises).

The numbers themselves are a joke. Kenyatta University leading the pack with a ridiculous 40.746 points? For law? What are they expecting, Nobel laureates straight out of high school? And the University of Nairobi and Mount Kenya University are right there, probably hoping to churn out more graduates who will end up in paralegal roles, or worse, just hanging around, degrees collecting dust. It's a rat race for a few prestigious slots, while the rest are left scrambling for the crumbs.

Then you have the "lower tier" institutions, where the cut-off points dip slightly. Suddenly, degrees from places like Moi University at a measly 33.665 are "on offer." It makes you wonder what quality of "lawyers" are being produced. Are they going to be the ones defending the common man, or will they be the ones drafting the loopholes for the powerful? It’s a gamble, and most likely, a losing one for the student.

The entire system seems rigged from the start. KUCCPS, the gatekeeper, sets the bar so high that it automatically excludes a huge chunk of hopefuls. You need to be a near-perfect student, with all the right subject combinations, just to *apply*. It's not about who has the sharpest mind or the most passion; it's about who ticked all the boxes on a bureaucratic checklist.

So, if you’re dreaming of a legal career, my advice is simple: have a backup plan. A big, fat, realistic backup plan. Because this LLB dream is turning into a nightmare for many, and the only people making a killing are those running the institutions and the endless hoops you have to jump through.
