The era of objective truth is over, and we killed it with a "submit" button. The recent demonstration by a journalist who tricked Google Gemini and ChatGPT into crowning him a champion hot-dog eater is not a quirky anecdote about tech-savviness. It is a chilling autopsy of the modern information ecosystem. We have built a global intelligence infrastructure that is incapable of distinguishing between a satirical blog post and a verifiable fact, and we have done so because speed is more profitable than accuracy.

The fundamental failure of AI is its indiscriminate hunger for data. In the desperate race for market dominance, companies like Google and OpenAI are scraping the internet with zero regard for the quality of the "food" they feed their models. This creates a feedback loop of stupidity. A single, unvetted webpage can rewrite the reality served to millions of users in under 24 hours. While the hot-dog example is trivial, the underlying mechanism is being weaponized to promote fraudulent medical claims and predatory financial scams.

This systemic rot is an extension of the same pattern seen in global development and politics. Much like the empty promises detailed in [The Mandera PR Circus: Big Words, Small Checks, and the Usual Script](/posts/the-mandera-pr-circus--big-words--small-checks--and-the-usual-script), Silicon Valley provides a shiny, high-tech facade to mask a void of substance. We are being sold a "knowledge engine" that is actually just a sophisticated mirror for our own digital trash.

The real catastrophe, however, is human behavior. Data shows that users are increasingly relying on AI-generated summaries without ever clicking through to a source. We are witnessing the total surrender of critical thinking. When people stop verifying information, they become easy prey for anyone with enough SEO knowledge to trick a crawler. 

Silicon Valley’s defense is usually that they are "fixing" the models after the fact. But a truth engine that requires constant manual intervention to stop it from lying is not a tool-it is a liability. We have entered a period where the loudest and most "optimized" voice wins, regardless of whether that voice is telling the truth or just hallucinating for the sake of engagement. If you can "hack" reality in twenty minutes with a fake blog post, then reality no longer exists. It is just a product of whoever gamed the algorithm last.
