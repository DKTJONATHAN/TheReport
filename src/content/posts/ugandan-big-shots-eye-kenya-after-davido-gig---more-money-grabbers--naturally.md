Nairobi. Always a sucker for a shiny new thing, aren't we? Now it’s the Ugandans who have decided our city is the next goldmine. Fenon Events, Swangz Avenue, Talent Africa Group - all these big names from Kampala are quietly setting up shop here. Why? Because we keep throwing money at them, that's why.

And the latest to join the party is SEEV AFRICA, a Kigali outfit. Apparently, they’re the masterminds behind that Davido concert in Kigali. And the Basketball Africa League after parties. Now they’re sniffing around Kenya, ready to “expand their tentacles,” as if we don’t have enough people trying to squeeze us dry. They’re even eyeing awards for some Brazilian music - because that’s *exactly* what we need more of.

It’s all about the aesthetics changing, apparently. More makeup, more colour, bolder outfits. Sounds familiar, doesn’t it? It’s the same old song and dance, just with a different accent. They’ll promise you the world, put on a show that looks good on Instagram, and then disappear with the profits, leaving us with the bill and a hangover.

This is just another chapter in the never-ending story of who’s going to profit from Kenya's disposable income. We’ve seen it with tech, with real estate, and now it’s events. It makes you wonder if anyone’s actually looking out for the local talent, or if we’re just a convenient ATM for our neighbours. It’s a tired script, and honestly, we should know better by now. It’s not that different from how these "recycled scandals and pocket-change bail" keep happening in our own backyard, just a more glamorous, foreign version.

So, brace yourselves. The Ugandan invasion is here. They’ve seen a profitable ground, and they’re not here to admire the view. They’re here to take your money. Just like everyone else.
