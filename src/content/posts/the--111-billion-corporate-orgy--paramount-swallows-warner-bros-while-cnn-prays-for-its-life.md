Oh, look, another day in Hollywood where the rich get richer and the "art" gets sold to the highest bidder like a used car at a backyard swap meet. Paramount Skydance just dropped a cool $111 billion to swallow Warner Bros Discovery whole, outmaneuvering Netflix in a six-month mid-off. Netflix essentially looked at the price tag and said "thanks, but we have enough mediocre reality shows to fund." This isn't just a merger; it is a hostile takeover of our collective nostalgia, and I for one am exhausted by these corporate musical chairs.

Imagine a world where Harry Potter, Jon Snow, and Ethan Hunt all report to the same billionaire daddy. That is the reality coming in 2026. Paramount is grabbing HBO, CNN, and Nickelodeon, creating a media monster that will probably reboot "Friends" for the fiftieth time just to pay off the interest on that massive loan. It is the same kind of soulless commercialism we see everywhere, much like how [The Pulpit For Sale: Kiengei’s Holy U-Turn](/posts/the-pulpit-for-sale--kiengei-s-holy-u-turn) reminds us that even the most "sacred" institutions are usually just chasing a fat check.

The real tea, however, is brewing at CNN. The newsroom is currently a collection of vibrating, anxious wrecks because their new boss is David Ellison. David is the son of Larry Ellison, a man who treats Donald Trump like a long-lost brother. CNN has spent years being the thorn in Trump's side, and now they are being handed over to a Republican donor's kid. Mark Thompson is out here sending "stay calm" emails to the staff, but we all know that is corporate-speak for "start updating your LinkedIn profiles, sweeties."

Let us not forget that Paramount already owns CBS News, which recently had to pay Trump $16 million to settle a lawsuit over a Kamala Harris interview. If you think David Ellison is going to protect "editorial independence" while his father's friend is breathing down his neck, I have a bridge in Malindi to sell you. They are already talking about merging CBS News and CNN behind closed doors, which is basically code for "trimming the fat and making sure nobody says anything too spicy about the right wing."

Of course, the regulators still have to weigh in. California's Attorney General Rob Bonta is making noise about an investigation, and the U.S. Department of Justice might actually do their jobs for once. But in this industry, money usually shouts louder than ethics. By the third quarter of 2026, the media landscape will be one giant, beige monoculture run by a handful of guys who probably do not even know how to use a streaming app. Hollywood is officially dead; we are just watching the vultures pick at the carcass.
