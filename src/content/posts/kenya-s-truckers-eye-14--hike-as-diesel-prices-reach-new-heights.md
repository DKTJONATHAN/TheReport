So, the word on the street is that our truckers are looking to slap a 13-14% surcharge on their services. Why? Because diesel prices have decided to go on a joyride to the stratosphere, setting a new record. This isn't some abstract economic report; this is your food, your building materials, your everything getting more expensive. The folks running these trucks are already juggling pennies, so don't expect them to absorb this hit. It’s going straight onto your bill.

This whole mess is like a domino effect, but instead of cute little plastic toys, we’re talking about the price of tomatoes and Irish potatoes going up. Inflation is already inching up, and while it’s technically below the target - who are they kidding? - the non-core pressures, the actual stuff we buy, are accelerating. And it’s no surprise when you consider how much we rely on imported food staples. Wheat, rice, cooking oil - all of it is already facing a global cost crisis before it even lands at Mombasa. Now, add Kenyan freight costs to that already grim picture.

The government tried to put a bandage on this gaping wound. They fiddled with VAT and threw some shillings from the Petroleum Development Levy Fund at the problem. Bless their hearts. But even with these ‘measures,’ diesel still managed to hit a historic high. KSh 206.84 a litre in Nairobi. That’s not a typo. It’s a direct consequence of all the drama in the Middle East and the shipping chokeholds. It’s a clear signal that the external shocks are hitting us hard, and our local efforts are just trying to keep the ship from sinking entirely.

This is precisely the kind of mess that makes you wonder about bigger issues. Remember when we talked about [THE PETROLEUM GUILLOTINE AND THE FEDS ETERNAL INTERREGNUM]? This is the practical, everyday manifestation of that. The costs trickle down, and eventually, they land squarely on the shoulders of the ordinary Kenyan trying to make ends meet. Don't expect a sudden price drop. Expect more of the same, probably worse.

The only comfort you might find is that you’re not alone in this. Everyone’s going to feel the pinch. From the smallest kiosk to the biggest factory, the cost of moving anything is going up. So, when you see that price hike on your next purchase, just remember where it all started: a record surge in diesel prices, fueled by global chaos and leaving us all to pick up the tab.
