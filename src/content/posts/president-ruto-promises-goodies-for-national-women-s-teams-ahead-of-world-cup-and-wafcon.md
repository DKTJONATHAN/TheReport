PRESIDENT RUTO PROMISES A FEW SHINIES FOR THE WOMEN'S TEAMS, BUT DON'T HOLD YOUR BREATH FOR THE FOLLOW-THROUGH

Ruto, fresh from the State House breakfast buffet, has thrown some crumbs to the Junior Starlets and Harambee Starlets. Apparently, he's heard their pleas, promised to match the men's financial handouts, and even thrown in medical insurance. It’s the usual song and dance: big promises, photo ops, and the faint hope that this time, things might actually change. We've seen this movie before, haven't we? Remember when [Dennis Oliech's Kawangware Loitering Sparks Concern - Ujana Ni Moshi, Indeed.](/posts/dennis-oliech-s-kawangware-loitering-sparks-concern---ujana-ni-moshi--indeed) and promises were made?

The captains, bless their hopeful hearts, reminded the President of the sweet deal Harambee Stars got during their CHAN 2024 run - a cool million per win. Now, the Starlets, facing powerhouse teams like the USA and hosts Morocco, are expecting similar treatment. Ruto assures them they "will not walk alone," which in Kenyan political speak usually translates to "we'll think about it, maybe, if the budget allows, and if it doesn't interfere with our personal slush funds." The Ksh 33 million in allowances already paid is a nice start, but let's be real, it’s pocket change compared to the potential payouts for the men.

The real kicker is the medical insurance. Players are worried about career-ending injuries, and Ruto has guaranteed comprehensive coverage, including surgery. This is a genuine concern, unlike the financial "match tokens" he's so eager to pledge. But will it actually materialize beyond this State House pronouncement? Considering how often we hear about [The Great Government Heist: When Your Impounded Bike Becomes the Inspector’s Side-Hustle](/posts/the-great-government-heist--when-your-impounded-bike-becomes-the-inspector-s-side-hustle) and other tales of mismanagement, skepticism is not just warranted, it's practically a survival skill.

So, while the Starlets head off to their World Cup and WAFCON campaigns with a renewed sense of optimism - or perhaps just a temporary reprieve from the usual financial anxieties - the rest of us will be watching. Waiting to see if these promises translate into actual support, or if they’ll fade away like [The Speculative Limbo of a Dying Digital Dream](/posts/the-speculative-limbo-of-a-dying-digital-dream). History, after all, has a rather tedious way of repeating itself, especially when it comes to government pledges.
