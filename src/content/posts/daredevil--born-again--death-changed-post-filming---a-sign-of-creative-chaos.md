So, the big death in *Daredevil: Born Again* wasn't even in the script. Apparently, the showrunners, in a moment of shocking ineptitude, decided to kill off Deputy Mayor Daniel Blake (Michael Gandolfini) *after* filming, tacking it on in the editing bay. This wasn't a strategic narrative pivot; it was a desperate scramble to make the story feel "earned," according to showrunner Dario Scardapane.

The original plan was for Blake to survive an encounter with Buck Cashman (Arty Froushan), only to have Cashman lie to Kingpin about it. Scardapane deemed this "kind of meh and a non-story," which, frankly, says more about his inability to craft compelling arcs than Blake's supposed lack of narrative importance. The idea that a death, a pivotal plot device, is a "mock-up" created in the eleventh hour underscores the shallow foundation of this production.

We're told the actors took the news well, with Gandolfini even agreeing it was the "right choice." Convenient. It's easy to agree when your character's demise suddenly gives the story a pulse it otherwise lacked. The showrunners admit they "just couldn't bring ourselves to do it" during filming, implying a sentimental attachment that actively hindered narrative integrity. This isn't a testament to their love for the character; it's a testament to their inability to commit to difficult storytelling decisions until forced by the looming deadline of the season finale, which, by the way, airs May 5th on Disney+. Expect more such "surprises" from a show that clearly prioritizes last-minute fixes over genuine planning.
