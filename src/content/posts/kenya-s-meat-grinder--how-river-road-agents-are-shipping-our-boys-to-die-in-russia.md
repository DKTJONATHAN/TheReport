If you think the hustle in Nairobi is bad, try being a truck driver from Kimende who ends up in a trench in Ukraine holding a bazooka instead of a steering wheel. Dancan Chege thought he was heading to Moscow to deliver cabbages to barracks, but the only thing he was delivering was his own life to the front lines. It is the classic Kenyan story: you lose your job, you ask a "friend" for a connection, and before you can say "Hustler Fund", you are in Russia getting fitted for a combat uniform. The agents are eating steak in Westlands while our boys are eating dirt in the "red zone".

Our very own National Intelligence Service finally woke up and smelled the gunpowder, reporting that over 1,000 Kenyans have been recruited for this mess. Eighty-nine are currently playing real-life Call of Duty, while dozens more are either in hospital or just "missing". Of course, the Russian Embassy says it is all "propaganda", but tell that to the family of Charles Waithaka, who came back in a story, not a coffin, after stepping on a landmine. While the government claims to be "repatriating" people, the truth is that recruitment agencies are colluding with rogue airport staff and embassy officials. It is a well-oiled machine designed to turn Kenyan desperation into Russian cannon fodder.

This level of betrayal by our own officials is nothing new. Whether it is selling out our youth to foreign wars or the [Exposed: CHAN 2024 Ticketing Scam](/posts/CHAN-tickets-Scam) where fake tickets and political hoarding locked out real fans, the theme remains the same: the common man is always the pawn. In the Russia-Ukraine case, the stakes are just higher. Instead of losing a few thousand shillings at Kasarani, you are losing your limbs in a forest near the border. The recruitment agents operate openly because they know the law is a suggestion for those with the right connections.

Dancan Chege had to literally act mad to survive. He spent twelve magazines shooting at trees and then started eating bullet casings just so they would send him to a military hospital. That is the level of creativity required to survive a Kenyan job-opportunity these days. Now he is back in Kimende, asking the government to hire him as a cop because he knows how to handle a bazooka. It would be funny if it was not so tragic. The man went looking for a driving job and came back a trained mercenary, while other mothers are burying empty graves in Nyeri because their sons' bodies are rotting in a country they could not find on a map.

The Prime Cabinet Secretary Musalia Mudavadi says he is going to Moscow to "emphasize" that this must stop. We have heard it all before. While they fly business-class to "negotiate", more boys are being promised well-paying jobs in the Gulf only to land in Sharjah and then Moscow. If the government really cared, they would start by cleaning up the recruitment agencies on River Road instead of making grand statements at press conferences. But as usual, in this country, you are on your own. If a deal sounds too good to be true, it probably involves a landmine and a Russian commander telling you to fight or die.
