The opening ten minutes were a masterclass in how to be boring. Neither Shabana nor Kenya Police wanted to risk anything, probably worried about getting their fancy boots dirty. It was all about keeping shape, not conceding, and generally looking as uninspired as possible. Possession was measured, which is just fancy talk for "nobody knew what to do with the ball."

As the match trudged on, things didn't exactly explode into life. Both teams "grew into the contest," which apparently means they managed a few more sterile passes. Clear-cut chances? Forget it. This was a defensive discipline clinic, a masterclass in how to be so solid you're practically a brick wall no one bothers to knock down. Shabana did manage to miss the target a couple of times, proving that even when they get a sniff, they're not exactly clinical.

The second half continued the thrilling pattern of absolutely nothing happening. Chances were as rare as an honest politician. Defensive solidity was the order of the day, which is code for "let's not lose by doing something stupid." It was a spell in front of goal so uneventful, you could have used it as a sleeping aid. It’s like watching paint dry, but with more running around.

Even in the final ten minutes, with the pressure supposedly mounting, the cautious approach persisted. Neither side was brave enough to commit, leaving the contest finely balanced - balanced in its utter lack of excitement. Late attempts to snatch a winner were probably just desperate flails of tired limbs. The result? A glorious 0-0 draw. Just what everyone came to Gusii Stadium for. It’s almost as predictable as some of the scams we’ve seen crop up, like the [CHAN 2024 Ticketing Scam](/posts/CHAN-tickets-Scam).

So, Kenya Police stay in third, Shabana in fourth. Points gained, but spirits? Probably as deflated as a week-old party balloon. This is football in Kenya, folks. Expect the unexpected, unless the unexpected is a thrilling attacking display, in which case, expect the predictable.

And for those wondering what happens to the money spent on tickets for this riveting spectacle, well, that's a story for another day.
