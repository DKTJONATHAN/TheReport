Ruto and Italy's President Mattarella Meet in Rome - More Handshakes, Same Old Promises?

WARNING: Another high-level meeting where leaders talk big about cooperation, but don't expect your pockets to feel any richer.

President Ruto, fresh from charming the Italians, is back in Kenya after hobnobbing with President Sergio Mattarella in Rome. The official story is all about "deepening cooperation" in trade, investment, climate action, and infrastructure. Sounds lovely, doesn't it? Like a well-rehearsed play where everyone smiles and shakes hands, promising a brighter future that usually never quite arrives for the common mwananchi.

They explored "new opportunities" to boost diplomatic and economic ties. Right. Because what Kenya really needs is more talk and more handshake photos, not tangible results that actually improve the lives of people struggling with everyday expenses. This visit is part of Ruto's grand plan to "expand global partnerships" and "attract investment." We've heard this song and dance before, haven't we? It's the same hustle for foreign cash, while back home, things like [fuel costs](https://www.the-star.co.ke/news/2024-04-18-ruto-explains-why-fuel-costs-more-in-kenya---because-were-middle-income-apparently/) remain a constant headache.

Italy, apparently, is a "key European Union member" and an "important partner." They're talking renewable energy, security, and development financing. All very important things, no doubt. But let's be real, how much of this translates into jobs for the youth or cheaper electricity for our homes? More likely, it means more contracts for foreign companies and more opportunities for… well, you get the picture.

The big takeaway from this Roman holiday? Expect more "future state visits, trade agreements, and joint development programmes." More paperwork, more delegates flying in and out, and more grand pronouncements. Meanwhile, the reality on the ground for most Kenyans remains unchanged. It’s a cycle of high-level meetings and empty promises, leaving us to wonder if all this international diplomacy is just for show.
