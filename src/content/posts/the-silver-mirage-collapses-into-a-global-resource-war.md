The so-called green revolution is the primary culprit. Central planners in Brussels and Washington mandated a transition to solar and electric vehicles while ignoring the basic chemistry required to build them. Solar panels are now silver monsters, devouring nearly a third of global supply. Next-generation cells are even hungrier. It is a self-defeating prophecy where the solution to climate change consumes the very resources needed to survive it.

The irony deepens with the arrival of solid-state batteries. Samsung's breakthrough promises 600-mile ranges but requires a kilogram of silver per unit. If even a fraction of the market adopts this, it will vacuum 60 percent of global mine production. The tech industry is driving a high-speed train toward a brick wall made of silver, and they have no brakes.

Mining cannot save us. Silver is mostly a by-product of copper and lead. You cannot simply flip a switch to produce more. If copper demand sags, silver production drops, regardless of the price. It is a supply-side trap that no amount of fiat printing can escape. The earth does not care about your monetary policy or your ESG targets.

While the civilian sector starves, the military-industrial complex and the AI gods are taking their cut. Modern missiles are one-way trips for silver; once they explode, that metal is gone forever. Meanwhile, AI data centers are inhaling silver for thermal management and conductivity. We are literally burning our future to power chatbots and guidance systems.

The paper empires of the COMEX are finally exposed as the frauds they always were. For years, banks sold silver they did not have, counting on investors to never ask for delivery. Now that the physical vaults are empty, the disconnect between paper promises and physical reality is absolute. The gold-silver ratio is collapsing, and the poor man's gold is becoming the most strategic asset on the planet.

The globalists promised a world of shared abundance and digital efficiency. Instead, they have delivered a resource-starved wasteland where only the most ruthless can secure the materials of the future. Much like the sterile narcissism of Nordic domesticity (/posts/the-sterile-narcissism-of-nordic-domesticity), our industrial system is a hollow shell, obsessed with its own reflection while the foundation rots away. The era of cheap silver is over, and the era of resource wars has begun.
