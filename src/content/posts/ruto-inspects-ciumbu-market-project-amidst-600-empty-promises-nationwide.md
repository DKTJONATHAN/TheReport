So, the President decided to grace Murang'a with his presence, poking around a market construction site they’re calling "modern." Apparently, this is all part of a grand plan to help the Mama Mbogas and other hustlers of this nation. They say KSh 2.5 billion is being sunk into 25 such markets in Murang'a alone, and 600 nationwide. You know, for "dignified and organised trading environments." Sounds lovely, doesn't it? Just like those other grand initiatives that disappear into thin air.

The big talk is about these 600 markets being "underway." Underway where? In the budget documents or on the ground? Because around here, we’ve seen plenty of groundbreaking ceremonies and very few finished products. This Ciumbu Modern Market is supposed to house over 200 traders. Let's hope it actually gets built and doesn't end up another white elephant, much like some of the promises we’ve heard before. It’s all about strengthening the informal sector, they say, by giving them "clean, secure, and well-equipped trading spaces." Right.

The President’s team is busy linking these market projects to boosting small businesses and creating jobs. They're also conveniently brushing past concerns about mismanagement of funds, like that whole school feeding programme fiasco. It's amazing how a shiny new market project can distract from where the money *actually* went. It reminds me of how some influencers might promise you the world with a ring light and a prayer, but you end up with nothing. Remember those Nigerian prophets? This feels similar, just with more concrete and less holy water.

Once these markets are "completed," we're told they'll ease congestion, improve hygiene, and boost trade efficiency. That’s the official line. But for now, it's just another development tour, another round of photo opportunities, and another hefty sum of public money being poured into projects that might, or might not, ever benefit the common mwananchi. The real question is, after all the fanfare, will these markets actually be worth the billions they’re costing, or will they just be another chapter in the endless saga of government promises?
