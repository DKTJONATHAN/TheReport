So, the Football Kenya Federation, bless their hearts, have finally managed to assign some bodies to officiate the SportPesa League matches for March 21st and 22nd, 2026. Round 25, they call it. Just another few games before the "business end" of the season, which I'm sure will be as predictable as a chai vendor's morning grind. They've even trotted out the "experienced center referees" and their entourage of assistants, fourth officials, and commissioners. Because, you know, that's what separates a decent league from one where you can practically smell the rot.

Let's not get too excited. They've published a whole list of names - Dominic Weda, Edwin Mangoli, Samuel Kuria, Adan Tadicha, Evas Kirimi, and so on. A veritable who's who of people who will be on the pitch while the real drama unfolds off it. It’s a shame these officials don't get the same attention as some of the "elites" who are constantly getting fleeced in the shadows. You can read all about that here: [THE ART OF THE SHAKEDOWN: HOW THE ELITE GET FLEECED IN THE SHADOWS](/posts/the-art-of-the-shakedown--how-the-elite-get-fleeced-in-the-shadows).

The FKF even drones on about how they're "dedicated to developing and promoting the beautiful game." Beautiful game? More like the beautifully corrupt game, if you ask me. They talk about "smooth matchday operations" and "security measures" with their cookies. It's all just noise to distract you from the fact that the real game is being played in boardrooms and back rooms, not on the pitch.

Honestly, what are we supposed to expect? The same old officiating blunders, questionable calls, and the underlying feeling that some results are more pre determined than others. It’s a system designed to keep itself afloat, not to actually elevate Kenyan football. Just another weekend of predictable drama, served up with a side of official incompetence. Don't say I didn't warn you.
