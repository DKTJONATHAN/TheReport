So, the Kenya Police Bullets are still at the top of this FKF Women's Premier League nonsense. Shocking, I know. They're playing Vihiga Queens today, who apparently have "given up the fight" for the trophy. What else is new? It’s always the same few teams at the top, while the rest are just making up the numbers, hoping to avoid the relegation scrap. This is what they call competition?

The Bullets are two points ahead of Ulinzi Starlets, who are apparently their only "threat." Threat? They just got hammered 3-0 by the Bullets last weekend. That's not a threat, that's a public humiliation. The Bullets have played fewer games and still managed to win more. It’s almost as if they’re actually trying to win. Imagine that.

Vihiga Queens, meanwhile, are lurking in fourth place, apparently aiming for a "top-five finish." Sounds like a plan for the also-rans. They’re coming off a win against Kayole Starlet, who are themselves hovering above the relegation zone. It’s a constant cycle of mediocre teams trying to avoid the bottom. Reminds me a bit of that [fuel import scandal](/posts/former-energy-chiefs-held-over-sh4-8-billion-fuel-import-scandal-released-on-police-bail) - lots of noise, not much substance.

Then you have Kayole Starlets playing Trinity Starlets, and Ulinzi Starlets facing Gideon Starlets. Gideon Starlets are apparently fighting for their Premier League lives. Good for them. Ulinzi needs to be at their best, because apparently, these bottom-feeders might actually put up a fight. And in the other match, Kibera Soccer Women are playing Bungoma Queens. Mid-table clash, you say? Exciting. It's all just a lot of running around a field, isn't it?
