Oh, look who is back in the headlines and, shocker, it is not for healing the blind or making money appear in bank accounts. Our favorite "Major 1," Shepherd Bushiri, is currently doing the most on social media to distance himself from the latest Special Investigating Unit report. The SIU has basically called out the Department of Home Affairs for being a glorified vending machine for visas, and it turns out some of our high-flying "prophets" and artists like Nigerian rapper 3GAR were allegedly at the front of the line with some very creative paperwork.

Bushiri is predictably screaming "witch-hunt" from the safety of Malawi, claiming he did everything by the book at the South African embassy. He is throwing around numbers like R90 million to justify his stay, essentially saying that if you have enough cash, the law is just a suggestion. It is the same kind of elitist posturing we see when things go south for the rich and famous. This whole "I bought my way in legally" defense feels just as suspicious as the [High-Flying Rhetoric and Empty Pockets: The Addis Ababa Special](/posts/high-flying-rhetoric-and-empty-pockets--the-addis-ababa-special) we have seen from other regional players who promise gold but deliver only glitter.

The drama gets even darker with Bushiri using the death of a disgraced immigration officer to score sympathy points. He claims the man was "innocent" and was hounded to his grave just for being a church member. It is a classic move from the Bushiri playbook: pivot from the fraud allegations to a tragic narrative where he is the persecuted hero and the government is the big bad wolf. Meanwhile, the SIU says these officials were basically turning their desks into ATMs for foreign influencers and "businessmen" who did not feel like waiting in line like the rest of us.

Home Affairs Minister Leon Schreiber is not playing along with the spiritual warfare narrative, though. He has already fired 20 officials and is looking to make sure they never work in government again. They are coming for those "irregularly obtained" visas, which means the "Major" and his rapper buddies might find their South African welcome mat permanently retracted. If only they could prophesize their way out of a forensic audit, but it seems the SIU is one congregation that is not buying what the prophet is selling.

Honestly, the hypocrisy is the only thing that is truly "substantial" here. You cannot claim to be a man of God while your name keeps popping up in reports about document fraud and systemic manipulation. Whether it is 3GAR trying to maintain his "boss" lifestyle or Bushiri protecting his empire, the truth is that the "favors" they claim they did not ask for are exactly what keep the wheels of corruption turning. Grab your popcorn, because this "witch-hunt" is just getting started, and the fire is looking very real.
