US Rights Group Raises Alarm Over Kenya's Passport Scandal, Cites Ruto's Shady Links with Sudan's RSF



Image Caption: So, Kenya's selling passports? Honey, I thought we were talking about designer handbags, not documents that apparently legitimize war criminals.





Image Keyword: Passport

So, the Human Rights Foundation, bless their little crusader hearts, has decided to chime in on Kenya's alleged passport free-for-all. Apparently, we're out here issuing passports to Sudanese war financiers and folks linked to the so-called "genocidal" Rapid Support Forces. And get this, they're not just pointing fingers; they're implicating President Ruto himself, suggesting this whole tango with the RSF might be aiding and abetting them in dodging international sanctions. Talk about a blockbuster scandal, darling.

Now, the specifics are juicy. We're talking about over 25 Sudanese nationals, including the younger brother of RSF commander Hemdeti, who's been sanctioned by the US for, you know, financing a paramilitary group and allegedly orchestrating ethnically motivated killings and sexual violence. Because why wouldn't you give a passport to someone with that kind of resume? It’s almost as if our immigration department is auditioning for a role in one of those dramatic international thrillers, like the ones I've seen about the managed decay of an American industrial corpse.

And it’s not just one rogue employee, folks. A leaked list from the Kenya Immigration Department shows a whole slew of Sudanese and Zimbabwean nationals with "questionable credentials" getting the passport treatment between August 2024 and February. We're talking RSF links and a Zimbabwean accused of corruption. Activist Boniface Mwangi is out here saying we're selling these passports for "the price of peanuts." Honestly, if that's the price, I might need to update my portfolio.

The outrage is palpable, with Kenyans rightfully questioning the integrity of our documents and leaders warning of a damaged international reputation. But guess who's been suspiciously quiet? The immigration department, under Director General Evelyn Cheluget, and Interior Cabinet Secretary Kipchumba Murkomen are playing deaf. This whole saga is more dramatic than a poorly scripted Kenyan soap opera for Asian bookies, and now that an international body is involved, the pressure is definitely on for someone to spill the tea.
