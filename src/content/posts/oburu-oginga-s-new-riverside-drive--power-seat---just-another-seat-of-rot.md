So, Oburu Oginga has found himself a new "seat of power" on Riverside Drive. Apparently, it's all part of his grand plan for 2027. One can only imagine the serious strategizing happening behind those shiny doors, or perhaps just more elaborate planning for who gets the best parking spot. It's all very reminiscent of the late Prime Minister's Capitol Hill operations, which, let's be honest, were more about the optics than actual progress for the common mwananchi.

The talk is that this new "nerve centre" is all about a pre-coalition with Ruto's UDA. Because, of course, what Kenya needs right now is more political musical chairs and less focus on the actual issues plaguing us. We've seen this movie before, folks. They promise change, they consolidate power, and then we're left picking up the pieces. Remember those "Ten-Point Lies"? Some things never change, do they? It's enough to make you wonder if there's any point in even paying attention, though I suppose some still cling to faith over frets.

Meanwhile, the Executive Office of the President, bless its heart, successfully argued that removing advisors would make it "inoperative." Makes perfect sense, right? Like a car without wheels is inoperative. The only thing inoperative is the idea that these people are actually working for us. State House, the undisputed champion of conspicuous consumption and outright contempt for integrity laws, continues to set the bar for what *not* to do.

And what of the loyal foot soldiers? Some MPs, like Godfrey Osotsi and Caleb Amisi, are apparently planning to skip some grand event. Probably got better things to do, like counting their blessings or figuring out how to survive the next price hike. It's a shame, really, that such dedication to the party often means a blind eye to the rot that festers within. It’s a cycle as predictable as tobacco tears leading to billion-shilling dividends for the select few.

This whole Riverside Drive spectacle just screams more of the same old song and dance. While they're busy creating new "seats of power" and forging unholy alliances, the rest of us are just trying to make ends meet. One can only hope that one day, the real power will lie with the people, not with those who occupy plush offices and make grand pronouncements. But until then, we'll just have to endure the usual political theatre.
