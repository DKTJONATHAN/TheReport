The President, in his infinite wisdom and just in time for his endless international excursions - a quick count puts him at 24 trips between late 2024 and September 2025, for those keeping score - has decided to move the national emergency response and disaster recovery function. Out of the Ministry of Public Service and smack into the Ministry of Interior. Convenient, isn't it? Especially when there's a cool Sh13.5 billion earmarked for "victims" of natural calamities.

Naturally, this has sent shivers down the spines of some top brass in the Public Service ministry. Word on the street is they were rather comfortable with their hands hovering over that emergency pot. Procurement linked to natural disasters is a goldmine, my friends, and it seems some were already planning their next mudslide-funded holiday. Now their dreams are washed away like so many homes in a flood.

It’s a classic Nairobi game, this shifting of dockets. The Constitution might run the IEBC, but it seems political expediency and the allure of public funds run the presidency. And while Ruto’s busy touring Ndhiwa, with local leaders suddenly forgetting politics and clamoring for "development requests" - which, let’s be honest, usually means development for their own pockets - the real power plays are happening behind closed doors.

This whole by-election drama in Ndhiwa, meant to test UDA’s internal democracy, is just another sideshow. The real test is whether these officials can spin this move as anything other than a move to control vast sums of money. It’s a stark reminder that when it comes to power and public funds, especially in a place like Kenya, the only constant is cynicism. It makes you wonder if some of these "crises" are more manufactured than natural, much like the AI-generated content you might stumble upon these days. Perhaps it's time to focus on the real dangers, like the perils of police custody.

The question remains: who will truly benefit from this shuffling? Is it the disaster victims, or the well-connected officials who know how to play the system? Given the history, I wouldn’t bet on the former. It's just another day in the Republic, where the money flows where the power shifts.
