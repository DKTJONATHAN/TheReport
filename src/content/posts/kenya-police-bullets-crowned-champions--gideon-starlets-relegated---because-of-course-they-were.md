So, Kenya Police Bullets are champions. Big surprise. They snagged the FKF Women's Premier League title on Sunday, likely after a thrilling 1-0 victory against Madira Soccer Assassins. Apparently, a single goal was enough to decide the fate of the season. Meanwhile, the Nyanza-based trio of Kisumu Allstarlets, Kisped Queens, and Gideon Starlets are heading down to the lower league. Just another day in the office for Kenyan football, where destiny is decided by a sliver of a goal difference.

It was a hard-fought 1-0 win, they say. For 41 minutes, neither side could find the net. Then, Elizabeth Okwaro, with an assist from Puren Alukwe, did the honours. The Law Enforcers ended up with a comfortable eight points above second-placed Ulinzi Starlets. Trinity Starlets rounded out the top three with their "impressive season." Impressive enough, I suppose, to avoid the usual Kenyan drama of last-minute collapses.

The real tragedy, if you can even call it that, is for Gideon Starlets. They managed a goalless draw against Trinity Starlets, but it was a draw that sealed their fate. Fifteen points in the bag, but an inferior goal difference meant they're heading down. It's a harsh reminder that in football, like life, sometimes the best you can do isn't good enough. While Police are basking in their moment of glory, the relegated teams are left picking up the pieces. It's the cycle of the game, and frankly, it’s as predictable as the sun rising in the East.
