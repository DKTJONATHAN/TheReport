So, Trent Alexander-Arnold, fresh from being snubbed by England - as if anyone cares anymore - decides to play the benevolent superstar after leaving Real Madrid training. He pulls over for a gaggle of fans, probably expecting a shower of adoration. Instead, he gets the flashing blue lights. Classic. It’s like trying to have a nice chat with a street vendor in Nairobi and suddenly realizing you’ve blocked traffic for three hours.

Apparently, the bright spark thought pulling over in the middle of the road was a great idea for an autograph session. Who knew that stopping your car willy-nilly could cause problems? It’s not rocket science, mate. It’s the same kind of thinking that leads to chaos when local politicians arrive, causing more disruption than their actual policies. It’s a wonder they didn't just arrest him on the spot and demand a bribe, the way things sometimes go down, remember when Kanja and the Rwanda Police Chief met in Nairobi? Same energy, different country.

The police, bless their hearts, had to step in because, shocker, people were getting annoyed. Traffic was backing up, and suddenly the jolly fan interaction looked like a potential accident waiting to happen. This is the sort of thing that makes you shake your head and think about how some people, even with all the money and fame, still manage to be completely clueless about basic common sense.

In the end, it was all just a bit of a drama. The police had a stern word, probably explained that Valdebebas isn't exactly a picnic spot, and then, get this, they asked for *selfies*. The very people who just told him off are now lining up for a snap. Talk about a complete turnaround. It just goes to show, you can’t please everyone, and sometimes, even the law enforcement can’t resist a bit of celebrity.

Still, at least he’s back in Madrid, presumably to get ready for a game. Probably more useful than sitting around moping about not being picked for England. And who knows, maybe next time he’ll find a car park. Or perhaps he'll just drive past, like most sensible people do when they don't want trouble.
