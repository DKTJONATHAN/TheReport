## Rivian Enhances R1 Lineup with Comprehensive Software Update

Rivian has rolled out a substantial over-the-air software update, version 2026.03, for its R1S SUV and R1T pickup electric vehicles. This update introduces a suite of new features and performance enhancements designed to benefit both new and existing owners, irrespective of their vehicle's generation or battery configuration. The focus is on improving convenience, expanding performance capabilities, and refining the overall user experience through software-driven innovation.

## Smartwatch Integration Deepens Connectivity

A cornerstone of the new update is the introduction of a dedicated Apple Watch application. This app transforms the smartwatch into a versatile digital key, offering a new level of convenience for Rivian owners. While second-generation R1 models already feature proximity-based automatic locking and unlocking, first-generation owners now gain similar digital key capabilities. By tapping the smartwatch screen, Gen 1 R1S and R1T vehicles can be locked or unlocked. Furthermore, with the app active and the Apple Watch connected to the vehicle via Bluetooth, drivers can put their car into drive without needing a physical key or an iPhone.

## Performance Modes Expanded for Broader Access

The update significantly broadens the availability of Rivian's performance driving modes. Sport Mode, previously exclusive to Performance Dual-Motor and Quad-Motor variants, is now accessible to all base Dual-Motor models across both Gen 1 and Gen 2 vehicles, encompassing both NMC and LFP battery configurations. This mode promises optimized power delivery and quicker acceleration compared to the standard driving setting. Additionally, all compatible Gen 1 and Gen 2 vehicles will now adjust their ride height to "Standard" when Sport Mode is engaged, a shift from the previous automatic suspension lowering.

Launch Mode, designed for rapid acceleration from a standstill, also sees expanded availability. Formerly restricted to second-generation Quad-Motor R1T and R1S models, it is now extended to include Gen 1 Quad-Motor vehicles and all Dual-Motor Performance EVs from both generations. Activating Launch Mode involves selecting Sport Mode, pressing a designated flag icon, and simultaneously holding the brake and accelerator pedals.

## Enhanced Comfort and Driver Assistance

Further refinements come in the form of an improved Kneel Mode, which automatically lowers the vehicle for easier entry and exit. A new "Lower" setting has been added, providing an average of one extra inch of suspension drop compared to the existing "Low" setting, with exact measurements varying by vehicle version and tire setup. On the advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) front, a critical improvement ensures that changing drive modes or various vehicle settings will no longer inadvertently disengage Highway Assist and Universal Hands-Free systems, enhancing continuity and safety during assisted driving.

## Optimizing Cold-Weather Performance

Addressing the needs of drivers in colder climates, the software update includes several cold-weather enhancements. A new indicator on the battery icon and within the Energy App now displays the precise amount of energy being used to warm the vehicle's battery. Additionally, a blue shading graphic on the Driver Display and in the mobile app will visually represent any temporary reduction in available range until the battery pack reaches its optimal operating temperature. Rivian has also stated improvements to the battery warm-up process during charging, aiming for more efficient range recovery and better pre-conditioning for peak performance.
