NTSA has finally released the menu for their new revenue collection drive. They are calling them 'Instant Fines', but let us be real - it is just a standardized price list for the guys in blue to know exactly how much to extort from you before you even lower your window. From the 'Smart License' to these tiered penalties, the government is making it very clear that being a Kenyan motorist is a luxury tax they intend to collect by any means necessary.

If you think you are having a bad day, try being caught with a dusty license plate or a missing inspection certificate. That is a cool 10,000 shillings gone. The same applies if you are caught speeding by just 16 to 20 kph. It is funny how they expect us to have prescribed speed governors when the roads themselves are one giant pothole designed to break your suspension. They have even criminalized 'obstruction' at the same price point, ignoring the fact that Nairobi's design is one permanent, state-sponsored obstruction.

Then we have the mid-tier robbery. Failing to obey a police officer will cost you 3,000 shillings, which is ironic because following their directions usually leads you straight into a 'kitu kidogo' trap. In this city, the law is a flexible concept depending on who is asking. Just look at the chaos at [Nyalenya Station: Where The Uniform Is Just A Bullseye](/posts/nyalenya-station--where-the-uniform-is-just-a-bullseye) to see how the system really works. Whether it is 5,000 for driving on a pavement or 3,000 for tinted windows, the goal isn't order; it is a balanced budget on the back of the common man.

Even the 'minor' offenses are designed to nickel-and-dime you to death. A seatbelt lapse is 500 bob, but if you are the owner and your vehicle isn't fitted with them, it is 1,000 per seat. That adds up faster than a matatu driver on a Monday morning. Using your phone while moving? 2,000 shillings. It is almost cute how they think people use phones for anything other than checking if they have enough M-Pesa to pay the very fine they are about to receive.

The speeding scale is the ultimate slap in the face. They will give you a 'warning' for going 5 kph over, which is basically an invitation for an officer to pull you over and see what else they can find. Once you hit 11 kph over, the price jumps to 3,000, and it hits the 10,000 ceiling before you have even realized you have stepped on the gas. In a country where the big fish fly in choppers to avoid the traffic they created, the rest of us are left to count our coins at every roadblock.

At the end of the day, these 'Smart Licenses' are not about technology; they are about tracking how much more blood they can squeeze from this stone. You can renew your license for 1,000 or pay the same amount for 'failing to produce it on demand,' but the result is the same: your pockets are lighter. Welcome to Nairobi, where the only thing faster than the cars is the rate at which the NTSA empties your wallet.
