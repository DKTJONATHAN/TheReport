Globally, the instability of the Indian economy sends a shudder through the BRICS narrative. If the supposed crown jewel of the Global South cannot manage its current account deficit, then the entire premise of a multipolar economic order is in jeopardy. Investors are watching the Reserve Bank of India with growing skepticism. They see a central bank playing a losing game of whack-a-mole with currency valuations while the underlying foundation remains termite-infested by bureaucracy and fiscal mismanagement.

This pattern of ill-preparedness is a regional contagion. Much like we saw when the Philippines declared an energy emergency over the Iran conflict (see: /posts/philippines-declares-energy-emergency-over-iran-conflict---another-ill-prepared-nation-stumbles), India is finding that its grandiose geopolitical ambitions are consistently undermined by basic domestic failures. You cannot lead the world when you cannot keep your own currency from hemorrhaging value every time the global oil market catches a cold.

The calls for a structural policy response are little more than code for a desperate scramble. Relying on currency intervention is like using a bucket to drain a sinking ship. Until India addresses the fundamental disconnect between its lofty rhetoric and its fragile fiscal reality, it will remain a volatility trap for international capital. The era of cheap growth is over, and the realists are finally starting to count the cost.
