So, the Kenya School of Government is now home to the 'Raila Amolo Odinga Regional Leadership and Governance Academy'. Sounds grand, doesn't it? Like we don't have enough places where people talk big about 'governance' and 'African transformation' while the roads outside remain craters. Now, some of Baba's old campaign manifestos are set to become classroom material. As if reading about promises will somehow magically fix a system built on something entirely different.

They're going to dissect these ideas - his manifestos - for senior public officials and 'emerging leaders'. As if the problem was ever a lack of *ideas*. We have manifestos bursting with grand plans every election cycle, then what? The moment power is grabbed, those shiny promises gather dust faster than an unread newspaper. I wonder if they'll include the parts about accountability, or perhaps how [Wanjigi Says Kenya Shouldn't Pay Debts Parliament Never Approved - Shocking, Right?](/posts/wanjigi-says-kenya-shouldn-t-pay-debts-parliament-never-approved---shocking--right) when the promises are all conveniently forgotten.

Meanwhile, Oburu has transitioned into the "hilariously ungovernable" ODM leader, trying to fill shoes that were always too big. Inheriting a political mantle is rarely about pure ideology, is it? It's about name recognition, loyalty, and the endless pursuit of clout. It just goes to show you, in this town, [blood is thicker than water, but clout is thicker than blood](/posts/blood-is-thicker-than-water--but-clout-is-thicker-than-blood---ming-li-vs--the-barbz) every single time. The tragedy of his death carries a sharper edge for those close to him, like his barber of 15 years, but for the public, it's just another chapter.

While they're busy turning manifestos into academic fodder, dissecting every 'idea' for 'democracy' and 'public service', I wonder if they'll teach the part about what happens after the elections. The part where the 'senior public officials' become untouchable and 'African transformation' remains a pipe dream for most Kenyans struggling with basic needs like food and shelter.

So, another academy, another legacy project. Good for the Odinga name, maybe. Good for a few people to get 'certificates' in 'leadership'. But will it change the price of unga? Will it fix our broken systems? Don't hold your breath. This country runs on a different kind of 'lesson', one learned the hard way on the streets, not in a brightly lit classroom dissecting old campaign documents.
