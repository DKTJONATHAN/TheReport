FKF President Hussein Mohamed Suspended Over Ksh42 Million Scandal - Another Day, Another Shambles











So, Hussein Mohamed, the big man at the Football Kenya Federation, has been told to step aside. Fancy that. Accused of siphoning off Ksh42 million meant for some football tournament. It’s like watching the same terrible movie on repeat. The National Executive Committee, or NEC as they like to call themselves, suddenly grew a conscience and decided enough was enough. Or perhaps they just realised the money was gone.

And it’s not just Mohamed. Abdullahi Yussuf Ibrahim and Acting CEO Dennis Gicheru are also being asked to take a walk. All this to "allow for investigations." We all know what that means. A lot of tea, a few stern emails, and eventually, probably nothing concrete, just more delays while the money has long since vanished into someone's pockets. The whole thing is a circus, and we’re all paying for the tickets.

In a move that surprised absolutely no one, Deputy President Macdonald Mariga has been shoved into the acting president role. Let's hope he’s not expecting miracles. Freezing bank accounts? Trying to appoint an independent auditor? This is what they do when they’re caught with their hands in the cookie jar. It's all about optics, making it look like they're doing something. Remember when Murkomen was beefing up security for our legislators? Seems like everyone’s looking out for themselves these days, even in sports. [Kenya's Legislators Get Tougher Bodyguards](/posts/murkomen-orders-paramilitary-training-for-sergeants-at-arms--kenya-s-legislators-get-tougher-bodyguards).

Mohamed, of course, is crying foul. "Malicious campaign," he calls it. "Cleaning house." Please. This is the same old story. Corruption fights back when it's threatened, or when it gets too greedy. He’s worried about his reputation? What about the state of Kenyan football? Millions of shillings disappear, and all he’s concerned about is his "lifetime of sacrifice." We’ve heard it all before.

Nine out of fourteen NEC members signed off on this. What about the other five? Did they conveniently have other pressing engagements, like counting their own cuts? The need to restore public confidence and safeguard integrity are the big words they throw around. It's all PR spin to make us believe there's a functioning system. It's a joke.

The Ksh42 million for CHAN is just the tip of the iceberg, I’m sure. Financial impropriety, conflict of interest, non-competitive procurement - sounds like standard operating procedure for these people. While they're busy pointing fingers and freezing accounts, the actual sport is probably still running on goodwill and prayers. Just another day in paradise.
