Here we go again. Another Principal Secretary is singing the same old song about value addition and large scale industrialization. They tell us agriculture is the backbone of the economy, contributing up to 30 percent of the GDP, yet the farmers carrying that backbone are walking on broken glass. We are still exporting raw materials like we are stuck in 1964, while the elites in Nairobi sip imported coffee and talk about global competitiveness. It is a tired script written in a boardroom where no one has ever actually held a hoe.

Let us talk about the structural bottlenecks. That is government-speak for the fact that 90 percent of our food depends on whether the clouds decide to show up. We have 1.3 million acres of irrigation potential, but apparently, we are too busy aligning policies to actually lay any pipes. Instead, we let the crops rot in the fields because we lack cold storage, then turn around and wonder why the cost of unga is higher than a politician's ego. It is a cycle of incompetence that no amount of renewable energy can fix.

The government is getting excited about hosting the Kenya International Investment Conference in 2026. Because that is exactly what a hungry nation needs. More five-star hotel buffets and glossy brochures for foreign investors. It feels just like [The 2026 ICT Budget: Another Digital Mirage for a Hungry Nation](/posts/the-2026-ict-budget--another-digital-mirage-for-a-hungry-nation), where we focus on the digital and the strategic while ignoring the empty stomachs on the ground. We are chasing 1.3 billion consumers under AfCFTA when we cannot even get a liter of milk to the next county without it spoiling in the heat.

We are importing edible oils and dairy derivatives that we could produce ourselves. This is not a clear industrial opportunity; it is a national embarrassment. We have the land, the people, and the renewable energy, but we lack the basic honesty to admit the system is rigged against the smallholder farmer. The Special Economic Zones they keep praising are just fancy gated communities for capital that will likely never trickle down to the mama mboga trying to survive the next drought.

They talk about climate resilience like it is a new app they just downloaded. Building resilience is not optional, but neither is it a buzzword you can scale commercially without fixing the corruption that eats the budget for every dam project. Until the government stops treating agriculture as a photo op for the next investment summit, we will keep exporting our wealth in raw form and importing our survival in plastic bottles. The only thing they are truly processing is our hope.
