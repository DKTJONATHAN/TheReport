Oburu Oginga's Grand Theatre of Illusions: Raila, ODM, and the Tragedy of Kenyan Politics

Oburu Oginga, they say, is "thrilled" and "terrorized." As if anything truly rattles the man these days. He's found his mojo, apparently, this elder statesman of the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM), a party currently as stable as a Jenga tower in an earthquake. He watches the tension ratchet up, the whispers turn to shouts, and all he does is laugh. Laughs it off. It’s a performance, isn't it? This "deadpan elder wisdom" that sounds suspiciously like a man who’s seen it all and expects nothing better.

Remember when he was "lost," throwing out "lonely" and "sad" lines? Now, one casual Swahili sentence - "Hatuwezi kuwa bila pawaaaa" - goes viral, a testament to the sheer desperation for *anything* to cling to in this political circus. Campaign rallies bleed into each other, a colourful cascade of empty promises and manufactured enthusiasm. It’s all very entertaining, if you like watching fools rush in. The Orange Democratic Movement, once a force, now looks like a sinking ship, and Oburu’s just there, chuckling at the cracks.

Let’s not forget how crucial the secretary-general position is in this whole chaotic Kenyan political system. It’s the "circuit board of party authority," they whisper, but in practice, it’s more like a tangled mess of wires that sparks unpredictably. President Ruto, meanwhile, is busy "rubbishing" opposition changes, a man who seems to understand that sometimes, the best way to win is to point out how utterly incompetent the other side is. And who can blame him when the management of Raila's legacy requires a skill set that seems to be in short supply?

The truth is, Kenyan politics is a disorganised, self-serving mess. Occasional crashes are not just inevitable, they're the main event. Sifuna, we're told, is on a "trajectory" for leadership. Let's hope his path doesn't involve navigating the same potholes that have swallowed so many before him. This whole drama, this "Greek tragedy" they're calling it, is just another act in the never-ending show. You want thrills? Try keeping your savings intact with the constant economic tinkering, or for that matter, understanding the latest attempt to curtail our freedoms with bills like the [Why Kenya’s New Cybercrime Bill Must Be Rejected: A Threat to Your Freedom](/posts/cyber-security-ammendment-bill). It’s all a grand performance, and we’re the ones footing the bill.
