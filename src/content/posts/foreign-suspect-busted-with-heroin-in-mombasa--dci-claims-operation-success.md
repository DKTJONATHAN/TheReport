So, the DCI brass are puffing their chests out, claiming a precision strike in Musufini, Mombasa. Apparently, they nabbed some "suspected foreign trafficker" and found enough heroin to keep half the coast high for a week. They're calling it a disruption, a well-oiled network dismantled. Sounds like a movie trailer, doesn't it? Except, this is real life, and we've heard this tune before.

They say the search revealed heroin packaged for distribution, a sophisticated operation designed to slip through the cracks. Of course it is. These guys aren't idiots; they're playing a game with us, and frankly, they're winning. We get a headline, a few arrests, and then it's back to business as usual. It’s the same old story, just a different batch of drugs and a slightly different headline. Reminds me of how easily our leaders can spin a narrative, much like the [eternal delusions](//posts/eternal-delusions--a-quiet-ep-for-a-country-loudly-drowning) we’re all supposed to believe in.

The DCI statement drones on about accountability and public trust. Cute. Meanwhile, the real question is how much of this stuff actually gets confiscated and how much ends up back on the streets, or worse, in the pockets of those meant to be fighting it. This isn't a "disruption"; it's a minor inconvenience for a much larger, far more entrenched machine.

And don't even get me started on the "probe expanding." That's code for "we'll look into it for a bit, then get distracted by the next shiny thing, or a more pressing budget allocation." We're talking about a "supply chain," reaching "multiple points." They're talking about it like it's a logistics company, not a poison peddled to our youth. It’s a tired script they’re reading from.

They're vowing to clamp down on the "proliferation of hard drugs." Good luck with that. While they're busy patting themselves on the back for this one arrest, the next shipment is already on its way, and more Kenyans are likely [selling themselves to the Russian front](//posts/the-hustle-is-a-death-trap--selling-kenyans-to-the-russian-front) just to survive. This "success" is just a drop in the ocean of despair.
