So, the Football Kenya Federation (FKF) has finally imploded. Surprise, surprise. President Hussein Mohammed is apparently out, tossed aside by his own National Executive Committee (NEC) over a cool Sh42 million scandal linked to some CHAN Pamoja tournament. They’ve even shoved Deputy President McDonald Mariga into the hot seat as acting head. You know, because putting a former player in charge always solves deep-seated structural problems. This is Kenya, after all, where logic takes a backseat to optics.

Naturally, Hussein Mohammed isn’t taking this lying down. He’s calling the whole meeting "rogue" and the decisions "null and void." His defence? The FKF constitution, of course. He claims they didn't follow the rules to call the meeting, and as President, he’s the one who calls the shots. He’s even quoting Article 38, Clause 2 like he’s some legal scholar, not a man allegedly embroiled in a Sh42 million missing fund. It’s the classic Kenyan playbook: when you're caught, blame the process. Reminds me of how everyone tries to outsmart the system, like those [lawyers and scientists training AI to steal careers](/posts/lawyers--scientists-training-ai-to-steal-careers--the-inevitable-career-obituary).

Now the federation is split down the middle, with one side screaming for a forensic audit and "public confidence," and the other claiming a blatant violation of due process. They’ve even gone as far as freezing bank accounts and calling in FIFA, CAF, and the Ministry of Sports. Because clearly, involving more people who often turn a blind eye will bring immediate justice. Mohammed is promising a big media address, no doubt another dose of "I'm the victim here" rhetoric.

This whole charade is just another Tuesday for Kenyan football. Sh42 million vanishes, leadership fights erupt, and the actual game, the one that millions of Kenyans actually care about, gets sidelined. Expect more statements, more counter-statements, and ultimately, the same old song and dance. The only thing this crisis is restoring is my faith in the predictability of Kenyan corruption.

Mohamed is going to give his media address on Saturday, 25th April 2026, at FKF Headquarters. Don’t hold your breath for any real solutions. It’s all about who controls the purse strings and the narrative. This is just the beginning of another beautiful mess, and we, the fans, will be left picking up the pieces, as usual.
