Athletics Kenya (AK) has finally emerged from their mahogany offices to announce the six-yes, only six-chosen ones heading to Poland for the 2026 World Athletics Indoor Championships. They are calling it a "strategic blend of youth and experience," but let-s be real: it looks like they just picked whoever had a valid passport and a coat heavy enough for Kujawy Pomorze. While the rest of the world is sending armies of athletes to battle for medals, Kenya is sending a squad small enough to fit into a single Probox. It’s giving "we spent the budget elsewhere" energy, and frankly, the fans are already rolling their eyes.

Leading the men-s side is Brian Omari Tinega in the 400m, because apparently, one lap is all we can handle these days. Then we have Noah Kibet and Jacob Krop, the latter of whom is expected to carry the 3000m on his back like he’s carrying the entire federation-s reputation. Over on the women-side, Mercy Adongo Oketch and Rosemary Longisa are representing the shorter distances, while Susan Lokayo Ejore takes on the 1500m. It is a cute little group, but if you are looking for the usual Kenyan dominance, you might want to lower your expectations. Indoor tracks are tight, dusty, and nothing like the open plains of Iten where these stars actually thrive.

The cynicism does not stop at the numbers. We all know how these international trips go. It is all smiles and tracksuits at the airport until the reality of the Polish winter hits. While AK talks about "confidence" and "podium finishes," the taxpayer is sitting here wondering why we are funding a six-person vacation to Eastern Europe. We have seen this movie before where the [2027: THE EARLY BIRD CATCHES THE KENYAN TAXPAYER-S LUNGS](/posts/2027--the-early-bird-catches-the-kenyan-taxpayer-s-lungs) because the planning is always three steps behind the hype and the accountability is zero.

If AK thinks we are going to be blinded by the "global indoor stage" buzzwords, they have another thing coming. The hypocrisy of claiming to "showcase depth" with a handful of runners is laughable. You cannot talk about depth when your team list is shorter than a celebrity marriage. We will be watching to see if these athletes actually deliver medals or if they just deliver a series of aesthetic "I am in Poland" Instagram stories. Good luck to the six, they are going to need it when they realize they have to outrun the embarrassment of being the smallest delegation in the arena.
