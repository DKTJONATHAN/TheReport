Welcome to the circus, darlings. For the past week, Kenya’s social media timelines have morphed into a national amphitheatre of fake shock and performative moralizing. Everyone and their grandmother has been hovering over their screens, dissecting whispered allegations and blurred clips like they are forensic experts. The star of the show? A foreign national everyone is calling “the Russian guy,” and the drama has spread like wildfire through every WhatsApp group and X thread in the 254.

It is honestly hilarious to watch the timeline go into a meltdown over these allegations while the same people were probably in those very clubs last weekend, begging for a selfie with the elite. While everyone is busy playing detective with blurred clips, it is hard not to notice how we only get loud when a foreigner is involved. It is a classic case of [GHANA ACTS WHILE KENYA NAPS: THE COWARDICE OF OUR DIPLOMACY](/posts/ghana-acts-while-kenya-naps--the-cowardice-of-our-diplomacy) playing out in our social circles. We would rather scream about a “Russian guy” than address the local predators who buy us shots every Friday night.

The hypocrisy is thick enough to cut with a knife. One minute, the "moral police" are out in full force, typing long-winded essays about safety and dignity. The next minute, they are in the DMs asking for the "unblurred link" and sharing memes that turn serious allegations into a punchline. We do not actually want justice; we just want something to talk about between our boring nine-to-five shifts. It is not about the victim or the truth - it is about the dopamine hit of a fresh scandal.

Let’s be real for a second, hun. This "Russian guy" is just the flavor of the week. By next Monday, we will have found a new villain to stone, and the cycle will repeat itself. We love to pretend we are horrified by the "messy truth" behind the glamour, but we are the ones who keep the cameras rolling. Stay woke, or at least keep your screen brightness down so we do not see you enjoying the drama a little too much. The internet never forgets, but it sure does love to pretend it has a conscience.
