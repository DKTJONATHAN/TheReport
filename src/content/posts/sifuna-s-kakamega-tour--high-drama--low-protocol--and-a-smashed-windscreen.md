Let us be real for a second. Edwin Sifuna and his Linda Mwananchi squad are doing the absolute most to stay relevant in the 254 news cycle. It is giving major main-character-energy, but without the actual planning. Western Kenya Police Commander Isaak Mahoud basically just rolled his eyes at the whole thing, telling the press he has not received a single official letter about their little Kakamega party at Amalemba Grounds. It is the kind of messy behavior we usually expect from a D-list socialite, not a group of senior politicians.

Apparently, the police are now expected to be mind-readers or, at the very least, full-time social media stalkers. Mahoud revealed he only knows about the rally because it is all over the feeds. It is the classic influencer move: post the invite on the Gram but forget to tell the landlord. Sifuna’s team is out here acting like they are too big for protocol, yet they will be the first ones to scream for help when the vibes go south. The commander had to remind them that while he will provide security to keep the peace, nobody actually came to his office to ask for assistance.

The drama between the Linda Mwananchi and Linda Ground factions is more chaotic than a Real Housewives reunion. While Sifuna’s team claims they are being targeted by rival goons, Mahoud is busy washing his hands of the whole mess, calling it purely political. It is honestly just another day in the circus where everyone wants the crown but nobody wants to follow the rules. This digital-first approach to chaos reminds me of how even our religious leaders are pivoting to tech-based schemes, much like [The Crypto Clergy: Why Kenyan 'Prophets' are Adopting Blockchain Tithing](/posts/crypto-clergy-kenyan-prophets-blockchain-tithing) who have traded traditional baskets for digital wallets to keep the coins flowing.

Of course, it would not be a Kenyan political rally without a little bit of "unscripted" violence for the cameras. The team’s sound truck, which they apparently had to borrow from a donor because their own gear is as unreliable as a celebrity marriage, got its windscreen smashed in Riat. It is all very dramatic, very messy, and very predictable. Godfrey Osotsi and James Orengo are playing the martyrs, vowing to march on despite the "chaos," while Governor Fernandes Barasa is playing the "cool host" who definitely did not invite them but will let them stay as long as they do not break the furniture.

At the end of the day, it is just another weekend of men in expensive suits arguing over who gets to hold the microphone. The police will show up to babysit, the crowds will get their few hours of entertainment, and we will all be back here next week for the next episode of "As the Politician Turns." Honestly, if they put half as much effort into paperwork as they do into their social media captions, maybe their trucks would actually make it to the venue in one piece. Stay messy, Kakamega!
