So, the President’s radio silence on this whole passport-selling kerfuffle is louder than a diva's backstage demands, isn't it? Taabu Tele over there is practically screaming into his email (taabu.tele@mail.com), wondering if this is the same "hands-on CEO" who usually has an opinion on everything from the price of chai to the geopolitical implications of a rogue pigeon. Honestly, darling, it's less "CEO" and more "absentee landlord" these days. We're all waiting for the truth, lest the opposition start flying misinformation kites higher than a badly managed KQ flight - remember those [BAGGAGE BLUES AND BOLLYWOOD BUSTS: KQ’S MUMBAI MELTDOWN](/posts/baggage-blues-and-bollywood-busts--kq-s-mumbai-meltdown)?

And speaking of things we expect but don't get, let's talk about Nairobi. Muriithi King'au (jmskingau37@gmail.com) is practically weeping about the blocked drains and the resulting floods. Floods! In the capital! Apparently, "mismanagement and seemingly endless politicking" are the real VIPs clogging up the service delivery channels. It's just a "tip of the iceberg" of woe, he says. Darling, I think the iceberg is actually the entire service delivery Titanic.

Then we have Ruth Gituma (rgitum@gmail.com), who's clearly got a brain that functions on more than just TikTok trends. She’s baffled by the sudden influx of project promises announced at rallies and, get this, funerals. Where does the money for these grand pronouncements actually come from? Is it tucked away in some secret "funerals and rallies" budget? Because my piggy bank is looking rather empty, and I've got big plans for a new patio.

And the audacity! Samuel Onyango (Samwel46@gmail.com) shares a story about a friend trying to get a measly Sh10,000 loan. You'd think the bank was doing him a favor, right? Wrong. The "field officer" apparently demanded a Sh1,500 bribe just to assess his creditworthiness. A kickback for doing their job? Is this the new "tough economic times" perk? I'm starting to think these banks are just a glorified front for a very exclusive bribery club.

Finally, Cleophas Ndolo (cleophasndolo@gmail.com) is calling out our beloved police force. Apparently, if you need to report a crime at 8 am, you'll find tumbleweeds at the Occurrence Book desk. But by 7 am? Traffic police are out in full force, doing what they do best: shaking down motorists. Is this "delay" some sort of avant-garde police reform? Because it looks less like reform and more like a masterclass in selective punctuality and extortion. Honestly, the only thing consistent is the inconsistency.
