So, they found bodies. Again. This time it's Kericho, a "shocking discovery" of about 32 bodies, mostly children, yanked from the earth. Turns out the court order was for 14, but the Kenyan reality always has to outdo itself, doesn't it? "Quite unusual," says the pathologist. Yeah, unusual is when you find a clean grave. This is just… us. Bodies piled in gunny bags, like refuse.

And the details? Seven adults, 25 children - infants and foetuses. Some body parts thrown in for good measure. The adult ones are already gone, decomposed to nothing. The kids are fresher. Tells you everything you need to know about who gets forgotten first and fastest in this country. They’re talking about autopsies, identifying them. Good luck with that. It's not like these were rich folks with burial plans. This is the unclaimed, the discarded.

The whole thing started with a "tip-off" from a whistleblower. Of course. Because if it weren't for people snitching on each other, how else would we find out how rotten things are? Apparently, 13 unclaimed bodies were released from a hospital in Nyamira for burial. But that doesn't explain the extra 19, does it? Or the fact that it's on land belonging to the National Council of Churches of Kenya (NCCK), who are now conveniently saying they had no clue. Funny how that works.

Two arrests so far - a public health officer and a cemetery caretaker. Small fry, no doubt. The real players are probably sipping tea somewhere, admiring their handiwork. Human rights groups are wailing about a "staggering and horrific escalation." They’re talking about mutilation and dismemberment. It’s not an escalation, it’s the same old story, just with a bigger body count. We saw it in Shakahola, didn't we? Cults, mass deaths, pastor denying everything. This is just a different flavour of the same rot, maybe less religious zealotry, more bureaucratic indifference and a dash of outright criminality. It makes you wonder if these bodies are just another casualty of the same systemic failures that lead to things like the [Hollywood's Creative Exhaustion: The Endless Cycle of Reboots and Relatives](/posts/hollywood-s-creative-exhaustion--the-endless-cycle-of-reboots-and-relatives) we see elsewhere, where things are just churned out without regard for quality or consequence.

The police are investigating "criminal activity." What a revelation. As if finding dozens of bodies, including babies, stuffed into bags in a mass grave isn't criminal activity enough. They’re sealing off the area, people are gathered, looking shaken. They should be angry. We should all be angry. But anger fades, and then we just move on to the next disaster. This is Kenya. We excavate the truth, only to bury it again under layers of bureaucracy and denial.

This isn't "unusual." This is our normal. The forgotten dead, piled up and then dug up, only to be forgotten again. What a country.
