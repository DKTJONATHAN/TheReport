So, the Auditor General has finally sniffed out the dirty laundry in the UDA, and it stinks. Apparently, while President Ruto is out there yapping about tax collection and building houses for the hustlers, his own party can't be bothered to cough up the Sh128.2 million in salaries that includes deductions for things like national insurance and that so-called affordable housing levy. Three years they’ve been at this, bleeding the system dry while pretending to be saviours. Classic Kenya.

The Auditor General’s report, a document that probably gave some folks sleepless nights, details how millions meant for statutory dues have vanished. It’s a nice little scheme, isn’t it? Tell the mwananchi they *must* pay their taxes, or else, but when it comes to your own outfit, suddenly the rules don’t apply. It’s almost as if the universal healthcare plans and revenue collection goals are just for show, a pretty package to distract from the real business: lining pockets.

And don't even get me started on the "ghost" project launches. PS Wachira supposedly fronting these phantom initiatives? It’s a well-worn playbook, this grift. Launch a project on paper, collect the funds, and then watch it disappear into thin air, just like Harambee Stars' chances of professionalism these days. [Harambee Stars: Where Professionalism Goes to Die](/posts/harambee-stars--where-professionalism-goes-to-die). It’s a con, plain and simple, and the ordinary Kenyan is always the one left holding the empty bag.

The irony is thicker than the smog over Industrial Area. The party that’s supposed to be leading the charge, the one pushing for financial responsibility, is caught red handed skipping its own obligations. They’ve even managed to turn the tables on their former allies, with ODM now telling its members to ditch campaigning for Ruto’s re-election. Smart move, considering the company they're keeping. It's a dog-eat-dog world out here, and it seems some dogs are just better at chewing the furniture than others.
