So, President Ruto decided to grace State House with the presence of some leaders from Isiolo, Marsabit, Garissa, Wajir, and Mandera. Apparently, they were there to "consult" on "accelerating development" in the Northern Kenya region. You know, the usual song and dance. We’ve heard this tune before, haven't we? It’s the same script, different date, and we’re all expected to believe this time it's different.

Health CS Aden Duale, along with governors from Wajir, Garissa, and Mandera, were paraded for this grand consultation. The agenda? "Key development priorities and government projects." Translation: more talking, less doing. Ruto keeps repeating his administration's commitment to the North, claiming it won't be marginalized anymore. Funny, because for years, "marginalized" has been the region’s unofficial motto, thanks to consistent underinvestment.

The President insists his government is "deliberately investing" in infrastructure, education, healthcare, water, and security. He talks about reversing historical trends with "equitable allocation of resources" and "accelerated implementation." It sounds good on paper, just like those grand plans for Nairobi's infrastructure that always seem to stall over payment issues. It’s always something, isn't it? Like the MICROSOFT DATA CENTER PUSH STALLED BY PAYMENT ISSUES - PREDICTABLE, ISN'T IT?

He drones on about every Kenyan deserving "quality public services and economic opportunities." And these "ongoing investments" in roads, electricity, water, schools, and health facilities are supposedly part of the "Bottom-Up Economic Transformation Agenda." Honestly, it all feels like a carefully crafted narrative designed to buy time and goodwill, while the real issues fester. We'll see how much of this "transformation" actually trickles down to the people who need it most.
