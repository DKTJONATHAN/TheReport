Honey, grab your tea because the "Green City in the Sun" is looking more like a "Black Market in the Basement." We have all seen the influencers posting those thirsty "Passport and Mimosa" photos in the airport lounge, but it turns out that blue booklet is about as exclusive as a Nairobi street party. While you are out here struggling to book an appointment for six months just to get a renewal, the immigration department has apparently been running a "Buy One Get One Free" sale for every international criminal with a suitcase full of cash. It is giving desperate, it is giving messy, and quite frankly, it is giving "I can get you anything for the right price."

The irony is absolutely delicious if you do not mind the taste of systemic failure. We are told to work hard, pay our taxes, and "brand" ourselves for the global market, yet the actual brand of the country is being sold off to the highest bidder. It is one thing for a socialite to fake a trip to Dubai using green-screen technology, but it is another thing entirely when the government is faking the identities of foreign nationals. We are out here trying to maintain an aesthetic of progress while the people in charge are busy turning our national security into a flea market. It makes you wonder if those [Nairobi’s 'Soft Life' Founders Just Got a New Address: The Remand Home](/posts/nairobi-s--soft-life--founders-just-got-a-new-address--the-remand-home) were actually the ones running the immigration desks from their cells.

If you thought getting a UK visa was hard before, just wait until the embassy realizes that a Kenyan passport is now about as reliable as a "Coming Soon" poster for a movie that never gets released. Our "allies" in the US and the UK are not going to be amused when they realize that the person sitting next to them in business class might have bought their citizenship at a discount. The "soft life" is about to get very hard for the rest of us. Expect more background checks, more delays, and more side-eyes from border agents who will treat your documentation like a suspicious DM from an ex. 

The most annoying part of this whole circus is how it affects the actual dreamers. Students, entrepreneurs, and the few honest professionals we have left are going to pay the price for this administrative circus. While the big fish are eating the country from the inside out, the rest of us are left holding a document that might soon be useless for anything other than a coaster for your drink. The government talks about diplomatic standing and regional hubs, but you cannot be a hub when your doors are swinging open for anyone with a bribe. It is time to stop the cap and admit that the only thing "world-class" about this system is the level of audacity.
