Duale: Laikipia Ebola Facility Open to All, Not Just Americans, He Says

Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale is trying to calm nerves about the new Ebola facility at Laikipia Airbase, insisting it's for everyone, not just Uncle Sam's citizens.

So, the government is throwing Sh1.7 billion of American money at a fancy Ebola quarantine centre at Laikipia Airbase. Duale, our resident cheerleader, assures us it's not some exclusive club for expats. Apparently, the Kenya Defence Forces, with their "robust medical wings," will be manning the show. Because when it comes to handling a deadly virus, who better than soldiers? We’ve seen how well our infrastructure handles even simple floods, remember [Nairobi Braces for Deluge, Infrastructure Fails Again As Officials Dither](/posts/nairobi-braces-for-deluge--infrastructure-fails-again-as-officials-dither)? But sure, let's trust them with Ebola.

The whole thing kicked off because of that Ebola outbreak in the DRC, and now everyone's scrambling to look like they're doing something. The US government is doling out cash, probably to feel good about itself and maybe to keep its own citizens safe, while we get to play host. Duale is quite happy to point out this is all part of a "broader strategy," as if we weren't already at risk before this multi-billion shilling announcement. And he's also warning politicians against making a song and dance about it. Typical.

Naturally, some people have actually raised valid concerns about transparency and public participation. Activists and medical unions have rightfully questioned the lack of input. But Duale, ever the smooth talker, dismisses it all as mere speculation, while simultaneously admitting the High Court had to step in to temporarily halt some aspects of the arrangement. This whole "legal challenges" bit suggests the initial plan wasn't exactly transparent, now was it?

He claims this is just one of eleven specialised centres they're setting up. Eleven! Because clearly, our existing healthcare system is just bursting at the seams with spare capacity, and what we really need is more specialized facilities that will likely end up being underfunded and understaffed, just like everything else. The World Health Organisation and Médecins Sans Frontières are shouting about the alarming spread of Ebola, and our response is to build a few more facilities and hope for the best.
