Honey, if you thought the music industry was the only place where veterans air out the dirty laundry on their way out the door, you clearly haven't been watching the Kabale Regional Referral Hospital scene. Dr. Filbert Nyeko just hit the mandatory retirement age of 60, and instead of just taking his pension and fading into the sunset, he decided to drop a truth-bomb track that has everyone gagging. At his sendoff ceremony-which was held at an interns hostel, talk about a budget venue-he basically told the government that the facility is a mess. From encroachers stealing land to wards that look like they were built for the cast of a black-and-white movie, the tea was piping hot.

The most scandalous part of the doctor's exit interview? The housing situation. Apparently, only 53 out of 300 staff members actually have a place to sleep on-site. The rest are probably dodging potholes and late-night drama just to show up to work. It is giving very much "struggling artist" vibes rather than "center of excellence." Dr. Nyeko was quick to point out that this lack of accommodation is the real reason for the chronic absenteeism and tardiness. It is the same old story we see with local leaders who focus on the wrong things, much like [Wavinya’s Road To Riches: How To Triple Debt and Ignore Potholes](/posts/wavinya-s-road-to-riches--how-to-triple-debt-and-ignore-potholes) where the branding is shiny but the foundation is crumbling.

But wait, the hypocrisy gets even deeper. Dr. Nyeko pointed out that 60 percent of the malnourished babies in the children's ward come from Rubanda district. For those of you who don't know your geography, Rubanda is the potato capital of the region. Imagine living in a literal field of Irish potatoes and still having nothing to eat because all the produce is being sold off for profit. It is the ultimate influencer move: selling the lifestyle while starving behind the scenes. The government is being asked to invest in health education, but let's be real, they can't even fix the wards that have been "outfashioned" since 1930.

Enter the new girl, Dr. Anne Marion Namutebi, who is stepping into this chaotic spotlight with the classic "I’m just here to learn" routine. She pledged to consult and collaborate, which is basically code for "don't blame me when things go south." She’s been at the facility for 11 years, so she knows exactly how messy the situation is, yet she’s promising a "healthy and productive population" with the humility of a pageant queen. We’ve seen these leadership transitions before, and they usually involve a lot of PR and very little actual change.

To top it all off, the Kabale hospital board decided to gift the outgoing director a heifer. Yes, a whole cow. While the hospital land is being snatched up by encroachers with questionable land titles, they’re handing out livestock like it’s a 19th-century dowry. The Deputy Resident District Commissioner, Michael Kyakashari, is out here promising to cancel those land titles and stop the "slum developments," but we’ve heard those empty promises before. Now that salaries have been "enhanced," the pressure is on the workers to perform, but it’s hard to be "result oriented" when you’re working in a building that belongs in a museum. Stay tuned, because this medical drama is far from over.
