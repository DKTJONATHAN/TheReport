Since mid-2025, our boys have been traded like cheap commodities on the global market. While you were busy complaining about the price of unga, hundreds of Kenyan soldiers have been dying in the freezing mud of Ukraine, fighting for the Russian army. It is a recruitment network that would make a human trafficker blush, yet Labour Minister Alfred Mutua is still sitting pretty in his office despite the scandal. In Nairobi, a massive body count is just a temporary PR headache before the next government lunch meeting. We are now exporting the only thing we have left-desperate young men-and this time, they are coming back in boxes.

Now the French are moving in to claim their piece of the cake. We are about to sign a military cooperation agreement that puts Paris in charge of training our army and "securing" our maritime borders. We are ditching our traditional masters in Washington and London for a new set of colonizers who speak with a different accent but have the same appetite for our resources. It is the same old story of Kenya being a pawn on a global chessboard, except we are the ones providing the wood for the board.

The presidential camp is already sharpening its knives for the 2027 race, treating the recent by-election victories as a laboratory for their next big scam. They are testing new strategies to keep us under their thumb while pretending to unify a country they have already broken. It is all part of a grand plan to stay in power at any cost, often relying on figures like [The Professor and the Politician: Ruto's New Intellectual Shield](/posts/the-professor-and-the-politician--ruto-s-new-intellectual-shield) to give their raw power grabs a thin coat of academic respectability.

Across the borders, the diplomatic "friendship" is as fake as a river road handbag. We are quietly begging Tanzania to release our nationals who are rotting in their jails, hoping "discreet contact" will fix a mess built on years of ego. Meanwhile, the rift with Kinshasa is finally complete. Félix Tshisekedi is so tired of Ruto’s games that he is ready to pull the DRC out of the East African Community entirely. The EAC is starting to look like a toxic family reunion where everyone is secretly planning to sue each other.

Even the news itself has become a luxury item. Africa Intelligence wants your subscription money to tell you what the elites are whispering over cognac in Westlands. They promise no ads and no consulting work, just the "low-frequency signals" of our impending doom. They want us to pay for the privilege of reading about how we are being sold out by the power players on the rise. In this city, the only thing more expensive than the truth is the silence of the men who run it.
