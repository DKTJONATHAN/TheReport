So, word on the dusty streets of Nakuru is that over a THOUSAND secondary school students have descended upon Kirobon Boys High School for the Annual Schools Rally 2026. Organized by the Seventh-day Adventist Church, because who else is going to tackle the existential dread of a 15-year-old in the digital age? The theme, "Truth in a World of Noise," is just *chef's kiss* perfect. Because, you know, nothing is more distracting than your parents nagging you or the latest episode of whatever everyone’s binge-watching.

Apparently, this shindig is all about mentorship, spiritual reflection, and "discussions on leadership and values." Because clearly, the internet hasn't covered that enough. Organizers are sweating bullets about how young minds are being swayed by "social media narratives and competing influences." Oh, please. As if young people haven't been navigating murky waters since, well, forever. It’s not like their future is being shaped by… say… [THE KEYBOARD JUDGE: WHY YOUR SOCIAL MEDIA RECEIPTS ARE THE ULTIMATE CAREER KILLER](/posts/the-keyboard-judge--why-your-social-media-receipts-are-the-ultimate-career-killer).

The speakers, bless their earnest souls, are urging these kids to stay grounded in "faith" and develop "strong character." Dr. Jeremiah Rogito wisely pointed out that young people need to "guard their minds carefully because their thinking shapes their future." Groundbreaking stuff. And Pastor Safeli Mlenga chirped about building lives on "strong spiritual foundations rather than reacting to every controversy." Oh, the blissful ignorance! I can just picture these kids, eyes wide, nodding along, while secretly wondering if their latest selfie will get enough likes.

Then there’s Pastor Peter Mwati, who thinks the church has a duty to guide youth towards "unity and positive leadership." And Pastor Dr. Robert Tinega is challenging them to translate faith into "discipline, service and integrity." All very noble, I'm sure. But let's be real, while they're busy reflecting on their "purpose" and building "resilience" against the "challenges of modern society," the rest of us are out here dealing with the actual noise. This rally is just another reminder that some people are still trying to put the genie back in the bottle, while the rest of us are busy trying to figure out how to make it grant us wishes.
