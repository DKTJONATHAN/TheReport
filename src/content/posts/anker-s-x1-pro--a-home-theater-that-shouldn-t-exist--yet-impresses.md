Anker has introduced a device that challenges conventional notions of portable entertainment: the Soundcore Nebula X1 Pro. Described as a "Google TV party on wheels," this audacious system combines a high-end 4K projector with a powerful five-speaker, 400W Dolby Atmos audio setup, creating a gadget that feels both absurdly ambitious and remarkably effective.

At its core, the X1 Pro inherits the lauded liquid-cooled, triple-laser 4K projector from Anker's Nebula X1. This projector is noted for its 3500 ANSI lumen brightness, vivid picture quality, and exceptionally quiet operation, positioning it as one of the brightest and most vibrant all-in-one portable projectors available. The Pro variant significantly amplifies this foundation with its integrated audio capabilities.

The primary differentiator for the X1 Pro is its sophisticated sound system. It features a substantial internal subwoofer capable of delivering wall-shaking bass. Complementing this are four battery-powered speakers-two front units that automatically unfold with a robotic flourish, and two rear units that detach with a firm push. These speakers contribute to a total of seven horizontal and four overhead channels for wireless 7.1.4 Dolby Atmos immersion. The system offers three distinct sound modes: a 2.1 configuration with all speakers docked, a 5.1.2 surround sound mode with the front speakers unfolded, and the full 7.1.4 separation when all four satellites are distributed around a room. Anker provides animated guidance to assist users in optimizing speaker placement, and a 4-mic array automatically calibrates audio to create an optimal "sweet spot."

The four satellite speakers operate wirelessly via a proprietary 5.8GHz Wi-Fi signal, ensuring low-latency, lossless audio. Testing revealed flawless connectivity, with speakers automatically syncing upon startup and entering sleep mode when the projector is off, conserving battery life which exceeds eight hours. The bass produced by the X1 Pro's 160W subwoofer is particularly noteworthy, at times proving almost overwhelming. Each front surround unit houses four 20W speakers for upward, center, front, and surround channels, while the smaller rear units contain two 20W speakers for upward and rear surround.

In its full 7.1.4 movie mode, with speakers optimally distributed and calibrated, the X1 Pro delivers an immersive experience comparable to dedicated home theater systems. Films like *Top Gun: Maverick* showcased convincing overhead audio effects, while *The Dark Knight* and *Blade Runner 2049* demonstrated clear dialogue, precise spatial audio, and profound bass. Even at 30 percent volume, the system generated significant acoustic impact, suggesting robust performance for outdoor movie nights.

Beyond its cinematic prowess, the X1 Pro doubles as a powerful Bluetooth speaker. In 2.1 audio mode with speakers docked, it rivals large party speakers, offering clean, bright audio for music. While some tracks benefited from bass reduction via an equalizer, the system maintained clarity up to about 50 percent volume, though fidelity began to diminish beyond 70 percent. This versatility allows it to function as a nearly silent music playback device, avoiding the typical fan noise associated with projectors.

Despite its innovative features, the X1 Pro presents practical challenges to its "portable" designation. Measuring 435 × 343 × 761mm (17.13 × 13.50 × 29.96 inches) and weighing 32.8kg (72.31 pounds), its integrated telescoping handle and wheels facilitate mobility only on hard, even surfaces. Transporting the unit up stairs or into a vehicle requires assistance, and its delicate optics and electronics are not suited for rough terrain. The device's IP43 resistance to water and dust is conditional, applying only when the lens cap is closed and speakers are retracted, a scenario unlikely if the unit is left exposed after use.

Intermittent Wi-Fi connectivity issues with Google TV were noted during testing, requiring a reboot to resolve, despite other devices maintaining a stable network connection. This disruption, while solvable, proved annoying during film viewing.

Priced at $4,999 / €4,999.98 / £4,499, the Anker Nebula X1 Pro represents a significant investment. Its unique blend of high-end projection and a powerful, adaptable audio system places it in a category of its own. The device's very existence, combining high-end projection with a complex, portable audio setup, speaks to a broader trend of ambitious innovation in the consumer electronics sector, where companies navigate the line between novel features and market viability, often leading to [disruption fears in volatile tech sessions](/posts/the-ai-paradox--cooling-inflation-fails-to-offset-disruption-fears-in-volatile-tech-session). For those prioritizing ultimate bass and an all-encompassing portable home theater experience, the X1 Pro delivers. However, for users primarily seeking a portable 4K projector with robust outdoor sound, the original Nebula X1 coupled with an optional speaker kit offers a more cost-effective solution, saving approximately $1,000.
