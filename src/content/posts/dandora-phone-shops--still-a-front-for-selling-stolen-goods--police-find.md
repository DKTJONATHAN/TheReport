So, the boys in blue - bless their tired souls - finally stumbled upon what everyone with half a brain and a working pair of eyes already knew: Dandora is a hub for all sorts of shady dealings. This time, it's a "mobile phone repair shop" masquerading as a legitimate business, conveniently located in Phase III, where they were apparently “concealing and distributing” pilfered goods. Because where else would you stash stolen phones and Kenya Power meters? A library?

Naturally, the National Police Service is puffing out its chest after a "targeted operation" that nabbed five unsuspecting suspects. Intelligence reports and heightened surveillance, they say. We all know what that means: someone finally bothered to look past the usual hustle. They managed to recover 21 phones and a dozen token meters, all presumably liberated from the unsuspecting citizens of Nairobi. Makes you wonder how many other "repair shops" are quietly dismantling our lives for spare parts.

This whole charade isn't exactly new, is it? Ordinary businesses becoming elaborate fronts for thievery is practically a Nairobi tradition. Especially in these densely populated areas where everyone’s trying to make a quick buck, no matter how. Most of these stolen electronics end up stripped for parts, a fitting metaphor for how our city is slowly being picked apart. It's a harsh reality, much like how [Nairobi Still The 'King' As Kenya's Top 5 'Richest' Counties Revealed](/posts/nairobi-still-the--king--as-kenya-s-top-5--richest--counties-revealed), yet economic disparities fuel these kinds of operations.

The police claim this is part of "ongoing efforts to dismantle organised criminal networks." Good luck with that. While they're busy raiding one phone shop, ten more will sprout up. And speaking of dismantling, the Kenya Copyrights Board was also busy confiscating illegal broadcasting equipment elsewhere. Because in Nairobi, crime comes in all flavours, from petty theft to illegal streaming. It's a symphony of illegality, and we're all just living in it.
