Turkana ECDE Coordinators "Trained" on "Inclusive Education" - More Hot Air?

This whole "inclusive education" charade in Turkana? Another government-backed program, this time with the do-gooders from Humanity and Inclusion (HI) and the Kenya Institute of Curriculum Development (KICD) throwing money around. Over 30 ECDE coordinators supposedly got a "training" that's meant to ensure no child is left behind. Yeah, right. Like a few workshops are going to fix systemic neglect.

Apparently, these coordinators are now "trainers of trainers." So, they'll cascade this profound knowledge to the underlings. David Otieno from HI Kakuma is all smiles, talking about how this will build a "critical mass of trainers." What it probably builds is a critical mass of people nodding along, collecting their per diems, and then going back to the same old struggles.

The KICD folks, bless their hearts, claim the training covered "approved curriculum designs" and "detailed measures to integrate accessibility into school infrastructure." John Muhoro from KICD even drops evidence about door widths and ramps. Fantastic. Meanwhile, the actual schools in Turkana are probably crumbling, and these coordinators are learning about how to make a ramp that's an inch wider. It’s like teaching someone to polish the brass on the Titanic.

The trainees, naturally, are singing praises. David Omakada calls it an "eye-opener," realizing that ECDE centres aren't just about "content delivery" but also "disability mainstreaming" and "adjusting the built environment." Shocking. They're confessing to using "wrong approaches" before. You don't say. It’s a classic Kenyan story: identify a problem, hold a meeting, pat yourselves on the back, and then wait for the next problem to arise so you can repeat the cycle. This is the same kind of performative action we see, just like the [high-cost of staged smiles and lakeside revolutions](/posts/the-high-cost-of-staged-smiles-and-lakeside-revolutions).

This whole exercise is happening amidst a "rapid increase" in ECDE centres and a "mass recruitment" of teachers. Translation: more bodies to manage, more schools to neglect, and the same old excuses for why things aren't actually improving. Adding refugee and cross-border populations to the mix? That's just throwing more fuel on the fire of an already struggling system. Don't expect miracles, folks.
