UGANDA DUCKS GACHAGUA'S RUMOURS: RUTO NOT BEHIND NTV, DAILY MONITOR SHUTDOWN

Warning: Kenyan politicians are still pointing fingers, even when the situation has nothing to do with them. Uganda says Rigathi Gachagua is barking up the wrong tree.

File image of President William Ruto (right) and his former DP Rigathi Gachagua at State House on December 18, 2023.

So, Rigathi Gachagua, our esteemed former Deputy President, decided to wade into Uganda's mess, linking President William Ruto to the shutdown of some media outlets there. Naturally, Uganda, through some chap named Daudi Kabanda from a group associated with Museveni's son, Muhoozi, has slapped that down. Apparently, Uganda makes its own decisions based on laws and the constitution, not some cosy chats between heads of state. Who knew?

Kabanda, bless his heart, even told Gachagua to mind his own business and focus on Kenya. Which, let's be honest, is probably a full-time job considering some of the past incidents we've covered, like that rather unfortunate KPA boss William Ruto road crash. But no, Gachagua had to go and accuse Museveni's son, Muhoozi, of being the military chief behind the closures, and then drag Ruto into it because, well, they're pals.

Gachagua's big concern seems to be a "wider trend of shrinking press freedom across East Africa." Oh, the irony. As if Kenya has been a beacon of journalistic freedom lately. He’s calling for vigilance, which is rich coming from someone who can't even keep his own political house in order. It's a classic case of blaming the neighbour when your own fence is falling down.

The whole NTV Uganda and Daily Monitor shutdown apparently stemmed from Muhoozi accusing them of "sustained biased reporting." Then, a meeting happened, a report was presented - the usual theatre. Muhoozi is now taking his findings to Museveni, who will no doubt make a decision that is, of course, completely independent and totally not influenced by anyone.

Honestly, it's exhausting. Kenyan politicians love to act like they're the arbiters of regional affairs, especially when it comes to anything involving international relations or potential scandals. They’ll jump on any bandwagon, even one that’s clearly labelled "Uganda Internal Affairs." Just focus on home, Rigathi. There’s plenty of mess to clean up right here.
