Ruto's Fuel Shock Plan: More Like a Damp Squib

WARNING: President Ruto claims to have a plan to protect Kenyans from fuel price shocks, but this 'comprehensive briefing' smells more like damage control than genuine relief.

Looks like the President had a chat with his ministers and central bank folks about this Middle East mess. Apparently, they're worried about fuel, food, and exports. Good to know they're "assessing the situation" while our pockets get emptier.

The big news is this "Government-to-Government fuel procurement deal." Ruto claims it's cushioning us from price hikes. Listen, if I wanted to hear about "prudent and forward-looking" interventions, I'd ask my landlord why rent keeps going up. This sounds like another fancy way to keep the lights on for the elites while we still cough up for every litre. Remember when we heard about Ruto potentially ceding control of Kenya Pipeline to Uganda? [Ruto Cedes Kenya Pipeline Control to Uganda's Museveni? Standardmedia.co.ke Reports Shocking Deal](/posts/ruto-cedes-kenya-pipeline-control-to-uganda-s-museveni--standardmedia-co-ke-reports-shocking-deal). Let's hope this fuel deal isn't another such "strategic intervention."

On the food front, we're told fertiliser is fine until September. Great. So, we can look forward to the same old struggle with food prices, just with slightly less fertiliser uncertainty. And tea? Apparently, it's growing. Good for the tea barons, I guess. The ports are busy too. More cargo, more money for someone. But the meat exports are in trouble, because, you know, logistics. So, the Trade and Agriculture ministries will "explore alternative solutions." Translation: more meetings, more paperwork, and us still waiting.

While the President beams about optimism in some sectors, the real question is: what about the rest of us? This whole "comprehensive briefing" is just a show. They'll keep "monitoring" and "taking decisive action" until the next crisis hits, and we'll be right back here, hoping for a miracle. Just like with the AFCON stadium promises, this might be another [Billion-Shilling Ghost](/posts/the-afcon-2027-billion-shilling-ghost-why-rutos-stadium-promises-might-be-kenyas-biggest-sports-scam).
