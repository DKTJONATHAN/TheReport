So, the big man, Ruto, has decided that Sh38.7 billion from selling off bits of Kenya Pipeline is best used to polish up Jomo Kenyatta International Airport. Apparently, this is just a "down payment" - 20 percent of the total Sh193.7 billion needed. They're calling it an "upgrade," I call it throwing good money after bad. This comes after they scrapped that shady deal with some Indian outfit, Adani, remember? Probably for the best, considering they got indicted for… well, you know how these things work.

And where is all this money coming from, you ask? From the "National Infrastructure Fund," which is supposedly going to have a cool Sh387.4 billion. Nice. This fund is supposed to be for "critical national infrastructure," not just making Nairobi's airport look pretty for the tourists and the well-connected. It’s all a bit too neat, isn't it? Privatisation proceeds, ring-fenced, they say. Sounds like a fancy way to say "government taking your money and deciding what to do with it."

They’ve also been talking about raising bonds or loans, backed by passenger service levies. So, every time you fly, you’re essentially funding this expansion. Sh50 for international, Sh600 for domestic. Just another way to tax us, disguised as a "security" for investors. Ruto himself says he's investing alongside them, so when they make money, "we also make money for our people." Translation: the government, which is really just a few people, will make money. Your money, their profit.

The "enhancement" areas sound thrilling: bigger terminals, better runways, and "modernisation of passenger processing systems." Basically, making it easier for those who can afford to fly to do so, while the rest of us are stuck in traffic just trying to get to the airport. And let's not forget the previous plans to bring in international lenders after the Adani mess. Japan, China, Europe, Africa - they all apparently sniffed around. It's a global game of who gets to build and profit from our shiny new airport.

This whole National Infrastructure Fund thing was signed into law in March 2026. Convenient timing. So now, the proceeds from selling off state assets like KPC, Sh106.3 billion of it already, are conveniently tucked away. It’s all about "de-risking" investments for the big players. They want to attract you, the global investor, but make sure you know the Kenyan government - or rather, a select group within it - is also getting a slice of the pie. Just like that time when Ruto’s ministry shuffle was a disaster for public service elites, this feels like another reshuffle of public funds for private gain.

This whole airport expansion sounds like a grand plan to line pockets, both local and international. They’re building a "new airport" for $1.5 billion, and the government is putting in 20 percent. Twenty percent of billions. Meanwhile, down here in the trenches, we're struggling to make rent, and the matatu fare keeps going up. But hey, at least JKIA will look good on Instagram, right? A monument to how our money disappears.
