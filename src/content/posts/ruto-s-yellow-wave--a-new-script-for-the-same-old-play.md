So, the yellow wave has supposedly "drawn first blood." Ruto’s UDA is busy celebrating by-election wins in Mt Kenya, Isiolo, and the Western region like they just discovered fire. To the rest of us stuck in this Nairobi heat, it is just another Tuesday where the elite play chess with our futures. They call it a "long march to 2027," but for the average person, it is just a long walk to nowhere while the cost of living keeps its boot on our necks.

The opposition is currently a joke without a punchline. They are "fragmented" and searching for cohesion in all the wrong places, probably at the bottom of a luxury hotel buffet. While they faff around trying to find their footing, UDA is tightening its grip on the battlegrounds. It is a "psychological blow," sure, but the only people truly feeling the pain are the voters who actually believed the "hustler" narrative would lead to more than just high taxes and higher stakes for the ruling class.

In the Western bloc, the DCP’s second-place finish proves that loyalty is a luxury no one can afford anymore. This "guaranteed stronghold" talk is for people who do not understand how the ground works. Just like the [Nyayo Mud and Misery: Gor Mahia’s Easy Walk Over Nairobi’s Broken Dreams](/posts/nyayo-mud-and-misery--gor-mahia-s-easy-walk-over-nairobi-s-broken-dreams), our political landscape is a mess of broken promises and slippery surfaces where only the most ruthless survive. The grip is tightening, but it feels more like a chokehold than a steady hand.

Then you have Mike Sonko whining about betrayal because his candidate "started disappearing from the ground." It is almost poetic. In a country where the reality of the street is a permanent struggle, seeing politicians complain about being ghosted by their own cronies is the only free entertainment we have left. The ground is moving, but mostly because it is caving in under the weight of all these inflated egos.

Ruto is reasserting authority over Mt Kenya, and the script is being written for the next five years of "authority and control." We are years away from the actual election, yet the campaign machines are already loud enough to drown out the sound of the crumbling infrastructure. It is the same players, the same tactics, and the same cynical outcome for everyone else who isn't invited to the victory party. The march to 2027 has started, but for most of us, we are just marching in place.
