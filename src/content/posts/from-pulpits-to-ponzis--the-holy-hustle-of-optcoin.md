In Nairobi, if someone says "Hallelujah" and "ROI" in the same breath, you should probably check if your wallet is still in your pocket. The Optcoin scandal is just the latest proof that the sanctuary has become the new stock exchange for those with more faith than sense. Thousands of Adventist congregants thought they were investing in a digital future, but they were actually just funding someone's new SUV. Optcoin had all the classic red flags: high returns, referral bonuses, and a website that vanished faster than a politician after an election.

The real kicker is the involvement of Pastor Paul Mwangi. This was not just some random guy in a suit; he was the former president of the Central Kenya Conference. He was the one encouraging people to consolidate their investments into Optcoin while serving as its regional director. Now he claims he was also a victim who lost a considerable sum. It is a tired story. This level of betrayal by leadership is as predictable as [The Great Government Heist: When Your Impounded Bike Becomes the Inspector’s Side-Hustle](/posts/the-great-government-heist--when-your-impounded-bike-becomes-the-inspector-s-side-hustle), proving that whether it is the police station or the pulpit, the hustle never stops.

The audacity of the "recovery fee" is the most Nairobi thing about this whole mess. After the platform collapsed, the victims were told to pay another Sh24,000 just to register for a chance to get their money back. It is the ultimate double-scam. With the DCI reporting over $43 million lost to crypto fraud in 2024 alone, you would think we would have learned by now. But when a man of God tells you to invest, the brain usually switches off and the mobile money pin gets entered before logic can even knock on the door.

Now we are expected to wait for a "public inquiry" that started on March 2, 2026. A commission will sit, papers will be filed, and everyone will look very serious for a week. Meanwhile, the East Central Africa Division's late-to-the-party warning from November 2025 remains a complete joke. They told ministers not to participate in "unlicensed or fraudulent" activities after the damage was already done. In this city, an inquiry is just a fancy way of saying the money is gone and we are just waiting for the next scandal to take over the headlines.
