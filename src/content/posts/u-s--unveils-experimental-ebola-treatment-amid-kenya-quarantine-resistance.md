US EXPERIMENTAL EBOLA DRUG AND KENYA QUARANTINE FACILITY: AMERICA'S LATEST GAMBLE

WARNING: The US is pushing an unproven Ebola treatment and a controversial quarantine facility in Kenya. Don't expect miracles, just more political maneuvering and potential public health chaos.

AMERICA BRINGS ITS EXPERIMENTAL CURE AND QUARANTINE PLANS TO KENYA

HEALTH

USA, KENYA, EBOLA

MONEY

The Yanks are at it again, doling out experimental medicine and setting up fancy bio-isolation centres in our backyard. This time it’s an Ebola antibody treatment, MBP-134, apparently good enough for their own folks but being tested on the rest of us. They say it’s got “strong results in animal studies” - comforting, isn’t it? Never mind it hasn’t finished the human trials, because, you know, outbreaks are just perfect opportunities for real-world guinea pigs. The FDA is waving its magic wand for “emergency investigational use.” Fancy words for “we’re winging it.”

And as if that wasn’t enough, they want to plonk a 50-bed quarantine facility in Laikipia. Naturally, the locals are up in arms, which is their right. Safety? Transparency? These are foreign concepts to those pushing these plans. The US Embassy, bless their condescending hearts, insists it’s all for our good, a “health security partnership.” They’ve even thrown Ksh20 billion our way, which is just a drop in the ocean compared to what they’ll get out of it, or what could go wrong. It’s like their old promises of partnership, which often leave us with the bill, as we saw when [THE DESERT MIRAGE IMPLODES AS GLOBALISM CATCHES FIRE](/posts/the-desert-mirage-implodes-as-globalism-catches-fire).

While they’re busy playing doctor and landlord, the outbreak rages on in Central Africa. They’re coordinating, supposedly, but who really benefits? We’re left with the potential fallout, the fear, and the lingering question of who’s really in charge. The experimental drug is in limited supply, and the exact number of doses is a state secret. Of course, it is. Everything’s always cloak and dagger.

This whole affair reeks of the same old song and dance. Big promises, experimental solutions, and us bearing the brunt of the uncertainty. The protests in Laikipia are just the beginning of the noise. You can bet your bottom shilling that the real story isn't about saving lives; it’s about control, influence, and maybe a bit of PR for Uncle Sam.
