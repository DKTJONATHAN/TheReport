Kenya Kwanza Chiefs Accuse Uhuru of Funding Opposition, Rip Into Critics

These politicians, trotted out to Taveta on July 24, 2026, spewed the usual rhetoric. One-term presidency? Rubbish, they say, just the opposition stirring up trouble. And President Ruto's re-election bid? Absolutely, because who else would keep the gravy train running? They even managed to drag the former President into it, claiming he's funding this "Linda Mwananchi" nonsense to make the current government look bad. As if they need help with that.

Senate Majority Leader Aaron Cheruiyot, bless his cotton socks, went full throttle, calling the movement members "drug dealers, washerati, extortionists, wezi na wakora." Apparently, their only mission is to smear Ruto and push this "one term" idea. It’s always a classic move: if you can't defend your actions, just call your opponents criminals. It’s a strategy as old as time, and frankly, as effective as a sieve in a drought.

Farouk Kibet, Ruto's aide, chimed in, urging Taita Taveta residents to stick with Kenya Kwanza, promising them more government handouts. He also managed a jab at anyone "promoting division and discouraging investment and tourism." Funny, because sometimes the biggest deterrents to investment and tourism are the very people making these speeches. And let's not forget the constant squabbling, remember when [Ministers and Communicators Squabbled During Fuel Crisis](/posts/kenya-fuel-crisis--ministers-and-communicators-squabble--president-steps-in---big-surprise)? That doesn't exactly scream stability.

Then came the delightful criticism of Democracy for the Citizens Party (DCP) leader Rigathi Gachagua, accused of "reckless and inflammatory remarks" that supposedly threaten national cohesion. Speaker Moses Wetang'ula also weighed in, defending tourism by condemning those who suggest tourists shouldn't visit. He vowed to "meet" anyone who tries to "hujumu uchumi wa Kenya." It's a bold stance, considering how much of Kenya's economy is already effectively [Chinese property](/posts/china-s-crbc-contracts-cross-sh1-2-trillion--kenya-s-infrastructure-now-officially-chinese-property).

Public Service Cabinet Secretary Geoffrey Ruku also joined the chorus, calling Gachagua irresponsible for urging people to leave the government. The overarching message from these Kenya Kwanza stalwarts? They are deeply committed to the President's second term, convinced Kenyans will reject "hatred, division and misinformation." It's a convenient narrative when you're the ones dishing it out.
