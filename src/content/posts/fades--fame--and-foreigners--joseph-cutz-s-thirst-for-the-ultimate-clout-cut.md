Nairobi is a city that thrives on crumbs of international attention, so of course we all had to stop and stare when iShowSpeed’s management slid into Joseph Cutz’s DMs. Joseph Rutebeza, who goes by Joseph Cutz because apparently a regular name does not sell "grooming experiences," finally got his big break. After a decade of cutting hair from Kinshasa to Lavington, he realized that the sharpest tool in his kit is not a pair of clippers - it is Instagram. It is the classic Nairobi hustle: stay ready so you do not have to get ready when a chaotic American streamer decides to tour Africa for the views.

The "Speed Does Africa" tour was essentially a circus on wheels, but Cutz played his part like a seasoned PR pro. He claims he was asked for "discretion" until Speed left the country, which is hilarious because everything Speed does is for the loudest possible audience. Let us be real - the secrecy was not for privacy; it was to ensure the "reveal" video got maximum engagement. Cutz described Speed as "easy" to work with, which is barber-speak for "he did not scream at me while I touched his hairline." It is the same kind of manufactured hype we see when [BONGO’S NEW DIVAS: THE GLITTER, THE GHOSTWRITERS, AND THE DIAMOND FACTOR](/posts/bongo-s-new-divas--the-glitter--the-ghostwriters--and-the-diamond-factor) try to convince us they are organic stars.

Joseph’s client list reads like a "Who’s Who" of people trying to stay relevant in Kenyan politics and entertainment. From Jalang’o to Jaguar, he has been the man behind the fades that grace our billboards and Parliament seats. But apparently, grooming a musician-turned-politician is not enough for the man from Kinshasa. He is already eyeing the big prize: President William Ruto’s head. Talk about high-profile ambitions. If he can survive Speed’s frantic energy, I suppose he thinks he can handle the "Hustler-in-Chief," though the stakes for a crooked hairline at State House are significantly higher than on a YouTube stream.

Ultimately, Joseph Cutz is not just a barber; he is a content creator who happens to hold scissors. He knows that in 2024, a haircut does not exist unless there is a ring light and a slow-motion video of the final reveal. While he talks about "expanding his business," what he is really doing is perfecting the art of the celebrity proximity-rub. He is riding the wave of iShowSpeed’s global audience to mask the fact that at the end of the day, a fade is just a fade. But hey, in a city where clout is the local currency, Joseph is currently the wealthiest man in Lavington.
