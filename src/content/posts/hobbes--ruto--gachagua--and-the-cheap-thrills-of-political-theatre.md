So, Hobbes was right, wasn't he? "War of all against all." Except here, it's not about survival in the wild; it's about survival in the Kenyan political jungle, and the battlefield is a funeral. Ol Kalou MP David Kiaraho's requiem mass, folks. A place for reflection, for mourning, for… throwing shade at the President and his deputy? Apparently, "solitary, poor, nasty, brutish, and short" now applies to our public discourse, even when someone's six feet under.

The President, bless his heart, is shocked. Shocked! As if the predictable, venomous dance between Ruto and Gachagua hasn't been the main act for months. "Certain actors intent to divide Kenyans for political gain," he says. Well, duh. That's the entire playbook, isn't it? And it's not exactly a new strategy. We've seen this kind of infighting before, from the halls of Parliament to… well, anywhere power is being contested. It makes you wonder if [Kenya Parliament: Can They Actually Order Anyone to Do Anything?]( /posts/kenya-parliament--can-they-actually-order-anyone-to-do-anything) because what they *do* seem to order is more drama.

The Mt Kenya exodus narrative is getting louder, isn't it? Gachagua and his crew are out there lobbing insults like grenades, and Ruto's team is dismissing them as "primitive." Primitive? Maybe they just want a less chaotic, more competent government. But who are we kidding? Competence is for people who aren't busy plotting their next power grab. This "emerging unity" among the dissidents? It's less about national unity and more about who gets to sit at the top of the food chain.

And the irony? The sheer, unadulterated, Kenyan irony is that we're dissecting this mess using a 17th-century philosopher. Hobbes, with his grim outlook, would probably just nod and say, "Told you so." We’re a democratic republic, they say. Yet, at a funeral, the only thing being buried is any semblance of dignity. The hope for a Ruto comeback in Mt Kenya? Dashed by the very squabbles he thrives on, or perhaps is now drowning in. It's a predictable, tiresome cycle, and we're all just here, watching the show.
