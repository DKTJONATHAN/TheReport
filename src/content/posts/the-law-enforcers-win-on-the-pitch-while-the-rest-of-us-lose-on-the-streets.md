So, the "Law Enforcers" managed to bully Sofapaka into submission at Vapor Grounds. Kenya Police FC walked away with a 2-0 win, acting like they just saved the city when all they did was beat a team that is currently a walking corpse. Nicholas Muyoti is talking about "remaining relevant" in the title race, but let us be honest, it is easy to stay relevant when your payroll is backed by the state while everyone else is out here scavenging for crumbs.

The goals from Kautiama and Abud Omar were clinical, sure, but playing against this version of Sofapaka is like taking candy from a baby who has already been robbed three times today. The 2009 champions are sitting at the bottom of the table with 14 points, looking more like a Sunday league side than a top-flight contender. They brought in eleven new players this month, because apparently, if you keep changing the tires on a car with no engine, it will eventually start. Coach Abdalla Juma says they are "wounded but not dead," but from where I am sitting, the smell of rot is getting hard to ignore.

It is almost poetic to see the police dominating the field while the institutions they represent continue [To Protect, Serve, and Pillage](/posts/to-protect--serve--and-pillage) out here in the real world. They dictated the tempo and controlled possession, showing the kind of discipline that is strictly reserved for the pitch and nowhere else in this country. It must be nice to have a "ruthless attitude" when you are playing a game, while the rest of us deal with the ruthless reality of their actual operations on the streets.

Down in Kisii, APS Bomet finally found a win against Kariobangi Sharks, moving off the bottom spot. It took them this long to remember how to play football, which says everything you need to know about the quality of this league. Meanwhile, Ulinzi and Homeboyz shared a boring point, and Bidco played out a goalless draw with Mara Sugar in Kirinyaga. It is the same old story: institutional teams with guaranteed salaries suffocating the life out of community clubs that actually have fans but no money.

Police are now seventh, sitting pretty with 31 points and eyeing the top four. They have the technical quality and the depth, mostly because they can afford to harvest talent from teams that are too broke to pay their rent. Sofapaka is still rooted at 18th, facing a relegation battle that they are almost certain to lose if they keep playing like strangers who just met at a matatu stage. This is not a "rebirth" of faith in the game; it is just a reminder of who holds the power and who gets left in the dirt.
