So, the former DP Rigathi Gachagua is out here complaining the police were late to the party in Kikuyu. Apparently, while gangs were busy playing roadblocks and terrorizing motorists on the Southern Bypass for a solid two hours, the boys in blue were busy… well, who knows? Probably polishing their boots or waiting for their chai. According to Gachagua, by the time they finally rolled up around 9:30 AM, the residents had already chased off the "goons" and tidied up the mess. Typical.

Gachagua, of course, can't just leave it at that. He's busy spinning this into a grand narrative of "organized crime" and accusing Ruto's administration of using these gangs as some sort of political muscle. He claims they're even working *with* the police now, turning the National Police Service into a bunch of hired thugs. Honestly, it's hard to tell where the theatrics end and reality begins with these guys. It's like a twisted version of [The Apology Parasite: Why Elite Athletes Weaponize Personal Drama to Steal the Spotlight](/posts/the-apology-parasite--why-elite-athletes-weaponize-personal-drama-to-steal-the-spotlight), just with more rocks and bonfires.

The whole Haiti security mission comparison is rich, too. Gachagua is implying it was some sort of advanced training for our police to learn how to collaborate with criminals. It’s a shame citizens have to be the ones to restore order when the supposed protectors are too busy playing make-believe. The police eventually did show up, dispersed the crowd, and reopened the highway, but only after the heavy lifting was done by ordinary Kenyans.

Meanwhile, Kikuyu MP Kimani Ichung’wah is dismissing Gachagua's claims as just more political noise, accusing him of trying to stir up trouble. He’s urging security agencies to stay sharp and protect everyone, warning against anyone trying to ignite unrest. It’s a classic Nairobi drama: politicians pointing fingers, blaming each other, while the public just wants to get to work without dodging flying rocks.

The Interior CS, Kipchumba Murkomen, has chimed in with a condemnation of political violence and a plea for the courts to get tough on these gangs. And DCI detectives nabbed a couple of suspects in Migori with a homemade gun, so at least *someone* is doing their job, even if it's miles away from Kikuyu. It all goes to show, when trouble brews, you can’t always count on the uniform to be there when you need them.

The whole incident is a stark reminder of the fragile state of security and the endless political games played out on the streets. While residents and traders are back to their normal routines, the lingering fear of renewed unrest hangs in the air, a familiar scent in this city. And the police? Well, they were apparently too busy to comment officially. Figures.
