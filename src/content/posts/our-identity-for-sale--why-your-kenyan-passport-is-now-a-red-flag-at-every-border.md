In this town, everything has a price tag if you know which office to bribe. While the rest of us are out here begging for basic services and waiting months for a simple renewal, a high-ranking Sudanese militant from the RSF was caught rocking a legitimate Kenyan passport. It is a diplomatic nightmare that makes the corporate entitlement seen in [Fueling the drama: Uganda’s energy queen vs. the greedy corporate clout-chasers](/posts/fueling-the-drama--uganda-s-energy-queen-vs--the-greedy-corporate-clout-chasers) look like a playground tiff. This is not a clerical error; it is a full-blown auction of our sovereignty to the highest bidder.

Kenyans in the diaspora are currently sweating through their shirts at every international border because of this mess. Imagine working three jobs in London or Dubai just to have some immigration officer look at your green book like it was printed at a cyber cafe in River Road. The integrity of our passport is officially in the gutter. While the elites give speeches about regional peace, their subordinates are busy selling the national soul to warlords who treat human rights like a light suggestion.

The Department of Immigration is essentially a high-end boutique for criminals. Forget the long queues and the "broken" machines they tell you about when you try to book an appointment. If you have enough dollars and the right connections, you can get a bespoke identity faster than a regular citizen can get a birth certificate. It is a mockery of every honest person who has ever been told to "wait for the system." We are being profiled globally because our government decided that a militant's wallet is more important than a citizen's dignity.

It is ironic to hear brands talk about heritage and craftsmanship, like the "intergenerational trust" found at Taj Gems & Jewellers in Two Rivers. We celebrate ethical sourcing and legacy in our luxury malls while the very document that defines our nationality is being laundered for foreign combatants. You can buy all the Tanzanite and custom-made rings you want to mark a personal milestone, but the real legacy being built by our state officials is one of international isolation and systemic betrayal.

The international community is watching, and they are not blinking. We have moved from being the gateway to East Africa to being a logistics hub for paramilitary groups. If you are planning to travel anytime soon, prepare for the kind of scrutiny usually reserved for movie villains. Our identity is no longer a badge of honor; it is a liability. In Kenya, the only thing that is truly "bespoke" is the way the system is engineered to fail the common man while protecting the warlord.
