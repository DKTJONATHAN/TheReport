Oh look, the Pentagon has a new fitness influencer. Pete Hegseth, our very own Secretary of Defense, decided the world needed to see him grunt through a 315-pound bench press instead of, say, updating us on those pesky carrier groups crossing the Atlantic. In a video that screams "I have a podcast about masculinity," Hegseth paraded his son, Gunner, into the gym to act as a human prop for his latest ego trip. It is the kind of performative "Alpha Dad" energy that makes you want to crawl into a hole and die of second-hand embarrassment.

The optics were as subtle as a sledgehammer. Wearing army camo and workout sleeves, Hegseth looked like he was auditioning for a straight-to-DVD action flick. He dragged his teenage son into the frame to spot him, only to turn into a drill sergeant the moment the kid actually tried to do his job. When Gunner’s hands dared to hover near the bar for safety, Hegseth barked "Don’t touch it!" with enough venom to start a small war. Imagine being a 15-year-old just trying to make sure your dad does not crush his windpipe, only to get yelled at for the sake of a Twitter hype video.

Behind the camera, his third wife, Jennifer Rauchet, played the role of the ultimate cheerleader, chirping "You did it, babe!" as if he had just brokered world peace instead of struggling through a single shaky rep. The Department of Defense rapid response account actually boosted this, proving that tax dollars are currently being funneled into a vanity project for a man who clearly misses his Fox News lighting. While some fans were impressed by the weight, most of us were just wondering why the man in charge of the military is acting like a mid-life crisis on steroids.

This whole display of manufactured toughness is just another layer of the Alpha facade we see from men in power. It reminds me of the curated perfection we see in high-end neighborhoods where the reality never matches the brochure. Much like the transition [from Guatemala’s stolen babies to Runda’s gated illusions](/posts/from-guatemala-s-stolen-babies-to-runda-s-gated-illusions), Hegseth is selling a sanitized, hyper-masculine version of reality while the actual defense part of his job seems to be an afterthought. He is more concerned with his War Room aesthetic than the fact that he looks absolutely unhinged shouting at a child for a social media promo.

The internet, as usual, had no mercy. Critics were quick to point out the insanity of having a kid who looks significantly lighter than the barbell spotting three plates. If the bar had actually slipped, Gunner would have been a pancake, but hey, at least the footage would have been viral, right? From people telling him to "go do your job" to users pointing out how "lame" he made the Navy's original video look, the consensus is clear: Pete is trying way too hard to be the tough guy.

Hegseth hasn't bothered to respond to the backlash, probably because he is too busy checking his bicep angles in the mirror. He claims to be focused on administration responsibilities, but when your official response team is posting gym content instead of troop welfare briefings, the priorities are pretty obvious. We are living in an era where the Secretary of Defense is just another content creator, and frankly, the Alpha Dad brand is already past its expiration date.
