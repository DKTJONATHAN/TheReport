Welcome to the 2027 auditions, where the script is written in betrayal and the actors are all fighting for the same bloated paycheck. President Ruto is currently playing a high-stakes game of musical chairs with the Deputy President seat, and he does not care who falls off when the music stops. This isn-t about leadership or moving the country forward-it is a cold, calculated reconfiguration of a winning coalition that has already started to rot from the inside.

Poor Kithure Kindiki is sitting in that office right now, probably wondering if his chair has a trapdoor. Ruto used him to shut up the Mt. Kenya crowd after the messy divorce from Rigathi Gachagua, but in this town, loyalty has the shelf life of unpasteurized milk. The moment someone with a bigger vote-bloc or a deeper pocket comes along, Kindiki will be escorted out the back door just like the man he replaced.

Now we have the ODM brigade, led by Oburu Oginga, openly salivating over the DP position. They call it a broad-based government, but we know a corporate takeover when we see one. It is the same old story where the ruling class rearranges the furniture while the house is on fire. It reminds me of how [Mbadi’s Statistical Fairytales Cannot Fill Empty Stomachs](/posts/mbadi-s-statistical-fairytales-cannot-fill-empty-stomachs), promising us growth and stability while the only thing growing is the list of people waiting for their turn to eat at the high table.

The most morbid part of this whole circus is the heartbeat away criteria. Political analysts love to talk about who is prepared to take over in case of a vacuum. They are basically measuring the DP for a suit they hope he never has to wear, while ignoring the fact that the current vacuum isn-t in the office of the President, but in the bank accounts of the average Kenyan. Whether it is Kindiki or an ODM surrogate, the result remains the same for the rest of us.

And just when you thought the stage was full, in walks Fred Matiang-i and Okiya Omtatah, adding more noise to an already deafening cacophony. Everyone wants to be a spokesperson or a candidate, but nobody wants to be a servant. By the time 2027 rolls around, we will have a dozen more coalitions and a hundred more promises, all designed to hide the fact that the game is rigged and the players are the only ones winning.
