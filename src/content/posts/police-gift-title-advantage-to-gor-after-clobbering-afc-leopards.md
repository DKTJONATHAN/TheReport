Police Gift Gor Mahia League Advantage After Thumping AFC Leopards

Ingwe's title hopes turn to dust as Kenya Police dismantle them. Another year, another dream deferred for the Leopards faithful.

Kenyan Police FC's decisive 3-0 victory over AFC Leopards at Nyayo Stadium on Saturday afternoon has effectively handed the Kenya Premier League title advantage to Gor Mahia. Ingwe, who had a golden opportunity to leapfrog their rivals after Gor’s own slip-up, instead imploded spectacularly, confirming the perennial disappointment that seems to follow them.

Leopards sat second, just one point adrift of the league leaders, and with Gor Mahia stumbling against Tusker, this was their moment to seize control. Instead, they gifted the game away. Burkina Faso’s Yves Koutiama opened the scoring for Kenya Police in the sixth minute, a stark reminder that even the best intentions can be shattered before the coffee has even cooled.

The onslaught continued. Eric Zakayo doubled the lead in the 31st minute, leaving the Leopards, affectionately known as ‘the felines’, clinging to straws. The half-time whistle offered little respite, as Charles Ouma added a third goal for ‘Afande’ just before the break. It was a performance that screams of the same old story, of dashed hopes and predictable outcomes. This kind of mismanagement on the pitch feels as familiar as the e-waste piling up in Korogocho.

This isn't just about a football match; it's about a pattern of underperformance that fans have endured for years. While some may point to the excitement of world-class football hitting our screens, for many in Nairobi, the real game is navigating the daily hustle. One can only imagine the frustration of those who invested their hopes in Ingwe, only to see them crumble so easily.
