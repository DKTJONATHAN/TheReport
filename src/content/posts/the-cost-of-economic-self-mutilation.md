These are not just demand shocks that shift consumption to domestic firms. They are supply shocks that hike costs for everyone, including non-tradable services that never even see the border. The model highlights a propagation matrix that acts like a slow-burning fuse. Even if a tariff is temporary, the price hikes get baked into the system through staggered adjustments. We are looking at endogenous stagflation-a self-inflicted wound that forces central banks into a corner where they must choose between crushing output or letting inflation run wild.

The cynical truth is that the dream of a self-contained national economy is a house of cards. When a large power tariffs intermediate goods like semiconductors or basic materials, it does not just hurt the exporter; it raises the floor for every domestic manufacturer. The result is a wealth transfer away from the home country, facilitated by currency volatility and risk-sharing wedges that standard models conveniently ignore. This is not about protecting the worker; it is about liquidating the future to win a news cycle.

Even the threat of a tariff is enough to poison the well. Expectations drive immediate exchange-rate collapses and pricing shifts long before a single container is stopped at a port. The data shows a massive US decoupling that does not actually bring production home; it just reroutes it through a more expensive, less efficient global maze. While the hegemon isolates itself, the rest of the world scrambles to rewire trade routes, leaving the domestic consumer to pay the premium for a hollowed-out version of sovereignty.

Just as Nairobi’s annual drowning is a predictable result of failed infrastructure (see /posts/nairobi-s-annual-drowning--23-dead-in-the-city-of-failed-concrete), this looming global downturn is the inevitable consequence of a failed intellectual framework. Policymakers are using 19th-century tools to manage a 21st-century network. The result will be a persistent, grinding decline in real GDP and a permanent loss of purchasing power, all while the architects of this mess take a victory lap in front of the cameras.
