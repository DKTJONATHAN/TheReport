JOMO SAFE? DOUBTS ON JARAMOGI, THE UNIT THAT GUARDS THE PRESIDENT

The Kenyan President's security detail, a unit steeped in colonial paranoia and political rivalry, is facing scrutiny after recent breaches. It seems even the Big Man isn't as untouchable as we're led to believe.

The Special Force Unit, born from British SAS advice back in '64, was meant to be "small, loyal, tough and highly skilled." Funny how "loyal" seems to have been the bit that got lost in translation somewhere along the line. The "top secret" report detailing its formation reeks of the same old mistrust and suspicion that has always plagued this country, a potent cocktail that breeds these "security lapses."

We've seen intruders get close enough to the President to probably smell his aftershave. This isn't just about a few slip-ups; it’s a recurring theme. Every power shift, every perceived wrong, becomes an excuse for chaos. It’s a tired script, really, from colonial days right up to now. Remember when a bold demand in London rewrote our independence timetable? The echoes of that historical unease still reverberate, fueling the paranoia that birthed this elite unit in the first place.

And what about the whispers of espionage and sabotage? Sounds like a soap opera, doesn't it? Except this is real life, and our leaders are the star players. It makes you wonder, with all this internal drama and external threats, if anyone truly has our best interests at heart. It’s a good thing we’re all so good at navigating this mess, perhaps as good as those trying to make sense of the death of authenticity in Nairobi’s digital elite. We’re all just trying to survive, aren't we?
