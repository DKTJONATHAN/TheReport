Oh, Daniele De Rossi. The man who apparently thinks he's above the fray, lecturing us peasants about how *hard* it is to pick a team. After a win against Roma, no less, he’s decided to grace us with his presence at a press conference, ready to dissect the very fabric of his managerial genius. Apparently, we're all just "gossiping" when we dare to question his decisions. How *dare* we? I mean, who are we to doubt a man who's been in football for "20 years"? As if that's some kind of magical immunity from criticism. It's almost as if the man wants us to believe he's some kind of footballing oracle, immune to the very human elements of ego and the occasional bad call.

He's particularly irked by the idea that his player choices might have something to do with his former stomping ground, Roma. The *shocking* revelation! Apparently, if they'd lost, he'd be the one groveling for forgiveness. But since they won, it's all "mind-boggling viciousness." Please, Daniele, spare us the melodrama. We all know the world of football is a cesspool of backroom deals and preferential treatment, and your little tantrum only confirms that. It’s like watching a Kardashian complain about paparazzi attention - the sheer hypocrisy is exhausting, but we’re here for it.

De Rossi goes on to wax lyrical about his "process of decision-making," as if he's inventing cold fusion. He talks about "attitude, tactics, and physical condition" like he’s discovered a new planet. And then, the ultimate humblebrag: quoting Antonio Conte. "Make a mistake using your own head and your own ideas." How profound. It's almost as if these managers exist in a vacuum, completely detached from the fans who actually pay their salaries. Honestly, it’s enough to make you want to revisit some of our past stories about how the 'Fake News' Trap Is Dead, because these guys are masters of their own narrative control.

He's also apparently got a player who can be "fully fit for selection," which is just *thrilling* news for anyone invested in the dramatic arc of a professional athlete's knee. And Sabelli? Apparently, he's been *underestimated*. Who knew? It’s almost as if football clubs are full of individuals who perform better when they feel valued. Revolutionary stuff, truly. Leali, the goalkeeper who's "giving us a hand," is also mentioned. He's being a "professional" despite feeling "sidelined." Oh, the humanity! The sheer struggle of these pampered athletes. It's enough to make you forget about the real-world issues, like Nairobi’s 'Soft Life' Going Underwater.

The man is practically vibrating with indignation that anyone would dare to suggest his line-up choices are anything other than pure, unadulterated tactical genius. He insists he picks players based on who has the "better chance of helping me win the match today." Because, of course, that's the *only* possible motivation. Not a hint of sentimentality, not a whisper of favoritism, just cold, hard footballing logic. Bless his little manager heart. It's almost quaint, this attempt to portray himself as some stoic chess master, when in reality, he’s just another talking head in the never-ending circus of professional sports.

So, let’s all just applaud Daniele De Rossi for his brave stand against the tyrannical forces of public opinion and the rampant "gossip." It's so refreshing to hear a man in power admit that sometimes, just sometimes, he makes decisions based on his own head. Groundbreaking. Now, if you'll excuse me, I need a strong drink to process this seismic revelation.
