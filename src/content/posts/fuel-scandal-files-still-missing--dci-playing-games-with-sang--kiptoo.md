So, Joe Sang, the former Kenya Pipeline boss, and Daniel Kiptoo, the big shot at EPRA, along with some fellow named Liban Mohammed, got themselves nabbed a week ago. Arrested. For importing substandard fuel, apparently. And cooking up data. And breaking procurement laws. Sounds about right.

But here's the punchline, courtesy of the Standard: the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) hasn't even bothered to hand over the files. A week. What are they doing, waiting for a better offer? Or perhaps they're just enjoying the drama. This whole circus around substandard fuel and dodgy deals wouldn't be the first time we've seen such tomfoolery. Remember the stories about how even Chinese phones managed to take over Africa's market and data? It all points to the same old song and dance of weak oversight and greedy hands.

The official reason for their arrest is the usual suspects: substandard fuel and cooked books. Apparently, they used some industry data to justify importing the dodgy stuff and then waltzed past procurement laws like they were suggestions. It’s the same old story in this country, isn’t it? Some get rich, the rest of us get the short end of the stick, and the evidence disappears faster than a free beer at a political rally.

And now, a week later, the prosecutors are still waiting for the actual paperwork. It’s like watching a bad play where the actors have forgotten their lines and the director is out drinking. This whole process smells fishy, and not in the good way. It’s just more proof that justice in Nairobi moves at the speed of a snail stuck in traffic on Mombasa Road.

This whole charade of arrests followed by missing files is just another Tuesday in Kenya. You can subscribe to newsletters and read all about it, but until the actual evidence surfaces and people are held accountable, it’s just noise. We’ll be lucky if this doesn't end up like those "18 Years of Shooting Blanks" where the KDF finally found a working gun - eventually, and probably after much complaining.

The promise of "journalism with depth, courage, and independent stories" is all well and good, but right now, what we need is the actual story, not just the headlines of arrests and missing files. We need to see those documents, see the charges stick, and see some real consequences. Until then, it’s just more hot air and recycled scandals.
