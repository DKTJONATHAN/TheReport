The Government Spokesperson wants us to believe that the opposition is a headless chicken, and honestly, they aren’t wrong. Without the doyen of politics to hold their hands, the remaining factions are just a group of disgruntled ex-ministers crying because they lost their seat at the high table. They are singing an incoherent hymn of "wantam vs tutam" while we, the common citizens, are stuck in the middle of their power games. It is the same old script: use the youth for maandamano, cry about "plots" to kill people without proof, and hope for sympathy votes. But let’s be real, the opposition’s track record is as dismal as a dry tap in Eastlands.

However, don't let the government’s shiny PR fool you either. They talk about "miracles" like the Social Health Authority (SHA) as if we don't know the reality on the ground. For every "Joseph" who gets free treatment, there are ten other Kenyans being turned away because the system is offline or the hospitals haven't been paid. Calling it a "transformation" is a stretch when most of us are one hospital bill away from total poverty. They are selling us a Singapore dream while we are still navigating a third-world reality where even basic safety becomes a revenue stream.

Every time the state claims they are protecting us, my wallet starts to ache. It is never about service; it is always about the next levy or the next fee. Even something as simple as [The CA’s 'Safety' Warning Is Just Another Tax Trap for the Broke](/posts/the-ca-s--safety--warning-is-just-another-tax-trap-for-the-broke) shows exactly how the system works. They wrap their hunger for our money in the flag of "public interest" and expect us to clap. Whether it’s phone registration or housing levies, the goal is the same: squeeze the hustler until there is nothing left but dust.

Then there is the NYOTA program and the so-called affordable housing. They claim to be empowering 121,800 entrepreneurs with "apprenticeships" and stipends. In Nairobi terms, that is just a fancy way of saying they are giving the youth crumbs to keep them from hitting the streets again. The affordable housing project has turned the country into a construction site, but who are these houses for? Most of us are just paying the mandatory levy so that someone else can have a "plum position" in the executive. It’s a brilliant scheme if you’re the one holding the shovel, not the one paying for the dirt.

The Spokesperson claims President Ruto is "way ahead" of his competitors. That might be true, but he is also way ahead of the average Kenyan's ability to pay for his vision. The BETA plan sounds less like a roadmap to prosperity and more like a software update that keeps crashing your phone. They talk about paying billions in pending bills for roads, but my estate still looks like a lunar landscape every time it rains. We are being told to focus on the "tremendous work" done in three years, but all I see is a "Broad-Based" government that just added more mouths to the feeding trough.

At the end of the day, we are stuck between an opposition that wants power for the sake of it and a government that treats the national budget like a personal piggy bank. The vision is clear, the focus is sharp, and the energy is high-but it’s all directed at keeping the elite in their motorcades while we figure out how to survive the next tax cycle. The "little miracles" they talk about are just sparks in a dark room, and frankly, I’m still waiting for someone to turn on the lights.
