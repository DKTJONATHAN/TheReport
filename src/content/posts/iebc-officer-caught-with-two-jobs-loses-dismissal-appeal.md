So, Calisto Oyugi, an ICT officer at the IEBC, decided he was good enough for two public sector paychecks. Turns out, the law doesn't quite see it that way. The Employment and Labour Relations Court, in a moment of shocking adherence to rules, upheld his dismissal. Apparently, you can't just break the law and then expect to get away with it. Who knew?

This whole drama comes as the IEBC is busy asking voters who registered before 2012 to do it all over again. I guess they're still cleaning up the mess from previous elections, or maybe just trying to find more ways to keep us all in a perpetual state of electoral anxiety. Meanwhile, we've got heads of directorates sitting in acting capacities for over two years. It's a real testament to efficiency, isn't it?

And it gets better. Investigations have apparently uncovered some real gems: duplicate payroll names, promotions that smell fishy, and payments to people who likely only exist on paper. It’s the same old song and dance, really. This is the kind of mismanagement that makes you wonder if we’re actually exporting meat and importing impunity, just like some people have been saying.

This whole circus reminds me of the endless political games we play. You have Prof Francis Aduol trotting before the Senate to explain himself, while the new batch of seven commissioners and the chairperson are just starting their six-year term. Let's just hope they don't pick up any bad habits from the previous occupants. Given the track record, I wouldn't hold my breath.
