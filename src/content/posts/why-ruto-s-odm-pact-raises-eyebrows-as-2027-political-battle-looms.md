WHY RUTO’S ODM SHENANIGANS RAISE EYEBROWS AS 2027 POLITICAL SHAM LOOMS
Ruto's pact with ODM is just another Nairobi drama. Expect betrayal, broken promises, and a scramble for crumbs.
ODM wants the Deputy President seat? Good luck with that. Ruto will promise the moon and deliver dust.
Politics
2027 Elections, Ruto, ODM
Negotiations
With 16 months to this circus they call an election, the political rats are already scurrying. President Ruto's little dance with the ODM is less a strategic alliance and more a desperate attempt to patch up his already crumbling Kenya Kwanza coalition. They're not talking about building a better Kenya; they're just dividing the spoils before the pie is even baked.

ODM, predictably, is pushing for a "structured power-sharing framework" - read: they want a bigger slice of the pie and the Deputy President seat is their opening gambit. This has sent shockwaves through the UDA camp, not because they care about good governance, but because it threatens their own positions. The presidency is safe in Ruto's hands, but beneath that, it's a free-for-all over key positions that control the cash and the cameras.

Oburu Oginga’s bold declaration about the DP post is just chest-thumping to gain leverage. Deputy President Kithure Kindiki is playing coy, talking about forgiveness and elders, while his allies are already sharpening their knives. This is how it works: the politicians posture, the loyalists bark, and the public gets fed the same old empty promises. Remember when we thought things might change? It’s like a recurring nightmare, or maybe just another episode of "Eternal Delusions: A Quiet EP for a Country Loudly Drowning."

Junet Mohamed, never one to mince words, is making it clear ODM isn't coming to the table as a junior partner. They're reminding everyone that their MCAs, MPs, Senators, Governors, and Woman Reps are their own, and if Ruto messes with them, they'll trot out their own presidential candidate. This whole “respect each other” talk is just a thinly veiled threat to remind everyone that ODM can cause problems. And they will.

Meanwhile, Ruto’s current allies like Mudavadi, Wetang'ula, and Kingi are suddenly looking very vulnerable. Mudavadi folded his party into UDA, losing his bargaining chip. Wetang'ula’s dual role as Speaker and party leader is always a point of contention, perfect ammunition for political rivals. And Kingi, despite his early loyalty, might just find himself on the chopping block if it means accommodating ODM. It’s a ruthless game of musical chairs, and no one is safe.

The real fight is for the gravy train ministries: Interior, Treasury, Defence, Health, Foreign Affairs. These are the levers of power, the sources of endless patronage. ODM wants a formal, written agreement to be treated as an "equal partner," not a junior affiliate. This isn’t about serving the people; it’s about securing their own power and influence, ensuring the next four years are just as corrupt and self-serving as the last.
