MIDEAST MADNESS: SH400M FOR EXPAT EXODUS AS IRAN CRISIS HITS HOME

So, the government wants a cool Sh400 million to ferry back Kenyans who apparently decided that dodging bombs in the Middle East and getting roped into fighting in Russia was a brilliant career move. Because apparently, our diplomats are brilliant at facilitating, but not so much at actually *paying* for the escape when the chickens come home to roost, and the chickens are, you know, covered in shrapnel.

Ambassador Hellen Gichuhi, bless her heart, is trotting before Parliament asking for the cash, telling tales of 15 poor souls, including kids and students, being plucked from Iran and en route to Nairobi. She’s painting a picture of thousands of Kenyans in the Middle East suddenly realizing that "escalating war" isn't just a catchy news headline, but an actual threat to their "wait and see" attitude. It's almost as if they didn't expect their employers to bail when the gas plants started going boom.

Then we have the whole Russia-Ukraine kerfuffle. Apparently, some Kenyans, lured by promises of decent salaries, willingly signed up to fight. Now, Kamukunji MP Yusuf Hassan rightly points out that these aren't unwitting victims, but folks who *chose* this path. He’s got a point; personal responsibility, remember that? Yet, the committee chair, Belgut MP Nelson Koech, comes back from Russia with rosy tales of Kenyans living it up, earning good money, conveniently downplaying the body count. It makes you wonder who’s really pulling the strings, and if this whole "repatriation" is just a convenient way to clean up a mess that could have been avoided, a situation eerily similar to the power plays that can push [Kenya Pushed to the Brink by Phone Calls and Politics](/posts/stolen-ballots--power-plays--kenya-pushed-to-the-brink-by-phone-calls-and-politics).

The good ambassador also touches on the murky waters of human trafficking disguised as migration, unregulated recruiters, and fake jobs. It’s the same old song and dance, isn't it? Kenyans chasing dreams abroad, only to find themselves in the crosshairs of political conflicts, rising nationalism, and the ever-present threat of deportation from places like the US. The Sh14 million already allocated in the supplementary budget feels like a drop in the ocean for what sounds like a never-ending drama.

Honestly, 500,000 Kenyans in the Middle East, and we’re scrambling for Sh400 million because *some* of them are in trouble? It’s a classic Nairobi realist’s lament: we’re always patching up holes instead of building a solid foundation. We’re good at facilitation, but when it comes to preventative measures or genuine accountability for those who exploit our citizens, suddenly the coffers are empty until the crisis hits, and then it’s a desperate plea for more cash.
