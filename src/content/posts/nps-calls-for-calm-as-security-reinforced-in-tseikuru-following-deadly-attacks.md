NPS Urges Calm Amidst Brutal Retaliatory Attacks in Tseikuru as Security is "Reinforced"

The National Police Service is "calling for calm" in Tseikuru, Kitui County, after a string of deadly retaliatory attacks between two communities left at least 12 dead. Police say they've identified a pattern and are sending in a "high-powered security team."

This is the usual song and dance, isn't it? "Condemn the attacks," "must be stopped immediately." Meanwhile, people are getting hacked down like weeds. First, a herdsman goes missing on March 29th. The next day? Four dead. Then, a month later, on April 25th, seven more bite the dust in Kwa Kamari. It's a clear pattern of revenge, just like any other day in this country.

The Inspector General, Douglas Kanja, has "dispatched" a "high-powered security team." Big words, very impressive. Eliud Lagat, the Deputy Inspector General, is reportedly "on the ground." Let's see how much ground he covers before the next incident. Reinforcements are also supposedly en route. I hope they're not just sending more tea and biscuits.

The police promise an "exhaustive investigation" to a "logical conclusion." We'll believe that when we see it. They're urging community leaders to "speak the language of peace and reconciliation," while simultaneously vowing to pursue "criminal culpability and ultimate justice." It's a bit like asking a hyena to preach vegetarianism, isn't it? They’ve even given out toll-free numbers for tips, as if anyone in their right mind would trust the system enough to snitch.

They want us to report anything to the nearest police station or use their fancy numbers. #FichuaKwaDCI, they call it. Good luck with that.
