So, AC Milan. Supposed to be consolidating, fighting for… what exactly now? The Scudetto is gone, a distant memory. Now it’s about survival. Survival of what? Dignity? A top-three finish? Please. This wasn't just a defeat; it was a public dissection at San Siro. Udinese came, saw, and clinically dismantled them. Three goals, each one a stab to the gut of what was supposed to be a competitive team.

Massimiliano Allegri, the mastermind, rolled out a new formation. Four-three-three. Rafael Leao shoved into the middle. Fresh legs. The result? Utter chaos. Confusion on the pitch, imbalance in their play, and a spectacular collapse. We saw glimpses of danger, Pulisic weaving, Leao flashing a chance over, but it was all an illusion. The cracks were there, just waiting for someone to exploit them.

And exploit them Udinese did. Nicolo Zaniolo, tearing through Milan's midfield like it was made of tissue paper. A deflected cross, Maignan wrong-footed. A warning, a loud one, that was completely ignored. Then came the second. Zaniolo, again the architect, a precise delivery met by Ekkelenkamp’s header. Between defenders who looked like they were statues. The jeers at halftime said it all. This wasn't just bad football; it was embarrassing football.

Allegri, of course, shuffled the deck, pushing bodies forward. For a fleeting moment, there was pressure. Modric tested the keeper, a spectacular save, a shot off the bar. A false dawn. Because then came another lightning counter, another clinical finish, drilled low into the corner. Game over. Three-nil. Rafael Leao, the symbol of their capitulation, leaving the pitch to whistles.
