KENYA COURT BACKTRACKS ON ABORTION RIGHTS: WOMEN STILL PAYING THE PRICE

Nairobi’s moral compass is spinning faster than a matatu on Ngong Road. Just when you thought the courts might offer a sliver of sanity on women’s reproductive health, they’ve gone and slammed the door shut. The High Court, in its infinite wisdom, had declared access to abortion a fundamental right. Then, *poof*, the appeals court swoops in, yanking that right away like a bad debt. Because apparently, “life begins at conception” is the gospel, and anything else is just inconvenient for some men in robes.

This isn't just some legal wig-waving; this is about real women, real lives. Seven Kenyan women die *every single day* thanks to these backstreet butchers, a direct consequence of a law stuck in colonial mud. The 2010 constitution supposedly allows for abortions when a woman’s life or health is at stake. But try telling that to the police who’ll shake you down for every shilling you have, or the courts that’ll drag you through the mud for daring to seek an end to a dangerous pregnancy. It’s a racket, plain and simple, and women are the ones getting fleeced.

Remember that 16-year-old girl in Kilifi? Arrested in her hospital bed after a botched abortion, then tossed into juvenile prison because she couldn't afford bail. Her and the clinician who tried to help her were treated like common criminals. The High Court saw the injustice, quashed the charges, and said enough was enough. But the appeals court? They’d rather investigate if the girl’s health was *truly* in danger before she sought help. Because clearly, a bleeding, in-pain teenager is just exaggerating. It’s a system designed to punish, not protect.

The Centre for Reproductive Rights is calling it “deeply disappointing.” Of course they are. They’re the ones dealing with the fallout while the judges make pronouncements from on high. This whole mess highlights the utter legal chaos that’s been plaguing reproductive healthcare in Kenya. It’s a cycle of harassment, extortion, and ultimately, death, all happening under the noses of people who claim to uphold the constitution. It makes you wonder if they’ve ever heard of the concept of dignity, or freedom from cruel treatment. This isn't about abstract rights; it's about preventing the kind of carnage we see on our roads, [Blood, Billions, and the Big Lie: Kenya’s Road Carnage Industry]( /posts/blood--billions--and-the-big-lie--kenya-s-road-carnage-industry), but for women's bodies.

The numbers are staggering. Over 790,000 induced abortions in one year alone. And more than 300,000 of those women needed medical attention because the first attempt went sideways. This isn't a fringe issue; it's a public health crisis being actively ignored by the very people supposed to be solving it. The appeals court ruling is a slap in the face to every woman in this country who will now face even greater risks. They say they’ll appeal to the Supreme Court. Let's see if that’s just another way to kick the can down the road, or if there’s any hope left for actual justice.
