Market volatility is not a "risk" in the modern era-it is the natural state of a world where supply chains are held together by prayer and precarious shipping lanes. India likes to pretend it is the new global anchor, but the facade is fading. Whether it is the threat of Trump-era visa tantrums or the shadow of Adani’s legal woes in New York, the nation is constantly one headline away from a capital flight. The "foundational strengths" mentioned by the RBI are really just a cushion for the elite while the average consumer pays the price at the pump.

Look at how the real giants play the game. While China charms Saudi Arabia with dollar bonds to undermine the West, India is left scavenging for scraps of stability. The acquisition sprees of old veterans like Reliance or L&T look less like strategic growth and more like desperate consolidation before the inevitable storm hits. It is a cynical game where the winners are merely those who lose their wealth at a slightly slower rate than their neighbors.

This is not just about spreadsheets and stock scores. It is about a world where the global south is used as a buffer for the ambitions of the powerful. We see this pattern everywhere, from the corporate boardrooms of Mumbai to the front lines where foreign interests dictate the cost of human life. As noted in [Moscow's Newest Plumbing Project: Kenyan Bodies on the Front Line](/posts/moscow-s-newest-plumbing-project--kenyan-bodies-on-the-front-line), the periphery is always the first to bleed when the center loses its grip. Economic "disruption" is just a polite, sanitized term for the systemic exploitation that keeps the global engine running.
