So, the big wigs in President Ruto's corner are apparently busy trying to figure out how to stop Rigathi Gachagua from being Rigathi Gachagua in Mt Kenya. Twelve strategies, they say. Twelve ways to "tame the mountain." Sounds like a lot of hot air and backroom deals. You'd think after all this time, they'd realise the mountain doesn't get "tamed," it just… is.

From the whispers coming out of State House and the Deputy President's office, it's all about "Operation Tame the Mountain." Ruto himself is out there telling people the opposition has no plan, which, let's be honest, is the standard playbook when you don't have much to show for yourself. It's amazing how quickly they forget that talking is cheap, and actual governance is hard.

Apparently, both Gachagua and some fellow named Dr. Karanja "lost their way in the mountain of politics," a poetic way of saying they don't know their backside from their elbow when it comes to the long game in Kenyan politics. The source material even admits conflict is inevitable. It always is, isn't it? Especially when egos are involved and everyone's scrambling for their own piece of the pie. Just like how [the EAC is a house of cards](https://www.example.com/posts/arusha-s-high-stakes-begging-bowl--why-the-eac-is-a-house-of-cards), these political alliances are built on shaky foundations.

And what's Gachagua doing? Telling the President to ignore the critics. Classic. Meanwhile, Ruto's "allies" - you know, the ones who suddenly become very vocal when it suits them - are busy telling everyone that Gachagua is just playing the victim to get sympathy. It's a tired old song and dance. They'll keep circling, making noise, and probably end up right back where they started, just with more tired politicians and less money in your pocket.
