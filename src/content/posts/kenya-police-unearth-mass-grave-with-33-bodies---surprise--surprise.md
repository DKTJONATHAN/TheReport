Kenya Police Unearth Mass Grave With 33 Bodies - Surprise, Surprise.

Another day, another gruesome discovery. This time it's western Kenya, where authorities have dug up at least 33 bodies from a mass grave, suspected to have been trucked in from a hospital morgue. Just another Tuesday, it seems, in this country of ours.

Homicide detectives, bless their hearts, found the remains of adults and children, even dismembered bits in gunny sacks, buried at a church-owned cemetery in Kericho. The head of the Directorate of Criminal Investigations, Mohamed Amin, claims they were "transferred from Nyamira District Hospital to a private cemetery." Naturally, he wants to know if this was done legally, as if the law ever applies to everyone equally. Apparently, unclaimed bodies in Kenyan morgues can be disposed of after 14 days, but only with a court order. Funny how court orders seem to disappear when it's inconvenient.

The bodies, whose identities are still a mystery - shocker - are undergoing autopsies. Local whispers suggest unidentified people, using a government vehicle, did the dirty deed, with some gravediggers having the nerve to tip off the police. Residents are calling for a "thorough investigation," as if that's ever yielded anything substantial beyond more paperwork. They want to know if the government was involved or if some other shadowy group is to blame. Oh, the suspense.

This isn't even the first time this has happened in the last three years. We had hundreds of bodies linked to some cult leader in Kilifi in 2023, and then a paltry 9 bodies fished out of a dumpsite in Nairobi this year. It’s almost as if there's a pattern here, a consistent disregard for human life, much like the stories we've covered before about [Amazon’s 30,000-soul cull](/posts/the-smile-turns-into-a-scar--amazon-s-30-000-soul-cull), where the scale of the "culling" was equally chilling.

And to top it all off, this whole grave fiasco coincides with growing concerns about police misconduct. Missing Voices reports a steady rise in extrajudicial killings and enforced disappearances. But hey, at least our police are efficient at… well, *something*. Let's just hope they find some court orders for this one.
