So, the KRA, bless their bureaucratic hearts, has decided that your perfectly curated Instagram feed is now just another source of untaxed income. They’re deploying this fancy new AI tool, the Intelligence Analysis Tool (IAT), to sniff out tax cheats. Apparently, your lavish lifestyle and those "gifted" trips aren't fooling anyone anymore, darling. Get ready for social network analysis to reveal your hidden connections to that questionable drop-shipping scheme you’ve been promoting. It’s all about data-driven tax administration now, which sounds suspiciously like "we're watching you."

And speaking of things going south, it seems 18 SACCOs are staring at a significant financial black hole after KUSCCO Mutual Assurance landed itself in statutory management. Imagine converting Ksh660 million in claims into shares, only to have the whole thing go kaput. KUSCCO was supposedly trying to offload its stake to a shady buyer for Ksh1.6 billion to cover a massive Ksh13.3 billion debt - a heist, the report says! Now, without fresh capital, these SACCOs might as well start planning their liquidation parties. It’s a real mess, and frankly, it makes you wonder if anyone in these financial institutions is actually awake, or if they’re all too busy chasing the digital gold rush.

On the slightly less disastrous but still eye-rolling front, the government is apparently considering spending Ksh300 million to recover unpaid Hustler Fund loans. Yes, you read that right. Ksh300 million to send out "awareness campaigns." Because, clearly, flashing cash and expecting people to pay it back was just too simple. They’ve got Ksh12.5 billion still defaulted. And while we're on the subject of questionable spending, this whole situation reminds me of those PR stunts that seem to cost more than the actual solution. Remember when we talked about [Free SHA for Inmates While You Starve? Zakayo's Newest PR Stunt Is a Prison Break for Logic](/posts/free-sha-for-inmates-while-you-starve--zakayo-s-newest-pr-stunt-is-a-prison-break-for-logic)? This feels cut from the same cloth.

In slightly more glamorous (and potentially unstable) news, EABL has snagged a new Group Chief Financial Officer, Justin Mollel, just in time for Asahi Group Holdings to swoop in and buy a majority stake. Risper Ohaga, the first African woman to hold that CFO gig, is moving on to bigger things at APA Apollo Group. It’s all very corporate musical chairs, isn't it? Meanwhile, Centum Investment Company has finally sold off its last bit of Sidian Bank, ending a 22-year saga. They made a tidy profit, apparently, but let's not forget that failed Ksh4.3 billion deal with Access Bank back in 2022. Always a dramatic exit, these financial players.

And for those of you navigating Nairobi’s treacherous roads, good luck. KeNHA is busy rerouting everyone and their mother due to "technical investigations and stabilisation work." Just when you thought you’d mastered the traffic chaos, they throw in new diversions. It’s enough to make you want to ditch your car and become a digital nomad. Oh wait, KRA is watching your online activity. So, it seems there’s no escape, really.

Finally, in a move that will surely delight the Uber and Bolt drivers who are practically running on fumes, the Ministry of Transport is planning a national pricing model. They want to end price undercutting and low driver earnings. Because apparently, the current system of chaotic price wars and driver exploitation was just too… stable. They’re benchmarking global standards, which I'm sure will be a comfort to drivers earning pennies while passengers enjoy cut-price rides. It’s all about fair returns, affordability, and sustainable growth - code for "we're going to make this complicated for everyone involved."
