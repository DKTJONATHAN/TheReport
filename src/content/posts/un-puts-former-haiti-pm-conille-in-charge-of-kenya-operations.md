So, the UN has decided to park Garry Conille, the former Prime Minister of Haiti - a man whose tenure sounds more like a revolving door than effective leadership - right here in Kenya. He's been handed the shiny title of UN resident coordinator. Apparently, he's taking the reins today, which, knowing how these things go, probably means a lot of paperwork and even more meetings.

His last stint as PM was a six-month affair in 2024. What else was happening then? Oh right, Kenyan troops were being deployed to Haiti. A noble cause, perhaps, but let's be honest, Haiti’s gang problem is a hydra-headed beast, and a few thousand troops weren't exactly going to lop off all the heads. The UN-backed force is being rejigged, and our boys are expected to pack their bags soon. Funny how these grand interventions often fizzle out, leaving the mess for someone else to clean up. It reminds me a bit of [The Great Political Con: Why 'Friendly Fire' is Just Code for More Eating](/posts/the-great-political-con--why--friendly-fire--is-just-code-for-more-eating).

Conille's history isn't exactly brimming with stability. He served another four months as PM back in the early 2010s, and guess what? Both terms ended in a spectacular fashion, fueled by the usual political infighting that seems to be the national sport in Haiti. So, a man who couldn't keep his own house in order is now being tasked with coordinating UN efforts in Kenya. Let's just hope he doesn't bring his old habits with him.

He's been with the UN since 1999, doing all sorts of roles, including a stint with UNICEF. That’s a lot of years collecting a paycheck from a global organization that, at times, feels more like a bureaucracy than a savior. Now, he's trading the chaos of Port-au-Prince for the relative order of Nairobi. One can only hope his experience navigating the treacherous political waters of Haiti has somehow prepared him for the subtle nuances of Kenyan office politics. Or maybe, he's just another cog in the UN machine, moving from one problematic posting to the next.
