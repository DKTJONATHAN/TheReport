Wetangula Rallies Western Behind Ruto's Re-election Claiming Development, We'll See.

Oh, the usual song and dance. Speaker Wetangula, in his infinite wisdom, is out there in Western Kenya, telling everyone to buckle up and support President Ruto's next stab at the presidency. Apparently, this Kenya Kwanza outfit has miraculously delivered development projects that are so "tangible" they'll make your eyes water. He's talking about reviving Mumias and Nzoia sugar factories, which, if you believe the hype, have suddenly turned cane farmers into millionaires and every sugar mill is now a humming engine of prosperity. Right.

Then there's the infrastructure marvel. Wetangula drones on about roads and the SGR extension, promising a breezy three-and-a-half-hour commute from Nairobi to Western Kenya. This sounds less like development and more like a convenient excuse for another photo op. And the Level Six hospitals? Let's just hope they're more than just fancy brick and mortar, because we all know how quickly those promises can evaporate. He's even got the audacity to claim we're heading for "Singapore" - a bit ambitious, don't you think, considering the current economic climate?

He's showering praise on Ruto, saying he's the only one distributing projects equitably. Funny, because I've seen my fair share of "development" that ends up benefiting a select few while the rest of us are left to navigate the potholes. And the plea to register to vote and acquire IDs? Standard procedure, of course. They need those numbers to look good, to justify the endless rallies and the "boda boda empowerment drive" that probably just serves as a backdrop for more political grandstanding. It's a shame some of these leaders can't focus on genuine issues instead of just shouting slogans.

The governors are chiming in, too, painting a picture of a Western Kenya "locked under President William Ruto." They're aiming for a whopping 4 million registered voters. More numbers, more promises. Governor Lusaka waxes lyrical about how Bungoma has benefited, mentioning a stadium and the Nzoia revival. He even dares to suggest Ruto is criticized because he's *not* hoarding development like his predecessors. It's a bold claim, considering how many regions still feel left behind.

Meanwhile, the MPs are busy throwing shade. Tindi Mwale is calling Gachagua a tribalist, while Didmus Barasa points fingers at Uhuru Kenyatta for financing "divisive politics." Innocent Mugabe echoes the sentiment about Gachagua, claiming resources were only channeled to one part of the country when he was in power. It's a mudslinging contest disguised as a debate about development. And Sirisia MP Waluke? He's just adding his voice to the chorus, saying Ruto's record surpasses others. It's all very convenient, isn't it? This entire exercise feels like a well-rehearsed play, with everyone reading their lines about development and progress, while the real issues probably get lost in the noise. It makes you wonder if any of this is truly about the people, or just about securing the next term. It reminds me of how our young men are being shipped off to places like Russia, a situation that seems to be driven by desperation and a lack of viable opportunities here in Kenya’s meat grinder.

And let's not forget the whispers about the SGR extension. While the promise of cutting travel time is enticing, the history of stalled projects and inflated budgets in this country makes me skeptical. We've heard these grand plans before, only to see them fizzle out or become white elephants. The government's eagerness to waive import duty on electric vehicles, as stated by President Ruto, is another development, but how much of that will actually trickle down to the average Kenyan struggling with the cost of living is yet to be seen. It all sounds good on paper, but the reality on the ground is often a different story.
