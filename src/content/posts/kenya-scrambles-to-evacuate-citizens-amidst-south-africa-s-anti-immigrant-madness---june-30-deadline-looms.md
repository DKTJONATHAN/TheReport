So, Kenya is apparently racing to drag its citizens out of South Africa. Why? Because some local mobs have decided foreigners need to pack up and leave by the end of the month. Because, you know, Africa's great at solving its problems by kicking out its neighbours. Makes perfect sense.

And wouldn't you know it, our dear diplomats are struggling. Turns out a good chunk of these "evacuees" don't have their papers in order. Temporary travel passes, they're calling them. Temporary fixes for a permanent mess, more like. This whole fiasco is just another reminder that our governments are as prepared as a chameleon in a rainbow. It’s like they’ve never heard of the concept of [Sustainability in Today's Economy: Can Anyone Afford It? Or Is It Just Another Fancy Word for Broke?](/posts/sustainability-in-today-s-economy--can-anyone-afford-it--or-is-it-just-another-fancy-word-for-broke).

They're blaming "competition, masculinity and apartheid’s psychological scars." Oh, please. It’s always some fancy excuse. The real reason is simpler: people are desperate, and when they're desperate, they lash out. And guess who's the easiest target? The foreigner trying to make a living, often lured by fake promises online that land them in even more trouble.

And the "local organisers" who are doing the door-to-door intimidation? Give me a break. This isn't just about economic pressure; it's about good old-fashioned tribalism and a warped sense of ownership. Every time there's a sniff of trouble, it’s the same old story: “Go back where you came from.” As if anyone has a choice about where they were born.

This whole thing is just another sad, predictable chapter in the ongoing saga of African disunity. We’re great at talking about Pan Africanism in fancy summit halls, but when it comes down to it, we’re just a collection of nations looking out for number one, and often at the expense of our own people abroad. Another day, another reminder of the institutional mirage of secure digital scarcity - or in this case, the scarcity of basic human decency.

So, while Kenya scrambles to collect its scattered citizens, spare a thought for the mess this leaves behind. More costs, more paperwork, more diplomatic headaches, and absolutely zero progress on the root causes. But hey, at least it makes for a dramatic headline.
