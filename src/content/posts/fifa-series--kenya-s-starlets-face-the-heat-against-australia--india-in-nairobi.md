So, the Harambee Starlets are apparently about to face their "biggest test." This week, Nairobi gets to host a few high-ranked teams for the 2026 Fifa Series. Australia, ranked a cool 119 places higher, and India, 67 places ahead. Sounds like a recipe for a good time, if your idea of a good time involves watching our girls get thoroughly outclassed. It’s the kind of thing that happens, isn’t it? We cheer, we hope, and then we explain why it was a learning experience.

Meanwhile, the continental showpiece in Morocco has been pushed back. Not that it matters much. Whether it’s March or July, the outcome for our teams is usually the same. And let’s not forget our own local leagues. AFC Leopards scraping a win against Kariobangi Sharks 1-0. Exciting stuff. Makes you wonder if these guys are practicing for the big leagues or just how to win by the smallest possible margin. It’s the Kenyan way, I suppose. Get by.

This whole "clear favourites" situation with coaches? That’s as old as time. Always someone getting the nod, while others are left wondering what they need to do to even get a sniff. It’s like that [Kenya Police FC Steal Three Points Off KCB FC, Because Of Course They Did](/posts/kenya-police-fc-steal-three-points-off-kcb-fc--because-of-course-they-did) match. Sometimes, the reasons for results are just… there. No grand strategy, just the way things are.

And then there’s the Harambee Stars. Suffered a penalty shootout loss in their Fifa Series match. Another heartbreak. Like that time we talked about the [The Bitter Aftertaste of Kenya’s $3.5 Billion Tea Scam](/posts/the-bitter-aftertaste-of-kenya-s--3-5-billion-tea-scam). Big promises, grand plans, and then… a penalty shootout. Or a scam. Same difference in the end, really. Just a different flavour of disappointment.

Still, they’re going to Abidjan for more high-profile friendlies. Because more friendlies, more opportunities to… well, to have more friendlies. It’s a cycle. We’ll hear about the progress, the development, the future. And then we’ll watch the next match and see if anything has actually changed. Don't hold your breath.
