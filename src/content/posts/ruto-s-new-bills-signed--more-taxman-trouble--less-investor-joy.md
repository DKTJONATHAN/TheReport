Ruto's New Bills Signed: More Taxman Trouble, Less Investor Joy

The President has given his nod to the Income Tax Bill, Special Economic Zones (Amendment) Bill, and the Technopolis Bill. Don't let the fancy talk of "streamlining" and "international best practices" fool you. This is just more government hot air designed to make us feel better about the same old song and dance.

So, they're "rationalizing" Capital Gains Tax to be more "international." What that really means is they've found a new way to squeeze more out of you when you try to make a quick buck, or a slow one for that matter. And this "predictable and competitive business environment"? We've heard that tune before, usually right before they introduce another fee or a hidden clause that makes doing business here feel like navigating a minefield. Remember when we talked about [THE PREDATORY STATE AND THE EXODUS OF CAPITAL](/posts/the-predatory-state-and-the-exodus-of-capital)? This is just another chapter in that thrilling saga.

Now, these Special Economic Zones are getting bigger, including oil and gas. Great. More areas to give tax breaks to the big players while the rest of us are left to fend for ourselves. Ten-year licenses? That's just a fancy way of saying they're locking in deals for the well-connected, while small businesses get the short end of the stick, as usual. They claim it's to accommodate "large-scale capital investments," which is code for "we're more interested in flashy projects than nurturing local growth."

And the Technopolis? A "comprehensive legal framework" for "technology-driven enterprises" and "innovation." More hubs, more government services, more promises of attracting "global investment, talent, and innovation." It sounds like another shiny distraction, a digital utopia on paper that will likely end up being another bureaucratic maze, just with faster internet.

Honestly, the only thing predictable about these bills is that they'll be used to justify more government spending and, surprise surprise, more taxes. The "ease of doing business" will remain a distant dream for most of us struggling to keep our heads above water.

Let's be real: this is just the government putting lipstick on a pig. They’re rearranging deck chairs on the Titanic while telling us we’re sailing towards a golden age. Don't hold your breath waiting for your life to get easier.
