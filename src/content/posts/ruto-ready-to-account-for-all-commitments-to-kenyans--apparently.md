Ruto Ready To Account For All Commitments - Or So He Says

President Ruto claims his government has delivered on all manifesto promises over the last three years, urging citizens to judge leaders on development records rather than divisive politics.

In Kapsabet, Nandi County, on Thursday, March 12, 2026, the President confidently stated his readiness to be held accountable for every commitment made to Kenyans. He painted a picture of a government that has successfully navigated its agenda, laying the groundwork for future elections where tangible progress will be the sole metric of leadership.

"Our visible and impactful development record sets the pace for the next election, where leaders will be judged on their track record or on an agenda that speaks to the needs of the people," he declared. This grand pronouncement, of course, conveniently sidesteps the daily grinds of Nairobi where promises often get lost in the shuffle. We’ve seen this song and dance before, haven’t we? The President also took a jab at the usual suspects: personality cults, tribal rhetoric, and hot air. He insisted that such outdated political maneuvering has no place in a nation striving for progress and unity. While it’s nice to hear talk of unity and reduced tribal divisions, it’s a shame these sentiments don’t always translate into actual policy on the ground. Let’s not forget the endless debates about what truly constitutes national progress. We saw a similar pipe dream with Kasarani Tourism And The Wafcon Pipe Dream.

Ruto concluded by detailing his grand plans for national transformation, emphasizing that government initiatives are precisely calibrated to meet community needs and build a sustainable future. President Ruto, in his usual confident stride, has declared his readiness to be held accountable for every single promise made to the Kenyan people. Speaking from Kapsabet town in Nandi County on Thursday, March 12, 2026, he asserted that his government has diligently fulfilled all the commitments outlined in its manifesto over the past three years. According to him, the tangible progress achieved across various sectors forms the bedrock for the next electoral cycle, where citizens will supposedly cast their votes based on real development, not just fancy slogans or divisive tactics.

"Our visible and impactful development record sets the pace for the next election, where leaders will be judged on their track record or on an agenda that speaks to the needs of the people," he proclaimed. It's a noble sentiment, certainly, and one that many of us in Nairobi would love to see materialize. However, given the usual political theatre, one can't help but wonder if this is just another round of carefully crafted rhetoric, designed to sound good while the real issues continue to simmer. We’ve seen similar grand pronouncements before, like the optimistic outlook in THE GAVEL MEETS THE RUNGUS: MARAGA’S REALITY CHECK.

The President also took a swipe at what he termed as "empty rhetoric, personality-driven, divisive, and tribal politics," stating there will be no room for them. He argued that such approaches have historically stunted national progress and fractured unity. While the intention might be to foster a more cohesive nation, the reality on the ground often paints a different picture. The path to national unity is a winding one, and sometimes grand declarations fall short of addressing the deep-seated issues.

Concluding his address, Ruto outlined his administration's local and national transformation programs, emphasizing their design to directly address community needs and build sustainable development for all Kenyans. The message is clear: unity, reduced tribal divisions, and national cohesion are the order of the day as the next election approaches. Whether these promises will translate into lasting change remains to be seen, but the President is certainly setting the stage for a performance review based on delivery.
