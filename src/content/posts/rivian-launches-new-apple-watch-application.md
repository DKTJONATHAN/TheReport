Rivian has introduced a new application for the Apple Watch, designed to seamlessly integrate essential vehicle controls and real-time information with users' wearable technology. This development aims to enhance convenience and streamline the interaction between Rivian owners and their vehicles, moving critical functions from a smartphone to the wrist.

The application offers immediate access to several core vehicle controls. Owners can lock or unlock doors, vent windows, or activate the alarm with a simple tap on their Apple Watch. For more nuanced adjustments, the digital crown allows users to set a target state of charge for their vehicle's battery or modify the cabin temperature. The app also features a customizable design, enabling users to select up to four quick controls for instant access to their most frequently used functions.

Accessing and driving a Rivian vehicle is further simplified through the new app. For Gen 1 drivers, tapping the "lock" icon and having the app open prepares the vehicle for immediate use. Gen 2 customers benefit from passive entry functionality; once their digital key is configured, the vehicle automatically unlocks as they approach, facilitating a more effortless entry and departure.

This new application is engineered as a natural extension of the driving experience, featuring a cohesive design that mirrors the Rivian app on the iPhone. Users can add glanceable battery status to their chosen watch face, keeping the vehicle's range prominently displayed alongside their daily activity metrics. The app visualizes the specific vehicle color and variation, while iconography, colors, and controls for Climate, Energy, and Gear Guard maintain consistency across platforms, even featuring the Gear Guard yeti on the smaller screen.

The introduction of this Apple Watch application underscores a broader trend towards integrating personal technology more deeply into the automotive ecosystem. By placing vehicle controls directly on a wearable device, Rivian is addressing the demand for immediate accessibility and a more connected user experience. This move represents a further step in the digital transformation of the automotive experience, placing significant vehicle functions directly onto a personal device. This trend towards pervasive digital control over physical assets raises broader considerations about how technology reshapes our interactions and dependencies, a theme often explored in discussions concerning the expanding reach of digital systems, as highlighted in pieces like [The Panopticon’s Expansion: Why Your Ethics Are Irrelevant to the Machine](/posts/the-panopticon-s-expansion--why-your-ethics-are-irrelevant-to-the-machine).

Rivian indicates that this initial release is just the beginning, with plans to update the Apple Watch app over time with additional features designed to continually improve the user experience on the go. This commitment suggests an ongoing evolution of the digital interface between owners and their Rivian vehicles.
