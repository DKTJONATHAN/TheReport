If you have been wondering why your favorite "entrepreneur" influencer has suddenly stopped posting their daily "rise and grind" motivational quotes, Interpol might have the answer. Twenty-seven suspects in Kenya were recently hauled off in a massive sting operation targeting fake investment scams. These geniuses were allegedly using messaging apps and fictitious testimonials to lure people into "top global corporates." It is the same old script: post a picture next to a rented car, talk about "financial freedom," and then rob your followers blind for the price of a cheap brunch.

The audacity of these people is actually impressive if you do not think about the victims for too long. They were asking for initial investments as low as fifty dollars. Imagine being a whole "international scammer" and risking jail time for the price of a pair of knock-off sneakers. These scammers showed their victims fabricated dashboards that looked like they were swimming in money, but the moment anyone tried to withdraw their cash, the system magically "blocked" them. It is giving very much "scam-core" aesthetics, and honestly, the hypocrisy of calling this an "investment" is the funniest thing I have heard all week.

This was not just a local mess, either. The operation spanned sixteen African countries, proving that the "Yahoo yahoo" boys in Nigeria and the "investors" in Nairobi are all part of one big, messy family. In Nigeria, the heat got so bad that these scammers are reportedly fleeing to Ghana to avoid asset seizures. They even uncovered a residential property built by a syndicate leader specifically to act as a hub for their crimes. It is like a reality show house, but instead of finding love, they were finding ways to steal your grandmother’s pension.

The desperation to look successful on the internet is driving everyone to the edge of a cliff. Just like how [Bernard Kibet Koech Receives Four-Year Doping Ban](/posts/bernard-kibet-koech-receives-four-year-doping-ban) proved that shortcuts in the world of professional sports never end well, these cyber scammers are finding out that their "investment" shortcuts lead straight to a cold cell. Whether it is performance-enhancing drugs or performance-enhancing bank accounts, the truth always has a nasty habit of coming out when the authorities start looking at the data.

Over in Côte d’Ivoire, the situation was even grimmer. They arrested fifty-eight people for mobile loan fraud that targeted the most vulnerable populations. These apps promised quick, unsecured loans but ended up harvesting personal data and using abusive debt-collection practices. It is the ultimate "pick me" behavior but for criminals. They pretend to be your savior just to ruin your life and steal your identity. If your "quick loan" comes with a side of harassment and identity theft, it is not a loan- it is a trap.

At the end of the day, this whole operation, which exposed losses of over forty-five million dollars, is a wake-up call for everyone living for the "gram." Your favorite "lifestyle coach" might just be a "phishing coach" in disguise. Interpol seized thousands of devices and took down over a thousand fraudulent social media accounts. The next time you see someone promising a thousand percent return on a fifty-dollar investment, do yourself a favor and just buy a pizza instead. At least the pizza is real, unlike these people's "global corporate" connections.
