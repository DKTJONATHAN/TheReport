So, a U.S. citizen, one of those do-gooders working for a "humanitarian organization" in Congo, has gone and tested positive for Ebola. The CDC, bless their bureaucratic hearts, are "working with" everyone to "prevent further transmission" and "identify close contacts." It's the same old song and dance, isn't it? While they're busy holding hands and singing Kumbaya, the death toll is climbing and cases are popping up in neighboring Uganda. Shocking, I know.

This isn't even the first time a foreigner has gotten a taste of Congo's hospitality. An American doctor got it earlier this week and was whisked off to Germany. Remember when Trump’s lot were talking about a fancy new facility in Kenya for these exposed Yanks? Yeah, that got shut down faster than a Nairobi matatu at a roadblock. Apparently, Kenyan courts have more sense than some of these politicians.

The whole thing was apparently declared an outbreak on May 15th, though the disease had been having a field day for weeks before anyone official bothered to notice. The World Health Organization, the ultimate masters of "too little, too late," probably figured it would just sort itself out. And the cherry on top? It's the rare Bundibugyo virus, with no vaccine or treatment to speak of. Just adds a little extra spice to the whole "impending doom" cocktail.

And let's not forget the usual suspects that make containing anything in eastern Congo a laugh riot. Funding gaps? Check. Attacks on health centers? Check. An ongoing conflict that's more of a permanent fixture than the Nairobi traffic? Double-check. It's a miracle anyone even bothers trying. Though, I suppose some folks are clinging to hope that these new clinical trials might actually do something, which is cute. Almost as cute as watching our own politicians argue over things that don't matter, proving Congress's paralysis is a wake-up call for the obvious, much like this Ebola mess.

This whole situation is just a stark reminder of how things work, or rather, don't work, when you peel back the glossy veneer. It's a mess, as usual, and the people who are actually there, doing the dirty work, are the ones left to deal with the fallout. The rest of us will just read about it, tut-tut, and move on to the next disaster. Because that's what we do.
