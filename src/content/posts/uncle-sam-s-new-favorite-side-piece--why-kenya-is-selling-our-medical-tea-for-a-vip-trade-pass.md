While the rest of us are struggling to afford a basic data bundle, our government is out here playing high-stakes "besties" with Washington D.C. Principal Secretary Regina Ombam led a delegation to the U.S. this week, looking for a trade framework that sounds more like a complicated prenup than a partnership. They are calling it "Reciprocal Trade," which is fancy talk for "we want your money, and you want our data." It is giving very much "climbing the social ladder" energy, with our leaders desperate to be seen in the same room as the big players while the rest of us are left holding the bill.

The real tea, however, is not the macadamia nuts or the tea exports they keep bragging about. It is the messy health deal that has Busia Senator Okiya Omtatah acting like the only person at the party with a functioning brain. Omtatah is rightfully dragging the administration for signing a deal that shares everything from HIV status to mental health records without so much as a "by your leave" from Parliament. It is like a celebrity iCloud hack, except the government is the one handing out the passwords and pretending it is for our own good.

President Ruto is out here doing damage control, claiming the Attorney General reviewed everything with a "fine-tooth comb." We have heard that one before. He insists they are only sharing "de-identified" data, as if a "trend" or a "total" cannot be traced back to the very people paying for these bloated systems. Just like how [Scholarship Won-t Fix the Potholes: The Great Public Finance Myth](/posts/scholarship-won-t-fix-the-potholes--the-great-public-finance-myth) exposes the lies we are told about where our money goes, this health deal is a classic case of creating a parallel governance structure that bypasses the rules we actually have in place.

Let us talk about the hypocrisy of "Intellectual Property" rights taking center stage in these talks. They want to safeguard innovations and technology, but let’s be real- the only thing they are safeguarding is the profit margins of the elite. While Kenyan creatives are out here getting their work stolen and their royalties ignored, the government is busy worrying about the IP of high-value agricultural products. It is the same old story: protect the big business, ignore the actual talent that keeps this culture alive.

To top it all off, we are doing all this flirting while Donald Trump is slapping a 10 percent universal surcharge on everything we send over there. We are basically paying a cover charge to enter a club where the bouncer already hates us. The Trade Cabinet Secretary is boasting about a 425 billion shilling trade volume, but when you factor in the costs of losing our national sovereignty and our medical privacy, the math just is not mathing. It is high-fashion politics with a bargain-bin soul.
