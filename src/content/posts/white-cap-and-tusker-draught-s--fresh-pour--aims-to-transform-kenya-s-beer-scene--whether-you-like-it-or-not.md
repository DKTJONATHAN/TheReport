So, the story goes, Kenyans have always liked their beer warm, straight from the bottle. It's what we know, it's what we grew up with. Now, KBL, with their fancy new portable draught machines, wants to drag us, kicking and screaming, into the 21st century of 'brewery-fresh' pours. Apparently, the old draught machines were too big and too expensive, which is a shame for all those outlets that couldn't be bothered.

Now they're rolling these contraptions out everywhere, turning every gathering into a marketing opportunity. From the dust of the WRC Safari Rally to the roar of rugby fans, it's all about the 'experience' and 'connecting'. It’s all very well and good for them to talk about ‘moments people remember and share’, but is it really changing anything, or just slapping a branded tap on a party? It feels like another way to make us spend more for something we didn't even know we needed.

They're even showing up at Formula 1 viewing parties, adding a layer of 'sophisticated banter' to our sports obsession. Because what’s a debate about racing lines without a draught beer in hand, right? This whole ‘draught evolution’ means more places, more events, and more people being served this supposedly superior beer. It’s a relentless push, isn’t it? Makes you wonder if they’re trying to solve a problem or create one, all while pretending it’s progress.

It’s a bit like how some politicians promise the moon and stars, only for taxpayers to end up footing the bill later. We’ve seen it before, and we’ll likely see it again. [Mandago's Sh1.1 Billion Loan Saga: 'Eat Now, Pay Never' For Taxpayers](/posts/mandago-s-sh1-1-billion-loan-saga---eat-now--pay-never--for-taxpayers). This beer push feels similar - a big splash, a lot of noise, and then we’re left with the reality of whether it’s actually better, or just more expensive and more complicated.
