THE BIG TALK AT THE AMBASSADORS CONFERENCE: RUTO'S PROMISES TO TURN KENYA'S POTENTIAL INTO 'REAL' GAINS

Don't get your hopes up. Another conference, more fine speeches about unlocking markets and attracting investment. President Ruto, flanked by the usual suspects like Mwangi from Equity and CS Mudavadi, wants our ambassadors to stop just charming foreigners and actually bring home some serious cash. They're talking about "results driven diplomacy" and "tangible benefits." We've heard it all before, haven't we? It's always about potential, never about what's actually in our pockets.

Mudavadi, bless his heart, is reminding everyone that Kenya is "influential" because Ruto chairs COMESA. Right. Meanwhile, Equity's James Mwangi is preaching about domestic savings, as if Kenyans have spare shillings lying around after paying taxes and trying to afford basic commodities. He’s right about one thing though: relying too much on foreign capital can indeed strain the economy, a lesson we seem determined to learn the hard way, perhaps another KPC fire sale situation if we're not careful.

The big shiny new toys are these digital platforms, BiasharaLink and Deal House. The idea is to make our embassies trade hubs, tackling Africa's "trade execution gap" - which basically means all the talk and no deals. They’re supposed to help SMEs and women-led businesses. Let’s see if these platforms can actually connect anyone to anything beyond more bureaucracy, or if they’ll just become another expensive digital ghost town.

This whole push for "economic diplomacy" is supposed to be a shift from ambition to action. They want foreign exchange, jobs, and wealth. Sounds lovely. But when you look at the faces in those photos - the same faces always at the top - you have to wonder if the "real, tangible benefits" are meant for us, the wananchi, or just for those already swimming in it. It’s a nice narrative for the press, but the reality on the ground is usually a lot less glamorous.
