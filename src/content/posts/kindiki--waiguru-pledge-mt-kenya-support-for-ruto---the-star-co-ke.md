KINDIKI, WAIGURU TEAM UP TO PEDDLE MOUNT KENYA SUPPORT FOR RUTO RE-ELECTION - ANOTHER DAY, ANOTHER SHAKING











So, the Deputy President and the Kirinyaga Governor are out there, doing their song and dance, trying to convince the Mt Kenya region to keep President Ruto in power. They're waving around road projects and electricity connections like they're giving out free mandazi. The message is clear: vote for us, get development. Forget the opposition, they've got nothing but hot air. It's the same old story, isn't it? Keep the money flowing to your side, or you'll be left building your own house and then having to abandon it before you can even move in.

Kindiki, bless his heart, even used an analogy about building a house. Real creative, that. He's basically saying if you don't stick with the ruling party, you'll miss out on all the "benefits" of your vote. And Waiguru, she's echoing the same tune, parading her administration's "achievements" and warning against divisive rhetoric. It’s all about clinging to power, isn't it? Just like how some might be trying to maintain a fragile rebound in something as volatile as Bitcoin, it all depends on the circumstances, and right now, the circumstance is staying in government.

They're talking about Sh29 billion for roads and Sh440 million for electricity. Big numbers, meant to impress. And this County Aggregation and Industrial Park, co-funded at Sh500 million. Sounds fancy, but let's be real, it's all about tying the region to the national government's apron strings. Waiguru even brought up historical precedent, reminding everyone that the Mt Kenya region has always benefited when it's been "in government." It's a calculated move, playing on old fears and aspirations.

And then there's the little undercurrent of ambition. Waiguru, who's been eyeing the deputy presidency, and Kindiki, who's also in the running. They're pledging to work together to ensure the Deputy President's position stays in Mt Kenya. Of course, they are. It's a strategic alliance, a temporary truce in the grand game of political chess. They're even endorsing the local MP for governor, ensuring continuity within their party. Because if the opposition takes over, heaven forbid, those "gains" might be reversed. The horror!

The whole visit was a masterclass in political theatre, a blend of development announcements and unity messaging. They're whipping up support, reminding everyone that national leadership is a numbers game. And if you're not aligned, well, you might as well be singing some sad tune by Ella Mai at a deserted stadium. They're making sure the Mt Kenya vote is in the bag for Ruto, using the same old tactics of promises and veiled threats. Don't be fooled by the shiny new roads; it's the same old political machinery at work.
