Zakayo is at it again, dangling a carrot that won't even be ripe until July 2026. President Ruto is out here promising prison wardens a third phase of salary increments, but the catch is you have to survive the current economy for another two years to see a single cent of it. It is the classic Nairobi hustle where you are told the check is in the mail while the roof above your head is literally rotting away.

Speaking of roofs, the big man claims he will build 28,000 houses for prison and probation officers. We have heard these affordable housing songs before, yet the actual data shows the number of police and APs dropped from 108,013 to 104,080 in just one year. It is hard to swallow a recruitment and housing fairy tale when the boots on the ground are disappearing faster than a politician's conscience after an election.

While the government dreams of 2026, the current reality is a mess of manual record-keeping and dusty files. Relying on paper in 2024 is a joke, and much like how [Major League Baseball Talent Valuations Shift as ZiPS Projections Highlight 2026 Performance Volatility](/posts/major-league-baseball-talent-valuations-shift-as-zips-projections-highlight-2026-performance-volatility), trying to predict if these prison reforms will actually be worth anything by 2026 is a fool's errand. When your data is scattered across overburdened offices, it is no wonder officers like Wambua are getting caught for aiding escapes while the system sleeps.

The directive to release inmates with less than three months to go is just a desperate attempt to decongest cells that are probably older than the republic. They call it reform, but we know it is just a lack of space because the system cannot manage what it already has. Between Kipkurui and Kaikai getting fired for neglect and the systemic failure of manual filing, the Kenya Prisons Service looks more like a sieve than a correctional facility.

In Nairobi, we know that ground things are different. You can promise the moon in 2026, but the wardens are still living in shacks today. It is all optics and task force recommendations that look good on a PDF but mean absolutely nothing to the officer who hasn't seen a pay raise reflect in his bank account while the cost of unga keeps climbing. Don't hold your breath for 2026; in this city, even tomorrow is not guaranteed.
