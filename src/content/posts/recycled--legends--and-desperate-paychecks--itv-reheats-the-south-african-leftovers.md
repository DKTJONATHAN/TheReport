ITV is at it again, digging through the bargain bin of former reality stars to populate another season of "I’m A Celebrity... South Africa." Apparently, the debut in 2023 was "successful" enough to justify dragging another group of "memorable" campmates away from their dwindling Instagram endorsement deals. Let us be honest: if these people were actually legends, they would not be agreeing to eat kangaroo appendages for a second time just to stay relevant. It is a tired format for a tired group of celebrities who have run out of options.

Ant and Dec are returning as hosts because, let us face it, they are the only ones left who can pretend this is exciting. They are promising "epic" and "extreme" trials that are supposedly on a whole new level. It is the same old script wrapped in a new package, designed to make us believe that watching a former soap star scream at a spider is high-stakes drama. The stakes are only high for their bank accounts, and the only "legendary" thing about this is the audacity of the PR team.

The show is leaning heavily on the South African landscape to provide some much-needed gravitas. ITV is selling us these "spectacular locations" as if we have not seen this tourism board brochure a thousand times. It is a classic move: use a beautiful backdrop to distract the audience from a mediocre, recycled production. We have seen this play before, where [A South African Win and a President’s Photo-Op: The Green Grass of Karen Hides the Brown Reality](/posts/a-south-african-win-and-a-president-s-photo-op--the-green-grass-of-karen-hides-the-brown-reality) showed us exactly how the elite use scenery to hide the brown reality of their failures and lack of originality.

This time, they are letting the public decide the "ultimate legend" in a live 2026 grand final broadcast from London. Because nothing says "surviving the wilderness" like a studio in London with air conditioning and a luxury green room. It is a blatant cash-grab for votes and engagement, ensuring that the "shocks and showdowns" are curated just enough to keep the sponsors happy while the viewers think they actually have power over the outcome.

To make matters worse, we are getting a vodcast hosted by Sam Thompson and Kemi Rodgers. Because if there is one thing the world needs, it is more "unpacked" content where people over-analyze scripted camp drama for Spotify and YouTube. It is a relentless cycle of noise designed to keep you trapped in the ITV ecosystem. If this is where "legends" are made, then the bar for legendary status has officially hit the floor. Expect more of the same performative struggle and manufactured tears.
