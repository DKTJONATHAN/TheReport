So, the internet is abuzz because someone finally managed to take a "good" iPhone photo of the moon. The supposed breakthrough, shared on Reddit, involves a convoluted video recording trick to gain marginally better control over focus, exposure, and zoom. This allows the average user to transform the moon from a "bright detail on a completely dark background" that appears "about half the width of one fingernail on an outstretched hand" into something slightly less pathetic. Groundbreaking stuff, truly.

Meanwhile, on a more elevated plane - literally - Artemis II astronauts are apparently also taking moon photos with their iPhones. Their secret weapon? They've traveled all the way to the moon. And to really nail the shot, they've gone to the extreme measure of switching off the spaceship's lights. Because apparently, even celestial bodies require a specific, incredibly difficult-to-replicate lighting environment.

This entire narrative is, of course, a masterclass in misdirection. While amateur photographers are being fed crumbs of "clever hacks" for their pocket-sized devices, the real story is that the only way to truly capture a decent moon photo with an iPhone is to be in orbit around it, or even better, on its surface. The fact that these astronauts are carrying iPhones on a multi-billion dollar lunar mission, and that this is being presented as some sort of relatable photography tip, is less about technological advancement and more about the pervasive marketing push of the tech industry, even reaching into the void of space.

So, good luck copying the astronaut's "trick." Unless you have access to a NASA budget and a spacecraft, you'll be sticking with the fingernail-sized moon and the video recording workaround. It's a stark reminder that while technology may advance, the fundamental barriers of physics and economics remain stubbornly in place for the rest of us. The moon will remain a distant, difficult subject for your phone, while astronauts have the ultimate advantage: proximity. And perhaps, a slightly better lighting setup.
