Just when you thought the theatre of the absurd had reached its final act, Nuru Okanga decides to remind us that in Kenya, politics is just show business for people who cannot sing. Our favorite professional mourner-turned-activist has taken to Facebook to announce a new political party where he is supposedly the Secretary-General. Apparently, shaving his head and wailing at the Nation Centre was not enough to secure a permanent spot in the limelight, so now he is tagging along with Edwin Sifuna and Babu Owino to "save" the youth. It is giving clout-chasing at a level even our local socialites would find exhausting.

The new faction, operating under the "Sisi ni Sifuna" banner, is basically the political version of a messy breakup. After Sifuna was kicked out as ODM Secretary-General in a move that had more drama than a Nollywood script, he decided to start his own club. It is hard to take the Linda Mwananchi tour seriously when the main characters are more famous for their social media antics than their policy papers. They claim to be channeling youth energy, but it looks more like they are channeling a desperate need to stay relevant in a landscape that is moving on without them.

We all remember the performance Okanga gave after Raila’s passing, where he turned grief into a spectator sport with his wife Melab. This new party launch, scheduled for July or August, is just the next episode in [The Post-Raila Vacuum: 5 Leaders Fighting for the 5 Million 'Baba' Votes](/posts/the-post-raila-vacuum-5-leaders-fighting-for-the-5-million-baba-votes) saga that has left everyone wondering who will inherit the crown. While they accuse the current ODM leadership of betraying the legacy, one has to wonder if they are more upset about the elite deals or the fact that they were not invited to the table to sign them.

Babu Owino is out here calling the ODM National Executive Committee illegal, while Sifuna acts shocked that he was ousted without a formal hearing. Please, darlings, this is Kenyan politics, not a corporate HR meeting. If you are not in the inner circle, you are on the menu. The irony of these men complaining about suppressing dissent while they try to build a cult of personality around their own names is almost too delicious to ignore. It is the same old script with a Gen Z filter applied to make it look fresh for the 2027 general elections.

At the end of the day, whether it is the Linda Mwananchi rallies in Kakamega or the tear-gas-filled photo ops, the goal remains the same: staying in the headlines. They are betting on the generational frustration of voters who are tired of the old guard, but they forget that voters can smell a manufactured movement from a mile away. If Okanga thinks being a Secretary-General is the same as being a professional grassroots mobiliser, he is in for a rude awakening. But hey, at least it gives us something to talk about while we wait for the next real scandal to drop.
