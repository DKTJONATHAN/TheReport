We love to brag in this country. We beat our chests about being the world's largest exporter of black tea while the people actually picking the leaves can barely afford a loaf of bread. But look at the map, my friend. Our "top ten" customers look like a list of places you would only go if you had a death wish or a very expensive mercenary contract. Pakistan, Egypt, Yemen, Iran-it is not a market, it is a maritime risk assessment. We are tied to the hip of countries that are either bankrupt or busy dodging missiles.

Iran has already shown us the door after some "misappropriated" currency and a license row that left our shipments gathering dust. While the big bosses in Nairobi try to negotiate, the smallholder farmer is left holding a bag of leaves that nobody wants to buy. It is the same old story of corporate greed and government incompetence. Even as [Sasini Sells Kiambu Estate for Sh7.9bn as NSE Stocks Rise](/posts/sasini-sells-kiambu-estate-for-sh7-9bn-as-nse-stocks-rise), the average mwananchi is being told to wait for a "market reopening" that might never happen because of some drone strike in Tehran.

Then you have the Red Sea. The Houthis are out there playing target practice with cargo ships, forcing vessels to take the long way around the Cape of Good Hope. You think that extra month at sea is free? It is not. The insurance companies are eating the margins, the shipping lines are padding their pockets, and the Kenyan farmer is getting hit with the bill. Tea is light, sure, but the arithmetic of war is heavy. By the time that tea reaches a shelf in Dubai, it has cost more in freight than it is worth in flavor.

Our biggest buyer, Pakistan, is now exchanging artillery fire with Afghanistan. Their economy was already on life support, and now they are diverting cash to a border war. If they stop buying, or if their currency collapses any further, the auction floors in Mombasa will turn into a graveyard. We are also banned in Sudan because our leaders decided to play diplomat with the wrong rebels. It is a masterclass in how to ruin a golden goose by letting it fly directly into a war zone.

Diversification is just a fancy word the "suits" use to keep us from panicking. The reality is that we are stuck. When your entire economy relies on selling a luxury leaf to people who are currently being bombed, you are not a "global leader"-you are a hostage to fortune. Enjoy your tea while you can, because at three dollars a kilogram, the only thing getting richer is the dirt the tea grows in.
