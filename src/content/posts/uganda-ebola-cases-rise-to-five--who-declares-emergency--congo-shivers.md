So, Uganda's got another three cases of Ebola, bringing their pathetic little outbreak to a grand total of five. Just enough to make everyone jump. The health ministry, in its infinite wisdom, is scrambling to trace contacts. Because, you know, that always works out perfectly when people are running for their lives.

And the World Health Organization? They've gone and declared it a "public health emergency of international concern." Groundbreaking. Meanwhile, the real action is happening next door in the DRC, where they’ve got nearly 750 suspected cases and 177 suspected deaths. But hey, at least Uganda's only at five. Small mercies, right?

The WHO blames late detection, no vaccines, rampant violence, and people just being too mobile. In other words, the usual recipe for disaster in that region. It’s almost like they *want* things to go south. This latest wave in Uganda includes a driver and a health worker, predictably. Because who else would be dumb enough to get close to the infected? One woman, a Congolese visitor, even managed to travel from near the border all the way to Kampala before finally deciding to seek care. A real globetrotter, that one.

The follow-up that caught her? A tip-off from a pilot. A pilot. Imagine that. People actually talking to each other. Still, don't expect any miracles.
