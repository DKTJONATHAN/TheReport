Former RBI Governor Duvvuri Subbarao’s warning about hardening inflation expectations suggests that the rot is already sinking into the public psyche. When supply shocks morph into demand shocks, the central bank loses its ability to steer the ship with mere interest rate tweaks. India is discovering that being the fastest-growing major economy means very little when you are a hostage to the Strait of Hormuz. Importing 85 percent of your fuel needs is not a strategy; it is a vulnerability that the Iran war has ruthlessly exposed.

The international community should view the RBI’s downward revision of GDP growth as the first crack in a much larger dam. India’s reliance on the Middle East for 50 percent of its crude and nearly all of its liquefied petroleum gas creates a single point of failure that can paralyze global supply chains. As the rupee crawls toward record lows against the dollar, the widening trade deficit will force New Delhi to burn through foreign exchange reserves just to keep the lights on.

Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s recent plea for citizens to stop buying gold and traveling abroad is the ultimate admission of fragility. This is not the behavior of a confident superpower; it is the behavior of a nation terrified of a balance-of-payments crisis. When a government asks its population to curb fuel use and pause traditional gold purchases to save foreign currency, the "shining" facade has officially been replaced by a grim survivalist reality.

The global fallout will be felt far beyond South Asia. If India’s domestic demand craters due to persistent core inflation and energy-driven austerity, the global economy loses its primary counterweight to the stagnation seen in the West. Much like the political pawns discussed in the Istanbul Mayor Imamoglu corruption trial (/posts/istanbul-mayor-imamoglu-s-corruption-trial--another-political-pawn-on-the-board), economic figures are being manipulated to serve a narrative that no longer matches the street-level reality of rising costs and shrinking options.
