MARAGA'S STATE FAILURE WARNING AS KIKUYU CLASHES AND SOUTHERN BYPASS CHAOS ESCALATE

Former Chief Justice David Maraga is sounding the alarm, warning that Kenya is teetering on the brink of state failure due to an alarming rise in armed militias and outright political violence. He’s urging citizens to get off their butts and vote, because apparently, the ballot box is the last stop before Kamiti.

This isn't just noise. Maraga's talking about organized attacks on innocent folks, with the security forces apparently more interested in their tea breaks than actual intervention. He's pointed fingers at the Southern Bypass, Kikuyu, Kitale, and Kisumu - places where the only thing organized seems to be the chaos. It’s the same old story, really. Remember when we thought things were bad with that whole Bradley Marongo mess? [FROM STREET HERO TO APARTMENT PUPPET: THE SHORT REIGN OF BRADLEY MARONGO](/posts/from-street-hero-to-apartment-puppet--the-short-reign-of-bradley-marongo), this feels like déjà vu, but with more actual bullets.

And who's stirring the pot this time? None other than Rigathi Gachagua, making a grand entrance into Kikuyu Town like a rock star, despite the police trying their best to be party poopers. Teargas flew, stones were hurled, and the usual Kenyan drama unfolded. Gachagua’s crying foul, claiming the government is out to get him. Honestly, who has the time for such theatrics when the country’s apparently going south?

Meanwhile, Kimani Ichung’wah is pointing fingers straight at Gachagua, calling it all a coordinated attack. Interior CS Kipchumba Murkomen is condemning the “political thuggery” and begging the courts to show some backbone. It’s a classic Kenyan blame game, each side spinning their own version of reality while the rest of us dodge flying debris.

The whole situation along the Southern Bypass, with bonfires and gangs harassing motorists, just adds to the circus. While Maraga preaches civic duty, the politicians are busy trading insults and accusations. The police are out in force, keeping a lid on things in Kikuyu, but you can bet the tensions are still simmering. It’s a wonder we haven’t completely imploded yet.

Ultimately, Maraga’s warning is less a prophecy and more a statement of the bleeding obvious. We’re a nation that thrives on drama, and when the politicians run out of new scandals, they invent new riots. The question is, will anyone actually listen, or will we just keep on this merry dance towards whatever state failure looks like from the inside?







Former Chief Justice David Maraga has thrown a grenade into the political arena, warning that Kenya is hurtling towards state failure. His diagnosis? A surging tide of armed militias and enough political violence to make a seasoned gangster blush. Maraga’s solution? A desperate plea for citizens to register and vote, as if the ballot box is suddenly going to sprout legs and march this administration straight to Kamiti.

The former top judge isn't mincing words. He’s talking about organized attacks on civilians, met with what he calls the security forces’ “lukewarm” response. We’re seeing this rot manifesting in places like Nairobi's Southern Bypass, Kikuyu Town, Kitale, and Kisumu. Armed thugs are roaming free, and the police are apparently too busy counting their shillings to intervene effectively. This whole mess is starting to feel like one of those botched [The Airbnb Chronicles: When ‘Clout’ Becomes the New Colonization](/posts/the-airbnb-chronicles--when--clout--becomes-the-new-colonization) schemes, but with far deadlier consequences.

And who’s the star of this particular show of chaos? None other than former Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua, who apparently made a defiant, tear-gas-dodging entrance into Kikuyu Town. He’s there with opposition figures, stirring up crowds while clashes erupt. Gachagua’s quick to blame the government for orchestrating the violence to keep him from his adoring fans. It’s the oldest trick in the book, and frankly, quite exhausting.

Meanwhile, Kikuyu MP Kimani Ichung’wah is playing his part, calling for investigations and pointing the finger squarely at Gachagua. Interior CS Kipchumba Murkomen is adding his voice to the chorus of condemnation, decrying the “political thuggery” and begging the Judiciary to finally do something impactful, instead of their usual song and dance.

The chaos isn’t confined to Kikuyu. Earlier, the Southern Bypass was a scene straight out of a disaster movie, with gangs blocking roads and terrorizing motorists. It’s a perfect illustration of Maraga’s warning: when the state’s response is weak, the void is filled by the most ruthless. The political posturing and accusations are flying thick and fast, with Gachagua and Ichung’wah locked in a bitter feud.

Maraga’s warning isn't just about militias; it’s about the erosion of public trust. When the institutions meant to protect us are perceived as weak or complicit, people start to look for their own solutions, and that's a dangerous path. The question remains: will this be another dire warning ignored, or will the citizens finally rally and demand accountability, before we’re too far gone to even recognize the country we once knew?
