So, Collins "Voke" Ochieng and Timothy Mgowe decided to take their violent roadshow from the quiet shops of Bondo to the humid streets of Mombasa. At 22 and 23 years old, these boys should be worrying about entry-level salaries or how to pay off a HELB loan. Instead, they were allegedly busy firing multiple rounds in broad daylight and abducting businessmen in Kisauni. It is the kind of career path that starts with social media fame for all the wrong reasons and ends in a Bamburi Fisheries apartment with a loaded AK-47 under the bed.

The DCI is patting itself on the back for linking the duo to the Bondo CCTV footage that went viral earlier this month. We all saw those videos. Masked men, heavy artillery, and the kind of brazen confidence that only comes when you know the police are usually ten steps behind. While the detectives talk about "painstaking investigations," the rest of us are left wondering how two youths manage to ferry a military-grade rifle across half the country without anyone noticing. It seems our security checkpoints are more interested in petty bribes than checking for magazines and ammunition.

It is the same old story of [Nyayo Mud and Misery: Gor Mahia’s Easy Walk Over Nairobi’s Broken Dreams](/posts/nyayo-mud-and-misery--gor-mahia-s-easy-walk-over-nairobi-s-broken-dreams) where the system fails the youth and the youth return the favor by terrorizing the tax-paying public. In Kisauni, they reportedly snatched a businessman, took his valuables, and dumped him at Mombasa Cement like yesterday's trash. It is a cycle of violence that feels as permanent as the potholes on Jogoo Road. The recovery of ten mobile phones suggests they were building quite the inventory of stolen lives before the joint team from the Crime Research and Intelligence Bureau finally decided to gatecrash their party.

Now we are told that forensic ballistic experts are analyzing the recovered weapon. They suspect it is the same one used to cause "widespread panic" in Siaya. Of course it is. Why buy two guns when one does the job of ruining lives perfectly well? The DCI promises there is "no safe haven" for criminals, but that sounds like a tired script we hear every time a high-profile arrest is made. For every Voke they put in a cell, there are five more waiting in the wings, convinced that an AK-47 is a faster way to a lifestyle they cannot afford than honest work.

The suspects are being processed for court, where the real circus begins. We will see the usual parade of files, the mentions, and the eventual silence as the news cycle moves on to the next tragedy. Meanwhile, the people of Bondo and Mombasa are expected to sleep better tonight because two guys were caught with one gun. In a country where the vultures are always fed and the rest of us are for sale, this feels less like a victory for justice and more like a brief intermission in a very long, very violent play.
