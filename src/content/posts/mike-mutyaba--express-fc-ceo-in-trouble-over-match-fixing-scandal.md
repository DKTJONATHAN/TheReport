MIKE MUTYABA, EXPRESS FC CEO IN TROUBLE OVER MATCH-FIXING SCANDAL - WHAT A SHOCKER!
Mike Mutyaba and the Express FC CEO are caught in a sordid match-fixing scandal. Talk about hitting rock bottom.
FUFA suspends suspects in a match-fixing scandal.
East Africa
Gossip, Football, Scandal
Football
Here we go again. Just when you thought Ugandan football couldn't sink any lower, FUFA comes trotting out with another exposé of alleged match-fixing. This time, the esteemed Mike Mutyaba, a former Cranes star and vocal critic of FUFA president Moses Magogo (oh, the irony!), finds himself in the hot seat. Apparently, his "criticism" might have been a smokescreen for something far less savory. It’s almost as juicy as the drama surrounding [KSI’s Newest Plaything: Why the Dagenham and Redbridge Buyout Smells Like a PR Stunt](/posts/ksi-s-newest-plaything--why-the-dagenham-and-redbridge-buyout-smells-like-a-pr-stunt), except this time, it’s about kicking balls, not buying clubs.

The alleged scheme involves a December 2024 clash between Kitara FC and Express FC. According to FUFA's "betting alert" from FIFA, the game was supposedly fixed for betting purposes, with the foreknowledge that Express FC would spectacularly lose and concede at least five goals. Who needs Hollywood writers when you have Ugandan football officials? The preliminary findings conveniently paint Mutyaba as the mastermind, having allegedly introduced one Ivan Katende, a repeat offender in these match-fixing whispers, to Express FC CEO Ashraf Miiro. Together, they supposedly cooked up this delightful little plot.

And the players? Well, it wouldn't be a true scandal without some on-field pawns. Goalkeeper Emmanuel Kalyowa and defender Mbazira Ashraf are allegedly the poor souls roped into "executing the fix on the field of play." One can only imagine the pep talks they received. Mutyaba, in his newfound managerial role, conveniently took charge of technical decisions while the actual coach was conveniently absent. It's a classic tale of ambition, greed, and perhaps a desperate attempt to stay relevant in a sport that’s seemingly losing its way.

FUFA is now playing the responsible adult card, handing the matter over to state authorities, specifically the Criminal Investigations Directorate. They are touting their commitment to "safeguarding the integrity of the sport." Bless their hearts. Meanwhile, suspicious betting accounts linked to Katende are said to have won millions from a Tanzanian betting company, which, in a twist of fate, decided to block the payout after detecting the fraud. So, not only did they try to fix a game, they couldn't even cash in on their ill-gotten gains. Talk about a double failure.
