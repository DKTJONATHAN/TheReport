The viral infection leaves millions with chronic pain, increased stroke risk, and lifelong nerve damage-yet vaccination rates remain dangerously low.

Ann Garner, like many others, harbored a naive perception of shingles as a "mild" ailment. Her personal encounter in 2024 shattered this illusion. Had she been privy to the stark descriptions employed by other cultures - the Norwegian "hell's fire" (_helvetesild_) or the Arabic "[belt of fire](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3885217/)" - perhaps her understanding, and subsequently her preparation, would have been more aligned with the grim reality. This widespread underestimation, coupled with insufficient preventative measures, paints a bleak picture of public health preparedness against a significant viral threat. The failure to act on readily available preventative measures, despite the clear and present dangers of chronic pain, nerve damage, and heightened stroke risk, is a testament to a profound societal apathy towards personal well-being.
