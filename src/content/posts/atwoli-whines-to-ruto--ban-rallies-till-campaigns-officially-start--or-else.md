So, Francis Atwoli, the ever-vigilant guardian of our labouring masses, has decided it's time for President Ruto to play referee for our political gladiators. He wants rallies banned until the IEBC, bless their bureaucratic hearts, officially opens the campaign period. Because, you know, people gathering and expressing their opinions before the designated season is apparently the same as unleashing a plague of locusts.

Atwoli's argument? This premature political tomfoolery is "fueling tension" and, gasp, "distracting from governance priorities." As if these guys ever prioritized anything other than their next power grab. It’s the same tired song and dance: blame the symptoms, ignore the disease. We've seen this movie before, haven't we? It's like watching [Rich Men Shouting in Suits: The Negreira Circus is Just a Power Trip](/posts/rich-men-shouting-in-suits--the-negreira-circus-is-just-a-power-trip), just with more mud-slinging and less football.

And it's not just Atwoli flapping his gums. Interior CS Kipchumba Murkomen is also on the warpath, calling on the Judiciary to throw the book at anyone caught causing trouble at these rallies. He’s practically begging the courts to "act decisively." He wants them to partner with his ministry and crack the whip on the financiers and organisers. Because, according to Murkomen, these folks are "terrorizing Kenyans." You'd think with all the actual terror happening, they'd have bigger fish to fry, but hey, what do I know? I'm just a realist.

This whole circus is just a distraction, a well-rehearsed play to keep us focused on the noise while the real decisions are made behind closed doors. We're stuck in a perpetually charged political environment, and these rallies are just the cheap theatrics. Meanwhile, actual problems like our crumbling infrastructure, as evident when you look at [Afcon 2027: Kenya's Stadiums Still a Joke, Caf Says](/posts/afcon-2027--kenya-s-stadiums-still-a-joke--caf-says), get pushed further down the priority list. It's all about keeping the masses agitated enough to vote, but not so agitated that they start asking the *real* questions.
