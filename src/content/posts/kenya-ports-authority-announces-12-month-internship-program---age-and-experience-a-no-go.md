Kenya Ports Authority Announces 12-Month Internship Program - For the Lucky Few

WARNING: Another government job opportunity with a catch. Unless you're fresh out of school and under 27, don't even bother. KPA is offering internships, but they've already set up a wall for anyone over a certain age. Classic Kenyan efficiency, right?

The KPA, bless their bureaucratic hearts, wants to "provide young adults with the opportunity to gain hands-on experience." Translation: they need cheap labour for 12 months, and they're picking and choosing from a very specific demographic. If you graduated between 2023 and 2025 and haven't managed to snag any experience since, and you're still under the ripe old age of 27, then congratulations, you're in their golden window. For everyone else, better luck next time, or maybe just go join those striking quarry workers.

The list of departments is long - Container Terminal Operations, ICT, Marine Engineering, Human Resources - all the places where real work happens, and where they can milk some fresh talent. They even warn against "canvassing," as if that's the biggest problem facing this country, not the systemic issues that lead to situations like the [South C Building Collapse: Cost of Corruption and Incompetency in Kenya Exposed](/posts/south-c-building-collapse-corruption-incompetency-kenya).

And, of course, it's strictly online. Because who has time for actual human interaction when you can streamline the rejection process? Only successful candidates will be contacted, naturally. They claim to be an equal employer, urging people with disabilities to apply. Let's see how many actually get a fair shot.

This whole thing reeks of the same old song and dance. They dangle a carrot, but the eligibility criteria are so narrow, it’s like they’re not actually looking for the best, just the youngest and most desperate. It’s a breeding ground for more of the same, where experience is a crime and age is a disqualifier.

Still, if you fit their narrow mould, go ahead and apply. Just remember to fill in those forms accurately. They're looking for *your* details, after all. Just don't expect miracles.
