Honestly, if you thought the only thing Americans were good at was over-hyping mid-tier soccer, think again. Derick Jones and Yaw Yeboah just proved that the real action isn't in the goals - it is in the gambling apps. MLS just slapped them with lifetime bans because they could not resist betting on their own games during the 2024 and 2025 seasons. It is giving "struggling influencer trying to sell fake tea," except instead of tea, they were selling out their professional souls for a quick payout on a yellow card.

The investigation found that these two geniuses actually colluded to make sure Jones got booked in a game against the Red Bulls. Imagine being so bad at your job that you have to orchestrate a foul just to see your bank account move. It is pure comedy. While everyone else is out here trying to be the next Messi, these guys were playing chess with their careers and losing badly. If they put this much effort into their actual footwork, maybe they would not be looking for work in the Chinese first tier or, even worse, nowhere at all.

Commissioner Don Garber is out here doing his best "disappointed father" routine, talking about match integrity and "enhancing education." Please. We all know the league lives on betting sponsorships, but the moment the players want a piece of the pie, it is a "threat to competition." The hypocrisy is so thick you could cut it with a dull knife. They want the gambling revenue, but they want the players to act like saints. It is just another day in the soccer circus where the clowns are being punished for actually performing.

Now, Yaw Yeboah has scampered off to China, probably hoping nobody there reads the Western tabloids. Meanwhile, Derick Jones is sitting around without a club, probably realizing that life as a disgraced athlete is a special kind of bleak. It is starting to look like his career is headed toward a dead end, much like how [Ruai is the New Human Warehouse and Nobody is Surprised](/posts/ruai-is-the-new-human-warehouse-and-nobody-is-surprised) when things get shoved out of sight and forgotten. One day you are a pro, the next you are a cautionary tale for the next generation of greedy benchwarmers.
