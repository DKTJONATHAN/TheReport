So, the President's been busy appointing people. Rita Kavashe is the new Chancellor at Pwani University, Paul Russo at Kibabii, and Kevit Desai at Kabianga. Five years each. Just enough time to settle in, enjoy the perks, and then what? Nothing changes, really. It's like rearranging deck chairs on the Titanic, only this ship is slowly sinking under a mountain of debt and bad decisions.

Then we have Elias Ngugi Mwenda and Lily Monica Nafula, appointed to handle corruption cases. Bless their hearts. They’ll be drowning in paperwork before they even get started, while the real big fish continue to swim in their oil slicks. And the High Courts in Nyahururu will now "supervise" Rumuruti and Maralal Magistrate Courts. More bureaucracy, more layers of pointless oversight. It’s enough to make you want to scream.

Meanwhile, Geoffrey K. Maritim is the new Non-Executive Chairperson of the National Employment Authority. Because what we really need is another committee to discuss unemployment while people starve. Naiyanoi Ntutu and Mwangi J. Nderitu are joining the boards of ICT and Communications authorities respectively. Exciting stuff. Hopefully, they can figure out why our internet is slower than a donkey cart.

And the women’s fund gets a re-appointment for Yvonne Nalokitoo Tonkei, plus two new faces: Wesley Kipchumba Keitany and Ebla Haji Aden. At least someone's getting funded. We also have Beatrice Mwarania Mburugu and Saphina Chelagat looking into Female Genital Mutilation. Good. But let's not forget the land acquisitions for that ever-expanding SGR. More land gone, more people displaced, all in the name of "development." It’s a familiar tune, and it usually ends badly for the common man. You know, like when the Middle East Mayhem Means Kenya's Economy is Toast.

The Kabete National Polytechnic gets a new chairperson and a re-appointment, while Kipsoen National Polytechnic gets a new member. The Special Economic Zones Authority also has a re-appointment. It's a constant churn of faces, but the system remains. The Lake Basin Development Authority board has a whole crew of re-appointments. They'll be busy discussing… something. And the Kenyatta National Hospital board has a re-appointment too. Let's just hope they don't run out of bandages.

And as if that wasn't enough, we have KUSCCO Limited facing a winding-up petition. Sounds like the savings and credit cooperatives are going the same way as many other promising ventures in this country. Plus, properties at Kosele and Rangwe Police Stations, and some uncollected goods in Ruaka and Eldoret, are all heading for the auction block. Just another week in paradise, where the little guy loses and the deals get made behind closed doors.
