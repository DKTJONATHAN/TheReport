Harambee Starlets coach Beldine Odemba has unveiled a provisional squad of 31 players for the upcoming inaugural FIFA Women’s Series, slated to be held in Nairobi from April 11th to 15th, 2026. The tournament will see Kenya host Australia, India, and Malawi at Nyayo National Stadium, marking another opportunity for the national team to face diverse international competition. The players are expected to converge in camp on Wednesday, April 1st, 2026, a date that might raise a few eyebrows.

Notable inclusions in the expanded squad include Marion Serenge, earning her maiden senior call-up after impressive performances with St. Noa FC in Uganda, where she has netted six goals in five outings. Terry Engesha also makes a return to the national team fold following a resurgence in form with Kenya Police Bullets. Mercy Airo is another familiar face back in contention, having impressed with Ulinzi Starlets.

The tournament is positioned as a crucial part of the Harambee Starlets' preparations for the 2026 Women’s Africa Cup of Nations in Morocco. Odemba emphasized the value of facing teams with different playing styles and international experience, aiming to assess progress and identify areas for improvement. Australia enters the series as the highest-ranked nation at 15th, followed by India (67th) and Malawi (153rd), while Kenya currently sits at 134th. This gap in rankings highlights the challenge ahead, much like the disparities seen in other regional competitions.

The opening day on Saturday, April 11th, will feature two matches: Australia versus Malawi at 2:00 pm, and the Harambee Starlets taking on India at 6:00 pm. The competition concludes on Wednesday, April 15th, with the third-place play-off and the final, both scheduled for the afternoon and evening. While the Football Kenya Federation highlights the use of cookies for site enhancement and security, the real test will be whether this series translates into tangible improvements on the pitch, a challenge that has plagued Kenyan football for years, as seen in past disappointments like the CHAN 2024 exit.

The 2026 Women’s Africa Cup of Nations is scheduled to take place in Morocco from July 25th to August 16th, 2026. The FIFA Women’s Series provides a platform for the Starlets to test their mettle against varied opposition, a necessary step if they aim to make a mark in continental football. However, the perennial issue of inconsistent performance and development remains a persistent concern.

The provisional squad list includes goalkeepers Lilian Awuor (Farul Constanta, Romania), Annedy Kundu (Kenya Police Bullets), and Lilian Adhiambo (Trinity Starlets). Defenders named are Ruth Ingosi (Simba Queens), Norah Ann (Kenya Police Bullets), Elizabeth Ochaka (Kenya Police Bullets), Lorine Ilavonga (Ulinzi Starlets), Euphrasier Shilwatso (Kayole Starlet), Vivian Nasaka (Hakkarigucuspor, Turkey), Janet Mumo (Kibera Soccer Women), Elizabeth Muteshi (Trinity Starlets), Alice Mideri (Vihiga Queens), Leah Andiema (Kenya Police Bullets), and Enez Mango (Farul Constanta, Romania). In midfield, the selected players are Mwanalima Adam (HB Koge, Denmark), Fasila Adhiambo (Simba Queens), Martha Amunyolete (Vihiga Queens), Mercy Airo (Ulinzi Starlets), Airin Madalina (Vihiga Queens), Vidah Sharon (Kenya Police Bullets), Terry Engesha (Kenya Police Bullets), and Lorna Nyarinda (Kenya Police Bullets). Forwards named are Elizabeth Mideva (Ulinzi Starlets), Shaline Nambengele (Ulinzi Starlets), Eglay Mukhwana (Trinity Starlets), Shirleen Opisa (Amus College, Uganda), Marion Serenge (St. Noah, Uganda), Elizabeth Wambui (Simba Queens), Valerie Nekesa (Soccer Assassins), Linda Kihara (Kayole Starlet), and Jentrix Shikangwa (Simba Queens).
