NDC: Gbajabiamila Out! Fake Agency, Appointment Sales, The Usual Nigerian Dance.
The opposition NDC is screaming for the Chief of Staff's head over claims he orchestrated a scam with a phantom agency, allegedly selling public appointments. Just another Tuesday in Naija.
NDC claims the Chief of Staff was involved in a fake agency that siphoned public funds and sold jobs.
Politics, Scandals, Nigeria
Gbajabiamila
The Nigeria Democratic Congress (NDC) is in a predictable frenzy, demanding the immediate removal of President Tinubu's Chief of Staff, Femi Gbajabiamila. The allegations are juicy, involving a non-existent Presidential Foreign Intervention Promotion Council (PFIPC) and the alleged sale of public appointments. Apparently, this phantom agency was used to "siphon public funds" with Gbajabiamila's supposedly active collusion. It’s the same old tune, just a different orchestra playing it.

The NDC, through its mouthpiece Osa Director, is flabbergasted that an agency the Presidency claims doesn't exist could open multiple high-level bank accounts at the apex bank. For a domiciliary account, you need more paperwork than a visa application to heaven. Yet, this "fictitious agency" apparently sailed through with flying colours. It makes you wonder if the Accountant-General's office is run by amateurs or willing participants in this grand theatrical production of corruption. The Presidency owes Nigerians an explanation, but let's be real, they owe us a lot more.

Then come the truly eye-watering claims: Gbajabiamila allegedly demanded a hefty 48% of the agency’s "take off grant" - a sum that would make your eyes water. And the Director-General of this ghost agency, Prince Mathew Adeniyi Adeyemi, allegedly bought his position for N600 million through the Chief of Staff. N400 million allegedly paid, with N200 million still dangling. It’s like a twisted version of a reality TV show, but with actual public funds and real consequences for the rest of us. The NDC's statement drips with the sentiment that the current administration has "entrenched and legitimised corruption as a way of governance in Nigeria." Frankly, it's hard to argue with that. We've seen this play before, remember when [Samsung Abandons TriFold: Predictable Retreat from Costly Tech Experiment](/posts/samsung-abandons-trifold--predictable-retreat-from-costly-tech-experiment)? Some experiments just prove too expensive to sustain.

Adding a dash of intrigue, the NDC points to the suspicious death of an alleged intermediary between Adeyemi and Gbajabiamila, Mr Babatunde Tanimola, who died in a hotel fire the day after the Chief of Staff filed a police petition. Adeyemi himself claims to have survived assassination attempts. It’s all very dramatic, like something out of a Nollywood thriller, but with a Nigerian price tag. The NDC’s demands are standard: Gbajabiamila out, an independent investigation, and for the EFCC, ICPC, and police to get involved. They want the "culture of impunity and the brazen sale of public appointments" to end. A noble wish, but in this theatre of the absurd, the show, unfortunately, goes on.
