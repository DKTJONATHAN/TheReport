Oh look, another year, another reason for artists to pretend they are working while sipping artisanal coffee in the middle of nowhere. One Groove has finally blessed us with the line-up for the 2026 AmaGroove Residency at the Kromdraai Impact and Co-Creativity Hub. From March 22 to 29, international artists and local favorites will gather to "co-create." In industry speak, that usually means three days of nursing hangovers and four days of trying to find a charger that works in the South African bush.

The funding for this little retreat is where things get truly interesting. We have the usual suspects like the Riky Rick Foundation and Msaki’s ALTBLK, but then you see the Embassy of Denmark. It is hilarious how these "cultural exchanges" always seem to require a European stamp of approval to feel legitimate. It reminds me of the broader trend of [shadow capital and stadiums: how foreign-funded advocacy shapes the american sports landscape](/posts/shadow-capital-and-stadiums--how-foreign-funded-advocacy-shapes-the-american-sports-landscape) because, let us be honest, who is really pulling the strings behind these "fearless" creative hubs?

The schedule is packed with writing sessions and the ever-essential "wellness programmes." Because nothing says cutting-edge music production like a group of people who haven’t had a hit in three years doing downward dog together. They claim this fosters "artistic growth," but we all know it is just a way to pad the grant reports so the curators can keep their jobs. If masterclasses actually worked, we would not be stuck listening to the same three chord progressions on every radio station.

Curators Msaki, Muneyi, and Mishy Kope are the ones babysitting this group of fearless and curious creatives. I suppose someone has to make sure the innovation actually results in a song and not just a very expensive Instagram photo dump. They are promising a listening session at Flame Studios in Braamfontein on March 27, but do not get too excited. It is open to a "limited public audience," which is just a polite way of saying industry gatekeepers and their favorite sycophants.

One Groove calls this group diverse and collaborative, but I call it a very expensive networking event disguised as a revolution. While they explore diverse musical styles in the safety of a hub, the rest of the industry is actually out here trying to survive the real world. We will see if the 2026 cohort produces anything that lasts longer than the flight back to Copenhagen, or if this residency is just another echo chamber for the elite.
