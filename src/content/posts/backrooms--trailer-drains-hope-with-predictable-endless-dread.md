So, the internet’s latest obsession, this "Backrooms" phenomenon, has now spawned a feature film. Apparently, a creepypasta, a web series, and now a movie - all built around the concept of endless, nonsensical rooms. The appeal is clear: a potent cocktail of sickening dread and an almost pathological fascination with the absurd. It’s the modern equivalent of staring into an abyss, except this abyss is wallpapered in a sickly yellow.

This new trailer, unlike the previous teaser, actually bothers to show us its cast. We’ve got Chiwetel Ejiofor, Renate Reinsve, Mark Duplass, Finn Bennett, and Lukita Maxwell - a collection of talent seemingly tasked with looking perpetually unnerved in a maze. The script, penned by Will Soodik, offers a synopsis that’s as enlightening as a blank wall: "A strange doorway appears in the basement of a furniture showroom." Groundbreaking.

Ejiofor’s character, bless his heart, stumbles into this bizarre subterranean realm beneath "Cap’n Clark’s Ottoman Empire." Naturally, he can’t resist the urge to explore the monotonous, yellow-hued corridors, despite every primal instinct screaming at him to turn back. It’s the classic "man enters forbidden place and can’t stop himself" trope, wheeled out yet again for our supposed edification.

The article suggests that the film might be better if it never explains who built these rooms or why. This isn’t a profound insight; it’s a pragmatic acknowledgment that the appeal of "The Backrooms" lies precisely in its lack of logic. The mystery is the entire point, the hook designed to keep people engaged with the sheer, unadulterated pointlessness of it all. Expect more dread, more confusion, and likely, more yellow.

"Backrooms" is set to release on May 29th, promising audiences another dose of existential ennui wrapped in a horror flick package. Whether it offers anything beyond a fleeting sense of unease remains to be seen, but given its origins, the odds are not in favor of profound revelation.
