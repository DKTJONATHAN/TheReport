Honey, if you thought the drama in the influencer circles was peaking, you clearly haven't been watching the main stage at the Ministry of Interior. Brian Kagoro, the man who basically carries the Open Society Foundations on his back, just got the unceremonious "thank you, next" from the Kenyan government. On January 27, over twenty human rights groups, including the Law Society of Kenya, started screaming from the rooftops because Mr. Kagoro was deported without so much as a goodbye brunch. It is giving very much "scared ex-boyfriend" energy as the state prepares for the 2027 General Elections by clearing out anyone who knows how to read a budget or a constitution.

The Police Reforms Working Group is absolutely livid, claiming this is the start of criminalizing "civic funding." Translation for those of you who don't speak NGO-speak: the government is tired of people having the money to tell them they are doing a bad job. They are citing Article 36 and 27 like they are the holy scriptures, reminding everyone that seeking foreign funding is actually legal. But let’s be real, when has the law ever stopped a good plot twist? It is [Same Script, Different Date: Nairobi’s Annual Near-Miss with Disaster](/posts/same-script--different-date--nairobi-s-annual-near-miss-with-disaster) as the authorities recycle the same old "foreign interference" narrative to distract us from the fact that life is getting too expensive for even the middle class to afford their oat milk lattes.

The hypocrisy is truly the best part of this production. Our dear President spends half his life in international forums talking about democracy and human dignity, but back home, his administration is acting like a jealous gatekeeper. They are using immigration powers to punish anyone who shows a bit of "cross-border solidarity." If you are an activist or a researcher right now, you might want to check if your curtains are closed because the surveillance levels are reaching "obsessive stalker" status. They want to frame legitimate advocacy as a threat to national security, which is the ultimate gaslighting move.

The Law Society and their friends are calling for "fair, evidence-based legal processes," but since when does the executive branch care about due process when they can just use summary action? They want Brian Kagoro out because he knows too much and does too much. It is a dangerous precedent, but in this city, we are used to seeing the powerful bypass the courts whenever the truth gets a little too loud. By framing peaceful protests and youth-led organizing as some kind of foreign plot, the government is just trying to deflect from the socio-economic grievances that are actually making people angry.

So, while we wait for the next episode of "Who Can We Deport Next?", remember that a well-resourced civil society is apparently the biggest villain in the government’s eyes. They want us to believe that anyone with an international connection is a spy, while they continue to ignore the real issues. Stay tuned, because if this is how they are starting the year, the 2027 election season is going to be more toxic than a reality show reunion. Grab your popcorn, because the legal battles are just getting started and the PR teams are working overtime.
