Ruai used to be the place you went to buy a "ka-plot" and dream of a retirement home, but apparently, it has upgraded to a transit lounge for human misery. On Wednesday, detectives from the Transnational Organised Crime Unit decided to actually earn their salary by raiding a residential property where 70 foreign nationals were being packed like sardines. We are talking about 66 Ethiopians and four Eritreans waiting for god-knows-what in some dusty corner of Nairobi.

Of course, the police "busted" a syndicate, but do not hold your breath for the big fish. They caught one Kenyan man at the scene who is now "assisting with investigations." In Nairobi terms, that usually means he is the sacrificial lamb while the masterminds-the ones with the tinted Prados and deep pockets-are already setting up a new "office" in another suburb. This city does not sleep; it just finds new ways to trade in desperation.

It gets worse when you realize this is not just about local thugs. National Assembly Majority Leader Kimani Ichung’wah recently spilled the beans on how rogue state officials from immigration and the DCI are basically the travel agents for these syndicates. While the government brags about [East Africa's Economic Transformation: 5.9% Growth Surge Drives Regional Leadership in 2025](/posts/nairobi-infrastructure), the real growth seems to be in the business of selling people to fight in the Russia-Ukraine war. The economy is surging, alright, but mostly for the people trading lives for dollars.

These traffickers are not just operating in the shadows; they are part of the machinery. Ichung’wah mentioned collusion involving the National Employment Authority and even foreign missions. It makes you wonder how many of these 70 victims were actually "rescued" and how many are just being moved from one holding cell to another while the paperwork for their next destination is forged by a bored clerk at Nyayo House.

The US Department of State says we are Tier 2, which is just fancy diplomatic talk for "you are trying, but you are still failing." South Africa and Brazil got downgraded, and we are supposed to feel better because we did not? Give me a break. As long as a human being can be stored in a house in Ruai like bags of cement, the only thing we are leading in is the export of misery. Welcome to the real Nairobi, where everything, and everyone, has a price tag.
