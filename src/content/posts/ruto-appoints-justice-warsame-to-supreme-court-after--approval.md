Ruto's Latest Judge Appointment: Another Seat Filled on the Supreme Court Treadmill

So, President Ruto's decided to give Justice Mohamed Warsame another shiny promotion, shoving him onto the Supreme Court bench. Apparently, Parliament gave him a thumbs up after all that vetting, which, let's be honest, is usually just for show. Another vacancy filled, another legal eagle ready to churn out rulings. Just another day in the Kenyan judiciary, where positions are filled and the wheel keeps turning.

The official line is that this appointment restores the court to its "full constitutional complement." Because, of course, a missing judge is like a gaping wound in the fabric of justice, or something equally dramatic. Warsame's been at the Court of Appeal since 2012, so he's got the experience, the seniority, the whole package. They say he'll "strengthen the Supreme Court," which probably means he'll be good at disagreeing with the others in a very dignified, legalistic way.

Now, they're all waiting for the "formal swearing-in," as if the real work hasn't already happened behind closed doors. He'll join the Chief Justice and the rest, ready to "shape landmark rulings." We'll see how "landmark" they actually are. Remember how climate resilience is often just another PDF for the suits to file? It’s likely this is just another bureaucratic step, more paperwork, more pronouncements that might or might not trickle down to us regular folk.

The text also throws in some random quotes about barbaric citizens killing each other and Kirinyaga benefiting from Ruto's administration. It's like they just fished them out of a hat. And then there's Rigathi Gachagua talking about a single opposition candidate, and a KWS calf rescue. Honestly, it reads like a bad news cocktail where they just dumped everything in. It’s hard to tell what’s relevant and what’s just noise to pad out the story.

This whole process, from the Judicial Service Commission interviews to the parliamentary scrutiny, is supposed to be a rigorous demonstration of meritocracy. But in Kenya, we know that "competitive" often translates to who knows who, or who played the game best. Warsame's elevation is just the latest act in this long-running drama. We’ll just have to wait and see what kind of "landmark rulings" emerge from this newly completed ensemble.
