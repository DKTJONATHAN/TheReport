So, Kenya Police FC decided to grace us with their presence and, against all odds, actually won a football match. Beat KCB FC 2-1, apparently. Head coach Nicholas Muyoti’s late resurgence continues, a phenomenon as common in Nairobi as a traffic jam on Mombasa Road. They needed this, of course, after being thoroughly embarrassed by Posta Rangers, a team that’s supposedly fighting relegation. Defence of their title? More like a slow, painful crawl towards mediocrity.

KCB, on the other hand, are in a bit of a funk. One win in five games. Sounds about right for most of the teams in this league. But Police started on the front foot, launching hopeful long balls. Bless their hearts. It worked in the 12th minute when Hernest Malonga headed it, the KCB keeper fumbled like he was trying to catch a greased pigeon, and Yves Koutiama, bless his simple task, slotted it in. The Mozzart Bet Cup champions, as they so grandly call themselves, could have doubled their lead, but Eric Zakayo hit the side netting. Typical.

Jacques Ndecket, the Congolese centre back, eventually made it two from a corner kick in the 35th minute. Just when you thought it was over, KCB managed to pull one back in added time. The second half was a dreary affair, KCB pushing for an equalizer and Police just trying to hold on. Much like Nairobi’s office market is chasing shadows while Kampala cashes in, some teams chase wins and others… well, they just exist. The score remained 2-1.

And now Police are third. Forty points. Same as Shabana, who apparently lost to Gor Mahia. KCB are still languishing in eighth. It’s all very exciting if you get paid to watch this stuff, I suppose. For the rest of us, it’s just another Tuesday, another football match, another reminder that in this city, sometimes the most you can hope for is a predictable outcome. Or at least, an outcome that doesn't involve too much unexpected effort.
