So, we have the latest cinematic offering from the digital age: "Backrooms." It’s a product of the internet, evolving from a creepypasta to a web series and now, inevitably, a feature film. The appeal, we are told, is a "sickening dread" and "undeniable fascination" with an endless, nonsensical arrangement of rooms and hallways. This isn't art; it's a consumer product designed to tap into primal anxieties, repackaged for mass consumption.

This new trailer, unlike its predecessor, deigns to show us the actors - Chiwetel Ejiofor, Renate Reinsve, Mark Duplass, Finn Bennett, and Lukita Maxwell. They are tasked with confronting this "unnerving place." The premise, as laid out by the synopsis, is as thrilling as watching paint dry: "A strange doorway appears in the basement of a furniture showroom." Yes, from the depths of "Cap’n Clark’s Ottoman Empire" emerges a labyrinth of yellow-hued rooms. Ejiofor’s character, naturally, can't resist poking around, despite the obvious lack of any good reason to do so.

The filmmakers, notably 20-year-old Kane Parsons, aka "Kane Pixels," are content with the mystery. They hope the film never explains who built these rooms or why. This isn't a bold artistic choice; it's an admission of creative bankruptcy. They've tapped into a superficial fear of the unknown and the mundane, mistaking a lack of narrative for profundity. The "frights" are not born from character or plot but from the sheer, unadulterated pointlessness of it all. It’s a cinematic void, and audiences are expected to find meaning in its emptiness. The film opens May 29, ready to exploit that hunger for cheap thrills and hollow dread.
