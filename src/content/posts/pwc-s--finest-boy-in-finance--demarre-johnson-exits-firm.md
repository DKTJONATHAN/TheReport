So much for that brief flash of glamour. Demarre Johnson, the 23-year-old PwC associate who managed to snag a spot in Interview magazine's "Finest Boys in Finance" spread, has quietly exited the firm. PwC confirmed his departure, noting he left in mid-February. Naturally, they're spinning it as unrelated to his brush with semi-fame, but the timing is, shall we say, convenient.

Johnson, a Babson College grad, seemed to revel in the attention, even acknowledging that "controversy sells." He apparently anticipated the backlash, musing that people would "clown us because we think we're pretty." And they did. The photo shoot, featuring other junior bankers from institutions like Goldman Sachs and Barclays, ignited a firestorm of commentary on social media. Wall Street insiders grumbled about the perceived arrogance and the violation of unwritten rules, particularly the one about not upstaging your superiors.

The question of whether these budding financiers sought employer approval for their modeling gig hung in the air. Goldman Sachs made it clear their media relations department was *not* involved. The other firms, predictably, offered silence. Johnson, who has maintained a visible online presence, has been uncharacteristically tight-lipped since the news of his departure broke. He previously spoke about being mindful of his social media, stating he wouldn't want an associate tarnishing the company's image with a single video. It seems, however, that a magazine spread, even one he claims was about more than just looks, was enough to seal his fate. While other participants have retreated from the spotlight, Johnson, in typical social media fashion, reposted feedback about the story, adding a few crying emojis to indicate his "viral" status. The "finest boy" is now just another casualty of a system that rewards conformity over fleeting celebrity.
