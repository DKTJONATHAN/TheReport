So, the grand old game of football in Kenya has stumbled again. This time it’s our very own Football Kenya Federation (FKF) president, Hussein Mohammed, out on allegations of financial misconduct. Apparently, the 2025 African Nations Championship (CHAN) tournament is the sweet spot where the money vanished. FIFA is watching, by the way. Yes, the big boys are taking notice. It's not exactly a surprise, is it? When there's a whiff of money, especially public money, our local football bosses seem to have a special talent for making it disappear faster than a free beer at a wedding.

Enter Donald Mariga, the former Inter Milan midfielder, now playing the role of acting president. He’s been busy confirming all this drama, assuring everyone that various bodies - EACC, IRA, PPRA, CAF, and yes, FIFA - are all “reviewing and inquiring.” Mariga’s urging them to "fast-track" things. Bless his optimistic heart. In Nairobi, "fast-track" usually means "we'll get around to it when we feel like it," especially when it involves powerful people. It’s like watching the endless saga of [Nairobi’s Favorite Game: Hunt the Whistleblower While the Thief Escapes](/posts/nairobi-s-favorite-game--hunt-the-whistleblower-while-the-thief-escapes).

Apparently, the National Executive Committee (NEC) had to act because of "serious governance, financial and constitutional violations." Big words for "money's missing, and we can't pretend we don't see it." They're even freezing accounts and reviewing contracts, which sounds proactive, but let's be real, how much evidence will actually be preserved before it conveniently "disappears" or gets “misplaced”? This whole affair is unfolding just as Kenya is supposed to be co-hosting the PAMOJA AFCON tournament. Mariga promises everything is fine, but who are we kidding?

And then there’s Mohammed, hitting back, calling the allegations "malicious" and a "campaign to tarnish his name." He’s playing the victim, claiming it’s "revenge" for his "steadfast positions." Sound familiar? It’s the classic playbook. Blame everyone else, deny everything, and hope the storm passes. He’s willing to cooperate, of course, but only with "unfounded and malicious reports" circulating. This whole spectacle is a perfect illustration of how [The Great Gazetted Recycle: Why 'Continuity' is Just Code for State Capture](/posts/the-great-gazetted-recycle--why--continuity--is-just-code-for-state-capture). Kenyan football, always offering the best entertainment.
