WAIGURU'S MOUNTAIN OF LIES: RUTO'S "BEST INTERESTS" AND THE SECOND TERM GRAB

Waiguru thinks we're daft. Claiming President Ruto has Mt. Kenya's "best interests" at heart is richer than a Kileleshwa landlord. Apparently, a few Cabinet slots and Permanent Secretary positions are enough to buy our loyalty, while the real decisions are made elsewhere. She warns against "political cold" if we don't fall in line, as if the opposition has nothing to offer but a one-way ticket to oblivion. It's the same old song and dance, trying to keep us tethered to power, even if it means selling our souls.

The notion that Mt. Kenya would "plunge itself in political cold" if not supporting the current administration is pure alarmist hogwash. Waiguru conveniently forgets that "the Mountain" isn't a monolithic entity with a single brain cell. This is about survival, plain and simple, and clinging to a sinking ship with promises of a second term for Ruto. It's a classic case of "your identity for sale while the elite hide in Runda," isn't it? We’re being told to not gamble with our votes, while they're already playing a rigged game.

Let's talk about "development promises." The Rironi-Mau Summit Road project, a 233-kilometer behemoth, is cited as progress. Meanwhile, other stalled projects are magically resurrected, and agriculture is supposedly blooming with expanded marketing channels for coffee. It’s the same old playbook: point to a few shiny projects and declare victory, while ignoring the potholes in our actual lives. The farmers might be seeing *some* fruits, but is it enough to justify mortgaging our future?

And the gall of suggesting Waiguru herself is ripe for a national position, even the DP slot, is mind-boggling. Area leaders are tripping over themselves to endorse her, as if her development record in Kirinyaga is some kind of national blueprint. Nominated senators chiming in about a woman leader on the decision-making table? It’s all performative, a carefully orchestrated move to ensure the status quo remains firmly in place, with the same old faces benefiting from the same old system.

The entire narrative is about keeping Mt. Kenya "on the decision making table." Translation: keep them placated and voting the right way. Waiguru repeats that "the people of the Mountain formed this Government and therefore we cannot leave it and start over again." This isn't about progress; it's about inertia and a deep-seated fear of losing the crumbs they’ve been handed. They’re selling us a future that looks suspiciously like the past, just with a different face at the top.

And of course, the pièce de résistance: food donations for Easter and Sh. 50,000 cash for women groups. It’s a classic distraction, a little bit of bread and circuses to soften the blow of the political maneuvering. While they’re patting themselves on the back for "empowerment programs," the real question is whether this administration has truly delivered anything substantial beyond promises and political positioning.
