So, President Ruto, in his infinite wisdom, has decided to bless us with a "raise" for the struggling masses. Up to Sh4,363.31 extra for some workers. Let's break that down, shall we? That's what, a few extra mandazi a week? A slightly less painful bus fare? Don't get too excited, this isn't exactly a ticket out of hardship, it's more like a band-aid on a gaping wound.

The agricultural sector gets a whopping 15 percent increase in basic minimum wages. And the rest of us in the urban jungle? A slightly less impressive 12 percent. It sounds good on paper, doesn't it? Especially when you hear it from the man himself at some fancy Labour Day fete. But let's be real, these increases are usually swallowed whole by the ever-rising cost of everything else. It’s the same old song and dance, just with slightly different numbers.

Remember when we thought things might change? When there was talk of more than just lip service? It's like watching [Riggy G's New Gospel: Same Wolves, Different Sheepskin](/posts/riggy-g-s-new-gospel--same-wolves--different-sheepskin) all over again. Promises are cheap, and actual relief for the common Kenyan is even cheaper. This might be enough to keep a few people from starving, but it's hardly a path to prosperity.

And while we're talking about struggling and trying to make ends meet, don't forget about the other side of the coin. The news about those suspects linked to stolen phones? That's just a small glimpse into the underbelly of this economy, where some people are clearly doing much better than the rest of us. It makes you wonder where all that extra money is *really* going, doesn't it?

So, yes, a raise. A few more shillings to juggle. Just enough to keep you tethered to the grind, hoping that one day, the numbers might actually make a difference. Until then, keep your eyes open, your wallet tight, and maybe consider renewing that Nation.Africa subscription. They're the only ones who seem to be telling it like it is, even if it's not always pretty.
