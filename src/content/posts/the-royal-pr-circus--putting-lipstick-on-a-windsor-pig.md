So, Andrew Mountbatten-Windsor finally gets his collar felt by the police, and the rest of the firm decides the best course of action is to play dress-up and cut ribbons. It is the classic Windsor playbook: when one of yours is accused of being a predator, just put on a hat and smile at a camera. They call it "keeping calm and carrying on," but in Nairobi, we call it a cover-up with better tailoring. The monarchy is rocking on its foundations, yet they expect us to be impressed that they can still find their way to a gala.

The King is out here handing out awards to higher education institutions for research on ending violence against women. The irony is so thick you could cut it with a ceremonial sword. They are making sure everyone knows these were "long-planned" engagements. Of course they were. God forbid anyone thinks the Palace can react to a crisis with anything other than a pre-printed schedule and a stiff upper lip. It is all about the optics of being busy while the basement is on fire.

Then we have Sophie, the Duchess of Edinburgh, doing the usual rounds in Somalia and Kenya. She is here under the banner of the Preventing Sexual Violence in Conflict Initiative. It is the perfect distraction: fly to the Global South to look like a saint while your own family tree is rotting from the inside. We have already seen how [The Silent Corridor: What the Epstein Files Reveal About Kenya’s Invisible Geopolitics](/posts/the-silent-corridor-what-the-epstein-files-reveal-about-kenya-s-invisible-geopolitics) hints at the kind of connections these royal visits are meant to bury under a mountain of press releases and handshakes.

Even the Queen is getting in on the act, hosting Gisèle Pelicot at Clarence House. Writing to a survivor is one thing, but using her as a prop for a photo opportunity just as the Epstein scandal reaches a boiling point is peak PR. They are using the real pain of survivors to build a fortress around their own reputation. It is a calculated move to show "support" for women while the most famous member of their family is linked to the exact opposite.

But of course, the media is foaming at the mouth over William and Kate. The golden couple is out in force, and we are supposed to believe that their "grace" means the monarchy will emerge intact. It is a fairy tale for people who do not want to look at the grime under the fingernails of the elite. As long as Kate looks good in a coat, the public is expected to forget about the police investigations and the sordid details of the Epstein files.

The monarchy does not survive on divine right anymore; it survives on the fact that people are easily distracted by shiny things. They want you to focus on the "world-leading research" and the overseas tours so you do not ask why it took so long for the law to catch up with one of their own. It is a masterclass in distraction, but for those of us watching from the outside, the smell of desperation is stronger than the scent of any royal bouquet.
