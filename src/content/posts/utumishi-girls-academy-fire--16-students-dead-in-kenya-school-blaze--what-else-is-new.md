So, another one. Sixteen young lives snuffed out in a fire at Utumishi Girls Academy in Gilgil. The authorities, bless their hearts, are “investigating” - as if this is some shocking, unprecedented event in Kenya. We've got injured students, anxious parents, and ministers trotting out condolences like they're handing out party favors. This is Kenya, people. Fires in boarding schools aren't news, they're a grim, predictable Tuesday.

The Education Minister, Julius Ogamba, was there, probably practicing his solemn face. He drones on about the fire starting on the first floor, how the dorm was “completely destroyed.” Of course it was. Fires don't gently tickle buildings, they devour them. And the response teams? They put it out by 3 AM. By then, the damage was done. Just like it always is. They'll release students to their parents today, assuming their parents are still around to collect them.

The Interior Minister, Kipchumba Murkomen, wants us to be patient and avoid speculation. Patience? For what? For the next disaster? For them to figure out it was probably arson because some disgruntled teen was fed up with the conditions, or maybe just a faulty wire in a dorm meant for twice the number of students? It’s a sad, distressing situation, says Police Commander Masoud Mwinyi. Gee, you don't say. Some students apparently ran off in shock. Probably the smart ones, realizing the adults in charge were as useless as ever.

Let's not pretend this is a bolt from the blue. Fires aren't uncommon in Kenyan boarding schools. We've had deadly incidents before, usually arson by angry pupils or, surprise, accidents. Overcrowding, unlocked windows, blocked exits - the usual suspects. It’s a recipe for disaster that’s been simmering for years. Remember the Machakos county tragedy in 2001? Or the 21 who died in central Kenya just this year? This is just the latest chapter in a long, sad story. It makes you wonder if all the talk about [THE INFINITE GRIND: HOW AI TURNS EFFICIENCY INTO A CORPORATE NOOSE](/posts/the-infinite-grind--how-ai-turns-efficiency-into-a-corporate-noose) is really addressing the fundamental inefficiencies and neglect that lead to these kinds of preventable deaths.

Parents are travelling from afar, anxious, then sad, then grateful if their child survived, even with a broken leg from jumping out a window. That's the "success" story here. While some parents are praying for their missing children, others are just relieved their kid isn't one of the 16 dead. It’s a constant cycle of fear, tragedy, and hollow promises.

So yes, we'll pray. We'll be patient. And we'll wait for the next school fire, because in Kenya, that's just how it goes. The authorities will investigate, the politicians will offer condolences, and nothing will fundamentally change. Sixteen more lives lost. Just another day in paradise.
