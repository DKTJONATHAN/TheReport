Honey, if "clout" paid the bills, Gor Mahia would be the wealthiest entity in East Africa. But alas, we live in a world where real money talks and social media "likes" just walk. Despite a supposedly thrilling 2-1 win over Mathare United this past Saturday, the green army’s pockets are looking a bit light. They managed to scrape together KSh 941,200, failing yet again to hit the KSh 1 million mark. It is giving very much "big brand, small budget" energy, and frankly, I am bored with the excuses.

This is not just a one-off embarrassment; it is a whole trend. This makes it the sixth home game in a row where K’Ogalo has failed to cross that million-shilling threshold. It seems the only time the fans actually show up with their wallets is when they want to scream at their arch-rivals AFC Leopards. Without a derby to fuel the ego, the stadium seats are looking lonelier than a rejected contestant on a dating show. Even with a modest increase from the KCB game, the math just is not mathing for the self-proclaimed kings of Kenyan football.

Honestly, watching the club struggle to turn their pitch success into actual revenue reminds me of how [Mbadi’s statistical fairytales cannot fill empty stomachs](/posts/mbadi-s-statistical-fairytales-cannot-fill-empty-stomachs) because numbers on a spreadsheet do not equal cash in the hand. The club can post all the "thank you" notes they want on social media, but at the end of the day, you cannot pay players with "good support" and digital heart emojis. If the fans are as loyal as they claim to be, then why is the gate collection looking like a secondary school fundraiser instead of a professional league payout?

On the actual grass - because I suppose we have to talk about the sport - Ebenezer Adu Assifuah and Enock Morrison did the heavy lifting. They secured the win with a late penalty that probably had the three people in the VIP section sweating. They are now nine points clear at the top of the table, which is great for the trophy cabinet but clearly does nothing for the club’s liquidity issues. It is a classic case of being "rich" in points but "broke" in spirit and gate receipts.

The Charles Akonnor-coached side is currently on a seven-match unbeaten run and looks set to bag their 22nd league title. But let us be real: if you are winning the league and your fans still would rather watch the highlights on a leaked TikTok clip than pay for a ticket, do you even have a fanbase? Gor Mahia is aiming to bounce back from a trophyless season, but they might want to look into bouncing back their bank balance first before they start acting like the untouchable giants they think they are.
