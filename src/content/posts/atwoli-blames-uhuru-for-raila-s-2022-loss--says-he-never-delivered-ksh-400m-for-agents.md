ATWOLI SHAMES UHURU OVER HIS RAILAS 2022 LOSS, CLAIMS NO AGENTS MONEY DELIVERED

Atwoli, the COTU boss, has finally spat out what many in Western Kenya have been whispering: Uhuru Kenyatta is a liability, not an ally. Apparently, the former president promised Raila Odinga cash for agents, Ksh.400 million to be exact, and then conveniently forgot. Now, Atwoli is telling the Western folks to wise up and stop clinging to Uhuru like a bad hangover. It’s a classic Nairobi hustle, really. Someone promises the moon, delivers dust, and then expects applause.

The bitter pill Atwoli is forcing down the throats of Western politicians is that Uhuru’s supposed backing in 2022 was as real as a politician’s promise. He claims Odinga was left high and dry, without the agents he desperately needed at polling stations. It’s a story as old as time: promises are made, money goes missing, and the foot soldiers are left holding the empty bag. Atwoli’s rallying cry for unity next year sounds more like a desperate plea to avoid another public humiliation.

The cracks within ODM were never really hidden, were they? Now, they've exploded into the open with Sifuna and Junet Mohamed trading blows over campaign funds. Sifuna, bless his heart, actually thanked Uhuru for “bankrolling” the campaign, admitting that most of the money came from State House. But then, in true political fashion, he turns around and accuses Junet of hypocrisy, of being the one who allegedly ate Uhuru’s cash while now calling it “dirty.” It’s a circus, and we’re all paying for the tickets.

Sifuna’s speech at that burial sounds like a scene straight out of a cheap drama. He's throwing around accusations of Junet pocketing money meant for the party, then conveniently forgetting where it came from. He’s reminding everyone about Raila’s supposed million votes in Mt. Kenya, a feat he credits to Uhuru. This entire saga is a masterclass in deflection and blame-shifting. They were all in Azimio, all benefiting from whatever Uhuru threw their way, and now they’re squabbling over the crumbs. It's like watching a bunch of hyenas fight over a carcass, conveniently forgetting who brought it to the scene in the first place. It makes you wonder if our politicians are more interested in lining their pockets than actually leading. It’s almost as bad as when the Canvas Down hack threatened schools, showing a similar level of incompetence and disregard for the public good.

Atwoli’s warning to Western Kenya politicians is a harsh dose of reality. He’s basically saying, "Don't get caught holding Uhuru's hand again." It’s a cynical take, sure, but looking at the wreckage of 2022, it’s hard to argue. This is the Nairobi way: promises are cheap, loyalty is fleeting, and the people are always the last to know the truth. The question now is, will these politicians listen, or will they stumble into the same trap next year, blinded by the same false hope?
