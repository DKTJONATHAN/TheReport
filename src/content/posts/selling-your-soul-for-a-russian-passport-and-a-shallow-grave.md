Welcome to the Kenyan dream, where "Kazi Majuu" is just a fancy term for being a human shield in a war that is not yours. While the elites in Nairobi sip tea and talk about "bottom-up" economics, over 1,000 young men have been sold a lie about security jobs paying Sh300,000. It is the classic hustle: you think you are going to Istanbul for a better life, but you end up in a freezing trench in Ukraine wondering why you ever believed a social media ad from a guy named Festus.

The numbers coming out of Parliament are just statistics to politicians like Kimani Ichung-wah, but they are nightmares for mothers in the village. Twenty-eight missing, eighty-nine on the frontline, and dozens more hospitalised with missing limbs. These boys were promised Russian citizenship, but the only thing they are getting is a one-way ticket to a morgue. It is funny how we see stories about [Why Tiny Guyana Is Currently Giving Every Superpower The Middle Finger](/posts/guyana-energy-underdog-defies-the-superpowers) while our own leaders are basically pimping out the youth to anyone with a spare tank and a rifle.

Do not act surprised that the system is rotten to the core. The intelligence report admits that staff from the Directorate of Immigration and the DCI at JKIA are in on the deal. These are the same people who look at you like a criminal for carrying an extra bottle of perfume, yet they can facilitate a whole battalion of recruits to slip through the gates for a small fee. In this country, everything is for sale, including the lives of twenty-somethings who are too desperate to see the trap until they are holding a Kalashnikov in a foreign forest.

Peter Kamau and the families at Jeevanjee Gardens are crying to Musalia Mudavadi and Alfred Mutua, but let us be real. Pleading for "diplomatic engagement" when the police are busy blocking you from marching to the Russian Embassy is a sick joke. The government is more interested in keeping its diplomatic ties clean than in bringing back the boys who were duped. They tell you to verify jobs through the Ministry of Labour, the same ministry that probably cannot even find its own files half the time.

If you are a young person in Nairobi thinking about that "logistics job" in Moscow, remember that the recruiters are likely eating your "processing fee" while you are being redirected to a training camp. The "Kazi Majuu" initiative is just another way to recycle the poor into fertilizer for foreign soil. Stick to the local hustle; at least here, when the system kills you, your mother might actually have a body to bury instead of just a "missing" report from a parliamentarian.
