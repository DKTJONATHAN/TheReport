So, the "Smiling Assassin" has decided that breaking every record in the 1500m book is getting a bit repetitive. Faith Kipyegon just smashed another world record in Eugene, and while the sports commentators are busy salivating over "sports science" and "legendary chapters," let us talk about what is actually happening. At 32, Faith is looking at the track and seeing a whole lot of sweat for a lot less money than the marathon circuit offers. That 10km debut in Monaco was not just a victory; it was a loud, expensive soft launch for her departure from the middle-distance world.

The athletics elites are already trying to sell us on the "next big thing" to fill her spikes. They are throwing names like Dorcus Ewoi and Nelly Chepchirchir at us like they are ready for the crown. It is the same old industry cycle - build them up, call them a "prodigy," and then act shocked when they cannot maintain the pace. It reminds me of the usual PR fluff we see in other industries, much like [The Cannes Mirage: Selling Dreams in France While the Local Creative Scene Rots](/posts/the-cannes-mirage--selling-dreams-in-france-while-the-local-creative-scene-rots) where the glitz and glamour are used to hide the fact that there is no real plan for what comes after the main star leaves the stage.

Faith is following the Eliud Kipchoge blueprint to a T. They want us to believe this is a natural "evolution," but it is a calculated move to secure the bag before the younger girls like Caren Jepchirchir find their second wind. Even Beatrice Chebet is sniffing around the 1500m, and that is not even her specialty. It is giving "desperate for a podium" energy. When the top athletes start hopping between distances like they are trying on shoes at a clearance sale, you know the specialization era is dying because everyone is just chasing the highest bidder.

Let us be real: the "Smiling Assassin" title is cute for marketing, but the track is about to get very dusty once she fully transitions to the 42km grind. The Kenyan athletics scene loves to talk about "dominance," but they are currently leaning on a 32-year-old to keep the lights on. If the "youngsters" from the school games do not step up soon, we are going to be watching 1500m races that look more like a slow Sunday jog than a world-class event. Faith is moving to the roads because she knows the track is a young girl’s game, and she is too smart to get caught lagging behind.
