Let us be real for a second. The streets were full of "revolutionaries" until the hashtags stopped trending and the data bundles ran out. Now, the same influencers who were posting aesthetic protest selfies are back to their regular programming of tea and outfit checks. The narrative that the youth were "misled" by social media is actually the funniest joke of the week. It is not that they were misled-it is that they were bored and looking for a new backdrop for their TikToks. Now that the dust has settled, the true clout chasers are being exposed for exactly what they are.

Speaking of jokes, President Ruto is out here playing the ultimate "cool boss" role. He is making light-hearted remarks about his Deputy, Kindiki, while discussing the Nithi Bridge and road projects like they are just two bros hanging out at a car wash. It is all very cute on camera, but we know the vibes. This is high-stakes theater designed to distract us from the fact that the political script is being rewritten behind closed doors. They want us to focus on the banter while the real deals are signed in ink that never quite dries.

Since the political "death" of Raila, the landscape looks like a messy reality TV show reunion where everyone is fighting for the center seat. With the old lion finally out of the way, the President has become the top dog of this national chess game. But do not let the talk of "broad-based government" fool you into thinking anyone actually likes each other. Much like the lies we are fed about international cooperation, [the great divorce: why global unity is a marketing scam](/posts/the-great-divorce--why-global-unity-is-a-marketing-scam) perfectly describes this local circus. These people are not united; they are just stuck in the same elevator and trying not to scream.

The award for "Most Shameless" definitely goes to ODM. They are now pushing for the Deputy President seat as their "fair share" for supporting the government. It is giving "desperate ex-best friend trying to move into the master bedroom." They call it the price of support, but we all know it is just a desperate grab for relevance. They spent years acting like the moral compass of the country, only to fold the moment a shiny new title was dangled in front of them. The hypocrisy is so thick you could cut it with a knife.

In the end, the "genuine achievements" of the government are being ignored because the drama is just too good to look away from. Whether it is road projects or political reshuffles, the only thing being built here is a bigger stage for the same old performers. We are just the audience, paying for the tickets through our taxes while they argue over who gets the best lighting. Stay tuned, because this season of "As The Seat Turns" is just getting started, and the plot twists are getting more ridiculous by the hour.
