So the latest Nairobi gossip train is chugging along with claims that Timothy Kimani Ndegwa, or Njugush as you peasants know him, has secretly put all his properties and cars under his mother’s name. Apparently, this is all tied up with some separation drama with his wife, Celestine Ndinda. Because of course it is.

The whole circus started with a clip from Radio Maisha, bless their hearts for trying to keep up with the Joneses. Then, Edgar Obare, our resident digital detective, drops some alleged NTSA documents. But surprise, surprise, the documents supposedly show both Njugush and Ndinda listed as owners of the matatus. So much for the elaborate conspiracy of hiding assets from the missus via Mama.

This whole thing is as predictable as a politician’s promise. It's the same tired song and dance we see everywhere. Remember that Moroccan footballer, Hakimi? Same story, different continent. People love to dissect the finances of anyone who dares to make a decent living. It’s like watching hyenas fight over a carcass, except the carcass is someone else’s bank account.

Njugush and Ndinda, they built their brand, TTNT, from scratch. Met in college, fell in love, got married, the whole Kenyan fairytale. They've churned out skits, shows, online content - a respectable hustle. It’s amazing how quickly people jump to the worst conclusions, especially when it comes to successful couples. It’s almost as if some people get a kick out of seeing others fail, a bit like the sentiment behind [The War Dividend: Profiting From a Burning Middle East](/posts/the-war-dividend--profiting-from-a-burning-middle-east).

Look, if they're smart enough to register their assets under their own names, good for them. It’s called common sense, not some elaborate scheme. This whole "assets under mother's name" thing is just the usual Kenyan social media fodder designed to get clicks and keep the rumour mill grinding. Don't be surprised when the next "scandal" involves them accidentally buying a doughnut with the wrong credit card.

The truth is, most of these "celebrity secrets" are just manufactured drama. Njugush and Celestine are a success story, built on talent and hard work. Let them enjoy their success without the constant nitpicking and baseless accusations. Nairobi has enough real problems to worry about, like the ever-increasing cost of everything, to get bogged down in this nonsense.
