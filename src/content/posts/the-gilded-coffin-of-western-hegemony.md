Central banks are currently clutching 15 percent of every ounce ever pulled from the earth. This is not investment. It is hoarding. The BRICS+ coalition is vacuuming up over 1,000 tonnes a year because they recognize that the greenback is a promissory note written in disappearing ink. By creating a structural price floor, these nations have turned a static element into a high-velocity weapon of de-dollarization. They are not playing the market. They are building a bunker.

The international impact is a landscape of fractured trust. Much like the saltwater magic and the great Kenyan fire sale, where national assets are liquidated to satisfy external masters, the global south is realizing that paper promises are a trap. This strategic accumulation of bullion is a cold, hard rejection of the rules-based order that only seems to benefit the people who write the rules. Gold is the silent witness to the slow-motion collapse of the post-1945 financial architecture.

The institutional pivot toward bullion is the ultimate vote of no confidence in the American experiment. When the Fed moves the goalposts on inflation, they admit the game is rigged. The decoupling from traditional bonds proves that the world no longer trusts the debt of a nation that spends more on interest than on its own future. We are witnessing a strategic retreat into the only asset that does not require a politician’s signature to hold value. The era of cheap credit and American exceptionalism is buried under 1,000 tonnes of new BRICS+ gold.
