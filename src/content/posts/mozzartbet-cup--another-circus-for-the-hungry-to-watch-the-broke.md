The standout joke of the draw is record champions Gor Mahia facing off against a team literally named BB Bread. I suppose it is fitting that a team named after a basic commodity is playing, considering most of these players are probably wondering where their next meal is coming from. BB Bread supposedly "stunned" AFC Leopards in the last round, but in a league where the big cats are often toothless and broke, a bakery team winning is not a miracle-it is just a sign of how far the standards have fallen.

Then we have Shabana versus Muhoroni Youth, a match they claim will be "tightly contested." In this city, "contested" usually means whoever has the loudest fans or the most influential friends behind the scenes wins. It is the same logic that governs the rest of our broken systems, where having the right status is like owning [the parliament sticker: your VIP permit to kill and keep partying](/posts/the-parliament-sticker--your-vip-permit-to-kill-and-keep-partying). If you are not part of the inner circle, you are just running around in the dirt for the amusement of people who do not even know your name.

Bidco United and Tusker are set to "lock horns" in an all top-tier affair. Both sides supposedly boast "Premier League pedigree," which is a fancy way of saying they have slightly better jerseys than the amateur teams. Expect ninety minutes of sideways passes and missed opportunities, followed by a post-match interview where everyone pretends the officiating was fair. It is a "competitive fixture" only if you ignore the reality that the house always wins, and the house is currently managed by people who treat the sport like a private ATM.

The governing body claims to be "dedicated to developing and promoting the beautiful game," but the only thing they are developing is a thicker skin against criticism. While they talk about "fairy-tale runs" and "cup glory," the rest of us are looking at the calendar and wondering how many more "unforgettable moments" we have to endure before someone actually fixes the infrastructure. March 7th and 8th will come and go, the trophies will be lifted, and the cycle of mediocrity will continue just as planned.
