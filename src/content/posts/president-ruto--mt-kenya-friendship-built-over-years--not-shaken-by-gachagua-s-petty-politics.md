President Ruto: Mt Kenya Friendship Built Over Years, Not Shaken By Gachagua's Petty Politics

The President is out here flexing, reminding everyone that his Mt Kenya connections are not just for show. While he was busy at some memorial service in Nyandarua, he made it clear that the region's votes in 2022 were a big deal, and he's not about to let anyone, especially his former deputy who was conveniently in attendance, mess with that. Apparently, his friendships are "deeply rooted," not some casual fling.

Ruto’s message was pretty straightforward: he knows who voted for him, and he's not stupid. He's been cultivating this relationship for years, so a few political squabbles or Gachagua's supposed "disagreements" aren't going to break it. He's the President, he doesn't need permission to travel anywhere, and he's going to answer his critics with "actions and deeds," not with childish political fights. It’s all about the "broad-based government" and unity, you see.

This whole drama is playing out against the backdrop of Gachagua's impeachment - a situation that apparently drew leaders from rival camps to the same memorial. It’s a classic Nairobi theatre, where everyone shows up to eye each other and pretend they're all there for the same noble cause, while secretly calculating their next move.

Ruto’s insistence that his relationship with Mt Kenya is solid even as his ties with Gachagua fray seems like a calculated move. It’s a classic political maneuver: emphasize your independent strength and downplay any perceived weakness. He’s trying to show that he can stand on his own two feet, even if it means alienating people he once worked closely with. It's all part of the game, isn't it? Just like how the [Ruto Signs Coffee Act: New Board, Same Old Squeeze](/posts/ruto-signs-coffee-act--new-board--same-old-squeeze) saga reminded us that political promises often have a different flavor in reality.

Meanwhile, the rest of us are left to wonder when the actual governing will take precedence over these constant political posturing sessions. But hey, at least the President is traveling freely and building his "friendships."
