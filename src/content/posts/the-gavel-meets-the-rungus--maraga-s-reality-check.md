Welcome to the theater of the absurd, where a former Chief Justice thinks he can drive a caravan through the heartland of the "Hustler" kingdom and come out with his dignity intact. David Maraga, the man who once thought he could nullify an election and go home to sleep, is finally getting a taste of the real Nairobi - and it doesn’t taste like justice. His Ukatiba Caravan, intended to register the youth under the #GenZ15MillionStrong banner, got a warm welcome in Tharaka Nithi that involved less civic education and more "Tutam" chants and hired banners. If you thought the bench was tough, try the Chuka University parking lot.

Maraga is out here acting shocked that the police are looking the other way while goons do the heavy lifting. Someone should tell the CJ emeritus that in this country, the police aren't there to protect your right to vote; they’re there to ensure the status quo remains as unbothered as a politician on a Tuesday afternoon. We’ve seen this script before. Whether it’s Sifuna mourning a supporter in Kitengela or Gachagua and Kalonzo being ghosted at Jogoo House by an IG who clearly had better things to do, the message is clear: if you aren't with the program, you're the problem.

The irony is as thick as the smog on Mombasa Road. Maraga says he was inspired by the Gen Z protests where "young people were shot like rats," but he seems to think that a hashtag and a colorful bus will change a system that feeds on intimidation. It’s the same cold-blooded efficiency we see in the corporate world. Whether it’s a government "optimizing" the voter list with a few well-placed rungs or a tech giant slashing its workforce without a second thought, the human element is always the first thing to go. Just look at how the global elites operate; as we noted in [THE SMILE TURNS INTO A SCAR: AMAZON’S 30,000-SOUL CULL](/posts/the-smile-turns-into-a-scar--amazon-s-30-000-soul-cull), efficiency and empathy are allergic to one another. Maraga’s "souls" are just numbers to the regime, just like those 30,000 workers were to Bezos.

So, here we are. Maraga is tweeting condemnations while the "acolytes" are sharpening their tools for the next rally. The opposition is crying about "unholy alliances" between goons and officers as if they didn’t use the same playbook when they were the ones holding the keys to the kingdom. It’s a cycle of cynicism that keeps this city spinning. You want to register to vote? Great. Just don’t be surprised when the person you’re voting against is the one holding the pen and the padlock. In Nairobi, we don’t believe in miracles; we believe in who has the bigger crowd and the louder whistle. Welcome to the campaign trail, David. It’s a lot dirtier than a courtroom.
