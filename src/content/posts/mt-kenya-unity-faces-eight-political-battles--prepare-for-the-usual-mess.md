So, the bigwigs are scrambling to keep the Mt Kenya vote bloc together for 2027. Bless their hearts. It's like trying to herd cats, but with more backstabbing and carefully crafted speeches designed to sound like they actually care. We're talking about at least eight threats, mostly from their own kind, fighting over market share in twelve counties. Nairobi, Kiambu, Nakuru - you know the drill. Expect the usual song and dance.

President Ruto, ever the showman, is already escalating the "war of words." His goal? To solidify his base and secure that re-election. Because what else is new? Meanwhile, Rigathi Gachagua is out here complaining about key sectors being controlled by a select few. Isn't that the whole point of politics, Riggy G? To put your cronies in charge? It's a classic tale as old as time, much like the unending debate around personal freedoms, whether it's [the Kenya Rastafarians High Court Cannabis Ban Challenge](/posts/kenya-rastafarians-high-court-cannabis-ban-challenge--spiritual-right-or-just-another-hustle) or who gets to control the national purse.

This whole Gema thing, born from succession fears back in the day, has always been a beast of its own. From a cultural association to a political heavyweight, it’s seen it all. Now, Gachagua, Kalonzo, and Muturi are probably swapping notes on how to play the game, or more likely, how to survive it. It's a never ending cycle of ambition and backroom deals, where every handshake hides a potential stab.

Ultimately, this is just another predictable chapter in Kenya's political playbook. The Mt Kenya region, touted as a powerful voting bloc, will likely fragment under the weight of its own leaders' ambitions. We'll see the usual pronouncements, the grandstanding, and the eventual disappointment of those who actually believe things will change. Just brace yourselves for the circus.
