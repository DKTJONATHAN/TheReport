Bethesda Game Studios director Todd Howard has moved to clarify expectations surrounding Starfield's long-anticipated major update, stating definitively that it "is not Starfield 2.0." The comment aims to calibrate community speculation that has been building for roughly a year, ever since the studio promised "a lot of exciting things planned" following the release of the Shattered Space add-on.

Speaking in a recent podcast, Howard confirmed that significant work on Starfield content has been underway and that details will be shared "really soon." He expressed confidence in the upcoming changes, noting, "It's the kind of thing where if you love Starfield, we think you're going to love this. It's updates and things that change the game, not in an isolated way, but [in a] sort of meta [way] - using outer space and things in ways that we haven't."

However, Howard also provided a crucial caveat for those who may have found the game lacking upon its initial release. "If Starfield is something that didn't connect with you right away, or you bounced off it or found it boring in places, I don't think this is going to change that fundamentally," he added. This statement suggests the update will enhance the experience for existing fans rather than entirely redefine it for new or lapsed players.

The update is described as "soonish" in its release, with Howard explicitly stating it is not a final act for the game. "No, we're doing more Starfield stuff coming up - we've been laying that out. It's something we see continuing for a while," he affirmed, signaling a sustained commitment to the title beyond this imminent release. While Starfield launched in 2023 to middling critical reviews, it appears to have cultivated a substantial player base, with Howard referring to it as a "Game Pass hours beast" due to its extensive playtimes.

Bethesda Game Studios, which employs approximately 500 people, has recently seen a renewed focus on its Fallout franchise, buoyed by the strong reception of a second television series. One commentator noted that the series had successfully reignited excitement for the games, highlighting the potential impact of cross-media adaptations.

Despite ongoing work on Starfield and the success of Fallout, the majority of the studio's workforce is currently dedicated to the development of The Elder Scrolls 6. Howard characterized the upcoming Elder Scrolls title as a return to a more "classic" style of game for the studio, drawing parallels to the acclaimed Skyrim or Oblivion, distinguishing it from what he termed the "creative detour" represented by Fallout 76 and Starfield. This indicates a strategic shift in the studio's design philosophy for its flagship fantasy RPG.
