So, Marvel is extending its cinematic reach all the way to the end of the decade. Groundbreaking. After a brief drought, they're promising a trickle of two films over the next two years, followed by an onslaught of three in 2028 and at least two more in 2029. This isn't confidence; it's a frantic effort to keep the lights on. _Avengers: Doomsday_ and _Avengers: Secret Wars_ are positioned as grand finales, a desperate gambit to reignite interest before the inevitable "Mutant Saga" kicks off, presumably with a rehashed _X-Men_ film.

The plan is transparent: milk the nostalgia, introduce new flavors to an already oversaturated market, and hope nobody notices the creative well is running dry. _Black Panther 3_ is trotted out as a marquee event, as if a sequel to a film whose relevance has long passed is a guaranteed draw. Beyond that, expect more _Thor_, more _Shang-Chi_, and another _Deadpool_. It's a predictable formula designed to keep the franchise churning, regardless of artistic merit or audience exhaustion.

This relentless output, especially after the supposed "Multiverse Saga" closure, is a stark admission that Marvel can't afford to stop. They're not innovating; they're simply doubling down on what worked, and praying it still does. Expect more vague announcements at industry events, more thinly veiled promises, and a steady stream of content designed to fill screens and, more importantly, revenue streams. The real question isn't what these movies will be about, but how long this hamster wheel of sequels can keep spinning before it grinds to a halt.
