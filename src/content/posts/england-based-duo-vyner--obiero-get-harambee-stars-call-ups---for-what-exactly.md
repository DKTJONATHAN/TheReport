England-Based Duo Vyner, Obiero Get Harambee Stars Call Ups - For What Exactly?

So, the Football Kenya Federation (FKF) has gone and done it again. Benni McCarthy, bless his heart, has unearthed two more lads from England, Zak Vyner and Zech Obiero, to pad out the Harambee Stars squad for this so called "Fifa World Series". Apparently, it's all about "meaningful international friendly matches" between nations that normally wouldn't bother with each other. Sounds like a fancy way of saying we're going to get thoroughly trounced by teams we've never heard of, all in the name of "football development".

Kenya's been lumped in with Rwanda, Estonia, and Grenada. Estonia, a European nation, and Grenada, a Caribbean island. We're talking serious geographic gymnastics here, all so some Fifa suits can tick their boxes. The players are supposed to be in camp by March 22nd, then off to Kigali two days later. Don't expect any miracles; this is just another exercise in throwing money at a problem that probably needs a good shake-up from the ground up. It's reminiscent of how some of our leaders seem to operate, a bit like that [Sifuna's Kakamega Roadshow: When Political Clout Chasing Becomes a Bad Music Video](/posts/sifuna-s-kakamega-roadshow--when-political-clout-chasing-becomes-a-bad-music-video).

Vyner, 28, and Obiero, 21, are being courted because they've got Kenyan blood. They met with FKF bigwigs like McDonald Mariga, who frankly should know better by now. Vyner plays for Wrexham in the Championship, and Obiero is on loan at Tranmere Rovers in League Two. These are not exactly global powerhouses. The hope, of course, is to build a "formidable squad" for the 2027 Africa Cup of Nations. We’ll see how that pans out when they’re actually playing for something other than bragging rights and travel expenses.

We've got 16 foreign-based players in this 23-man squad, with the rest from local clubs. Other "notable inclusions" like Clarke Oduor, Job Ochieng, and Mohammed Bajaber are thrown in. Bajaber apparently missed the Chan tournament due to injury and hasn't played for the national team since. Ochieng has been doing his thing for Real Sociedad "B" and even got a sniff of La Liga. It’s a mixed bag, as always.

The goalkeeper situation looks as stable as a wet bar of soap. Byrne Omondi is at Gor Mahia, Brian Bwire is in South Africa with Polokwane City, and Ian Otieno is also in South Africa with Richards Bay. Defenders are a similar mix of local talent and overseas players. Midfielders are scattered across Europe and Tanzania. The forwards list is a real who's who of players who might get a plane ticket home if things go south quickly.

So, here we are again. Harambee Stars, the perpetual underdogs, trotting off to a Fifa Series tournament. Let's just hope they don't embarrass themselves too much. But knowing our luck, and the way things are run, I wouldn't bet on it. It's just another performance to watch, another cheque to write, and probably another round of disappointment.
