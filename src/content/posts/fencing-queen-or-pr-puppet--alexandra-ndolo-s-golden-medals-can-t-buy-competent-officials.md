Oh, look at us pretending we care about niche sports. Alexandra Ndolo thought she was doing something revolutionary by bringing fencing to a country that usually only recognizes you if you are running through a forest or chasing a pigskin. She won the gold, she did the "prove yourself" dance, and what did she get? A big fat zero in her bank account and a "please leave a message after the tone" from the Ministry of Sports. It is the classic Kenyan heartbreak: perform like a queen but get treated like a nuisance.

The hypocrisy is actually breathtaking. While Ndolo was busy submitting receipts like a desperate intern, her face was being plastered all over Team Kenya promotional posters for Paris 2024. Talk about being used for the "aesthetic." They wanted the prestige of her world-class status without paying the invoice. It is giving major "payment in exposure" vibes, and we all know you cannot pay for a flight to a global championship with likes and retweets. Even [HELB Scammers Are Giving More Effort Than Your Favorite 'Soft Life' Influencers](/posts/helb-scammers-are-giving-more-effort-than-your-favorite--soft-life--influencers) at this point, because at least those scammers are consistent with their hustle.

When she dared to ask where the money went, the system did what it does best: gaslighting and intimidation. Ndolo claims she was threatened with a ban for the "crime" of asking for the support she was promised. Imagine threatening to ban the only person making you look good on the global fencing stage. It is like a toxic ex who tells you that you will never find anyone better while he is actively stealing from your purse. They even went as far as withholding her competition registrations. If that is not peak hater behavior, I do not know what is.

But wait, the mess gets even deeper. To cover their tracks, these officials reportedly told younger athletes that Ndolo was the one "eating" all the budget. They turned the youth against the veteran just to hide their own incompetence. It is a classic "divide and rule" script that we see in every corner of this city. They painted her as the villain while she was out there self-funding her way to represent a flag that barely wants to acknowledge her existence unless there is a glossy photo op involved.

So here we are, celebrating the "champions" on social media while the actual talent is being bullied into silence behind the scenes. Kenya loves a winner, but only if that winner comes with a low maintenance fee and does not ask too many questions about where the "allocated funds" actually went. Ndolo has already proven she is world-class; it is just a shame she is dealing with a third-class administration that would rather see her fail than see her paid. Stay petty and keep your receipts, because in this game, the only thing being fenced is the truth.
