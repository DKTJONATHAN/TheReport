Oh look, Patrick Dempsey is back on the red carpet, proving that nothing - not even the passage of time or the death of a "dear friend" - can keep a man from his spotlight. At 60, he is still playing the silver fox card at the 2026 BAFTAs, dragging his 24-year-old daughter, Talula Fyfe, along for the ride. It is the classic Hollywood move: if you are starting to look like a relic, just stand next to a Gen-Z relative to soak up some of that youth by association. 

Talula looked fine, I guess, but let us be real. This is just another case of the nepo-baby conveyor belt in action. It is all part of [The Recycling Plant: How Hollywood’s Latest Slate Confirms Cultural Stagnation](/posts/the-recycling-plant--how-hollywood-s-latest-slate-confirms-cultural-stagnation) where we are forced to watch the same families rotate through the same ceremonies until we all lose our minds. Patrick is there to "present," which is just industry code for needing to remind people he is still relevant enough to be invited to London.

The real kicker here is the timing. It has been what, three days since Eric Dane passed away? "McSteamy" barely had time to get cold before "McDreamy" was suit-shopping for an awards show. Eric’s battle with ALS was brutal and short, only ten months of public struggle before he succumbed at 53. But apparently, the mourning period in Tinseltown lasts exactly as long as it takes to fly private from LAX to Heathrow.

Patrick has been very busy lately, conveniently sharing "heartbreaking details" about Eric’s final days just in time for his own press cycle. It is a masterclass in performative grief. Nothing sells a presenter slot like a little proximity to tragedy, right? He spent the week spilling intimate details about a dying man’s struggle and then spent Sunday posing for the flashes like he did not have a care in the world. 

We see you, Patrick. The industry loves a "dignified" survivor, especially one who can transition from a funeral atmosphere to a champagne-fueled gala without missing a beat. It is the kind of hollow professionalism that makes Hollywood so exhausting to watch. While fans are still processing the loss of a Grey-s Anatomy legend, the survivors are already back to business, making sure their tuxedo fits and their lighting is perfect.
