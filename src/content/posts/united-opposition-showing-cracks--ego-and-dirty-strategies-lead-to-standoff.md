So, the so called "United Opposition," forged in the desperate scramble to remove President William Ruto, is already showing its true colours. This wasn't about unity; it was a marriage of convenience, and the honeymoon is over before it even began. Now, instead of plotting to dethrone the current regime, they're busy tearing each other apart over who gets to be the "chosen one" for 2027. Predictable.

Publicly, you'll see photos of former President Uhuru Kenyatta and Rigathi Gachagua shaking hands, all smiles and camaraderie. Don't be fooled. Behind the scenes, it's a grubby contest of egos and clashing strategies. We're talking about a Gachagua-Kalonzo Musyoka bloc versus a Fred Matiang'i-Peter Munya faction. It's less about a shared vision and more about who can grab the biggest slice of the pie. This is Nairobi politics, not a kumbaya circle.

The death of Raila Odinga, God rest his soul, has left a vacuum. And as they say, nature abhors a vacuum, especially when there's political power to be had. The "cathedral is being partitioned," and everyone's scrambling for the best pews. Raila's own ODM party is likely feeling the heat from his hard-line stance against the broad-based government, proving that even within their own ranks, loyalty is a fluid concept when power is on the line.

This whole spectacle is a stark reminder of how things work here. Remember when we talked about how [Nairobi is Drowning and the Politicians are Only Floating Words](/posts/nairobi-is-drowning-and-the-politicians-are-only-floating-words)? Same script, different players. They'll promise unity, they'll pretend to work together, and then it'll all devolve into the same old squabbles. With an estimated 12 million Kenyans yet to register as voters, this infighting is just handing Ruto a free pass. Don't say I didn't warn you.
