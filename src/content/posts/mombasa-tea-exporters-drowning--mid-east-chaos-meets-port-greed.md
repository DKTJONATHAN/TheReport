So, the Mombasa exporters are wailing again. Apparently, their precious tea isn't reaching foreign tables as easily as it used to. Their excuse? The "ongoing Middle East conflict" and, shocker, the "inefficiencies at the Port of Mombasa." As if we didn't see that coming from a mile away. It's always something, isn't it?

They talk about "threats to business operations and cash flow." Oh, boo hoo. What did they expect? That the world would stop spinning just so their tea leaves could make a smooth exit? The port is a black hole of bureaucracy and delays, and now they're adding geopolitical instability to the mix. It's a recipe for disaster they seemingly cooked up themselves.

It’s not like this is new. We’ve seen plenty of disasters in this country, from floods to whatever else the heavens decide to throw at us. Remember when we discussed [49 Dead, 16 Police Stations Flooded: Nairobi Realist's Take on Another Disaster](/posts/49-dead--16-police-stations-flooded--nairobi-realist-s-take-on-another-disaster)? Disasters are practically a national pastime. But this? This is self-inflicted, amplified by international events.

What’s truly infuriating is that these exporters will likely expect a bailout or some special treatment, while the rest of us are just trying to navigate the daily circus. They want us to subscribe to their newsletters for "updates" and "special offers." Special offers on what? More excuses? Give me a break.

They're probably hoping for some government intervention, some magical solution that will untangle the mess. But let's be real. The port is a beast that feeds on inefficiency, and the Middle East is a mess that shows no signs of cleaning up. So, the tea might continue to sit, the cash flow will continue to dry up, and the exporters will continue to whine. It's the Kenyan way.
