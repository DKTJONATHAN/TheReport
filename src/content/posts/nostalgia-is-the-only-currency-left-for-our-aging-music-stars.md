Juliani is out here in 2026 trying to sell us a version of history that smells like mothballs and unfulfilled promises. Declaring Jua Cali the greatest artist Kenya has ever produced is the kind of safe, nostalgic take you give when you realize the ground has moved on and left you behind. It is easy to talk about unmatched influence when the current crop of artists is too busy fighting for likes to build a legacy.

The ground Juliani keeps mentioning is a fickle beast. Back then, you had to actually have talent to be a celebrity, but now, notoriety is the only thing that sticks. It is a far cry from the days of Genge when you look at how [Nairobi’s 'Soft Life' Founders Just Got a New Address: The Remand Home](/posts/nairobi-s--soft-life--founders-just-got-a-new-address--the-remand-home) just by faking it on Instagram. Being a national icon in a country that changes its heroes faster than its fuel prices is a title that does not mean much anymore.

Then comes the ghetto therapy bit. It is the same old story of gunshots and hardship used to polish a brand for the middle-class. We get it, Dandora was tough, but using those difficult realities to explain why you are playing house music now feels like a reach. Most people in this city do not use lyrics as therapy; they use cheap liquor and silence. Art developing a society is a nice dream for an interview, but it does not pay the electricity bill in a bedsitter.

Juliani also wants us to know he is not just a gospel artist. He is questioning things. It is the classic Nairobi move: get the church audience to build your career, then spend the rest of your life trying to convince the cool kids you are actually a rockstar. The canvas can change anytime, sure, but if you keep changing the paint, eventually you just end up with a messy blur that nobody wants to buy.

At the end of the day, calling Jua Cali the biggest artist ever is just safe. It is a way to respect the past without having to admit that the current scene is a chaotic mess of one-hit-wonders and ghostwriters. Jua Cali transitioned the industry to celebrity status, but he forgot to leave a map for the ones who came after. Now we are just left with legends who talk about the good old days while the rest of us are just trying to survive the week.
