So, William Samoei is playing Big Brother to the Coast, telling these perpetual squabblers to kiss and make up or the State largesse dries up. Apparently, our dear CS for Mining, Hassan Joho, is busy trying to grab whatever political turf he can before the next election, hoping to bag that running mate spot in 2027. Naturally, a bunch of his own party folks, UDA members down there, are having none of it. This is classic Kenya - leaders fighting over crumbs while the actual work gathers dust.

It’s not just about Joho’s ambition, though. The Senate, bless their bureaucratic hearts, have decided that 560 pending reports are too many and are now threatening to hold back funds if county bosses don't play ball. Talk about holding the country hostage over paperwork. Meanwhile, governors are out there, pretending to be proactive, buying medical equipment and closing markets. Probably just a show for the cameras, a way to look busy before the next wave of corruption allegations hits.

And the ferry? Don't even get me started. Motorists are still queuing up, day in and day out, for a service that’s as reliable as a politician’s promise. These never ending challenges are apparently just part of the Kenyan experience. We’ve seen this circus before, the same old song and dance.

It’s the same old game, isn’t it? Politicians angling for power, threatening each other with the withdrawal of "State power and 'fire'," whatever that even means. It’s a wonder anything gets done. They're all too busy playing musical chairs to actually govern.

This whole saga with Joho just highlights the deep divisions and endless power struggles. A section of politicians from the region have already made their stance clear - they reject Joho's overtures. It’s a chaotic mess, and who suffers? The ordinary Kenyan, of course. We’re left to navigate the potholes, the stalled projects, and the constant political theatrics.

So, Ruto’s ultimatum is just another episode in this never ending political drama. Unite or lose power. It’s a threat, a tactic, and ultimately, a reflection of the self serving nature of politics in this country. We’ll see if any of them actually listen, or if it’s just more hot air.
