Let's be real, the pressure to have a "Snatched" waist in Nairobi is officially out of control. We see the influencers on TikTok acting like their flat tummies came from lemon water and "good vibes," but the real tea is much darker than a detox juice. Everyone is looking for a shortcut, and the latest trend is Mounjaro. It promises a "Baddie" body with zero effort, meaning no gym and no meal prep. But while you are busy chasing that Instagram aesthetic, the people selling this stuff on social media are basically playing Russian roulette with your life.

This drug, tirzepatide, is supposed to be for serious medical issues, not for someone who just wants to fit into a size small for a weekend in Diani. It tricks your body into thinking it is full, which sounds like a dream until you realize it can also cause your gallbladder to quit and your kidneys to fail. While our government is busy with other things, like how [the Gava is using your taxes to pay keyboard warriors](/posts/is-the-government-using-bots) to distract us from real issues, these illegal clinics are operating right under their noses. They are selling unregistered poison for a cool 120,000 shillings and then blocking you the moment things go south.

Take the case of Nancy Muthoni, who just wanted to lose weight without the stress of a treadmill. She found a Facebook page, because that is where all great medical decisions are made apparently, and was told there were no side effects. A random "nurse" showed up at her house, stabbed a needle into her stomach, and left. By that evening, she was convulsing and covered in rashes. The seller? She claimed she was out of the country and hit the block button immediately. That is the level of "customer service" you get when you buy your health from a sponsored post.

It gets worse. Nancy was pregnant and did not know it. Because these "medical professionals" are more interested in your M-Pesa balance than a pregnancy test, they did not screen her. The toxins from the unregulated drug were so potent that her baby's heart stopped. She lost her child all because of a Facebook ad promising a shortcut to nowhere. It is the same kind of over-hyped disappointment we see everywhere else. Honestly, these weight loss scams are giving major [Talanta Stadium or Talanta Scam](/posts/talanta-stadium-or-talanta-scam--kenya-s-afcon-dreams-are-giving-broke-influencer-vibes) energy, with all the big promises on a glossy brochure but nothing but a hollow mess in reality.

The Pharmacy and Poisons Board is finally piping up to say that Mounjaro is not even registered in Kenya. There are legal alternatives like TIZA-Q, but those require a real doctor and a real prescription, things that "lazy" slimmers clearly want to avoid. If you are about to let some random person from WhatsApp inject you with an unidentified toxin just to lose a potbelly, you need more than a diet; you need a reality check. Stop trusting every "sponsored" post you see, because a flat stomach is not worth a coffin.
