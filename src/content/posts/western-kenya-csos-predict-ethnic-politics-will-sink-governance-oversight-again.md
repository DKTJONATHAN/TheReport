So, the folks in Western Kenya are finally waking up to the same old song and dance. They’re crying about "ethnic politics" creeping back for the 2027 elections, as if this country has ever been about anything else. These civil society groups, bless their idealistic hearts, are worried this mess will "weaken accountability" and "reverse gains in governance oversight." Honestly, what gains? We’re still here watching public funds disappear faster than a politician’s promise.

These "stakeholders" under some project called TUNU (which sounds suspiciously like a snack) met in Kisumu to tell leaders to focus on "issue-based politics" instead of tribal loyalties. They’ve noticed that since late 2025, it's all been about who’s from where and who’s allied with whom. They're warning that this tribal circus will "divert attention from key governance priorities" like, you know, making sure your taxes aren't siphoned off and that you actually get water and electricity. Groundbreaking stuff.

The TUNU Project Coordinator, Ken Anjejo, is practically shouting from the rooftops that Kenya is doomed to repeat its electoral history. He’s seeing the same pattern: accountability goes out the window when the political competition heats up. He’s begging voters to look at performance, not tribe. Easier said than done when your entire identity is wrapped up in who your "people" are. And, of course, he’s reminding everyone to register and vote - as if that’s going to magically make leaders responsible.

The sad reality is, these heightened political games always mean less scrutiny on public money. It’s a feeding frenzy waiting to happen. These CSOs are calling for leaders to present "policy-driven agendas" on healthcare, education, and all those other nice-to-have things. They’re even telling the media to stop amplifying divisive nonsense. As if the media hasn’t been doing that for decades. Let's be real, this whole fuel price hike situation is a perfect example of how global forces and government incompetence combine, and you can bet ethnic politics won’t fix [Kenyans Feel The Fuel Price 'Pain' Again - Ruto Says It's Global Forces, Of Course](/posts/kenyans-feel-the-fuel-price--pain--again---ruto-says-it-s-global-forces--of-course).
