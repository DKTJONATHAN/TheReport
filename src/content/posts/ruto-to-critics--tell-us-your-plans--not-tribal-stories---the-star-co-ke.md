Ruto To Critics: Give Us Your Plans, Not Tribal Stories, Or You're Just Full of Hot Air

President Ruto, in his usual fashion, has decided to grace us with his pronouncements on national unity. Apparently, this tribalism and hatred he speaks of is a new phenomenon in Kenyan politics, not something that's been the bread and butter of elections for decades. He’s telling leaders to ditch the "you're from coast, I'm from town" nonsense and just present their development agenda. It's almost as if he's just discovered that some politicians rely on these tired old tricks.

He was at some church in Kajiado, preaching unity and respect, no doubt while his own party members are busy fueling the very divisions he decries. He wants us to focus on policies and solutions, not identity. It's a lovely thought, isn't it? Like asking street urchins to focus on their stock portfolios. He’s basically saying, “Stop the petty squabbles, here’s my agenda.” As if the current state of affairs is solely due to a lack of clear agendas and not, you know, the systemic rot.

And then there's the bit about his own relentless work schedule. Ruto says he’s booked solid for six months, busy transforming Kenya. He’s comparing us to Singapore, Malaysia, and South Korea, claiming we’ve fallen behind because we’ve lost focus. It’s a classic move: deflect from current criticism by pointing to historical development disparities. It’s easy to point fingers at lost time when you're the one currently in charge of the clock, and frankly, his talk about acceleration sounds suspiciously like the same old hustle, just with more urgent pacing. We’ve heard promises of transformation before; this sounds like just another iteration, perhaps with a tighter deadline. The energy he’s putting into defending his schedule could probably power a small town, which, given the state of our electricity, might be more useful than another sermon. It's the kind of relentless pursuit that makes you wonder if they're trying to catch up or just outrun the inevitable audit.

He attributes the administration's "steady progress" to God's favour and commitment to projects. Right. Because when things actually move forward, it's always divine intervention and unwavering dedication, not the hard-earned cash of citizens or the tireless efforts of people trying to make ends meet. It’s a convenient narrative, isn't it? Blame the divisive politicians for the lack of unity, then claim divine and personal dedication for any perceived progress. It’s a masterclass in managing perceptions, a skill many of those HELB scammers could learn from, given the effort they put into their scams.
