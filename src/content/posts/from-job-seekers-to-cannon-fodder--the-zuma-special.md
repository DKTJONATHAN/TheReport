In this life, if the deal looks too good to be true, you are probably the product being sold. A group of seventeen South Africans just learned this the hard way after being shipped off to Russia. They thought they were going for "bodyguard training," which is the international code for "easy money and looking cool in suits." Instead, they found themselves on the front line of the war in Ukraine, probably wondering where the buffet and the ear-pieces were while shells were raining down on their heads.

The sheer level of delusion required to sign a contract written in a language you do not understand is honestly impressive. These men arrived in Russia and put pen to paper on documents they couldn't read, only to realize they weren't guarding a VIP, they were the human shields. It is the classic "Kanairo" hustle but on a geopolitical scale. You think you are being recruited for a high-end security gig, but you wake up in a trench with a rifle and zero instructions on how to survive a drone strike.

What makes this even messier is the recruiter involved. Duduzile Zuma-Sambudla, the daughter of former President Jacob Zuma, has been named as the one who allegedly set this whole circus in motion. She denies it, of course, saying she thought it was all "lawful." It is the same old story of nepotism and family ties gone wrong, much like [Hollywood’s Creative Exhaustion: The Endless Cycle of Reboots and Relatives](/posts/hollywood-s-creative-exhaustion--the-endless-cycle-of-reboots-and-relatives), where the same names keep popping up to ruin things for everyone else while claiming they had the best intentions.

Now, President Cyril Ramaphosa is out here thanking Vladimir Putin for "helping" to return the men. It is peak Stockholm Syndrome. You thank the guy whose military almost turned your citizens into statistics? That is the kind of diplomatic gymnastics that keeps Africa at the bottom of the food chain. Ramaphosa is acting like Putin did him a massive favor by letting these men go, while back home, they are likely to face investigations for being illegal mercenaries. You cannot win.

The reality is that these men were desperate for a way out of the South African economy, and they were served a nightmare on a silver platter. Five people have already been arrested, including a radio presenter, because apparently, even the media is in on the human-trafficking-to-war-zones pipeline. It is a grim reminder that in the world of the powerful, the poor are just expendable assets used to settle scores in wars they don't understand for leaders who do not care if they come back in one piece or a body bag.
