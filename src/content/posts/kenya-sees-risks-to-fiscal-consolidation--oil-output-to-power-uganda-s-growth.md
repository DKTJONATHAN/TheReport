Kenya Fiscal Woes Loom, Uganda's Oil Dreams; Everyone Else Just Hopes for Scraps.

Here we go again. East African finance ministers are out here with their shiny new budgets, and guess what they're worried about? Not whether they can actually deliver, but how some far-off war in Iran is going to mess things up. Oh, and how to keep their hands on the national piggy bank without anyone noticing too much.

Kenya's lot is spouting the usual doom and gloom. The finance minister, John Mbadi, is already admitting their fiscal deficit target is a pipe dream, thanks to "global geopolitical events." Translation: things are bad, and they'll probably get worse, and it's not entirely their fault - blame the universe. He’s talking about cutting the deficit gradually, but knowing how these things go, it’ll be slower than a snail on holiday. And let’s not forget the "climate-related shocks" - because apparently, the sun and rain are now working against the government's budget. Never mind the deadly protests over fuel prices; that's just a minor inconvenience to the grand fiscal plan. Goldman Sachs, bless their hearts, wants "concrete evidence" of cuts. Good luck with that.

Meanwhile, over in Uganda, they're getting giddy about oil. Finance Minister Henry Musasizi is predicting double-digit growth, all thanks to oil production kicking off. He's painting a rosy picture of jobs, better incomes, and shiny new schools and hospitals. It’s the same old song and dance: a new resource arrives, and suddenly all problems are solved. Let's just hope this oil money doesn't vanish faster than a politician’s promise, or end up being another way for some to get rich while the rest of us are still stuck dealing with the fallout, much like some Kenyans who found themselves ensnared in a [Myanmar job scam trap]( /posts/myanmar-job-scam-trap--over-100-kenyans-stuck--39-jailed).

Tanzania's got their own set of worries, focusing on "domestic revenue collection" because the foreign handouts are drying up and debt is piling up. They're even spinning the Iran war as an "opportunity" - talk about making a silk purse out of a sow's ear. Apparently, they can be a transhipment hub for ships dodging the conflict. And some optimistic soul thinks investors will flee the Gulf for Tanzania. Right. Because nothing screams "safe investment" like a region that’s perpetually teetering on the edge.

So, while Kenya frets about the world and Uganda dances on oil fumes, the rest of us are just left hoping for the best and bracing for the worst. It's the same old East African script: big pronouncements, questionable execution, and a healthy dose of wishful thinking.
