UN Confirms Kenya Police Abuse Cases in Haiti: What Else Is New?

This UN report reads like yesterday's news, just with more victims and a different postcode. The mission meant to bring order is apparently bringing its own brand of chaos, proving that some things never change, no matter the uniform.

So, the UN, in all its bureaucratic glory, has confirmed what many suspected: members of the Kenya-led Multinational Security Support (MSS) mission in Haiti have been involved in sexual abuse. Four separate cases, involving girls as young as 12, have been investigated and, astonishingly, verified. This isn't some rumour whispered in a dark alley; this is official UN business, detailing how our supposed peacekeepers turned predator. The irony of an anti-gang force engaging in such depravity is thick enough to choke on.

The report, blandly titled "UN report on Special Measures for Protection from Sexual Exploitation and Abuse," lists these incidents as 'violations corroborated'. Corroborated, yet most cases are still 'pending'. Pending? What exactly is pending? The paperwork? The next scandal? It seems accountability is as elusive as a clean conscience for some of these characters. The fact that an internal investigation was conducted in one case with no details provided is just a masterclass in stonewalling. We've seen similar "investigations" before, haven't we? It reminds me of how some local tenders disappear into thin air, like that [Ksh95M tender scandal](/posts/mcas-under-probe-over-ksh95m-tender-scandal) that probably went to the highest bidder's cousin.

UN spokesperson Stéphane Dujarric babbles about "robust mechanisms" and "credibility." Bless his heart. He’s talking about preventing future abuses while the current ones are still gathering dust in 'pending' files. Meanwhile, a new contingent from Chad arrives, supposedly with stricter measures. Let's see how long that lasts before they too discover the "magic trick" of turning a blind eye, just like how BAT Kenya [turns tobacco into billions](/posts/tobacco-tears-and-billion-shilling-dividends--the-bat-kenya-magic-trick). And all this drama unfolds just as our own boys are packing their bags, mission supposedly accomplished, leaving behind a trail of alleged devastation.

It's a predictable pattern, isn't it? Send in the troops, claim the moral high ground, and then the reports trickle out, revealing the rot beneath the surface. We send our officers to "de-escalate gang violence" in a "war-torn nation," and they end up causing their own brand of terror. It's a shame, really, considering the potential for good, but then again, when has that ever been the dominant story in this world? It’s just another example of how we’re [selling the green giant for spare change](/posts/selling-the-green-giant-for-spare-change), both literally and figuratively.
