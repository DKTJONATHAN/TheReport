So, David Mathenge, the man they call Nameless, is apparently having a good chuckle. Why? Because some idle Kenyans have decided he's part of the Illuminati. Yes, the same guy who gave us hits like "Megarider" and "Ninanoki" is now being accused of consorting with dark forces, all just weeks before he hits his 25-year mark in the music industry. Honestly, the things people come up with when they have too much time on their hands.

He's taken to social media, naturally, to address this "ufala tu za watu idle" - which, for those not fluent in Nairobi slang, means pure foolishness from people with nothing better to do. He’s even joking that his song "Majitu" might be the culprit, promising not to sing it at his upcoming "Nameless @25" concert on August 15th. One wonders if singing "Juju" will also land him in hot water with the conspiracy theorists. It's all a bit much, isn't it?

This whole circus is happening while he's supposed to be celebrating a quarter-century of Kenyan pop music. He rose to fame way back in 1999, winning a star-search contest. Now, he wants us to forget these ridiculous whispers and just come celebrate his music. A noble thought, but let's be real, the internet never forgets, especially when it comes to juicy gossip. It makes you wonder if even the fuel scandal stories we've covered are less absurd than this.

Nameless, along with his equally famous wife Wahu, has been a fixture in Kenyan music for ages. He's blended genres, won awards, mentored young talent, and raised three kids. He's done his time, paid his dues, and now he's being linked to secret societies. It’s the kind of fame that comes with its own peculiar set of problems, and this Illuminati nonsense is just the latest installment.

At the end of the day, this is just Nameless trying to salvage some dignity and get back to the music. He's inviting everyone to leave the conspiracy theories at the door and enjoy a night of his hits. Whether people will actually leave the "dark forces" chatter behind remains to be seen. After all, in Kenya, there’s always a new rumor to chew on, especially when someone’s reaching a significant career milestone.

So, hats off to Nameless for trying to turn this into a comedy. But let's be honest, the fact that these kinds of rumors even gain traction is a testament to the bizarre landscape of online chatter we all navigate. Maybe he should just stick to singing about love and life, and leave the "dark forces" to the actual tabloids.
