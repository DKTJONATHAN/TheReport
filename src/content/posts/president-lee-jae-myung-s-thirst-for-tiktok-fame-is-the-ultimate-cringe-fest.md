Oh, honey, pull up a chair because the second-hand embarrassment is reaching lethal levels. South Korea's President Lee Jae Myung just decided that the best way to handle national affairs is to pivot to video-content creator. He launched his TikTok account this Saturday, and instead of discussing trade deals or defense strategies, he is out here begging for likes and making finger hearts like a K-Pop trainee who just failed their first evaluation. "Something big is coming," he teased on X, and apparently, that "big" thing was just a desperate plea for internet validation from the youth.

It is truly tragic to watch a man of his stature reduced to pressing a digital "subscribe" box on a document like it is some high-stakes legislation. We have seen this movie before where politicians trade their dignity for a few seconds of viral fame. It reminds me of the mess back home when we saw the [NO PHONE, NO CLOUT: IEBC TRIES TO STOP THE TIKTOK-IFICATION OF KENYAN ELECTIONS](/posts/no-phone--no-clout--iebc-tries-to-stop-the-tiktok-ification-of-kenyan-elections) because everyone wants to be an influencer instead of a leader. Lee is literally out here doing "aegyo" while the rest of the world is dealing with actual geopolitical drama.

Let us talk about the blatant hypocrisy for a second. The Presidential Office claims this move is about "expanding communication," but we all know it is a transparent attempt to kiss up to the Gen Z demographic. Even more hilarious is the claim that using a platform operated by China is an effort to address "anti-China sentiment" in South Korea. Right, because nothing screams "national security" like a president dancing on an app that has been banned from government devices in half the Western world. While he is busy being a digital darling, Seoul is distracted by court cases over Louis Vuitton bag refits. It is all just one big, expensive smoke screen.

While Lee is busy perfecting his lighting and hand gestures, the real world is actually on fire. Israel is launching attacks on Iran and declaring states of emergency, with missiles rocking the region, but I suppose that does not fit the "vibe" of his TikTok aesthetic. It is hard to take a leader seriously when he looks like a 12-year-old idiot trying to secure a brand deal. Strength and character have been replaced by ring lights and filters, and quite frankly, it is exhausting to watch.

If this is what modern leadership looks like, then we are all doomed to a future of press releases delivered via trending dance challenges. Lee Jae Myung might get his "likes" and his "subscribes," but he has lost every ounce of gravitas in the process. Stick to the Blue House and leave the finger hearts to the teenagers, honey. You are a president, not a backup dancer for a girl group. The world needs a leader, not another TikTok star with a ring light and a dream.
