NAIROBI'S FINEST STILL PLAYING DRESS UP IN HAITI

So, our boys are back. 215 of them, fresh off the plane from Haiti, apparently having 'restored security'. Right. Because Haiti, with its capital still choked by gangs and its politics in shambles, suddenly decided to sort itself out the moment our boys in uniform showed up. Don't make me laugh. They were there to prop up a failing system, and now they're home, probably expecting a medal.

This whole "Multinational Security Support" mission sounds like a fancy name for a glorified escort service for a government that can't even control its own streets. And now we're told about this new "Gang Suppression Force," 5,500 strong, with Kenya still pulling the strings. Because clearly, the first try was such a roaring success, we need more money, more people, and more Kenyan involvement. It's like watching a bad Nollywood sequel, but with real guns and real money being thrown around.

This GSF, apparently backed by the US and Panama, is supposed to be the big solution. But surprise, surprise, the 'resource constraints' are already showing. Kenyan personnel are still the backbone, months after this supposedly new entity was launched. It’s the same old song and dance. We send our people, they get caught in the crossfire, and we're left wondering what it was all for. Reminds me of those talks about [The Singapore Delusion: Ruto’s Big Dreams and Kenya’s Empty Pockets](/posts/the-singapore-delusion--ruto-s-big-dreams-and-kenya-s-empty-pockets) - all grand promises, very little tangible outcome.

The UN Security Council gives it the nod, mandating them to "reclaim gang-controlled territory." Sounds brave on paper. In reality, it's a dangerous game of cat and mouse in a country teetering on the edge. And who's to say our officers are even equipped for this kind of mess? We're good at chasing down petty criminals here in Nairobi, not fighting heavily armed cartels in a foreign land.

The Interior Principal Secretary was out there, spouting platitudes about professionalism and courage. Of course he was. Someone has to pretend this is all going according to plan. But let's be real, these deployments are a drain on our resources and, more importantly, on our officers' lives. And for what? So a few international bodies can tick a box and say they're 'doing something'?

At the end of the day, it’s just another round of Kenyan tax money and Kenyan lives being sent off to solve problems that aren't ours, in places that are already beyond saving. And when it all inevitably goes sideways, who do you think will be left holding the bag? Not the international bigwigs, that's for sure.
