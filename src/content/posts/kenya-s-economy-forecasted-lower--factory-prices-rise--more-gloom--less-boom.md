So, Brian from The Kenyan Wall Street is here to tell us what our dwindling bank accounts already know. The Treasury, in its infinite wisdom, has decided Kenya will grow slower than they thought back in 2026. Apparently, all those faraway wars and supply chain hiccups are to blame. Funny how they can still find glittery bits like lower interest rates and a "resurgent" stock market to distract you, while the real world keeps biting. They're setting us up for slower growth, but at least it’s not because everything's falling apart here at home. Yet.

Remember that brief period when manufacturers weren't bleeding money? Well, that window has slammed shut. Factory-gate prices are creeping up again, thanks to fuel and transport costs that never seem to go down. These businesses are finally realizing they can't just absorb these hits forever, so expect to pay more for everything. Whether this is just a blip or the start of a proper price hike for us consumers is the million-dollar question. Just don't expect them to be as generous as HELB scammers who are giving more effort than your favorite 'soft life' influencers.

And just when you thought the government might actually be on top of something, they declare they’ve stopped accumulating new pending bills. This is supposed to be a "major milestone" in fiscal discipline. We’ll see about that. Meanwhile, they’re still grappling with how to regulate digital platforms, debating whether to copy Europe’s playbook or actually look at what’s happening in our own backyard. Because, you know, evidence is so last season.

Then there's Airtel Money, which is apparently no longer a side gig but a star waiting for its big break on the London Stock Exchange. They're boasting about millions of customers and growing revenues, hoping investors will fall for the hype. We'll see how much they're actually willing to sell and if anyone believes their lofty valuations. Good luck to them.

On a slightly less depressing note, SBM Bank Kenya seems to be doing alright, with profits jumping and loans exceeding a decent chunk of change. And Nairobi is apparently getting an underground railway. Maybe one day we'll actually get somewhere on time. And let’s not forget the AI buzz. ALX Enterprise is hosting an executive breakfast on "Leading in the Age of AI." Because nothing says "future-proofing" like a fancy breakfast.
