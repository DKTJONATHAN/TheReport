Oh darlings, gather around because the desperation for a green card is real. Our very own former Orlando Pirates starlet, Mbekezeli Mbokazi, finally made his home debut for Chicago Fire, and the PR machine is working overtime to make it sound like a Grammy-winning performance. The 20-year-old started his first home match of the season against CF Montreal, trying to convince us that moving to the Windy City was a career upgrade and not just a way to escape the brutal South African tabloids.

The drama at Soldier Field was more intense than a leaked voice note from a Kenyan socialite. Chicago managed a 3-0 win, but let us be honest, it was messy. Jonathan Dean got himself a red card after a VAR review in the 56th minute, leaving the team to scramble like a C-list influencer when the ring light breaks. It is all just another layer of the fake-it-till-you-make-it culture we see everywhere, from the pitch to the streets where [The New Wash Wash 2.0: How Nairobi Scammers Trade Fake Gold for AI Deepfakes](/posts/new-wash-wash-20-nairobi-scammers-ai-deepfakes-mulot) has become the latest trend for those desperate for a quick come-up.

While the Men in Red were busy playing defense for 40 minutes, our boy Mbokazi was out there trying to look busy. Fotmob gave him a 7.6 rating, which is basically the participation trophy of the sports world. Sure, he had five clearances and five recoveries, but can we talk about that passing? Only 21 out of 32 attempts found their target. A 66 percent accuracy rate? Honey, if a singer missed that many notes during a live set, we would be booing them off the stage before the first chorus.

Coach Gregg Berhalter might be smiling for the cameras after his first home win, but the cracks are showing. Hugo Cuypers and Jonathan Bamba had to save the day with late goals because the defense was basically a "God help us" situation. Chicago is heading to face Columbus Crew next, where the odds of them winning are about as high as a one-hit wonder topping the charts twice. Mbokazi needs to buckle down in training if he wants to stay in the starting XI, because in this industry, you are only as good as your last viral moment.

At the end of the day, it is just another Sunday in the life of a player trying to maintain the "I have made it" aesthetic on Instagram while fighting for scraps in a league that barely knows his name. We see you, Mbekezeli. We see the hustle, the misses, and the desperate need for Gregg's approval. Stay tuned, because if this performance is any indication, the next match against D.C. United is going to be a masterclass in survival or a total flop.
