DIPLOMATIC BLUNDER: SULUHU REPRIMANDS RUTO OVER 'TANGA REFINERY' GAFFE

Ruto's grand plans hit a sour note as Tanzanian President Samia Suluhu openly questions his unilateral announcement of a refinery project, exposing a serious diplomatic misstep.

Ruto welcomed to State House, Dar es Salaam, by President Samia Suluhu at the start of his two-day State Visit to Tanzania.

Politics
Kenya, Tanzania, Diplomacy
Gaffe

Blunder

Well, well, well, seems our President Ruto can't even announce a business deal without stepping on somebody's toes. This whole "Tanga refinery" saga is just another classic example of Kenyan swagger meeting East African reality. President Samia Suluhu Hassan, no fool she, called him out publicly for announcing a mega oil project in her backyard without so much as a "by your leave." Apparently, our man was too busy with his infrastructure financing conferences, hobnobbing with Ugandan President Museveni and that Nigerian oil baron Dangote, to remember basic diplomacy. It’s like he forgot Tanzania isn’t just another county to be bossed around.

The whole thing was a show, wasn't it? Suluhu’s brief, unscripted jab in Kiswahili, questioning why she wasn't in the loop about a refinery planned for Tanga, landed like a ton of bricks. It was a subtle but pointed reminder that even with all the talk of regional cooperation and industrialisation, some decisions still need to be made with, you know, the host country's consent. Ruto’s subsequent apology, offering to build it in Mombasa if he'd known, was just a clumsy attempt to smooth things over, but the damage was done. You can almost hear the whispers in Dar es Salaam about Kenyan arrogance.

Ruto’s defense about this being a "big opportunity" for Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, and South Sudan, and his sermon about not exporting raw materials and retaining jobs, sounds good on paper. But when you announce it to the world before consulting your neighbours, it just smells like a power play. This is the same old story, isn’t it? Kenya trying to be the big brother, dictating terms. We saw it with that Afcon bid debacle, [Sh14.47bn Deficit: Kenya's Afcon Dreams Crumble Under Budget Black Hole](/posts/sh14-47bn-deficit--kenya-s-afcon-dreams-crumble-under-budget-black-hole), where our own lack of planning and coordination cost us. Why should this refinery be any different?

He even had the nerve to call the Tanzanian public "lucky" to be discussing a refinery in Tanga. Lucky? Really? That's the kind of patronising talk that gets people riled up. And let's not forget the whispers about this aligning with Aliko Dangote's own plans - maybe this was just a convenient announcement to get the ball rolling for his interests, using Ruto as the mouthpiece. The push for local refining is understandable, especially with oil discoveries in Turkana, but doing it this way just breeds "unnecessary suspicion," as he himself put it, ironically.

The notion that this won't affect Uganda's Hoima refinery is also a bit rich. Ruto dismisses it as "no headache," but when you're talking about a regional hub and consolidating oil resources, competition and implications are inevitable. It’s all about who benefits and who controls the narrative. This whole saga just highlights the persistent disputes and lack of trust that plague regional cooperation. Maybe next time, instead of grand announcements at conferences, he should try a quiet chat over chai with President Suluhu. It might save everyone a lot of embarrassment.
