So, the Controller of Budget, Margaret Nyakang’o, has blown the lid off another county shambles. Apparently, between July last year and March this year, our esteemed devolved units collectively decided to ditch digital payrolls - the ones taxpayers actually fund for efficiency - and shove Sh5.19 billion into manual payments. Because, you know, why use a system when you can have a party?

Forty-three counties, the report says, are playing hide-and-seek with the money. Some are even dishing out cash with vouchers, a method so ripe for abuse it’s practically an invitation for ghost workers to collect their monthly dues. Samburu, Garissa, and Kirinyaga are apparently the "best performing." Best at what? Best at pretending to be efficient while the rest of the country is left wondering where the development funds went? It's a classic case of [The Broad-Based Buffet: When Enemies Share a Plate While You Starve](/posts/the-broad-based-buffet--when-enemies-share-a-plate-while-you-starve).

This report is a slap in the face to anyone who still believes these devolved units are a path to progress. Instead, it highlights a system seemingly designed to siphon off public cash. It’s not about priorities; it’s about how much can be skimmed before anyone notices. And of course, with politicians constantly looking for ways to grease their own palms, the EACC’s warning about branding leading to prosecution is just noise in the wind. They’ll just rebrand their corruption.

Meanwhile, we’re stuck with the illusion that we’re getting value for our taxes. Just like the [Switch 2 USB-C Docks: The Illusion of Choice for the Gullible Gamer](/posts/switch-2-usb-c-docks--the-illusion-of-choice-for-the-gullible-gamer), this whole "devolution" thing feels like a carefully crafted deception. They promise us progress, but deliver ghost workers and empty coffers. The only thing they're good at importing is debt and excuses.

So, next time you hear about a new county project or a politician boasting about their achievements, remember this Sh5.2 billion manual payroll scandal. It's the real story. It’s the real cost of our governance. And it’s why your wallet feels perpetually lighter.
