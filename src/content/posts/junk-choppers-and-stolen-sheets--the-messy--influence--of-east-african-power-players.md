Oh, honey, pull up a chair because the "power couples" of East African politics are having a bit of a wardrobe malfunction, and by wardrobe, I mean their entire military inventory. Remember those Uganda People’s Defence Forces (UPDF) junk choppers? It is the ultimate lesson in "expectation vs. reality." While the media focuses on the boring numbers, let us talk about the pure, unadulterated audacity of trying to pass off scrap metal as a high-end defense flex. It is giving the same energy as a D-list socialite carrying a knock-off Birkin to a red carpet event.

And the drama does not stop at the hangar. We have Minister Matia Kasaija and Henry Musasizi apparently living their best "Home and Garden" lives using iron sheets meant for the vulnerable. Honestly, the hypocrisy is stunning even for my cynical standards. It is one thing to be a messy celebrity, but it is quite another to build your brand on stolen roofing material. They are complaining about not being prosecuted while their colleagues take the fall, proving once again that in this industry, it is not about what you did, but who you know in the front row.

Meanwhile, Washington is acting like the ultimate bouncer at the global club. They just designated Solomy Balungi Bossa and Rwanda’s army chief Vincent Nyakarundi as "persona non grata." It is essentially the diplomatic version of being blacklisted from the VIP lounge. Washington says they want to make the "licit trade" more appealing, which is just a fancy way of telling these guys that their side-hustles in the arms market are officially out of style.

This whole situation reminds me of my time documenting the [Doha Diaries: When the Glitter Meets the Grime and the Embassy Panics](/posts/doha-diaries--when-the-glitter-meets-the-grime-and-the-embassy-panics) because the pattern is always the same. Behind the shiny suits and the official press releases, there is always a layer of grit and desperation. Whether it is arms deals in the shadows or iron sheets in a backyard, the goal is always the same: maintain the facade of power while the foundation is literally rusting away.

At the end of the day, these players are just influencers with bigger budgets and deadlier toys. They want the glamor of the global stage without following the rules of the game. But as we have seen with the junk choppers, you can only fly on hot air and stolen goods for so long before the entire thing comes crashing down. Stay messy, East Africa, because I will always be here to catch the debris.
