So, Millicent Omanga has finally decided to grace us with her profound political insights. Apparently, three years ago, she saw the writing on the wall - or maybe it was just the dust from all those unfulfilled manifesto promises that made her eyes water. Now she's jumped ship, trading Ruto's sinking vessel for Rigathi Gachagua's shiny new Democracy for the Citizens Party (DCP). Because who needs principles when you have options, right?

She claims it's all about a "steady erosion of principles." Funny, I thought that was just the standard operating procedure in Kenyan politics. It's like complaining about pigeons in Nairobi - yes, they're there, and they're everywhere, but what did you really expect? It makes you wonder what she was expecting in the first place. Perhaps she was hoping for some kind of political fairy tale where leaders actually deliver on their word. Bless her heart.

This whole saga reminds me of those influencer types who get caught up in the drama. Remember that Master Wong character who thought being viral meant you were untouchable? Then bam, straight to a police cell. Just goes to show, the show must go on, but sometimes the curtains fall pretty hard. Omanga’s move is just another act in the never-ending Kenyan political theatre, full of sound and fury, signifying… well, you know.

And let's not forget how often "Ruto" keywords dominate the online conversation, especially when things get messy. It's almost as if the public has a radar for these sorts of political gymnastics. The TikTok algorithm, for instance, is probably having a field day with this latest defection, churning out content that perfectly captures the fickle nature of political allegiance in this country. It’s all just a game, really.

One wonders what Gachagua is promising her. More airtime? A front-row seat at the next political rally? Or perhaps just a listening ear, which is more than she apparently got from Ruto. The truth is, in politics, it’s rarely about ideology. It’s about who’s offering the better deal, who has the more promising platform, or, let's be honest, who can offer the next best opportunity to stay in the game.

So, here's to Millicent Omanga, the latest defector in Kenya's never-ending political merry-go-round. May her new political home be less… principled, and more profitable. Because at the end of the day, isn't that what this is all about? Selling our secrets for a ten percent surcharge, or in this case, a better political gig.
