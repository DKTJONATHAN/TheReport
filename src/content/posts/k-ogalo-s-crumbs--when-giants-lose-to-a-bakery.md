So, Gor Mahia finally found a way to make us laugh without even trying. Losing 1-0 to a team called BB Bread is not a "thrilling upset," it is a cry for help. Imagine being a "heavyweight" and getting sliced and served by a Nairobi East regional league side. It is embarrassing. At this rate, the only thing rising in Kenyan football is the dough in the BB Bread ovens, because K'Ogalo's reputation is currently flatter than a chapati left out in the Embakasi sun.

The tournament organizers want us to call this "unpredictable" and "inspiring." I call it a total lack of standards. When a regional side eliminates both AFC Leopards and Gor Mahia back-to-back, it does not mean the minnows are world-class. It means our top-tier clubs are basically social clubs with better jerseys. These guys are following in the footsteps of Compel, another "giant killer," which is just code for a team that actually bothered to show up while the pros were busy thinking about their next salary advance.

Then we have the so-called "dominant performances." Ulinzi Stars hitting Zoo FC for seven and Nairobi United putting eight past BMC Allstars? That is not football; that is a crime against the spirit of the game. It is easy to look like prime Barcelona when you are playing against guys who probably spent their morning at a construction site or balancing a ledger. It is typical Kenyan bullying - pick on the weakest person in the room so you can pretend you are a king for ninety minutes.

Honestly, these [HELB Scammers Are Giving More Effort Than Your Favorite 'Soft Life' Influencers](/posts/helb-scammers-are-giving-more-effort-than-your-favorite--soft-life--influencers) and certainly more than the Gor Mahia midfield did on Sunday. At least the scammers have a goal and a consistent work ethic. Watching "former champions" like Bandari struggle to squeeze a 2-1 win over Bumbani Stars tells you everything you need to know about the quality gap. It is non-existent. Our football system is just a collection of teams waiting for the next bankruptcy notice.

Kuta Collections is now the only non-league side left, keeping the "spirit" of the cup alive. Let us be real - they are just the next lamb for the slaughter once they face a team with a fitness coach. While the FKF talks about "developing and promoting the beautiful game," most of us are just watching a slow-motion car crash in a muddy field. The Round of 16 is coming, but do not expect glory. Expect more mediocrity wrapped in a fancy sponsor's logo.
