So, the esteemed opposition leaders, including Gachagua and Musyoka, decided to grace Kikuyu town with their presence. Big mistake. Turns out, the local MP, Ichung’wah, decided he'd rather have a quiet day and called in the riot squad. Tear gas flew, cars got dented, and supporters scattered like roaches when the lights come on. Classic Nairobi politics - turn a rally into a tear gas convention.

Naturally, Gachagua, being the dramatic type, decided to hold court on the highway, complete with a bulletproof vest and a helmet. Because nothing screams "man of the people" like looking like you're about to invade a small country. He blamed Ichung'wah, of course. Who else? It's always someone else's fault when the plan goes south, isn't it?

But the real stars of the show were the "goons" who decided to capitalize on the chaos. While the police were busy being tear-gassing enthusiasts, these charming individuals were busy extorting money from motorists on the Southern Bypass. Because why not add a little petty crime to the political drama? It’s like a free-for-all, and everyone’s a winner, especially the thugs. This whole scene is a stark reminder of the dark underbelly of viral AI fruit videos and the predictable misogyny that often follows; sometimes, the real drama isn't online.

The whole day was a masterclass in how not to run a country. The opposition couldn't hold their meeting, businesses shut down, and the roads turned into a war zone, or at least a very expensive parking lot thanks to the goons. By evening, Kikuyu town was still firmly in the grip of disorder, with tear gas, gunfire, and a healthy dose of lawlessness. Just another Saturday in Kenya, where promises are cheap and chaos is always on the menu.

Funny how both sides are pointing fingers. Gachagua wrote a letter to the Inspector General, warning of a plot. Ichung'wah, in turn, accused Gachagua of orchestrating the whole thing. It's a classic he-said, he-said, with the citizens caught in the middle, coughing and paying off thugs. Apparently, "peaceful meetings" are a foreign concept when political egos are involved.

And the media? Oh, they got their share of the fun too. Tear gas canisters to the lap, guns pointed at drivers, threats of shooting. Just another day at the office, documenting the glorious incompetence and violence that passes for politics here. Truly inspiring stuff.
