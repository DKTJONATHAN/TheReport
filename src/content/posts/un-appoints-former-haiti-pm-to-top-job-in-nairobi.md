UN Appoints Ex-Haiti PM Conille to Top Nairobi Job, Because Apparently Stability is Overrated

So the UN, in its infinite wisdom, has decided to parachute a former Haitian Prime Minister, Garry Conille, into Nairobi as their new Resident Coordinator. Apparently, Dr. Stephen Jackson's tenure was just *too* stable, and we needed a shake-up. This is the same Conille who was Prime Minister of Haiti for a grand total of six months in 2024, during a period that sounds like a dumpster fire of epic proportions. Gangs, governance crises, the whole shebang. And Kenya, bless its heart, even sent cops to help him clean up the mess. Now he’s here, coordinating things. Just what we needed.

Conille, they say, brings "over two decades of experience." Which, translated from UN-speak, probably means he’s been shuffled around enough desks to know how to fill out a form. He’s been a Resident Coordinator before, in Jamaica and Burundi. Let's just hope Kenya doesn't end up looking like those places. His UNICEF gig, where he supposedly "spearheaded programmes," sounds suspiciously like more paperwork and photo ops. But hey, at least he’s got that international development flavour, right? Just like those Kenyan influencers who are trading brain cells for clout in the whole [Solai Settlement Saga](/posts/the-solai-settlement-saga--why-kenyan-influencers-are-trading-brain-cells-for-clout).

Remember when Conille jetted into Nairobi in October 2024, hobnobbing with President Ruto about strengthening security? All that talk about the Multinational Security Support mission for Haiti, the one Kenya was apparently leading the charge on? Well, that whole mission is now being restructured, with Kenyan officers already heading home. It’s almost as if sending a bunch of our boys into a raging inferno wasn't the best long-term strategy. But don't worry, Chad will be taking over. Because, you know, that always works out so smoothly.

The kicker? Conille was impeached and booted from his PM gig in Haiti a month after that little Kenyan visit. Impeached! By his own Transitional Presidential Council! Because of "political infighting and power struggles." This is the man the UN has entrusted with coordinating their operations in *our* country. It’s like hiring a chef who’s famous for burning down his own kitchen to run a Michelin-starred restaurant. I'm sure his extensive experience in leadership during crises will be *invaluable* here. After all, we've got so few of those ourselves, haven't we?

This whole appointment just screams "we're out of good ideas." Conille's track record is less "saving the world" and more "navigating a sinking ship." But hey, at least he'll be getting paid. And who knows, maybe he’ll bring some of that Haitian "governance crisis" expertise with him. We’re always open to new ways of doing things, especially when they involve more bureaucracy and less actual progress. Just another day in paradise, I suppose.
