CAF Shouts "Kenya's Afcon Prep A Mess"

CAF finds Kenya's Afcon venues unacceptable, refurbishment stalled, and foreign player recruitment intensifying. The country is falling behind in its co-hosting bid.

CAF has officially thrown a wrench in Kenya's dreams of co-hosting the 2027 Africa Cup of Nations. It seems our proposed venues are about as ready as a politician before an election - full of promises and empty in reality. The Confederation of African Football, the big wigs in charge, has apparently looked at our stadiums and declared them "not good enough." Shocking, I know. After all, who needs proper facilities when you have ambition, right?

And while CAF is busy pointing fingers, our own Football Kenya Federation (FKF) is frantically trying to patch things up. They're apparently going all out to poach players of foreign descent, as if that's going to magically fix crumbling infrastructure. It’s a classic Kenyan move: a big announcement, followed by a scramble to look like we know what we’re doing, all while the actual work is either delayed or non-existent. This whole situation is starting to feel as predictable as [Ruto's Ministry Shuffle: A Sh13.5 Billion Disaster for Public Service Elites](/posts/ruto-s-ministry-shuffle--a-sh13-5-billion-disaster-for-public-service-elites).

The refurbishment works, the ones supposed to make our stadiums "up to standard," have apparently hit a roadblock. Stalled. Just like so many other grand plans in this country. It’s almost as if the money for these projects has vanished into thin air, a common occurrence when it comes to public funds. We're supposed to be co-hosting a major continental event, but it looks more like we're just hoping for the best, a strategy that rarely pays off.

All signs point to Kenya being woefully behind schedule. Uganda and Tanzania are probably looking at us and shaking their heads, wondering if they partnered with the right country. The next four months are supposedly "make-or-break," which is rich considering we've already broken whatever chance we had by not doing the work when we had the time. It's a familiar story, a tale of ambition outpacing capability, and a lot of grandstanding with very little substance.
