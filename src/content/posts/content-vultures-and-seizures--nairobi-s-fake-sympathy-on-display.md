Nairobi is a city that eats its own, and if you need proof, look no further than the launch of the so-called Bahati Empire. Jackson Ngechu Makini, the man we all know as CMB Prezzo, recently went on a podcast to describe how he nearly checked out while the "crème de la crème" of the city watched through their phone screens. It was not just a simple faint; it was a full-blown seizure. While Prezzo was on the floor fighting for his life, the glitterati were busy adjusting their focus and checking their lighting.

The desperation for relevance in this town has reached a terminal stage. It is the same flavor of hunger we see [From Sidelines to Search Engines: The Desperate Pivot of the Multi-Hyphenate Reporter](/posts/from-sidelines-to-search-engines--the-desperate-pivot-of-the-multi-hyphenate-reporter) where everyone is chasing a click to stay afloat. Prezzo admitted he did not even know what was happening until he woke up with a bitten tongue and saw the worried faces of the few people who actually cared. In a room full of "friends" and industry peers, empathy was a rare commodity.

Thank God for 2Mbili, who apparently was the only one in the building with a functioning moral compass. The comedian had to play security guard and human shield, grabbing a banner to cover Prezzo so the content-hungry vultures would stop recording a man at his most vulnerable. 2Mbili even had to safeguard the rapper's watch and phone, because in Nairobi, if the seizure does not kill you, the onlookers will certainly rob you blind before the ambulance arrives.

Prezzo’s realization that people "felt nothing" is the most honest thing to come out of that fake-glitz event. It is a cynical cycle that never ends. While Prezzo was seizing, other "celebrities" like Fridah Mwaka are out here reminding us that politicians used to lure them with Ksh 2.5 million cars. It is all part of the same ecosystem of greed. Whether it is a politician buying a news anchor or a guest filming a veteran rapper’s medical emergency, the goal is the same: leverage someone else for your own gain.

This is the Nairobi reality. You are only as good as the content you provide, even if that content is your own potential death. Prezzo survived the seizure, but the image of those glowing phone screens recording his struggle should be a wake-up call. If you fall down in this city, do not expect a hand up; expect to be tagged in a "Breaking News" post before you even regain consciousness. The "Empire" Bahati is building is clearly made of cold stone and even colder hearts.
