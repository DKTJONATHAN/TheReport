Another day, another script from State House that reads like it was translated from a DC memo. While the Middle East turns into a literal furnace, President Ruto has decided to play the role of the grieving bystander, conveniently forgetting how the fire started. His March 2 statement condemning strikes on the UAE, Qatar, and the rest of the Gulf gang was a masterclass in selective amnesia. He listed the victims like a roll call at a funeral but somehow forgot to mention the guy holding the matches or the ones who fanned the flames in the first place.

It takes a special kind of "statesman" to ignore the fact that Iran was retaliating after the US-Israel offensive took out their Supreme Leader. But here in Nairobi, we don't do context; we do what the donors tell us. By omitting any mention of the US and Israel, Ruto isn't being a peacemaker. He is just making sure his Western credit score stays high enough to secure the next round of loans. It is the same old story: selling our voice to the highest bidder while pretending we are "neutral" leaders on the global stage.

This obsession with looking good for the international community while our own house is a mess is becoming a national brand. We are so busy trying to mimic the polished facades of foreign powers that we ignore the rot at home. We have seen this before, where [The Singapore Dream is Just a Nairobi Nightmare in Disguise](/posts/the-singapore-dream-is-just-a-nairobi-nightmare-in-disguise) serves as a reminder that importing foreign visions usually ends with us being the ones footed with the bill. We are chasing a seat at the big table, but we are clearly just there to serve the drinks.

If you listen closely to the silence in Ruto's statement, you can hear the gears of the IMF grinding. You don't bite the hand that feeds you, even if that hand is currently busy lighting a regional inferno. Calling out Iran while ignoring the Western aggression that triggered the response isn't diplomacy; it's a PR stunt for the Pentagon. It's embarrassing to watch a sovereign nation act like a ventriloquist's dummy, speaking words that aren't its own.

Let's stop pretending Kenya is a "neutral arbiter" in these conflicts. We are a country in debt, and in the world of global politics, the debtor doesn't get to have an opinion that isn't pre-approved. As the Gulf burns and the body count rises, don't expect any truth from State House. Expect more of this sterile, one-sided commentary that keeps the dollar flowing and the reality at bay. We aren't leading; we are just following the trail of breadcrumbs left by our masters.
