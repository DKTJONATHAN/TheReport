Oh, darling, the irony is just dripping, and for once, it is not just the Nairobi rain. Our favorite Kenyan singer-songwriter has crawled out of her year-long hibernation to drop an EP titled 'Eternal.' She is calling it a 'quieter and more intentional sonic pivot.' In industry speak, that usually translates to 'the label cut the budget and I can no longer afford the dancers.' It is cute how these artists pretend they are making a soul-searching choice when the mainstream simply stopped streaming their loud, auto-tuned messes.

While she is busy being intentional in a dry studio, at least 42 Kenyans have been swept away by the actual water. The government finally woke up on Sunday to admit the death toll nearly doubled, thanks to the flash floods that turned Jomo Kenyatta International Airport into a very expensive swimming pool. It is hard to focus on a 'quiet' EP when the sound of 172 vehicles being recovered from floodwaters is the only thing trending. But hey, as long as the aesthetic is 'Eternal,' who cares if the commute is underwater, right?

Our very own President William Ruto has ordered relief food from the strategic reserves, which is hilarious because we all know those reserves are as empty as a socialite’s promises. It is the same old script: tragedy hits, the military gets deployed for a photo op, and the big man pretends he is in control. While he acts like [Washington's Choirboy: Ruto's Selective Memory in the Gulf Inferno](/posts/washington-s-choirboy--ruto-s-selective-memory-in-the-gulf-inferno), his citizens are literally being swept away by rivers bursting their banks. It is a masterclass in performative leadership.

If the rain does not kill the vibe, the fuel prices certainly will. Thanks to the drama in the Middle East, oil prices are set to skyrocket again. This means your favorite influencers will soon be posting 'throwback' photos because they cannot afford the gas to drive to a new location for content. From Kenya to Ghana, everyone is feeling the pinch as currencies weaken. Even the cocoa farmers in Ghana are giving up on chocolate to lease their land to illegal miners. Apparently, being a 'hustler' now means destroying the soil just to buy a bag of rice.

And speaking of mess, West Africa is giving us a political soap opera that no one asked for. Guinea's Mamady Doumbouya just dissolved 40 political parties because they forgot to file their homework-I mean, financial statements. Opposition leader Cellou Dalein Diallo is calling for 'direct resistance' from his comfortable exile, which is easy to say when you are not the one facing the tear gas. It is all just a cycle of coups, contested terms, and 'resistance' videos on TikTok.

Meanwhile, Tunisia is planning to drop 3 billion dinars on an airport expansion while the rest of the continent is struggling to keep the lights on. They want to move 18.5 million travelers by 2031, probably so people can escape the rising costs of living even faster. Whether it is soldiers getting caught in crossfires in Lebanon or civilians fleeing Akobo in South Sudan, the world is a mess, but at least we have a 'quiet and intentional' EP to listen to while the ship sinks. Stay messy, everyone.
