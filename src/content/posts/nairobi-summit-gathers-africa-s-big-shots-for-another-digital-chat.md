So, Africa’s top suits and tech nerds are all converging on Nairobi for the Connected Africa Summit 2026. From April 27th to 30th, The Edge Convention Centre will be buzzing with speeches, promises, and probably a lot of lukewarm coffee. Apparently, this is the "most senior gathering focused on the digital economy this year." Which translates to: the people who talk about change will be talking about change, while the rest of us keep grinding.

This year’s theme is “Uniting Africa’s Innovation for an Inclusive Digital Market.” Sounds nice, doesn’t it? Like a unicorn farting rainbows. They keep going on about how this edition builds on past themes like "unlocking growth beyond connectivity" and "vision to reality." The real kicker this year? "Measurable delivery and sustained action." Let’s see how long that lasts before it devolves into the usual corporate waffle. AI, DPI, and cybersecurity are the buzzwords, because apparently, we're still surprised these are important.

They’re talking about moving “beyond dialogue into implementation,” aligning investments, and strengthening collaboration. It’s the same old song and dance, just with shinier buzzwords. Jessy Maruti from the ICT Authority is quoted saying, "Africa cannot fully unlock its digital potential while operating in fragmented systems." You don’t say! Maybe they should start by fixing the potholes on Waiyaki Way. They’re even launching a Connected Africa Secretariat, chaired by Kenya, to ensure "continuity, accountability, and long-term collaboration." Good luck with that.

William Kabogo, bless his heart, is assuring us that the "global digital economy presents immense opportunity for our youth." Yes, the same youth who are still struggling with basic internet access in many areas. And Huawei, bless their tech-obsessed souls, is all about "talent development" and supporting universities. It's all very noble, but it reminds me of how companies often talk about empowering the masses while quietly consolidating their own power. It’s a bit like watching a tech giant touting its "ethical AI" while simultaneously being investigated for data misuse. The digital panopticon is real, folks.

Of course, Safaricom’s Frankline Okata is also chiming in about "ideas to execution" and "measurable impact." And the likes of MIDIS Group and Hewlett Packard Enterprise are partnering up, ready to offer their "scalable and sustainable IT solutions." It’s a regular tech circus, with everyone promising to lead Africa into a digitally empowered future. Let’s just hope they remember to bring some actual change this time, and not just more brochures and networking events. The question is, will this summit finally bridge the gap between their pronouncements and the reality on the ground, or will it just be another expensive talking shop?
