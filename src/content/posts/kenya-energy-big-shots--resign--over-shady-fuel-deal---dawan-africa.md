KENYA ENERGY BIG SHOTS "RESIGN" OVER SHADY FUEL DEAL - SAME OLD SONG AND DANCE

THEY STOLE KSH 4 BILLION AND SOLD US GARBAGE FUEL. NOW THEY'RE "RESIGNING." WE SHOULD ALL BE SHOCKED.

Top energy honchos, Daniel Kiptoo of EPRA and Joe Sang of KPC, plus PS Mohamed Liban, have suddenly decided to pack their bags. Apparently, the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) finally caught up with them over this "controversial fuel importation deal." It’s almost as if they thought we wouldn’t notice KSh 4 billion vanishing into thin air, along with fuel that could probably rust a car.

The details are as murky as the fuel they’re peddling. A ship, the MV Paloma, apparently offloaded some sub-standard, overpriced junk into our supply chain. The Office of the President, in its infinite wisdom, confirmed President Ruto has "received" their resignations. Convenient, isn't it? Just when the heat gets too much, they decide to gracefully exit, probably to count their ill-gotten gains in peace.

This isn’t exactly a shocker. We’ve seen this movie before, haven’t we? It’s the same old story of greed and questionable deals, leaving ordinary Kenyans to foot the bill. It makes you wonder if any of these "handshakes" with foreign dignitaries actually result in anything tangible for us, or if it's just more smoke and mirrors. We’ve got folks drowning in floods and now we're expected to fuel our cars with toxic waste. It's like the folks in charge are living in a different Kenya entirely.

And it doesn't stop with the top dogs. Oh no. The DCI is sniffing around for others, including a deputy director and a senior KPC manager. The net is "widening," they say. Let’s hope this isn’t just another performance for the cameras before everyone forgets and moves on to the next crisis. Because frankly, this whole "staged smiles and lakeside revolutions" thing is getting tiresome.
