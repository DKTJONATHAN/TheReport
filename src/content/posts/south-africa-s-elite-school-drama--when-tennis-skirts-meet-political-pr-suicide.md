Honey, if you thought the music industry was messy, you clearly have not been watching the private school circuit in Johannesburg lately. We have a classic "Mean Girls" reboot happening, but with higher stakes and way more lawyers. It all started when the girls from Roedean decided they were not exactly "vibing" with a tennis match against King David High School. While the girls wanted a quiet boycott to protest the school's heavy-handed Zionist branding, the adults decided to turn it into a high-octane PR disaster that has everyone on social media picking sides.

These Roedean students were not just being difficult; they were reacting to the fact that King David looks more like a military compound than an educational institution. Last time they visited, it was all military-grade weapons and political posters at the gates. It is like trying to master [The Nairobi Math: How to 'Walk' 500km Without Losing Your Breath](/posts/the-nairobi-math--how-to--walk--500km-without-losing-your-breath) - you are putting in a lot of mental effort just to end up somewhere you never wanted to be. The kids tried to walk away quietly, but the King David deputy principal jumped on a voice note faster than a thirst-trapping influencer, screaming "antisemitism" before anyone could even find their racket.

Enter Phuti Mogale, the now-former principal of Roedean, who clearly missed the "How to Spot a Trap" seminar. In a move that defines amateur hour, she called her counterpart at King David to "pick her brain" about why her students were uncomfortable. Newsflash, honey: in this industry, nobody is picking your brain; they are just looking for a reason to record you for the receipts. And that is exactly what happened. The King David headmistress, Lorraine Srage, caught Mogale fumbling her words on tape and used it to fuel a narrative that Roedean was the villain of the week.

The fallout was predictable and frankly, exhausting to watch. Roedean did what every panicked institution does when they are scared of losing their sponsors: they offered up their Black headmistress as a sacrificial lamb. Mogale resigned, the board chair stepped down, and the mainstream media swallowed the "antisemitism" bait hook, line, and sinker. It is the ultimate corporate "re-brand" where the actual issue - a school literally naming its sports houses after political figures - gets swept under the rug while everyone argues over the semantics of a leaked phone call.

At the end of the day, King David is operating less like a school and more like a PR machine for a specific ideology. They have the national anthem of another country playing at assemblies and students "cosplaying" as soldiers on school trips. But when anyone points out the hypocrisy of bringing that energy to a high school tennis match, they pull the ultimate "cancel culture" card. If these schools spent as much time on their backhand as they do on their legal threats, maybe we would actually see some sport instead of this scripted drama.
