'I Completely Lost My Temper!' Uli Hoeneß Couldn't Stop Bayern's Medical Meltdown

Bayern Munich's legendary doctor, Hans-Wilhelm Müller-Wohlfahrt, is dredging up old drama, crying foul about his fallout with Pep Guardiola back in 2015. Apparently, after a "terrible day" losing to Porto, Pep pointed fingers at the good doctor for the squad's long injury list. As if the players themselves had nothing to do with it.

Müller-Wohlfahrt, now 83, whines in *Der Spiegel* about being blamed in front of the whole team. He calls it "utterly absurd," claiming he wasn't going to accept being scapegoated. The man even admits to losing his temper, shouting at Pep and banging his fist on the table so hard the crockery rattled. Sounds like a mature professional handling a high-pressure situation, doesn't it?

The fallout led to the doctor and his entire medical entourage walking out after 38 years. He blames the absence of Uli Hoeneß, who was busy with tax evasion charges, for the whole mess. Apparently, if the big boss had been around, Pep would have just swallowed his pride. Funny how rich people always find someone else to blame. It reminds me of the endless talk about leadership and how little actual accountability there is, whether it's in football or [Ruto Carries Africa's Voice From Nairobi to G7 - Don't Hold Your Breath](/posts/ruto-carries-africa-s-voice-from-nairobi-to-g7---don-t-hold-your-breath).

Guardiola, meanwhile, kept it classy, just saying he respected the doctor's decision. But behind the scenes, it was a power struggle from day one. Pep, the genius tactician, apparently thought he was joining a world-class medical setup, only to find "chronic injuries" and players supposedly taking too long to heal. He also apparently had a problem with Müller-Wohlfahrt not being a 24/7 club employee, preferring to jet around with Usain Bolt or the national team. Priorities, right?

The whole thing really kicked off with Thiago. Guardiola's golden boy got an injection from a Spanish doctor without the club doctor's okay, which, shocker, didn't help and led to another injury. Even Pep admitted it was a "big mistake." Then came the Benatia incident, where Guardiola sarcastically clapped at the medical team after an injury. It’s clear there was no trust, just a manager who thought he knew better than an experienced physician.

Müller-Wohlfahrt eventually came back in 2017, only to leave again three years later with barely a nod. He claims the "football business" has become colder, more impersonal, and that Bayern used to be like a family, unlike now. He’s heard things are better under Guardiola’s "protégé," Vincent Kompany, which makes him happy. Maybe some people just prefer a touchy-feely environment over winning trophies.
