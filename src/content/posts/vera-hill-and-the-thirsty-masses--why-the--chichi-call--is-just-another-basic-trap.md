Oh, look. Another day, another "leaked video" for the internet's most desperate to drool over. This time, the bait is Vera Hill, the Siargao-based lifestyle influencer known as ChiChi. The "ChiChi Call" is trending, and frankly, it is embarrassing how many of you think a girl with a clean record would suddenly have her private life spilled across Telegram and Reddit. Spoiler alert: there is no video. You are not getting a "full MMS," you are just getting played by scammers who know exactly how predictable your curiosity is.

This whole circus is a carbon copy of the fake "Pinay Gold Medalist" scandal that bored people were chasing a few months back. The script never changes, only the faces. Cybersecurity experts have confirmed that these links are just digital traps designed to harvest your data. If you are clicking on these "links in bio" or joining shady Telegram groups, you aren't just thirsty; you are a liability to your own privacy. It is the same tired hustle, and honestly, the lack of creativity from these scammers is almost as insulting as the scam itself.

When you click that suspicious link, you aren't watching a video; you are being watched. The page might look like a streaming site, but it is actually logging your IP address and begging for your Facebook login under the guise of "age verification." It is the digital equivalent of [Riggy G's New Gospel: Same Wolves, Different Sheepskin](/posts/riggy-g-s-new-gospel--same-wolves--different-sheepskin) where the predator just changes the outfit to catch the next batch of gullible followers. Some of these links even try to make you install "plugins" that are actually malware designed to record your keystrokes and drain your bank account while you search for non-existent drama.

Let us talk about the legal side, because being a gossip-monger is one thing, but being a criminal is another. Under India’s IT Act and the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, forwarding or creating non-consensual deepfakes or intimate content is a one-way ticket to a five-year prison sentence. Even just passing the link along to your group chat can land you in a mess of fines and legal drama. Vera Hill has zero history of controversy, yet her name is being dragged through the mud because people cannot resist a "leaked" headline.

The internet is a playground for the bored, but this trend is just plain dangerous. If you are still hunting for the "ChiChi Call" after being told it is a phishing scam, you probably deserve the malware coming your way. Treat influencers like actual human beings for once and stop feeding the cybercriminals who profit from your obsession with celebrity downfall. The only thing truly "leaked" here is your personal information to a basement-dweller in a hoodie.
