UDA is popping champagne again, claiming a "public vote of confidence" because they managed to grab Isiolo South and a few ward seats in Embu and Kakamega. Let us be real for a second. This was not a contest; it was a solo run. When the opposition is too "low in confidence" to even show up to the playground, of course the bully is going to take all the marbles. They call it a sweeping victory, but in the streets of Nairobi, we know it just means the machine is well-oiled while the rest of us are struggling to find grease for our own pans.

The so-called United Opposition is busy playing house in Kisii and Nyamira, coronating Fred Matiang-i as if a title and a fancy ceremony can stop the UDA steamroller. While they are distracted by internal power struggles and "strategic" tours hundreds of kilometres away, they leave the gates wide open. It is a pathetic display that reminds me of how [India Confronts Strategic Vulnerabilities in the Global Artificial Intelligence Race](/posts/india-confronts-strategic-vulnerabilities-in-the-global-artificial-intelligence-race) except instead of high-tech software, it is our own democratic process that is showing its fatal, low-tech flaws. If you do not even show up to the fight, do not complain when the other guy claims the belt by default.

This "Broad-Based Government" nonsense is just a polite way of saying the elites have decided to share the loot. Watching ODM split down the middle is the only entertainment we have left. On one side, you have the old guard like Senator Oburu Oginga trying to secure a retirement plan with UDA. On the other, you have the youthful faction led by Edwin Sifuna trying to keep the fire burning in a house that is already mostly ash. By the time their national delegates conference rolls around in March, there might not be a party left to decide a position for 2027.

Ruto might be smiling now, but these victories are hollow. They are built on low voter turnout and a populace that is more worried about the price of sugar than who sits in a county assembly seat in Western or Mount Kenya East. The IEBC is still the favorite whipping boy for the losers who claim vote rigging and state-sponsored violence, but the truth is simpler: people are tired. We are tired of the same names, the same broken promises, and the same "significant political showdowns" that change absolutely nothing for the person sitting in a matatu. If this is the "test of strength" for the next General Election, then we have all already lost.
