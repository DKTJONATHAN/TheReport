Ruto's Development Promises: Silencing Critics with Roads and Rhetoric

President William Ruto is once again making grand pronouncements about development projects, this time vowing to silence his critics with tangible results. He’s urging everyone to ditch tribalism and hate, painting himself as the great unifier, all while conveniently asking for your vote in 2027. It’s the same old song and dance, just with a fresh coat of tarmac.

The President’s message is clear: his administration is all about uniting Kenyans through development. He's accusing his opponents of peddling ethnicity and discrimination, conveniently forgetting the very nature of political campaigns. He claims to have formed a "broad-based government" to bring everyone together, a move that conveniently brought on board those he vehemently opposed just yesterday. It’s all about unity, of course, as long as it serves the agenda.

Ruto is touting infrastructure projects as his answer to criticism, pointing to roads like the Mto Mwagodi-Dawida-Mbale-Wundanyi-Bura Junction Road. He’s promising this 60-year-old wait will end by February, a direct jab at those who doubted him. He’s also pushing the narrative of connecting Kenya and its neighbours with a new railway line from Voi to Holili, eventually aiming for Bujumbura. It’s all about boosting transport, trade, and agriculture - the usual buzzwords to get people excited about concrete.

Meanwhile, Taita Taveta Governor Andrew Mwadime is singing the President’s praises, lauding the infrastructure investments. It's always good to see leaders giving credit where it's due, especially when it aligns with the current narrative. This kind of development could indeed change things significantly for the county, but let's see if these projects are more than just election season promises. Perhaps if the government focused more on delivering services rather than grand infrastructure projects that benefit a select few, we might see genuine progress, like we’ve seen with the strategic investments at places like [JKIA](/posts/why-modernising-and-expanding-jkia-is-a-strategic-investment).

Ruto Promises Development to Silence Critics

Ruto's claims of unity are convenient, and the focus on infrastructure conveniently distracts from other pressing issues.

Roads

President William Ruto, in his characteristic style, has declared that he will silence his critics with a barrage of development projects. His message, delivered amidst the unveiling of various infrastructure initiatives, is a familiar one: abandon tribalism and divisive politics, and embrace unity through tangible progress. He's painting himself as the ultimate unifier, conveniently forgetting that such grand pronouncements often precede a request for continued support, specifically for his 2027 re-election bid.

The President is making a strong case for his administration’s focus on uniting Kenyans through development, accusing his opponents of propagating political ethnicity, discrimination, and outright abuse. He claims his government is a "broad-based" entity designed to bring all Kenyans together, a sentiment that conveniently overlooks the political maneuvering involved. It's all about unity, he insists, as long as that unity serves his narrative.

Ruto is leaning heavily on infrastructure projects, like the Mto Mwagodi-Dawida-Mbale-Wundanyi-Bura Junction Road, as proof of his commitment. He’s promising swift completion for a road that has been in the pipeline for over 60 years, a direct challenge to those who have doubted his promises. The revival of the Voi to Holili railway line, aiming to connect Kenya with Tanzania and beyond, is another feather in his cap, promising to boost transport and trade. These are the kind of projects that look good on paper, and hopefully translate to actual improvements for citizens, much like strategic investments in national infrastructure such as [JKIA](/posts/why-modernising-and-expanding-jkia-is-a-strategic-investment).

Taita Taveta Governor Andrew Mwadime has added his voice, praising the President's commitment to infrastructure development. It's certainly heartening to see leaders acknowledging tangible progress. While these road and rail projects hold the promise of reviving transport, trade, and tourism, one can only hope they are more than just campaign optics and truly benefit the people they are intended to serve.
