In Nairobi, we have a saying that if a deal looks too good to be true, you are probably the product being sold. IG Douglas Kanja is currently making a lot of noise about "getting to the bottom" of a syndicate that sent Kenyan youths to the frontlines of the Russia-Ukraine war. It is the usual script: big promises of Sh350,000 monthly pay and foreign citizenship, only for the recruits to find themselves holding a rifle after three weeks of "training." We are not exporting tea and coffee anymore; we are exporting cannon fodder.

Festus Arasa Omwamba, the director of Global Face Limited, finally had his day in the Kahawa Law Courts after playing hide-and-seek at the Moyale border. He pleaded not guilty, of course. Prosecutors say he lured 22 victims with promises of "legitimate overseas employment." Instead of a desk job in a shiny office, these kids were sent to combat zones. Three came back with injuries that a Sh1.2 million bonus will never fix, and one did not come back at all.

What is truly sickening is how deep this rot goes. Majority Leader Kimani Ichung’wah spilled the beans in Parliament, claiming that rogue officials in government agencies and even diplomatic missions helped this happen. This is not just one "rogue" director; it is a systemic failure. It is just like the political games we see every day, where [The Orange Peels Off: Why 'Linda Mwananchi' is Just the Latest Political Rebrand](/posts/the-orange-peels-off--why--linda-mwananchi--is-just-the-latest-political-rebrand) exposes how these sharks just swap skins while the same old corruption eats the youth alive.

While the DCI claims to be "very busy," we all know how this ends. A few low-level recruiters will be paraded for the cameras to satisfy "public interest," while the real architects of this trade keep sipping tea in their air-conditioned offices. The "flight risk" argument for Omwamba is hilarious because the real risk is for every young Kenyan who thinks a flight to Russia is an escape from the poverty of Nairobi. 

We are told to "remain calm" and "follow official guidance" by embassies that were allegedly part of the problem. It is a dark joke. When your own country treats you like a commodity to be traded for a Russian bonus, "calm" is the last thing you should be. The trenches of Ukraine are cold, but the hearts of those selling our brothers are much colder. Our youth are being sold by the thousand, and the "investigations" are just the background music to the funeral.
