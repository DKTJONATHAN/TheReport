Bandari FC is at it again, selling us dreams wrapped in salty ocean air. After getting thoroughly schooled 2-0 by Police FC, the Dockers have retreated to their usual defense mechanism: the promise of "restructuring." Goalkeeping coach Razak Siwa is out here talking about "reflection" and "moments of hesitation," as if the team just forgot how to play football for ninety minutes. In this city, we call that a lack of ambition, not a temporary lapse in concentration.

Siwa's composed tone is almost impressive if you ignore the fact that his team looked like they were stuck in Mombasa traffic while Police FC were cruising on the expressway. He says they will reorganise and plan for the next matches, which is the standard KPL translation for "we have no idea why we are losing, but please keep paying our salaries." It is the same tired routine we see across the sports world, much like [Aiden Markram’s masterclass in saying absolutely nothing](/posts/pr-scripts-and--team-soldiers---aiden-markram-s-masterclass-in-saying-absolutely-nothing) when things go south.

The Dockers are currently sitting seventh on the table with 32 points. That is the definition of mid-table purgatory. They are "competitive" enough to stay in the league but irrelevant enough that nobody actually fears them on a Wednesday afternoon. Siwa claims their plans were "less" than those of the law enforcers. At least he is honest about being outsmarted, but honesty does not put points on the board or fix defensive gaps that you could drive a Matatu through.

Now they are hyping up the March 8 fixture against Mara Sugar as if it is a Champions League final. Apparently, this is where the "big changes" will magically appear. If you believe that, I have some "beachfront" property in the middle of the Chalbi Desert to sell you. Expecting tangible improvement from a team that struggles to sustain control in key phases is a tall order. They will likely huff and puff in front of their home supporters, only to deliver another masterclass in disappointment.

Then comes the trip to Kakamega to face Homeboyz on March 13. By then, the "restructuring" narrative will probably have shifted to "building for next season." This cycle of failure is as predictable as rain in April. Tightening organisation and sharpening communication are just fancy words for "try not to concede two goals in the first half." Until Bandari stops talking and starts playing, they are just another KPL side making up the numbers.
