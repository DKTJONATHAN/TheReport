Welcome to the new Nairobi, where even the leafy suburbs of Runda offer no sanctuary from the hungry wolves. On a Sunday morning, while most people were busy pretending to be holy, gospel singer Jimmy Gait was getting a violent reality check. At 10:00 a.m. on February 22, 2026, the "hurting" wasn't just a song lyric; it was his literal state after being accosted by armed individuals. In this city, "going about your business" is basically an open invitation for someone to come and take it from you.

The attackers didn't just want his phone and cash; they wanted to leave a lasting impression. Using sharp objects, they sliced his hands and fingers, turning a routine Sunday into a bloodbath. It is a grim reminder that [Nairobi Is A Crime Scene And Nobody Is Coming To Save You](/posts/nairobi-is-a-crime-scene-and-nobody-is-coming-to-save-you) regardless of your social status or how many gospel hits you have on the radio. The thugs in this town have lost all respect for the "secure" gated communities we love to brag about.

Of course, the gospel industry's favorite cameraman, DJ Mo, was on hand to document the trauma for social media. Nothing says "brotherly love" like making sure the camera is rolling while your friend is visibly distressed and dripping blood in a medical facility. The video shows a broken man, a far cry from the polished artist we see on screen. Gait claims it is only by "God's grace" he survived, which is the standard Kenyan way of saying the police failed and the system is a joke.

After the struggle, the robbers walked away with his mobile phone and an undisclosed amount of cash, leaving Gait to be rushed to St. Teresa Thindigua for emergency treatment. The irony is thick enough to choke on. You spend millions to live in Runda for the security, yet you end up in a hospital bed because someone wanted your smartphone at ten in the morning. It is a pathetic state of affairs that no amount of prayer is going to fix.

Now his peers are calling for "increased security measures" as if another gate or a bored watchman will stop a determined criminal. These calls for safety are the same tired scripts we hear every time a celebrity gets a taste of the real world. Nairobi is a city of predators and prey, and on Sunday, the predators won. Jimmy Gait is recovering, but the illusion of safety in our high-end estates is dead and buried.
