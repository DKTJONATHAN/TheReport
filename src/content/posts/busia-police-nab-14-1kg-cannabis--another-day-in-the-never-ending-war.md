Busia. Saturday. Routine patrols, they say. More like stumbling over someone else's misfortune. Fourteen point one kilos of suspected cannabis found in an unattended sack at the bus stage. Apparently, some boda boda rider decided this was a good place to ditch their… livelihood. The National Police Service is calling it a success, another notch on their belt in the "war on drugs." Right.

They found it, they searched it, they weighed it. Nine parcels, all wrapped up neat and tidy in yellow cellotape. Because presentation matters, I suppose. Now the investigations begin, of course. Trying to figure out where it came from, who's involved. As if they haven't seen this movie before. It's the same old song and dance, just a different sack, a different day.

This whole "intensified nationwide campaign" sounds grand, doesn't it? Intelligence-led operations, routine patrols, and "enhanced collaboration with the public." Translation: they're hoping someone else does the hard work for them. Maybe if they spent less time chasing small fry and more time tackling the systemic rot, things might actually change. But that would require actual effort, wouldn't it?

It’s all part of the grand illusion, isn't it? A bit of theatre to make you feel like something's being done. Meanwhile, back in the real world, the economy is still gasping for air. If you thought the constant threat of economic collapse from geopolitical drama was bad, imagine what a few more of these "victories" will do for the price of… well, everything. It’s almost as predictable as [Google’s Gemini 3.1 Pro launch](/posts/google-asserts-dominance-in-generative-ai-with-launch-of-gemini-3-1-pro) changing the AI landscape.

So, yes, 14.1 kilograms of cannabis seized. A small win for the NPS, a minor inconvenience for whoever lost their stash. For the rest of us? Just another headline in a country that seems perpetually stuck in neutral, waiting for the next crisis or the next pointless bust. They'll arrest someone, maybe. They'll make a show of it. And tomorrow, someone else will be sitting at that bus stage with another sack. That's Nairobi realism for you.
