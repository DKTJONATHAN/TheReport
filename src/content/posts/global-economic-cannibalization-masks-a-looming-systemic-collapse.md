This divergence is fueled by the Cantillon Effect, a reality where those closest to the source of credit reap the rewards while the rest suffer the resulting inflation. Today, that new money is not reaching the working class; it is being funneled into AI-driven portfolios and exclusive asset classes. While the bottom half of the wealth distribution survives on negative equity, the technocratic top is funding a private, endogenously generated boom. It is a closed loop of prosperity that requires no participation from the general public to sustain its valuations.

On the global stage, this fracture is even more lethal. While the West distracts itself with the optimism of green energy and silicon, fragile economies in Africa and South Asia are being systematically cannibalized. Countries like Egypt, Pakistan, and Ethiopia are not merely lagging; they are being crushed by a combination of energy shocks and Western monetary tightening. These nations are the lower leg of the K, forced to export their dwindling wealth to pay foreign creditors while their own infrastructures crumble. This is not a failure of the market, but its ultimate objective.

The political fallout of this reality is not a sudden, clean revolution, but a slow and poisonous alienation. When the national economy ceases to be a shared experience, the concept of a common good evaporates entirely. This explains the massive non-participation in modern democracies; people have realized that the macroeconomic data reported by the state has nothing to do with their personal survival. Much like the stagnant delusions seen in the [Dockers Adrift: Bandari FC's 'Big Changes' Are Just The Same Old Coastal Delusions](/posts/dockers-adrift--bandari-fc-s--big-changes--are-just-the-same-old-coastal-delusions) report, the global leadership offers the optics of progress while the foundations are being stripped for parts.
