So, the Americans, with their fancy technology and "global leadership," have decided that a little Ebola outbreak is too much for their own backyard. And where do they think is a good place to dump their sick? Our very own Kenya. Apparently, our government - the one we pay to look out for us - is in on it. And their response to a perfectly reasonable inquiry from the Daily Nation? Crickets. Absolute silence. This is exactly the kind of "cooperation" that makes you wonder who's really calling the shots.

Health Cabinet Secretary Aden Duale was apparently bombarded with ten very specific questions: Why Kenya? Where will this "facility" be? When are these potentially infected individuals showing up? And, crucially, do *we* face any extra risk? The fact that the facility is still "pending approval" tells you everything you need to know about the urgency and the consideration given to Kenyan lives. Meanwhile, the DRC is a mess, their capital city cancelling training camps and fleeing to Belgium. Easy for them to run, isn't it?

This isn't some abstract problem happening far away. Ebola has already hit Uganda, and unlike the Zaire strain, this Bundibugyo variant has no approved vaccines or treatments. So, the US wants to ship their potential carriers here, to our soil, where we're already struggling with our own issues. It’s a classic move, really. They get to manage their problem without the messy optics, and we get to potentially deal with the fallout. It’s a stark reminder of how the global order works, something we touched on in [THE GOLDEN GRAVEYARD: HOW DE-DOLLARIZATION IS BURYING THE OLD WORLD ORDER](/posts/the-golden-graveyard--how-de-dollarization-is-burying-the-old-world-order).

The whole situation reeks of the same hypocrisy we see elsewhere. Remember how they fret over every little thing when it suits them, but when it comes to managing a disease that might tarnish their image, they're happy to export the risk? It's the same old song and dance, just with a deadlier tune. What's next? Are we going to become the world's designated dumping ground for every problem they can't be bothered to solve themselves? I wouldn't be surprised.
