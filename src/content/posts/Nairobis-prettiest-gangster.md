# Nairobi's Prettiest Gangster: The Rise and Fall of Claire Njoki Kibia

Claire Njoki Kibia, known on Nairobi’s streets as Clea Adi Vybz, became a legend in Kenyan crime history at just 17. Her story is a tapestry of boldness, loyalty, and tragedy—a tale that gripped the city and left a lasting mark. From her early days in Murang’a to her final moments in Kayole, Claire’s life was shaped by choices that still spark debate and reflection.

***

## Early Life and Descent into Crime

Claire was born in Kiharu, Murang’a, but her life quickly became rooted in Nairobi’s Kayole estate. She dropped out of school in Form 1 and soon found herself drawn into the company of older youths who introduced her to a life of crime. The allure of fast money, designer clothes, and the power that came with being part of a notorious gang was irresistible. Claire quickly rose through the ranks, becoming a recruiter and informer for the Gaza gang, a feared criminal group operating in Nairobi’s estates.

***

## The Gaza Gang and Claire’s Role

The Gaza gang, known for its violent robberies and murders, was under constant police watch. Claire wasn’t just a member; she was a key figure, using her charm and fearlessness to recruit new members and ferry weapons. Her beauty and boldness made her stand out, but it was her loyalty and courage that made her a legend in the underworld. The gang’s offshoot, Warembo Sacco, became an exclusive enclave for female thugs, and Claire was at the center of this new wave of crime.

***

## Romance with Mwane Sparta

Claire’s romance with Mwane Sparta, another feared gangster, was the stuff of legend. Their bond was a symbol of devotion in a world where trust was rare and betrayal was fatal. Mwane was fiercely protective of Claire, and their relationship was a beacon of loyalty amidst the chaos of their criminal lives. Their love story became a symbol of the deep connections that can form in the most dangerous environments.

***

## The Events Leading to Her Death

The events leading to Claire’s death unfolded in May 2017. Police had been trailing the Gaza gang for weeks, following a string of violent robberies and murders in Kayole. Claire was believed to be at the center of these activities, and the police were determined to apprehend her. On the afternoon of May 11, 2017, Claire was spotted in Kayole alongside several accomplices. The police, acting on intelligence, moved in to arrest her and her associates.

***

## The Standoff and Shootout

What followed was a tense standoff. According to police reports, Claire and her accomplices refused to surrender and opened fire on the officers. The police responded, and a shootout erupted. Claire was fatally wounded in the exchange and died at the scene, alongside four of her accomplices. The incident sent shockwaves through Nairobi, not just because of Claire’s notoriety, but because it exposed the fragility behind her fearless persona.

***

## Aftermath and Legacy

Claire’s death marked the beginning of an all-out war on those associated with her and the Gaza gang. The police intensified their crackdown, and within weeks, several more gang members were apprehended or killed. The city mourned her loss, not as a criminal, but as a symbol of a generation caught between dreams and danger, between the allure of the streets and the harsh reality of the law.
