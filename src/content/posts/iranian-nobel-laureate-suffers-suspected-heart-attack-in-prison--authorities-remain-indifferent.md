Another inconvenient truth surfaces from Iran's gulags. Narges Mohammadi, a Nobel laureate who has dedicated her life to advocating for human rights, is reportedly suffering from a severe medical crisis in prison. Her family claims she experienced what appears to be a heart attack, found unconscious and requiring immediate medical intervention from fellow inmates.

The Free Narges Coalition paints a grim picture: Mohammadi's health was already "extremely poor," characterized by paleness, weakness, and significant weight loss. The incident on March 24th, where she was found unresponsive with eyes rolled back for over an hour, is a stark indicator of her deteriorating condition. While cellmates administered aid and the coalition asserts she was revived with medication, the regime's response is, predictably, obstructionist.

Authorities have allegedly refused to transfer her to a hospital or permit consultation with a specialist. Their transparent excuse? "It is wartime." This convenient justification is used to deny basic medical attention to political prisoners, a practice that showcases the Iranian government's contempt for international humanitarian norms and its own laws, which supposedly mandate the release of non-dangerous prisoners during wartime if their safety cannot be guaranteed.

Mohammadi's pre-existing heart and pulmonary conditions are well documented, making her current predicament even more alarming. The claim that she suffered debilitating headaches, nausea, double vision, and visible bruises following a "violent arrest" further underscores the brutal reality faced by those who dare to challenge the status quo.

The family's demand for immediate release for a thorough medical examination is not a request for leniency; it is a basic plea for survival. Yet, history teaches us that such pleas often fall on deaf ears when dealing with authoritarian regimes. The international community can decry the situation, but until tangible pressure is applied, the fate of Narges Mohammadi, like countless others, will likely remain a testament to the callous indifference of those in power. The regime's priorities are clear: control and suppression, even at the cost of human life.
