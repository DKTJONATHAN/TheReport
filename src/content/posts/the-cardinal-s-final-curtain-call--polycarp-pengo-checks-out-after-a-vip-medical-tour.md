Dar es Salaam is currently buzzing, and for once, it is not about a Diamond Platnumz scandal or a leaked DM. The Catholic Church’s ultimate VIP, His Eminence Polycarp Cardinal Pengo, has officially taken his final bow. After a long stint in India trying to dodge the inevitable since late 2025, the Archbishop Emeritus decided he had seen enough of the world. He touched down at Julius Nyerere International Airport at 6:00 p.m. on February 19 and was gone by 10:00 p.m. Talk about a quick turnaround. Some might call it poetic; I call it a very expensive way to say goodbye to the 255.

While the TEC Secretariat is busy praising his tireless fight for "moral values," let us be real for a second. Pengo was the original influencer before Instagram even existed. He had an entire generation of followers calling themselves "Sisi ni Watoto wa Kardinali Pengo." It is a classic case of branding that would make a Kardashian jealous. But beneath that gentle, soft-spoken exterior, he was the ultimate gatekeeper of what was "right" and "wrong." Much like [the Hall of Shame where the NFL’s moral gatekeeping is a fraud](/posts/the-hall-of-shame--why-the-nfl-s-moral-gatekeeping-is-a-fraud), the religious elite love to set the bar sky-high for the masses while living in a completely different reality of private jets and specialized overseas hospitals.

The Secretary General, Fr. Charles Kitima, actually had the audacity to ask the faithful to pray for God to forgive the sins Pengo committed as a human being. It is refreshing to hear the Church finally admit that even the guys in the fancy red hats have skeletons in their closets. We are usually told these leaders are practically untouchable, but apparently, even a Cardinal needs a spiritual cleanup crew at the end. It makes you wonder what kind of "weighty matters" were really going on behind those Vatican-adjacent doors during the corruption scandals of the 2000s that he so "boldly" addressed.

Pengo was a regular at the papal conclaves, helping elect both Benedict XVI and Francis. He was essentially a kingmaker in a dress, yet he kept that "calm manner" even when dragging the government for filth. It is the kind of passive-aggressive energy we usually reserve for Twitter beefs, but he turned it into a high art form. He knew exactly how to stay relevant from his birth in 1944 until the very end, proving that if you have enough "gentle character," you can get away with saying just about anything to people in power without getting canceled.

Now that he is gone, the Jakaya Kikwete Heart Institute has one less high-profile patient, and the Church has a massive hole to fill in its PR department. The "Children of Cardinal Pengo" are left wondering who will tell them how to live their lives next. He lived through eras of corruption, political violence, and several popes, only to succumb hours after returning home from a medical trip. If that is not a dramatic series finale, I do not know what is. Rest in peace, your Eminence; the gossip mill will take it from here.
