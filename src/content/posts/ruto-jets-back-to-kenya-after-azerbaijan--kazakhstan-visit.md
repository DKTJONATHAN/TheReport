Ruto's Five-Day Jaunt: Azerbaijan, Kazakhstan, and Back to the Coast's Empty Promises

WARNING: Another presidential trip, more grand pronouncements, and a flurry of ribbon-cuttings. Expect the usual theatre as Ruto returns to oversee "development" projects, while the real issues remain firmly on the back burner.

PRESIDENT RUTO LANDS IN MOMBASA AFTER FOREIGN EXCURSIONS

POLITICS
RUTO, FOREIGN TRIPS, NAIROBI REALISM
JET

So, the President decided to grace us with his presence again. Five days jetting off to Azerbaijan and Kazakhstan, attending forums and shaking hands. Apparently, he was busy strengthening "diplomatic and economic relations," which in Kenyan terms usually translates to more photo ops and less tangible benefit for the common man. He even picked up a shiny medal in Kazakhstan - one of their highest honours. Must be nice to be recognized internationally when back home, the economy is still doing its usual tightrope act, and you wonder if [cartels are still cashing in](https://www.nairobirealism.com/posts/ruto-s--stable--economy--inflation-down--cartels-still-cashing-in?).

Back on Kenyan soil, he landed quietly in Mombasa. No grand reception for the return of the prodigal son, just a quick touchdown before diving headfirst into a "five-day development tour" of the coast. Get ready for the familiar script: launching this, inspecting that, breaking ground for the other. Title deeds for six counties are on the agenda - let's hope these actually reach the people who need them and aren't just another avenue for some well-connected individuals to profit.

From the fish market in Lamu to modern markets in Kilifi and beyond, the itinerary is packed. We're talking road construction, power substations, and more market foundations. It's all very impressive on paper, isn't it? But after all the speeches and the ceremonial shovels, will any of this truly transform the lives of ordinary Kenyans, or is it just another round of appeasing certain regions before the next political scramble? It’s starting to feel like [who really won in the Ruto-Raila broad-based govt pact](https://www.nairobirealism.com/posts/march-7-aftermath--who-really-won-in-the-ruto-raila-broad-based-govt-pact--don-t-hold-your-breath.) - and it certainly wasn't the hustler.

One can only hope that amidst the grand gestures and the promises of enhanced trade and tourism with places like Serbia and Georgia, the President remembers the actual "market access" issues faced by Kenyan farmers. But let's be realistic. This is Nairobi, and the reality is often a lot more grubby than the glossy pronouncements suggest. It’s the same old song and dance, just with a slightly different international backdrop.

And the irony isn't lost on us, is it? While foreign dignitaries are awarded medals and bilateral agreements are signed on ICT and space technology, the rest of us are still navigating the complexities of simply having a valid Kenyan passport, which has become [the new VIP access for the international sanctioned elite](https://www.nairobirealism.com/posts/forget-the-blue-tick--the-kenyan-passport-is-the-new-vip-access-for-the-international-sanctioned-elite). The President is back, but the fundamental issues remain. Let the games continue.
