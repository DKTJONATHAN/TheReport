The squad recently suffered a significant blow in the Copa del Rey semifinals against Atletico Madrid, followed by a domestic defeat to Girona. These results have not only heightened the risk of elimination from cup competition but have also allowed Real Madrid to seize the lead in the league standings. In response, Flick has integrated Rashford and Pedri back into team drills, signaling a potential return to the lineup for the upcoming fixture against Levante. While their participation has been limited to partial segments of the sessions, their presence is viewed as a vital psychological and tactical lift for the roster.

The absence of Pedri has been particularly felt within the Barcelona midfield. Sidelined since late January following a muscle injury sustained during a Champions League encounter with Slavia Prague, the Spaniard provides a unique combination of defensive work rate and ball retention. Analysts suggest that the lack of such a profile contributed to the team's recent loss of structural cohesion. Despite the urgent need for his creativity, the technical staff is expected to exercise caution regarding his minutes to avoid a recurrence of the injury during the most demanding phase of the season.

Flick has utilized the recent interval to address systemic errors through intensive video analysis. Following the loss at Girona, the manager granted the squad two days of rest before convening for a one-hour tactical review. The focus remains on rectifying defensive lapses and improving efficiency in front of the goal, where the team has struggled to convert opportunities despite the individual brilliance of players like Lamine Yamal. This period of reflection is intended to stabilize the group before they enter the final stretch of the championship.

The broader landscape of European football continues to shift as Barcelona seeks to reclaim its dominance. While Real Madrid prepares to welcome back Jude Bellingham for their match against Osasuna, former Barcelona icons Lionel Messi and Sergio Busquets continue to achieve historic milestones with IFFHS recognition. As the race for the European Golden Shoe intensifies among the likes of Harry Kane and Kylian Mbappe, Barcelona's ability to successfully reintroduce their injured stars will be a decisive factor in the hunt for silverware.
