Just when you thought the Kenyan social media scene couldn't get more desperate for engagement, some "lifestyle guru" decided to tell the world that the Standard Gauge Railway is basically a free-for-all. Apparently, the new hack for the broke and bougie is to book a ticket to Voi but "accidentally" forget to get off until the train hits Nairobi. It is the kind of cheap-skate behavior that people try to package as a revolutionary life hack, but honey, it is just called fare evasion.

Kenya Railways had to take time out of their busy schedule of managing delayed departures to remind everyone that ticket inspectors actually exist. Imagine the embarrassment of being hauled off the Madaraka Express in handcuffs just because you wanted to save a few coins to buy an overpriced cocktail at a Nairobi rooftop bar. The corporation was blunt: if you try to overstay your welcome, you will be handed over to the police. It is giving "broke but trying to stay relevant" energy, and frankly, we are tired of it.

This whole mess is just another symptom of the [Clout, Chaos, and Corpses: Why Kenya’s Social Media Feed Is a Crime Scene](/posts/clout--chaos--and-corpses--why-kenya-s-social-media-feed-is-a-crime-scene) culture that has taken over our timelines. People are so obsessed with looking like they have mastered the system that they forget the system actually has scanners. It is the same reason why people are currently falling for "2026 New Year Gift" scams in the middle of the year. If you truly believe Kenya Railways is handing out 9,000 shillings for answering a two-question survey, then you deserve the heartbreak that comes with your empty bank account.

The desperation does not stop at fake giveaways. We also have to deal with the bottom-feeders posting fake job advertisements for managerial roles using "outlook.com" email addresses. If you think a multi-billion shilling government corporation is recruiting its legal services manager via a random free email account, you need more than just a job; you need a reality check. The hypocrisy is loud-everyone complains about corruption until they see an opportunity to cheat a train ticket or "win" money they didn't work for.

At the end of the day, the SGR is not your playground for social media experiments. Whether it is fake recruitment posters or delusional travel hacks, the obsession with taking shortcuts is making everyone look messy. Stay in your lane, pay for your actual destination, and for the love of everything stylish, stop taking financial advice from people whose only asset is a ring light and a TikTok account. Success is not a "Voi to Nairobi" skip, and neither is your dignity.
