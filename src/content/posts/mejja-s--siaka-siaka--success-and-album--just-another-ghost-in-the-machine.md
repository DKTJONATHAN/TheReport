So, Mejja, the supposed "legend," finally decided to drop an album, "Mtoto wa Khadija," after two decades of churning out... well, whatever it is he does. And surprise, surprise, one song, "Siaka Siaka," is apparently the hottest thing since ugali got a TikTok dance. People are doing challenges, it's dominating social media. Groundbreaking. Just like how George Ruto donating KSh 1m at his sister's hotel is supposed to be news. It's all a show, isn't it?

He’s out here performing at Blankets and Wine, thrilling fans who probably just want to forget their rent is due. And now he’s thinking about his own concert. Because that’s what happens when a song goes viral for five minutes - suddenly you’re the next big thing. He’s even talking about a countrywide tour, followed by an Australian tour. Must be nice to have the time and the cash to chase these pipe dreams.

Apparently, releasing an album was a "momentous" decision, guided by "instinct and timing" for 2025. Give me a break. Most artists rush these things. Mejja prefers to "take his time," which explains why "Siaka Siaka" took three years. Three years for a song that's going to be forgotten by next year. This is the same old story, the illusion of digital salvation with these streaming platforms. He even had issues with Spotify, multiple profiles. Bien-Aimé Baraza had to bail him out. Typical.

He claims the song was born from a "spontaneous studio moment." A spontaneous moment where he was struggling to keep up with the beat. That's not genius, that's just being slow. And the collaboration with Fik Fameica? More talk of East African unity. Let’s see how long that lasts before someone pulls a stunt that reminds everyone this is the entertainment business, not a Kumbaya circle.

Mejja's advice to upcoming artists? Be persistent and treat it like a business. Oh, profound wisdom. Especially after his mother tried to push him into the Kenya Defence Forces. He couldn't even hack the running tests. So much for military precision in his music career.

Ultimately, this whole "Mtoto wa Khadija" saga is just another chapter in the endless cycle of Kenyan entertainment. A few hits, some hype, a dash of self-importance, and then... back to the grind. Don't get too excited, darling. It’s all just noise.
