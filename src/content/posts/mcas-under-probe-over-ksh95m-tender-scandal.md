MCAs Caught in Ksh95M Embu Tender Mess - Shocker, Right?

Another day, another scandal. This time it's Embu MCAs and county bigwigs allegedly lining their pockets with nearly Ksh95 million through dodgy tenders and shell companies. Apparently, using "proxy companies" they secretly own to snatch public contracts is the new trend. Who knew?

The Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC) is supposedly "investigating," which in Kenyan terms usually means a lot of tea drinking and paperwork before everyone gets a slap on the wrist and forgets about it. This time, it’s not just about the Ksh95 million tender itself, but also about how a Revenue Collection System contract got an "extension" without following any rules. Because why would they?

And it’s not just Embu. The EACC is also sniffing around Vihiga for a Ksh5 million housewarming party - yes, a party - for the County Speaker. Clearly, public funds are just a fun playground for these folks. Then there’s Bungoma, where Ksh3.7 million was apparently blown on a Christmas tree lighting and another Ksh2.8 million on a "benchmarking trip" to Uganda. Imagine the taxpayer’s face when they hear that. It makes you wonder if these leaders ever think about anything beyond their own comfort, unlike some people who have to navigate the complex world of professional contracts, as seen in reports about figures like Catarina Macario.

The EACC promises "further action, including possible prosecutions," depending on their "findings." We’ve heard this song and dance before. Until we see actual heads roll and money clawed back, this is just more noise to distract us from the everyday hustle. It's the same old story of public resources being treated like personal piggy banks.
