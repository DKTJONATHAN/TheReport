So, March 9, 2026. A date that will live in infamy, or at least in the annals of Kenyan corruption. Apparently, our esteemed National Security Council Committee, led by Chief of Staff Felix Koskei himself, decided it was a brilliant idea to ditch the government-to-government fuel deals. Why? Because, as usual, someone somewhere had a better plan, likely involving fatter pockets. This meeting, just weeks into some war in Iran that probably won't even affect our oil prices in the long run, set the stage for this whole fuel fiasco.

Then, like clockwork, Energy CS Wandayi tells One Petroleum, owned by a certain businessman Jaffer, to yank 60,000 tonnes of super petrol off the shelves. Because, of course, someone didn't think this through. This isn't just a minor hiccup; it's a full-blown crisis threatening to derail President Ruto's Sh106 billion Kenya Pipeline Company privatisation. Imagine that - a grand plan, and it's already sputtering like an old kerosene lamp thanks to a fuel scandal. It reminds me of when your impounded bike becomes the inspector’s side hustle; it’s all about who profits and who pays. [The Great Government Heist: When Your Impounded Bike Becomes the Inspector’s Side-Hustle](/posts/the-great-government-heist--when-your-impounded-bike-becomes-the-inspector-s-side-hustle)

And let's not forget our show of solidarity with the UAE and Gulf countries over those Iranian attacks. We’re all about showing support when it’s convenient, especially when it might grease the wheels for some shady deals. Meanwhile, the real consequences are felt by us, the common mwananchi, who will likely see fuel prices go through the roof while the powerful play their games.

The predictable fallout? PS Liban, Kenya Pipeline Company MD Sang, and Epra Director-General Kiptoo have all resigned. Funny how when things go south, some people suddenly remember they have other, more important things to do. This isn't about national security; it's about national greed, plain and simple. The rot runs deep, and this fuel scandal is just another symptom of a system that's always looking for the next opportunity to fleece the public.
