So, the Nairobi-Mombasa highway is at it again. This time, some poor sod driving gypsum worth a king's ransom gets hijacked like it's Tuesday. The lorry, the fancy building dust - all gone. And guess what? The police, bless their hearts, managed to round up six people, including a woman. Probably the bait. Because let's be real, this isn't about chivalry; it's about exploiting weaknesses.

And don't think these thugs are just amateurs. They're linked to a whole string of gun attacks. Imagine this: four or five young men, masked up, looking like they raided a police station for their outfits - jackets, boots, the whole nine yards. They're the shadowy figures of the night, turning a perfectly normal drive into a high-stakes game of survival. During the day, the road looks fine, but when the sun dips, the real Nairobi starts showing its ugly face.

It's a whole ecosystem of crime out there. While these highway bandits are busy making off with fortunes, you've got your illicit alcohol peddlers hiding in bushes, conducting their business like they're revolutionaries. It’s a constant hustle, a desperate scramble for cash by any means necessary. It makes you wonder if any of this is even new, or just a different flavour of the same old exploitation we've seen throughout history, even in digital forms like THE BLOCKCHAIN IS JUST A DIGITAL REFINEMENT OF ANCIENT SLAVERY.

The whole "women luring drivers" angle? That’s just another tool in their arsenal. They'll use whatever works. A pretty face, a damsel in distress, whatever gets you to slow down, to trust. Then BAM. You're just another statistic, another lorry owner left counting their losses. This isn't some elaborate plot; it's just raw, brutal opportunism on a road that’s become a free-for-all. And the courts? They’re probably drowning in cases, as seen in reports that show COURT FILINGS SKYROCKET PAST 620,000 AS JUDICIARY DRAWS IN CASES. So, good luck getting justice.
