Karua Tells Kenya, US: Ebola Plan Dead After People Die

The Kenyan and US governments are still trying to push their ridiculous Ebola quarantine facility in Nanyuki, even after people have started dropping like flies over it. Martha Karua, bless her cynical heart, is asking the obvious: why are you still banging your head against a wall when the wall is literally bleeding? It’s a 50-bed holding pen for Americans coming from Congo. Because *that’s* exactly what Nanyuki needs - a magnet for disease.

The latest round of protests saw police firing live rounds, killing one more demonstrator. That’s three lives gone, just like that. All because these geniuses in government think it’s a brilliant idea to build a potential disease hotspot in a town that clearly doesn’t want it. The residents are raising valid points about health and safety, but who listens to the little people when there’s foreign money and supposed "progress" to be had? It’s the same old story, isn't it? Remember the 'fuel scandal'? The opposition threatened protests then too, but what changed? Very little.

Karua's statement is just adding to the chorus of "Are you deaf?" directed at Nairobi and Washington. Legal challenges, public outrage, and now dead bodies - it's a trifecta of reasons to pull the plug. But no, they’ll probably just send in more heavily armed men to "restore order" and call it a day. This isn't about public health; it's about power and potentially lucrative deals that benefit a select few while the rest of us get the fallout.

The whole situation reeks of the same old dance we do in this country. The police crackdowns, the empty promises, the eventual quiet down of the public. It’s like that Busia drug seizure - a big show, but is anything really changing? People are protesting for transparency and consultation, basic things you'd think a government would offer. Instead, they get bullets and tear gas. This Ebola facility is just another symptom of a deeper rot, a casual disregard for the lives of ordinary Kenyans.
