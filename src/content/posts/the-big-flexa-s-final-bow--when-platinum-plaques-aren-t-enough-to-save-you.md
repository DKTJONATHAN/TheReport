Let's talk about the meteoric rise and the even faster crash of the industry's favorite success story, Costa Titch. Back in late 2021, when "Big Flexa" dropped alongside C’Buda M and the rest of the crew, you couldn't enter a club in Nairobi or Jozi without hearing those logs. Constantinos Tsobanoglou - or "Mr Big Flexa" as he loved to be called - managed to do what most struggle with: he became inescapable. He had the moves, the energy, and a cast of dozens of dancers that made everyone forget he was essentially a guest in a genre that usually moves much slower than his frantic career path.

The industry loves a success story almost as much as it loves a tragedy. Costa was out here collecting platinum plaques like they were loyalty points at a grocery store. Four times platinum in South Africa and a cool 30 million views on YouTube for a single video? That is the kind of math that makes label executives salivate. He was busy thanking the "Titch Gang" and crying during Smirnoff-sponsored dinners, while the rest of us wondered if the "Mr Big Flexa" persona was ever going to let the actual human catch a breath.

Then came the desperate push for the "global" stage. You know the drill: find a legacy act like Akon, sign to Konvict Kulture, and hope the remix does what the original already did. It is the classic pivot we see time and again. We are currently witnessing [the death of authenticity](/posts/the-death-of-authenticity--why-nairobi-s-digital-elite-is-trading-influence-for-algorithms) across the board, where artists are encouraged to trade their genuine vibe for whatever numbers-driven strategy a label can cook up. Costa was no longer just a dancer; he was a commodity being prepped for an East African tour with Diamond Platnumz and global dominance.

Collaborations with Teni, Mayorkun, and Ycee weren't enough to satisfy the machine. The industry always wants more. His co-manager, Nivo, was already mapping out the next tour to East Africa just days before the end. It is funny how the industry works - you are "meant to go" on another tour, you are "meant to" go global, but the body usually has its own itinerary. The pressure to stay relevant while "Big Flexa" went 4x platinum is enough to make anyone's heart skip a beat, whether they are ready for it or not.

In his final interview with BBC Radio, he talked about his dream of going global. It is the same dream sold to every kid with a ring light and a TikTok account. He wanted the world to hear the record, but the world is a fickle place that moves on to the next viral dance challenge before the first one's body is even cold. Death knocked at his door on a Saturday night, and suddenly the awards from Soundcity MVP and the Afrimas don't seem to matter as much as the quiet he never got to enjoy.

So, here we are, mourning another star who was pushed to the absolute limit of his "flex." While the labels will keep counting the streaming royalties from the Akon remix, the "Titch Gang" is left with nothing but old YouTube clips and the bitter realization that the music industry is a meat grinder. Rest in peace, Costa, but let this be a lesson to the rest of the "authentic" stars: the spotlight is bright, but it is also incredibly hot.
