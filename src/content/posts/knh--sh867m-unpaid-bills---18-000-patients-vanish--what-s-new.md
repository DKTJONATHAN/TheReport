So, Kenyatta National Hospital, our esteemed "national" hospital, has managed to misplace Sh867 million and 18,000 patients. Apparently, they just... walked out. No fanfare, no "please pay your outstanding balance," just gone. It's almost impressive, if it wasn't so infuriatingly predictable. This isn't some magical disappearing act; it's a systematic failure, and we're all footing the bill, one way or another.

Remember that story about the security guard escorting a new mother out of the hospital, days after she’d given birth? Turns out, this isn't the first time "security" at KNH has been questionable. We’ve had patients murdered, slit throats, and missing CCTV footage. It's a wonder anyone dares to step foot in there, let alone give birth. But hey, where else are you going to go when your belly is swollen and your wallet is empty?

And then there's the case of Kennedy Kalombotole, a security guard who's apparently a suspect in not one, but two killings. He was apparently seen walking with a new mother and her baby. Now, the Sunday Nation is sniffing around his brief stay at someone's home, interviewing caregivers. Because, you know, a hospital meant to save lives is now also a breeding ground for alleged murderers, and the security detail is part of the problem.

This whole Sh867 million debt saga just adds another layer to the steaming pile of dysfunction. It’s a classic Kenyan tale: grand pronouncements, massive budgets, and then… nothing. It’s like watching our country become a private subsidiary of global interests, [Rent-A-Sovereignty: How the IMF is Turning Kenya into a Private Subsidiary](/posts/rent-a-sovereignty--how-the-imf-is-turning-kenya-into-a-private-subsidiary), where the important things get lost in the shuffle of paper and promises.

The hospital claims 18,000 patients walked out with these unpaid bills. Either KNH has a serious problem with patient tracking, or they're just writing off millions because, let's be honest, who’s going to chase down 18,000 people? It’s easier to just let them go, add it to the pile of problems, and hope for a miracle. Maybe they’re hoping for a tourism boom, where [5 Million Visitors or 5 Million Delusions? Kenya’s New Tourism Wonder is Just Influencer Bait](/posts/5-million-visitors-or-5-million-delusions--kenya-s-new-tourism-wonder-is-just-influencer-bait). Yeah, right.

Honestly, the only puzzle here is why anyone is surprised. KNH is a microcosm of our nation: chaotic, underfunded, and riddled with scandals. The real mystery is how we keep expecting anything different.
