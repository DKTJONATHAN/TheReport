Kithure Kindiki is out here in Embu trying to sell us a "system," as if we have not been systematic victims of this government's experiments for years. He says UDA is a party of structures and that they are not just here for the noise. It is the same old lecture-room arrogance packaged as political strategy. In Nairobi, we know that when a politician talks about "building structures that last," he really means he is figuring out which gatekeepers to pay off so the votes "organise" themselves into the right boxes by February 26.

Kindiki wants to be the calm, intellectual face of a machine that is currently coughing up smoke. He talks about listening to the people, but the only time these people listen is when the motorcade slows down enough to drop some loose change. It is a desperate attempt to look orderly, but the chaos is already baked into the DNA of the party. It reminds me of the [Kenyan School Placement Chaos 2025: The KJSEA Grade 10 Disaster, Kisumu-to-Mandera Glitch, and What Parents Must Do Now](/posts/kjsea-grade10-placement-chaos-2025) where the state promises a seamless digital transition but leaves everyone stranded and confused. This "system" Kindiki is bragging about is likely just as glitchy.

Then you have Rigathi Gachagua, the man who was once the President's pit bull, now reduced to a whistleblower with a grudge. He is claiming to have secret recordings, calling the President a liar and a master of doublespeak. It is peak Kenyan comedy. One day you are sharing tea at State House, the next day you are threatening to leak the "financial offers" your boss made to buy your silence. Gachagua is behaving like a jilted lover with a folder of screenshots, and frankly, it is embarrassing for everyone involved.

The President is not taking it lying down either. He is hitting back, accusing his former deputy of being a warmonger within the government. It is a classic case of the pot calling the kettle black. They spent years fighting each other under the same roof while the cost of living was choking the rest of us, and now they want us to take sides in their messy divorce. The President calls Gachagua a fighter, but we all know that in this administration, everyone is fighting for their own stomach.

By the time these by-elections arrive, the "systems" Kindiki is so proud of will just be more ways to keep the elite fed while the common person starves on a diet of roadside speeches. Whether it is Kindiki's "organisation" or Gachagua's "noise," the result is the same for the person on the street. It is all just theatre, and we are the ones paying for the tickets with our taxes and our sanity. These people do not have a dream; they have a ledger, and we are not on the profit side of it.
