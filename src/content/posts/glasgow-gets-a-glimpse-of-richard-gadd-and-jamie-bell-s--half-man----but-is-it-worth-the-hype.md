So, the BBC and HBO have graced Glasgow with their presence, churning out yet another "gripping" six-part series called "Half Man." Apparently, it's all about brotherhood, violence, and the oh-so-fragile male ego. Created by Richard Gadd, the man who gave us *Baby Reindeer* and apparently now thinks he's television's most influential person (bless his heart), this drama stars Jamie Bell, who, let's be honest, peaked with *Billy Elliot*. They were spotted lurking around Glasgow's tenement flats, looking all serious and important. Probably contemplating their next PR move.

Gadd, who is apparently also starring in this masterpiece, teams up with Bell, who plays a character named Niall. The plot, as far as I can gather, involves two inseparable men, Niall and Ruben, whose brotherly bond is tested by death and circumstances. Thirty years later, Ruben shows up at Niall's wedding acting "strangely," which, predictably, ignites a violent event and a trip down memory lane. It all sounds rather dramatic and, dare I say, a bit predictable. I mean, how many times can we watch men grapple with their emotions before it just becomes tedious?

The supporting cast includes a smattering of familiar faces and some newcomers, all apparently playing younger versions of the main characters or their mothers. Mitchell Robertson and Stuart Campbell are Niall and Ruben's younger selves, while Neve McIntosh and Marianne McIvor play their respective mothers. With a cast list longer than my patience for mediocre television, one can only hope they bring something more than just looking pretty in front of Glasgow's brickwork.

The series is set to drop in April on BBC iPlayer and HBO Max, so brace yourselves for the inevitable think pieces and online dissection. Will "Half Man" be another critical darling that the rest of us just don't "get"? Or will it be a beautifully shot, yet ultimately hollow, exploration of male relationships? Given the current climate of celebrity overexposure, especially after Gadd's recent whirlwind, I'm leaning towards the latter.
