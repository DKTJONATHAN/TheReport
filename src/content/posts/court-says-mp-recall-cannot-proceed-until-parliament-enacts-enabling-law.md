COURT SAYS MP RECALL CAN'T HAPPEN UNTIL PARLIAMENT DOES SOMETHING ABOUT IT. SHOCKER.

The High Court has decided that Kenyans can't kick out their useless MPs, not yet anyway. Apparently, the right to recall, which is supposed to be in the constitution, needs a whole new law to actually work. So, until Parliament finally bothers to write one, our elected officials are safe from any public accountability. Just another day in Nairobi.

The judge, Roselyne Aburili, basically told everyone to chill and wait for Parliament to sort itself out. She said the whole recall petition was premature because MPs are already "considering amendments" - which usually means a lot of hot air and very little action. This is after the electoral commission, the IEBC, already admitted they had no framework because some "key recall provisions" got struck down back in 2017. So, we've been in this mess for years.

The petitioners, bless their optimistic hearts, thought the constitutional right was "self-executing" and wanted the court to make the IEBC use whatever bits of the old law were left, or even the MCA recall system. But the court decided intervening would be "judicial interference." Apparently, we can't have the courts messing with Parliament's "constitutional mandate." It's like watching a toddler promise to clean their room, and you're supposed to just wait patiently, knowing it's never going to happen.

Justice Aburili did throw them a tiny bone, saying the petition didn't need to go to Parliament first, which is something. But the main point stands: Kenyans can't recall their MPs. This whole charade is just another reminder that when it comes to holding politicians accountable, the system is designed to fail. It's a classic case of the Kenya Creative Economy sucking up global cash while real issues like this get kicked down the road, [Nairobi investors eyeing the spoils](/posts/kenya-creative-economy-sucks-up-global-cash--nairobi-investors-eyeing-the-spoils).

So, we're stuck with the same old faces, the same old promises, and the same old inability to do anything about it. The Elections (Amendment) (No. 2) Bill, 2024, is the magic fix, apparently. Let's all hold our breath and wait for that legislative miracle to materialize. Until then, happy to have elected our representatives, who are now officially unrecallable.
