Ruto's Budget: Gachagua Calls It A "Killer" As Economy Battle Intensifies

Gachagua launches a scathing attack on the proposed 2026/27 budget and Finance Bill, calling them a direct assault on ordinary Kenyans and urging Parliament to reject them. He accuses the government of prioritising taxation and borrowing over essential services.

Mr Gachagua, speaking from the Democracy for the Citizens Party (DCP) headquarters, didn't mince words, labelling the Finance Bill 2026 as a "killer" that will tax Kenyans into poverty and "break households." He accused the government of waging a "terrifying economic war" with its Sh4.82 trillion budget and the accompanying Finance Bill, which he described as a "twin evil" threatening workers, farmers, small businesses, and low-income households already reeling from the high cost of living. This comes as Treasury Cabinet Secretary John Mbadi prepares to present the documents to Parliament, amid significant public scrutiny.

The former Deputy President pointed out the government's insatiable appetite for debt, claiming it intends to borrow over Sh1.1 trillion in the next financial year, adding to the already crushing Sh13 trillion public debt. "The government is working first for lenders, then for the people," he stated, highlighting a worrying trend where the nation seems to be serving its creditors rather than its citizens. This excessive borrowing and the focus on tariffs are reminiscent of [THE DEBT TRAP’S NEW TEETH: HOW TARIFFs ARE STRANGLING THE DOLLAR’S DOMINANCE](/posts/the-debt-trap-s-new-teeth--how-tariffs-are-strangling-the-dollar-s-dominance), demonstrating a consistent pattern of financial mismanagement.

Furthermore, Gachagua lambasted the administration for neglecting crucial sectors like healthcare, education, and agriculture, while showering the presidency and administrative spending with increased allocations. He questioned the future of Kenyan children with reduced education funding and the state of agriculture, a lifeline for millions, with only two percent of the budget. "Why is this regime at war with our children and our youth? Why are you killing our future?" he demanded, painting a grim picture of a government actively undermining its own people.

The Finance Bill 2026/27 faced his fiercest condemnation, labelled the "worst in the history of Kenya." He singled out proposals like a 16 percent VAT on digital payment commissions and a hike in excise duty on mobile phones, arguing these would disproportionately burden ordinary citizens, young people, and small businesses. Gachagua insisted, "Kenya cannot tax its way to prosperity," advocating instead for reduced waste, sealed revenue leakages, and timely payment of pending bills. The DCP's alternative plan includes slashing the budget, eliminating new borrowing, abolishing the housing levy, and significantly boosting agriculture and healthcare funding.

Gachagua urged Parliament to reject both the budget and the Finance Bill, imploring citizens to track their representatives' votes. He warned that those who support the "reckless regime" would be held accountable. The DCP proposes a drastically reduced budget of Sh3.67 trillion, with agriculture receiving Sh300 billion and health Sh450 billion, while public administration spending is slashed. This vision aims to balance the budget without additional debt and focus on sectors that directly improve livelihoods, a stark contrast to the current administration's perceived priorities.
