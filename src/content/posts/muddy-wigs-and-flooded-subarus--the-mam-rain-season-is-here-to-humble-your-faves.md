Oh, darling, grab your umbrellas and your waterproof mascara because the weather man has decided to officially ruin the aesthetic. While you were busy planning your "Soft Girl Era" outdoor picnics, KeNHA and the NTSA have dropped a reality check that is heavier than a cheap synthetic wig in a downpour. Director General Luka Kimeli basically told everyone to pipe down and stay off the roads, but we all know our local celebrities will still be out there trying to catch a vibe in a flood zone just for the "gram."

According to the official statement, we are looking at a messy March-April-May season that is going to be the ultimate test for those "Subaru Boys" who think speeding through puddles is a personality trait. The NTSA is screaming about fog and slippery roads, yet somehow, I bet we still see someone’s "Man Crush Monday" posting a steering wheel selfie while hydroplaning at 100km/h. It is the hypocrisy for me - wanting to look "fast and furious" while the authorities are literally begging you to exercise extreme caution and maintain safe distances.

It is especially hilarious for the content creator crowd who just recently got their lives back. Remember how [TikTok Ban Canceled: Why Kenya's Clout-Thirsty Influencers Are Breathing Again (For Now)](/posts/tiktok-ban-canceled--why-kenya-s-clout-thirsty-influencers-are-breathing-again--for-now)? Well, they might have their apps back, but the rain is about to snatch their outdoor lighting. Good luck filming those "Day in my life" vlogs when your compound looks like a swamp and the power goes out every time a single drop of rain hits a transformer. The Met Department says the peak is coming in April, so expect a lot of "authentic" indoor content because their luxury rentals probably have leaking roofs.

KeNHA says they are "continuously monitoring" the roads, which is government-speak for "we hope the drainage holds up better than your last relationship." They are warning pedestrians to avoid walking near fast-moving traffic, which is great advice unless you are an aspiring socialite trying to do a street-style photoshoot in the middle of a deluge. The risk of reduced visibility is real, but apparently, the only thing some of these people can see is their own reflection in a rain-slicked pavement.

For those living in the Highlands or the Lake Victoria Basin, do not pack away those boots yet; your misery is projected to last well into June. It is going to be a long season of mud-splattered outfits and canceled red-carpet arrivals. If I see one more "King of the Streets" post from someone whose car is currently floating down a bypass in a low-lying area, I might just lose it. Stay safe, stay dry, and for the love of everything fashionable, please stop trying to make "rainy day chic" happen - it is just damp.
