The Kenya Navy is back at it again, serving us the usual polished PR about Exercise Cutlass Express 2026. They call it "precision and naval decorum," but for anyone living in the real Nairobi, it sounds like a very expensive boat ride. While we are here dodging potholes and fighting the rising cost of unga, our ships are "seamlessly transitioning" from Seychelles to Mauritius. They talk about "Passage Exercises" and "farewell manoeuvres" as if they are performing a ballet on the water, but the only thing moving with precision is the money leaving our national treasury.

They are bragging about seizing one tonne of methamphetamine. Don't get me wrong, drugs are bad, but let us be real for a second. We catch one tonne on the high seas while the big fish in Nairobi continue to operate with total impunity. These "intelligence-driven operations" are just a drop in the ocean. The authorities love to showcase these wins to distract us from the fact that [Kenya Is Rotten To The Core](/posts/new-post) and the real criminals are usually the ones signing the deployment orders. If they were serious about security, our borders would not be as porous as a second-hand sieve.

Then there is the "training" part of the mission. They have "Officers Under Training" exploring Mauritius’ coastal heritage and hiking Signal Mountain. It must be nice to have the taxpayer fund your hiking hobby and your "familiarization visits." While our youth are struggling to find work, these junior officers are playing friendly football matches with the Mauritius National Coast Guard. It is not a military operation; it is a sponsored sports day in the sun. They call it "fostering camaraderie," I call it a vacation for the well-connected.

The peak of the hypocrisy happened on the Kenya Navy Ship JASIRI. They hosted a "Request the Pleasure of Your Company" reception. Imagine that. Senior government officials and "senior police officers" clinking glasses and sipping fine drinks while the Sea Lanes of Communication they claim to protect are only used to import more expensive goods we can-not afford. They talk about "renewed energy" and "appreciation," but the only people feeling energized are the ones who got a free trip to Port Louis.

They claim these missions are "lifelines" for our daily economic well-being. If these sea lanes are so secure, why is everything from fuel to cooking oil still priced like a luxury item? Operation BAHARI MOJA 02 is just another chapter in the long book of "How to Spend Public Funds Without Improving Public Lives." They can execute all the coordinated fleet manoeuvres they want, but until the average Kenyan sees a change in their pocket, all this "maritime security" is just expensive theatre on the Indian Ocean.
