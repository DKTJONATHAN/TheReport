KNH, Moi University Top List of Institutions Paying Hundreds of Staff Outside Payroll

The government's payroll is a sieve, leaking money for hundreds of public servants who are being paid off-the-books by institutions like Kenyatta National Hospital and Moi University. This isn't just sloppy accounting; it's a gaping hole in financial control, a breeding ground for ghost workers, and a testament to the government's uncanny ability to squander public funds.

Kenyatta National Hospital (KNH) and Moi University are leading the pack in a scandalous display of financial mismanagement, with hundreds of their employees drawing salaries from outside the official payroll system. The Public Service Commission (PSC) report, which nobody in their right mind would actually read if they had a choice, reveals that KNH is playing Santa Claus with 1,136 officers, while Moi University is not far behind with 695. Together, they account for nearly half of all public servants getting paid through these "alternative payment arrangements." This isn't innovation; it's a deliberate way to hide the true cost of government bloat and, let's be honest, probably line a few pockets.

The PSC is wringing its hands, calling it a "potential loophole" that "weakens financial controls" and "obscures the true size of the public wage bill." Oh, really? You don't say. Meanwhile, for the last year, 3,868 public officers across 72 institutions have been getting paid in this shadowy fashion. A whopping 20.1 percent of these individuals have been on this off-the-books gravy train for over 12 months. And the payment period for more than half of them? Undisclosed. It’s like a state-sponsored magic show, where money disappears into thin air.

Public universities, predictably, are the sector with the highest proportion of these "off-payroll" employees. It’s almost as if their primary function has become something other than education. State corporations and semi-autonomous government agencies aren't far behind, proving that anywhere the government has its grubby hands, waste and inefficiency are sure to follow. This is the same government that can't even get your passport processed on time, a saga that often involves more intrigue than a Nairobi VIP special.

And if you thought it couldn't get any worse, get this: 1,566 officers reportedly went unpaid during this period. KNH, the star player in this payroll drama, accounted for 1,135 of those unlucky souls. No explanation, of course. The government, it seems, is quite adept at both paying people who don't exist and not paying people who actually do the work. The total outstanding salary arrears? A cool Sh251.38 million. Meanwhile, the Employment Act dictates salaries should be paid at defined intervals, but clearly, that only applies to the plebs, not the well-connected.
