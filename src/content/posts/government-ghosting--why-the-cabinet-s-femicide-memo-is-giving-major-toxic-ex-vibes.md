Oh, look. Another deadline has come and gone, and the Kenyan government is officially ghosting us harder than a C-list influencer after a failed brand deal. Wednesday marked the end of the thirty-day window President William Ruto gave his office to submit that memorandum for the Gender Based Violence Taskforce report. It is giving very much "the check is in the mail" energy. While our leaders are busy posing for high-definition photos and pretending to care about the common mwananchi, the actual paperwork that could save lives is sitting in some dusty office, probably being used as a coaster for expensive tea.

Let’s get real for a second because the hypocrisy is thicker than a Nairobi socialite's foundation. We spend all day scrolling through "couple goals" posts and "soft life" aesthetics, but the data from Odipo Dev and Africa Uncensored tells a much nastier story. Apparently, your home is not your sanctuary - it is the most dangerous place you can be. Over 70 percent of femicides are committed at home by the very intimate partners who are likely posting "Happy Birthday Queen" captions on the gram just days before things turn lethal. It is a messy, tragic cycle that the "glamour" industry loves to ignore until it is time to post a black square for clout.

The report "Counting The Cost" is the kind of reality check that no one in the entertainment circles wants to face. While we are distracted by the latest industry beef or wondering if [Vera Hill and the Thirsty Masses: Why the 'ChiChi Call' is Just Another Basic Trap](/posts/vera-hill-and-the-thirsty-masses--why-the--chichi-call--is-just-another-basic-trap) is actually worth the hype, women are being stabbed and strangled. The response times from the police and the judiciary are slower than a dial-up connection in the nineties. It takes an average of four years to conclude a case in Nairobi or Mombasa? That is not "due process," that is a deliberate middle finger to the victims and their families.

This national crisis is not just a "women's issue" either, even though the patriarchy loves to frame it that way to avoid accountability. Look at what happened to Steve Godia in Umoja - scalded by his girlfriend and left with severe burns. The violence is everywhere, and it is messy, but the government’s reaction is predictably lukewarm. They have had the Hon. Nancy Baraza report for seven months now. Seven months! In the world of pop culture, seven months is enough time for a celebrity to get married, divorced, and launch a skincare line, yet the Cabinet cannot even submit a single memorandum.

It is time for the appointing authorities to stop acting like they are on a permanent vacation. We do not need more speeches or "technical working groups" that lead to nowhere. We need that national emergency strategy operationalized yesterday. The #EndFemicideKE movement and the Amnesty International crew are calling them out, and honestly, it is about time. If the government can move mountains to tax our side-hustles, they can surely find the time to prioritize the safety of the people. Submit the memorandum or admit that the "people-centered" rhetoric was just another scripted PR stunt.
