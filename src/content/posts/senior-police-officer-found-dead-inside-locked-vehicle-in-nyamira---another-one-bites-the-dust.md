SENIOR POLICE OFFICER FOUND DEAD INSIDE LOCKED VEHICLE IN NYAMIRA - ANOTHER ONE BITES THE DUST

Another day, another senior officer found dead in his whip. This time it's Allan Kandie, OCS at Kibugat Police Station, taking his final breath in a locked car in Nyamira. Apparently, some busybodies noticed his vehicle parked for too long and raised the alarm. Because in Nairobi, if you're parked longer than it takes to buy mandazi, something's wrong.

The "response" was swift, as always. Officers had to smash the window to get to the poor chap. Inside, there he was, lifeless on the front seat. No visible injuries, mind you, just some mucus oozing from the nose and an empty bottle of alcohol lurking under the passenger seat. Sounds like a classic "tough day at the office" scenario, doesn't it? He apparently left home at 3 AM, probably to start his "duty" early, or maybe just to get a head start on the drinking.

This whole situation just reeks of the usual Kenyan drama. You wonder how many of these "sudden deaths" among our esteemed officers are actually investigated properly, or if they just get filed away with a shrug and a "postmortem will tell." It's a shame, really, that the police force, which is supposed to be a pillar of order, seems to be riddled with its own issues. It makes you wonder if the shadows of duty are really impacting them as much as this article on [Kenya Police Posts Left in the Dark by Duty Shadows](/posts/kenya-police-posts-left-in-the-dark-by-duty-shadows---streamlinefeed-co-ke) suggested.

The body's cooling off at Our Lady of Mercy Ibara Mortuary, waiting for some doctor to tell us the obvious - he likely drank himself to death. The car's been impounded, no doubt to be dusted for prints that will lead to absolutely nowhere. Investigations are "ongoing," which is code for "we'll get to it when we get to it, maybe."
