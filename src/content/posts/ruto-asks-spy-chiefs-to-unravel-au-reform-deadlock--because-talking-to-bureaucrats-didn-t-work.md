Ruto's Spy Game: AU Reform Stuck, Now He's Asking the Shadow People Why

President Ruto, the grand champion of AU reforms, has apparently run out of ideas. Instead of, you know, actually *doing* something to fix the shambles that is the African Union, he's decided to have a chat with the continent's spymasters. Yes, the guys who are supposed to be watching our backs are now tasked with figuring out why the bigwigs can't agree on anything. It's like asking the bouncers why the party guests are fighting; they might know who started it, but they can't actually stop it.

He claims the AU is "not fit for purpose" - a revelation that has shocked absolutely no one in Nairobi. For years, we've watched these endless meetings, these grand pronouncements, and what has it achieved? Mostly just more paperwork and a bigger bill for taxpayers. Paul Kagame, bless his departing soul, already warned about the "parallel structures" designed to "frustrate and delay" reforms. It seems the AU bureaucracy is as stubborn as a matatu driver who's missed his stop.

Ruto's grand plan involves asking these intelligence chiefs to "figure out what kind of African Union that we need". As if they haven't got enough on their plates tracking down actual threats, now they’re supposed to solve the existential crisis of a bloated, inefficient bloc. He's even thinking of ditching the rotational chairmanship, which I'm sure will go down well with the leaders who use it to polish their egos. It’s all starting to sound like another one of those grand pronouncements that will end up buried under a pile of well-intentioned but ultimately useless reports. We’ve seen this movie before, remember? It feels a lot like [The Give to Gain Delusion: Why Nairobi’s Glass Ceiling Is Made of Reinforced Concrete](posts/the-give-to-gain-delusion--why-nairobi-s-glass-ceiling-is-made-of-reinforced-concrete).

The truth is, the AU's consensus-based decision-making is the real villain. One stubborn president, one "nyet" vote, and poof - all progress goes up in smoke. While Ruto is busy recruiting his intelligence allies, the real problem is the system itself, a system designed for endless deliberation, not decisive action. It’s a golden cage, and everyone’s too comfortable inside to break out.

The fact that countries like Niger and the Central African Republic are already fed up tells you everything you need to know. They’re feeling the AU’s failures directly. But instead of tackling the fundamental flaws, Ruto is taking the long, convoluted route through the shadows. It's a classic case of trying to fix the symptoms while ignoring the disease, all while promising a future that feels perpetually out of reach.

At least the intelligence chiefs will get a nice trip to Diani. Maybe they can use their downtime to brainstorm actual solutions, or perhaps just enjoy the coastal breeze while the continent's future remains stuck in neutral. It’s the African way, I suppose: talk, talk, talk, and hope for the best.
