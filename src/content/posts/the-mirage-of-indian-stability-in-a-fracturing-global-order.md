The US indictment of the Adani Group serves as a stark reminder that the global financial system is not a neutral playground. It is a controlled environment where Western regulators can dismantle Eastern giants with a single filing. This is not just about corporate governance; it is about the weaponization of the US legal system to remind India Inc. who ultimately holds the keys to overseas fundraising. Any pretense of a level playing field has been discarded in favor of raw power politics.

To make matters worse, the specter of a returning Trump administration has the Indian professional class in a state of controlled panic. The H-1B visa is being treated as a bargaining chip rather than a merit-based entry point. When the US decides to shut its doors, India-s labor surplus becomes a liability rather than an asset. The forced ingenuity of Indians trying to stay in the US is not a success story-it is a symptom of a nation that still relies on the table scraps of Western empires.

While China attempts to bypass the US financial hegemony by selling dollar bonds in Saudi Arabia and launching its own operating system, India remains tethered to a system that is actively hostile to its interests. The acquisition sprees of veterans like Reliance and L-T are less about expansion and more about building moats before the global storm hits. The markets might show momentum, but that is just the frantic movement of capital looking for any port in a storm before the lights go out.

The talk of Budget 2025 and expert advice is merely a distraction for the masses. Retail investors are being lured into a burning building with promises of high returns and momentum stocks. In a world where Huawei is building digital walls and the US is building physical ones, the RBI-s focus on domestic stability is nothing more than an admission that they have lost control over the external factors that actually matter. The global order is not just fracturing; it is being recycled into weapons of economic war.
