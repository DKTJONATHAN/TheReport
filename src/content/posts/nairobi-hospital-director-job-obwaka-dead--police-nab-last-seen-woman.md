Nairobi Hospital Director Job Obwaka Dead: Police Nab Last Seen Woman

The sudden death of a prominent Nairobi Hospital director has led to the arrest of a woman who was reportedly with him when he collapsed.

So, another big shot bites the dust. Dr. Job Obwaka, 83 and a gynaecologist at Nairobi Hospital, apparently keeled over in Kitengela after a spot of personal errands. Naturally, he wasn't alone; he was with a 45-year-old woman, who the police are now calling a "key person of interest" and have promptly locked up. Because, of course, when a wealthy old man collapses, the first thing you do is blame the woman he was last seen with. Standard procedure in this city.

The official police report, filed at Kilimani Police Station, paints a picture of a man being rushed to his own hospital, only to be declared dead on arrival. They mention he had a history of medical conditions, but the "exact cause of death remains undetermined." Which is code for "we don't know yet, but let's make it spicy." The scene was documented, processed, and the body is now at Lee Funeral Home, awaiting a postmortem. Because a good old autopsy is always the best way to find out what happened when someone drops dead, especially if there's a woman involved.

This whole drama comes hot on the heels of Obwaka’s recent headlines about alleged governance disputes at the hospital. Apparently, he and some other directors were arrested not too long ago. The Kenya Medical Practitioners, Pharmacists and Dentists Union (KMPDU) wasn't happy about it. Funny how these things unravel, isn't it? One minute you're in a boardroom spat, the next you're collapsing in Kitengela, a place that’s seen its fair share of questionable events. You might remember that [The Kitengela Bloodbath: New Year, Same Old Rungus](/posts/the-kitengela-bloodbath--new-year--same-old-rungus) piece we did, showing how that area can be a real magnet for trouble.

The arrested woman, described as a "business partner," is being held as investigations intensify. The DCI is on the case, which means they'll be digging and digging, looking for something, anything, to pin on her. Because in Nairobi, if you're the last one standing with a deceased prominent figure, you're automatically suspect number one, regardless of medical history or the general chaos of life. It’s a cynical world, and this death just adds another dark shade of grey to it.
