Shell's Safari Rally Drivers: Just Another Shell Game?

Oh, Kenya. We love our rallying, don't we? And bless Vivo Energy Kenya for splashing out on seven drivers for the 2026 Shell Autocross and, of course, the grand Safari Rally. Because nothing says "supporting Kenyan talent" quite like a massive fuel conglomerate slapping their name on a few cars, right? One can only hope this sponsorship is more substantial than the recent "investigation" into their V-Power fuel quality. Talk about a mixed message, Shell.

Patrick Masinde, their Retail Manager, chirped about "supporting Kenyan rally talent" at the Shell Northview station. Cute. Meanwhile, Shell's global stage is littered with partnerships with Ferrari and Hyundai Motorsport. So, is this a genuine investment in our local scene, or just a very elaborate way to make sure their brand is plastered all over the place while they figure out if their fuel is actually up to snuff? It’s giving "look over there!" vibes.

Let's not forget this is the same Shell that's also the title sponsor for the Motorsports Excellence Awards. Talk about covering all your bases. It makes you wonder if the "excellence" they're recognizing is actually for driving fast, or for being really, really good at getting sponsorships. Especially when you consider how much money is involved in these events. It’s almost as if these grand pronouncements of support are just a distraction from the real business of, well, business. It reminds me of the endless talk about infrastructure funds - [Sh5 Trillion For The Boys: The New National Infrastructure Fund Is Just A Bigger Plate](/posts/sh5-trillion-for-the-boys--the-new-national-infrastructure-fund-is-just-a-bigger-plate) - a lot of noise, but is it actually changing anything for the little guy?

And who are these lucky seven? Tinashe Gatimu/Caroline Gatimu, Rajveer Thethy/Laban Cliff Onserio, Lisa Christoffersen/Christabel Wacuka, Neel Gohil/Wayne Fernandez, Michuki Hinga/Mwangi Kioni, Ishmael Azeli/John Ngugi, and Suleiman Munyua. I’m sure they’re thrilled, but let's be real, is this about nurturing raw talent or just picking names that sound good on a press release? I'm not holding my breath for a deep dive into their training regimens.

The Safari Rally is a big deal, no doubt. But when a multinational giant like Shell gets involved, it’s always worth asking who's really benefiting. Is it the drivers, or is it Shell boosting its image and potentially distracting from… other issues? It’s a classic case of the shiny object syndrome, and we’re all just supposed to marvel at the sponsorship announcement without asking too many uncomfortable questions.

So, here's to the seven drivers. May your engines roar, your tires grip, and may your sponsorship deal actually translate into something more than just free fuel and a fancy press photo. Because in this game, as in life, it's often about who you know and whose name is attached to the biggest cheque.
