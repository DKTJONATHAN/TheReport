So, Infinix, the company that keeps churning out phones like matatus on Waiyaki Way, has decided Kenya’s booming creator economy is their next gold mine. They’re pushing this NOTE 60 Pro, a gadget loaded with enough megapixels to make you feel like a professional… until you realise you’re still using it to film your dusty Airbnb. Apparently, it charges like lightning, but let’s see how long that “self-healing battery” actually holds up. Remember when we thought those online kindness campaigns were revolutionary? Now it’s just another way to hawk products. [Viral Kindness in Kayole: Kenya's Fragile Safety Net Now a Digital Circus](/posts/viral-kindness-in-kayole--kenya-s-fragile-safety-net-now-a-digital-circus) taught us not to get too sentimental about digital trends.

They’re throwing in fancy tech like a secondary screen on the back, which is basically a glorified notification light, and a processor that sounds impressive but will probably be obsolete faster than a trending TikTok dance. The whole “professional-grade tools at accessible price points” is just marketing speak. Accessible for whom? The few who can actually afford KES 41,999 for a phone, or the masses who are still trying to get by? This “#CreatePro” campaign is just another slick way to get young Kenyans hooked on the latest gadget, feeding into the endless cycle of consumption.

And the influencers they’ve roped in? Collo Blue, Foi Wambui, Mutua Matheka - all talented, sure. But let’s be real, they’re there to make you feel like buying this phone will magically transform your mediocre selfies into award-winning masterpieces. They’re even dipping their toes into esports. Because apparently, mashing buttons furiously on a screen is now considered a legitimate creative pursuit worthy of sponsorship. It’s a broad definition of creativity, and a very convenient one for a phone company trying to sell phones.

The user-generated content challenge is the same old song and dance. Share your “creative work,” tag the brand, and maybe, just maybe, you’ll get a shoutout. It’s all about building hype and collecting data. By the time you realise this phone isn’t the key to your YouTube stardom, they’ll have already launched the NOTE 70 Pro. And in August, while you’re busy chasing likes with your new gadget, some bigwigs will be having breakfast forums about sustainability. Makes you wonder if they’re considering the environmental cost of this constant upgrade cycle.
