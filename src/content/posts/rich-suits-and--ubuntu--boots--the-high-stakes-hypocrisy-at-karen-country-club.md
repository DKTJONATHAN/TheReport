If you thought the only thing happening at Karen Country Club this week was a bunch of middle-aged men failing at their swing, think again. The NCBA Corporate Golf Day was less about the sport and more about the "strategic" cannibalism of Kenya’s biggest sectors. While you are counting coins for your next data bundle, the high-fliers were busy toasting to Nedbank taking a massive bite out of NCBA and Diageo passing the Tusker baton to the Japanese. It is the same old story: the rich get richer, the brands get foreign, and we get a front-row seat to the sell-out of the century.

James Gossip-yes, that is actually his name, and no, I could not make that up-was busy preaching about Kenya being a "natural anchor" for investment. It is funny how these "anchors" always seem to involve massive stakes being sold to international conglomerates while the local "mwananchi" is told to be "dynamic" and "digital-first." They are calling it "long-term value creation," but we all know it is just a polite way of saying the corporate elite are cashing out while the going is good.

Let us talk about the "UBUNTU Strategy 2026-2030." Because nothing screams "humanity towards others" like a bank that finances 60 percent of the sugar industry while the farmers are still waiting for a decent payday. This "belief-driven approach" is the latest corporate trend, much like how [THE APOLOGY PARASITE: WHY ELITE ATHLETES WEAPONIZE PERSONAL DRAMA TO STEAL THE SPOTLIGHT](/posts/the-apology-parasite--why-elite-athletes-weaponize-personal-drama-to-steal-the-spotlight) shows us how the famous use "empathy" to mask their own self-interest. It is a masterclass in performative kindness, wrapped in a luxury gift bag and handed out on a manicured lawn.

And because no corporate event is complete without a side of greenwashing, NCBA handed out 5,000 tree seedlings to the winners. I am sure those trees will totally offset the carbon footprint of the fuel-guzzling SUVs that crawled into Karen for this little shindig. They want to grow 10 million trees by 2030, but maybe they should start by growing some transparency about where all these "deepening capital flows" are actually landing. Spoiler alert: it is probably not in your savings account.

At the end of the day, the "Connect Plus" platform and the "sophisticated markets" are just more buzzwords to keep the masses distracted. While they play through on the eighteenth hole, the rest of the country is left wondering why "mutual growth" always seems to look like a one-way street heading straight to the bank. Stay cynical, Kenya. The grass might be green in Karen, but the motives are as muddy as a water hazard after a heavy rain.
