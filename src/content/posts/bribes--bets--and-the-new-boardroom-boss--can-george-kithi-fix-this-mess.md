Honey, the revolving door at Talanta Plaza just spat out a General and brought in a lawyer. George Kithi is the new man holding the purse strings for the Sports, Arts and Social Development Fund, and I am already yawning at the predictability of it all. Cabinet Secretary Salim Mvurya was all smiles handing over the keys to the kingdom on March 4, thanking Jackson Tuwei for his "exemplary leadership." Please. We all know that "exemplary" is government-speak for "he did not get caught doing anything we cannot explain away in a press release."

The SASDF is basically the piggy bank for every athlete and creative in this country, but the money comes from a place that is anything but creative: your gambling habits. Yes, dear, every time you lose your rent on a multi-bet, you are technically "funding the arts." With the fund sitting at a cool Sh13.5 billion, it is no wonder the suits are fighting for a seat at the table. Kithi comes in with all those fancy degrees from the University of Nairobi and Salford, but can he handle the stench of an Sh11 billion stadium scandal? 

It is hilarious how they talk about "nurturing talent" while the actual talent is out here starving or getting caught in the crossfire of poor management. If we are being honest, most of these appointments feel like another chapter in the same tired book of political rewards. As we have seen before, [Red Cards and Razzle Dazzle: Why Your Favorite 'Hustlers' Are Actually Just Common Thieves](/posts/red-cards-and-razzle-dazzle--why-your-favorite--hustlers--are-actually-just-common-thieves) is basically the national anthem for anyone watching how these funds are really spent. They promise us "meaningful and impactful work" while the budget for actual social development like health and education was quietly stripped away from the fund in 2022.

So, George, welcome to the shark tank. You have three years to prove you are more than just another High Court advocate looking for a lucrative side hustle in the "creative economy." Between the betting taxes and the global presence posturing, we will be watching to see if any of that Sh13.5 billion actually reaches the artists or if it just evaporates into the pockets of the well-connected. Stay cynical, darlings, because the glamor is usually just a very expensive coat of paint on a very leaky boat.
