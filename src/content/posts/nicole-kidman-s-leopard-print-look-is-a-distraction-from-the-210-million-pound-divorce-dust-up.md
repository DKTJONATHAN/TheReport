Nicole Kidman is currently stomping through New York in a leopard-print coat, looking for all the world like she does not have a single care in her Botox-to-perfection vicinity. She is supposedly "hunting" for criminals in her upcoming Amazon Prime series, Scarpetta, but we all know the real hunt is for a way to make a messy split look like a graceful exit. At 58, you would think she would be tired of the PR treadmill, yet here we are, watching her promote a forensic pathologist role while her own marriage gets autopsied by the tabloids.

The show drops next Wednesday, but the real drama is the finalization of her divorce from country star Keith Urban. They have officially called it quits, and now comes the fun part: dividing a staggering 210 million pound property portfolio. It turns out that when you are a "superstar pair," you do not just fight over who gets the cat or the good toaster. You fight over an entire skyline in Sydney and several multi-million pound mansions that most people could not even afford to walk past.

According to the legal papers, they are trying to play nice by keeping whatever properties are already in their respective names and splitting the rest to their "mutual satisfaction." We have heard that one before. It is funny how celebrities always find [Sophie Turner on why being at your lowest is liberating](/posts/sophie-turner-on-why-being-at-your-lowest-is-liberating) only after they realize they have enough real estate to house a small army. There is nothing quite as liberating as knowing you have several backup penthouses to cry in while the lawyers tally up the bill.

Their obsession with the Landmark Latitude complex in Sydney is particularly unhinged. They bought their sixth apartment in the same high-rise just last year for nearly 6 million pounds. Apparently, five luxury apartments in one building were not enough to save a marriage. They have over 13 million pounds wrapped up in that one complex alone, including a "home office" that probably cost more than your entire neighborhood. It is hoarding, but make it fashion, I suppose.

They first started buying into this Sydney block back in 2009, and they just could not stop themselves from floor-space-gluttony. From the 420-square-meter pad they snagged for 4.45 million pounds to the 5.2 million pound neighbor they added in 2012, it is a wonder they even ran into each other in the hallways. It is the ultimate Hollywood hypocrisy: singing about simple love and country roads while living in a 19th-floor fortress of solitude that requires a map to navigate.

Now that the paperwork is signed and the "mutual satisfaction" has been achieved, Nicole can get back to what she does best: playing gritty characters in expensive coats. Keith will likely go back to his guitars and whatever is left of his portion of the 210 million pounds. In the end, the leopard print is just camouflage for the fact that even the biggest stars are just regular people with very, very expensive lawyers and way too many sets of keys to return.
