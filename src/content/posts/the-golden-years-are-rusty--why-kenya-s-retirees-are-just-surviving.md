The Retirement Benefits Authority wants us to believe everything is fine because 51 percent of retirees claim life is better after work. Better than what? Better than a 40-year grind in a dusty office with a boss who couldn't remember your name? When 32 percent of people say their top priority is just staying alive through personal health, that is not a success story. It is a confession that our healthcare system is a predatory beast waiting to pounce the moment you stop receiving a monthly payslip.

Then you have the 21 percent who are obsessed with the basic act of buying food. Let that sink in. These people worked for three decades just to reach a stage where their primary goal is not travel or leisure, but avoiding starvation. It is the same kind of systemic neglect we see in other sectors, much like the [institutional betrayal of our youth](/posts/truphena-wambui-72-hours-of-resilience) that leaves every generation fending for itself against a cold, bureaucratic machine that prefers statistics over human dignity.

The "Black Tax" never truly dies in this country; it just changes form and gets heavier. Fourteen percent of these retirees are still educating their children, and four percent are even paying for grandchildren. In a functional society, your kids should be the ones taking you to Diani for a holiday, not asking for tuition fees while you are trying to manage your blood pressure. This is not a testament to family values. It is a glaring sign that the economy has failed to provide jobs for the young, forcing the old to bleed their pensions dry.

Let us talk about that 22 percent who are brave enough to admit life is worse now. These are the honest ones who realized too late that a pension statement is just a piece of paper that cannot buy a single dose of insulin in a private clinic. The RBA can polish these numbers and talk about "healthcare security" all they want, but the reality on the streets of Nairobi is that retirement is just a slower, more expensive way to realize the government never had your back to begin with.
