So, the Americans, with their fancy Human Rights Foundation, are finally noticing what we've been saying all along. Kenya, our beloved country, is allegedly selling passports to Sudanese war financiers and anyone with ties to the so-called Rapid Support Forces. You know, the genocidal lot. And our government? Radio silence. Typical.

Apparently, President Ruto's "alliance" with these characters is so tight, he's willing to risk implicating us all in helping them dodge international sanctions. More than 25 Sudanese nationals, including some heavy hitters like Algoney Hamdan Daglo Musa, Hemdeti's brother, have supposedly gotten Kenyan passports. These are the same people accused of buying weapons, financing militias, and, you know, committing atrocities. But hey, peanuts, right? That's what some activist said, and who can blame him when you see these deals happening?

This isn't even new. A leaked list showed passports being processed for Sudanese and Zimbabwean nationals with questionable backgrounds. We’re talking RSF links and corruption accusations. It's like a shady international swap meet happening right under our noses, and the irony isn't lost on me, especially with the current discussions around [AFCON 2025 vs AFCON 2027: Can East Africa Match Morocco’s Gold Standard?](/posts/afcon-2025-morocco-vs-afcon-2027-east-africa-ambitions). Africa trying to shine on the global stage while our own house is a mess.

Kenyans are understandably furious, questioning how these documents were issued. Leaders are warning about the damage to our passport's credibility. But the people in charge of immigration and the interior ministry? Still no detailed response. They’re probably busy counting the proceeds, or maybe they're just hoping we'll all forget about it by next week. It's the Kenyan way, after all.

Now that a US-based rights group has chimed in, the pressure will surely ramp up. More scrutiny, more noise. Let's see if our leaders can actually be bothered to explain why we’re apparently becoming a haven for war criminals and corrupt officials. My money's on them finding a way to blame someone else. It's always easier than taking responsibility.
