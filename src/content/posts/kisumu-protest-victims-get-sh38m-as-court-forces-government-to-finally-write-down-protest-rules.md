So, the High Court in Kisumu, in its infinite wisdom, has decided that our dear government needs to get its act together and write down some actual rules for public demonstrations and the police's penchant for using "force." Apparently, 90 days is enough time to sort out the chaos that has been brewing, all in the name of "certainty" for our rights under Article 37 and "clear guidelines" for the National Police Service. Because, you know, clarity is what we're all about in this country.

And what do these proposed rules bring? Fines, of course. Up to a hundred grand, or three months in the slammer, if you dare to protest outside the designated zones. Because freedom of expression apparently comes with a price tag and a preferred postcode. Let's not forget the proposed Bill, which will ensure that if Parliament gets its way, those punitive fines will be a reality. It’s all about managing the narrative, isn't it?

Meanwhile, investigations into whatever mess prompted this whole song and dance are still going nowhere, thanks to a delightful cocktail of propaganda, negative perceptions, and everyone pointing fingers at each other. It’s a classic Kenyan special, a toxic blend that keeps the wheels of justice firmly stuck in the mud. Perhaps it's all connected to the "Shiny Rocks and Shady Bots" we've been hearing about, which seem to be causing quite a stir among the elite.

The push for aggressive tech solutions also continues, all while the public pressure mounts. It's the usual playbook: distract, delay, and then offer a shiny new gadget to solve a problem that requires fundamental change. Ninety days. Let's see how much actual change happens in that time. My bet? Not much beyond a few more official-looking documents and perhaps a new set of excuses.
