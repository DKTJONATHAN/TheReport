President Ruto is back at it again, acting like the head prefect of the international community. While we are out here struggling with the price of unga and wondering why our favorite influencers are still faking their lifestyles on Instagram, the President is busy condemning strikes in the UAE, Qatar, and Saudi Arabia. Honestly, his "defining and perilous moment" social media post felt more like a scripted monologue from a mid-budget political thriller than an actual policy statement. It is giving major "I want a Nobel Peace Prize" energy while the rest of us are just trying to survive another chaotic Monday in this city.

It is absolutely hilarious how we are being called to "de-escalate" global conflicts when the vibes in our own capital are practically radioactive. If you think the Middle East is the only place with a crisis, you clearly have not been paying attention to the local scene lately. Let us be real for a second: [Nairobi Is No Sanctuary For The Brave](/posts/nairobi-is-no-sanctuary-for-the-brave) especially when you realize that our leaders care more about their Twitter impressions in Bahrain and Oman than the actual safety of the people they represent at home. The hypocrisy is so thick you could cut it with a blunt knife, yet we are supposed to applaud this diplomatic cosplay.

Meanwhile, the local media is trying so hard to keep us distracted with a buffet of "thought leadership" and "bold" programming. We have Janet Mbugua and Eric Lattif trying to "Fix the Nation" while Dr. King’ori uses "humour and relatable wit" to mask the fact that we are all collectively losing our minds. It is the classic Kenyan entertainment cycle: the government ignores the local fire to go play firefighter abroad, and the media creates a thousand podcasts like "Speaking of Gen Z" to discuss why we are all so stressed out. They want to "Business Redefine" the economy on NTV, but most of us are just redefining how many meals we can skip.

I see you, Anyiko Owoko, giving us "VIP Access" to music stars and "timeless interviews" while the digital creative economy is basically just people navigating the gig world by the skin of their teeth. We are out here listening to "Letter To My Younger Self" and crying about adulthood struggles, but who is writing a letter to the current administration telling them to focus on the home front? The irony of promoting "Paradise Lost" through the story of Louis Otieno while the current state of our entertainment industry is its own version of a lost paradise is not lost on me. Everyone is chasing a "premium content" tag while the reality is strictly budget.

At the end of the day, whether it is "Gumzo La Sato" or "The Politalk," the narrative remains the same. We are being fed a diet of high-stakes global drama and low-stakes lifestyle banter to keep us from asking why the "regionalisation of conflict" matters more than the regionalisation of empty pockets. Stay woke, because while they are "unpacking matters" of finance policy on TV, your favorite celebrity is probably one bad brand deal away from a "Health Diary" episode. The glamor is a facade, the politics is a performance, and the only thing being "de-escalated" is our bank balances.
