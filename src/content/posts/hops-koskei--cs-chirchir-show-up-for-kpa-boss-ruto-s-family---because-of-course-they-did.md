So, the KPA boss, Captain William Ruto, decided a holiday trip to Arusha with the family was a good idea. Turns out, it ended in a rather nasty collision with a parked trailer. A 18-year-old daughter, Mitchell Jelimo Kemboi, didn't make it. The rest of the family got to sample the joys of Kenyan hospital food before being discharged, probably with a hefty bill and a side of trauma.

Naturally, when one of the big wigs goes through something like this, the usual suspects have to show up. Chief of Staff Felix Koskei and Roads CS Davis Chirchir graced the scene with their presence. You know, to offer their condolences. Because what else are they going to do? Actually *fix* the roads that lead to these kinds of accidents? Nah, that's too much effort.

It's always the same script, isn't it? Something bad happens, and the suits descend, all furrowed brows and handshakes. They'll make all the right noises, pose for the cameras, and then go back to their air-conditioned offices. It’s a performance, a public relations exercise. Does it ease the pain of losing a child? Doubtful. But it makes them look like they care, which is the important part for them.

This whole episode just highlights the usual chaos. A family heading for a holiday, a stationary trailer on the road - a recipe for disaster that’s all too common here. It makes you wonder if anyone’s actually paying attention to the state of our infrastructure, or if they’re too busy with the theatrics. The young Jelimo will be laid to rest in Kabarak. Another life cut short, another family left to pick up the pieces. And the officials? They'll have their photo ops, their well-rehearsed words of sympathy, and then the news cycle will move on. Because in Nairobi, a tragedy is just another headline until the next one comes along.
