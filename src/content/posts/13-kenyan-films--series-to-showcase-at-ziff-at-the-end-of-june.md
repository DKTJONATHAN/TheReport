13 Kenyan Films, Series Get ZIFF Spotlight - Because Someone Has To

ZIFF, the Zanzibar International Film Festival, is rolling out the red carpet for 13 Kenyan creative works from June 24 to June 27. This year's theme, "AI and the Art of Storytelling," is just as exciting as trying to predict the next big drama on *Single Kiasi*. Apparently, these films are supposed to be "catalysts for regional social and economic growth." Let’s see if they can even catalyze a decent plot.

The festival, also known as the ‘Festival of the Dhow Countries,’ is trotting out a lineup of Kenyan talent. We've got two short films vying for glory, three documentaries that might bore us to tears, and one feature film that probably cost more to promote than to make. Then there are five Kenyan series, which means more endless scrolling and probably more questionable acting. And to top it off, two more shorts are showing, just so we can be extra sure we've seen enough.

Among the hopefuls in the feature fiction category are titles like *Bobo*, *The People Shall*, *Kikuyu Land*, *After the Abyss*, *Mizani*, and *Lazizi*. Apparently, the Kenyan film industry thinks these are the best we've got to offer. We also have *Kash Money Season 1* and *Single Kiasi Season 4*, proving that even when discussing the future of storytelling with AI, some things never change - like the struggle to make a decent series.

Then there's *Adam to Eve*, *Goat*, and *Our Brother* all hoping to impress. Let's not forget *Last Broadcast From Earth* and *The Swap*, which are conveniently tucked away out of competition, so nobody has to feel too bad if they’re terrible. It’s all very prestigious, of course, but it’s worth remembering that even with all this cultural output, we’ve still got desperate situations back home, like that Ghanaian woman trying to traffic a child to Kenya - another day, another hustle.

ZIFF, June 24-27, Zanzibar. AI and Storytelling. Will any of these films actually say something meaningful, or will it just be more noise? Let's just hope the free snacks are good.
