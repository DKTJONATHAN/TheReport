And just like that, the circus rolls into town, a full nine years early. Rift Valley leaders, led by Nandi Governor Stephen Sang, have decided that Moses Wetang'ula is the next big thing for 2032. Because, you know, planning for a presidential bid almost a decade out is exactly what Kenyans need right now. As if 2027 isn't already going to be a battle royale, these folks are playing political chess on a board that hasn't even been fully laid out.

They're showering Wetang'ula with praise, crediting him for Ruto's 2022 victory and fawning over his 'distinguished' leadership of the National Assembly. Please. This isn't about Wetang'ula's stellar performance-it's about positioning, backroom deals, and making sure their own seats at the gravy train are secured for the foreseeable future. The script is as old as the hills, and the lines are delivered with the usual practiced insincerity.

Governor Sang, Melly, Adagala-all singing from the same hymn sheet, talking about 'leadership, experience, and statesmanship.' What they're really talking about is leverage. This isn't a genuine endorsement of a future leader; it's a strategic move to show loyalty, demand favors, and keep the money flowing. While the rest of us are sweating over the price of unga and rent, these political elites are already swinging their influence, much like the [While You Sweat, They Swing: The Great Kenyan Sell-off at Karen](/posts/while-you-sweat--they-swing--the-great-kenyan-sell-off-at-karen) that never seems to end.

Let's be real. 2032 is a lifetime in Kenyan politics. Many a political career has withered and died in less time. These early declarations are nothing more than noise, a distraction from the real governance issues we face today. It's the same old cycle of self-interest parading as public service, and the only people who truly benefit are the ones making the pronouncements. Don't hold your breath for any meaningful change.
