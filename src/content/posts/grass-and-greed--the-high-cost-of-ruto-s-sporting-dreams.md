President Ruto stood at the lush Karen Country Club on Sunday, surrounded by the elite and the smell of expensive cologne, to tell us that stadiums are the cure for drug abuse. It is a classic Nairobi move - provide a playground when the people are asking for a kitchen. He claims that spending billions on the Talanta Sports City, soon to be named after Raila Odinga, is for the youth. In reality, it is just another way to recycle political favors while the rest of us wonder where the next rent payment is coming from.

The government is obsessed with "modern infrastructure" while the basic survival of the average mwananchi is ignored. They want to build 21 more facilities across the country, even after Parliament tried to block a 4 billion shilling budget hike for the 2027 Afcon hosting. It is hard to take these sporting dreams seriously when our national sovereignty feels like it is being auctioned off. Just look at how [Borrowed Light: Kenya’s 'Independence' is Now a Subscription Service to Ethiopia](/posts/borrowed-light--kenya-s--independence--is-now-a-subscription-service-to-ethiopia) highlights our growing reliance on others for basic needs. We are building world-class tracks for a population that is too tired from walking to work to use them.

Then there is the joke of the new golf curriculum. Ruto thinks golf is the future for kids in the slums who can barely afford a school uniform. He says it is no longer just a sport for the rich, yet the winner of the Kenya Open took home 60 million shillings while the best Kenyan finisher, Njoroge Kibugu, was rewarded with what amounts to political pocket change. It is easy to talk about "tapping talent" when you are not the one wondering if the "talent" can afford the bus fare to the training ground.

Naming a stadium after Raila Odinga is the ultimate cynical cherry on top. It is a distraction tactic designed to keep the old guard happy while the youth are told to go play outside so they do not notice the economy collapsing. We do not need 60,000 seats to watch a game; we need 60,000 jobs so we can afford to buy a ticket. The "social vices" the President is so worried about are not caused by a lack of stadiums, but by the crushing weight of a government that prefers concrete to people.
