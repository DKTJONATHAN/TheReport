So, Iran decides to lob some cluster bombs at Tel Aviv because Israel "assassinated" their security chief. Big deal. Ali Larijani, gone. So what? This is the same song and dance we always hear, just with different names and places. Apparently, these cluster warheads are a nightmare to intercept, scattering like shrapnel from a broken promise. Two dead, they say. Just a warm-up, I'm sure, before the next big escalation. The way things are going, it's like watching some madmen play with matches next to a petrol station, and then acting surprised when it all goes up in flames.

And then there's the nuclear power plant near Bushehr. No damage, no injuries, just a little oopsie near a place that could literally blow us all up. The IAEA chief is wringing his hands, calling for "maximum restraint." Restraint? In this part of the world? That's like asking a hyena to practice vegetarianism. Israel and the US are all about stopping Iran's nukes, but they're busy killing Iran's top brass. It’s a classic case of [GOVERNANCE BY VIBES: HOW FKF TURNED THE LEAGUE INTO A VILLAGE KIOSK](https://example.com/posts/governance-by-vibes--how-fkf-turned-the-league-into-a-village-kiosk), where logic took a permanent holiday.

The new supreme leader in Iran, Mojtaba Khamenei, isn't interested in peace talks. He wants the US and Israel to grovel, admit defeat, and pay up. Because that's how peace is made, right? By humiliation and reparations. Meanwhile, the death toll keeps climbing, with thousands dead in Iran and hundreds in Lebanon. The Strait of Hormuz is shut down, oil prices are through the roof, and the World Food Programme is warning of mass hunger. Everyone's scrambling, but nobody wants to get their hands dirty.

Trump, bless his little ego, is out there complaining that NATO and other allies are being "foolish" for not jumping into this mess. Apparently, he doesn't "need" their help, even though he's the one who started it. The EU foreign policy chief is saying nobody wants to risk their people's lives for some oil tankers. Shocking, I know. Diplomacy? Food crises? Fertilizer shortages? Nah, let's just keep bombing each other and see what happens. It’s a much more efficient way to ruin lives.

The US has been bouncing around reasons for this war, and legal bases seem to be a foreign concept. The head of their counterterrorism center even resigned, saying Iran wasn't an immediate threat. But hey, why let facts get in the way of a good old-fashioned conflict? Iran, in return, is lashing out at its neighbours, hitting everything from diplomatic missions to oil infrastructure. Saudi Arabia is calling for a meeting, probably to discuss how to avoid the inevitable fallout, or maybe just to look busy.

So, Iran attacks Tel Aviv, Israel likely retaliates, the world economy weeps, and ordinary people get caught in the crossfire. Just another day in the neighbourhood. The only thing predictable is the chaos, and the fact that someone, somewhere, is making a fortune off all this misery. It’s the way of the world, and pretending otherwise is just naive.
