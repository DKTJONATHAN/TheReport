So, Mitchelle Joyce Akoth Oruko, or Mjaka Mfine as the internet knows her, has decided to play dress-up. In her latest stunt, she’s rocking a man’s haircut and a goatee at a barbershop. The online masses, apparently easily impressed, are calling it a "daring transformation" and "nailing it." Honestly, in this city, seeing someone change their hairstyle is about as shocking as a politician making a promise.

The buzz is all about how she’s flipped the script, with mostly women apparently "drooling" over her new masculine persona. It’s a reversal of the usual male comedians who slap on some lipstick for a cheap laugh. Mjaka has apparently found her calling in… well, looking like a bloke. I suppose in a world drowning in content, anything to stand out, right? It’s not exactly rocket science, or, dare I say, the kind of insightful commentary you’d find about, say, [Ruto's Western Tour: New Lights For Old Promises](/posts/ruto-s-western-tour--new-lights-for-old-promises).

Let's not get too carried away. This is a content creator. Their job is to create content. And if a fresh haircut and a bit of facial hair gets the clicks and the "buzz," then mission accomplished. It’s a visual gag, plain and simple. The fact that it has "ladies" supposedly drooling just tells you more about the current state of online entertainment than it does about Mjaka’s artistic genius.

Meanwhile, while Mjaka is busy with her follicular explorations, other stories are unfolding. There’s the usual drama: a lady calling out a Senior Surgeon for being a deadbeat dad despite his apparent wealth. Sounds about right for Nairobi. And somewhere in Eldoret, some “Kikuyu beauties” are just trying to enjoy a Mugithi event. You see, life goes on, with or without a goatee.
