So, the Football Kenya Federation Premier League has decided to grace us with another thrilling round of “will they, won’t they” at the top of the table. AFC Leopards, bless their hopeful hearts, are sniffing around the summit after three months in the wilderness. Meanwhile, Gor Mahia, the perennial overachievers, are trying to cling on like a desperate politician to power. It’s all happening at Nyayo National Stadium, where Ingwe is set to "host" Kenya Police. A win for Leopards means they'll taste the sweet, fleeting air of first place. Don't get too excited, it's probably just a temporary reprieve.

Tomorrow, the real drama unfolds - or rather, it doesn't. Gor Mahia, currently perched atop the pile with a comfortable two-point cushion, welcomes Bandari to Kasarani. Gor's coach, Charles Akonnor, has bravely vowed they won't let Ingwe slip past them. Bless his optimism. He clearly hasn't spent enough time in this city to understand that consistency is a myth, much like affordable housing. We've seen this dance before; Gor surges, Gor stumbles, and then suddenly it's a dogfight. Remember when they had a nine-point lead? Gone. Just like that. This whole chase reminds me of our politicians promising change - a lot of noise, very little substance.

The motivation for Leopards is clear: their first title since 1998. A noble pursuit, I suppose, if you ignore the decades of mediocrity. But they'd be wise to remember Kenya Police, the "law enforcers," are lurking in fourth place. Leopards have a dismal record against them, winning only once in their last five meetings. So, while they're dreaming of the trophy, the reality might be another dose of disappointment served with a side of police brutality, metaphorically speaking. This whole league is a microcosm of Kenya, really - a constant struggle with fleeting moments of perceived success, and the ever-present possibility of external forces derailing progress. It’s a bit like our city, Nairobi, drowning in its own mess while the politicians float empty promises, as seen in [/posts/nairobi-is-drowning-and-the-politicians-are-only-floating-words].

And then there's the side show: Shabana bringing back Osborne Monday. Fired from Murang’a Seal, now an assistant somewhere else. The merry-go-round of Kenyan football management never ceases to amuse. While the big boys battle it out, the rest are just trying to survive, like many businesses in this country struggling to stay afloat amidst economic shifts. Meanwhile, Kariobangi Sharks, bless their consistent hearts, have had the same coach since 2016. A rare display of stability in a chaotic landscape. But don't expect them to be celebrating titles anytime soon. This league is a theatre of the absurd, where hope is a currency that's perpetually devalued.
