Let us be real for a second. The modern pulpit has more in common with a Coachella stage than a place of worship. Whether you are in Nairobi or Nashville, the gospel has been rebranded as a luxury lifestyle choice. In Kenya, we have pastors who treat their congregations like ATMs, while in the US, they have turned the Bible into a campaign flyer. It is all about the brand, the aesthetic, and, of course, the collection plate. If you think your favorite televangelist is there for your soul, I have a bridge in Shakahola to sell you.

Take a look at the local scene. We have Victor Kanyari and his legendary KSh 310 "seed" scandal. It was basically the original crowdfunding scam before GoFundMe even existed. Then there is James Maina Ng’ang’a and Ezekiel Odero, who have turned the ministry into a high-stakes performance art piece. These men are not just preachers; they are the ultimate influencers. They know exactly how to trigger the algorithm of fear and desperation to keep the "donations" rolling in. It is messy, it is loud, and it is incredibly profitable.

But the Americans are not doing any better. Over there, the church has basically become a PR wing for the Republican party. You have figures like Paula White-Cain and Franklin Graham acting like they are on a press tour for a political candidate rather than a deity. They talk about "whiteness" and "nationalism" like they are reading from a script written by a spin doctor. It is the ultimate crossover episode nobody asked for: Jesus meets the MAGA hat. The hypocrisy is so thick you could cut it with a designer communion wafer.

The spiritual hustle is exhausting. It is the same cycle of empty promises and predatory billing. Much like how [the 24-hour economy is a mirage and your subscription is a ransom note](/posts/the-24-hour-economy-is-a-mirage-and-your-subscription-is-a-ransom-note), these religious empires treat followers like recurring revenue streams rather than human beings. You are not a sheep in a flock; you are a monthly active user in a database. If you stop paying your "tithes," do not be surprised when your access to "divine favor" gets canceled.

The darkness gets even deeper when you look at the Shakahola tragedy. Paul Mackenzie took the ultimate "stan" culture to a lethal extreme, convincing people to starve for a VIP pass to heaven. It is what happens when we stop questioning the "celebrity" status of these leaders. When the pulpit becomes a stage for staged miracles and profane outbursts, the credibility of the entire industry takes a hit. Scripture says you shall know them by their fruits, but all I see is a whole lot of rotten produce.

At the end of the day, whether it is financial power in Kenya or political power in the States, the "soul" of the faith has been traded for a seat at the table. These leaders are more interested in their global travel and "divine favor" credentials than actual pastoral care. It is all about the spectacle, the revenue, and the supremacy. So, before you send that next mobile money transfer to a "prophet," ask yourself: are you funding a mission, or are you just paying for someone's business-class flight?
