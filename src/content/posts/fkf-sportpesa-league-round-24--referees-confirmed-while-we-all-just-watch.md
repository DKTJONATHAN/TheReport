So, the Football Kenya Federation, in all its infinite wisdom, has decided to grace us with the names of the people who'll be pretending to officiate the SportPesa League this coming weekend. Apparently, they need cookies to make sure the bots don't clog up their site. Because that's the real problem, isn't it? Not the state of the game itself. From Friday the 13th to Sunday the 15th, we'll have a parade of referees supposedly ensuring fair play. Let's see how that goes.

The weekend kicks off with Posta Rangers versus Kenya Police at Kasarani Annex. Fancy names, same old story. Then it's a full slate, culminating in Ulinzi Stars vs. Gor Mahia, which is always a spectacle, usually of the questionable refereeing kind. The FKF, bless their hearts, are dedicated to "developing and promoting the beautiful game." Which, translated, probably means collecting membership fees and issuing directives nobody truly follows.

We've got a whole lineup of matches, each with its own appointed commissioner and referee. Michael Mwangi will oversee Posta Rangers vs. Kenya Police. Kelvin Maina will be at Bandari vs. Mara Sugar. Brook Philips will try to keep order at APS Bomet vs. Bidco United. Beatrice Oganga will be at Sofapaka vs. Nairobi United. And so on. It's a veritable who's who of people whose decisions will likely be debated more than the price of maize.

And let's not forget the "rescheduled" match for Wednesday the 18th. Because nothing screams efficiency like shuffling fixtures. It makes you wonder if this whole operation is run on more than just cookies. Perhaps it's fueled by the same kind of systemic rot that sees [The Law Enforcers Win On The Pitch While The Rest Of Us Lose On The Streets](/posts/the-law-enforcers-win-on-the-pitch-while-the-rest-of-us-lose-on-the-streets).

The FKF claims their use of cookies is to enhance our browsing experience and protect against automated bots. Right. Because clearly, the biggest threat to Kenyan football isn't corruption or mismanagement, but rogue computer programs. They're so focused on the digital pests, they probably miss the real ones eating away at the sport from the inside.

So, there you have it. The official list of who's who on the pitch. Whether it translates to a decent game of football or just another weekend of predictable drama remains to be seen. But hey, at least we'll have names to blame when things inevitably go south.
