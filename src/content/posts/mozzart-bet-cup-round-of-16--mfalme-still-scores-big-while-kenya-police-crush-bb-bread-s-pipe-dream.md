Giant killers BB Bread, whose "fairytale run" had everyone buzzing, got a rude awakening. Kenya Police FC, a "top-flight side" with all the luck of the draw, managed to scrape a 1-0 win. "Experience edged it," they say. Or maybe just having a deeper pocket and a team that doesn't have to worry about whether they can afford to travel to the next match. BB Bread exited with their heads held high, I’m sure. That’s what you say when you get booted out.

Then there's Mfalme FC, the "Division One outfit" that "stunned" Murang'a Seal with a 5-1 thrashing. "No signs of inferiority," they claimed. Dominating proceedings. Clinical finishing. Sounds like someone forgot to tell them they were supposed to lose. Still, a 5-1 win for a lower-tier team? Impressive, I guess. But don't get too excited. These are the same kinds of stories we see before reality bites. Shabana FC also did their bit by knocking out Kuta Collection FC, and Nairobi United saw off Posta Rangers. All very… predictable.

As we head into the quarter-finals, they’re hyping up the "mix of established giants and fearless underdogs." The same old song and dance. The Mozzart Bet Cup might be a tournament where "anything can happen," but what usually happens is that the teams with the most resources and the most connections tend to win.
