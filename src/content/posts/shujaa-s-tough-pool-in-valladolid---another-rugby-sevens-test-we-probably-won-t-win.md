So, Shujaa are apparently going to Spain for some rugby sevens thing in Valladolid. And naturally, they’ve been thrown into the deep end of Pool A. We're talking South Africa, Great Britain, and Australia. This isn't a "major test," it's a public humiliation waiting to happen. South Africa will likely steamroll them, Britain will play their boring disciplined game, and Australia will run rings around them. Just another day in Kenyan sports, really.

They're trying to spin this as building on "positives" from Hong Kong. Spirited displays? Attacking threat? Please. They got beaten 14-5 by Australia there, exposing all their usual defensive blunders and turning their supposed attack into a slow crawl. It’s the same old song and dance - a few flashes of effort followed by the inevitable crash and burn. Don't expect miracles, especially when we're talking about improving consistency and making "key moments." That's like asking a boda boda rider to win the Grand Prix.

The article mentions Great Britain might be their "best chance" for a win because they finished lower in Hong Kong. Oh, the optimism! Britain might have ended up below Kenya in the standings, but that doesn't mean they'll roll over now. This is the World Championship, not a friendly kick-about. And let's not forget that "fighting spirit" in Hong Kong that somehow got them to the quarter-finals after beating the USA. That was a fluke, a brief moment where the stars aligned before reality reasserted itself.

The goal of a top-eight finish? Cute. With this draw, even a top-ten finish would be a minor miracle. We’ve seen this before, haven't we? Remember when the news about the potential [US-Israel-Iran War] had everyone worried about how Nairobi's pockets would empty? This is the sporting equivalent - you see the potential for disaster, and then it happens. Let's just brace ourselves for the usual post-tournament analyses of what went wrong, the promises of improvement, and the same old lineup come next season.
