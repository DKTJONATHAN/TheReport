Sounds about as authentic as a reality TV reunion special, doesn't it? I mean, who are they really trying to impress here? My guess is it’s less about celebrating genuine artistry and more about lining the pockets of the same old industry players who’ve been milking the "African renaissance" narrative for years. They’ll probably have some carefully curated "identity" on display, because God forbid we see anything remotely messy or unmarketable.

And let's talk about these "cookies" they keep mentioning in their fine print. Apparently, they're essential for basic site functionality. Sure, Jan. It’s all about control, darling, just like how some leaders try to control the narrative. Anyone expecting a true, raw explosion of African talent is probably going to be disappointed. This sounds less like a celebration and more like a polished product, meticulously designed for maximum commercial appeal and minimum genuine impact.

It’s a performance, a carefully constructed illusion.
