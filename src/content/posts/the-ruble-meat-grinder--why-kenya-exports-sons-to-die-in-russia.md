Only in Kenya do we export human beings like they are surplus tea leaves, then act shocked when they come back in boxes. Dennis Bagaka Ombwori and Oscar Ojiambo are the latest names on a list that is growing faster than the national debt. These men were not looking for glory; they were looking for a way out of the Nairobi grind. They thought they were signing up for security jobs in Qatar, but instead, they found themselves in the freezing trenches of Eastern Europe. It is the ultimate Kenyan tragedy: being so desperate to escape the "hustle" that you run straight into a drone strike.

The recruiters promised them 350,000 shillings a month and a Russian passport, which is a lot of money for someone with no prospects. They should have known that [Lessons from Azziad: How Life Decisions Shape Your Destiny](/posts/lessons-from-azziad-life-decisions-shape-your-destiny) usually involve more than just a quick buck, but when you are starving, you do not read the fine print. These recruits were given nine days of preparation and a rifle. That is not a career move; it is a suicide mission with a slightly better paycheck.

Now we have the National Intelligence Service dropping reports like they just discovered a fire that has been burning for months. Over 1,000 Kenyans are out there, with dozens already missing or dead. The MPs in Parliament are acting "extremely shocked" for the cameras, yet Majority Leader Kimani Ichung-wah is already pointing fingers at embassy officials. It is the same old Nairobi story - when things go south, everyone starts looking for someone else to blame while the brokers who cashed the recruitment checks are nowhere to be found.

The Russian Embassy claims they have nothing to do with "rogue schemes," and that they do not issue visas for military purposes. If you believe that, I have a bridge in Nyali to sell you. With recruits traveling through Uganda and South Africa to avoid detection, this is clearly a highly organized human trafficking pipeline. Our government can track a five-shilling mobile money tax with surgical precision, but they somehow failed to notice hundreds of former KDF soldiers disappearing to Moscow.

In Sikonga village and the backstreets of Nairobi, mothers are wailing for sons who will likely never have a grave. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs says they are planning a trip to Russia to "negotiate," but diplomacy is for people with leverage, not for people who provide spare parts for someone else's war. The "hustler" dream has officially gone global, and it is turning out to be a nightmare written in Cyrillic for families who just wanted a better life.
