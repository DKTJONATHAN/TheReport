Welcome to the Kenyan theatre of the absurd. The DCI is busy "sensitizing" officers at the Kenya School of Government in Embu and some lush spot in Naivasha. While the rest of us are struggling with the cost of living, our detectives are being "equipped" with knowledge on how to spot money laundering. It is a bit late for that, isn't it? The wash-wash culture in Nairobi is so ingrained that you can smell the bleach on the currency from kilometers away.

This sudden rush to learn about financial intelligence comes only after the Financial Action Task Force (FATF) dumped Kenya on the "grey list." It is the international version of being put on probation because you cannot stop playing with dirty money. We have been caught with our hands in the cookie jar, or rather, our financial systems are so porous that even a child could move illicit billions through them without a single hiccup.

Director Amin talks about disrupting criminal networks and denying offenders the proceeds of crime, but we have heard this song before. It is the same lack of backbone we see in other sectors, much like how [GHANA ACTS WHILE KENYA NAPS: THE COWARDICE OF OUR DIPLOMACY](/posts/ghana-acts-while-kenya-naps--the-cowardice-of-our-diplomacy). Instead of actual arrests of the big fish who fund political campaigns with blood money, we get four-day seminars and nice hotel lunches paid for by the taxpayer.

They say they are training everyone from the Inspectorate to the senior ranks in "asset tracing and recovery." In this city, asset tracing usually means finding out which politician’s mistress owns which apartment block in Kilimani. It is hard to believe that a few sessions at KARLO in Naivasha will suddenly turn a system designed for kickbacks into a bastion of transparency and accountability. 

Let us be real. The sophisticated and borderless financial crimes the DCI is worried about are often happening with the tacit approval of the same institutions meant to stop them. Unless these "sensitization" programs end with actual handcuffs on people who matter, this is just another per-diem-fueled circus designed to pacify foreign investors. The grey list is our new reality, and no amount of Embu-based training will wash away the stain.
