So, the whispers were true. The Kenya Pipeline Company, that vital artery pumping fuel across our nation, has apparently been handed over on a silver platter. Uganda, of all places, now owns a chunky 20.15 percent of it. Our own leaders, specifically William Ruto, are being accused of outfoxing themselves into giving away control of something so critical for our fuel security. Classic.

They say it was an Initial Public Offer, a fancy way of saying they sold pieces of the company. And who swooped in to grab a big chunk? Uganda, through their national oil company. So much for "Kenya First," eh? Now, when it comes to fuel transport and distribution, our neighbours have a serious say. Just what we needed, more foreign influence.

This whole saga smells like the same old story of our strategic assets ending up in the hands of outsiders. Remember how Nairobi’s office market is chasing shadows while Kampala cashes in? [Nairobi’s Office Market Is Chasing Shadows While Kampala Cashes In](/posts/nairobi-s-office-market-is-chasing-shadows-while-kampala-cashes-in). It seems our neighbours are playing the long game, and we're just playing catch-up, or worse, deliberately handing them the ball.

And don't even get me started on the news blog's blurb about subscribing for updates. "Journalism with depth, courage, and independent stories that challenge power." Please. While they’re busy selling access to our resources, we’re supposed to pay to read about how it happened. It's a real "black gold" situation, just another black hole for our taxes and our national pride.

It’s a bitter pill to swallow when you realize that the very infrastructure meant to guarantee our fuel supply might now be dictated by a foreign power. This isn't about economic growth; it's about who holds the reins. And judging by this deal, it's looking less and less like Kenyans.
