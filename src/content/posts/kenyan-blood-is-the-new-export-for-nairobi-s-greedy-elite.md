In this country, if you are not the one eating, you are definitely on the menu. The latest expose on how our young men are being shipped off to the front lines of the Russia-Ukraine war is just another Tuesday in Kenya. We are not just exporting coffee and tea anymore; we have moved on to human shields. A middleman named "Red" has basically confirmed what we all suspected: our security establishment is just a collection of toll stations where everyone from a DoD Brigadier to a DCI officer has a price tag.

The logic is simple and heartless. You take the most desperate, unemployed youth from the National Youth Service and tell them they are worth 1.6 million shillings. That is the price of a life in the eyes of the Russian recruiter Mikhael Lyapin and his local goons. If you thought the [Minnesota Cartel laundering billions into local resorts](/posts/minnesota-cartel-feeding-our-future-kenya-laundering) was a slap in the face to honest Kenyans, this recruitment pipeline is a full-on headbutt. It is a systematic bribe machine where every signature on a travel document costs a few thousand shillings while a family in the village loses a son.

Imagine being holed up in Great Wall Gardens in Mavoko, watching combat videos and trying to learn basic Russian phrases like "don't shoot." These boys were being prepared for a meat grinder while the officials who cleared them were likely planning their next holiday. Some of these recruits faked their military experience just to escape the crushing poverty of Nairobi, only to find themselves in a trench thousands of miles away. It is the ultimate "fake it till you make it" story, except the "making it" part usually involves coming back in a casket - if you come back at all.

There are reportedly over 1,000 Kenyans currently stuck in Russia, and the government is as silent as a grave. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs is issuing e-visas like they are flyers for a club night, yet they claim they do not know what is happening. We are living in a state where the people meant to protect you are the ones selling you to the highest bidder. Do not be fooled by the big salaries and the bonuses; in this deal, the only ones truly getting paid are the ones sitting in air-conditioned offices in Nairobi, watching their bank accounts grow while the body count rises.
