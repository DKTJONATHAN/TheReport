KeNHA Urges Motorists to Observe Road Safety Ahead of Safari Rally: Is Anyone Actually Listening?

Oh, the glamour! The dust! The sheer, unadulterated chaos of it all. Safari Rally Kenya 2026 is revving up, folks, and apparently, the folks at KeNHA have decided it's their civic duty to, you know, *warn* us. As if the throngs of international visitors and local thrill-seekers aren't already a recipe for traffic pandemonium, now we have official pronouncements about speed limits and "observing road safety regulations." Darling, please. It's a rally. People are going to go fast. It’s not like they’re attending a Beth Rigby interview, where the only danger is a misplaced comma.

KeNHA’s little social media plea, dropped on Thursday the 12th (of course, it’s always a *specific* Thursday, isn’t it?), is basically them shouting into the void. "Quick on the road, slow on the street," they chirp. Bless their naive little hearts. They’re worried about us normal folks mingling with the rally drivers on public roads, as if the "hundreds of thousands of fans" descending upon Naivasha aren't already going to turn the Great Rift Valley into one giant, unruly street party. It’s the WRC, not a quaint village fête.

They’re reminding everyone to obey traffic police and marshals, as if a rogue rally enthusiast is going to suddenly stop their mud-splattered conquest because a polite constable asked them to. And the advice for drivers to "schedule their travels early enough" and "anticipate temporary roadblocks"? Honey, if you’re not already scheduling your life around the potential for utter gridlock during a major event, you’re living in a different country altogether. It’s almost as if KeNHA believes responsible driving is a foreign concept, perhaps something akin to [the sterile narcissism of Nordic domesticity](posts/the-sterile-narcissism-of-nordic-domesticity).

Let’s be real, the Safari Rally is a legendary beast, and its return to the WRC calendar is all about spectacle. It’s about those dusty, rocky roads, the unpredictable weather, and the sheer audacity of drivers navigating it all. While KeNHA’s responsible citizenry shtick is… quaint, the real story here is the inevitable circus that follows. Between the screaming engines and the selfie-snapping crowds, let’s just hope the only thing getting "wrecked" is the competition, not our collective sanity.
