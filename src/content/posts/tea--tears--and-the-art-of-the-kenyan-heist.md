Welcome to Kenya, where the only thing more consistent than the rain in April is the smell of a Sh2.6 billion scandal rotting in the sun. Iran’s Ambassador, Dr. Ali Gholampour, is out here doing the work our own DCI should be doing - pointing fingers at the "traders and officials" who turned a tea consignment into a private ATM. Tehran has shut the door on us, and frankly, who can blame them? When you deal with a broker who replaces high-quality leaves with dust and pockets the difference, you don’t come back for seconds.

The real victims aren’t the suits in the Ministry offices; it’s the small-scale farmer in Central and Rift Valley who thought "hustler" meant hard work, not being the target of a high-level grift. These growers are now staring at warehouses full of tea that no one wants to buy because a few well-connected individuals decided they hadn't eaten enough this year. We’re told the government is "addressing" the fallout, which in Nairobi speak means waiting for the public to get distracted by the next big thing.

And what a distraction we have! While the tea industry collapses, the headlines are busy feeding us the usual soap opera: Ruto and Uhuru clashing in dark rooms and ODM tearing itself apart like a pack of hungry wolves. It’s a classic Kenyan diversion. If you keep the people arguing about who will be Kingpin in 2027, they won’t notice the Sh2.6 billion-sized hole in the national pocket. It’s theater for the poor, paid for by the poor.

Even reading about this mess feels like a scam. You get hit with a "premium access" paywall or told to disable your ad-blocker just to find out how you’re being robbed. They want you to pay for the privilege of knowing that your economy is being run into the ground. It’s like paying a thief a subscription fee to get a monthly itemized list of what he stole from your living room. 

If you’re feeling the urge to flee to Canada like everyone else in your WhatsApp group, join the queue. Canada is being sold as the ultimate "start a new life" kit, but let’s be honest - it’s just a mass exodus of the disillusioned. We are a country of migrants-in-waiting because the "Realists" among us know that the system isn't broken; it’s working exactly how it was designed to. It was designed to enrich the few and leave the rest of us to "tap-tap-tap" on TikTok for gifts.

This culture of the "quick meal" isn't just in the tea board; it’s everywhere. From the high-level diplomatic scandals to the [The TikTok Begging Economy: How Kenyan Creators Extract Millions Through Emotional Appeals](/posts/tiktok-begging-syndicate-kenyan-creators-emotional-appeals-millions), the script is the same. Everyone is looking for a shortcut. Whether it's faking a tea export or faking a tragedy on a livestream, the goal is to extract as much as possible before the marks wake up. Well, the Iranians are awake. The question is, when will we be?
