So, the Sh4.8 billion fuel scandal is officially a clown show. Three energy bosses have already packed their bags, and now, whisperings are reaching the highest offices, pulling in not one, but *two* Cabinet Secretaries. Opiyo Wandayi and Lee Kinyanjui - suddenly looking very interested in what went down with that fuel. Funny how these things only surface when the spotlight’s on, isn't it?

The government's suddenly all about "cracking the whip" and "investigating possibilities." Possibilities? They're investigating if some chaps thought it was a brilliant idea to sell us dirtier, cheaper fuel by exploiting global crises. This isn't a "possibility," it's standard operating procedure for some people in this country. We're not buying half-baked excuses, Ruto.

And just when you thought the drama couldn't get more predictable, the Kenya Pipeline Company privatisation, this grand Sh106 billion dream project, is now teetering. Because, you know, why build something solid when you can undermine it with rotten fuel and shady deals first? It's a miracle this whole thing hasn't collapsed already, though I wouldn't hold my breath.

PS Liban, MD Sang, and DG Kiptoo are out. Resigned, they say. Amidst investigations. It’s the same old script: scapegoats fall, the real masterminds probably get a pat on the back and a promotion to a different ministry. This is Kenya, after all. It makes you wonder if anyone in power truly believes in anything other than their own pockets. Just remember when Ruto told the Coast leaders to unite or face the consequences? Looks like the "fire" is spreading everywhere. /posts/ruto-tells-coast-leaders--unite-or-state-power-and--fire--go

The president might be talking tough, but let's be real. The "whip" usually just gets passed around. These scandals are as regular as the matatu touts' shouts. We're left with the substandard fuel, a lighter wallet, and the same old questions about who’s *really* in charge and who’s benefiting from the mess. The only thing consistent is the consistent disappointment.
