Oh look, another day, another photo op at State House Entebbe. While the rest of us are out here fighting for our lives and trying to figure out why the cost of living is doing gymnastics, President Yoweri Museveni was busy hosting former Tanzanian First Lady Anna Mkapa. It is the usual script: "deep and cordial relations" and "strengthening ties." Translation? A bunch of rich people sitting on gold-trimmed chairs, sipping expensive tea, and talking about "regional integration" while their citizens wonder if they can afford a loaf of bread.

Janet Museveni was there too, looking every bit the Minister of Education and Sports, though one wonders if she actually knows what is happening in a regular classroom these days. The trio spent their time commending "enduring friendship" and "mutual respect." It is honestly giving "Old Money" vibes, but without the actual grace. They are talking about collaboration in trade and security, but let us be real: these meetings are mostly just high-stakes social clubs for the political elite who have been in the game since the dawn of time.

Mrs. Mkapa was all smiles, acknowledging the historical bond between Uganda and Tanzania. It is cute how they keep recycling these phrases like they are trying to win a high school debate. It reminds me of how some leaders are stuck in a [Zakayo's First-World Fever Dream and the Sh550 Million Alliance Handout](/posts/zakayo-s-first-world-fever-dream-and-the-sh550-million-alliance-handout), promising us a utopia while they just shuffle money between their friends. The hypocrisy is so thick you could cut it with a knife, yet we are supposed to applaud every time a motorcade moves.

At the end of the day, these "courtesy calls" are just a way to remind the masses who is still in charge. They talk about "social development" and "partnerships" like they are launching a new charity, but we all know the only things developing are their bank accounts and their social calendars. Until these "strong bilateral relations" actually mean cheaper fuel or better roads for the person on a boda boda, it is all just expensive theater staged for our boredom. Stay cynical, darlings.
