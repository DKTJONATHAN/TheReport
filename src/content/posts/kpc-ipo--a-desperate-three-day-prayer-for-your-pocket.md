They are calling it a "three-day extension for inclusivity," but let us be real for once. The Capital Markets Authority is essentially begging for a miracle because the KPC IPO is currently smelling like a damp squib. With only ten percent of the target collected by last week, the government has realized that Kenyans are not as gullible as they used to be. You can dress up a pig in roadshows and influencer campaigns, but if the math does not make sense, the pockets stay zipped.

CS John Mbadi is out here comparing our investment habits to last-minute voter registration. It is a tired narrative that ignores the fact that we actually have brains. Investors are not "late"; they are simply looking at the nine-shilling price tag and wondering why they should pay a premium for something analysts say is worth barely four bob. This reminds me of [The Illusion of Meritocracy: Why Professional Sports is Just Financial Darwinism](/posts/the-illusion-of-meritocracy--why-professional-sports-is-just-financial-darwinism), where the system is designed to benefit the owners while the rest of us are sold a dream that never pays out.

While the Privatization Authority talks about "transparency," the reality is that institutional and international players have already grabbed the juicy parts. They want the retail "mwananchi" to jump in now just to provide liquidity for their eventual exit. If the fair value is truly around four point six shillings as some suggest, buying at nine is not an investment - it is a donation to the National Treasury to help them manage their mounting debts.

Do not be fooled by the shiny talk of a one hundred and sixty-three billion shilling asset base or the convenience of dialing a USSD code to lose your money. A profitable company is only a good buy if the entry price is right. Right now, this offer looks like another attempt to offload a sixty-five percent stake at an inflated rate before the reality of the market sets in on March 9. If you want to play the hero, go ahead, but do not say Belinda did not warn you when those CDS accounts reflect a sea of red.
