MARAGA URGES KENYANS TO VOTE OUT RUTO - ELSE TRAGEDY AWAITS

Former Chief Justice David Maraga is painting a grim picture, warning that another term for President William Ruto will be a "tragedy" for Kenya. He's urging citizens to unite and show the current administration the exit in 2027.

Well, well, well. Look who's decided to pipe up now. The former Chief Justice, David Maraga, has finally noticed what the rest of us have been screaming about for ages - that this country is going down the drain. He’s out there in Kisii, holding hands with opposition types and talking about how another Ruto term would be a "tragedy." As if things aren't tragic enough already with the cost of living going through the roof and the constant show of incompetence from the top. But hey, at least he’s finally admitting it, I guess. "Tushikamane pamoja tuiondoe hii serikali," he says. Easy for you to say when you’re not the one struggling to put food on the table, mzee.

Maraga claims he’s been listening to the "wananchi" during his "Ukatiba Caravan" engagements. Apparently, people are fed up with "wizi wa mali ya uma" - the theft of public resources. Shocking, I know. Who would have thought that rampant corruption would be a problem? He’s talking about uniting leaders to "give back the country." Give back what, exactly? The shillings they’ve already siphoned off? This whole "Linda Mwananchi" initiative sounds like another one of those theatrical performances where politicians pretend to care while quietly arranging their next power grab. It’s all part of the same tired game.

He’s been meeting with all sorts of political heavyweights, from governors to senators, all discussing "constitutionalism" and the "rule of law." Sounds important, doesn’t it? But let’s be real. These are the same folks who often seem to forget about the Constitution when it suits them. Remember when we talked about how the very laws meant to protect us can sometimes feel like they just chain us back to the old ways? [Constitution Frees Us? More Like It Chains Us Back To Old Ways](/posts/constitution-frees-us--more-like-it-chains-us-back-to-old-ways). This feels like more of the same political maneuvering, disguised as civic duty.

Maraga’s call for unity and adherence to the Constitution is all well and good on paper. But when you look at the reality on the ground, it’s hard not to be cynical. Kenya is indeed a rich country, bursting with potential, but it’s been bled dry by those who are supposed to be its custodians. The endless cycle of promises and disappointments is enough to make anyone jaded. While they’re busy with their consultations and strategizing for 2027, the rest of us are just trying to survive the day.

The former CJ is now ready to back a united opposition candidate. That’s a big step for someone who used to wear the robes of impartiality. But is this about genuine change, or just swapping one set of faces for another? The "political realignments" are happening, and everyone’s positioning themselves for the next round. Let’s just hope this isn't just another elaborate charade that leaves the common Kenyan even more disillusioned than before.
