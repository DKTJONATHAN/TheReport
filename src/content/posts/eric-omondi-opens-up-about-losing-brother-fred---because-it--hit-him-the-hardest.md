So, Eric Omondi is out here, on Jeff Koinange's couch, weeping about losing his brother Fred. Apparently, this tragic road accident back in June 2024, where Fred decided to take a joyride on the wrong side of the road on a motorbike - a classic move, really - hit Eric harder than anything else. Because, you know, death is just a *personal inconvenience* when it happens to someone you know.

And it's not just the grief, oh no. Eric's also admitting Fred was the *real* talent, even confessing to stealing jokes. Shocker. It’s always the ones who claim to be the comedians who are just glorified copycats, isn't it? This whole display feels a bit like that whole [Performative Romance: The Heavy Cost of Kenya's Influencer Industrial Complex](/posts/performative-romance--the-heavy-cost-of-kenya-s-influencer-industrial-complex---streamlinefeed-co-ke) thing we’ve seen, a lot of noise for… well, for what, exactly?

Fred, poor chap, was a pillion passenger on a motorbike that met a Mitsubishi bus head-on. Died instantly. The rider? Well, he got to experience Mama Lucy Kibaki Hospital. Probably got better treatment than some people who actually pay for it. But Eric’s the one who’s “broken.” It's always about how it affects *them*, isn't it? Even when it comes to death, it's a competition of who suffers more.

Eric says Fred’s death was worse than his *biological* brother's. So, there's a hierarchy of grief now, is there? And he admits Fred was more talented. I’m just waiting for the next phase where he starts blaming the bus company, the road conditions, or maybe even the sun for shining at the wrong angle. Anything to keep the spotlight on himself.

Honestly, this whole exposé just reeks of opportunism. A grieving brother is one thing, but turning it into a public spectacle for sympathy and maybe a few more clicks on whatever platform he's peddling nowadays? That's just peak Nairobi realism, I suppose. We all have to make a living, even if it’s by dissecting our own tragedies for public consumption.
