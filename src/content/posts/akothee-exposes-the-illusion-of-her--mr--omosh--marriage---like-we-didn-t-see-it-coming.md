Akothee, never one to shy from a spectacle, is now "reflecting candidly" on her blink-and-you-miss-it marriage to Swiss national Dennis Schweizer, aka "Mr. Omosh." After a ridiculously lavish Nairobi wedding in April 2023, the union spectacularly imploded. Colour me shocked. It’s almost as if some relationships built on public display and rapid-fire romance have a shelf-life shorter than a politician’s promise.

Her grand revelation? She was "love bombed." Right. Because extreme generosity from a new partner - all bills paid, flight tickets booked, lavish holidays, a card with cash - is definitely not a classic, glaring red flag for those who understand that [The Billable Hour Always Wins Over the Game](/posts/the-billable-hour-always-wins-over-the-game) in most adult interactions. "Omosh boxer paid bills," she says, as if this was some groundbreaking sign of devotion rather than a calculated, if temporary, investment. He booked business class to France, didn't let her pay a shilling. All very romantic, or very strategic, depending on how cynical you are. (And I am very cynical.)

Akothee now admits she was "blinded" and genuinely believed she had "arrived" with a "young sexy and loving" man. This explains her aggressive defence of him against public "critics" at the time. Of course she was defensive. No one wants to admit they’ve been taken for a ride, especially when they've built an entire public narrative around finding "the one." It’s a common script: the public figure parades a seemingly perfect partner, deflects all criticism, then, when it all goes south, comes clean with a "lesson learned" narrative.

So, the "illusion" of her 'Mr. Omosh' marriage is now officially exposed. Not that it needed much exposing for anyone with half an eye on reality. The story is as old as time: grand gestures, inflated expectations, and a very public crash-landing. Maybe next time, the "candid reflection" can happen *before* the lavish wedding, saving everyone the drama, and us the predictable headlines. But where's the entertainment in that, right?
