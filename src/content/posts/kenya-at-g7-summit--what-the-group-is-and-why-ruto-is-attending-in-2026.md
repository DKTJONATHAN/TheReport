KENYA AT G7 SUMMIT: RALLYING FOR AFRICA'S PITANCE IN 2026
WARNING: THIS SUMMIT IS A PERFORMANCE, DON'T EXPECT REAL CHANGE FOR KENYA OR AFRICA.
RUTO IN FRANCE, ASKING FOR SCRAPS FROM THE GLOBAL TABLE.
POLITICS
G7, Africa, Diplomacy
Champagne

So, President William Ruto is off to Évian-les-Bains, France, hobnobbing with the G7 fat cats. The world's "most advanced economies" - read: the same ones who got us into half these messes - are meeting to discuss the world's problems. And Kenya, naturally, is invited as a guest, probably to be lectured on how to manage our "emerging challenges" like artificial intelligence, after they've already figured out how to weaponize it. The G7, a club of rich democracies who trace their roots back to a panic over oil prices, now meets annually to talk about everything from economic stability to climate change. It's basically a high-level complaining session where actual binding laws are conveniently absent.

Kenya’s inclusion, they say, is because of its "growing diplomatic and economic role." Or perhaps it's just tokenism to make the whole thing look more diverse. Being the only East African country there means Ruto gets to stand on a slightly bigger soapbox, but let’s be real, Africa’s "stronger voice" in global decision-making usually amounts to polite nods and promises that evaporate faster than dew on a Nairobi street. He's there to push for reforms in global finance and argue for Africa’s inclusion, wanting concrete proposals on how Africa can access "concessional resources." Translation: we need cheaper loans because your perceived risk assessment is nonsense. It’s a familiar song, isn't it? Remember when Ruto asked spy chiefs to unravel the AU reform deadlock? [Talking to bureaucrats didn't work](https://www.example.com/posts/ruto-asks-spy-chiefs-to-unravel-au-reform-deadlock--because-talking-to-bureaucrats-didn-t-work) either, and now he's at the G7.

The agenda, as always, is a laundry list of global crises: economic pressures, wars in Ukraine and the Middle East, climate change, energy security, and the ever-present AI threat. While leaders like Trump and Macron try to secure "solid, serious agreements" on Iran and talk tough on Ukraine, Ruto is expected to champion Africa's potential. He’s quoted saying Africa will "write the rules" for AI, which is a bold statement when you consider how many of our own digital infrastructure issues are still being sorted out. It’s a nice sentiment, but the reality is Africa is more likely to be a consumer of AI developed elsewhere, or worse, a testing ground for its less ethical applications.

He’s hoping to leave with concrete proposals on how Africa can access affordable credit. Africa is “not going to be merely a consumer,” he insists, but a co-creator. It's a vision that sounds good in speeches delivered from grand podiums, but the historical track record of these G7 summits suggests more platitudes than palpable progress. While Kenya's relations with France are growing, and Ruto highlights cooperation, we've seen this dance before. The summit is an opportunity, yes, but an opportunity for whom? For the G7 to appear inclusive, or for Africa to actually gain leverage? I'm not holding my breath.
