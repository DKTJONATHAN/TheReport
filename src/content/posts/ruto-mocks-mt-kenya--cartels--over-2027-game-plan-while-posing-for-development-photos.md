So, here we are again. President William Ruto, fresh from God knows where, is in Murang'a, playing the martyr and railing against a "tiny and corrupt political cartel" he claims is trying to control Central Kenya's vote for 2027. Apparently, these individuals think they can keep regional voting blocs in their pockets and make him kneel. Please, as if anyone actually believes that. He’s just serving all Kenyans, you see, the ones who bothered to tick his name on the ballot back in 2022.

He’s busy laying foundation stones for markets and handing out milk coolers, the classic bread and circuses. All this while lecturing about freedom to associate and make independent choices. It’s rich, coming from a man who seems to have a new favourite hobby of collecting favours. He’s not kneeling to anyone but God and the "wananchi," he declared, as if that's some profound revelation. The man is ready to account for all his commitments to Kenyans, apparently, but first, he's got to deal with these imaginary gatekeepers.

The President’s solution to critics lacking vision? Accuse them of using insults and empty rhetoric. Meanwhile, he's the one throwing around terms like "cartel" and dismissing legitimate political concerns as self-interest. The KSh366 million Kabati Market, the KSh343 million Kangari Modern Market, billions for housing, roads, and electricity - it’s all part of the grand show. And don't forget the tea and coffee farmers, suddenly blessed with better prices and access to China's 1.5 billion consumers. Sounds almost too good to be true, doesn't it?

Deputy President Kithure Kindiki is chiming in, of course, saying the development record will speak for itself. Because nothing says "accountability" like a few new markets and shiny road projects. It's all part of the narrative, the carefully constructed image of a leader tirelessly working for the masses, while secretly battling shadowy figures who dare to question his absolute authority. Just another day in the Kenyan political circus.
