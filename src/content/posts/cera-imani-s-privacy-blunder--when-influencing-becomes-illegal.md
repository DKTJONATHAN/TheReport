Darling, grab your tea because the influencer bubble just popped a little bit more. We all know the drill: a pretty face, a few thousand followers, and a brand deal that makes them think they are untouchable. Waithera Imani, or Cera Imani as she is known to her 200,000 Instagram followers, just learned that the Data Protection Commissioner is not one of her besties. Apparently, being a lifestyle guru does not mean you get to play fast and loose with people’s private information.

The tea is that Cera has been ordered to cough up Sh50,000 after her promotional antics on TikTok and Instagram crossed a legal line. For someone who lives for the aesthetic, this is a pretty ugly look. It turns out that spamming people for the sake of a paycheck has consequences. Sh50,000 might be just a weekend’s worth of soft life for some, but it is the principle of the thing that counts. The regulator is finally looking at these creators and saying, "Enough is enough."

The victim here, a Mr. Omwenga, was not having it. He was tired of getting unsolicited texts from CJ’s eatery, a place he never gave his number to. It is the classic case of big brands and their shiny influencers thinking they can just harvest data like it is free candy. We have seen this brand of entitlement before, especially when looking at the mess in [Vodka, Villains, and Viral Links: The Hypocrisy of the 'Russian Guy' Scandal](/posts/vodka--villains--and-viral-links--the-hypocrisy-of-the--russian-guy--scandal) where digital ethics went out the window. The lack of integrity in the digital space is truly staggering.

Cera’s defense probably involved a lot of hair flipping and "I did not know," but the law is the law. In a world where university students are being dragged into court without evidence and influencers are being fined for basic privacy breaches, the landscape is getting messy. The hypocrisy is the best part: these creators demand privacy when they are caught in a scandal, yet they have zero respect for yours when there is a promotional coin to be made.

Let this be a lesson to all the aspiring content creators out there. Your ring light might be bright, but it does not blind the regulators. If you are going to use your platform to harass citizens with unwanted marketing, you better have your wallet ready. Cera Imani might want to spend less time on her transition videos and more time reading the Data Protection Act. Stay tuned, because this is just the tip of the iceberg in the influencer accountability era.
