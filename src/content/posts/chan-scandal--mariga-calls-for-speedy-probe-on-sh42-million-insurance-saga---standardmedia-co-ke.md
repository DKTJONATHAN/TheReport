CHAN Scandal: Sh42 Million Insurance Saga - Mariga Calls for Speedy Probe (As If That'll Help)











CHAN Scandal: Sh42 Million Insurance Saga - Mariga Calls for Speedy Probe (As If That'll Help)

So, Football Kenya Federation (FKF) has managed to lose Sh42 million meant for tournament insurance. Shocking, I know. Acting president McDonald Mariga, a man who suddenly found himself in charge after some NEC members decided to "step aside" (read: get booted), is now calling for a "speedy probe." He's also saying they'll freeze accounts and review contracts. It’s all very dramatic, like a poorly written soap opera.

The whole mess kicked off after an expose revealed that Sh42.4 million in brokerage fees were wired to a company that apparently wasn't even licensed to operate. Naturally, the suspended president, Hussein Mohammed, is crying foul, claiming the NEC meeting that suspended him wasn't valid and that CAF, not him, secured the insurance. He’s also on his usual anti-graft crusade, which conveniently started right after he was suspended.

Mariga’s plan to "clean up the mess" involves getting a whole host of bodies involved - the EACC, IRA, PPRA, CAF, and FIFA. Let’s see how much speed they can actually muster with that many cooks in the kitchen. He's assuring everyone, especially partners like SportPesa and Azam, that business as usual will continue. Because when has a Kenyan football federation ever been "business as usual"?

The irony is thick enough to cut with a Kenyan knife. While Mariga talks about integrity and transparency, it's hard not to recall the ticketing chaos during the CHAN 2024 tournament, where Kenyan fans were locked out. It seems like every major football event here is just an opportunity for someone to line their pockets, and the fans are always left holding the empty bag. This whole "cleaning up the mess" narrative feels more like a PR stunt than a genuine effort to fix things.

Mohammed’s defence, that fighting corruption attracts backlash, is a classic deflection. When you’re accused of losing millions, pointing fingers at "graft fighters" doesn't exactly inspire confidence. It’s a familiar song and dance: accusations fly, suspensions happen, and then we wait for the next inevitable scandal to break. Hopefully, this time, someone actually finds the money, but don't hold your breath.

Mariga is promising to convene an urgent NEC meeting soon. Perhaps they can use the opportunity to discuss how to avoid losing millions next time, or maybe just plan the next round of suspensions. In the meantime, FKF partners are expected to keep their faith, and the public is told to remain calm. Because that's what we do best, isn't it? Remain calm and watch the money disappear.
