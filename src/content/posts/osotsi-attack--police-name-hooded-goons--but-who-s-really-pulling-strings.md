Osotsi Attack: Police Name Hooded Goons, But Who's Really Pulling Strings?

Just another day in Kenya. A senator gets roughed up, and the police trot out a few low-level names like they've cracked the Da Vinci Code. Don't hold your breath for justice, just prepare for more of the same.

So, Vihiga Senator Godfrey Osotsi decided to take a walk, presumably without adequate backup, and ended up on the wrong end of some hooded thugs. Carlos Owiti, Eric Otieno, Vincent Odhiambo - names in a police report, just pawns in a game we'll never fully understand. The story says the senator’s security held back to avoid public shootings. Of course, because a little beating is preferable to a full-blown chaotic shootout that might actually inconvenience someone important.

Meanwhile, back at the ODM party HQ, they’re busy holding meetings. With Raila Odinga gone, it’s a free-for-all, the "cathedral" being carved up by eager hands. They’re supposedly settling on a name for something. Probably another position to fill, another mouth to feed. All this drama, costing a cool Sh40 million for a convention. That's a lot of money to decide who gets the next slice of the pie, isn't it? It’s almost as if [Kenya is not a country; it’s a limited liability company](/posts/kenya-is-not-a-country--it-s-a-limited-liability-company), where the shareholders are always fighting over the spoils.

The hooded attackers are just the symptom, not the disease. The real infection is in the halls of power, where deals are made and politicians are sacrificed. This whole Osotsi incident is just a distraction, a bit of noise to keep us looking at the wrong place while the real looting continues.
