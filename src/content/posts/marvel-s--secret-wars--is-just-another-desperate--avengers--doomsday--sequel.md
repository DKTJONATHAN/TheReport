So, Marvel is finally "saying goodbye" to its beloved multiverse with "Avengers: Doomsday" and "Secret Wars." Don't get too excited. While "Doomsday" is the current flavor of the month, "Secret Wars" is being touted as the *real* showstopper. Kevin Feige, bless his heart, is hinting at a "very different way" these two films will connect, distinct from the lazy "Part I/Part II" structure of "Infinity War" and "Endgame." This is clearly code for "we're not sure how to tie it all together, but we'll make it sound profound."

Let's be clear: this is not about groundbreaking storytelling. It's about leveraging a tired concept and roping in every actor they can find who has even a tangential connection to a past Marvel iteration. The talk of "ambition" and using the multiverse to do things "legally or creatively" they couldn't before is just corporate speak for "we're throwing everything at the wall to see what sticks, and hoping you'll pay for the privilege." They've spent years building this convoluted multiverse, and now they're frantically trying to bring it to a close with a spectacle that's more about reunion tours than genuine narrative payoff. Expect a lot of familiar faces, a lot of CGI, and precious little substance.

"Avengers: Doomsday" lands in theaters December 18, followed by "Avengers: Secret Wars" on December 17, 2027. Prepare to be underwhelmed, just like always.
