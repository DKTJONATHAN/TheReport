So, Rigathi Gachagua, the man who was *this close* to being second-in-command, is now throwing around claims of Ksh.103 billion being "misappropriated" by the Social Health Authority (SHA). According to him, our esteemed Health Cabinet Secretary, Aden Duale, is neck-deep in this alleged siphoning. Wote, Makueni County. The birthplace of more hot air than actual progress, apparently. Gachagua is spitting fire, accusing Duale of trying to pull a fast one with a company that’s supposedly draining the nation's coffers, a scheme hatched from the defunct NHIF.

Duale, naturally, is crying foul. He’s throwing the ball back, daring Gachagua to produce a CR12 document - a piece of paper, mind you - to prove his alleged ownership in Convergence Network Limited. Duale claims Gachagua is just spewing tribal venom because he can't prove anything concrete. It's the classic Kenyan playbook: deny, deflect, and accuse the other side of being the real problem. And as for the claims about a director being a proxy just because they share a similar ethnic background? Pure, unadulterated, Nairobi-level cynicism. It's a "racial bookkeeping" exercise, Duale calls it. Brilliant.

Then we have Deputy President Kithure Kindiki, swooping in to defend the SHA like a knight in shining armour, dismissing Gachagua as a "prophet of doom." He brags about 32 million Kenyans now covered, a massive jump from the NHIF's 7 million in 2022. While this sounds good on paper, we've seen these numbers before, haven't we? It’s the same old song and dance, a distraction from the real rot. Kindiki’s speech about how the opposition "did nothing" is rich, considering the constant squabbles and saboteurs we see even within the current government.

This whole SHA saga is just another episode in the never-ending Kenyan drama. While they’re busy pointing fingers and slinging mud, the real issue is the systematic setup, the diversion of funds, and the potential collapse of a system meant to help us. It’s a stark reminder of how our hard-earned money disappears into thin air, much like the promises of better governance we hear every election cycle. It makes you wonder if there’s any real hope for progress, or if we’re just destined to watch these same old characters play the same old games, perhaps with a nod to the past, like [More Coins for the Boys in Blue: A Price Tag on Our Silence](/posts/more-coins-for-the-boys-in-blue--a-price-tag-on-our-silence).

The truth is, whether it's Ksh.103 billion or a fraction of it, the fact that such accusations are flying between the highest offices speaks volumes about the integrity of the system. Gachagua and Duale are just two sides of the same coin, playing their roles in a grand theatre of deception. We, the citizens, are left to pick up the pieces, or rather, the lack thereof.

Ultimately, this isn't about who's right or wrong; it's about the blatant disregard for public funds and the constant manipulation of public discourse. While they argue about CR12 documents and ethnic proxies, the health sector, and by extension, our well-being, hangs precariously in the balance. Expect more of this, folks. It's just another Tuesday in Nairobi.
