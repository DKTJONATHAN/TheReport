In Nairobi, we are used to being told to wait for things that never arrive. Now, Health CS Aden Duale has stepped up to the microphone to let parents know that the Rotavac vaccine - the one meant to keep infants from literally dehydrating to death - is on a "temporary" hiatus. Apparently, the manufacturer, Bharat Biotech, is busy upgrading their facility. It is the classic Nairobi excuse: "I am almost there, just stuck in traffic," except the traffic is a global supply chain and the person waiting is a sick baby.

Gavi, the Vaccine Alliance, sent the memo that deliveries for 2026 and 2027 are going to be a mess. It is funny how these "upgrades" always seem to happen when we need the medicine the most. We are looking at a shortage that stretches into next year because someone in a boardroom decided that mid-production was the best time to renovate the office. It feels like dealing with a dysfunctional family where everyone talks about "progress" while the house burns down. Much like how [The EAC is a Deadbeat Dad with a Grand Vision](/posts/the-eac-is-a-deadbeat-dad-with-a-grand-vision), the Ministry of Health is excellent at painting a picture of a healthy future while failing to provide the basics for the present.

As of early March, we have exactly 4,000 doses left in the national store. In a country where thousands of children are born every week, that is not a "stock level," it is a rounding error. That supply will be gone before the month ends, and the next shipment is not expected until the end of April - and that is assuming the January delay does not happen again. If you have a child at 6, 10, or 14 weeks, the government’s official advice is basically to cross your fingers and hope for the best.

The Ministry says this disruption is not about "safety or quality," as if that makes the empty shelves any easier to swallow. They are telling us to "strengthen diarrhoea prevention measures" like handwashing and using safe water. Thanks for the tip, bwana. In a city where half the taps are dry and the other half are pumping something that looks like tea, telling people to just "wash hands more" is a special kind of tone-deaf. It is the medical version of telling a hungry person to just look at pictures of food until dinner arrives.

We are told UNICEF and other partners are "fast-tracking" the next batch, but we have heard this song and dance before. Redistribution across counties is just moving the same few crumbs around a different plate. While the big men in suits assure us that protecting children is their "highest priority," the reality on the ground is a lottery where the prize is a vaccine that should have been there in January. If you are a parent in this city, you better start boiling that water and praying, because the government’s plan B is just more advice you cannot afford to follow.
