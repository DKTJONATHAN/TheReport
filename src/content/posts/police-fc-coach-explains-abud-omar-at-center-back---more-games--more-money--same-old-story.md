So, Nicholas Muyoti, the man in charge at Kenya Police FC, has decided Abud Omar, a left-back who's spent most of his life defending the left flank, is now a central defender. Apparently, it's all about "wisdom" and "accommodating others." Because, you know, when you've got a guy who's good at one thing, the logical next step is to shove him somewhere else and call it a masterstroke. It’s like they think we’re all born yesterday, expecting applause for basic squad management.

And wouldn't you know it, the team is doing… okay. They've conceded fewer goals, and Omar is being hailed as some kind of defensive savior. Muyoti is beaming, praising the "experience, quality, and leadership skills" - all the things you’d expect a seasoned player to have, regardless of where you stick him. It's the same tired narrative we see everywhere: "strategic brilliance" when it's just a convenient reshuffle to make room for the shiny new foreign toys. This whole charade reminds me of [Sacrifice at the Louvre: A Director Resigns to Mask Institutional Rot](/posts/sacrifice-at-the-louvre--a-director-resigns-to-mask-institutional-rot), where the blame game always finds a convenient scapegoat or a dazzling distraction.

Speaking of those foreign signings, Muyoti is over the moon. Koutiama, Malonga, Quansah, Ndecket - all "brilliant," apparently. It’s always the same song and dance. New faces arrive, big promises are made, and suddenly, the homegrown talent is suddenly surplus to requirements or conveniently repurposed. The money flows, the club inches up the league table a bit, and everyone's supposed to feel uplifted. Meanwhile, the real issues, the systemic rot, get swept under the rug, much like the promise of genuine security in [The Myth of the Digital Safe Haven](/posts/the-myth-of-the-digital-safe-haven).

Right now, Police are fourth. Fourth! Fourteen points behind the leaders. It’s a respectable position, I suppose, if you’re easily impressed by mediocrity disguised as progress. Muyoti’s "wisdom" has them playing averagely, but hey, at least they're not conceding too many goals. That's the win, apparently. We're meant to celebrate the crumbs while the real feast goes to the ones who know how to actually orchestrate a victory, not just shuffle players around like a tired card dealer.
