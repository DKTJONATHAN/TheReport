So, Rigathi Gachagua, fresh from his court battle where he apparently got Sh50 million for being treated unfairly (while still getting impeached, mind you), is now playing the unity card. He's trotting out the same old song and dance: a single opposition presidential ticket for 2027. Apparently, his advisors, clergy, and "professionals" (read: people who tell him what he wants to hear) have decreed it's not negotiable. As if they haven't tried this before and ended up with the usual chaos.

He's promising to rally "millions" of supporters, including "allies of former President Uhuru Kenyatta." Right. Because that coalition worked wonders last time. Gachagua's also doing this 45-day "consultation" retreat in Nyeri. Translation: he’s going to try and convince everyone he’s the best bet, and if that fails, he’ll *graciously* support someone else. The self-importance is almost palpable, isn't it? It's all about optics, darling, making sure he looks like the kingmaker, even if his own ambitions get a polite tap on the head.

The man claims he's confident he'll get the ticket, citing his "political network" and "grassroots support." Sure, Rigathi. We all know how well those networks translate into actual votes when the chips are down. He’s even boasting about how he "mobilised four million votes to make him president" and now plans to "mobilise 10 million voters to take him home." That's the kind of self-aggrandizing talk that gets you nowhere but back to the drawing board. It’s a familiar script, this whole “I was crucial to his rise, now I’ll be crucial to his fall” routine. It’s the kind of political theatre that keeps the rest of us entertained, I suppose.

He says the opposition has finished its "mobilisation phase" and needs "consensus-building." Funny, because last I checked, the Nairobi streets are still dealing with the fallout of failed infrastructure, like the constant flooding we've been reporting on, which seems like a more pressing issue than these political machinations. [Nairobi’s Annual Drowning: 23 Dead in the City of Failed Concrete](/posts/nairobi-s-annual-drowning--23-dead-in-the-city-of-failed-concrete). But sure, let’s focus on a hypothetical 2027 race while the immediate problems fester.

The whole point, according to Gachagua, is to avoid "splitting the vote." A noble goal, if you actually believe any of these politicians can achieve it. He thinks he’s consolidated Mount Kenya support and built alliances nationwide. We'll see how that pans out. For now, it's just more hot air from a man who seems to think his past influence guarantees future success. Let's just hope this "consultation" doesn't involve any more questionable legal decisions or public funds being tied up in "damages."
