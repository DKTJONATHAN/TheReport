The markets have finally stopped believing the fairy tales of a swift reversal. The usual reaction to energy shocks-expecting a growth slowdown to naturally curb inflation-has been discarded in favor of a much darker outlook. This time, the inflationary dragon is too large to ignore. Investors are pricing in a tightening of monetary policy that will squeeze the life out of a stagnating economy. It is a pincer movement of higher costs and higher interest rates, designed to crush the consumer under the weight of geopolitical incompetence.

One must look closely at the "opacity" of private markets that the ECB is suddenly so concerned about. For years, regulators allowed private equity and credit to grow into a multi-trillion dollar shadow, feasting on the technology and software sectors with zero transparency. Now that the era of cheap money has evaporated, these funds are locking their doors and blocking redemptions. It is the classic endgame of a leveraged bubble: the exits are always much smaller than the entrance, and the latecomers are the ones who get trampled.

Much like Nairobi-s office market chasing shadows while Kampala cashes in, the Eurozone finds itself on the wrong side of the global trade ledger. As a net energy importer, the Euro is being cannibalized by the US dollar. The terms-of-trade deterioration is merely a polite, academic way of saying the entire continent is getting poorer in real time. The ECB-s Macro-Finance Financial Conditions Index is tightening, which is central-bank-speak for the walls are closing in.

Philip Lane might point to the Winter Olympics in Italy to explain away service inflation, but such distractions are insulting. Wage growth is decelerating because workers have lost all leverage in a world of surging energy costs and fragmented supply chains. The credibility the ECB prides itself on is simply the market-s grim acceptance that there is no way out of this trap without significant economic misery. The blood in the Strait of Hormuz has already turned the Eurozone-s balance sheets a permanent shade of red.
