Darling, I hope you have your bags packed, because the Kenyan passport just went from "East African Powerhouse" to "Suspect at Terminal 5." While we were all busy obsessing over who is dating who in the Nairobi social scene, a high-ranking Sudanese militant-slash-warlord was spotted globetrotting with a legitimate Kenyan passport. Yes, honey, the brother of the RSF commander has better travel perks than you do. It is giving very much "influencer for international conflict" vibes, and frankly, the PR disaster is more expensive than a custom suite at the Kempinski.

The irony is absolutely delicious if you have a dark sense of humor. We have brands like Taj Gems & Jewellers talking about "intergenerational trust" and "bespoke heritage" from their shiny new spot at Two Rivers Mall, while our own government is treating our national identity like a knock-off handbag on River Road. It is the ultimate hypocrisy. We are told to invest in "3D jewellery design" and "ethical gemstones" to prove our status, but what is the point of a diamond-encrusted bunny pendant if you are being strip-searched at immigration because some warlord bought your country's soul for top dollar?

This whole mess reeks of the same old corruption we see every Tuesday. It is honestly just another version of [Blood Money Or A Bonus? The Cost Of Keeping The Cops Happy](/posts/blood-money-or-a-bonus--the-cost-of-keeping-the-cops-happy) because someone at the Department of Immigration clearly decided that a Sudanese "strategic buyer" was worth more than the dignity of the entire Kenyan diaspora. While you are saving up for "investment-grade gemstones" to secure your future, a foreign combatant is using your flag to bypass sanctions. The "soft luxury" Taj Gems keeps talking about is looking real soft compared to the hard reality of a compromised biometric registry.

Let us talk about those "bunnies" for a second. Taj Gems says they symbolize "gentleness" and "affection." Isn't that sweet? Too bad the Kenyan state is showing us zero affection by allowing a "deeply entrenched syndicate" to sell our sovereignty. It is hilarious that we are supposed to be "architecting personal narratives" through our jewellery while the government is rewriting our national narrative as a DIY passport kiosk for the highest bidder. I guess "bespoke" now applies to citizenship too - if you have the cash, they will tailor-make a Kenyan identity just for you.

The diaspora is rightfully fuming, and who can blame them? They are the ones out there working three jobs to send remittances back home, only to be treated like criminals abroad because of a "bureaucratic slip-up" that is actually a national security threat. We are out here trying to position ourselves as a "modern luxury landscape," but the world sees us as a country where everything - and I mean everything - is for sale. The only thing "limited edition" about being Kenyan right now is our ability to travel without a headache.

So, the next time you are at Two Rivers Mall looking at "loose investment-grade gemstones," just remember: the most valuable thing you own is currently being auctioned off to warlords. Stay petty, stay cynical, and maybe keep your "bunny" pendant hidden - you wouldn't want to look too "Kenyan" at the airport right now. It is a messy world, darlings, and we are all just paying for the privilege of being sold out.
