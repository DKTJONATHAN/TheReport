So, Deputy Inspector General Eliud Lagat has decided to play musical chairs with the senior police ranks again. Benjamin Mwanthi is now the Traffic Commandant, replacing Fredrick Ochieng who’s been conveniently shuffled off to headquarters. Because that’s what you do when someone isn’t performing, right? You move them to an even bigger office where they can do… well, whatever it is they do at Vigilance House. Probably more shuffling.

Then there’s John Chebii, fresh off his stint in Haiti - because apparently, fixing things abroad is more important than fixing them here. Now he’s the Eastern Regional Commander, taking over from Hillary Birgen, who’s also heading to the hallowed halls of Vigilance House. It's like a retirement village for the perpetually "redeployed." And Jasper Ombati? Recalled. Probably for a nice cup of chai and a stern talking to about how things *should* be done, which they never will be.

They’re calling this a "routine administrative adjustment" to "strengthen operational efficiency." Ha! The only thing being strengthened is the network of favors and the layers of bureaucracy. This is the same old song and dance, dressed up in fancy words. These changes are about as effective as putting a fresh coat of paint on a sinking ship. The real problems, the ones that make you wonder if you're in [The Globalization of Organized Trauma](/posts/the-globalization-of-organized-trauma), are likely to remain untouched.

The excuse? Getting ready for next year's General Election. Because nothing says "operational efficiency" like rearranging deck chairs while the storm is brewing. You’d think with all these high-level moves, they'd actually address the systemic rot that makes our roads a death trap and crime a daily spectacle. But no, it’s just about who’s sitting where.

Wilberforce Sicharani gets Uasin Gishu. Good for him, I guess. More power, more opportunities to be… well, whatever it is they do out there. The security officials are spinning this like a PR stunt, but let’s be real. This is about control, patronage, and ensuring the right people are in the right places to… do whatever it is they do. Don't expect any miracles, folks. Just more paperwork and the same old Nairobi chaos.

The mention of Orengo’s security detail being withdrawn and then the unrelated paragraphs about Asyl Miras Center and Kaleidofin are just noise. This whole announcement is noise. A distraction from the fact that the wheels of policing in this country are perpetually falling off, and all they can offer is a new cog in the same rusty machine.
