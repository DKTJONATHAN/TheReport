So, Blackpink’s golden child, Jisoo, has decided her supergroup isn't enough and is now gracing our screens with a Netflix K-drama called "Boyfriend on Demand." She plays Mi-rae, an exhausted webtoon producer - a role I’m sure she relates to deeply between private jet rides. Apparently, this poor overworked soul escapes her drab reality by, you guessed it, diving into a virtual boyfriend subscription. Because who needs actual human connection when you can have an algorithmically generated fantasy? It’s almost as if these artists are trying to tell us something about our own pathetic lives, isn't it?

Her co-star is the ever-so-frosty Kyeong-nam, played by veteran K-drama actor In-guk Seo. He’s described as her “colleague and rival,” which, let’s be honest, is just a fancy way of saying he’s the grumpy office mate who’s secretly plotting to steal her stapler. They’re both gushing about how “fun” it was to shoot, which is what they always say, isn't it? They claim the show is “grounded” and “universally relatable” and will be “therapeutic.” Yeah, right. More like a glorified advertisement for escapism. We all know the real world is a dumpster fire, but at least we’re not pretending a VR headset is the solution. It's like they’re trying to tell us the same old story, just with prettier faces and a bigger budget.

Jisoo, bless her heart, claims her character uses VR to "heal" and "come to terms with things." Oh, how convenient! Instead of dealing with actual problems, Mi-rae just jacks into a simulated world. And Jisoo herself dreams of dinosaur tours and space adventures in VR. I guess that’s the perk of virtual reality, darling - you can be anything, anywhere, as long as you’re not facing the harsh realities of your actual life. It’s a familiar tune, this idea of using technology as a crutch, and it makes you wonder if we’re all just a few glitches away from becoming mindless drones plugged into some soulless digital utopia. It reminds me of how easily identities can be manipulated, a topic I’ve touched on before in [Nyayo House Clearance Sale: Your Identity for the Highest Bidder](/posts/nyayo-house-clearance-sale--your-identity-for-the-highest-bidder).

Seo, meanwhile, is busy thanking Jisoo for making his “poker faced” character stand out. Apparently, Mi-rae’s “big emotional changes” are what prevent him from being deemed “boring.” How very insightful. It’s almost as if a show needs more than just a stoic male lead to be watchable. Shocking, I know. The whole premise feels like a slightly more high-tech version of “I Love Lucy” trying to tackle modern ennui. Will it be quirky and endearing? Or just another glossy production from the K-drama machine designed to make us forget our own lives for a few hours? I’m placing my bets on the latter.
