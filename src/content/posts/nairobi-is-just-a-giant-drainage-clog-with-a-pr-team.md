Welcome to the Green City in the Sun, where three hours of rain turns the entire capital into a poorly designed water park. The Kenya Red Cross spent Friday listing roads like they were reading off a casualty list, because in Nairobi, a heavy downpour is basically a declaration of war. From Pipeline to Westlands, the city has officially surrendered to the water, leaving property damaged and residents wondering why they pay taxes for infrastructure that dissolves at the first sight of a cloud.

The body count is at eight and counting, because apparently, drowning in a city street is a standard Friday night activity now. Six people were swept away by rivers that used to be roads, and two more were electrocuted because Nairobi's power grid is about as safe as a wet toaster. While the Kenya Red Cross teams are out there doing search and rescue, the rest of us are left to realize that our lives are worth less than the bitumen on Uhuru Highway.

The flooding didn't discriminate between the slums and the suburbs. While some argue that [Runda Is Just A Fancy Name For A Crime Scene](/posts/runda-is-just-a-fancy-name-for-a-crime-scene), the real crime this week was the state of South C and Nairobi West. Over 50 cars were ruined in South C alone, submerged in water that probably contains things no human should ever touch. If you were stuck on Thika Superhighway or Lang'ata Road near T-Mall, you weren't in traffic - you were in a floating graveyard for Japanese imports.

Nairobi police boss George Seda says teams were out there, but it was impossible to do much while the city was underwater. That is the Nairobi way: wait for the disaster to happen, then explain why you could not do anything about it. Over 100 vehicles are now scrap metal, overturned on roadsides like dead fish, all because the city's drainage system is a relic from a time when the population was a fraction of what it is now.

Now the government has activated emergency response measures after the Kenya Meteorological Department gave the warning that everyone already knew was coming. It is the same old script. They warn us it will rain, the rain kills us, and then they act surprised. As long as the people in charge keep eating the money meant for pipes and planning, we will continue to swim to work every time the sky turns grey.
